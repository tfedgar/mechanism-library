Peliosis hepatis is a rare condition characterized by blood-filled cavities within the liver parenchyma, typically resulting from chronic drug exposure or infection PMID 16078496. It is mechanistically linked to hepatotoxicity from agents like anabolic steroids and tamoxifen, which can cause sinusoidal collapse and blood accumulation without fibrosis PMID 41850840. This condition matters because it represents a severe, potentially fatal structural liver injury that can lead to rupture or hemorrhage PMID 810650.
