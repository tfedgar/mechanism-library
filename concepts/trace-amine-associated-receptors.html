trace amine-associated receptors

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Trace amine-associated receptors (TAARs) are G protein-coupled receptors that bind endogenous trace amines like tyramine and octopamine to modulate the sympathetic nervous system PMID 36551221. TAAR1 agonists, such as JP-14, enhance insulin secretion and suppress appetite, offering a mechanism to treat obesity and metabolic disorders PMID 41155326. Additionally, ligands like guanabenz target TAAR1 alongside α2-adrenoceptors to improve metabolic and cardiovascular parameters PMID 37624466.
