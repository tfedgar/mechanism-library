MECHANISMS

Androgen receptor

AR (NR3C4)

The androgen receptor (AR, gene NR3C4) is the primary target of testosterone, DHT, anabolic-androgenic steroids (AAS) and SARMs. Ligand binding -> nuclear translocation -> transcription of anabolic genes. ESTABLISHED effects: skeletal-muscle hypertrophy; stimulation of renal/hepatic erythropoietin and suppression of hepcidin (-> raised red cells); sebaceous gland and (with DHT) hair-follicle effects; prostate growth; negative feedback on the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis. SARMs are tissue-selective AR modulators (more muscle/bone, less prostate/skin) but still suppress the HPG axis. Reported issues linked: acne, hair loss (via DHT), polycythemia, testicular shutdown/infertility, prostate symptoms, mood/aggression. Many AAS 'side effects' are NOT direct AR effects but downstream (estrogen conversion, HPG suppression, erythropoiesis, hepatic lipase).
