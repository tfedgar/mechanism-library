hormone-sensitive lipase

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Hormone-sensitive lipase (HSL) is the rate-limiting enzyme that removes the first fatty acid from stored triglycerides in adipocytes [Established]. It plays a critical role in mobilizing energy by breaking down lipids, such as when cAMP signaling increases its activity to reduce fat deposition [ 34871537]. Additionally, HSL helps deliver cholesterol for steroidogenesis by hydrolyzing stored cholesteryl esters in tissues like the adrenal gland [ 17208360].
