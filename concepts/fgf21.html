FGF21

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Fibroblast growth factor 21 (FGF21) is a circulating hormone that binds to the FGFR1/beta-klotho complex to activate signaling in white adipose tissue and muscle [Established]. This interaction upregulates genes involved in glucose uptake and lipogenesis, providing insulin-sensitizing and metabolic effects [Established]. Mechanistically, FGF21 levels are regulated by growth hormone and can be inactivated by fibroblast activation protein, linking it to collagen turnover and liver fat reduction [Evidence].
