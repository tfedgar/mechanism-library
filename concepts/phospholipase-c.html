phospholipase C

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Phospholipase C (PLC) is an enzyme that, upon activation by G-proteins, cleaves membrane lipids to generate the second messengers IP3 and DAG [Established] 20056829). IP3 triggers the release of intracellular calcium, while DAG activates protein kinase C (PKC), creating a signaling cascade that regulates processes like hormone secretion and ion channel modulation [Established] 20056829). This pathway is critical for translating extracellular receptor binding into specific cellular responses, such as the reduction of potassium currents in cardiac myocytes [Evidence] 20056829).
