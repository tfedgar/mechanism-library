CREB

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

CREB (cAMP response element-binding protein) is a transcription factor that regulates gene expression, often activated via phosphorylation by signaling cascades like the ERK pathway PMID 21220406. It serves as a critical downstream target for cellular processes, including the modulation of mitochondrial function and energy metabolism through the CREB/PGC-1α axis PMID 41204270. Dysregulation of CREB activity, such as through mutations in its coactivator CBP, can lead to developmental defects like those seen in Rubinstein-Taybi syndrome PMID 22133875.
