endothelial nitric oxide synthase

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Endothelial nitric oxide synthase (eNOS) is an enzyme that produces nitric oxide to regulate vascular tone and maintain endothelial health [Established]. It requires cofactors like BH4 and is activated by phosphorylation at Ser1177 to exert anti-inflammatory and anti-arteriosclerotic effects [ 24827148]. Impaired eNOS function is mechanistically linked to arterial stiffness, hypertension, and atherosclerosis risk [ 24827148].
