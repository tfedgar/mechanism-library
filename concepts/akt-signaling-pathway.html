Akt signaling pathway

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

The Akt signaling pathway is a critical intracellular cascade that promotes cell survival and metabolism by regulating processes like protein synthesis and apoptosis [Established]. Mechanistically, it functions by phosphorylating downstream targets such as GSK3β and p70S6K, which helps cells withstand environmental stresses like UVB radiation and supports growth factor-mediated survival [Evidence] PMID 12070137. This pathway is frequently activated by upstream signals like IGF-1 and PI3K to coordinate cellular responses to metabolic and immune cues [Evidence] PMID 40805020.
