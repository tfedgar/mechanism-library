estrogen receptor

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Estrogen receptors (ERs) are nuclear and membrane proteins that bind estradiol to regulate gene transcription and rapid signaling pathways [Established] 23562765). This binding allows estrogens to control diverse physiological functions, including reproduction, metabolism, and neuronal protection [Established] 38904201). The receptors' ability to interact with other nuclear receptors, such as the androgen receptor, provides a mechanism for cross-talk in both healthy and pathological states [Evidence] 41446225).
