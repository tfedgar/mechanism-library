glucocorticoid receptor

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

The glucocorticoid receptor (GR) is a nuclear receptor that mediates the effects of steroid hormones by regulating gene transcription [Established]. It drives physiological responses such as metabolic homeostasis and stress adaptation, while its prolonged activation can trigger skeletal muscle atrophy [Evidence] [ 40806744]. Additionally, GR signaling in the placenta regulates insulin-like growth factor-I, linking prenatal hormone exposure to fetal development [Evidence] [ 27500692].
