ghrelin

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Ghrelin is a stomach-derived peptide that acts as an endogenous ligand for the growth hormone secretagogue receptor (GHS-R1a) [Established] 36009817). Mechanistically, it drives appetite by altering hypothalamic circuit function and regulates energy balance through cross-talk with leptin [Evidence] 23565498). It also exerts cardiovascular protective effects by suppressing sympathetic activity and promoting angiogenesis [Evidence] 36009817).
