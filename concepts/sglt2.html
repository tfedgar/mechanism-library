SGLT2

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

SGLT2 is a transporter in the proximal renal tubules responsible for reabsorbing the majority of filtered glucose [Established]. Inhibiting SGLT2 reduces glucose reabsorption, thereby increasing urinary glucose excretion and lowering the renal threshold for glucose [Established]. This blockade also increases sodium delivery to the distal tubule, which contributes to glomerular hemodynamic regulation [Evidence] 38801992). These mechanisms allow SGLT2 inhibitors to reduce cardiovascular and kidney disease risks beyond simple glucose lowering [Evidence] 39142859).
