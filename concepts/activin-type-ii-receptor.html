activin type II receptor

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Activin type II receptors (ActIIR) are central regulators of skeletal muscle atrophy that signal downstream effects of ligands like myostatin and activin A [Established] 41896993). Pharmacological inhibition of these receptors blocks atrophy signals, thereby preserving muscle fibers and improving motor outcomes after nerve injury [Evidence] 41896993). This mechanism addresses secondary muscle loss that persists even after inflammation resolves in immune-mediated neuropathies [Evidence] 41896993).
