MECHANISMS

gonadotropin-releasing hormone

Gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) is a hypothalamic peptide that drives pulsatile release of gonadotropins from the anterior pituitary to regulate gonadal activity [Established] 42309751). Its secretion is tightly controlled by steroid feedback and neural networks, such as the KNDy system, which links stress to reproductive function [Evidence] 42401315). Because GnRH analogues suppress the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis, they are mechanistically used to treat sex hormone-dependent diseases like endometriosis and prostate cancer [Evidence] 37622483).
