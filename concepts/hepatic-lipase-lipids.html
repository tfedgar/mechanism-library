Hepatic lipase & lipids

LIPC / CETP / HMGCR

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS (anadrol, dianabol, winstrol, superdrol) markedly INDUCE hepatic lipase (LIPC) and shift lipid metabolism → sharply LOWERED HDL-C and RAISED LDL-C (atherogenic dyslipidemia). This is a characteristic, ESTABLISHED, often dramatic effect of orals (HDL can fall to single digits), more than of injectable testosterone. Statins (HMGCR) and ezetimibe address LDL but do not fully reverse the HDL crash; stopping the oral does. Reported issues linked: crashed HDL, raised LDL/ApoB on lipid panels; long-term atherosclerosis concern (a CV-risk driver independent of blood pressure).
