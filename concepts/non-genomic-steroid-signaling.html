MECHANISMS

non-genomic steroid signaling

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Non-genomic steroid signaling refers to rapid, receptor-mediated cellular responses that occur independently of nuclear transcription, such as the activation of kinase cascades like PI3K/Akt or ERK PMID 41180179. This mechanism is mechanistically distinct from canonical genomic actions because it regulates immediate cellular functions and metabolic balance without requiring new gene expression PMID 15862817. It matters because these rapid pathways can drive significant physiological outcomes, including neuroprotection and oocyte maturation, often complementing or acting alongside traditional nuclear receptor signaling PMID 41180179.
