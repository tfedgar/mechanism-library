hepatic lipase

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Hepatic lipase is an enzyme that hydrolyzes lipids in HDL and LDL particles, a process mechanistically driven by the induction of the *LIPC* gene [ 15307663]. Oral anabolic steroids markedly increase hepatic lipase activity, which accelerates the clearance of HDL and alters LDL composition, leading to a characteristic crash in HDL-C and rise in LDL-C. This enzyme-mediated shift is a primary driver of atherogenic dyslipidemia in users of 17-alpha-alkylated androgens.
