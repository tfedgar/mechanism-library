dipeptidyl peptidase-4

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-4) is an enzyme that breaks down incretin hormones, so inhibiting it increases circulating levels of intact GLP-1 and GIP [Evidence] 41631452). This mechanism enhances insulin secretion and improves metabolic control in type 2 diabetes [Established]. While DPP-4 inhibitors show benefits for fibrosis and cardiac function in animal models [Evidence] 40023609), they generally do not significantly reduce major adverse cardiovascular events in human trials [Evidence] 32770420).
