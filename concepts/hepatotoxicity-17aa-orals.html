MECHANISMS

Hepatotoxicity (17aa orals)

Liver / BSEP(ABCB11) / MRP2

17-alpha-alkylation makes oral AAS resistant to first-pass liver breakdown but HEPATOTOXIC. ESTABLISHED mechanisms: cholestasis (impaired bile transport via BSEP/ABCB11, MRP2), raised liver enzymes (ALT/AST/GGT), and at the severe end peliosis hepatis and hepatic adenoma. Effect is dose/duration dependent and largely reverses on cessation; injectables are far less hepatotoxic. Liver-support supplements (TUDCA, NAC) have LIMITED evidence; avoiding/limiting orals is the real mitigation. Reported issues linked: raised liver markers, jaundice/dark urine, right-upper-quadrant discomfort on orals.
