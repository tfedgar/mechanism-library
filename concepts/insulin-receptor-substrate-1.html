insulin receptor substrate 1

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Insulin receptor substrate 1 (IRS-1) is a key intracellular adaptor protein that transduces signals from insulin and IGF-1 receptors to regulate cellular metabolism [Established]. Mechanistically, it acts as a central hub for insulin signaling, where its inhibition or loss disrupts downstream pathways such as autophagy suppression in the heart and synaptic plasticity in the brain [Evidence]. Impaired IRS-1 function is a hallmark of brain insulin resistance, contributing to neuronal dysfunction in conditions like Alzheimer's disease [ 40706971].
