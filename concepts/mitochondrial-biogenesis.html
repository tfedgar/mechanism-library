mitochondrial biogenesis

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Mitochondrial biogenesis is the cellular process of generating new mitochondria to maintain energy supply and redox balance [Established] 41680976). It serves as a critical component of mitochondrial quality control, allowing cells to adapt to metabolic demands and environmental stress [Evidence] 42123457). This mechanism is essential for preserving structural integrity and function in tissues with high energy requirements, such as the heart and skeletal muscle [Evidence] 26370848).
