diacylglycerol

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Diacylglycerol (DAG) is a lipid intermediate in fat synthesis that also acts as a messenger in signaling systems. It is produced when phospholipase C hydrolyzes membrane phospholipids, subsequently activating protein kinase C to regulate processes like cell differentiation. This role is established in growth hormone-induced preadipocyte differentiation, where DAG production drives the protein kinase C signaling pathway.
