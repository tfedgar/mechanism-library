sympathetic nervous system

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

The sympathetic nervous system is an autonomic pathway that triggers "fight-or-flight" responses, such as increasing heart rate and releasing glucose, to support sudden physical activity [Established]. Mechanistically, excessive sympathetic excitation driven by endoplasmic reticulum stress or autophagy dysfunction serves as a pathological basis for cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease PMID 41683708. Additionally, sympathetic tone actively inhibits bone mass accrual, a function that can be modulated by parasympathetic signaling PMID 20197056.
