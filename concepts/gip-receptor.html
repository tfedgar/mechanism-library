GIP receptor

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

The GIP receptor is a G-protein-coupled receptor that primarily signals through Gαs proteins to increase intracellular cAMP levels [Established] 38984950). This signaling cascade regulates pancreatic β-cell insulin secretion and modulates appetite centers in the brain [Evidence] 39114288). Consequently, activating this receptor contributes to glucose homeostasis and food intake regulation, often enhancing the effects of GLP-1 agonists [Evidence] 37897727).
