beta-oxidation

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Beta-oxidation is the established [Established] metabolic pathway that sequentially cleaves fatty acid chains into acetyl-CoA units within the mitochondria to generate energy. This process relies on specific enzymes like enoyl-CoA hydratase and thiolase, while requiring additional isomerases to handle the cis-double bonds found in unsaturated fats PMID 41901314. It matters mechanistically because it links lipid breakdown to the citric acid cycle and ketone body synthesis, providing essential fuel and protecting against ischemic damage.
