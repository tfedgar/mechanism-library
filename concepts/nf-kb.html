NF-kB

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

NF-kB is a pivotal transcription factor that mediates inflammatory responses and regulates various biological processes and diseases [Established] 38433280). It functions within key signaling cascades, such as the TLR4/NF-kB/MAPK pathway, to modulate mitochondrial autophagy and tissue injury [Evidence] 39487520). Consequently, inhibiting NF-kB activation serves as a mechanism to reduce inflammation in conditions like diabetic cardiomyopathy and neuroinflammation [Evidence] 41613961; 42043487).
