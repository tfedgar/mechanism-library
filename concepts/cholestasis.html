cholestasis

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Cholestasis is a condition characterized by the impaired flow of bile, often resulting in elevated bilirubin and jaundice PMID 16436902. It serves as a key driver for liver fibrosis, contributing to the progression of end-stage liver disease PMID 42087905. Mechanistically, the condition involves complex cellular responses, such as the loss of β-catenin in cholangiocytes, which promotes hepatocyte reprogramming and vascular remodeling PMID 41514249.
