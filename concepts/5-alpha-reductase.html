MECHANISMS

5-alpha-reductase

SRD5A1 / SRD5A2

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

5-alpha-reductases (SRD5A1, SRD5A2) convert testosterone → dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a far more potent AR agonist, in skin, hair follicle, prostate. ESTABLISHED: DHT drives androgenic alopecia (male-pattern hair loss) and prostate growth. Finasteride (SRD5A2) and dutasteride (both isoenzymes) inhibit it → used to slow hair loss/BPH. IMPORTANT second role: SRD5A also produces NEUROSTEROIDS (e.g. allopregnanolone from progesterone), so 5-AR inhibition can affect mood/anxiety/CNS — the proposed basis of 'post-finasteride syndrome' (CONTESTED, persistence debated). Reported issues linked: hair loss (DHT-driven), and for 5-ARi users: libido/erectile changes, mood/cognitive symptoms.
