phosphocreatine

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Phosphocreatine is a high-energy phosphate compound that acts as a rapid buffer to maintain ATP levels during periods of intense cellular demand, such as muscle contraction PMID 39796530. It functions mechanistically through the phosphocreatine shuttle, facilitating efficient energy transfer between mitochondria and sites of ATP consumption PMID 41373706. This system is critical for sustaining cellular energy homeostasis and supporting high-energy processes in tissues like skeletal muscle and osteosarcoma cells PMID 41873283.
