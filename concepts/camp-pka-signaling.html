cAMP / PKA signaling

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

cAMP is a second messenger that activates Protein Kinase A (PKA), which then phosphorylates target proteins to alter their activity [Established] (). This signaling cascade regulates diverse cellular processes, including cardiac contractility and macrophage polarization [Evidence] 15045518, 40240584). By modifying enzymes and transcription factors, cAMP/PKA signaling directly influences cell function and gene expression [Established] ().
