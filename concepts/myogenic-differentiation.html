myogenic differentiation

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Myogenic differentiation is the process where myoblasts fuse and activate muscle-specific genes to form mature muscle fibers [Established]. This transition is mechanistically driven by the inactivation of GSK-3β, which triggers both cell fusion and gene expression changes [Evidence]. Key regulators like MyoD and Numb further control this differentiation by responding to signals from androgens and Notch pathways [Evidence].
