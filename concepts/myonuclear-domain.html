myonuclear domain

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

The myonuclear domain theory posits that new myonuclei are added to muscle fibers only when hypertrophy exceeds a specific threshold, traditionally cited as ≥26% [ 29509639]. However, recent evidence suggests this threshold may be lower, with nuclei added during smaller increases in fiber size [ 29509639]. This concept is mechanistically significant because it links nuclear addition to the permanent retention of nuclei, which underlies skeletal muscle memory [ 41346689].
