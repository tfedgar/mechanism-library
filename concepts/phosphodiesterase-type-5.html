phosphodiesterase type 5

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5) is an enzyme that breaks down cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP) in penile smooth muscle [Established]. By inhibiting PDE5, drugs like sildenafil prevent cGMP degradation, sustaining the signal for vascular relaxation and erection [Evidence] 15115191). This mechanism is central to treating erectile dysfunction, where natural cGMP signaling is often impaired [Evidence] 16042713).
