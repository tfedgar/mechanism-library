MECHANISMS

HPG axis

GnRH / LHCGR / FSHR (LH, FSH)

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

The hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis: hypothalamic GnRH → pituitary LH and FSH → testicular Leydig cells (LHCGR, make testosterone) and Sertoli cells (FSHR, support spermatogenesis). ESTABLISHED: exogenous androgens/AAS suppress GnRH/LH/FSH → collapse of INTRATESTICULAR testosterone and impaired spermatogenesis → testicular atrophy, low sperm count/INFERTILITY, and post-cycle hypogonadism. This is an INDIRECT effect (feedback), not a primary-target action. hCG mimics LH (LHCGR agonist) to preserve testicular function/fertility; SERMs (clomiphene) raise LH/FSH centrally. Recovery after long/high-dose use can be slow or incomplete. Reported issues linked: infertility, testicular shrinkage, post-cycle low-T (fatigue, low libido, ED, depression).
