GH / IGF-1 axis

GH / IGF1 / IGF1R

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Growth hormone (GH) and GH secretagogues (ipamorelin, sermorelin, CJC-1295) raise IGF-1 (made in liver), which signals via IGF1R for tissue growth. ESTABLISHED effects: increased lean tissue, lipolysis, collagen; at higher/chronic doses, INSULIN RESISTANCE and acromegaly-like tissue/organ growth (including cardiac and visceral), carpal-tunnel/edema, and possible promotion of existing neoplasia (proliferative signaling). Co-use with insulin compounds the hypoglycemia/metabolic risk. Reported issues linked: water retention, numb/tingling hands, raised blood glucose, organ/gut growth ('GH gut'), heart concerns.
