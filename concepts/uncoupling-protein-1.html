uncoupling protein 1

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Uncoupling protein 1 (UCP1) is a mitochondrial protein that dissipates the proton gradient across the inner mitochondrial membrane, allowing protons to leak back without generating ATP Evidence 40983641). This uncoupling mechanism converts chemical energy directly into heat, a process essential for thermogenesis in brown adipose tissue Established 40983641). While traditionally viewed as exclusive to thermogenic adipocytes, recent evidence suggests UCP1 may also be present in nonadipose tissues, though its specific roles there remain under debate Speculation 41348594).
