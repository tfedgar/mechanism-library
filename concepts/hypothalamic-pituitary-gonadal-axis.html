hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

The hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis is a regulatory cascade controlling reproductive development, sexual maturation, and fertility [Established]. It operates through a coordinated sequence where hypothalamic neurons release GnRH to stimulate gonadotropin secretion, ultimately driving gonadal hormone production and gametogenesis [ 31147553]. This axis integrates internal signals, such as leptin and insulin, to link energy availability with reproductive function [ 42219385], and external cues, like melatonin, to adjust seasonal breeding patterns [ 41169682].
