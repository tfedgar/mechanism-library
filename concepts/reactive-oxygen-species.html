Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Reactive oxygen species (ROS) are chemical moieties that act as essential signaling molecules for cellular homeostasis at physiological concentrations [Established]. When produced in excess, they cause oxidative stress, leading to damage of DNA, proteins, and lipids [Evidence]. This dual role makes them key mediators in both normal physiology and the pathogenesis of chronic diseases [Evidence]. [ 40806653]
