satellite cells

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Satellite cells are the resident stem cells of skeletal muscle that drive tissue regeneration and hypertrophy by proliferating and differentiating into new muscle fibers [Established] 42165230). Their activity is mechanistically regulated by signaling pathways such as AKT/mTOR and calcium-dependent cues, which maintain viability and protein synthesis under stress [Evidence] 40531264, 42317724). These cells decline in number and function with age, contributing to sarcopenia, but can be enhanced by stimuli like exercise and electrical stimulation to alleviate muscle loss [Evidence] 42317724).
