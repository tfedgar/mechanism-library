adenosine receptor

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Adenosine receptors are a protein family in humans that regulate physiological processes by binding adenosine, a neuromodulator that generally promotes sleep and suppresses arousal [Established]. Caffeine acts as an antagonist at these receptors, particularly the A1 and A2a subtypes, thereby blocking adenosine's inhibitory effects to increase alertness and modify behaviors like anxiety PMID 36584765. This mechanism is significant because adenosine signaling influences critical systems, including cognitive function in ADHD and mitochondrial health in skeletal muscle PMID 35111479.
