MECHANISMS

Erythropoiesis & iron

EPO / EPOR / HAMP(hepcidin) / ERFE

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Androgens stimulate erythropoietin (EPO) and act on the EPO receptor (EPOR), and SUPPRESS hepcidin (HAMP) while raising erythroferrone (ERFE) → more iron available for red-cell production. ESTABLISHED NET EFFECT: raised red blood cell mass / hematocrit (secondary polycythemia/erythrocytosis), especially with testosterone, boldenone (EQ), and oxymetholone. Raised hematocrit increases blood viscosity → hypertension and thrombotic risk (stroke/MI/VTE concern). Managed by dose reduction or therapeutic phlebotomy/donation. Reported issues linked: high hematocrit/hemoglobin on bloodwork, headaches, hypertension, clotting concern.
