AMPK

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

AMPK is a conserved enzyme complex that acts as a central cellular energy sensor, becoming activated when intracellular AMP levels rise during nutrient stress or exercise PMID 41574046. It restores energy balance by inhibiting ATP-consuming anabolic processes, such as fatty acid and cholesterol synthesis, while simultaneously promoting catabolic pathways like mitochondrial β-oxidation. This dual regulation allows AMPK to maintain intracellular homeostasis across various tissues, including skeletal muscle, where it is essential for glucose uptake and mitochondrial content.
