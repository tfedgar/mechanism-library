biased agonism

Mechanism at a glance

molecularcellulareffectcaveat

Biased agonism is a pharmacodynamic characteristic where a ligand selectively activates specific downstream signaling pathways (such as cAMP) while minimizing others (such as β-arrestin recruitment) at the same receptor [Evidence]. This mechanism matters because it can decouple therapeutic efficacy from adverse effects, such as receptor desensitization, thereby optimizing safety and tolerability in treatments like those for diabetes and obesity PMID 40461942. By fine-tuning GPCR-mediated signaling, biased agonists offer a potential strategy to improve the therapeutic index of next-generation medicines PMID 38451873.
