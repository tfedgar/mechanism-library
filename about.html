How each monograph is made

Every compound is researched against a 100k-chunk biomedical corpus (PubMed, full-text reviews, trials) and the public bio-databases, then written as a plain-language mechanism with a process diagram. A second, adversarial pass checks it for over-certainty, overclaiming, and citation mismatches before it ships. Each entry is then expanded into a full reference — receptor pharmacology, pharmacokinetics, dose-response, a side-effect-mechanism table, an evidence table, comparisons, history, open questions, and monitoring.

Evidence tiers

The signature here is honesty about human evidence. Every claim carries a certainty tag, and every compound a tier:

  • Human / approved — RCTs and/or regulatory approval.
  • Limited human data — some human data, small/early/unapproved.
  • Rodent / preclinical — no human data; rodent and in-vitro only.

Citation integrity

Every PMID in the library is checked against NCBI to confirm it resolves to a real record and is not retracted. At last audit: 3,129 unique citations, 100% resolving, 0 fabricated, with 3 retracted papers found and flagged (Dihexa, Noopept, Taurine) rather than hidden. References on each page link straight to PubMed.

What this is not

This is educational mechanism content, not medical advice, and it contains no dosing protocols. "How it works" and "what to watch" are not "how to use it." Many of these compounds are unapproved, illegal in sport, or studied only in animals — the tiers say so plainly.
