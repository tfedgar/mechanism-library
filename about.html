How each monograph is made

Every compound is researched against a 100,000+ chunk biomedical corpus, then written as a plain-language mechanism with a process diagram. A second, adversarial pass checks it for over-certainty, overclaiming, and citation mismatches before it ships. Each entry is then expanded into a full reference — receptor pharmacology, pharmacokinetics, dose-response, a side-effect-mechanism table, an evidence table, comparisons, history, open questions, and monitoring.

Where the evidence comes from

Grounded answers are built only from evidence-grade sources — never from forum or social anecdote (that is kept strictly separate, and used only to understand what people are asking):

  • Peer-reviewed literature — PubMed & Europe PMC abstracts, with a dedicated pull for meta-analyses / systematic reviews (top of the evidence pyramid) and toxicology / adverse-event case reports (the harm-mechanism side).
  • Curated pharmacology — mechanism-of-action from ChEMBL, and quantitative binding affinities (Ki/EC50/IC50) from the IUPHAR/BPS Guide to PHARMACOLOGY, each with the primary citation.
  • Official drug labels — FDA / DailyMed prescribing information: mechanism, boxed warnings, contraindications, and interactions for the approved compounds.
  • Clinical-trial records, full-text reviews, and reference textbooks.

Evidence tiers

The signature here is honesty about human evidence. Every claim carries a certainty tag, and every compound a tier:

  • Strong human data — backed by human clinical trials (RCTs / controlled studies).
  • Limited human data — some human data, small/early/unapproved.
  • Rodent / preclinical — no human data; rodent and in-vitro only.

Citation integrity

Every PMID in the library is checked against NCBI to confirm it resolves to a real record. Beyond that, every source is screened against the Retraction Watch dataset at index time — retracted papers and expressions of concern are excluded from the evidence tiers before they can ever ground an answer, not caught after the fact. At last audit: 3,129 unique citations, 100% resolving, 0 fabricated. References on each page link straight to PubMed.

What this is not

This is educational mechanism content, not medical advice, and it contains no dosing protocols. "How it works" and "what to watch" are not "how to use it." Many of these compounds are unapproved, illegal in sport, or studied only in animals — the tiers say so plainly.
