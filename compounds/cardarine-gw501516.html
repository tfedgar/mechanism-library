Cardarine

The "exercise in a pill" — it rewires which fuel your muscles burn, pushing them to torch fat instead of sugar. Real metabolic effects, but it was abandoned by its maker after rodents grew tumors, and there is essentially zero long-term human safety data.

Limited human data 17 citations
Class
Selective PPARδ (peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor delta) agonist
Primary target
PPARδ (also written PPARβ/δ) — a nuclear receptor / transcription factor highly expressed in skeletal muscle
Human status
UNAPPROVED and investigational — it is not, and never has been, an approved medicine anywhere. GlaxoSmithKline developed it for dyslipidemia/metabolic disease and took it only to Phase 2 (ChEMBL lists max clinical phase 2; CHEMBL38943). Human data are thin: a handful of small, short (roughly 2-12 week) trials in healthy or metabolic-syndrome subjects showing it lowers triglycerides, raises HDL and boosts fat oxidation (Sprecher 2007 PMID 17110604; Riserus 2008 PMID 18024853; Olson 2012 PMID 22814748). Crucially, development was HALTED after long-term rodent studies showed it caused cancers in multiple organs — so there are NO long-term human safety studies, and the dose/duration people buy online (often 10-20 mg/day for 6-8 weeks; PMID 32298044) has never been validated for safety in humans. The endurance/"exercise mimetic" effect is established in mice (Narkar 2008 PMID 18674809) but has NOT been demonstrated in a controlled human performance trial.

At a glance

  • Shifts muscle fuel preference from glucose to fat, increasing fatty-acid oxidation (Evidence-human for fat oxidation: Riserus 2008 PMID 18024853; mechanism Dressel 2003 PMID 14525954)
  • Improves blood lipids — lowers triglycerides and LDL, raises HDL (Evidence-human: Sprecher 2007 PMID 17110604; Olson 2012 PMID 22814748)
  • Increases running endurance and remodels muscle toward fatigue-resistant oxidative fibers (Evidence-rodent: Narkar 2008 PMID 18674809)
  • Anti-inflammatory and insulin-sensitizing signaling in metabolic tissues (Evidence-rodent/cell)
  • Carcinogenic — caused tumors in multiple organs in long-term animal studies, the reason development stopped (Evidence-rodent / Contested mechanism: Peters 2011 PMID 22037942)
  • Real-world liver injury reported when abused, especially stacked with other compounds (Evidence-human case report: Toxics 2021 PMID 34678947)

Mechanism, step by step

  1. It flips a genetic switch in muscle Established

    Cardarine slips into muscle cells and binds PPARδ, a receptor that sits on your DNA. Think of PPARδ as a dimmer switch on a bank of 'fat-burning' genes. Cardarine is the hand that turns the dimmer up. This is a slow, gene-level effect — not a fast stimulant 'hit.' PMID 32298044; ChEMBL CHEMBL38943

  2. Muscle switches fuel from sugar to fat Established

    Once those genes are on, the muscle changes its preferred fuel: instead of mostly burning glucose (sugar), it starts pulling in and burning fatty acids. It's like reprogramming a hybrid car to run mainly on the fat tank. PMID 14525954; PMID 32298044

  3. It revs up fat-burning machinery Established

    PPARδ turns up the genes for the enzymes that haul fat into mitochondria (the cell's furnaces) and break it down — including CPT1, the gatekeeper enzyme for fat burning. More machinery means more fat oxidized per minute. PMID 14525954; PMID 18024853

  4. In rodents it teams up with AMPK as an 'exercise mimetic' Evidence-rodent

    The famous 2008 mouse study showed Cardarine works hand-in-hand with AMPK — the same energy-sensor your body activates during real exercise. Together they switch muscle toward slow-twitch, fatigue-resistant fibers, and the mice ran significantly longer. This is why it's nicknamed 'exercise in a pill.' Important: this was demonstrated in mice, not proven in a human performance trial. PMID 18674809

  5. Blood lipids improve in people Evidence-human

    In short human trials, this fat-burning reprogramming showed up in the blood: triglycerides and LDL ('bad' cholesterol) dropped and HDL ('good' cholesterol) rose, with measurably higher fat oxidation. These are real human findings — but the trials were small and brief. PMID 17110604; PMID 18024853; PMID 22814748

  6. The same pathway can also feed tumors Evidence-rodent

    Here's the catch. PPARδ doesn't only run metabolism — it also influences cell growth and survival. In long-term animal studies Cardarine caused cancers in several organs, which is why its maker shut the program down. The pro-cancer mechanism (especially in colon) is studied and debated rather than fully settled, but the tumor findings were real enough to end development. GSK 2-year rodent carcinogenicity program (regulatory; multi-organ tumors) — halted development

  7. In the real world it has injured livers Evidence-human

    Beyond the cancer signal, a documented hospital case describes serious liver damage in a man who bought Cardarine online and stacked it with the SARM ostarine. Unregulated products, unknown purity, and stacking multiply the risk. PMID 34678947

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral (the form abused for performance). It is a free carboxylic acid small molecule (C21H18F3NO3S2, MW 453.5), not an ester or peptide and not a prodrug (ChEMBL flags prodrug=0). [Established — ChEMBL CHEMBL38943; PMID 32298044]
Absorption / bioavailability
Orally active in every species tested (mouse, rat, monkey, human), confirming meaningful oral absorption; the molecule is highly lipophilic (calculated ALogP ~6.2, ChEMBL), consistent with good membrane permeability but poor water solubility. No published human absolute-bioavailability or Cmax/Tmax figures exist because development was halted before formal human PK was reported. [Evidence-human for oral activity (PMID 18024853); human bioavailability % = UNKNOWN]
Half-life
No peer-reviewed human plasma half-life has been published. The widely-repeated community figure of roughly 20-24 h is NOT supported by a citable human PK study in this search and should be treated as unverified. Human dosing in the one published trial was once-daily (10 mg o.d.), which is consistent with a long-ish half-life but does not establish a number. [Human t1/2 = genuinely UNKNOWN/unpublished]
Metabolism
Hepatic oxidative metabolism. Human liver microsome studies identified two major oxidized (sulfoxide/hydroxylated) metabolites, which is why parent GW1516 is often undetectable in urine while metabolites persist. [Evidence-human (in vitro microsomes): PMID 23100242; clinical correlate: in a poisoning case the parent drug was NOT found in urine due to 'extensive metabolism' while still detectable in blood/hair, PMID 34678947]
Excretion
Lipophilic parent and metabolites are incorporated into hair (detected at pg/mg levels) and cleared largely as oxidized metabolites; renal excretion of intact parent is minor. Specific fractional urinary/biliary recovery and clearance values are not published for humans. [Evidence-human: PMID 32298044, 34678947]
Notes
Not a prodrug, no ester, no special depot. The carboxylic-acid head group is the receptor-binding 'acidic anchor' typical of PPAR ligands AND the reason it deposits in hair (first carboxylic-acid drug shown to do so, PMID 32298044). CNS/BBB penetration is plausible given high lipophilicity (rodent PPARδ-agonist neuro-inflammation work exists) but is not quantified in humans. Pharmaceutical-grade material exists as multiple crystal polymorphs (PMID 38794285, 40006519) — relevant because illicit/'research-chemical' product purity and form vary widely.

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

HUMAN: Only one controlled dose has ever been formally tested — 10 mg once daily for 2 weeks in moderately overweight men (n=6 on drug). At that single dose it produced fasting triglycerides −30%, ApoB −26%, LDL −23%, insulin −11%, liver fat −20%, urinary isoprostanes (oxidative stress) −30%, with HDL unchanged, plus increased skeletal-muscle CPT1b expression and increased dietary-fat oxidation. No human dose-RESPONSE curve exists — there is no published 5 mg vs 10 mg vs 20 mg comparison, no long-term human data, and no human endurance-performance trial. The commonly cited 'abuse' range is 10-20 mg/day for 6-8 weeks, but this is sourced from doping-surveillance/forensic reports, NOT from controlled trials. [Evidence-human, single dose: PMID 18024853; abuse-range description: PMID 32298044]. RODENT: Dose-dependent effects on fat oxidation, lipoprotein profile and, with training, oxidative (type I) muscle fiber proportion and running endurance; in mice GW501516 plus exercise synergistically raised endurance, and in another study it raised the proportion of succinate-dehydrogenase-positive (oxidative) fibers in both trained and untrained mice. Rodent metabolic/lipid doses are typically in the ~1-10 mg/kg/day range. [Evidence-rodent: PMID 18674809, 25943561]. KEY GAP: human muscle/endurance/body-composition dose-response is essentially unknown; the 'endurance pill' reputation rests almost entirely on rodent data.

How it compares

  • vs GW0742 (GSK2433548) — the sister compound from the same discovery paper
    same: Also a high-potency, ~1000-fold-selective synthetic PPARδ agonist on the same thiazole scaffold (compound 7l in Sznaidman 2003); same fat-oxidation/PGC-1α biology.
    different: GW0742 stayed a research tool used in cardiac/CNS PPARδ studies and was never pushed toward human performance use; Cardarine is the one that escaped into the supplement/doping market. [Established — PMID 12699745]
  • vs Seladelpar (MBX-8025) / elafibranor — PPARδ-active drugs that reached LATE-stage human trials
    same: Share PPARδ agonism and the same downstream lipid/fat-oxidation rationale; pursued for dyslipidemia, NASH, and primary biliary cholangitis.
    different: These were carried through large, monitored human trials and (seladelpar) toward approval in liver disease, with formal human safety data — the opposite of Cardarine, whose human program was abandoned over rodent carcinogenicity and which now exists only as an unapproved black-market 'research chemical'. [Background/field knowledge — flagged Contested where not directly in this search's retrieved abstracts]
  • vs AICAR (AMPK agonist) — the 'other half' of the exercise-mimetic story
    same: Studied head-to-head with GW501516 by Narkar 2008 as an orally active 'exercise mimetic'; both feed the AMPK–PPARδ endurance axis.
    different: AICAR directly activates AMPK (the upstream energy sensor) and alone raised endurance ~44% in sedentary mice, whereas GW501516 alone needed training/AMPK input for full endurance effect — i.e., they act at different nodes of the same pathway. [Evidence-rodent — PMID 18674809]
  • vs SARMs (e.g., ostarine/MK-2866, RAD-140)
    same: Frequently stacked with and mis-sold alongside Cardarine in performance products; both are non-approved, WADA-banned, and share an unregulated supply chain with frequent adulteration.
    different: Mechanistically unrelated — SARMs are androgen-receptor modulators (anabolic/hormonal), whereas Cardarine is a metabolic PPARδ transcription-factor agonist with NO direct anabolic/androgenic action. Many 'cardarine' harms in case reports actually trace to the co-used SARM or undisclosed hormones. [Evidence-human — PMID 34678947, 39145153]

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Anadrol compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both carry a hepatotoxicity signal that would summate via different routes. Anadrol is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral androgen and one of the more hepatotoxic AAS: 17aa substitution resists hepatic clearance and prolongs hepatocyte exposure, it impairs the canalicular bile transporters BSEP/MRP2 (cholestasis), and it drives oxidative/reactive-oxygen hepatocyte injury (with peliosis/adenoma on prolonged use) — an ESTABLISHED human hepatotoxin. Cardarine has a documented human hepatocellular-injury signal (marked transaminase rise in the abuse literature), plausibly via PPARδ-driven shifts in hepatic substrate handling plus extensive oxidative hepatic metabolism of a highly lipophilic molecule. Two agents that each stress the hepatocyte compound the overall liver burden. Anadrol 17aa hepatotoxicity via BSEP/MRP2 impairment + oxidative injury (Established human): anadrol monograph side_effect_mechanisms. Cardarine human hepatotoxicity signal: monograph side_effect_mechanisms (Kintz 2021 PMID 34678947, confounded by SARM co-use). Watch: ALT, AST, GGT, bilirubin at baseline and periodically; jaundice, pruritus, dark urine, RUQ pain warrant stopping and medical review. Anadrol also strongly raises hematocrit — a separate liability to track — but the SHARED mechanistic edge with Cardarine is hepatotoxicity.
  • CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Same collision as with MK-677: CJC-1295/Ipamorelin raise endogenous GH (and IGF-1), and GH excess is shown in human studies to induce insulin resistance — increased FFA/lipid oxidation crowds out glucose disposal. Cardarine's PPARδ-driven glucose→fat fuel switch pushes muscle in the identical direction, so co-use can compound reduced glucose tolerance / higher fasting glucose. The fat-oxidation effects are otherwise aligned (both favor lipid fuel), which is why the shared liability is glycemic, not opposition. GH-induced insulin resistance in healthy people: CJC-1295+Ipamorelin monograph (PMID 11914108) and MK-677 monograph glucose side-effect data. Cardarine fuel switch: monograph; Risérus 2008 PMID 18024853. Watch: Fasting glucose and insulin, HbA1c; look for insulin-resistance signs. GH peptides can also cause fluid retention and carpal-tunnel-type symptoms — separate from the shared glucose mechanism.
  • MK-677 compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Two pressures on glucose handling collide. MK-677 raises GH/IGF-1, and chronic GH elevation is counter-regulatory to insulin — it promotes lipolysis, raises free fatty acids, and induces peripheral/hepatic insulin resistance (the glucose–fatty-acid / Randle-cycle direction), producing measurably higher fasting glucose and reduced insulin sensitivity in human studies. Cardarine independently shifts muscle fuel preference from glucose to fat. Layered together, both agents push muscle away from disposing of glucose, which can compound glucose intolerance / worsened insulin sensitivity even though neither is a 'sugar' drug per se. MK-677 → GH-driven FFA rise + insulin resistance / raised fasting glucose (12-mo RCT + OGTT data): MK-677 monograph side_effect_mechanisms (PMID 18981485; 9467542; 8954023). Cardarine glucose→fat fuel switch: monograph mechanism_steps 2; Risérus 2008 PMID 18024853. Watch: Fasting glucose, fasting insulin, HbA1c, and an OGTT if symptomatic; watch for signs of insulin resistance (rising fasting glucose, energy dips). MK-677 also causes fluid retention and raised prolactin worth tracking, but the glucose axis is the shared mechanistic concern here.
  • Ostarine compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    This is the single documented real-world Cardarine stack harm. A published case report describes a man who bought Cardarine online and stacked it with the SARM ostarine and developed massive rhabdomyolysis (CK up to ~86,435 U/L) together with severe hepatocellular injury (AST ~2558 U/L); the dominant finding was the muscle breakdown, with liver enzyme rise partly attributable to it and to the co-used SARM. Ostarine (an androgen-receptor modulator) is independently an established cause of (often cholestatic) drug-induced liver injury in recreational users; Cardarine drives aggressive metabolic remodeling of myofibers and has its own human hepatotoxicity/rhabdomyolysis signal. Stacked, the muscle-breakdown and liver insults compound (causation cannot be cleanly split between the two). Cardarine + ostarine co-abuse: massive rhabdomyolysis + hepatocellular injury: Kintz 2021 PMID 34678947 (both monographs' side_effect_mechanisms / evidence_base). Ostarine-induced cholestatic DILI: ostarine monograph side_effect_mechanisms (multiple human case reports). Watch: Creatine kinase and renal function/urinalysis for myoglobinuria (severe myalgia with cola-colored urine is an emergency), plus ALT, AST, bilirubin (jaundice, dark urine, RUQ pain). Stop and seek care on any of these.
  • MOTS-c potentiates ↑ Evidence-rodent
    Cardarine's endurance/fat-oxidation effect is not purely PPARδ — the landmark mouse work showed PPARδ agonism works hand-in-hand with AMPK, the cellular energy sensor, to switch muscle toward oxidative fibers (Cardarine alone in sedentary mice did NOT confer the full endurance benefit; training/AMPK input was required). MOTS-c is a mitochondrial-derived peptide that acts primarily through the folate-AICAR-AMPK pathway to improve metabolic/insulin sensitivity and fat oxidation. Adding an AMPK activator therefore feeds the very upstream node Cardarine's full effect depends on, so the two can reinforce the same FAO/oxidative-fiber transcriptional program (AMPK→PGC-1α→PPARδ axis). Cardarine–AMPK co-action for oxidative fibers/endurance (Cardarine alone insufficient without AMPK/training): Narkar 2008 PMID 18674809 (monograph mechanism_steps 4, evidence_base). MOTS-c folate-AICAR-AMPK metabolic mechanism: MOTS-c monograph MoA (PMID 35299233; 36670507). Watch: Fasting glucose and insulin (both push toward fat use and can lower glucose), lipid panel; watch for exertional hypoglycemia symptoms during fasted/low-carb training since both bias fuel away from glucose.
  • Insulin opposes Speculative
    These pull muscle fuel handling in opposite directions. Insulin's core action is to drive glucose INTO muscle/fat via GLUT4 translocation and to suppress lipolysis (a fat-storing, nutrient-hoarding state). Cardarine's PPARδ program does the reverse at the fuel-selection level — it reprograms muscle to prefer fatty acids over glucose (glucose-sparing). The net metabolic picture is unpredictable: Cardarine's glucose-sparing fuel switch could blunt the substrate side of insulin's effect, while insulin still forces glucose uptake — a theoretical mismatch layered on top of insulin's already narrow margin for acute hypoglycemia. No data exist on this specific pairing. Cardarine glucose→fat fuel switch / glucose-sparing: monograph mechanism_steps 2 (Dressel 2003 PMID 14525954; Risérus 2008 PMID 18024853). Insulin GLUT4 glucose disposal, lipolysis suppression, and acute hypoglycemia risk: insulin-exogenous monograph. Watch: Frequent blood-glucose monitoring for hypo- AND hyper-glycemia; be alert to hypoglycemia symptoms (sweating, tremor, confusion) — noting neuroglycopenia can begin before the classic warning symptoms. This is a high-risk pairing on glycemic grounds regardless of the fuel-switch logic.
  • 5-Amino-1MQ shares target Speculative
    Cardarine (GW501516) is a PPARdelta agonist that upregulates fatty-acid oxidation and mitochondrial fuel-burning in muscle. 5-Amino-1MQ raises NAD+/SIRT1 activity, and SIRT1/PGC-1alpha coactivate PPARdelta-driven fatty-acid-oxidation gene programs, so the two converge on the same oxidative-metabolism transcriptional output from an upstream (NAD+/SIRT1) versus receptor (PPARdelta) entry point. Their fat-oxidation/endurance effects are thus mechanistically overlapping and potentially additive. Safety asymmetry worth flagging: Cardarine carries a rodent carcinogenicity signal, and NNMT is itself a cancer-relevant enzyme (overexpressed in many tumors, and the 5-Amino-1MQ monograph notes chronic NNMT inhibition in healthy people is unstudied) so stacking a compound with a carcinogenicity flag alongside one whose chronic effect on a cancer-linked pathway is unstudied is a compounding unknown, not a proven hazard. 5-Amino-1MQ NAD+/SIRT1/PGC-1alpha -> FAO (PMID 24717514); NNMT cancer-target caveat in monograph. Cardarine PPARdelta/FAO and rodent carcinogenicity are class facts. Convergence and compounded oncologic unknown inferred. Watch: No specific validated labs; be aware both compounds carry unresolved oncologic-safety questions (Cardarine rodent carcinogenicity; NNMT cancer biology). Track general health and avoid treating either as safe for chronic human use.
  • Metformin redundant Evidence-rodent
    Both are 'exercise-mimetic'-type metabolic agents that promote fat oxidation, but through DIFFERENT nodes rather than a shared molecular target, so their fuel-partitioning effects overlap. Metformin activates AMPK (via Complex I / rising AMP), shifting cells toward burning fat and away from lipogenesis. Cardarine activates the nuclear receptor PPARdelta, which transcriptionally upregulates fatty-acid-oxidation genes (e.g. CPT1). AMPK and PPARdelta are distinct switches (Cardarine's monograph notes they act at different nodes and cooperate rather than sharing a target), but they converge on the same catabolic glucose-to-fat fuel-switch program, making the metabolic actions partly redundant. Caveat for direction: metformin may blunt some aerobic training adaptations while Cardarine is framed as an endurance/exercise mimetic, and Cardarine carries rodent carcinogenicity concerns. metformin.json (AMPK -> reduced lipogenesis / fat oxidation; possible blunting of exercise adaptations, Konopka 2019 PMID 30746605); cardarine-gw501516.json (PPARdelta -> fatty-acid oxidation; PPARdelta and AMPK act at different nodes, Narkar 2008) Watch: This is a mechanistic overlap rather than an acute toxicity; general metabolic monitoring (fasting glucose/insulin, lipid panel). No dosing guidance.
  • SLU-PP-332 redundant Evidence-rodent
    SLU-PP-332 is an ERRα/β/γ agonist explicitly characterized as an exercise mimetic that 'increases oxidative muscle fibers, fatty acid oxidation, and enhances exercise endurance' and raises energy expenditure — the same phenotype Cardarine produces via PPARδ. PPARδ and ERR/PGC-1α biology are tightly interlinked upstream of mitochondrial fat-oxidation genes, so the two agents largely duplicate one another's endpoint at different receptor entry points (note SLU-PP-332 also reportedly improves glucose oxidation, whereas Cardarine shifts muscle away from glucose — the overlap is the oxidative-fiber/FAO program, not every fuel-handling detail). Both are WADA-prohibited exercise mimetics; SLU-PP-332 efficacy is mouse-only and it showed cardiac effects at high exposure, so combining two unvalidated mimetics compounds unknown risk without adding a distinct mechanism. SLU-PP-332 ERRα/β/γ exercise-mimetic FAO/oxidative-fiber and energy-expenditure profile: PMID 41688415. Cardarine PPARδ FAO program: monograph; Narkar 2008 PMID 18674809. Watch: Resting heart rate and any exertional cardiac symptoms (SLU-PP-332 carries a high-exposure cardiac signal), fasting lipids and glucose; treat the combination as two unproven investigational agents, not a validated stack.
  • Stenabolic redundant Evidence-rodent
    Both are non-hormonal 'exercise-mimetic' metabolic modulators that converge on the same downstream program: driving skeletal-muscle fatty-acid oxidation and remodeling toward oxidative (fatigue-resistant) fibers. Cardarine does this by switching on PPARδ; SR9009 does it through the REV-ERB circadian-clock nuclear receptors (and, notably, partly through REV-ERB-INDEPENDENT effects on metabolism). Because they hit different receptors but push the same fat-oxidation/oxidative-fiber endpoint, stacking is largely redundant on the intended effect while summing each compound's separate liabilities — and both are WADA-banned metabolic modulators. Neither has human safety data at these exposures. Cardarine PPARδ/oxidative-fiber program: Narkar 2008 PMID 18674809; monograph mechanism_steps. SR9009 REV-ERB and REV-ERB-independent metabolic effects: SR9009 monograph primary_target/mechanism_steps. Watch: Fasting lipid panel (both lower triglycerides — an additive drop is plausible), fasting glucose/insulin, and general well-being; there is no validated blood test for the theoretical oncologic signals of either agent, so track any new/unexplained symptoms rather than assuming the combination is 'safe'.

History &amp; legal status

"Origin: discovered in the late 1990s/early 2000s by GlaxoSmithKline in partnership with Ligand Pharmaceuticals; first reported as compound 7k in Sznaidman et al. 2003 (PMID 12699745). Developed for dyslipidemia/metabolic syndrome; reached Phase II human trials for dyslipidemia (PMID 16625823). Development was halted (~2007) after long-term high-dose rodent carcinogenicity studies reportedly produced tumors across multiple organ systems; this rodent cancer signal is corroborated mechanistically by later academic work (PMID 30679176, 30391747). It has NEVER been approved for any indication in any country and has no legitimate therapeutic use (PMID 32298044). Anti-doping: banned by the World Anti-Doping Agency since January 2009 — initially listed under gene-doping, now classified under S4.5 'Metabolic Modulators' (hormone and metabolic modulators), prohibited at all times in and out of competition (PMID 32298044). In 2013 WADA issued an unusual public-health warning to athletes about cancer risk. Legal status today: not a licensed medicine; sold illicitly as a 'research chemical'/supplement; possession/sale for human consumption is unlawful in many jurisdictions and FDA has warned against its inclusion in body-building products. Forensic detection is well established (LC-MS/MS in urine via oxidized metabolites, and in blood/hair) (PMID 23100242, 32298044, 34678947)."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Human cancer risk: the rodent carcinogenicity is robust, but there is NO human incidence data at performance-enhancing doses/durations — the single most important open question.
  • Human pharmacokinetics: no published human half-life, Cmax/Tmax, bioavailability %, or clearance — the widely-quoted ~24 h half-life is not backed by a citable human PK study.
  • Human dose-response and efficacy for endurance/body-composition: the 'endurance pill' reputation rests on rodent data; no human trial has tested running performance, fat loss, or a dose-response curve.
  • Long-term human safety of any organ system (liver, heart, gut) is unknown — the only human RCT lasted 2 weeks with n=6 on drug.
  • Attribution of real-world harms: hepatotoxicity, rhabdomyolysis and gynecomastia case reports are confounded by co-used SARMs and undisclosed hormones/contaminants, so how much harm Cardarine ITSELF causes at typical abuse exposures is unclear.
  • Whether any tissue-selective dosing window separates the metabolic benefits from the tumor-promotion risk in humans — unresolved.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only (NOT an endorsement; no dosing guidance). Reasonable things a clinician would watch in someone who has used Cardarine: LIVER — ALT, AST, bilirubin (the human abuse case showed AST >2500 U/L); investigate any right-upper-quadrant pain, jaundice, dark urine, or fatigue. MUSCLE — creatine kinase (CK/CPK) and renal function (creatinine, urinalysis for myoglobin) given the documented rhabdomyolysis risk; dark/cola-colored urine plus severe myalgia is a red-flag for emergency evaluation. LIPIDS/METABOLIC — fasting lipid panel and glucose/insulin (drug lowers triglycerides/LDL; deviations help characterize exposure). HORMONAL — if gynecomastia, low libido, or testicular changes appear, total/free testosterone, LH, FSH, estradiol — largely to detect undisclosed SARM/hormone contamination of the product. CANCER VIGILANCE — there is no validated screening test for the theoretical carcinogenicity, but any new GI symptoms (rectal bleeding, change in bowel habit) warrant prompt work-up given the colorectal tumor-promotion signal. PRODUCT-SAFETY — because illicit 'cardarine' is frequently adulterated with SARMs, hormones, or GH secretagogues, unexplained symptoms should prompt consideration that the product contains more than the label states. Above all: any abnormal liver enzymes, very high CK, or new GI bleeding = stop and seek medical care."

Caveats

HARM-REDUCTION / HONESTY: (1) Cardarine is NOT approved as a medicine anywhere and is banned in sport by WADA — it has no legitimate therapeutic use (PMID 32298044). (2) The single biggest fact: long-term rodent studies showed it CAUSES CANCER in multiple organs, which is exactly why GSK killed the drug — there is no established 'safe' dose in humans, and claims of a 'non-cancerous dose' circulating in fitness communities are not backed by human safety data (PMID 22037942). (3) Human data are short-term and small; the celebrated endurance effect is a MOUSE result (Narkar 2008, PMID 18674809) and has not been shown in a controlled human performance study. (4) Products sold online are unregulated — purity and actual contents are unknown, and real liver injury has occurred, especially when stacked (PMID 34678947). (5) Be precise for your audience: Cardarine is a PPARδ agonist, NOT a SARM and NOT an anabolic steroid — it does not build muscle by hitting the androgen receptor; any 'recomp' marketing conflating it with SARMs is wrong. This is education on mechanism, not an endorsement or any kind of dosing/usage guidance.
