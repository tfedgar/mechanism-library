GHRP-2

A synthetic ghrelin-mimic peptide that flips the pituitary's "release growth hormone" switch — and, like ghrelin, also turns on hunger. It pulses your own GH; it is not GH itself.

Human / approved 25 citations
Class
Synthetic growth hormone secretagogue (GHS) — a peptidyl ghrelin-receptor (GHS-R1a) agonist
Primary target
Growth hormone secretagogue receptor type 1a (GHS-R1a), the Gq/11-coupled GPCR for ghrelin — on anterior-pituitary somatotrophs and in the hypothalamus (arcuate nucleus)
Human status
Not an approved therapeutic drug for body composition or anti-aging anywhere mainstream. Its best-established human use is as a diagnostic GH-stimulation test (the 'GHRP-2 test') for hypothalamic-pituitary GH-deficiency, including a marketed diagnostic in Japan (pralmorelin, GPA-748). Robust human data exist for its acute effects: large GH pulse, modest cortisol/ACTH and prolactin rise, and increased appetite. It is sold on the gray market as an unregulated 'research peptide' / sold in supplements; it is a WADA-prohibited substance. There is NO good long-term human evidence that GHRP-2 builds muscle, burns fat, improves recovery, or extends healthspan in healthy adults — those claims are extrapolated from the GH→IGF-1 axis and from anecdote, not demonstrated.

At a glance

  • Potent, pulsatile release of the body's OWN growth hormone (most-studied effect; strong human data)
  • Synergizes with GHRH and partially bypasses the somatostatin brake → larger combined GH pulse
  • Indirect downstream rise in IGF-1 (via the GH pulse), the presumed route to any tissue/repair effects
  • Increased appetite / food intake — the ghrelin-mimic signature (demonstrated in humans)
  • Modest, non-selective bump in cortisol/ACTH and prolactin (more than ipamorelin; a key differentiator)
  • Used clinically as a diagnostic GH-stimulation test, not as an approved therapy in most countries

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Binds the ghrelin receptor (GHS-R1a), not the GHRH receptor Established (human + receptor pharmacology)

    GHRP-2 is a man-made mimic of ghrelin (your stomach's hunger hormone). It docks onto the same receptor ghrelin uses — GHS-R1a — which sits on the GH-making cells of the pituitary and on hunger neurons in the brain. It does NOT work through the separate GHRH receptor. The cleanest proof in humans: GHRP-2 still raises GH in patients whose GHRH receptor is genetically broken, so it must be acting through the other (ghrelin) receptor. PMID 11443201 (Gondo 2001, GHRH-receptor-mutant patients); PMID 9798733 (Chen 1998, not via GHRF receptor); PMID 15646370 (Doi 2004, KP-102/GHRP-2 pharmacology)

  2. Receptor fires a Gq/PLC/PKC + calcium signal Evidence-in vitro / cell-based (receptor biology shared across ghrelin/GHS ligands)

    GHS-R1a is a Gq-coupled receptor. When GHRP-2 switches it on, the cell runs the classic phospholipase-C cascade: it makes second messengers (inositol phosphates), pushes intracellular calcium up, and activates protein kinase C — including a downstream ERK1/2 kinase arm. That calcium spike inside the somatotroph is the immediate trigger for dumping stored GH. PMID 16582936 (Bouzo-Lorenzo 2016, GHSR-1a→PLC/PKCε→ERK1/2); PMC review: GHSR intracellular signaling and regulation

  3. Pituitary somatotrophs release a pulse of growth hormone Established (extensive human data)

    The end result of step 2 is a sharp, dose-dependent burst of GH released from the pituitary into the blood — a pulse, not a flat permanent elevation. GHRP-2 is one of the most potent peptide GH-releasers tested in people; the GH spike is large and reproducible, which is why clinics use a GHRP-2 test to probe pituitary function. PMID 9285939 (Arvat 1997, GH/PRL/ACTH/cortisol in man); PMID 35795807 (Suzuki 2022, GHRP-2 test); PMID 38958228 (Onuki 2024, robust GH response in adolescents)

  4. Synergy with GHRH; partly overrides somatostatin's brake Established (human physiology), mechanism of somatostatin interplay Evidence-human/rodent

    GH release is normally a tug-of-war: GHRH presses the accelerator, somatostatin presses the brake. GHRP-2 works on a third lever (the ghrelin receptor), so combining it with GHRH produces a much bigger GH pulse than either alone, and it can release GH even when the somatostatin brake is active. It amplifies the body's own rhythm rather than replacing it. PMID 9285939 (Arvat 1997); PMID 9186261 (Ghigo 1997 review, GHRPs); PMID 9465289 (Camanni 1998 review)

  5. Downstream of the GH pulse: liver IGF-1 rises Established that GH→IGF-1 (general endocrinology); GHRP-2-specific sustained IGF-1 gains in healthy adults are less well-characterized

    The released GH travels to the liver and other tissues and drives production of IGF-1, the main mediator of GH's growth and tissue-repair effects. So GHRP-2's 'anabolic' reputation is indirect — it raises your own GH, which raises IGF-1. The size and duration of this depends on pulsatility and is not the same as injecting GH directly. PMID 25025070016 is invalid — use PMID 25025? ; PMID 28400207 (Sigalos 2018 review, GHS safety/efficacy); PMID 32257855 (Sinha 2020, GHS in body composition)

  6. Off-target endocrine spillover: cortisol, ACTH and prolactin nudge up Established (human)

    Because the ghrelin-receptor system overlaps with stress and lactation circuits, GHRP-2 is not perfectly GH-selective: in humans it also produces a modest rise in ACTH/cortisol and prolactin. The effect is smaller than its GH effect and smaller than dedicated stimuli (CRH/TRH), but it is real and is a differentiator versus more 'clean' secretagogues like ipamorelin. PMID 9285939 (Arvat 1997, direct human comparison); PMID 19682503 (Kageyama 2009, GHRP-2 stimulates ACTH synthesis, mouse pituitary)

  7. Hypothalamic arm: appetite goes up (the ghrelin signature) Evidence-human

    GHRP-2 also hits ghrelin receptors on hunger neurons (NPY/AgRP) in the hypothalamus. In a controlled human infusion study it increased food intake in healthy men — the same appetite-driving action as natural ghrelin. For users this is a practical, often unwanted, effect and a clear sign the drug is acting as a true ghrelin mimic, not just a GH lever. PMID 15699539 (Laferrère 2005, GHRP-2 increases food intake in healthy men)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. GHRP-2 (pralmorelin; D-Ala-D-2-Nal-Ala-Trp-D-Phe-Lys-NH2; CAS 158861-67-7; ChEMBL CHEMBL106593; freebase MW ~818 Da, formula C45H55N9O6; HELM dA-dNal-A-W-dF-K-am) is a synthetic hexapeptide AGONIST at the growth hormone secretagogue receptor type 1a (GHS-R1a, the ghrelin receptor), a Gq/11-coupled GPCR on anterior-pituitary somatotrophs and hypothalamic arcuate neurons [Established; PMID 15646370, 15230633; ChEMBL CHEMBL106593]. It is a 'super-analog' of GHRP-6: in perifused ovine pituitary cells it was ~10-fold more potent than the earlier GHRP-6/GHRP-1, with potency similar to GHRH and a maximal GH effect at ~100 nM [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 8169551]. Note a published caveat: in ovine somatotrophs and rat GC cells GHRP-2's action could be blocked by a GHRH(GRF)-receptor antagonist yet GHRP-2 did NOT signal through the transfected human GHRH receptor itself (no cAMP/Ca2+ rise in GC/R+ cells), confirming a distinct GHS receptor while implying functional cross-talk with the GHRH system [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 8169551, 9798733]. A precise human GHS-R1a Ki/EC50 specifically for GHRP-2 was NOT found in the tools run this session (commonly cited low-nanomolar affinity could not be verified here) [stated gap]. Secondary target: like hexarelin, GHRP-2 binds the scavenger receptor CD36 (relevant to cardiovascular/anti-oxidative effects), shown by GHRP-2 suppressing vascular oxidative stress in ApoE-/- mice [Evidence-rodent; PMID 19819949]. It is NOT GH-release-selective: it co-stimulates ACTH/cortisol and (weakly) prolactin in humans [Established; PMID 9285939].

Signalling cascade. Agonist binding triggers a conformational change coupling GHS-R1a to Gaq/11 -> phospholipase C (PLC) activation -> hydrolysis of PIP2 into IP3 + diacylglycerol (DAG). IP3 releases ER Ca2+ (initial [Ca2+]i spike); DAG activates PKC, which inhibits K+ channels causing membrane depolarization that opens voltage-gated (L-type) Ca2+ channels for sustained Ca2+ influx; the high [Ca2+]i drives exocytosis of GH secretory granules [Established; PMID 16582936 and PMC OA review on GHSR signaling]. A PLC/PKCepsilon arm also activates ERK1/2 [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 16582936]. GHS-R1a shows high constitutive (ligand-independent) activity and synergizes with GHRH: GHRP-2/ghrelin amplify GHRH-driven cAMP in a PKC-dependent manner, and GHRP-2 also acts at the hypothalamus to lower somatostatin tone and release GHRH, which is why its GH effect is less suppressible by somatostatin than GHRH's and is largely lost after hypophysectomy/median-eminence lesion [Established/Evidence-rodent; PMID 15646370]. In the arcuate nucleus GHRP-2/KP-102 induces c-fos and activates NPY/AgRP orexigenic neurons, the basis of its appetite effect [Evidence-rodent; PMID 8804723].

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Parenteral peptide. Clinically validated as IV (diagnostic GH-stimulation test, typically 1 ug/kg IV); also given subcutaneously and intranasally in research; was developed by Kaken/Tulane in buccal, oral and depot formats but oral bioavailability is low (it is a peptide and not orally robust) [Evidence-human; PMID 15230633, 9285939, 15699539, 25869809]
Absorption / bioavailability
As a 6-amino-acid peptide it is subject to gut proteolysis; oral/buccal forms were explored precisely because plain oral delivery is inefficient. Quantitative human %F was NOT found in tools this session [gap]. Intranasal and SC routes give measurable systemic exposure and GH responses [Evidence-human; PMID 25869809, 15699539]
Half-life
A precise human plasma t1/2 was NOT retrieved this session [gap]. Functional/biological window is short: GH rises within minutes of IV dosing and the diagnostic test is read over ~60-120 min; urinary detection is governed by metabolism (parent + metabolites detectable up to ~47 h after nasal dosing), reflecting rapid clearance of the parent [Evidence-human; PMID 35795807, 25869809]
Metabolism
Cleared by peptidase/proteolytic cleavage rather than CYP metabolism. Characterized human metabolites: the N-terminal fragment D-Ala-D-2-Nal-Ala-OH (designated AA-3), plus 'GHRP-2 free acid' and 'GHRP-2(1-3) free acid' — i.e. sequential exopeptidase trimming and C-terminal deamidation [Established; PMID 20552695, 25869809]
Excretion
Renal: unchanged GHRP-2 and metabolite AA-3 are recoverable in urine after IV dosing; after nasal dosing GHRP-2, its free acid and the (1-3) free acid are detectable in urine up to ~47 h (basis of WADA anti-doping detection) [Established; PMID 20552695, 25869809]
Notes
Not an ester/prodrug — it is the active peptide itself (the dihydrochloride salt, WAY-GPA-748/CHEMBL2105772, is the clinical form). Poor oral drug-likeness (QED ~0.05, 2 Lipinski violations, PSA ~256, 21 rotatable bonds) consistent with parenteral peptide behavior [Established; ChEMBL CHEMBL106593/CHEMBL2105772]. CNS access: GH/appetite effects involve a hypothalamic site of action, indicating functional central activity at least via circumventricular/arcuate regions [Evidence-rodent; PMID 8804723, 15646370]

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

GH release is steeply dose-dependent and species-conserved. HUMAN (acute, diagnostic/research): IV 1 ug/kg produced a strong GH rise exceeding equimolar GHRH; raising to 2 ug/kg IV gave a further significant increase, demonstrating dose-response within the diagnostic range; effect preserved in the elderly [Evidence-human; PMID 9285939]. A diagnostic GH-peak cutoff of ~15 ug/L (after standard IV dose) separates GH-deficient from healthy subjects [Evidence-human; PMID 15230633]. SC infusion 1 ug/kg/h for 270 min in 7 healthy men raised GH AUC ~13-fold over saline and increased ad-libitum food intake by ~36% [Evidence-human; PMID 15699539]. In GHRH-receptor-null patients a single dose still gave a 4.5-fold GH rise vs ~79-fold in controls — confirming partial GHRH-independent pituitary action but showing an intact GHRH system is needed for the FULL response [Evidence-human; PMID 11443201]. ANIMAL: in conscious rats GHRP-2 (KP-102) out-performed injected GHRH; 3 weeks of dosing gave modest body-weight/organ-weight gains; in swine GHRP-2 ED50 for GH ~0.6 nmol/kg (more potent but lower max efficacy than GHRP-6/ipamorelin) [Evidence-rodent; PMID 15646370, 9849822]. KEY GAP: a true chronic human dose-response for body-composition/IGF-1 outcomes (the bodybuilding use-case) has NOT been established — human data are almost entirely single-dose diagnostic studies, and tolerance/desensitization on repeated dosing is expected from receptor biology but not quantified in humans [Speculative/gap].

How it compares

  • vs GHRP-6
    same: Same GHS-R1a agonist mechanism; both raise GH AND appetite (orexigenic) and both raise cortisol/prolactin
    different: GHRP-2 is the more potent 'super-analog' (~10x in vitro) with a stronger GH push per microgram; GHRP-6's hunger effect is often described as more pronounced relative to GH
  • vs Ipamorelin
    same: Both GHS-R1a agonists that release GH
    different: Ipamorelin is GH-SELECTIVE — at >200x its GH-ED50 it did not raise ACTH/cortisol, and minimally affects prolactin/appetite, whereas GHRP-2 reliably co-elevates cortisol and stimulates hunger. GHRP-2 is more potent but less 'clean'
  • vs Hexarelin
    same: Both synthetic hexapeptide GHRP-6 super-analogs; near-identical human endocrine profile (GH, PRL, ACTH, cortisol); both also bind CD36
    different: Very similar potency/spectrum; hexarelin shows somewhat more documented direct cardiac/CD36 effects, but in head-to-head human testing GH/PRL/ACTH/cortisol responses were essentially equivalent
  • vs MK-677 (ibutamoren)
    same: Same GHS-R1a target and GH/IGF-1 + appetite outcomes
    different: MK-677 is a non-peptide, ORALLY active, long-half-life secretagogue giving sustained 24-h GH/IGF-1 elevation; GHRP-2 is an injectable peptide with short, pulsatile action better matching physiological GH pulses

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Nandrolone compounds risk ⚠ Established (both raise prolactin; 19-nor progestogenic-prolactin effect documented)
    Both compounds independently raise prolactin by different routes: GHRP-2 through non-selective lactotroph stimulation at the pituitary, and nandrolone (a 19-nor) through its progestogenic activity, which drives prolactin-mediated effects. Run together the prolactin burden adds up, worsening the low-libido / erectile-dysfunction / prolactin-type gynecomastia picture. Key mechanistic caveat: an aromatase inhibitor will NOT fix this because it is a prolactin/progesterone problem, not an estrogen one. GHRP-2 monograph step 6 (prolactin rise); Nandrolone monograph key_effects (progestogenic + prolactin-raising, not AI-fixable, PMID 19941958) Watch: Prolactin; libido and erectile function; breast-tissue changes. A dopamine agonist addresses the prolactin arm but not nandrolone's progesterone-receptor arm.
  • Trenbolone compounds risk ⚠ Established (19-nor prolactin/progestogenic effect; GHRP-2 prolactin rise in humans)
    Like nandrolone, trenbolone is a 19-nor with intrinsic progestogenic activity (binds the progesterone receptor with affinity above progesterone) that tends to raise prolactin. Stacked with GHRP-2's own non-selective prolactin bump, the two compound the prolactin load - additively worsening sexual dysfunction and prolactin-driven gynecomastia. Aromatase inhibitors do not address this because the driver is prolactin/progesterone, not estradiol. GHRP-2 monograph step 6 (prolactin rise); Trenbolone monograph (progestogenic, binds PGR above progesterone, raises prolactin, not AI-fixable) Watch: Prolactin; libido/erectile function; breast-tissue changes. A dopamine agonist targets only the prolactin arm, not trenbolone's progesterone-receptor activity.
  • CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin potentiates ↑ Established (GHRH+GHRP synergy in humans); the specific triple-combination outcome is unstudied
    GHRP-2 activates the ghrelin receptor (GHS-R1a, Gq-coupled) on the somatotroph, while the CJC-1295 arm of this stack activates the SEPARATE GHRH receptor (GHRHR, Gs-coupled) on the same cell. Pulling two different levers - Gq/PLC/Ca2+ (GHRP-2) plus Gs/cAMP (GHRH) - while GHRP-2 also blunts somatostatin's brake, produces a supra-additive GH pulse larger than either alone. This is the GHRH+GHRP synergy the GHRP-2 monograph documents (step 4). Caveat: the Ipamorelin component is itself a GHS-R1a agonist, so it is REDUNDANT with GHRP-2 at that receptor; the genuine synergy is the GHRH (CJC-1295) arm. GHRP-2 monograph step 4 (PMID 9285939 Arvat 1997; PMID 9186261 Ghigo 1997); CJC-1295+Ipamorelin monograph (complementary GHRHR/GHS-R1a pathways) Watch: IGF-1 as the on-target readout of the amplified GH signal; fasting glucose/HbA1c since a larger GH pulse worsens insulin sensitivity; fluid retention, joint aches and carpal-tunnel-type numbness as GH-excess signals.
  • Sermorelin potentiates ↑ Established (GHRH+GHRP synergy)
    Sermorelin is a GHRH(1-29) analog acting on the pituitary GHRH receptor (Gs-coupled, raises cAMP). GHRP-2 acts on the distinct ghrelin receptor GHS-R1a (Gq-coupled). Because GHRH presses the accelerator while GHRP-2 works a third lever and partially overrides the somatostatin brake, the two produce a much larger combined GH pulse than either alone - the classic GHRH+GHRP complementary-pathway potentiation, not merely additive. GHRP-2 monograph step 4 (PMID 9285939; PMID 9186261); Sermorelin monograph (GHRH-R agonist, PMID 9360512) Watch: IGF-1 (on-target amplification); fasting glucose and insulin sensitivity (GH is counter-regulatory); signs of GH excess (edema, arthralgia, paresthesia).
  • T3 potentiates ↑ Evidence-human for GH-raises-T4-to-T3 conversion and GH+T3 calorigenic synergy; the GHRP-2-specific magnitude is inferred
    GH and thyroid hormone are metabolically intertwined. GHRP-2 raises endogenous GH, and GH increases peripheral T4-to-T3 conversion and energy expenditure - an effect partly reproduced by T3 supplementation, and GH+T3 act synergistically on resting energy expenditure in humans (though GH's calorigenic effect is NOT solely via T4-to-T3 conversion). Adequate thyroid hormone is also permissive for the GH-to-IGF-1 axis. So T3 can amplify the calorigenic/metabolic side of a GHRP-2-driven GH rise; conversely GH can shift TSH/free T4, so thyroid status is worth watching. Corpus PMID 8636344 (GH increases peripheral T4->T3; synergistic REE with T3, but GH calorigenesis not solely via conversion); PMID 10077348 (GH increases T4->T3 conversion); GHRP-2 monograph (raises endogenous GH->IGF-1) Watch: Full thyroid panel (TSH, free T4, free T3) - GH can shift T4/T3 and TSH; resting heart rate and signs of thyrotoxicosis with added T3; T3 is muscle-catabolic at higher exposure, so track lean mass.
  • Tesamorelin potentiates ↑ Established (class-level GHRH+GHRP synergy); specific pair not trialed
    Tesamorelin is a stabilized GHRH(1-44) analog on the GHRH receptor (Gs/cAMP). Paired with GHRP-2's ghrelin-receptor action (Gq/Ca2+), the two receptor pathways converge on the same somatotroph for a supra-additive GH pulse, with GHRP-2 additionally easing the somatostatin brake. Same complementary-receptor logic as any GHRH+GHRP pairing; tesamorelin brings strong GHRH-arm human data (visceral-fat and IGF-1 effects). GHRP-2 monograph step 4; Tesamorelin monograph (GHRHR agonist, pulsatile GH/IGF-1 rise) Watch: IGF-1; fasting glucose/HbA1c (both raise GH, which opposes insulin); fluid retention and joint symptoms.
  • Cabergoline counteracts ↓ Established mechanism (GHRP-2 raises prolactin in humans; cabergoline lowers it); the specific pairing is extrapolation
    GHRP-2 is a non-selective secretagogue that produces a modest but real rise in prolactin (off-target lactotroph stimulation via the same Gq/PLC machinery). Cabergoline is a long-acting dopamine D2 agonist that impersonates dopamine on lactotrophs, shutting down cAMP/PKA- and calcium-driven prolactin secretion - the physiologic brake GHRP-2 nudges against. So cabergoline directly counteracts the prolactin arm of GHRP-2's spillover (relevant if libido drops or galactorrhea appears). GHRP-2 monograph step 6 & side_effect_mechanisms (prolactin rise, PMID 9285939); Cabergoline monograph (D2 agonist lowers prolactin; addresses prolactin-driven, not progesterone-driven, effects) Watch: Serum prolactin (confirm it is actually elevated first and that it normalizes); libido/erectile function. Cabergoline is not benign - watch for nausea and orthostatic dizziness; heart-valve concern is cumulative-exposure-related and low at endocrine exposure.
  • Insulin counteracts ↓ Established (GH-induced insulin resistance in humans; insulin hypoglycemia lethality documented)
    Opposing actions on the glucose axis. GHRP-2 raises the body's own GH, and GH is a counter-regulatory hormone that induces insulin resistance and raises blood glucose (documented for GH in healthy humans). Insulin does the opposite - it drives glucose into cells and lowers blood glucose. So on the glucose axis insulin directly counteracts GHRP-2's GH-driven hyperglycemia. The danger is that this is not a clean cancel-out: layering a hypoglycemia agent onto a hyperglycemia-inducing peptide widens the glucose swing, and insulin is the one agent that can cause fatal hypoglycemia within an hour, with warning symptoms appearing only AFTER cognition (neuroglycopenia) already begins. GHRP-2 monograph side_effect_mechanisms (worsened insulin sensitivity/hyperglycemia, PMID 28400207); Insulin monograph key_effects (GH-induced insulin resistance PMID 11914108; inverted hypoglycemia warning order PMID 3403713) Watch: Frequent blood glucose (both the GH-driven rise and the insulin-driven crash); HbA1c over time. Harm-reduction note: insulin's acute hypoglycemia risk is life-threatening and its warning signs are delayed/inverted - this is a monitoring red flag, not a stacking endorsement.
  • GHRP-6 redundant Established (shared GHS-R1a target); desensitization Evidence-in vitro
    GHRP-6 is the parent hexapeptide that GHRP-2 was derived from and hits the identical target, GHS-R1a. Stacking them does NOT recruit a second pathway - they compete for and saturate the same receptor, so the GH benefit is largely redundant, while the off-target liabilities STACK: both drive appetite (arcuate NPY/AgRP), both raise cortisol/ACTH and prolactin, and repeated same-receptor activation promotes homologous desensitization (a second GHS pulse within ~1 h is blunted). Net effect is compounded side effects with little extra GH. GHRP-2 monograph (GHS-R1a agonist; steps 6-7; desensitization PMID 8169551); GHRP-6 monograph (same GHS-R1a target, appetite/cortisol/prolactin) Watch: Appetite/weight/body-fat trend (additive hunger); morning cortisol and ACTH; prolactin if libido drops or galactorrhea appears; watch for a diminishing GH response (tachyphylaxis) with frequent same-receptor dosing.
  • Hexarelin redundant Established (shared GHS-R1a; near-identical human endocrine spectrum)
    Hexarelin is another GHRP-6 super-analog with a near-identical human endocrine profile to GHRP-2 (GH, prolactin, ACTH, cortisol), acting on the same GHS-R1a. Combining the two is largely redundant for GH but compounds the shared spillover (cortisol/ACTH, prolactin) and accelerates GHS-R1a desensitization. Hexarelin adds a secondary CD36 (scavenger-receptor) action not shared by GHRP-2, but the overlap that matters for stacking is the identical ghrelin-receptor mechanism. GHRP-2 monograph comparisons (hexarelin 'essentially equivalent' human GH/PRL/ACTH/cortisol); Hexarelin monograph (GHS-R1a + CD36; raises ACTH/cortisol/prolactin, PMID 10404825) Watch: Morning cortisol/ACTH and prolactin (compounded HPA and lactotroph stimulation); GH/IGF-1 for evidence of blunting; appetite and weight.
  • MK-677 redundant Established (identical GHS-R1a target); MK-677 glucose effect Evidence-human
    MK-677 is an orally-active nonpeptide full agonist at the SAME receptor GHRP-2 uses (GHS-R1a). Because MK-677 gives sustained 24-h receptor occupancy while GHRP-2 gives sharp pulses, adding pulsatile GHRP-2 on top of continuous MK-677 offers little extra GH (the receptor is already driven) but compounds the shared downsides: additive appetite/food intake and, critically, additive GH-induced insulin resistance and rising fasting glucose, which MK-677 alone reliably causes in humans. GHRP-2 monograph (GHS-R1a agonist); MK-677 monograph (raised fasting glucose/reduced insulin sensitivity, Evidence-human) Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c (compounded GH-driven insulin resistance is the key hazard); appetite/weight; IGF-1 to gauge whether the added peptide is doing anything beyond MK-677 alone.

History & legal status

"Originated from Cyril Bowers' GHRP work at Tulane University (US) with Polygen (Germany); GHRP-2/KP-102 was developed by Kaken Pharmaceutical (Japan), which held worldwide rights and sublicensed US/Canada to Wyeth (formerly American Home Products) as WAY-GPA-748/GPA 748. USAN 'pralmorelin' assigned 1997 (-relin/-morelin stem). Primary development was as a DIAGNOSTIC agent for GH deficiency / hypothalamo-pituitary function (KP-102D) and for short stature/pituitary dwarfism (KP-102LN, Phase II in Japan); the US GH-deficiency program with Wyeth was discontinued. Tulane held US Patent 6,468,974 (2002). It reached the market in Japan as a diagnostic GH-stimulation test agent but was never approved as a therapeutic/performance drug; max clinical phase in ChEMBL is 2. Regulatory status today: it is NOT an FDA-approved drug for body composition; it is a research chemical sold gray-market. It is explicitly on the WADA Prohibited List (S2, peptide hormones/GH secretagogues) — banned in sport at all times — which is why validated urinary detection methods exist [Established; PMID 15230633, 20552695, 25869809; ChEMBL CHEMBL106593]."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No human chronic dose-response: virtually all human data are single-dose diagnostic studies; the effect of repeated bodybuilding-style dosing on IGF-1, lean mass, fat mass and safety is unquantified [gap].
  • Magnitude and time-course of tachyphylaxis/desensitization in humans is unknown — in-vitro pituitary cells desensitize within ~1 h, but how repeated real-world dosing blunts GH pulses is not characterized [Evidence-in vitro only].
  • Exact human pharmacokinetics (plasma half-life, oral/SC/intranasal bioavailability) were not established in the literature retrieved this session.
  • Long-term cancer-risk question shared by all GH/IGF-1 raisers (IGF-1 is mitogenic) is unstudied for GHRP-2 specifically [Speculative].
  • Clinical significance of CD36 binding (cardiovascular/metabolic effects beyond GH) in humans is unresolved — shown only in rodent models [Evidence-rodent].
  • Whether chronic HPA-axis (cortisol) activation from repeated dosing causes meaningful metabolic or psychological harm over time is unknown.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (NOT a protocol, no dosing): Track IGF-1 and, where available, GH dynamics as the on-target readout that the compound is acting — and to flag supraphysiologic exposure. Watch fasting glucose and HbA1c / insulin sensitivity because GH is counter-regulatory and can raise glucose. Monitor MORNING cortisol and ACTH given GHRP-2's documented HPA activation; persistent elevation or, conversely, signs of HPA disruption warrant stopping. Check prolactin if libido/galactorrhea symptoms appear. Watch for fluid retention/edema, joint aches, and carpal-tunnel-type numbness/tingling (GH/IGF-1 class effects) — these signal excess and should prompt dose reduction or cessation. Track appetite, weight and body-fat trend, since increased hunger is an expected effect. Because gray-market product is frequently adulterated or mislabeled (glycine-modified analogs, wrong content found in seized vials/supplements), purity/source verification is itself a safety measure. None of this constitutes medical advice; clinically meaningful changes should be evaluated by a clinician. [Established/Evidence-human: PMID 28400207, 9285939, 15645295, 15699539, 30051972]"

EVIDENCE CAVEATS: (1) The strongest GHRP-2 human data are ACUTE single-dose/infusion endocrine-response studies and diagnostic-test studies — not chronic efficacy trials; do not present acute GH spikes as proof of long-term anabolic benefit. (2) Downstream signaling detail (PLC/PKCε/ERK1/2, calcium) is largely cell-based/in-vitro work on the ghrelin/GHS-R system generally, not GHRP-2-specific in humans — labeled accordingly. (3) ACTH/cortisol/prolactin spillover and the appetite effect are genuine differentiators vs. 'selective' secretagogues (e.g., ipamorelin) and should not be glossed over. (4) Gray-market product identity is a real hazard: published forensic analyses found GHRP-2 (and a glycine analogue) misidentified/adulterated in seized vials and supplements (PMID 30051972, PMID 20878896) — purity/identity cannot be assumed. (5) WADA-banned; relevant for any athlete audience.
