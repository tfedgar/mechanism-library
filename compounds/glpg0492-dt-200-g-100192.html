GLPG0492

Main target: Androgen receptor. Evidence is animal- and lab-only — no human trials yet.

EvidenceRodent / preclinical
ApprovalNot FDA-approved

A discontinued non-steroidal SARM from Galapagos that showed clean muscle-vs-prostate separation in rodents and reached Phase 1 for muscle wasting/Duchenne, but was never taken further in patients and has no published human efficacy or safety data.

Rodent / preclinical 13 citations
Class
Non-steroidal selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM); 4-(hydroxymethyl)diarylhydantoin chemotype (structurally related to the anti-androgen enzalutamide/nilutamide hydantoin scaffold, but engineered as a tissue-selective partial agonist)
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR, NR3C4; ChEMBL target CHEMBL1871, UniProt P10275, gene AR) — acts as a partial agonist that is anabolic in muscle/bone while sparing prostate in rodents
Human status
EMERGING / DISCONTINUED PIPELINE COMPOUND — do not overstate. GLPG0492 (Galapagos; ChEMBL CHEMBL2178100, max_phase 1) completed three Phase 1 studies in healthy volunteers: NCT01130818 (first-in-human single-ascending-dose, incl. food-effect/relative-bioavailability in elderly, n=28, completed 2010); NCT01397370 (multiple-ascending-dose, 14 days, n=24, completed 2011); NCT01538420 (muscle protein-synthesis PD in men + hormone effects in postmenopausal women, n=26, completed 2012). All three are now listed on ClinicalTrials.gov with sponsor Lakefront Biotherapeutics NV, and ALL have has_results=false — NO results are posted. There is NO published human efficacy or safety dataset and no patient (Duchenne, sarcopenia, cachexia) trials. The compound was NOT approved anywhere and appears to have been discontinued. Essentially all efficacy and selectivity evidence is rodent or in-vitro. SARMs as a class have been on the WADA Prohibited List since January 2008; much of the recent GLPG0492 literature is anti-doping detection methodology (human, equine, bovine), not therapeutic development. It is not a legal medicine and is sold only as an unapproved research chemical.

At a glance

  • Preserved/increased muscle mass, strength and fatigue resistance in castrated, immobilized and dystrophic rodents (rodent evidence only)
  • Muscle-anabolic activity strongly dissociated from prostate growth in rats (the defining SARM property; a rodent finding)
  • Reduced muscle fibrosis and modestly improved diaphragm force in the exercised-mdx Duchenne mouse model
  • Orally developed (rat oral bioavailability F > 50%); reached three completed Phase 1 trials in healthy volunteers
  • Designed as a target for a short-term muscle protein-synthesis signal in healthy volunteers — but no results were ever posted
  • No demonstrated human clinical benefit — never advanced beyond Phase 1; now a WADA-prohibited doping agent detectable in urine/blood

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

7 steps1 established 5 rodent / preclinical 1 contested / speculative

  1. Binds the androgen receptor as a partial agonist Established

    GLPG0492 is a small orally-active molecule that slots into the ligand-binding pocket of the androgen receptor (AR), the same protein testosterone and DHT use. It is a partial agonist: it switches the receptor partway on rather than fully on like testosterone does. ChEMBL classifies it as an AR MODULATOR with direct target interaction, tagged a SARM. The pure (S)-(-) enantiomer is the active drug. The original discovery paper explicitly identifies it as a 'partial agonist of the human androgen receptor.' PMID 22957947 (J Med Chem, DOI 10.1021/jm300281x — 'partial agonists of the human androgen receptor'); ChEMBL CHEMBL2178100 mechanism (MODULATOR, direct_interaction=true, target CHEMBL1871)

  2. Activated AR moves to the nucleus and switches androgen-responsive genes on or off Evidence-in vitro

    Once bound, the AR-ligand complex translocates into the cell nucleus and binds androgen-response elements on DNA, recruiting coactivator/corepressor complexes that turn muscle- and bone-relevant genes up or down. The tissue-selective behaviour of SARMs is thought to arise here: the partial-agonist conformation is proposed to recruit a different balance of coregulators than testosterone, and this may differ between muscle and prostate. This coregulator/conformation model is general SARM biology (shown in vitro for other SARMs) and has NOT been mapped in detail for GLPG0492 specifically; the only GLPG0492-specific structural work is in-silico docking suggesting its AR binding mode differs slightly from RAD-140 and GSK-2881078. PMID 18801930 (Mol Endocrinol 2008 — SARM S-22 distinct genomic/nongenomic AR signaling, in vitro); PMID 30825507 (J Steroid Biochem Mol Biol — in-silico modeling suggests GLPG0492 binds the AR LBD slightly differently from RAD-140 and GSK-2881078)

  3. In muscle, AR signaling tips protein balance toward synthesis and away from breakdown Evidence-rodent

    Androgen signaling in skeletal muscle raises protein synthesis (via Akt/mTOR) and lowers protein degradation by suppressing the FoxO-driven muscle-specific ubiquitin ligases atrogin-1/MAFbx and MuRF1, which otherwise tag muscle proteins for destruction. GLPG0492's own gene-expression work in immobilized-muscle mice showed it slows muscle loss by negatively interfering with the major signaling pathways that control muscle mass — consistent with this atrophy-pathway model, though the exact node it hits was not fully resolved for GLPG0492. The specific atrogin-1/MuRF1 and Akt/mTOR steps are established for androgens/other SARMs, not directly demonstrated for GLPG0492. PMID 25185887 (BMC Musculoskelet Disord, DOI 10.1186/1471-2474-15-291 — GLPG0492 slows muscle loss via muscle-mass signaling pathways); pathway support (not GLPG0492-specific): PMID 23116773 (testosterone→Akt/mTORC1/FoxO3a), PMID 20534726 (SARM suppresses atrogin-1/MuRF1)

  4. Net effect in rodents: muscle mass and strength preserved or increased Evidence-rodent

    Across orchidectomized (castrated) rats, hindlimb-immobilization mice and the exercised-mdx model, GLPG0492 increased or preserved muscle mass, fiber size, strength and fatigue resistance. In the original castrated-rat assay it produced anabolic activity on muscle that was 'strongly dissociated from the androgenic activity on prostate after oral dosing.' PMID 22957947 (orchidectomized rat, anabolic activity dissociated from prostate); PMID 25185887 (immobilization, dose-dependent partial prevention of atrophy); PMID 23523664 (exercised-mdx mouse, increased strength/fatigue resistance)

  5. Prostate and other reproductive tissues are largely spared (in rodents) Evidence-rodent

    The whole point of a SARM: in the same rodent studies where GLPG0492 built muscle, it did NOT drive the prostate growth that testosterone causes — in the immobilization model it was 'as efficacious as testosterone propionate at reducing muscle loss while sparing reproductive tissues.' The compound is characterized as a partial AR agonist in both the discovery paper and doping-analysis literature. This muscle-vs-prostate dissociation is the core selling point but is a rodent/in-vitro finding, not proven across human tissues; in the comparative in-vitro bioassay work all three benchmarked SARMs (including GLPG0492) were still androgenic in prostate carcinoma cells, so 'sparing' is a tissue-context effect, not an absence of prostate activity. PMID 25185887 (spared reproductive tissue, as efficacious as TP); PMID 22957947 (dissociation from prostate; 'partial agonist'); PMID 36762383 (doping review — 'acts as a partial agonist for androgen receptors')

  6. In dystrophic (mdx) muscle it also reduced fibrosis and improved diaphragm function Evidence-rodent

    Beyond simple anabolism, in the exercised-mdx Duchenne model GLPG0492 preserved running performance (an acute exhaustion test used as a surrogate of the 6-min walking test), modestly but significantly increased diaphragm force ex vivo, and reduced non-muscle (fibrotic) area and fibrosis markers — matching or approaching α-methylprednisolone and nandrolone comparators. This is the basis for the Duchenne rationale, but it is a single disease-model program, not human DMD data. PMID 23523664 (Pharmacol Res, DOI 10.1016/j.phrs.2013.03.003)

  7. In humans, only a short protein-synthesis signal was probed — long-term human efficacy was never established Speculative

    The one human pharmacodynamic study was designed to measure muscle protein fractional synthesis rate (phenylalanine uptake into muscle biopsies) in healthy men, plus hormone (‘hormone household’) effects in postmenopausal women, after ~1–2 weeks of dosing. No trial results are posted for any GLPG0492 study, and development did not advance to patient efficacy trials. Whether GLPG0492 meaningfully builds muscle or helps Duchenne patients in humans is unknown — no human outcome data exist. NCT01538420 (Phase 1 PD, muscle protein synthesis + postmenopausal hormone effects; COMPLETED 2012, no posted results); program not advanced

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. Direct partial agonist at the androgen receptor ligand-binding domain (AR, NR3C4; ChEMBL target CHEMBL1871 = human Androgen receptor, UniProt P10275, gene AR, HGNC:644). ChEMBL curates GLPG0492 (CHEMBL2178100) as an AR 'MODULATOR' with direct_interaction=true and disease_efficacy=true, mechanism comment 'selective androgen modulator (SARM)'. Chemically it is the (S)-(-) enantiomer of a 4-(hydroxymethyl)diarylhydantoin: (S)-(-)-4-(4-(hydroxymethyl)-3-methyl-2,5-dioxo-4-phenylimidazolidin-1-yl)-2-(trifluoromethyl)benzonitrile. Formula C19H14F3N3O3, MW 389.33, ALogP 2.86, QED 0.82, 0 Rule-of-Five violations, single defined stereocentre (chirality=1). SMILES CN1C(=O)N(c2ccc(C#N)c(C(F)(F)F)c2)C(=O)[C@@]1(CO)c1ccccc1; InChIKey VAJGULUVTFDTAS-GOSISDBHSA-N. The trifluoromethyl-benzonitrile hydantoin scaffold is shared with anti-androgens (nilutamide/enzalutamide family), but the 4-hydroxymethyl/4-phenyl substitution shifts the profile toward tissue-selective partial agonism.

Signalling cascade. Ligand binding → AR conformational change → nuclear translocation → binding of androgen-response elements → tissue-specific coactivator/corepressor recruitment. In muscle this is proposed to favour Akt/mTOR-driven protein synthesis and FoxO suppression of the atrophy E3 ligases atrogin-1/MAFbx and MuRF1; in prostate the partial-agonist conformation produces only weak transactivation, yielding the rodent muscle-vs-prostate dissociation. IMPORTANT: the precise coregulator map for GLPG0492 has not been published; the downstream Akt/mTOR and atrogin-1/MuRF1 steps are inferred from general androgen/SARM biology, while the GLPG0492-specific data are a gene-expression 'footprint' (immobilization model) plus in-silico AR docking.

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Oral (developed as oral solution and solid/capsule formulations; preclinical mdx efficacy work also used subcutaneous dosing in mice). Note: ChEMBL lists 'oral: false', but the Phase 1 program and discovery paper confirm oral development.
Absorption / bioavailability
Reported oral bioavailability F > 50% in rat in the discovery paper (PMID 22957947). Human relative bioavailability of oral solution vs solid formulation, fasted vs fed, was a specific stated objective of the first-in-human study (NCT01130818) in elderly subjects, but no human PK values are published.
Half-life
Not publicly reported for humans (single- and multiple-ascending-dose PK were collected in NCT01130818/NCT01397370 but no results are posted).
Metabolism
Extensively metabolised. In-vitro microsomal studies (equine liver, and human liver microsomes for doping assays) identified numerous phase I metabolites (mono-/di-hydroxylation, plus cleavage of the 3-methyl and 4-(hydroxymethyl) side chains) and phase II conjugates — e.g., the equine-liver-microsome doping study described a large metabolite panel (~39 metabolites; 21 phase I, 18 phase II) used for confirmation. Human CYP-enzyme identity/mapping is not published; NCT01397370 listed a CYP3A4-induction readout (urinary 6β-OH-cortisol/cortisol ratio) as an objective, but results are unpublished.
Excretion
Renal excretion of parent drug and conjugated metabolites in urine is exploited for anti-doping detection; quantitative human clearance/half-life values are not publicly reported.
Notes
Human PK was a stated objective of NCT01130818 (SAD, incl. food effect) and NCT01397370 (MAD), but remains unpublished, so all human PK statements are limited to study objectives, not data.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

Rodent dose-response is documented; human dose-response is not. In the exercised-mdx mouse (PMID 23523664), a 4-week single high dose (30 mg/kg s.c., 6 d/wk) increased strength and preserved fatigue resistance comparably to α-methylprednisolone (1 mg/kg i.p.) and nandrolone (5 mg/kg s.c.); a follow-up 12-week study across 0.3–30 mg/kg s.c. confirmed strength/fatigue benefits and showed efficacy at lower doses (dose-dependence). In the hindlimb-immobilization mouse (PMID 25185887), GLPG0492 dose-dependently and partially prevented atrophy with a trend to fiber hypertrophy, as efficacious as testosterone propionate while sparing reproductive tissue. In humans, dosing was ascending in Phase 1 with a stated goal of determining the maximum tolerated dose, and ~1–2 weeks of repeat dosing was used to probe muscle protein synthesis — but no human dose-response or MTD is published. NO human therapeutic dose exists and none should be inferred.

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Testosterone / anabolic steroids
    same: Both are AR agonists that build skeletal muscle and, in rodents, improve muscle mass and function
    different: GLPG0492 is a non-steroidal PARTIAL agonist engineered for muscle-vs-prostate dissociation (rat prostate spared where testosterone/TP causes hypertrophy); it is orally active and does not aromatise to estrogen. But testosterone has decades of human data and approved uses, whereas GLPG0492 has essentially none.
  • vs RAD-140 (testolone) and GSK-2881078
    same:
    different: Directly benchmarked against these in the Zierau 2019 in-vitro/in-silico study: all three are androgenic SARMs, but molecular docking suggested each binds the AR LBD slightly differently, and chemotypes differ (GLPG0492 = diarylhydantoin; RAD-140 = phenyl-oxadiazole; GSK-2881078 = indole). Unlike RAD-140 and GSK-2881078, which are widely used grey-market compounds, GLPG0492 is a discontinued pharma candidate with a defined Phase 1 program but no advancement and no posted human results.
  • vs Enobosarm (ostarine / GTx-024)
    same:
    different: Enobosarm (arylpropionamide chemotype) reached large Phase 2/3 human programs and has actual human lean-mass and physical-function data (e.g., PMID 22031847, a placebo-controlled Phase 2 in elderly men and postmenopausal women); GLPG0492 stopped at Phase 1 with no posted human results. Both share the SARM muscle-sparing-prostate concept and both are WADA-banned.
  • vs Corticosteroids (prednisolone/α-methylprednisolone, standard-of-care in DMD)
    same: In the mdx model GLPG0492 matched α-methylprednisolone on strength/fatigue endpoints
    different: The pitch is anabolic benefit WITHOUT steroid catabolic side effects (muscle wasting, osteoporosis, growth suppression) — but this advantage is shown only in mice; no head-to-head human DMD data exist.

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

Discovered at Galapagos (Nique et al., J Med Chem 2012, PMID 22957947) from a 4-methyl-4-(4-hydroxyphenyl)hydantoin series optimised into 4-(hydroxymethyl)diarylhydantoins; the (S)-(-) enantiomer became GLPG0492 (development codes DT-200, G-100192). Developed for musculoskeletal wasting (sarcopenia, cachexia) and later positioned for Duchenne muscular dystrophy on the strength of the mdx data (PMID 23523664). Three Phase 1 trials in healthy volunteers ran 2010–2012 (NCT01130818 first-in-human SAD with food-effect/elderly bioavailability; NCT01397370 MAD; NCT01538420 muscle-protein-synthesis PD + postmenopausal hormone effects); the sponsor is now listed on ClinicalTrials.gov as Lakefront Biotherapeutics NV. No results were ever posted (has_results=false for all three) and the program was not advanced into patient efficacy studies — GLPG0492 is a discontinued/shelved candidate, not an approved drug anywhere. Regulatory footprint since then is chiefly anti-doping: SARMs have been on the WADA Prohibited List since January 2008, and GLPG0492 is a named, detectable prohibited substance in human, equine and bovine testing.

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • Human efficacy: does GLPG0492 actually build muscle or help Duchenne/sarcopenia/cachexia patients? Only a short protein-synthesis surrogate was designed to be measured; no patient outcomes exist and no results were posted.
  • Human safety: no posted Phase 1 results — real-world tolerated dose, PK/half-life, and hormone/lipid/liver signals in humans are unknown.
  • Does the rodent muscle-vs-prostate selectivity translate to humans? SARM selectivity has historically translated poorly, and human HPG-axis and HDL effects were never reported for this compound.
  • Why was it discontinued — safety, PK, efficacy, or strategic/portfolio reasons? Not publicly disclosed.
  • Molecular basis of its tissue selectivity (coregulator recruitment map) is inferred/in-silico, not directly characterised for GLPG0492.
  • Long-term effects on bone, cardiovascular risk, fertility, and behaviour in humans are entirely uncharacterised.

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Framed strictly as harm-reduction for the reality that this is sold as an unapproved research chemical — NOT a protocol or endorsement, and NO doses or dosing schedules are provided. Anyone exposed to a SARM like this would, on general class grounds, warrant clinician-supervised monitoring of: (1) hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis — total/free testosterone, LH, FSH (SARMs can suppress endogenous production; watch for post-exposure hypogonadism, low libido, mood/energy changes); (2) lipids, especially HDL-cholesterol, which oral SARMs lower as a class; (3) liver function (ALT/AST/bilirubin), given the recurrent transaminase-elevation signal with oral non-steroidal SARMs; (4) hematocrit/hemoglobin (androgen-driven erythrocytosis); (5) in men, PSA and prostate symptoms despite rat prostate-sparing (human selectivity unproven, and GLPG0492 was still androgenic in prostate cells in vitro); (6) in women, virilizing signs (voice change, hirsutism, menstrual disruption) — some are irreversible. Because product identity and purity are unverifiable on the grey market, contamination/mislabelling is a real risk. The single most important point: there is no established human dose, no evidence of benefit, and it is a banned doping agent — the evidence base does not support use, and any exposure or symptoms should be discussed with a clinician.

Caveats

Human data are essentially absent: the entire efficacy and muscle-vs-prostate selectivity story rests on rodent (orchidectomized rat, hindlimb-immobilization mouse, exercised-mdx mouse) and in-vitro/in-silico work. Rodent tissue-selectivity has repeatedly FAILED to translate cleanly to humans for the SARM class (e.g., HDL-cholesterol suppression, HPG-axis/testosterone suppression, and liver-enzyme signals seen with clinically-tested oral SARMs are not captured by rodent prostate-sparing assays). No Phase 1 results are public, so the human safety profile, tolerated dose, PK, and lipid/hormone effects of GLPG0492 itself are effectively unknown. Note also that in the comparative in-vitro bioassays, GLPG0492 (like RAD-140 and GSK-2881078) was still androgenic in prostate carcinoma cells — 'prostate-sparing' is a tissue-context/partial-agonist effect in rodents, not an absence of prostate activity. The Duchenne rationale is one preclinical program; no DMD patient has been studied. Do not read 'partial agonist / prostate-sparing in rats' as 'safe' — SARMs still suppress endogenous testosterone/LH via negative feedback and lower HDL in humans as a class. This is an unapproved, discontinued compound and a banned doping agent, sold as a grey-market research chemical of unverified identity/purity. Nothing here is a protocol or endorsement.
