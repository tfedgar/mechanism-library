Enclomiphene

A pill that tricks the brain into thinking estrogen is low, so the body cranks up its OWN testosterone — without shutting down the testicles or sperm.

Human / approved 15 citations
Class
Non-steroidal selective estrogen receptor modulator (SERM) — acts as an estrogen receptor ANTAGONIST in the hypothalamus
Primary target
Estrogen receptor (ERalpha) on GnRH/feedback neurons in the hypothalamus (and to a lesser extent the pituitary)
Human status
Extensively studied in humans: phase II and two large phase III randomized controlled trials in men with secondary (hypogonadotropic) hypogonadism, plus PK/PD studies, and confirmed in a 2025 systematic review/meta-analysis of RCTs. Despite meeting testosterone-restoration endpoints, the developer (Repros Therapeutics, brand Androxal) did NOT obtain FDA approval, partly over sperm-concentration co-primary endpoints and the regulatory framing for hypogonadism. It is therefore NOT an FDA-approved drug; real-world use is off-label and via compounding pharmacies. A 2026 British Society for Sexual Medicine position statement reviews its potential role. Best supported as a fertility-sparing alternative to testosterone replacement in men with intact testes and secondary hypogonadism. NOTE: enclomiphene is investigational/UNAPPROVED by FDA (Androxal/enclomiphene did not gain approval); use is off-label/research.

At a glance

  • Raises total testosterone by ~274 ng/dL vs placebo (comparable to T-gel) while RAISING rather than suppressing LH and FSH
  • Preserves sperm counts and testicular volume — restoration of endogenous production, not exogenous replacement
  • Oral once-daily dosing; does not require injections or transdermal gels
  • Modest rise in estradiol (from aromatization of the extra endogenous testosterone), not from the drug aromatizing
  • Not an anabolic steroid: does not bind the androgen receptor, is not 5-alpha-reduced, is not progestogenic, and is not 17-alpha-alkylated (so it lacks the classic oral-AAS liver toxicity profile)
  • Cleaner isomer profile than clomiphene — avoids accumulation of the long-lived estrogenic zuclomiphene isomer

Mechanism, step by step

  1. It blocks the brain's estrogen 'off-switch' Established

    Normally, estrogen (made from testosterone) tells the hypothalamus to slow down — that's negative feedback. Enclomiphene is an estrogen receptor antagonist: it sits on those estrogen receptors in the hypothalamus and blocks estrogen from binding, so the brain stops sensing estrogen and behaves as if levels are low. PMID 19204885; PMID 27337642

  2. The hypothalamus speeds up its GnRH pulses Established

    With the estrogen brake released, the hypothalamus increases its pulsatile release of GnRH (gonadotropin-releasing hormone), the master signal that drives the rest of the axis. PMID 27337642; PMID 31063005

  3. The pituitary pumps out more LH and FSH Evidence-human

    Enclomiphene is the trans-isomer of clomiphene; it blocks estrogen receptors at the hypothalamus, lifting the brake on GnRH so the pituitary releases more LH and FSH. Across SERM trials (clomiphene + enclomiphene) this raises endogenous testosterone substantially while, unlike TRT, preserving sperm production. PMID 41066380; PMID 23875626

  4. LH tells the testicles' Leydig cells to make testosterone Established

    LH signals the Leydig cells inside the testes to produce endogenous testosterone. This is RESTORATION of the body's own production, not replacement from outside — so testicular size and function are maintained rather than shrinking. PMID 25044085; PMID 26496621

  5. FSH keeps sperm production running Evidence-human

    Because FSH stays high (FSH drives Sertoli cells and spermatogenesis), sperm counts are preserved or improved. This is the headline advantage over testosterone gel/injections, which suppress LH/FSH and can cause low sperm counts / infertility. PMID 25044085; PMID 26496621

  6. Total testosterone rises into the normal range Established

    Net result: total testosterone increased by roughly +274 ng/dL versus placebo across trials — a rise comparable to topical testosterone gel, but achieved through the body's own axis. Estradiol also rises modestly, because the extra testosterone is partly aromatized to estrogen (the drug itself is not aromatized). PMID 41066380; PMID 25044085

  7. Why it differs from plain clomiphene Evidence-human

    Clomiphene citrate is a 50/50-ish mix of two isomers: enclomiphene (trans), the anti-estrogenic isomer that does the useful work, and zuclomiphene (cis), a weakly ESTROGENIC isomer with a very long half-life that accumulates over weeks and may blunt benefits or cause estrogenic side effects. Enclomiphene is the isolated 'clean' isomer that clears the body faster. PMID 31063005; PMID 27511863

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Enclomiphene is the (E)/trans-stereoisomer of clomiphene, a triphenylethylene non-steroidal SERM (C26H28ClNO, MW ~406; citrate salt MW ~598; ChEMBL CHEMBL954 / citrate CHEMBL1200667). PRIMARY TARGET: the two nuclear estrogen receptors, ERalpha (ESR1) and ERbeta (ESR2). At the male hypothalamus and pituitary it behaves predominantly as an ER ANTAGONIST, blocking estradiol's negative feedback (Established for the net hypothalamic effect; Evidence-human for the LH/FSH/T rise). NOTE on numbers: a clean published human ERalpha vs ERbeta Ki/Kd for the pure trans-isomer is NOT well established in the indexed corpus, so a specific affinity ratio should be labeled Contested/under-characterized rather than quoted as fact. What IS established (Evidence-rodent/in vitro) is the ISOMER contrast: the trans-isomer (enclomiphene) is the predominantly anti-estrogenic, shorter-lived isomer, whereas the cis-isomer zuclomiphene is the longer-acting, more estrogen-AGONIST isomer (Turner 1998 rat tissue panel PMID 9724022; Huang & Miller ovine pituitary PMID 6401242, which also shows the antagonist/agonist split is species- and tissue-dependent). OFF-TARGET binding (Evidence-in vitro, ChEMBL/DrugMatrix radioligand panel, CHEMBL954): high-affinity binding to the emopamil-binding protein / 3-beta-hydroxysteroid-Delta8,Delta7-sterol isomerase (EBP) Ki ~1 nM, and to sigma-1 non-opioid receptor (SIGMAR1) Ki ~8 nM; moderate alpha-2 adrenergic receptor binding (alpha-2A Ki ~351 nM, alpha-2C ~215 nM, alpha-2B ~721 nM); weak/absent at adenosine, beta-adrenergic, acetylcholinesterase (inactive <50% at 10 uM). The EBP/sterol-isomerase affinity is the triphenylethylene class signature linked historically to lens/sterol effects. SERM tissue-selectivity principle: enclomiphene treats the RECEPTOR (competitive block) rather than cutting estrogen SYNTHESIS the way aromatase inhibitors do.

Signalling cascade. Hypothalamus -> testis cascade (Established mechanism, Evidence-human for output): (1) In eugonadal men, testis-derived testosterone is aromatized to estradiol; estradiol binds ERalpha on hypothalamic feedback neurons (kisspeptin/GnRH circuitry) and on pituitary gonadotropes, suppressing GnRH pulse amplitude/frequency and LH/FSH. (2) Enclomiphene competitively occupies hypothalamic/pituitary ERalpha, so estradiol cannot deliver its inhibitory signal -> the negative-feedback 'brake' is released. (3) Disinhibited GnRH neurons increase pulsatile GnRH secretion into the hypophyseal portal system. (4) GnRH acts on pituitary gonadotrope GnRH receptors (Gq/11 -> PLCbeta -> IP3/DAG -> Ca2+ and PKC) to increase synthesis and pulsatile release of LH and FSH. (5) LH binds Leydig-cell LHCGR (Gs -> cAMP -> PKA -> StAR/CYP11A1) driving endogenous testosterone synthesis; FSH supports Sertoli-cell function and spermatogenesis. The key clinical signature: because the testis is driven physiologically (LH/FSH UP), intratesticular testosterone and sperm production are MAINTAINED, unlike exogenous testosterone which suppresses LH/FSH. Effects persisted ~1 week after stopping in the PD study, consistent with slow receptor off-kinetics / isomer half-life.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral (the developmental form Androxal/enclomiphene citrate was an oral once-daily tablet). A retrospective case series also describes a compounded sublingual enclomiphene-mineral-oxide delivery format (PMID 42170362, n=15, low certainty / non-standard).
Absorption / bioavailability
Orally absorbed; a precise human absolute bioavailability figure for the pure trans-isomer is not well established in the indexed literature (Evidence-human gap). In the phase II PK substudy, oral 6.25-25 mg produced dose-related rises in serum drug and in testosterone within ~2 weeks (PMID 23875626).
Half-life
Enclomiphene (trans) is the SHORTER-lived isomer. Clinically important contrast: with chronic clomiphene dosing the cis-isomer zuclomiphene accumulates massively while enclomiphene stays low - in long-term male clomiphene users the median zuclomiphene:enclomiphene serum ratio was ~20:1 (ZUC median 44 ng/mL vs ENC 2.2 ng/mL; PMID 27511863), and in cycling women day-3 enclomiphene was undetectable while zuclomiphene progressively accumulated (PMID 10202872). Enclomiphene's terminal half-life is on the order of a day-or-few (much shorter than zuclomiphene's multi-week accumulation), but an exact validated human t1/2 number for the isolated trans-isomer is not firmly pinned in the indexed corpus (label as Evidence-human, approximate).
Metabolism
Hepatic. CYP2D6 is the primary enzyme metabolizing enclomiphene in vitro (human liver microsomes + recombinant CYP2D6; quinidine abolished metabolism, ketoconazole had little effect, i.e. CYP3A4 minor) - PMID 18445989 (Evidence-in vitro). A major metabolite is 4-hydroxy-enclomiphene (ChEMBL CHEMBL1056), analogous to tamoxifen's 4-OH active metabolite. Implication: CYP2D6 poor-metabolizer status or strong CYP2D6 inhibitors could alter exposure (Speculative for clinical magnitude).
Excretion
Triphenylethylene SERMs of this class undergo hepatic metabolism with biliary/fecal elimination of metabolites and enterohepatic recycling (the class basis for zuclomiphene's long persistence). A precise human urinary vs fecal split for enclomiphene is not well documented (Evidence-in vitro/animal; e.g. rabbit microsomal metabolism PMID 6117445).
Notes
ChEMBL lists enclomiphene/its citrate with a prodrug flag (active species includes the 4-OH metabolite, CHEMBL1056). It is a small lipophilic molecule (cLogP ~6.5) that crosses the blood-brain barrier - required for its central hypothalamic action. It is NOT an ester/depot. The 'natural product' flag in ChEMBL is a database artifact, not biology.

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

HUMAN (Evidence-human, secondary/obesity-associated hypogonadism): In the phase II PD/PK study, oral enclomiphene citrate 6.25, 12.5 and 25 mg daily all raised 24-h testosterone, LH and FSH dose-relatedly; after 6 weeks the 25 mg arm reached mean C0h total testosterone ~604 +/- 160 ng/dL vs ~500 +/- 278 ng/dL for testosterone gel (not statistically different), but enclomiphene RAISED LH/FSH while gel SUPPRESSED them (PMID 23875626). Phase II RCT (NCT01270841): enclomiphene normalized morning T and raised LH/FSH while preserving sperm counts, where T-gel suppressed spermatogenesis (PMID 25044085). Phase III (ZA-304/ZA-305, overweight men 18-60, ~16 weeks): both 12.5 and 25 mg restored TT into the normal range, raised LH and FSH, and maintained sperm concentration vs marked suppression on T-gel (PMID 26496621). Meta-analysis of SERM RCTs (clomiphene+enclomiphene): TT mean difference +273.8 ng/dL, LH +4.66 IU/L, FSH +4.59 IU/L vs placebo; no significant TT difference vs T-gel (PMID 41066380). KEY GAP: a formal validated minimum-effective-dose / full human dose-response curve specific to the pure trans-isomer is incomplete because the program stalled before approval; effects are robust across 6.25-25 mg but the optimal dose was never FDA-finalized. ANIMAL/IN VITRO: rodent tissue panels show dose-dependent anti-estrogenic (uterine) and partial bone-protective effects for the trans-isomer (PMID 9724022); ovine pituitary shows antagonism of E2-augmented LH at ~10^-6 M (PMID 6401242). Bottom line: human dose-response for HPG-axis output is established in the 6.25-25 mg/day oral range; everything finer is under-characterized.

How it compares

  • vs Clomiphene citrate (the parent racemic drug)
    same: Both are triphenylethylene SERMs that antagonize hypothalamic ER to raise LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone; both maintain spermatogenesis vs exogenous T; both FDA-approved indication history is in female ovulation induction (clomiphene) with off-label male use.
    different: Clomiphene is a ~62:38 racemic mix of trans-enclomiphene (anti-estrogenic, short-lived) and cis-zuclomiphene (estrogenic, long-accumulating). Enclomiphene is the PURE trans-isomer, designed to give the anti-estrogenic gonadotropin-raising effect while avoiding zuclomiphene's multi-week accumulation (ZUC:ENC ~20:1 on chronic clomiphene, PMID 27511863) and its estrogenic/visual baggage. Net: cleaner, more predictable HPG drive in theory.
  • vs Zuclomiphene (the cis-isomer)
    same: Same molecular scaffold and ER target; the other half of clomiphene.
    different: Zuclomiphene is the LONGER-acting, more ESTROGEN-AGONIST isomer that accumulates over weeks; enclomiphene is shorter-acting and predominantly antagonist. Their tissue effects diverge (Turner PMID 9724022; Huang/Miller PMID 6401242). Zuclomiphene accumulation is blamed for some of clomiphene's persistent estrogenic side effects.
  • vs Tamoxifen
    same: Both triphenylethylene SERMs metabolized to active 4-hydroxy metabolites; both can raise gonadotropins/testosterone in men and both carry the SERM class VTE flag.
    different: Tamoxifen is developed/approved for breast cancer (strong breast-tissue ER antagonism) and is commonly used in PCT/gynecomastia contexts; enclomiphene was developed specifically as an oral fertility-sparing TRT alternative for male secondary hypogonadism. Tamoxifen has more documented anti-estrogenic breast efficacy; enclomiphene's selling point is single-isomer cleanliness for HPG-axis restart.
  • vs Aromatase inhibitors (e.g. anastrozole)
    same: Both can raise endogenous testosterone in hypogonadal/obese men by reducing estrogenic negative feedback.
    different: AIs cut estradiol SYNTHESIS (block aromatase) and lower systemic E2 - risking bone loss and lipid effects; enclomiphene BLOCKS the estrogen RECEPTOR centrally while systemic E2 actually RISES (testis driven). Different mechanism, different estradiol direction, different bone/lipid risk profile.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Boldenone undecylenate compounds risk ⚠ Speculative
    Both can raise estradiol, so together they can compound estrogenic side-effect risk. Enclomiphene raises endogenous testosterone that is partly aromatized (lifting systemic E2) and does NOT block ER in breast/peripheral tissue, so its central ER antagonism won't neutralize peripheral E2. Boldenone can aromatize to estradiol, adding a second E2 source. Caveat: boldenone's aromatization is weak and its monograph notes it often LOWERS measured E2 on a testosterone base via mundane displacement chemistry, so the additive-estrogen effect is inconsistent rather than guaranteed. A separate, more reliable compounding concern is hematocrit — boldenone robustly raises red-cell mass. (Axis tension also applies: boldenone, an AR agonist, suppresses LH/FSH, opposing enclomiphene's central drive.) Enclomiphene monograph (side_effect_mechanisms: elevated estradiol; peripheral ER not blocked); Boldenone monograph (weak aromatization, 'estrogen-killer' reputation is folklore, often lowers measured E2; robustly raises hematocrit); corpus MoA card 'Estrogen receptors' Watch: Estradiol on a sensitive assay plus breast exam/symptoms (tenderness, swelling) for gynecomastia. Hematocrit/hemoglobin on a full blood count for boldenone-driven erythrocytosis. LH/FSH to see whether the androgen is suppressing the axis enclomiphene is trying to drive.
  • Anastrozole counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    The two act on opposite sides of the estrogen story. Enclomiphene blocks the estrogen RECEPTOR centrally, so systemic estradiol actually RISES (more endogenous testosterone is aromatized). Anastrozole blocks aromatase (CYP19A1), cutting estradiol SYNTHESIS so serum E2 FALLS. Both raise testosterone by relieving estrogenic negative feedback, but they push estradiol in opposite directions — so the AI counteracts the E2 rise enclomiphene produces, with a risk of over-correcting E2 into a crashed range. The SERM+AI pairing is used clinically for male hypogonadism/infertility, so the interaction is genuine rather than merely theoretical. Enclomiphene monograph (comparisons vs aromatase inhibitors; side_effect_mechanisms: elevated estradiol); Anastrozole monograph (blocks E2 synthesis; raises LH/FSH/T in men); corpus MoA card 'Estrogen receptors' Watch: Estradiol on a sensitive assay is the key lab — the AI can drive E2 too low. Watch for crashed-estrogen signs (low libido, joint aches, low mood) and adverse changes on a lipid panel and bone markers. Track LH/FSH and total/free testosterone to confirm the axis is still being driven.
  • Andarine counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    Andarine suppresses the HPG axis by acting as an AR agonist at the pituitary/hypothalamus, dropping LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone. Enclomiphene is a SERM that antagonizes estrogen receptors on hypothalamic feedback neurons, removing estrogenic negative feedback and driving LH/FSH back up to restart the axis. It therefore acts in the opposite direction on the same LH/FSH -> testosterone circuit that andarine shuts down. andarine-s-4.json (mechanism_step 5 HPG suppression); enclomiphene-enclomiphene-citrate-trans-.json (ERalpha antagonist raises LH/FSH); PMID 26496621; PMID 23875626 Watch: LH, FSH and total testosterone to confirm the axis is recovering; note enclomiphene can push FSH/LH high and may lower IGF-1. Harm-reduction context, not a dosing or PCT protocol.
  • Exemestane counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    Exemestane is a steroidal 'suicide' aromatase inactivator that lowers estradiol by irreversibly destroying the enzyme (blocking E2 synthesis); enclomiphene RAISES estradiol by increasing the endogenous testosterone that feeds aromatase. The AI thus counteracts enclomiphene's E2 rise — mechanistically the same receptor-block (enclomiphene, E2 up) versus synthesis-block (exemestane, E2 down) opposition as the other AIs. Enclomiphene monograph (comparisons vs aromatase inhibitors; side_effect_mechanisms: elevated estradiol); Exemestane monograph (~85-95% E2 suppression; raises T:E2 ratio in men); corpus MoA card 'Estrogen receptors' Watch: Estradiol on a sensitive assay to avoid over-suppression; watch crashed-estrogen symptoms (libido, joint pain/stiffness, mood) and a lipid panel. Track testosterone and LH/FSH to confirm intended axis drive.
  • Ligandrol counteracts ↓ Established
    Enclomiphene is the pure trans-isomer SERM that antagonises estrogen-receptor-alpha on hypothalamic feedback neurons, raising GnRH and thus LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone. It directly opposes the secondary hypogonadism Ligandrol induces through androgenic negative feedback — the intended counteracting mechanism for HPTA recovery — while (unlike clomiphene) largely avoiding the estrogenic zuclomiphene isomer. It counteracts the suppression only; it does nothing to the AR-mediated effects and only helps once Ligandrol is cleared enough for feedback to lift. enclomiphene-enclomiphene-citrate-trans-.json (ERalpha antagonist, raises LH/FSH/T); PMID 35783920; PMID 22459616 (LGD gonadotropin/T suppression) Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone to confirm the axis is re-firing; estradiol (rises with restored T). Symptomatic recovery of libido/energy alongside bloodwork.
  • Letrozole opposes Established
    Enclomiphene is the pure trans-isomer SERM antagonizing hypothalamic/pituitary estrogen-receptor feedback, raising LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone — and, as with clomiphene, the resulting testosterone aromatizes so serum estradiol tends to RISE (its own monograph flags that E2 rises, the opposite of an AI). Letrozole works the opposite way, blocking aromatase to drive E2 down. Their opposing effects on the estradiol level are the interaction: adding letrozole to enclomiphene can neutralize the very E2 rise enclomiphene produces, risking a crash. enclomiphene monograph step 6 & side_effect 'elevated estradiol' (E2 rises, opposite of an AI; PMID 25044085); clomiphene-citrate-clomid.json (shared SERM mechanism); letrozole.json expand.comparisons Watch: Sensitive serum estradiol, LH/FSH, total/free testosterone; if both are present, watch for over-suppression of E2 (joint aches, low libido, mood, bone-loss risk on extended use).
  • Methyltestosterone opposes Established
    Enclomiphene is a SERM that antagonizes hypothalamic ERalpha to remove estrogen negative feedback and thereby raise LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone. Methyltestosterone opposes this: as an AR agonist it directly suppresses GnRH/LH/FSH via androgen negative feedback, blunting the gonadotropin rise the SERM is meant to produce. The exogenous androgen fights the SERM's axis-restoring intent. Morales 1994 LH/SHBG suppression PMID 8072077; enclomiphene monograph (hypothalamic ERalpha antagonist raising LH/FSH); methyltestosterone monograph side_effect_mechanisms (HPG suppression) Watch: LH/FSH and total/free testosterone (expect a blunted gonadotropin/testosterone response), estradiol, and SHBG (methyltestosterone suppresses it, distorting free-hormone interpretation).
  • Nandrolone opposes Evidence-human
    Enclomiphene is a SERM that blocks estrogen receptors at the hypothalamus, removing estrogen negative feedback and raising GnRH -> LH/FSH -> endogenous testosterone. This opposes nandrolone's central HPG suppression and is used mechanistically to help restart the axis after the long-acting depot clears. Two important limits: (1) enclomiphene only blocks the ESTROGENIC feedback arm, whereas nandrolone suppresses via androgenic/progestogenic feedback too, so the block is incomplete; and (2) a brain-level SERM cannot override suppression while circulating exogenous androgen is still present, so it cannot meaningfully restart the axis until nandrolone has fallen — and it requires an intact, responsive pituitary and testes. Nandrolone monograph step 7 and key_unknowns (slow depot; PPAAH); Enclomiphene monograph steps 1-5 (hypothalamic ER antagonist raising LH/FSH/endogenous T) and caveats (cannot override active exogenous-androgen suppression; only works with responsive pituitary/testes) Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone and estradiol to confirm axis reactivation (LH/FSH should RISE; estradiol typically rises, opposite of an AI); timing matters given nandrolone's slow depot clearance (19-norandrosterone can remain detectable for months). Watch for SERM-class signs: visual disturbance (a red flag), mood change, breast tenderness, and clot warning signs.
  • RAD-140 opposes Evidence-human
    RAD-140 is a SARM (androgen-receptor agonist) that suppresses the HPG axis — its androgenic signal re-engages negative feedback and shuts down GnRH→LH→FSH and endogenous testosterone. Enclomiphene works in the opposite direction, blocking central ER to RAISE LH/FSH. While RAD-140 is present its suppression opposes enclomiphene's drive; enclomiphene is instead used to attempt recovery of the axis after RAD-140 is stopped, and it cannot override active SARM suppression. Enclomiphene monograph (caveats; HPG mechanism); RAD-140 monograph (HPG-axis shutdown; lowers HDL; DILI case reports); corpus MoA card 'HPG axis' Watch: LH/FSH and total/free testosterone to gauge suppression versus recovery (LH/FSH should rise once RAD-140 clears and enclomiphene acts). HDL/lipid panel (RAD-140 lowers HDL); liver enzymes given RAD-140's hepatotoxicity signal. Semen analysis if fertility is a concern.
  • Testosterone opposes Established
    They pull the HPG axis in opposite directions. Enclomiphene RELIEVES estrogenic negative feedback to drive endogenous LH/FSH and testicular testosterone. Exogenous testosterone (and its aromatized estradiol) does the reverse — it re-engages negative feedback at the hypothalamus/pituitary, SUPPRESSING GnRH→LH→FSH and shutting down the Leydig-cell drive enclomiphene is trying to stimulate. Critically, enclomiphene's brain-level mechanism cannot override exogenous-testosterone suppression: while on TRT the axis stays shut regardless of central ER blockade. The two also compound estrogenic and hematologic effects — both raise estradiol (enclomiphene via endogenous aromatization, TRT via the administered testosterone) and TRT independently raises hematocrit. Enclomiphene monograph (caveats: 'anyone already on exogenous testosterone is suppressed and enclomiphene's brain-level mechanism cannot override that'; comparisons vs T-gel); Testosterone monograph (HPG-axis shutdown; raises hematocrit and E2); corpus MoA card 'HPG axis' Watch: LH/FSH (suppressed by exogenous T — the tell that the axis is shut, not driven); total/free testosterone; estradiol (can rise from both); hematocrit/hemoglobin on a full blood count for testosterone-driven erythrocytosis. Semen analysis if fertility matters, since exogenous T suppresses spermatogenesis.
  • Trenbolone opposes Established
    Trenbolone is a strong androgen-receptor agonist that potently suppresses the HPG axis (GnRH→LH→FSH), collapsing endogenous testosterone and spermatogenesis. Enclomiphene raises central LH/FSH to drive the testis — so while trenbolone is present its suppressive feedback works directly against enclomiphene's axis-stimulating action, and enclomiphene cannot override active androgen suppression (it is used to attempt an HPTA restart only after such compounds clear). A wrinkle unique to trenbolone: it is progestogenic and tends to raise prolactin, and enclomiphene does nothing to counter that (prolactin control needs a dopamine agonist), so the progestin/prolactin-driven side-effect channel is not addressed by the SERM. Enclomiphene monograph (caveats: suppression cannot be overridden; mechanism_steps HPG drive); Trenbolone monograph (AR agonist, HPG suppression, progestogenic/prolactin, non-aromatizing); corpus MoA card 'HPG axis' Watch: LH/FSH (suppressed while trenbolone is active; should rise once it clears and enclomiphene drives the axis); total/free testosterone; prolactin (trenbolone-driven, not fixed by the SERM); estradiol; semen analysis for fertility recovery. Watch mood given both agents' CNS effects.
  • Clomiphene citrate redundant Established
    Enclomiphene IS the purified active trans-isomer of clomiphene — the exact molecule doing clomiphene's work. Commercial clomiphene is roughly a 50:50 mix of enclomiphene (the potent, shorter-acting ER antagonist that raises LH/FSH/T) plus zuclomiphene (a long-lived, weakly estrogenic isomer). Taking enclomiphene alongside clomiphene is pharmacologically redundant: they occupy the same hypothalamic/pituitary ERalpha, drive the same GnRH->LH/FSH->testosterone cascade, and stack the same ER-antagonist effect with no new mechanism — just more of the same target engagement, plus (from the clomiphene) the extra slow-accumulating zuclomiphene estrogenic load that pure enclomiphene avoids. Clomiphene and enclomiphene monographs (enclomiphene = purified trans-isomer of clomiphene, same ERalpha target and same GnRH->LH/FSH->T cascade); IDrugs review of enclomiphene as ER antagonist, PMID 19204885 Watch: LH/FSH, total/free testosterone, estradiol; there is no additive mechanism to expect beyond a single agent, and clomiphene adds slow-accumulating zuclomiphene (estrogenic) — watch for estrogenic and clomiphene-specific visual side effects that track the clomiphene component.
  • Human chorionic gonadotropin redundant Evidence-human
    Both aim to raise endogenous/testicular testosterone, but they enter the LH→Leydig pathway at DIFFERENT points via non-overlapping molecular targets — so they are largely redundant rather than sharing a target. Enclomiphene works at the TOP of the axis (blocks hypothalamic estrogen receptors → more endogenous LH/FSH → drives the testis physiologically). hCG BYPASSES the brain, acting directly as an LH-receptor (LHCGR) agonist on Leydig cells. They converge on the same downstream effector (Leydig-cell / intratesticular testosterone) by different routes — partly redundant, partly complementary. Because hCG raises intratesticular T and E2 and, through feedback, suppresses the pituitary's own LH output, it can blunt or work against enclomiphene's central 'restart' logic if the goal is axis self-sufficiency. Enclomiphene monograph (mechanism_steps 3-5, central drive); hCG monograph (LHCGR agonist on Leydig cells, downstream of the brain; raises E2); Clomiphene monograph (comparison: hCG acts downstream vs SERM upstream); corpus MoA card 'HPG axis' Watch: Total/free testosterone and estradiol (both can rise, E2 especially with hCG-driven aromatization). Track LH/FSH — hCG feedback suppresses endogenous LH, so a FALLING LH/FSH signals the pituitary drive enclomiphene is meant to build is being overridden. Hematocrit/hemoglobin on a full blood count; semen analysis and testicular exam if fertility is the goal.

History &amp; legal status

"Origin: a triphenylethylene developed from the clomiphene scaffold (clomiphene = Clomid, RMI-16289; enclomiphene = Isomer B / trans-clomiphene; USAN listed ~1974 for enclomiphene). Enclomiphene citrate was developed by Repros Therapeutics as 'Androxal' specifically as an ORAL, fertility-preserving alternative to testosterone replacement for men with secondary hypogonadism. It advanced through phase II and two pivotal phase III trials (ZA-304/ZA-305) and reached ChEMBL max_phase 3. Key unknowns / open questions

  • No FDA-approved finished product means no validated standardized human dose-response or multi-year safety dataset; short-term efficacy is robust but long-term CV/VTE/fracture/prostate outcomes are unquantified.
  • A clean published human ERalpha-vs-ERbeta binding affinity (Ki/Kd) and selectivity ratio for the pure trans-isomer is not well established - the 'ERalpha antagonist' label is largely extrapolated from clomiphene/isomer and tissue data.
  • Clinical role of high-affinity off-targets (emopamil-binding protein/sterol isomerase Ki ~1 nM; sigma-1 Ki ~8 nM; alpha-2 adrenergic) in visual or mood side effects is unknown.
  • CYP2D6-dependence (PMID 18445989) predicts altered exposure in poor metabolizers or with CYP2D6-inhibitor co-medication, but this is clinically uncharacterized for enclomiphene.
  • Long-term fertility/spermatogenesis durability and whether the HPG axis adapts beyond the ~16-week trial windows are unproven.
  • Whether enclomiphene shares the SERM-class VTE/thrombotic risk at therapeutic male doses is untested; and the bone/lipid consequences of chronically RAISING (not lowering) systemic estradiol in men are not characterized.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (no dosing guidance). Bloodwork commonly tracked at baseline and periodically when axis-stimulating SERMs are used: total + free testosterone with SHBG (confirm true restoration); LH and FSH (these should RISE with enclomiphene - a fall instead suggests the drug isn't working as intended or there is primary testicular failure); estradiol on a sensitive assay (E2 typically RISES, opposite of an aromatase inhibitor, so watch for high-E2 symptoms rather than a crash); hematocrit/hemoglobin; and a lipid panel. For fertility goals, semen analysis is the functional endpoint. CLINICAL SIGNS to watch and escalate to a clinician: new/worsening VISUAL disturbance (blurring, floaters, light sensitivity, persistent afterimages) - a recognized SERM red flag warranting stopping and ophthalmology review; mood change/irritability; breast tenderness or swelling (gynecomastia from rising E2); headache or hot flushes; and any clot warning signs (unilateral leg swelling/pain, sudden shortness of breath, chest pain) given the SERM-class thrombotic flag - seek urgent care. Interaction note: CYP2D6 is the main metabolizing enzyme, so strong CYP2D6 inhibitors (some antidepressants) are worth flagging to a prescriber. This is what to measure and when to escalate - not a protocol; dosing decisions belong with a qualified clinician."

Caveats

Mechanism and short-to-medium-term hormone effects are well-established from human RCTs; long-term efficacy and safety (cardiovascular, bone, mood) are NOT established — trials were mostly months, not years. It only works when the testes and pituitary can still respond (secondary/functional hypogonadism); it will not help primary testicular failure. The meta-analysis pooled clomiphene and enclomiphene and had high heterogeneity (I2 ~89% for testosterone), so exact magnitude varies by population. SERMs can have estrogen-AGONIST effects in some tissues, and rare reports of visual disturbances exist with clomiphene-class drugs. The drug is not an anabolic steroid and is not the same as taking testosterone — anyone already on exogenous testosterone is suppressed and enclomiphene's brain-level mechanism cannot override that.
