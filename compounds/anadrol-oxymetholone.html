Library / Anabolic-androgenic steroids

Anadrol

An old-school oral steroid that bloats you up fast — it crushes hepcidin to flood the bone marrow with red cells, but its 17-alpha-alkyl "armor" that lets it survive the liver is the same thing that poisons it.

Limited human data 17 citations
Class
Oral 17-alpha-alkylated anabolic-androgenic steroid (AAS); a synthetic DHT (5-alpha-androstane) derivative
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR) — a nuclear (ligand-activated transcription factor) receptor
Human status
Genuinely human-grounded for several effects — unlike most forum-favorite orals. Two double-blind, placebo-controlled phase III RCTs in HIV-wasting (n=89 combined report, eugonadal men AND women) show real weight/lean-mass gain AND dose-dependent liver toxicity (ALT/AST/GGT >5x baseline in 25-43% on drug vs ~0-8% on placebo) [PMID 12646793, 12815555-RETRACTED]. RCTs in dialysis patients confirm the erythropoietic boost on top of EPO and improved lean mass/insulin sensitivity, again with rising liver enzymes [PMID 21084036, 19356374]. Liver-tumor risk (adenoma, peliosis hepatis, hepatocellular carcinoma) is documented in case series, strongest in Fanconi anemia but present across diagnoses [PMID 15495253]. What is NOT human-quantified at bodybuilder doses: the magnitude of muscle gain, the famous water-weight/"bloat," lipid crash, and BP rise — these come from clinical/lower doses, mechanism, and anecdote, NOT from controlled trials at the doses forums use. Never extrapolate a dose. NO human dosing guidance is provided here.

At a glance

  • Strongly raises red blood cell mass / hematocrit / hemoglobin (its FDA-approved anemia use) — by boosting EPO and suppressing hepcidin to free iron [PMID 21084036, 11440282]
  • Genuinely anabolic in humans: increased weight, lean body mass, and body cell mass in placebo-controlled trials, in men and women [PMID 12646793, 12815555]
  • Marked, dose-dependent hepatotoxicity: enzyme elevations (25-43% >5x baseline in RCTs), cholestatic jaundice, peliosis hepatis, liver adenoma/carcinoma — the hallmark of oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS [PMID 12646793, 27372877, 15495253]
  • Cardiovascular strain: high hematocrit raises blood viscosity, blood pressure, and clot/stroke/MI risk [curated MoA; PMID 11440282]
  • Adverse lipid shift, especially suppressed HDL; pronounced water retention ('bloat') and gynecomastia despite being non-aromatizing [PMID 11440282]
  • Androgenic effects (acne, hair loss) and irreversible virilization in women; suppression of endogenous testosterone [PMID 11440282]
  • Improved insulin sensitivity reported in hemodialysis patients (HOMA-IR fell), tied to gains in fat-free mass [PMID 19356374]

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Binds the androgen receptor like testosterone's louder cousin Established

    Oxymetholone is a lab-made relative of testosterone (a DHT/5-alpha derivative). Inside the cell it latches onto the androgen receptor (AR), the same docking protein that natural testosterone uses. ChEMBL curates it as a full AR agonist (it switches the receptor ON, not partially). That's the single master switch behind essentially all its downstream effects. ChEMBL CHEMBL1200585 (AR agonist, phase 4); review PMID 11440282

  2. The activated receptor walks into the nucleus and rewrites which genes are read (genomic action) Established

    Once oxymetholone is bound, the AR changes shape, moves into the cell nucleus, and acts as a transcription factor — it sits on DNA and turns specific genes up or down. This is the 'genomic mechanism': effects come from changed gene expression (more muscle-protein machinery, altered blood and metabolic genes), which is why steroids act over days-to-weeks, not minutes. It is the same AR-driven genomic pathway that erythroblasts (red-cell precursors) use — human bone-marrow erythroblasts carry functional androgen receptors in their nuclei. AR nuclear receptor mechanism (Established); androgen receptor in human erythroblasts PMID 3260889

  3. Floods the bloodstream with red cells — by freeing up iron, not just nudging EPO Established

    This is Anadrol's signature trick and the reason it was approved for anemia. AR activation pushes the kidney to make more erythropoietin (EPO, the 'make red cells' hormone) AND — crucially — suppresses hepcidin (the liver hormone that locks iron away) while raising erythroferrone. Lower hepcidin = iron released to the marrow = far more raw material for red-cell production. Net result: hematocrit and hemoglobin climb. In dialysis patients, adding oxymetholone to EPO drove hematocrit/hemoglobin significantly higher than EPO alone. Curated MoA (EPO/EPOR/hepcidin/erythroferrone); RCT add-on to EPO PMID 21084036; review PMID 11440282 ; testosterone↑EPO/↓hepcidin PMID 24158761

  4. The high hematocrit is a double-edged sword: thicker blood, higher clot/stroke risk Evidence-human / Established (mechanism)

    The same red-cell surge that treats anemia becomes a hazard when you're not anemic. Pushing hematocrit up thickens the blood (raises viscosity), which raises blood pressure and the risk of clots, stroke, and heart attack. This is the mechanistic basis for the cardiovascular caution with high-RBC-raising compounds — Anadrol sits in the worst tier here alongside testosterone and boldenone. Curated MoA polycythemia issue-map; consistent with PMID 11440282

  5. Builds lean mass and body-cell mass — proven in humans, but at clinical doses Evidence-human

    Through AR-driven gene expression in muscle, oxymetholone is genuinely anabolic. In placebo-controlled trials of HIV-wasting patients it produced real weight gain (about 3-3.5 kg over 16 weeks vs ~1 kg placebo) and increased body cell mass — and it worked in BOTH men and women, including eugonadal (normal-testosterone) people. So the muscle-building is real human data — but at therapeutic doses, and trial 'weight gain' lumps together lean tissue AND fluid, not the dramatic bodybuilding-dose gains forums describe. Phase III RCTs PMID 12646793, 12815555-RETRACTED; dialysis RCT PMID 19356374

  6. The 17-alpha-alkyl 'survival armor' is exactly what poisons the liver Established (clinical) / Evidence-in vitro (oxidative-stress mechanism)

    To survive being swallowed, oxymetholone carries a 17-alpha-alkyl group — a chemical modification that blocks the liver from destroying it on first pass, making it orally active. The catch: that same change makes the molecule hard for the liver to clear, so it lingers and stresses hepatocytes. The leading mechanism is oxidative stress (reactive oxygen species overwhelming the cell). Clinically this shows as cholestatic jaundice (bile backs up) and, with longer/higher exposure, peliosis hepatis (blood-filled liver cysts) and liver tumors. In the HIV RCTs, 25-43% of treated patients had liver enzymes spike >5x baseline — dose-dependent — versus almost none on placebo. This hepatotoxicity is the defining downside of oral 17-alpha-alkylated steroids and is NOT shared by non-alkylated injectables. 17-alpha-alkylation + oxidative stress hypothesis PMID 27372877; liver-toxicity rates PMID 12646793, 12815555-RETRACTED; liver tumors PMID 15495253; review PMID 11440282

  7. Androgenic and hormonal side effects ride along with the anabolic ones Established (androgenic, lipids, suppression) / Contested (exact mechanism of its estrogenic bloat & gyno)

    Because it's an AR agonist acting body-wide, oxymetholone also drives classic androgenic effects: acne, oily skin, hair loss (in the genetically prone), and in women virilization (voice deepening, clitoral enlargement, facial hair — often irreversible). It also disrupts blood lipids (notably crashing protective HDL) and, paradoxically for a non-aromatizing DHT derivative, is strongly linked to estrogen-like effects such as gynecomastia and water retention — believed to act partly through progestogenic/estrogen-receptor pathways rather than aromatization. It suppresses the body's own testosterone production via negative feedback. Review PMID 11440282; adrenal/steroidogenesis profiling PMID 31884045

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Oxymetholone (CHEMBL1200585; C21H32O3, MW 332.5; cLogP ~4.4, PSA 57.5 — very lipophilic, 0 Rule-of-5 violations) is a 2-hydroxymethylene-17alpha-methyl derivative of DHT (5alpha-androstane backbone), so it is structurally a DHT analog, NOT a testosterone analog. ESTABLISHED mechanism: it is a direct AGONIST of the human androgen receptor (AR, ChEMBL1871/CHEMBL220 NR3C4), confirmed as its sole curated mechanism of action (ChEMBL get_mechanism: action_type AGONIST, direct_interaction true, disease_efficacy true; FDA DailyMed-referenced). CAVEAT ON AFFINITY NUMBERS: ChEMBL holds NO numeric AR Ki/EC50 for oxymetholone; a DrugMatrix off-target panel of 132 assays (AChE, adrenergic, adenosine, Ca-channel, etc.) returned <50% inhibition at 10 uM at every non-AR target — i.e. it is AR-selective with no meaningful off-target receptor binding [Established / in vitro panel]. Classic radioligand work (Saartok/Liao era, widely cited in reviews) places oxymetholone's relative AR binding affinity LOW versus testosterone/DHT — its potency is driven more by oral bioavailability and metabolic resistance than by tight AR binding [Contested — exact RBA values vary by assay]. Notably it does NOT aromatize (no CYP19A1 substrate site; the 17alpha-methyl/saturated A-ring blocks it), so its marked estrogen-like clinical effects are NOT from estradiol; 17alpha-methyl-DHT analogs can even act as weak competitive aromatase inhibitors [Evidence-in vitro].

Signalling cascade. Lipophilic oxymetholone diffuses across the plasma membrane and binds the ligand-binding domain of cytosolic AR, displacing HSP90 chaperones. The ligand-AR complex undergoes a conformational change, dimerizes, and translocates to the nucleus, where it binds androgen-response elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer regions and recruits coactivators (SRC/p160, CBP/p300) to drive transcription [Established, genomic pathway as already covered]. Downstream this raises muscle protein synthesis and nitrogen retention (positive nitrogen balance), and in bone marrow/kidney it stimulates the ERYTHROPOIESIS axis: androgens raise erythropoietin (EPO) and act on EPO-receptor-bearing progenitors, and crucially SUPPRESS hepcidin (HAMP) while raising erythroferrone (ERFE), liberating iron for hemoglobin synthesis -> rising RBC mass/hematocrit (the basis of its FDA anemia indication, and of the clot risk already covered) [Established]. A second, NON-genomic-adjacent branch is hepatic: as a 17alpha-alkylated oral it concentrates in the liver during first pass, inducing hepatic lipase (LIPC) and shifting lipoproteins (HDL down, LDL up) and impairing canalicular bile transporters (BSEP/ABCB11, MRP2) -> cholestasis [Established]. Mechanistic in-vitro data also show oxymetholone perturbs adrenal steroidogenesis (apparent CYP21A2 inhibition -> reduced corticosteroid, increased DHEA in H295R cells) [Evidence-in vitro, PMID 31884045].

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral only, as 50 mg tablets (clinical Anadrol-50). It is NOT an ester/prodrug — the molecule itself is active; the 17alpha-methyl group is a permanent structural modification, not a cleavable ester, so there is no depot/slow-release behavior like injectable esterified AAS [Established].
Absorption / bioavailability
17alpha-alkylation is the whole point of the molecule: it sterically blocks rapid hepatic 17beta-hydroxyl oxidation, so a meaningful fraction survives first-pass and reaches the systemic circulation orally. Like other oral 17aa AAS (e.g., methyltestosterone), absorption from the GI tract is rapid with serum peaks roughly 1-2 h post-dose [Established for class; exact oral %F for oxymetholone not well quantified in humans — Key gap].
Half-life
Short, on the order of ~8-9 hours is the commonly cited figure (consistent with the clinical once-to-thrice-daily dosing used in the HIV-wasting trial: 50 mg BID or TID). The corpus did not return a primary human PK half-life measurement for oxymetholone specifically, so this value is Contested/approximate rather than rigorously established [Contested].
Metabolism
Hepatic. Human in-vitro (liver S9) and urinary doping-control studies identify multiple phase-I metabolites via A-ring/3-keto reduction and hydroxylation — e.g., OMT-M3 (2alpha,17alpha-methyl-5xi-androstan-3alpha,6beta,17beta-triol) and the long-known 17-epimer / hydroxylated species — followed by phase-II glucuronide/sulfate conjugation. The 17alpha-methyl group is retained (no 17-keto products form, unlike non-methylated AAS) [Evidence-human, PMIDs 40866528, 22945829].
Excretion
Renal, predominantly as glucuronide/sulfate conjugates of the hydroxylated metabolites (basis of GC-MS/MS doping detection) [Evidence-human].
Notes
Being a DHT-derived 5alpha-reduced compound, oxymetholone is NOT a 5alpha-reductase substrate and is non-aromatizable. It is highly lipophilic (cLogP ~4.4) but its CNS/androgenic-CNS effects (mood, libido) are mediated by AR, not by a special BBB mechanism. Because it bypasses ester cleavage, the 'parent = active drug' simplifies onset but means liver exposure is high on every pass — the structural basis of its hepatotoxicity.

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

HUMAN (clinical, the strongest dose-response data): In the 89-patient double-blind RCT in HIV wasting, oxymetholone 100 mg/day (50 mg BID) and 150 mg/day (50 mg TID) BOTH produced significant lean/total mass gain over 16 weeks — total weight +3.5 +/- 0.7 kg (BID) and +3.0 +/- 0.5 kg (TID) vs +1.0 +/- 0.7 kg placebo; body cell mass +3.8 kg (BID, +12.4% of baseline) vs +2.1 kg (TID). Strikingly the 100 mg/day dose matched the 150 mg/day dose for efficacy but caused LESS hepatotoxicity (>5x ALT/AST/GGT rise in 25-27% at BID vs 35-43% at TID) — a clear toxicity dose-response with an efficacy plateau [Evidence-human, PMID 12646793 / 12815555-RETRACTED]. In aplastic anemia, an initial 150 mg/day (most patients) gave a 56% hematologic response with median 11.8 weeks to transfusion independence; androgenic side effects appeared mostly within the first month [Evidence-human cohort, PMID 40515826]. RODENT: NTP 2-year gavage in F344 rats used 3, 30, 100-150 mg/kg/day and showed a clear DOSE-RELATED erythrocytosis (rising RBC, Hb, hematocrit) and dose-related hypocholesterolemia, plus dose-dependent organ toxicity and neoplasia at the top doses [Evidence-rodent, PMID 12571678]. EXPLICIT GAP: there is essentially NO controlled human dose-response data at the supraphysiologic bodybuilding doses (commonly far above 150 mg/day) — efficacy and especially safety in that range are extrapolated/anecdotal, not trial-derived [Key unknown].

How it compares

  • vs Methandrostenolone (Dianabol)
    same: Both are oral 17alpha-alkylated AAS, both potent mass/strength builders with rapid water-weight gain, both hepatotoxic and both crash HDL.
    different: Dianabol is testosterone-derived and DOES aromatize to methylestradiol (estrogenic effects are true estradiol-driven, AI-responsive); Anadrol is DHT-derived and does NOT aromatize, so its bloat/gyno are not estradiol-driven and respond poorly to aromatase inhibitors. Anadrol is generally regarded as harsher on the liver and produces larger water/strength swings.
  • vs Oxandrolone (Anavar)
    same: Both are oral 17alpha-alkylated DHT-derived AAS; same AR-agonist core mechanism; both were FDA-approved (oxandrolone for weight gain/HIV wasting/burns).
    different: Oxandrolone is far milder — much less hepatotoxic, far less water retention and a cleaner side-effect profile at clinical doses, but also far weaker for mass. Anadrol gives dramatically more weight/strength but with substantially higher liver and lipid toxicity. (Oxandrolone's FDA approval was later withdrawn.)
  • vs Testosterone (e.g., enanthate, injectable)
    same: Both are AR agonists that raise lean mass, suppress the HPG axis, and stimulate erythropoiesis/hematocrit via EPO+hepcidin suppression.
    different: Testosterone is injectable, NOT 17alpha-alkylated, so it is minimally hepatotoxic and aromatizes to estradiol (AI-manageable gyno). Oxymetholone is oral, heavily hepatotoxic, non-aromatizing, crashes HDL much harder per the first-pass effect, and has a much shorter duration of action.
  • vs Nandrolone (Deca-Durabolin)
    same: Both build lean mass via AR agonism and both produce gyno that aromatase inhibitors do not fully fix.
    different: Nandrolone is an injectable 19-nortestosterone whose gyno is progestin/prolactin-mediated (PGR agonism, raised prolactin — needs a SERM or dopamine agonist), and it is only mildly hepatotoxic. Oxymetholone's estrogen-like effects come from a different (non-aromatizing, ER/progestogenic-candidate) route, and it is strongly hepatotoxic as an oral.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Anastrozole compounds risk ⚠ Contested
    A key teaching contrast. An aromatase inhibitor lowers estradiol by blocking testosterone->estrogen conversion, which counteracts the gyno/bloat of AROMATIZING androgens. But Anadrol does NOT aromatize (saturated A-ring, 17-alpha-methyl DHT derivative), so its estrogen-like gyno and water retention are NOT estradiol-driven - they are attributed to intrinsic ER/progestogenic-like activity plus mineralocorticoid sodium retention. An AI therefore has no estradiol substrate to act on for Anadrol's bloat and will not fix it. Worse, 17-alpha-methyl-DHT analogs can themselves act as weak competitive aromatase inhibitors in vitro, so ADDING an AI on top can compound estradiol suppression - over-crashing whatever estradiol comes from any co-run aromatizing compounds, causing joint/lipid/mood problems from low E2. Anadrol monograph pharmacology (does NOT aromatize; 17a-methyl-DHT analogs act as weak aromatase inhibitors [Evidence-in vitro]) + side_effect_mechanisms (estrogenic effects NOT estradiol-driven, Contested mechanism); anastrozole monograph (blocks aromatase, lowers estradiol) Watch: Estradiol (sensitive assay) - watch for symptoms of crashed E2 (joint pain/dryness, low libido, low mood, worse lipids) rather than expecting the AI to fix Anadrol's water/gyno. Lipid panel, since low E2 further worsens HDL.
  • Andarine compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Anadrol is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral anabolic steroid that is directly hepatotoxic (cholestatic/hepatocellular injury), and andarine carries a SARM-class drug-induced-liver-injury signal (its hepatic phase I/II metabolism, including nitroreduction, can generate reactive intermediates). Running two orally-processed, liver-stressing AR agonists together stacks injury risk on the same organ. andarine-s-4.json (side_effect_mechanisms: hepatotoxicity, SARM-class case reports PMID 35340496); anadrol-oxymetholone.json (17aa oral, hepatotoxic); PMID 41245032 (SARM DILI) Watch: Baseline and follow-up ALT, AST, GGT, alkaline phosphatase and total/direct bilirubin; watch for jaundice, dark urine, pale stool, right-upper-quadrant pain or unexplained fatigue and stop if they appear.
  • Boldenone undecylenate compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both are AR agonists that strongly stimulate red-cell production; the curated erythropoiesis MoA card names boldenone AND oxymetholone specifically among the most potent hematocrit-raising androgens. Combining two of the strongest erythropoietic AAS compounds their effects on red-cell mass add, pushing hematocrit and blood viscosity higher. They differ in that Anadrol is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral carrying a hepatotoxicity route that non-alkylated injectable boldenone lacks. Corpus MoA card 'Erythropoiesis & iron' / 'ISSUE: Polycythemia' both name boldenone + oxymetholone; Anadrol monograph (17aa hepatotoxicity, step 6); EQ monograph step 4 Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin (this pairing is among the most likely to trigger erythrocytosis); blood pressure; signs of hyperviscosity (headache, flushing, visual change). Because Anadrol adds 17-alpha-alkyl hepatotoxicity that boldenone does not carry, also track ALT/AST/GGT/bilirubin.
  • Cardarine compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both carry a hepatotoxicity signal that would summate via different routes. Anadrol is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral androgen and one of the more hepatotoxic AAS: 17aa substitution resists hepatic clearance and prolongs hepatocyte exposure, it impairs the canalicular bile transporters BSEP/MRP2 (cholestasis), and it drives oxidative/reactive-oxygen hepatocyte injury (with peliosis/adenoma on prolonged use) — an ESTABLISHED human hepatotoxin. Cardarine has a documented human hepatocellular-injury signal (marked transaminase rise in the abuse literature), plausibly via PPARδ-driven shifts in hepatic substrate handling plus extensive oxidative hepatic metabolism of a highly lipophilic molecule. Two agents that each stress the hepatocyte compound the overall liver burden. Anadrol 17aa hepatotoxicity via BSEP/MRP2 impairment + oxidative injury (Established human): anadrol monograph side_effect_mechanisms. Cardarine human hepatotoxicity signal: monograph side_effect_mechanisms (Kintz 2021 PMID 34678947, confounded by SARM co-use). Watch: ALT, AST, GGT, bilirubin at baseline and periodically; jaundice, pruritus, dark urine, RUQ pain warrant stopping and medical review. Anadrol also strongly raises hematocrit — a separate liability to track — but the SHARED mechanistic edge with Cardarine is hepatotoxicity.
  • DHB compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Two hazards stack. (1) Erythrocytosis: oxymetholone is one of the most potent hematocrit-raising androgens (its FDA anemia use), boosting EPO and suppressing hepcidin, and DHB independently drives the same axis, so combined red-cell mass rises faster than either alone. (2) Hepatic burden: Anadrol is 17-alpha-alkylated and overtly hepatotoxic (cholestasis, enzyme rise, peliosis, tumors), and DHB's ORAL form (M1T) is also 17-alpha-methylated with strong hepatic activity; injectable non-methylated DHB raised liver WEIGHT in rats (an organ-mass signal, not proven cholestatic injury). If DHB is taken as oral M1T alongside Anadrol, the two 17aa orals compound cholestatic liver-injury risk (impaired BSEP/ABCB11 bile transport); injectable DHB carries mainly the hematocrit half of this interaction. Erythropoiesis MoA card + Anadrol monograph (oxymetholone EPO up/hepcidin down); 17aa hepatotoxicity PMID 27372877, PMID 29521706; DHB/M1T hepatic activity Friedel 2006 (PMID 16621347), Parr 2011 (PMID 21990312) Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin; ALT/AST/GGT/ALP and bilirubin; jaundice, dark urine, right-upper-quadrant pain. Injectable DHB carries mainly the hematocrit risk; the additive hepatotoxicity applies specifically to the oral M1T form.
  • Dianabol compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both are oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS. The 17a-alkyl 'armor' that lets each survive first-pass metabolism is the same feature that makes them hepatotoxic (impaired canalicular bile transport via BSEP/ABCB11 and MRP2 -> cholestasis; at the severe end peliosis/tumors) and that shifts hepatic lipid handling to crash HDL and raise LDL. Running two 17aa orals stacks the identical liver-load and lipid-toxicity mechanism, so cholestatic and atherogenic injury is additive, not merely coexisting. Anadrol additionally drives red-cell mass hard (raises EPO, suppresses hepcidin to free iron), adding an erythrocytosis/viscosity burden Dbol contributes to less.   • Ephedrine compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Anadrol strongly raises red blood cell mass and hematocrit (boosting EPO and suppressing hepcidin), which increases blood viscosity, blood pressure, and clot/stroke/MI risk. Ephedrine layers on alpha-1 vasoconstriction and a pressor/tachycardic drive from beta-1 stimulation. Driving high-viscosity, erythrocytotic blood at ephedrine-elevated pressures and heart rate compounds the thrombotic and ischemic hazard beyond either agent alone; this is the same androgen-plus-sympathomimetic cardiovascular stacking seen in the ephedra + anabolic-steroid cardiomyopathy literature. PMID 11440282; PMID 15899737 Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin (viscosity), blood pressure, and heart rate; red-flag symptoms of clot or ischemia (sudden weakness/numbness, speech difficulty, chest pain, calf swelling) warrant urgent care.
  • Halotestin compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both are oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS whose alkyl group resists first-pass hepatic clearance and concentrates the drug in hepatocytes, where it drives cholestasis (impaired canalicular bile-acid transport), transaminitis, and at the severe end peliosis hepatis and hepatic adenoma/carcinoma. Running two 17aa orals together loads the same hepatocellular/cholestatic insult onto one liver rather than spreading it across non-alkylated injectables, so the hepatotoxic burden is additive. Fluoxymesterone is regarded as one of the more hepatotoxic orals; oxymetholone is dose-dependently hepatotoxic (25-43% of subjects >5x baseline enzymes in RCTs). Rising transaminases or bilirubin is a signal to stop and seek medical evaluation.
  • Insulin compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Anadrol improved insulin sensitivity in hemodialysis patients (HOMA-IR fell alongside lean-mass gain), i.e. it tends to LOWER the glucose-lowering work needed from a given insulin amount. Exogenous insulin already drives glucose down and carries acute hypoglycemia risk. Because the androgen's metabolic effect pushes glucose in the SAME downward direction, the two effects ADD - a compounding hypoglycemia hazard where a fixed insulin amount can drop glucose lower than expected. Anadrol monograph key_effects + evidence (HOMA-IR fell / improved insulin sensitivity in dialysis RCT, PMID 19356374); insulin monograph key_effects (glucose disposal via GLUT4 -> acute hypoglycemia risk) Watch: Blood glucose (fasting and symptomatic checks) for hypoglycemia - sweating, tremor, confusion. HbA1c/HOMA-IR to track shifting insulin sensitivity.
  • MK-677 compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    MK-677 raises GH/IGF-1 and causes fluid retention/edema plus insulin resistance and elevated glucose. Anadrol independently causes marked water retention ('bloat') and raises blood pressure. Run together, the two water-retention mechanisms compound - additive edema and BP elevation - while their glucose effects diverge (MK-677 worsens insulin resistance, Anadrol tends to improve sensitivity), making net glucose harder to predict. Anadrol monograph mechanism_step 7 + side_effect_mechanisms (marked water retention, hypertension); MK-677 monograph key_effects (GH/IGF-1 up, insulin resistance/raised glucose, water retention) Watch: Blood pressure and signs of fluid overload (edema, weight spikes, breathlessness); fasting glucose/HbA1c given MK-677's insulin-resistance effect.
  • Masteron compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both are AR agonists that stimulate erythropoiesis and raise red-cell mass. Anadrol is notorious for strongly raising hematocrit (it is FDA-approved for anemia, boosting EPO and suppressing hepcidin); Masteron raises red-cell mass as a class androgen effect. Run together the erythrocytosis is additive, pushing hematocrit/hemoglobin higher and increasing blood viscosity and clot/stroke risk. Both also suppress HDL, so the atherogenic lipid hit compounds as well. (Distinct hepatic routes: Anadrol is 17a-alkylated and markedly hepatotoxic; Masteron is injectable and much less so, so hepatotoxicity does not simply add.) Masteron monograph monitoring (hematocrit) and side_effect_mechanisms (lipids); Anadrol monograph (strongly raises hematocrit, suppressed HDL, hepatotoxicity) Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin and full blood count (polycythemia raises thrombotic risk); blood pressure; HDL/LDL lipid panel for the combined dyslipidemia; liver enzymes (mainly for Anadrol's 17a-alkyl hepatotoxicity).
  • Methyltestosterone compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Two mechanisms compound. (1) Both are 17aa oral AAS, so hepatotoxicity (cholestasis, peliosis) is additive on the liver. (2) Both are AR agonists that stimulate erythropoiesis; Anadrol is a notably strong hematocrit-raiser, so co-use pushes red-cell mass and blood viscosity higher than methyltestosterone alone, compounding thrombosis risk.   • Nandrolone compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Two overlapping harms compound here. First erythrocytosis: both AR agonists drive red-cell mass (EPO up, hepcidin down), and oxymetholone is one of the strongest hematocrit-raising steroids — it is FDA-approved for anemia — so the combination sharply compounds polycythemia (viscosity/thrombosis risk). Second, they DIVERGE on hepatotoxicity: oxymetholone is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral that stresses the liver (cholestasis, enzyme spikes) and crashes HDL, whereas nandrolone is NOT 17aa; so on liver/lipids Anadrol dominates and nandrolone adds little there, but the shared erythrocytosis is genuinely additive.   • Oxandrolone compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both are oral 17-alpha-alkylated DHT-derived AAS that lower HDL and both carry hepatotoxic potential (oxandrolone comparatively mild). Combined, they add first-pass hepatic burden and compound the HDL-crash/atherogenic lipid shift - the same class mechanism as with harsher orals, just with a milder partner. The additive lipid and liver signal still applies. Blood pressure as a CV-risk driver.
  • Stanozolol compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Stanozolol is another oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS and has caused fatal sub-massive hepatic necrosis/liver failure on its own. Paired with Anadrol, two 17aa orals compound cholestatic and oxidative liver injury through the same shared mechanism (impaired bile transport via BSEP/MRP2 + ROS). Both also suppress HDL, so the atherogenic lipid crash is additive.   • Superdrol compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both are oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS. The 17aa 'armor' that makes them orally active is exactly what makes them hepatotoxic - they resist first-pass clearance, linger in hepatocytes, impair canalicular bile transporters (BSEP/ABCB11, MRP2) and drive oxidative stress. Taking two 17aa orals together stacks first-pass hepatic load, and reviews note that using multiple orals simultaneously increases the frequency and severity of liver injury (raised ALT/AST/GGT, cholestasis, and at the severe end peliosis/tumors). Both also crash HDL, so atherogenic lipid toxicity compounds too.   • Testosterone compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both are androgen-receptor agonists that drive erythropoiesis by the same pathway - raising EPO and suppressing hepcidin to free iron for the marrow. Anadrol is one of the strongest hematocrit-raisers (its FDA anemia indication) and testosterone raises hematocrit too, so together their erythropoietic effects ADD, pushing hematocrit/hemoglobin higher than either alone. High hematocrit thickens blood (viscosity), and secondary polycythemia on testosterone is tied to more major adverse cardiac events and venous clots.   • BPC-157 counteracts ↓ Evidence-rodent
    Opposing actions on the liver. Anadrol is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral androgen that is markedly hepatotoxic (cholestasis, transaminase elevation, peliosis risk). BPC-157 is a broad cytoprotectant that, in numerous rodent models, counteracts drug-induced LIVER and gut injury (it protects hepatocytes and gut lining against NSAIDs, alcohol, and even paracetamol overdose). So BPC-157 could in principle blunt (counteract) the hepatocellular stress a 17aa oral imposes. IMPORTANT: this is a rodent cytoprotection story against DIFFERENT toxins, not proof it neutralizes anabolic-steroid cholestasis in humans, and it must never be read as license to run a hepatotoxic oral. Track liver function (ALT, AST, GGT, bilirubin) at baseline and periodically, and stop the androgen for rising enzymes or jaundice/dark urine regardless of peptide use. Anadrol also raises hematocrit, monitor CBC/hematocrit independently.

History &amp; legal status

"Developed by Syntex in 1959-1960 and marketed as Anadrol-50 / Anapolon (and older trade names Adroyd, Anasterone). FDA-approved indication is anemia caused by deficient red-cell production — acquired or congenital aplastic anemia, myelofibrosis, hypoplastic anemias from myelotoxic drugs — exploiting its erythropoietic effect; historically also used for hereditary angioedema, HIV/AIDS wasting, breast carcinoma palliation and to promote positive nitrogen balance. With recombinant EPO and modern immunosuppressive/transplant therapy, clinical use in wealthy countries declined, but it remains a low-cost option for aplastic anemia where transplant/ATG is inaccessible (e.g., the 2025 Thailand cohort). It is a DEA Schedule III controlled substance in the US under the Anabolic Steroid Control Act (1990, expanded 2004) and is WADA-prohibited at all times in sport (anabolic agent, S1). It was NTP-nominated specifically because of extensive illicit athletic use and suspected carcinogenicity. It is still manufactured as a prescription drug, so much bodybuilding-market Anadrol is diverted pharmaceutical or underground-lab product."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Quantitative human AR binding affinity (Ki/EC50) is not well established — ChEMBL has no numeric AR affinity for oxymetholone, and classic relative-binding-affinity values are assay-dependent and contested. Its potency seems driven by oral bioavailability more than by tight AR binding.
  • Human pharmacokinetics are poorly published — there is no rigorous primary human half-life or oral-bioavailability measurement in the corpus; the commonly cited ~8-9 h half-life is approximate.
  • The mechanism of its pronounced estrogenic-type effects (gynecomastia, severe water retention) in a NON-aromatizing molecule is genuinely unresolved — direct estrogen-receptor activity vs progestogenic-like activity vs mineralocorticoid effects are competing, untested hypotheses.
  • The molecular basis of 17alpha-alkyl hepatotoxicity remains 'poorly understood' (oxidative-stress hypothesis is unproven), so why Anadrol is harsher than other orals is not mechanistically settled.
  • Whether the clear rodent hepatocarcinogenicity translates to meaningful human hepatic-tumor risk at real-world doses is unknown — human evidence is case-report level only.
  • There is essentially no controlled human dose-response or long-term safety data at the supraphysiologic doses used in bodybuilding (well above the 150 mg/day clinical ceiling); all such use is extrapolated/anecdotal.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (NOT a dosing guide): LIVER — baseline and periodic ALT/AST/GGT, bilirubin and ALP; watch for jaundice, dark urine, right-upper-quadrant pain (signals of cholestasis/peliosis); imaging if hepatic masses suspected. In the HIV RCT, 25-43% of users had >5x transaminase rises, so liver markers are the single most important panel. HEMATOCRIT/HEMOGLOBIN — hematocrit and hemoglobin for erythrocytosis (viscosity/clot risk); flag rising values, headaches, hypertension. LIPIDS — full lipid panel (expect crashed HDL, rising LDL/ApoB) as a cardiovascular-risk driver. BLOOD PRESSURE — for fluid-retention-driven and viscosity-driven hypertension. CARDIAC — consider echocardiography/symptom review for breathlessness or reduced exercise tolerance (cardiomyopathy/LV-thrombus signals). ENDOCRINE — LH/FSH and total/free testosterone for HPG suppression; in men, testicular atrophy and fertility; in women, virilization (voice changes, hirsutism, clitoromegaly — some irreversible). GLUCOSE/metabolic given adrenal-steroidogenesis and metabolic signals. Most lab abnormalities improve after cessation, which is the key mitigation; persistent jaundice, a hepatic mass, an LV thrombus or any virilization in women warrant urgent medical review."

KEY GAPS for the teaching team: (1) Human RCT data exist (rare for an oral!) but only at CLINICAL doses (e.g., 50 mg bid/tid) in sick populations (HIV-wasting, dialysis, aplastic anemia) — NOT healthy lifters at the high doses forums use; do not extrapolate dose or magnitude. (2) The famous water-weight 'bloat,' gyno, and HDL crash are real and clinically reported but their exact mechanism (progestogenic/estrogenic action of a non-aromatizing DHT derivative) is Contested/Speculative — flag it honestly, don't hand-wave. (3) The oxidative-stress mechanism of 17a-alkyl hepatotoxicity is a published HYPOTHESIS (Evidence-in vitro), while the hepatotoxicity itself is Established human fact. (4) Liver-tumor risk magnitude cannot be quantified — the number of exposed users is unknown [PMID 15495253]. (5) Harm-reduction framing throughout; no dosing, no protocols.
