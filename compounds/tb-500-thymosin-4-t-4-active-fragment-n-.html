TB-500

A cell-skeleton "traffic controller" that frees up actin so repair cells can crawl to an injury, build new blood vessels, and dial down inflammation — proven biology, but almost no human muscle/tendon trial data.

Rodent / preclinical 23 citations
Class
Synthetic actin-sequestering peptide / β-thymosin fragment (regenerative, pro-migratory, anti-inflammatory, pro-angiogenic peptide)
Primary target
Monomeric globular actin (G-actin) — bound 1:1 via the LKKTETQ (residues 17–23) active site
Human status
Human data exists for the FULL thymosin β4 molecule (drug name RGN-259), NOT for the TB-500 fragment specifically, and only in topical ophthalmic use. Two Phase 2 randomized, placebo-controlled dry-eye trials (PMID 25826322; PMID 26056426) showed significant improvements in corneal staining and discomfort with a clean safety profile; Tβ4 also entered Phase 1/2 cardiovascular and dermal-wound trials (referenced in PMID 17495252, 22074294). CRITICAL HONESTY POINTS for the audience: (1) Essentially ALL of the muscle/tendon/ligament/'recovery' reputation rests on rodent and in-vitro studies — there are no published controlled human trials of injectable TB-500 for musculoskeletal injury (a 2026 scoping review confirms the evidence base is overwhelmingly preclinical). (2) The injectable 'TB-500' sold for physique/recovery use is the LKKTETQ fragment, which is largely characterized in equine doping-detection papers (PMID 23084823, 23318763), not human efficacy trials. (3) It is a WADA-banned substance in sport. So: target and direction of effect are well-established biology; magnitude and benefit in healthy humans for athletic recovery are unproven.

At a glance

  • Accelerated cell migration and wound re-epithelialization (skin, cornea) — Established mechanistically, Evidence-rodent for whole-tissue healing
  • Reduced inflammation (NF-κB suppression, lower IL-1β/chemokines) — Evidence-rodent/in vitro
  • Increased angiogenesis / new blood-vessel formation — Evidence-rodent
  • Reduced fibrosis/scarring via fewer myofibroblasts and TGF-β modulation — Evidence-rodent
  • Stem/progenitor cell mobilization and survival (anti-apoptotic) — Evidence-rodent
  • Systemic action from any injection site (peptide circulates) — plausible from pharmacokinetics; human efficacy at distant tissues unproven

Mechanism, step by step

  1. The active piece is one short sequence: LKKTETQ Established

    TB-500 is not the whole 43-amino-acid thymosin β4 protein — it's a synthetic copy of the tiny LKKTETQ stretch (positions 17–23), the exact part of Tβ4 that grabs actin. Commercial TB-500 is this fragment with an acetyl cap on one end. So everything TB-500 does has to flow through this one actin-binding handle. PMID 23084823; PMID 17468232

  2. It mops up loose actin monomers (G-actin sequestration) Established

    Thymosin β4 is the cell's single biggest sponge for free, unassembled actin building blocks (G-actin), binding them one-to-one. Think of it as holding a reserve pile of LEGO bricks so the cell isn't constantly gluing them into rigid walls. This buffered pool is what lets a cell rapidly rebuild its skeleton on demand. PMID 17468232; PMID 11579089

  3. On demand, it hands actin off so the cell can move Established

    When a cell needs to crawl, a partner protein (profilin) pries actin away from Tβ4 — they can even all hold the same actin at once in a 'ternary complex' — releasing bricks to build the pushing edge (lamellipodium) at the front of the cell. Experiments that flash-released Tβ4 inside live cells literally made them turn and change shape, proving it steers migration. PMID 11579089; PMID 11381088; PMID 9950774

  4. Faster migration = repair cells reach the wound Established

    Because it speeds cell crawling, Tβ4 helps the cells that actually do repair — skin keratinocytes, endothelial (blood-vessel) cells, and stem/progenitor cells — migrate into damaged tissue and lay down new structure. This is the core 'tissue repair' claim, and it follows directly from the actin mechanism above. PMID 22074294; PMID 11950239

  5. It calms inflammation by quieting NF-κB Evidence-rodent

    Beyond actin, Tβ4 suppresses the master inflammatory switch NF-κB and lowers pro-inflammatory signals (IL-1β and the MIP/MCP chemokines that recruit immune cells). In injured tissue this means less collateral damage from the immune response — though this is shown in cell and rodent injury models, not human muscle. PMID 11950239; PMID 17495249; PMID 30500399

  6. It promotes new blood vessels (angiogenesis) Evidence-rodent

    Tβ4 pushes endothelial cells to form new vessels and, in the heart, acts as a paracrine signal that wakes up dormant epicardium-derived cells to build coronary vasculature. In diabetic-nerve models it improved blood flow via the Angiopoietin-1/Tie2 pathway. New blood supply is what keeps regenerating tissue alive — but again, this is animal/cell data. PMID 17495252; PMID 30500399

  7. It reduces scarring (anti-fibrotic) Evidence-rodent

    Tβ4 lowers the number of myofibroblasts (the cells that dump collagen and form stiff scar), partly by tempering TGF-β signaling, so tissue can remodel into functional architecture instead of disorganized scar. Demonstrated in cardiac, dermal and other animal injury models. PMID 22074294; PMID 20536453

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. TB-500 is N-acetylated LKKTETQ — the actin-binding active site (residues 17–23) of the 43-aa parent protein thymosin β4 (Tβ4). The PRIMARY molecular target is monomeric G-actin, bound 1:1 [Established; PMID 23084823, 11311852]. The full Tβ4 peptide binds G-actin with MODEST affinity: dissociation constant (Kd) in the LOW MICROMOLAR range (~0.7–2 µM; commonly cited ~1–2 µM), deliberately weak so binding/release is fast and the cell can rapidly mobilise monomers [Established for full Tβ4; PMID 17468232 ('dissociation constant in the micromolar range allows for fast binding and release')]. NOTE a critical caveat for the YouTube audience: nearly all affinity/structure data are for the FULL 43-aa Tβ4, not the 7-residue TB-500 fragment. Structurally Tβ4 is an intrinsically DISORDERED peptide that folds into an extended conformation only upon binding actin — its N-terminal half (containing LKKTET) caps the actin barbed end and its C-terminal helix caps the pointed-end region, together blocking polymerisation [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 15329672 / PMC517612, Irobi 2004]. The LKKTETQ fragment alone retains actin-binding/sequestering character but binds more weakly and lacks the C-terminal contacts; its in-vivo potency relative to full Tβ4 in humans is NOT established [Contested]. Tβ4 acts as a SEQUESTRANT/buffer, NOT a classical receptor agonist or antagonist — it has no known G-protein-coupled or enzymatic receptor; downstream 'signalling' is largely indirect (released or surface-associated Tβ4) rather than via a defined high-affinity receptor [Established intracellular role; extracellular receptor Speculative].

Signalling cascade. Mechanism operates on two levels. (1) CYTOSKELETAL/intracellular: by buffering the G-actin monomer pool, Tβ4 keeps actin polymerisation-competent but restrained; when a migrating cell needs filaments, profilin (higher affinity) displaces Tβ4 from G-actin, delivering ATP-actin monomers to filament barbed ends to drive lamellipodial extension and directed cell migration toward injury [Established; PMID 11311852, 15329672]. This accelerates migration of keratinocytes, endothelial cells and progenitor cells into wounds. (2) EXTRACELLULAR/secreted Tβ4 (released from platelets, cross-linked to fibrin/collagen by factor XIIIa/transglutaminase, raising local concentration at clot/wound sites) drives a paracrine repair program [Evidence-human/in vitro; PMID 11978733]. Reported downstream effects — all from the PARENT molecule in animal/cell models, NOT proven for the TB-500 fragment in humans — include: pro-angiogenic upregulation of VEGF and endothelial tube formation; anti-inflammatory suppression of NF-κB (in diabetic neuropathy, via a miR-146a → IRAK1/TRAF6 → NF-κB axis) [Evidence-rodent; PMID 30500399]; modulation of Rho-kinase ROCK1 in cardiac remodeling [Evidence-rodent, MoA-corpus]; activation of Angiopoietin-1/Tie2 in neurovascular remodeling [Evidence-rodent]; and a recently described ErbB2/Raf1 → anti-apoptotic (↓Bad) cardiomyocyte-survival pathway whose blockade abolishes cardioprotection [Evidence-rodent + small human signal; PMID 41229390]. A SEPARATE pathway: Tβ4 is the precursor of the antifibrotic tetrapeptide Ac-SDKP (acetyl-Ser-Asp-Lys-Pro), liberated from the Tβ4 N-terminus by prolyl oligopeptidase and degraded by ACE — Ac-SDKP reduces cardiac/renal fibrosis and inflammation in hypertensive rodents [Evidence-rodent; PMID 15037553]. Whether the short N-acetylated LKKTETQ fragment generates Ac-SDKP is doubtful since it lacks the N-terminal SDKP sequence [Speculative].

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Injectable peptide. Research/grey-market TB-500 (N-acetylated LKKTETQ) is used subcutaneously or intramuscularly; the FULL synthetic Tβ4 used in human trials was given INTRAVENOUS (cardiac) or topical eye-drops (ophthalmic). No oral form — peptide would be digested [Established].
Absorption / bioavailability
Quantitative human SC/IM bioavailability of the TB-500 fragment is NOT published [unknown]. As a small, highly water-soluble, charged peptide it is expected to absorb reasonably from SC depots but undergo rapid proteolysis. The marketing claim of 'systemic action from any injection site' is plausible for a soluble peptide but lacks formal human bioavailability data [Contested].
Half-life
For FULL synthetic Tβ4 given IV to healthy humans (42–1260 mg), the plasma half-life was SHORT and notably DOSE-DEPENDENT — half-life INCREASED with increasing dose (saturable distribution/clearance), with single-dose PK roughly dose-proportional [Evidence-human; PMID 20536472]. Order-of-magnitude human elimination half-life is on the scale of hours, not days. For the 7-residue TB-500 fragment specifically, no validated human half-life exists; small linear peptides like LKKTETQ are typically cleared within minutes-to-low-hours [Evidence-in vitro/animal].
Metabolism
Proteolytic. In equine in-vitro and post-administration studies, N-acetylated LKKTETQ is degraded by exopeptidases to shorter fragments (e.g. N-acetylated LK detected as a urinary/plasma metabolite) [Evidence-animal; PMID 23084823, 23318763]. The parent Tβ4 is processed by prolyl oligopeptidase (liberating Ac-SDKP) and ACE (degrading Ac-SDKP) [Evidence-rodent; PMID 15037553]. No cytochrome-P450 involvement — clearance is peptidase-mediated.
Excretion
Renal — intact peptide and small metabolites appear in urine; this is the basis of anti-doping detection (LC-MS detected N-acetylated LKKTETQ down to ~0.01 ng/mL in equine urine) [Evidence-animal; PMID 23084823].
Notes
TB-500 is N-terminally ACETYLATED, which slows aminopeptidase degradation versus the free peptide (a stability modification, not a prodrug). It is NOT an ester/depot prodrug. Blood–brain-barrier penetration of the fragment is not established; CNS effects in rodent models used systemic full Tβ4 [Evidence-rodent]. Tβ4 can be covalently cross-linked to fibrin/collagen by factor XIIIa, creating a tissue reservoir that prolongs LOCAL residence beyond plasma half-life [Evidence-human; PMID 11978733].

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

Human dose–response for the TB-500 fragment is effectively UNKNOWN — there are NO published human dose-ranging trials of N-acetylated LKKTETQ for any indication; bodybuilding 'loading then maintenance' protocols (commonly cited 2–2.5 mg twice weekly) come from grey-market sources, not trials [Speculative/anecdote]. What IS quantified is for the FULL parent Tβ4: (HUMAN, IV) Ruff 2010 gave healthy volunteers single and 14-day daily doses of 42, 140, 420 and 1260 mg with a dose-proportional PK response and no dose-limiting toxicity across this ~30-fold range — i.e. a wide tolerability window but NO efficacy endpoint, so no human efficacy dose-response [Evidence-human; PMID 20536472]. (HUMAN, topical) RGN-259 Tβ4 0.1% eye-drops 6×/day for 28 days produced ~59% reduction in corneal fluorescein staining and ~35% symptom reduction vs vehicle in severe dry eye (n=9) [Evidence-human; PMID 25826322]. (HUMAN, cardiac) rhTβ4 within 8 h post-PCI reduced infarct area in the early-dosed STEMI subgroup (n=43) but NOT in the overall 96-patient cohort — a dose/timing-sensitive, non-significant overall signal [Evidence-human; PMID 41229390]. (RODENT) cardioprotection and antifibrotic effects are seen with systemic Tβ4 over multi-day courses (e.g. 7-day rhTβ4 prevented dysfunction/fibrosis 28 days post-I/R; 8-week dosing improved diabetic neuropathy) [Evidence-rodent; PMID 41229390, 30500399]. Bottom line for the audience: efficacy magnitudes are extrapolated from the parent molecule and animals; the fragment's human dose-response is a genuine data void.

How it compares

  • vs Full-length Thymosin β4 (Tβ4 / RGN-259 / RGN-352, the parent drug)
    same: Identical core actin-binding LKKTET motif and the same wound-healing, angiogenic, anti-inflammatory and cardioprotective biology; TB-500 is literally the active fragment of this molecule
    different: Tβ4 is the 43-aa intact protein with ALL the actual human trial data (Phase I IV safety, Phase 2 ophthalmic, STEMI RCT) and the full structural actin-capping interaction; TB-500 is a 7-residue acetylated fragment with NO dedicated human trials, weaker/uncharacterised affinity, and cannot generate the antifibrotic Ac-SDKP tetrapeptide. Much 'TB-500 evidence' is really Tβ4 evidence borrowed by extrapolation
  • vs BPC-157 (pentadecapeptide 'body-protection compound')
    same: Both are grey-market injectable 'healing/recovery' research peptides marketed for tendon/ligament/soft-tissue and gut repair, both pro-angiogenic, both lacking robust human efficacy trials and unapproved for systemic human use
    different: Different mechanism — BPC-157 acts largely through VEGFR2/nitric-oxide and growth-factor pathways and is NOT an actin-sequestering peptide; the two are often stacked but share no molecular target
  • vs Ac-SDKP (acetyl-Ser-Asp-Lys-Pro, the Tβ4 N-terminal cleavage product)
    same: Endogenously derived from the SAME parent protein (Tβ4) and shares the anti-inflammatory/tissue-protective theme
    different: Ac-SDKP's signature action is ANTIFIBROTIC (reduces cardiac/renal fibrosis), is generated by prolyl oligopeptidase and degraded by ACE, and is a different sequence entirely from LKKTETQ — TB-500 does not contain or produce it [PMID 15037553]
  • vs Profilin (endogenous actin-monomer-binding protein)
    same: Both bind G-actin monomers and participate in the same actin-buffering system
    different: Profilin has HIGHER affinity and PROMOTES barbed-end assembly (it hands actin to growing filaments), whereas Tβ4/TB-500 SEQUESTERS and restrains polymerisation; profilin out-competes and displaces Tβ4 from actin to release monomers — they are functional opposites within one regulatory loop [PMID 11311852]

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • GHK-Cu potentiates ↑ Evidence-rodent
    No shared molecular target: TB-500 (thymosin beta-4 fragment) sequesters monomeric G-actin to accelerate cell migration and up-regulates VEGF/angiogenesis and anti-fibrotic (TGF-beta) signaling; GHK-Cu supplies a separate copper-dependent collagen/GAG synthesis and MMP/TIMP-balanced remodeling program plus its own SPARC-matrikine chemotactic/angiogenic activity. The overlap is at the ENDPOINT (angiogenesis + tissue repair), where GHK-Cu adds the copper/matrix layer TB-500 lacks, so the effect is convergent/additive rather than receptor-shared. Human synergy is unproven; the pairing is community practice with animal-level mechanistic plausibility only. PMID 18644225 (GHK-Cu angiogenesis/matrix); PMID 7340824 (GHK copper binding/transport). TB-500 actin-sequestering/pro-angiogenic mechanism per its monograph Watch: Injection-site infection/sterile-abscess signs from non-GMP product. As with any dual pro-angiogenic stack, note any unexplained growing mass and stop; there is no validated human safety data for the combination.
  • BPC-157 redundant Evidence-rodent
    Both are grey-market repair peptides that converge on the SAME downstream outcomes by DIFFERENT molecular routes: BPC-157 drives angiogenesis via VEGFR2/Akt/eNOS-NO and primes fibroblasts via GHR up-regulation, while TB-500 sequesters G-actin to speed repair-cell migration, up-regulates the Ang1/Tie2 + VEGF angiogenesis axis, and suppresses NF-kB inflammation. They share the angiogenesis and anti-inflammatory endpoints (redundant/overlapping) but have NO shared molecular target, which is why they are marketed together as the 'Wolverine stack.' Because both independently promote new blood-vessel growth, they also ADD to the same theoretical pro-angiogenic concern (could in principle feed an occult tumor or pathological neovascularization). BPC-157 monograph (VEGFR2/eNOS PMID 27847966, 33051481; pro-angiogenic tumor concern PMID 40005999); TB-500 monograph (G-actin PMID 23084823; Ang1/Tie2 angiogenesis PMID 30500399; tumor-migration concern PMID 11311852) Watch: Both are WADA-banned and non-pharmaceutical-grade (endotoxin/purity risk at each injection site). Given the additive pro-angiogenic biology, keep age-appropriate cancer screening current and investigate any new or growing mass; avoid with active or recent malignancy. Watch injection sites for redness, swelling, nodules, or infection. No human trial has ever compared the combination to either peptide alone, so any 'synergy' claim is anecdote, not evidence.

History & legal status

"Thymosin β4 was isolated by Allan Goldstein and colleagues from calf thymus in the mid-1960s–70s as part of 'thymosin fraction 5'; originally mistaken for a thymic hormone, its true role as the principal intracellular G-actin-sequestering peptide was recognised by Dan Safer around 1990 [Established; PMID 17468232]. RegeneRx Biopharmaceuticals developed synthetic Tβ4 as a drug: RGN-259 (topical ophthalmic, for dry eye and neurotrophic keratopathy — reached Phase 2/3, e.g. NCT01393132) and RGN-352 (IV, for acute myocardial infarction and stroke). As of the literature reviewed, NO Tβ4/TB-500 product has full FDA marketing approval for systemic human use; ophthalmic Tβ4 remains investigational. TB-500 specifically is a VETERINARY/grey-market preparation of N-acetylated LKKTETQ; it emerged for animal (notably equine racing) and bodybuilding use and is NOT an approved human medicine [Established; PMID 23084823, 23318763]. It is PROHIBITED in sport: the World Anti-Doping Agency bans Tβ4/TB-500 (S2 'peptide hormones, growth factors, related substances' / actin-regulating peptides), and dedicated LC-MS doping-detection methods exist for human and equine samples. Legally it is generally sold only as a 'research chemical / not for human use', placing buyers outside any pharmaceutical quality control."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Does the 7-residue acetylated LKKTETQ fragment actually reproduce full Tβ4's in-vivo wound-healing/cardiac effects in HUMANS, or is the marketed efficacy purely extrapolated from the 43-aa parent and animal data? No head-to-head human data exist.
  • What is the real human pharmacokinetic profile (bioavailability, half-life, tissue distribution) of subcutaneous/IM TB-500? Only IV parent-Tβ4 PK is published.
  • Is there a defined extracellular RECEPTOR mediating secreted Tβ4's paracrine angiogenic/anti-inflammatory effects, or are these fully explained by actin buffering plus surface cross-linking? The signalling receptor remains unidentified.
  • Is the theoretical tumor-promotion/metastasis risk real at the doses people use? Mechanistic plausibility exists (migration, angiogenesis, miR-1 target) but no human carcinogenicity data.
  • Does chronic systemic dosing alter fibrosis through the Ac-SDKP/ACE axis, and does this interact with common cardiovascular drugs (ACE inhibitors)? Studied only in rodents and likely fragment-independent.
  • What are the long-term safety and immunogenicity consequences of repeated injection of unregulated, variable-purity TB-500 product over months-to-years? No surveillance data.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (NOT dosing guidance, and not a substitute for clinical supervision): Given the angiogenesis/cell-migration mechanism and the theoretical (unproven) tumor concern, anyone using this should have age-appropriate cancer screening current and avoid use entirely with any active or recent malignancy or unexplained mass [Contested risk]. Watch and document injection sites for redness, swelling, nodules, abscess or persistent pain — signs of contamination from unregulated product. Because product is not pharma-grade, vigilance for systemic infection signs (fever, malaise) after injection is warranted. Track any new or changing skin lesions/moles and any unexplained bleeding or unusual bruising (given platelet/fibrin cross-linking biology). For anyone with diabetic or proliferative retinopathy, be alert to vision changes given pro-angiogenic potential. Useful baseline and periodic bloodwork to discuss with a clinician: CBC, comprehensive metabolic panel (liver/kidney function for a renally-cleared peptide), CRP/ESR if any inflammatory signs, and a clinical cardiovascular review if there is any heart disease history (the cardiac data are early and dosing/timing-sensitive). Stop and seek medical care for any new lump, persistent localized pain/swelling, fever, or vision change. None of the human trials establish a safe chronic self-injection regimen — the honest monitoring message is that long-term safety is simply unknown."

Caveats

Fragment vs. full protein: TB-500 (LKKTETQ) reproduces the actin-binding/migration/healing active site, but the full 43-aa Tβ4 has additional activities (e.g., the ac-SDKP cleavage product, nuclear actin functions) that the fragment may not fully replicate — so 'TB-500 = Tβ4' is an oversimplification. Most mechanistic studies use full Tβ4. Extracellular signaling is still poorly defined: no classical Tβ4 cell-surface receptor is firmly established, so how injected peptide produces some extracellular effects remains mechanistically uncertain (Contested). Dosing/protocol claims circulating online (e.g. 2–2.5 mg twice weekly) come from low-credibility commercial sources, not trials. Theoretical oncologic concern: a peptide that boosts cell migration and angiogenesis warrants caution regarding tumor promotion — not demonstrated, but not excluded (Speculative). Purity/identity of grey-market 'research' TB-500 is unverified.
