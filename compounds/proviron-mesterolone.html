Proviron

Main target: Androgen receptor. Human evidence is still limited.

Works in humans?Limited human data
Approved?Not FDA-approved

An oral DHT-derived androgen that is NOT 17-alpha-alkylated — so it skips the classic oral-steroid liver hit, can't turn into estrogen, and acts mostly as a mild androgen plus an SHBG-binding "free-hormone" lever rather than a mass-builder.

Limited human data 15 citations
Class
Synthetic anabolic-androgenic steroid (AAS); orally-active dihydrotestosterone (DHT) derivative
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR / NR3C4) — the same intracellular receptor that testosterone and DHT activate
Human status
Approved drug (ChEMBL max_phase 4, USAN 1966; trade name Proviron/Pro-Viron, Schering), not withdrawn, no black-box warning on record. Historically prescribed for male hypogonadism, idiopathic male infertility/oligospermia, and some libido/androgen-deficiency indications — though controlled human data are weak: the largest double-blind RCT (WHO, n=248 couples) showed no significant fertility or semen benefit over placebo [PMID 2680994]. Widely used off-label/illicitly in bodybuilding and PED contexts as an oral DHT-derivative adjunct. Most mechanistic specifics beyond the shared AR pathway rest on animal (PMID 34112889) and in-vitro (PMID 31884045) data; human pharmacodynamic data are limited and dated.

At a glance

  • Mild androgenic effects (libido, sense of well-being, masculinizing signaling) with comparatively weak raw muscle-mass gain — used as an adjunct, not a base anabolic. [PMID 2680994, PMID 34112889]
  • Non-aromatizable: cannot convert to estrogen, so no drug-driven gyno or estrogenic bloat; popular as a 'hardening'/anti-estrogen feel agent (the no-aromatization part is solid; strong anti-estrogen action is community lore). [structure-based; PMID 17616252]
  • Binds SHBG and can raise the FREE fraction of co-administered testosterone — the mechanistic basis for the 'makes the cycle feel stronger / better libido' reputation. [PMID 27576188]
  • Suppresses the body's own testosterone and sperm production through HPG-axis negative feedback — not a true TRT and a failed fertility drug. [PMID 2680994]
  • DHT-type androgenic side-effect risk: male-pattern hair loss in the genetically predisposed, acne, prostate stimulation (DHT-driven AR effects). [corpus MoA card: Androgen receptor]
  • Hepatotoxicity is LOW relative to 17-alpha-alkylated orals because it lacks the 17-alpha-alkyl group that causes classic oral-steroid cholestatic liver injury — a key differentiator. [structure; contrast with 17-alpha-alkylated AAS, corpus]
  • In-vitro flag for adverse lipid/cardiovascular and blood-pressure risk: like other AAS it would be expected to worsen cholesterol, plus a cell-study signal of mineralocorticoid-driven hypertension potential. [PMID 31884045]

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

7 steps6 human-evidenced 1 preclinical

  1. It's a DHT molecule with an oral-survival tweak Established

    Mesterolone is dihydrotestosterone (DHT) with one extra methyl group bolted onto carbon-1. That 1-alpha-methyl is what lets it survive a trip through the gut and liver as a pill. Crucially, this is NOT the 17-alpha-methyl/ethyl group found on classic oral steroids like Dianabol or Anadrol — the position of the methyl is different, and that single structural fact drives most of what makes Proviron behave the way it does. In the body it is heavily broken down (the parent drug is barely detectable in urine), leaving 1-methyl androstane metabolites. PMID 17616252 (Ho 2007, structure 1alpha-methyl-5alpha-androstan-17beta-ol-3-one + extensive metabolism); ChEMBL CHEMBL258918

  2. Binds the androgen receptor and switches on androgen genes Established

    Like all AAS, mesterolone works by slipping into cells and binding the androgen receptor (AR). The hormone-receptor pair then moves into the cell nucleus and acts as a switch (a transcription factor) that turns on androgen-responsive genes — the program behind male characteristics, libido, and (at high enough androgen signaling) muscle protein building. This is the shared genomic mechanism for testosterone, DHT, and every anabolic steroid. corpus MoA card: Androgen receptor (NR3C4); PMID 37717994 (Vitam Horm 2023, AR as ligand-activated transcription factor)

  3. Cannot convert to estrogen (non-aromatizable) — and may even blunt it Established

    Because it is already a 5-alpha-reduced DHT skeleton, mesterolone has no double bond for the aromatase enzyme to grab, so it cannot be converted into estradiol the way testosterone is. That means no estrogenic side effects from the drug itself (no gyno, no estrogen-driven water retention) — the reason it earned a reputation as a mild 'anti-estrogen' or hardening agent. Note: claims that it strongly suppresses estrogen by competing at aromatase or blocking estrogen are largely community lore, not well-established in human data; the firm fact is simply that it does not aromatize. Inferred from 5alpha-reduced DHT structure [PMID 17616252]; aromatase requires non-reduced A-ring substrate (corpus: aromatase substrate-specificity literature). Anti-estrogen potency in vivo = Contested.

  4. Binds SHBG — the 'free hormone' lever Evidence-human

    DHT-type androgens like mesterolone bind tightly to sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG), the liver-made carrier protein that mops up testosterone and estradiol in the blood. Only FREE (unbound) hormone is biologically active. By occupying SHBG, mesterolone can nudge up the free fraction of whatever testosterone is circulating — this is the mechanistic basis for the popular claim that it makes a cycle 'feel' stronger or boosts libido, even though it adds little raw mass itself. The free-hormone principle is well established; the size of mesterolone's real-world SHBG effect in users is less firmly quantified. Free-hormone / SHBG principle: PMID 27576188 (Laurent 2016); corpus MoA card: SHBG. Magnitude of mesterolone-specific SHBG displacement = Contested.

  5. Low anabolic punch in the clinic — modest, real, but weak muscle effect Evidence-human (weak clinical effect) + Evidence-rodent/avian (anabolic at tissue level)

    Despite being an androgen, mesterolone is a poor mass-builder in people. In the largest controlled human trial (WHO, 248 infertile couples), it produced no meaningful improvement in sperm quality and only a non-significant bump in outcomes versus placebo — consistent with a weak systemic androgen. Animal work does show genuine anabolic action: in birds and rodents it drove muscle-fiber hypertrophy and satellite-cell (muscle stem cell) proliferation. So the muscle-building machinery responds, but the human dose-for-dose effect is modest — which is why it's used as an adjunct, not a primary bulking drug. PMID 2680994 (WHO double-blind RCT, no significant fertility/semen benefit); PMID 34112889 (Asfour 2021, mesterolone-driven fiber hypertrophy + satellite-cell proliferation in avian muscle)

  6. Suppresses your own testosterone via HPG-axis feedback Established

    Any androgen that activates the AR signals the brain (hypothalamus and pituitary) that 'there's plenty of androgen around,' which dials down LH and FSH and therefore the testicles' own testosterone and sperm production. This negative feedback is why androgens — including the supposedly 'mild' mesterolone — are NOT a true testosterone-replacement and historically failed as a fertility treatment: adding outside androgen can quiet the body's own production. corpus MoA card: Androgen receptor (HPG-axis negative feedback); consistent with null fertility result in PMID 2680994

  7. An unexpected in-vitro twist: pushes adrenal cells toward mineralocorticoids Evidence-in vitro

    A 2020 lab study screening 19 steroids found mesterolone (with its close cousins mestanolone and methenolone) disturbed adrenal hormone production — increasing mineralocorticoid output while decreasing adrenal androgens, apparently via interference with the CYP17A1 enzyme. Mineralocorticoids raise sodium retention and blood pressure, which the authors flagged as a possible route to hypertension/cardiovascular strain. This is cell-culture (H295R) data, not proven in living humans, so treat it as a mechanistic flag rather than an established clinical effect. PMID 31884045 (Patt 2020, H295R adrenocortical cells: mesterolone raised mineralocorticoid, lowered adrenal androgen biosynthesis via apparent CYP17A1 dysfunction)

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. Mesterolone (1α-methyl-5α-dihydrotestosterone; C20H32O2, MW 304.47; ChEMBL CHEMBL258918) is a 5α-reduced, non-aromatizable DHT derivative that acts as an androgen receptor (AR) AGONIST but is a comparatively WEAK AR ligand. (1) AR binding: in a recombinant rat AR ligand-binding-domain fluorescence-polarization assay its IC50 was 18,280 nM (pChEMBL 4.74) — i.e. low-micromolar, ~3-4 orders of magnitude weaker than high-affinity ligands such as methyltrienolone/R1881 (Established, ChEMBL bioactivity record, Bioorg Med Chem Lett 2017). In Saartok et al.'s classic competition assay vs [3H]methyltrienolone in rat/rabbit skeletal muscle and rat prostate cytosol, 1α-methyl-DHT (mesterolone) was the WEAKEST of the 'avid' AR binders, ranked methyltrienolone > nandrolone > methenolone > testosterone > mesterolone (Established-human/rodent tissue, PMID 6539197, Saartok 1984). The muscle:prostate AR-RBA ratio (a myotrophic-selectivity index) was near unity (~0.4-1.7) for most AAS including mesterolone, i.e. no intrinsic tissue selectivity. (2) The defining pharmacologic feature is its EXTREME affinity for sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG): displacement of [3H]DHT from human SHBG gave Kd = 0.25 nM (pKd 9.6) — one of the highest SHBG affinities recorded for any steroid (Established, ChEMBL, J Med Chem 2008). Saartok 1984 independently found mesterolone the single strongest SHBG binder of all AAS tested, with RBA ~4x that of DHT (DHT itself being the strongest endogenous SHBG ligand) (Established, PMID 6539197). This dual profile — modest AR agonism + dominant SHBG occupancy — is the molecular basis for its reputed use to lower SHBG and free up bound testosterone. (3) Because the 1α-methyl/5α-reduced A-ring blocks aromatase, mesterolone is non-aromatizable (no estradiol formation); it is also not a 5α-reductase substrate (already 5α-reduced) (Established, mechanistic/structural). Reported weak antiestrogenic/antigonadotropic effects in some andrology studies are AR-mediated central feedback rather than direct ER antagonism (Evidence-human, see evidence_base).

Signalling cascade. Mesterolone signals through the canonical genomic AR pathway. As a small lipophilic steroid (ALogP 4.21) it diffuses across the plasma membrane and binds cytoplasmic AR. Unliganded AR is held in an inactive complex with heat-shock chaperones (HSP90/HSP70, co-chaperone p23, immunophilins); ligand binding to the AR ligand-binding domain triggers a conformational change, chaperone dissociation, exposure of the nuclear localization signal, and AR homodimerization. The receptor-ligand complex translocates to the nucleus, where the two DNA-binding-domain zinc fingers engage androgen response elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer regions of target genes; AR then recruits p160/SRC coactivators, CBP/p300 and the Mediator complex to assemble the transcription pre-initiation machinery. In skeletal muscle this upregulates anabolic transcriptional programs (increased myofibrillar protein synthesis, satellite-cell activation/proliferation and myonuclear accretion) — a cascade directly demonstrated for mesterolone: oral dosing induced satellite-cell proliferation and myonuclear accretion preferentially in glycolytic fibers (Evidence-rodent/avian, PMID 34112889). The corpus textbook source notes the SHBG-binding hierarchy DHT >> T >> androstenedione is what mesterolone exploits at the carrier-protein level (textbook, PMC OA androgen-biology review). Because mesterolone is a poor AR transactivator relative to testosterone yet a powerful SHBG binder, much of its clinical/anecdotal effect is plausibly indirect — displacing endogenous testosterone/DHT from SHBG to raise the free-androgen fraction — rather than strong direct AR drive (mechanistic synthesis; Contested for the magnitude of the free-hormone effect in vivo).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Oral tablet, free (unesterified) alcohol — marketed as mesterolone 25 mg tablets (Proviron, Schering; ATC G03BB01). Not esterified and not injected; ChEMBL flags it as not parenteral/topical (Established).
Absorption / bioavailability
Orally active without 17α-alkylation because the 1α-methyl group sterically hinders hepatic 17β-hydroxyl oxidation, slowing first-pass inactivation — the same design rationale as methenolone/mesterolone-class 1-methyl steroids. Quantitative human oral bioavailability is not well characterized in the public literature (Evidence-human gap); drug-likeness is favorable (QED 0.73, 0 Lipinski violations, MW 304, PSA 37 Å²) consistent with good passive absorption.
Half-life
Commonly cited plasma half-life is short — on the order of ~12 hours — but this figure is not robustly documented by a primary human PK study in the sources retrieved this run; treat the specific number as Contested/low-certainty. Pharmacodynamic/androgenic action outlasts plasma clearance because of high tissue and SHBG binding.
Metabolism
Hepatic. Mesterolone is already 5α-reduced and 1α-methylated, so it is NOT a substrate for aromatase or 5α-reductase. Documented biotransformation is at the 3-keto and 17β-hydroxyl positions: reduction of the 3-keto to 3α- and 3β-hydroxy epimers and oxidation/reduction generating 1α-methyl-5α-androstan-3α-ol-17-one (M1), the 3β-ol-17-one epimer (M2), and 1α-methyl-5α-androstane-3α,17β-diol (M3), among others, characterized in horse liver microsomes and in vivo (the principal published metabolic map) (Evidence-rodent/equine + human doping data, PMID 17616252, Ho 2007). Phase II conjugation yields glucuronide and sulfate metabolites used as doping-control targets (Evidence-human, PMID 36843443, Drug Test Anal 2023). Enzyme-level CYP attribution (specific isoforms) is not pinned down in the retrieved literature (Evidence gap).
Excretion
Renal, predominantly as glucuronide- and sulfate-conjugated 1α-methyl-androstane metabolites detectable in urine; long-window metabolites underpin WADA detection protocols (Evidence-human, PMID 36843443).
Notes
No ester and no prodrug step — it is the active molecule itself (ChEMBL prodrug flag = 0). Non-aromatizable and non-5α-reducible, so it cannot convert to estradiol or to more potent/less potent 5α metabolites of the parent ring. Crosses into the CNS (lipophilic) consistent with reported central/behavioral effects in rodents (PMID 7198778). Because it is not 17α-alkylated, classic 17α-alkyl cholestatic hepatotoxicity is expected to be LOWER than for 17α-alkylated orals, though ChEMBL indexes it in a hepatotoxicity reference set (single reference, low frequency; assay-derived, not a strong human signal).

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

Human dose-response is sparse and was studied almost entirely in male infertility/hypogonadism, NOT in performance/anabolic contexts (no controlled human anabolic dose-response data exist). HUMAN (andrology): 25 mg/day for 100 days improved seminal fructose and some sperm parameters in pathospermia (open study, n=42; PMID 689818). 100-150 mg/day produced significant rises in sperm concentration/total count in mild-to-moderate (but not severe) idiopathic oligospermia (PMID 2887529, n=40 RCT; PMID 2892728, n=250). Crucially, the largest, best-controlled trial (WHO, 75 or 150 mg/day vs placebo, 6 months, n=157 idiopathic infertility) found NO benefit over placebo on pregnancy rate (9±3% placebo vs 12±4% and 16±? on mesterolone, not significant) — i.e. the apparent dose-response in open studies did not translate to a fertility benefit in a blinded RCT (Evidence-human, PMID 2680994). A consistent andrology observation is that mesterolone at these doses does NOT meaningfully suppress FSH/LH or testosterone in men with normal/low baseline gonadotropins (PMID 689818; PMID 2892728), implying weak central antigonadotropic potency at clinical doses — though it can blunt elevated gonadotropins. RODENT/AVIAN (anabolic): clear dose-dependent anabolic effect on muscle. In chickens, oral mesterolone 4-16 mg/kg every 3 days for 4 weeks increased body and muscle mass and induced hypertrophy + satellite-cell/myonuclear accretion preferentially in glycolytic fibers (dose-graded; Evidence-rodent/avian, PMID 34112889). In mice, mesterolone caused extrafusal fiber hypertrophy (Type I > Type II) and satellite-cell activation, additive with exercise (PMID 19854955). Cardiovascular harm is also dose-present in rodents: 2 µg/g body weight for 3 weeks worsened lipoprotein profile (raised LDL-c/VLDL-c, TG, total cholesterol) and cardiac remodelling in lipaemic mice (PMID 18808528). Bottom line: anabolic dose-response is established only in animals; the human anabolic/ergogenic dose-response is UNKNOWN and not derivable from the andrology data.

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Dihydrotestosterone (DHT, the parent androgen)
    same: Both are 5α-reduced, non-aromatizable pure androgens acting as AR agonists; both are among the strongest SHBG binders.
    different: Mesterolone carries a 1α-methyl group that (a) makes it orally active without 17α-alkylation by slowing hepatic 17β-OH oxidation, and (b) blocks 3-keto reduction enough to resist the rapid inactivation that destroys DHT's muscle activity. Saartok 1984 showed mesterolone binds SHBG ~4x more avidly than DHT yet is a weaker AR binder than DHT in prostate.
  • vs Methenolone (Primobolan; 1-methyl-Δ1-5α-androstanolone)
    same: Sibling 1-methyl 5α-androstane DHT-class steroid, non-aromatizable, mild, oral-active variants exist; both share the 1-methyl orally-stabilizing motif and similar metabolic targets in doping assays.
    different: Methenolone has a Δ1 double bond and is a stronger AR binder than mesterolone (Saartok ranking: methenolone > testosterone > mesterolone) and is used as an anabolic; mesterolone is positioned more as an ancillary/SHBG-binder than a mass-builder. The two are analyzed side-by-side in WADA phase-II metabolite methods (PMID 36843443).
  • vs Testosterone
    same: Both AR agonists driving anabolic/androgenic gene programs.
    different: Testosterone aromatizes to estradiol and is 5α-reduced to DHT; mesterolone does neither (terminal 5α/1α-methyl structure). Testosterone binds AR more strongly than mesterolone but SHBG less strongly. Functionally mesterolone is weaker anabolically and weaker at HPG suppression at clinical doses, but more 'androgenic-feeling' per AR occupancy and free of estrogenic side effects.
  • vs Stanozolol / methandrostenolone (17α-alkylated orals)
    same: All orally active synthetic AAS used non-medically.
    different: Those are 17α-ALKYLATED (hepatotoxic cholestasis risk) and bind AR weakly (stanozolol/methandienone RBA <0.05 in Saartok 1984) acting partly via non-AR/SHBG routes; mesterolone is 1α-methyl (not 17α-alkyl), so lower classic hepatotoxic profile, and its signature is SHBG binding rather than potent AR transactivation.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Anastrozole compounds risk ⚠ Contested
    Proviron is a non-aromatizing DHT-derived androgen that binds SHBG and is popularly regarded as a mild anti-estrogen (though strong anti-estrogen action is community lore, not established). Like Masteron, it provides no aromatase substrate, so anastrozole has nothing to block from it, but their estrogen-lowering tendencies can stack: Proviron's estrogen-lowering feel plus anastrozole's blockade of estrogen synthesis can compound into estradiol over-suppression. The genuine mechanistic edge is additive risk of a crashed-E2 state with no counteracting benefit gained, because Proviron does not raise estrogen in the first place; the additivity is inference, not trial-measured. proviron-mesterolone.json (non-aromatizable DHT derivative, SHBG-binding, 'strong anti-estrogen action is community lore'; key_effects); anastrozole.json side_effect_mechanisms (crashed E2 harm); corpus MoA Aromatase Watch: Estradiol watched for over-suppression; recognize that pairing a non-aromatizing androgen with a synthesis blocker offers no estrogen-control upside and raises crash risk. Track libido, mood, joint symptoms, HDL, and bone on chronic use.
  • Dianabol shares target Contested
    The one solid mechanistic link is SHBG: mesterolone is a DHT-derived androgen that binds SHBG tightly, and Dbol suppresses SHBG - both are SHBG levers, so together they push more free, active hormone (including Dbol's free estrogen). Note this can WORSEN, not fix, Dbol's free-estrogen-driven gyno/bloat. The popular 'anti-estrogen' framing for Proviron (competing at aromatase / blocking ER) is largely community lore and Contested in humans; the firm fact is only that mesterolone itself does not aromatize. Mesterolone is also NOT 17a-alkylated (oral activity comes from a 1a-methyl group), so unlike the oral-AAS edges it does NOT add to Dbol's 17aa hepatotoxicity - the real interaction is SHBG/free-hormone modulation, not a clean estrogen counteraction and not liver-compounding. Dbol monograph mechanism_steps 6-7 (estrogenic effects, SHBG crash); proviron-mesterolone.json (DHT-derived, NOT 17aa, binds SHBG raising free hormone; anti-estrogen potency in vivo Contested / largely lore) Watch: SHBG with free and total testosterone and estradiol (Proviron's SHBG binding shifts free-hormone interpretation, and a low-SHBG state can raise free estrogen); breast tenderness/gyno signs; note Proviron is itself a DHT androgen (hair/prostate/lipid effects) even though it spares the liver.
  • Methyltestosterone shares target Established
    Both act on SHBG to raise the free/active fraction of co-circulating androgens and estradiol, but by different routes: methyltestosterone (a 17aa oral) strongly suppresses hepatic SHBG production and binds SHBG weakly; Proviron (mesterolone) is the strongest SHBG binder among common AAS (~4x DHT) and displaces bound hormone. Together they drive SHBG-bound hormone into the free pool, so a modest-looking total testosterone can mask a high free level and unexpected estrogenic effects, a bloodwork-interpretation trap. methyltestosterone monograph (17aa AAS lower SHBG, weak SHBG binding); Morales 1994 SHBG suppression PMID 8072077; Saartok 1984 mesterolone strongest SHBG binder PMID 6539197 Watch: SHBG together with total AND free/calculated-free testosterone and estradiol; interpret 'normal' totals cautiously when SHBG is low. Watch for libido/estrogenic symptom shifts.
  • Masteron redundant Contested
    Proviron (mesterolone, 1a-methyl-DHT) and Masteron (2a-methyl-DHT) are both DHT-derived, non-aromatizing, already-5-alpha-reduced androgens marketed as mild 'anti-estrogens' that bind SHBG. Their mechanisms overlap heavily: neither aromatizes, both are 5-alpha-reduced (so finasteride does not blunt them), both are claimed to displace testosterone from SHBG to raise free-T, and both mildly oppose estrogen via the androgen receptor rather than by blocking aromatase. Stacking them is largely redundant on the estrogen/SHBG axis while adding androgenic side-effect load. Note the SHBG free-T claim itself is Contested - a measured drostanolone-SHBG affinity was never established. Masteron monograph pharmacology + key_unknowns (SHBG free-T unquantified, Contested/Speculative); Proviron monograph (DHT-derived, non-aromatizing, binds SHBG) Watch: SHBG and free/total testosterone (overlapping, hard-to-titrate effect of uncertain magnitude); androgenic side effects (hair, acne, prostate) which add rather than cancel.

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

Developed by Schering AG (Germany) as mesterolone (code SH-723; brand Proviron, also Pro-Viron, Androviron, Testiwop). USAN assigned 1966 (ChEMBL). Introduced clinically in the 1960s as an oral androgen for androgen-deficiency/hypogonadism states and male subfertility; ATC code G03BB01 (androstan derivatives). It reached max clinical phase 4 (approved drug) in ChEMBL and remains marketed in many countries (e.g. Europe, parts of Asia) as a prescription androgen, though it was never approved by the US FDA. It is a controlled anabolic-androgenic steroid: in the US it falls under Schedule III (Anabolic Steroid Control Act). It is prohibited in sport at all times by WADA (exogenous AAS, S1 class); extensive doping-control metabolite methods exist (PMID 17616252; PMID 36843443), and mesterolone is a recurrently detected agent in athlete testing. Not withdrawn for safety (ChEMBL withdrawn_flag = false).

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • Human anabolic/ergogenic dose-response is essentially unstudied — all controlled human data are in andrology/infertility (25-150 mg/day); the muscle-building dose-effect curve in athletes is unknown and only inferred from rodent/avian work.
  • Quantitative human pharmacokinetics are poorly documented in the public literature — the widely cited ~12 h half-life, oral bioavailability, Cmax/Tmax and exact CYP isoenzymes responsible for metabolism lack a clear primary-source anchor.
  • The real-world magnitude of the 'free-testosterone-raising via SHBG displacement' effect in humans is unquantified — the in vitro SHBG affinity (Kd 0.25 nM) is dramatic, but whether clinical doses meaningfully shift free-androgen fractions and downstream physiology in vivo is not demonstrated by controlled human data.
  • Degree of HPG-axis suppression at supraphysiologic 'cycle' doses is uncharacterized; andrology data show weak suppression at 25-150 mg/day, but extrapolation to higher non-medical doses is unsupported.
  • Long-term cardiovascular and prostate outcomes specific to mesterolone (vs the well-documented rodent lipid/cardiac harm) have no human longitudinal data.
  • Direct human AR transactivation potency and tissue-selectivity (e.g. relative skin/follicle vs muscle effects) have not been measured with modern reporter assays — most AR/SHBG numbers come from 1980s rodent-tissue competition binding.

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Harm-reduction monitoring (no dosing guidance). Bloodwork to watch over time: (1) Full lipid panel — total cholesterol, LDL-c, HDL-c, triglycerides; non-aromatizable DHT-class orals characteristically depress HDL and raise LDL/atherogenic fractions (rodent-demonstrated for mesterolone). (2) Liver enzymes — ALT, AST, ALP, bilirubin/GGT; lower risk than 17α-alkylated orals but still worth baseline + periodic checks. (3) HPG/endocrine panel — total and free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG (SHBG of particular interest given the drug's defining high SHBG affinity), and estradiol (expected unchanged since non-aromatizing); watch for suppressed LH/FSH/endogenous testosterone with higher exposure. (4) Hematocrit/hemoglobin — androgen-driven erythrocytosis raises thrombosis risk. (5) PSA and prostate symptoms in older males (DHT-class prostate stimulation). (6) Blood pressure — rodent data show BP elevation. Clinical/physical signs: acne and oily skin, accelerated scalp hair loss, mood/libido changes; in women any virilization (voice deepening, hirsutism, clitoromegaly) is a signal to stop because it can be irreversible. Cardiovascular symptoms (chest pain, palpitations, exertional dyspnea) warrant prompt evaluation given the cardiac-remodelling signal. This is monitoring guidance only — it is not medical advice or a protocol; abnormal results should be reviewed by a clinician.

Caveats

HARM-REDUCTION / TEACHING ONLY — no dosing given and none should be inferred. Key honesty points for the audience: (1) Proviron is a WEAK anabolic in humans — the largest human trial showed no significant benefit; the impressive muscle-fiber/satellite-cell anabolic data are from birds and rodents (Evidence-rodent/avian), not humans, so do not overstate mass gains. [PMID 2680994, PMID 34112889] (2) 'Anti-estrogen' reputation: the SOLID fact is that it cannot aromatize to estrogen (DHT-based, 5-alpha-reduced). Claims that it actively LOWERS estrogen by blocking aromatase or estrogen receptors are largely community lore and not well established in human evidence — flag as Contested. (3) The eye-catching 'raises blood pressure via mineralocorticoids/CYP17A1' finding is in-vitro cell-culture only (H295R adrenal cells) and has NOT been demonstrated in living humans — present it as a mechanistic hypothesis, not a proven clinical effect. [PMID 31884045] (4) Lower liver toxicity than Dianabol/Anadrol-type orals is a structural inference from the absence of a 17-alpha-alkyl group, NOT a clean human safety claim — it still carries the general AAS risks (adverse lipids, HPG suppression, prostate/hair/acne via DHT-type androgen action, and possible cardiovascular strain). (5) IMPORTANT terminology correction the YouTuber should not get wrong: Proviron is often lumped in with '17-alpha-alkylated oral steroids' — it is NOT one. Its oral activity comes from a 1-alpha-methyl group, which is exactly why the standard oral-AAS hepatotoxicity story does not map cleanly onto it. [PMID 17616252] (6) Human pharmacology data are old and sparse; much of the modern understanding is extrapolated from DHT biology and AR/SHBG principles rather than from mesterolone-specific human trials. All six cited PMIDs were checked live and are NOT retracted/under expression of concern (re-check at publish time; DB ingestion lags).

It's a DHT molecule with an oral-survival tweak. Mesterolone is dihydrotestosterone (DHT) with one extra methyl group bolted onto carbon-1. That 1-alpha-methyl is what lets it survive a trip through the gut and liver as a pill. Crucially, this is NOT the 17-alpha-methyl/ethyl group found on classic oral steroids like Dianabol or Anadrol — the position of the methyl is different, and that single structural fact drives most of what makes Proviron behave the way it does. In the body it is heavily broken down (the parent drug is barely detectable in urine), leaving 1-methyl androstane metabolites.
