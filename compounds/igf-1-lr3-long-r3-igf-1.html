IGF-1 LR3

A lab-engineered version of the body's main muscle-growth hormone, redesigned to dodge the proteins that normally switch it off — so it stays active far longer. Powerful on paper, but it has essentially never been tested in healthy humans.

Limited human data 21 citations
Class
Recombinant IGF-1 analogue / mutein (IGF-1R agonist); research-only peptide
Primary target
Type 1 IGF receptor (IGF-1R), a receptor tyrosine kinase on muscle and many other cell types
Human status
Evidence-rodent / Evidence-in vitro ONLY for the muscle-relevant claims. There are NO published controlled studies of Long R3 IGF-1 for muscle building, strength, or body composition in healthy humans — a PubMed search filtered to humans returns zero LR3 trials. Native IGF-1 (mecasermin) is an approved human drug for severe IGF-1 deficiency, but LR3 is a different, more potent molecule sold only as a gray-market 'research chemical.' One paper documents His-tagged Long-R3-IGF-1 seized as a black-market product (PMID 20675162), confirming real-world misuse without clinical data. Treat all 'it builds muscle in people' claims as forum hype, not evidence.

At a glance

  • Activates IGF-1R → PI3K/Akt/mTOR signaling, increasing muscle protein synthesis (Evidence-rodent / in vitro; PMID 32858949, 20593312)
  • Suppresses muscle protein breakdown by inhibiting FoxO transcription factors and the ubiquitin-proteasome system (Evidence-rodent; PMID 32858949)
  • Much longer-lasting and more potent per dose than native IGF-1 when injected, because it barely binds the IGFBP carrier proteins (Evidence-rodent; PMID 8708565)
  • Drives general organ and tissue growth, not muscle-selectively, when given systemically in animals (Evidence-rodent; PMID 7561636)
  • Lowers the body's own IGF-1, growth hormone and IGFBP-3 via negative feedback in animal studies (Evidence-rodent; PMID 9488001)
  • Insulin-like / hypoglycemia risk from spillover onto the insulin receptor and insulin-like signaling (Evidence-rodent; PMID 7683875) — a real-world danger flagged in user communities but never quantified in humans
  • Theoretical pro-growth signaling on existing tumor cells via IGF-1R (Contested/Speculative for LR3 specifically; IGF-1R drives proliferation broadly — PMID 12843187)

Mechanism, step by step

  1. What's changed vs natural IGF-1 Established

    Long R3 IGF-1 is native human IGF-1 with two engineered edits: position 3 swapped from glutamate to arginine (the 'R3'/'Arg3' part) and a 13-amino-acid extension added to the front (the 'Long' part). These edits are deliberate — they cripple the molecule's ability to be grabbed by its carrier proteins while leaving receptor binding largely intact. PMID 8919033 (design/characterisation of Long-R3-IGF-I muteins)

  2. It escapes the 'off-switch' carrier proteins (IGFBPs) Evidence-in vitro / Evidence-rodent

    Normally ~99% of IGF-1 in the body is bound by IGF-binding proteins (IGFBP-1 to -6), which act like handcuffs that keep it inactive and short-lived. Because of its structural edits, Long R3 binds these IGFBPs very poorly, so much more of it stays free and able to reach the receptor. This is the single defining feature of the molecule. (IGFBP-3 fails to suppress LR3 receptor signaling); PMID 8708565 (analogues that bind IGFBPs poorly are far more potent)

  3. Escaping the carriers makes it longer-acting and more potent Evidence-rodent

    Free, unbound peptide reaches receptors faster and the extra free fraction translates into greater biological effect per dose in animals. In infusion and injection studies, IGF-1 analogues that bind IGFBPs poorly were dramatically more potent than native IGF-1 — the basis of the popular claim that LR3 'lasts longer' (often cited as a ~20–30 h half-life vs minutes for free native IGF-1). Note the half-life figure comes from preclinical/pharmacokinetic work, not human dosing trials. PMID 8708565 (superior potency maintained by injection); PMID 7561636 (LR3 infusion pharmacology in guinea pig)

  4. It binds and fires the IGF-1 receptor (IGF-1R) Established (for IGF-1R activation in general); Evidence-in vitro (for LR3 specifically)

    Once free, LR3 docks onto IGF-1R, a receptor tyrosine kinase studded on muscle cells. Binding triggers the receptor to phosphorylate itself and recruit downstream signaling — the same receptor and the same trigger as natural IGF-1. LR3 is an agonist (a 'go' signal), just one that is harder to silence. (LR3 drives IGF-1R signaling); PMID 32858949 (IGF-1R as the muscle growth receptor)

  5. Downstream: the PI3K → Akt → mTOR muscle-building cascade Evidence-rodent / Evidence-in vitro

    Activated IGF-1R switches on the PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway, the master 'build muscle' circuit. mTOR ramps up protein synthesis (making new muscle protein), and Akt simultaneously shuts off FoxO transcription factors, which lowers the ubiquitin-proteasome 'demolition crew' that breaks muscle down. The net effect in animal/cell models is more synthesis and less breakdown — hypertrophy. PMID 32858949 (IGF-1 → PI3K/Akt/mTOR synthesis, FoxO/UPS suppression); PMID 20593312 (PI3K control of muscle hypertrophy/atrophy)

  6. The catch: it's not muscle-selective, and it talks back to your own hormones Evidence-rodent

    IGF-1R sits on almost every tissue, so systemic LR3 grows organs and other tissues too — not just the muscle you inject. In animals, systemic LR3 raised organ growth while paradoxically lowering the body's own IGF-1, growth hormone and IGFBP-3 through negative feedback. It can also spill onto the insulin receptor, producing insulin-like effects and hypoglycemia risk. PMID 7561636 (organ growth, falling endogenous IGF/IGFBP); PMID 9488001 (LR3 lowers GH and IGFBP-3); PMID 7683875 (insulin-like effects/hypoglycemia)

  7. The human gap and the cancer worry Contested / Speculative (for LR3 in humans)

    Everything above comes from rodents, livestock, and cell cultures. The same growth signal that builds muscle (IGF-1R → PI3K/Akt) also drives cell proliferation and survival, which is why high IGF-1 signaling is studied as a cancer-promoting axis. Whether LR3 meaningfully raises cancer risk in humans is unknown — there are no human safety data on LR3 at all. This is a flag, not a proven harm. PMID 12843187 (IGF-1 drives proliferation/telomerase in cancer cells — context for the concern); human LR3 trials: none found

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. IGF-1 LR3 (Long R3 IGF-1) is a recombinant 83-aa IGF-1 mutein: a 13-residue N-terminal extension fused to native IGF-1 carrying a Glu3->Arg3 substitution ("Long" + "R3"). [Established] It is a full AGONIST of the type 1 IGF receptor (IGF1R, a tyrosine-kinase receptor structurally homologous to the insulin receptor). [Established] The defining property is drastically REDUCED affinity for the six IGF binding proteins (IGFBP-1 to -6): in a bovine-embryo competition/Western-ligand-blot assay the affinity of Long-R3-IGF-I for IGFBPs was reported as 'at least 3 orders of magnitude lower' (~>=1000-fold weaker) than native IGF-I (PMID 11181549) [Evidence-in vitro]. Because little circulating peptide is sequestered by IGFBPs, a larger free fraction reaches IGF1R, making LR3 substantially more potent per mole than rhIGF-1 in cell and whole-animal assays. NUMERIC GAP: I did not retrieve a primary paper reporting an explicit IGF1R Kd/Ki for LR3 this run; affinity for IGF1R is generally described as similar-to-slightly-lower than native IGF-1, with potency gains driven by IGFBP evasion rather than receptor affinity. [Contested / under-quantified] Cross-reactivity: like IGF-1, LR3 can weakly activate the insulin receptor and the IGF1R/IR hybrid receptor at high concentrations (extrapolated from IGF-1 pharmacology; not separately quantified for LR3 in retrieved sources) [Speculative for LR3 specifically].

Signalling cascade. IGF1R is a pre-formed (alpha2-beta2) disulfide-linked receptor tyrosine kinase. Ligand binding to the alpha-subunits triggers a conformational change and trans-autophosphorylation of beta-subunit tyrosines in the activation loop, creating docking sites for IRS-1/IRS-2 and Shc. Two principal arms follow (PMID 37834331, review): (1) IRS-1 -> PI3K -> PIP3 -> AKT, which drives the anabolic/pro-survival outputs - AKT phosphorylates and inhibits FOXO, activates mTORC1 (protein synthesis, myoblast hypertrophy), inhibits GSK3 and the atrophy ubiquitin ligases (Atrogin-1/MuRF1), and promotes glucose uptake via GLUT4 (overlap with insulin signaling -> the hypoglycemia liability). (2) Shc/Grb2/SOS -> RAS -> RAF -> MEK -> ERK/MAPK, the predominantly mitogenic/proliferative arm. The review also notes IGF1R nuclear translocation and co-localization with MAPK acting as a transcriptional activator, plus potent anti-apoptotic signaling (PMID 37834331). [Established for IGF1R signaling; LR3 engages the same cascade - shown e.g. by anabolic AKT/mTOR pathway activation and myoblast proliferation after LR3/oIGF-1 infusion, PMID 36091374, Evidence-rodent/large-animal].

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Parenteral only (subcutaneous in animal studies; LR3 is a peptide and is destroyed by gastric/pancreatic proteases - oral Long-R3-IGF-I had NO systemic metabolic effect in neonatal calves, PMID 9483305). No approved human route. [Evidence-large-animal]
Absorption / bioavailability
No human bioavailability data retrieved. In neonatal calves, subcutaneous Long-R3-IGF-I (50 ug/kg/day) produced systemic effects (lowered plasma glucose and insulin) whereas the SAME dose given orally did not - indicating oral bioavailability is effectively nil and SC absorption is functional (PMID 9483305). [Evidence-large-animal]
Half-life
NO direct LR3 human half-life found this run. Mechanistic basis for 'long-acting': IGFBP evasion removes the buffering 140-kDa ternary complex (IGF-1 + IGFBP-3 + acid-labile subunit) that normally extends native IGF-1's circulating life - so the comparison is nuanced. For rhIGF-1 (mecasermin), reviews note serum half-life is SHORTER in patients lacking IGFBP-3/ALS than in normal subjects, and co-formulating with IGFBP-3 (mecasermin rinfabate) PROLONGS half-life (PMIDs 19627167, 16610982, 17380212). LR3's practical 'long action' in tissue is usually attributed to a larger sustained free/active fraction and slower IGFBP-mediated clearance, not necessarily a longer plasma t1/2. [Contested - human LR3 PK is a genuine gap]
Metabolism
Proteolytic degradation (peptidases); no CYP involvement (peptide hormone). Receptor-mediated internalization/lysosomal degradation after IGF1R binding is the expected clearance route (general IGF-1 pharmacology). [Established for peptide class; not separately measured for LR3 in retrieved sources]
Excretion
Renal handling/catabolism of peptide fragments expected (as for IGF-1); no quantitative LR3 excretion data retrieved. [Speculative for LR3]
Notes
Not an ester or prodrug - it is a single-chain engineered protein. Does not meaningfully cross the blood-brain barrier as an intact peptide (general IGF-1 property; not LR3-specific data here). Repeated LR3/IGF-1 dosing SUPPRESSES the GH->hepatic-IGF-1 axis: 4-day LR3IGF-I infusion in pigs cut plasma GH, endogenous IGF-1 AND IGFBP-3 (PMID 9488001) - a feedback effect relevant to interpreting bloodwork. [Evidence-large-animal]

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

HUMAN DOSE-RESPONSE FOR LR3 IS UNKNOWN - no human PK, efficacy, or safety trial of LR3 IGF-1 was found in this search (the entire human evidence base is for native rhIGF-1/mecasermin, a different molecule). Animal dose-response that IS documented: (1) Neonatal calves, SC Long-R3-IGF-I 50 ug/kg/day x7d lowered plasma glucose and insulin; same dose oral = no effect (PMID 9483305) [Evidence-large-animal]. (2) Pigs, LR3IGF-I 180 ug/kg/day x4d DECREASED average daily gain, feed intake, plasma insulin, IGFBP-3, endogenous IGF-1 and GH - i.e. in pigs LR3 was catabolic/growth-inhibiting, opposite to rats, showing species-dependent dose-response (PMID 9488001) [Evidence-large-animal]. (3) Guinea pig, LR3IGF-I 120 ug/day x7d selectively increased fractional weight of gut, kidneys, spleen and adrenals without increasing overall body-weight gain - organ-selective, not uniform growth (PMID 7561636) [Evidence-rodent]. (4) Fetal sheep, recombinant LR3/ovine IGF-1 infusion increased heart/kidney/spleen/adrenal growth and myoblast proliferation but did NOT raise muscle fractional synthetic rate or hindlimb mass over 1 week (PMID 36091374) [Evidence-large-animal]. Cross-species takeaway: effects are highly species- and tissue-dependent, often visceral-organ-selective, and can be anabolic (rats/myoblasts) or catabolic (pigs). Bodybuilding 'muscle-growth' dosing claims are NOT supported by any retrieved controlled human data.

How it compares

  • vs Native rhIGF-1 (mecasermin / Increlex)
    same: Both are full IGF1R agonists driving the same PI3K-AKT and RAS-MAPK cascade; both can cause hypoglycemia and share IGF-1's theoretical oncogenic liability.
    different: LR3 has the 13-aa N-terminal extension + Glu3->Arg3 substitution giving >=1000-fold lower IGFBP affinity (PMID 11181549), so a larger free/active fraction reaches receptors and effects are less buffered/longer-acting at the tissue level. Mecasermin is an FDA/EMA-approved drug for severe primary IGF-1 deficiency; LR3 is NOT a registered human drug.
  • vs Mecasermin rinfabate (rhIGF-1 + rhIGFBP-3 complex / iPlex)
    same: Delivers IGF1R agonism for IGF-deficiency states.
    different: Opposite design philosophy: rinfabate ADDS back the IGFBP-3 binding partner to PROLONG half-life and BLUNT hypoglycemia (PMIDs 16610982, 17380212), whereas LR3 deliberately EVADES IGFBPs. Rinfabate was FDA-approved but later withdrawn for short-stature use for legal reasons (PMID 19627167).
  • vs des(1-3)IGF-1 (truncated IGF-1 analog)
    same: Both are IGFBP-evading IGF-1 analogs used as research 'super-potent' IGF agonists; both engage IGF1R.
    different: des(1-3) lacks the first 3 N-terminal residues (no Long extension, no R3 substitution). In macrophage-precursor assays the analogs diverged from rhIGF-1 and from each other in differentiation effects, indicating non-identical biology despite shared IGFBP evasion (PMID 9867252).
  • vs Species-specific recombinant IGF-1 (e.g. ovine oIGF-1)
    same: Same receptor and anabolic/organ-growth signaling endpoints.
    different: oIGF-1 was engineered specifically because LR3's non-native sequence and IGFBP evasion remove physiologic bioavailability regulation; matched-species native IGF-1 retains IGFBP regulation while still promoting organ growth (PMID 36091374).

History & legal status

"Origin: Long-R3-IGF-I was engineered in the late 1980s-1990s (much of the foundational characterization came from the Cooperative Research Centre for Tissue Growth and Repair / University of Adelaide group - e.g. Bryant, Read, Wallace, Ballard, Tomas; PMIDs 8919033, 7561636) as a deliberately IGFBP-resistant, super-potent IGF-1 analog. Its primary legitimate uses are as a cell-culture supplement (boosting proliferation/yield in bioprocessing and research) and as a research tool in animal growth physiology; recombinant production systems continue to be optimized (e.g. Pichia pastoris fusion expression, PMID 37261455). Regulatory status: LR3 IGF-1 is NOT approved by FDA/EMA for any human therapeutic indication - the approved IGF-1 human drug is native rhIGF-1 (mecasermin/Increlex) for severe primary IGF-1 deficiency, plus the now-withdrawn mecasermin rinfabate/iPlex (PMID 19627167). LR3 sold for physique/performance use is a research chemical, not a pharmaceutical; IGF-1 and its analogs are prohibited in sport (WADA Prohibited List, class S2 peptide hormones/growth factors) [Established; WADA listing not separately retrieved as a PMID this run - flag for verification]. No retraction issues identified in the cited primary sources."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No human pharmacokinetics for LR3 itself: true circulating half-life, free-fraction kinetics, and whether 'long-acting' reflects longer plasma t1/2 or simply a larger unbuffered active fraction remain unquantified in humans.
  • No controlled human dose-response or safety data: every human-relevant safety inference borrows from native rhIGF-1; the dose at which LR3 causes hypoglycemia, organomegaly, or proliferative effects in humans is unknown.
  • Quantitative IGF1R binding for LR3: an explicit human IGF1R Kd/Ki and insulin-receptor/hybrid cross-reactivity for LR3 were not located - potency is inferred mainly from IGFBP evasion rather than measured receptor affinity.
  • Net cancer risk in humans from exogenous LR3: epidemiology links endogenous IGF-I to prostate cancer and rodent data show LR3 accelerates tumor growth, but the magnitude of risk from intermittent exogenous LR3 dosing is unquantified.
  • Species translation: effects flip between anabolic (rats/myoblasts) and catabolic/growth-inhibiting (pigs) and are often visceral-organ-selective - which human phenotype dominates is unknown.
  • Long-term structural reversibility: whether IGFBP-unregulated chronic IGF1R agonism causes persistent cardiac/visceral hypertrophy or fibrosis in humans is untested.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only (NOT a protocol, NO dosing guidance). Given the mechanisms above, the signs/labs a clinician would watch include: (1) Blood glucose - hypoglycemia is the most consistent IGF-1 adverse effect; symptomatic shakiness/sweating/confusion are red flags (PMIDs 9483305, 19198769). (2) HbA1c and fasting insulin over time - insulin-like signaling and altered insulin sensitivity. (3) The endogenous somatotropic axis - GH, total IGF-1 and IGFBP-3 can be SUPPRESSED by exogenous IGF activity (PMID 9488001), so a low endogenous IGF-1 does not mean low IGF signaling. (4) Cancer surveillance/vigilance - IGF-I is prospectively linked to prostate cancer (PMID 26921328) and LR3 accelerated tumors in mice (PMID 31285507); any new lump, unexplained weight change, or in men age-appropriate prostate evaluation warrant attention. (5) Signs of disordered/visceral growth - acromegaly-type features (facial coarsening, soft-tissue/organ enlargement), and cardiac symptoms given organ-selective growth signals (PMIDs 7561636, 36091374). (6) Lymphoid hyperplasia - tonsillar/adenoidal enlargement, sleep-disordered breathing (PMID 19198769). (7) Fundoscopic/retinal awareness - retinopathy concern was a reason IGF-1 insulin-sensitizer trials were halted (PMID 19198769). Escalate any of these to a physician; this is what to watch for, not endorsement of use."

Caveats

CRITICAL ACCURACY NOTES FOR THE SCRIPT: (1) Human-data gap is the headline — there are ZERO published human muscle/strength/body-comp studies on Long R3 IGF-1; do not let any animation imply proven human muscle gains. Mecasermin (plain rhIGF-1) is approved for a rare deficiency, but that is NOT LR3 and is not a bodybuilding indication. (2) The whole mechanism chain (steps 2–6) is grounded in rodent/livestock/in-vitro work — label it that way on screen. (3) The popular bro-science claim that injecting LR3 causes 'local-only' or 'site-specific' muscle growth is contradicted by the systemic-growth animal data (PMID 7561636) and is at best Contested; the forum quote in the corpus arguing 'no difference between rhIGF-1 and LR3 at the injection site' is anecdote, not evidence. (4) Half-life numbers (commonly '20–30 h') trace to preclinical pharmacokinetics, not human dosing trials — present as approximate/preclinical. (5) Real risks to mention honestly: hypoglycemia (insulin-receptor spillover, PMID 7683875), suppression of the body's own GH/IGF-1 axis (PMID 9488001), unregulated/contaminated gray-market product quality (PMID 20675162 documents a seized black-market batch), and theoretical tumor-promotion via IGF-1R (PMID 12843187) — frame the last as Speculative for LR3 specifically. (6) NEVER state a human dose as safe or effective; no such evidence exists.
