Vosilasarm

The community's "strongest SARM" is really RAD140/Testolone under its official name — a potent oral androgen-receptor agonist whose only human data come from small breast-cancer trials, not muscle-building, and those trials already show a prominent liver-enzyme signal.

Limited human data 8 citations
Class
Non-steroidal selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM); aryl-oxadiazole/cyanophenyl chemotype
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR, NR3C4) — high-affinity, tissue-selective agonist (human AR Ki ~7 nM; functional AR agonist EC50 ~0
Human status
PIPELINE / early clinical — no marketing approval anywhere. Human experience is limited to breast-cancer studies of the same molecule under different names. The first-in-human Phase 1 (NCT03088527; RAD140; Radius Health, later Stemline; n=22, postmenopausal ER+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer; COMPLETED 2020) IS PUBLISHED (LoRusso et al 2022, Clinical Breast Cancer, DOI 10.1016/j.clbc.2021.08.003): oral doses 50/100/150 mg once daily, MTD 100 mg/day, human terminal half-life ~44.7 h, frequent liver-enzyme elevations, clinical benefit rate 18.2% at 24 weeks and median PFS 2.3 months. It is now developed as EP0062 (Ellipses Pharma) in a modular Phase 1/2 study (NCT05573126) in AR+/HER2-/ER+ metastatic breast cancer — RECRUITING; the Phase 1 dose-finding module (Module A) has completed with an optimal dose selected, and Phase 2 expansion (including combinations with elacestrant, abemaciclib, everolimus, fulvestrant, exemestane) is ongoing with no efficacy results posted yet. It has NEVER been tested in a trial for muscle-building, athletic performance, or in healthy men. Non-clinical 'Testolone' use is via unregulated gray-market research chemicals; SARMs including RAD-140 are WADA-prohibited (S1.2 anabolic agents, banned since 2008) and are not legal dietary-supplement ingredients.

At a glance

  • Potent AR activation (in-vitro EC50 ~0.1 nM, Ki ~7 nM) — the pharmacologic basis for the 'strongest SARM' label
  • Increases muscle mass and bone mineral density in rodents (primate data show body-weight change but weak lean-tissue signal; not confirmed in healthy humans)
  • Antitumor activity in ER+/AR+ breast cancer models — the reason it is in human trials; a completed Phase 1 showed only modest activity (clinical benefit rate ~18%, median PFS 2.3 months)
  • Confirmed human AR engagement in the breast-cancer trial (SHBG fell, PSA rose); predicted to suppress endogenous testosterone/LH/FSH → possible low-T, testicular atrophy, fertility impact in men
  • Liver-enzyme elevations are common — seen both in the monitored clinical trial (AST ~59%, ALT ~46%) and as drug-induced liver injury in real-world consumers
  • Not aromatized to estrogen and not 5α-reduced to DHT (non-steroidal scaffold); long human half-life (~44.7 h)
  • No approved indication anywhere; human data limited to small oncology safety/PK studies; WADA-prohibited

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Identity: Vosilasarm IS RAD140 / Testolone / EP0062 Established

    Vosilasarm is not a new molecule. It is the International Nonproprietary Name (INN) assigned to RAD140 — the compound the fitness community calls 'Testolone' and buys as a research chemical. ChEMBL lists 'Vosilasarm', 'RAD-140', 'RAD140' and 'Testolone' as synonyms for one and the same structure (CHEMBL1672635, C20H16ClN5O2), and the clinical program now runs it as 'EP0062'. So the 'strongest SARM' the forums discuss and the 'pipeline breast-cancer drug' are the identical molecule under different names. ChEMBL CHEMBL1672635 (synonyms: Vosilasarm, RAD-140, Testolone); Miller et al 2011 ACS Med Chem Lett, PMID 24900290, DOI 10.1021/ml1002508

  2. Binds the androgen receptor with high affinity Established

    Vosilasarm slips into the ligand-binding pocket of the androgen receptor (AR), the same pocket used by testosterone and DHT. It binds tightly (human AR Ki ~7 nM in an R1881-displacement assay) and, being a non-steroidal small molecule, is not a substrate for 5-alpha-reductase or aromatase — it cannot be converted to DHT or estrogen. It is comparatively clean at the related progesterone receptor (IC50 ~750 nM, roughly 100-fold weaker than at AR). ChEMBL bioactivity CHEMBL1672635 (human AR Ki=7 nM, pChEMBL 8.15; PR IC50=750 nM, pChEMBL 6.12), source Miller et al 2011 PMID 24900290

  3. Activates AR as a potent agonist and drives gene transcription Established

    Once bound, vosilasarm acts as an agonist: the ligand-AR complex sheds chaperone proteins, translocates into the nucleus, dimerizes, and docks onto androgen-response elements in DNA to switch androgen-target genes on. In a cell-based AR functional assay (mouse C2C12 cells, osteoblast-differentiation readout) it was extremely potent (EC50 ~0.1 nM), which is the pharmacological basis for the 'strongest SARM' reputation. Note this is in-vitro potency, not proof of superior real-world muscle gain. ChEMBL bioactivity CHEMBL1672635 (AR agonist EC50=0.1 nM in C2C12 cells, pChEMBL 10), Miller et al 2011 PMID 24900290

  4. Tissue-selectivity: strong on muscle/bone, weaker on prostate (in animals) Evidence-rodent

    The 'selective' in SARM means it activates AR-driven anabolic genes in muscle and bone more than in prostate. In the original rat work, vosilasarm increased the weight of the levator ani (an androgen-responsive muscle) at low oral doses (active from ~0.03–0.5 mg/kg/day) while oral dosing failed to increase prostate weight even at 30 mg/kg. This tissue split is thought to come from differential AR co-regulator recruitment in different tissues. The clean separation is documented in rodents (and, for AR engagement, in primates), not confirmed in humans. Miller et al 2011 PMID 24900290 (Hershberger/levator ani vs prostate; ChEMBL assays CHEMBL1679748-54)

  5. Anabolic output: lean/muscle and bone signal (mostly rodent) Evidence-rodent

    Downstream of AR activation, the intended effects are increased skeletal-muscle protein synthesis, lean mass and favorable bone effects. This is well demonstrated in rats — RAD140 raises muscle mass and bone mineral density in rodent models. Caution on primates: in the discovery study, oral vosilasarm increased cynomolgus-monkey body weight but did NOT significantly change lean-tissue or fat-mass readouts in that assay, so the primate 'lean mass up' story is weaker than often implied. Crucially, an anabolic muscle-growth benefit has never been demonstrated in a healthy-human trial. Miller et al 2011 PMID 24900290 (cynomolgus lean-tissue/fat 'Not active', body weight increased; ChEMBL CHEMBL1679755-58); rodent muscle/BMD Puskas et al 2025 Physiol Rep PMID 40680216, DOI 10.14814/phy2.70463

  6. In breast cancer, AR agonism opposes estrogen-driven growth Evidence-rodent

    The reason a 'muscle drug' is in oncology trials: AR is expressed in roughly 75% of estrogen-receptor-positive (ER+) breast cancers, and switching AR on can antagonize ER-driven proliferation. In ER+ patient-derived xenografts, vosilasarm/EP0062 showed antitumor activity comparable to selective estrogen receptor degraders (SERDs), including in tumors with ESR1, PIK3CA or PTEN mutations, by inducing AR-target genes and lowering S-phase cell-cycle proteins; adding palbociclib enhanced the effect in some resistant tumors. This is the actual clinical rationale being tested — distinct from bodybuilding use. Monserrat et al 2026, npj Breast Cancer, PMID 42310300, DOI 10.1038/s41523-026-00993-2

  7. Suppresses the body's own testosterone (HPG feedback) Established

    Because vosilasarm is an AR agonist, central feedback reads it as 'plenty of androgen around' and dials down the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis: GnRH, then LH and FSH fall, so the testes cut their own testosterone output and sperm production. HPG suppression is an Established consequence of any androgen/SARM, not a special toxicity. Direct human proof of AR engagement exists from the breast-cancer trial (SHBG fell in 18/18 patients and PSA rose in 16/20), but that trial was in postmenopausal women, so the specific depth and reversibility of testosterone suppression in men taking vosilasarm has not been formally characterized. HPG-axis feedback (curated AR MoA; SARMs suppress HPG); human AR-engagement surrogates (SHBG↓, PSA↑) LoRusso et al 2022 Clin Breast Cancer PMID 34565686, DOI 10.1016/j.clbc.2021.08.003

  8. Hepatotoxicity signal — in both real-world users AND the monitored trial Evidence-human

    Two independent lines of evidence flag the liver. (1) In the published first-in-human Phase 1 trial of pharmaceutical-grade drug, treatment-emergent AST elevation occurred in 59%, ALT in 46% and raised bilirubin in 27% of patients, with Grade 3/4 AST/ALT elevations — so a hepatic signal appears even with quality-controlled material and monitoring. (2) Multiple case reports describe cholestatic or mixed drug-induced liver injury (jaundice, itching, high enzymes) in young men who took over-the-counter 'RAD-140/Testolone' for weeks to months, usually resolving after stopping. The exact molecular mechanism is not fully worked out, and gray-market adulteration can compound attribution in consumers, but the enzyme signal is now documented in controlled dosing too. Phase 1 hepatic AEs LoRusso et al 2022 PMID 34565686; consumer DILI Barbara/Leung et al 2022 Ochsner J PMID 36561105; SARM DILI PMID 39328701, PMID 36945289

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. High-affinity, non-steroidal androgen receptor (AR/NR3C4) agonist. Human AR binding Ki ~7 nM (pChEMBL 8.15, R1881-displacement assay); functional AR agonist EC50 ~0.1 nM in a mouse C2C12 osteoblast-differentiation readout (pChEMBL 10). Modest off-target progesterone-receptor binding (IC50 ~750 nM, pChEMBL 6.12, ~100× weaker than AR). Not a substrate for aromatase (no estrogen conversion) or 5α-reductase (no DHT conversion) because it is non-steroidal. Molecular formula C20H16ClN5O2, MW ~393.8, aryl-oxadiazole/cyanophenyl scaffold, single stereoisomer, zero Rule-of-Five violations (QED 0.67).

Signalling cascade. Ligand binds AR ligand-binding domain → chaperone (HSP90) dissociation → conformational change → nuclear translocation → AR homodimerization → binding to androgen-response elements → tissue-selective co-regulator recruitment → transcription of anabolic (muscle/bone) genes; in ER+ breast cancer, AR activation induces AR-target genes that antagonize estrogen-receptor–driven proliferation and reduce S-phase cell-cycle proteins. Central AR agonism triggers negative feedback that lowers GnRH/LH/FSH. Human AR engagement is evidenced clinically by falling SHBG and rising PSA in treated patients.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral (developed as an orally bioavailable SARM; clinical oncology dosing is oral, once daily).
Absorption / bioavailability
Preclinical oral bioavailability 27% in rat and 65% in rhesus monkey (Macaca mulatta). No formal healthy-human bioavailability figure published; human once-daily dosing was supported by the long half-life measured in the Phase 1 trial.
Half-life
Human terminal half-life ~44.7 hours, measured in the published first-in-human Phase 1 trial (LoRusso et al 2022), supporting once-daily dosing. This supersedes earlier community estimates and any 'unknown' status.
Metabolism
Hepatic metabolism. In-vitro microsomal half-life reported as >2 h in rat, monkey and human microsomes (screening-level, not a clinical value). The EP0062 protocol excludes strong CYP3A4 inhibitors/inducers, implying meaningful CYP3A4 involvement and a drug-interaction liability.
Excretion
Human excretion route not fully characterized in the public literature; metabolism/elimination has been studied for doping-control purposes.
Notes
Once-daily oral dosing. CYP3A4-relevant drug interactions likely; QTc monitoring is built into the clinical protocol (screening excludes QTcF >470 ms and concomitant QT-prolonging drugs).

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

Preclinically, anabolic (muscle/levator-ani) effects appeared at low oral doses (~0.03–0.5 mg/kg/day in rats) while oral prostate stimulation was absent even at 30 mg/kg — the basis of the tissue-selectivity claim. In humans, dosing has only been explored as dose-escalation/MTD work in cancer patients: the Phase 1 (NCT03088527) tested 50/100/150 mg once daily with an MTD of 100 mg/day, and the EP0062 Phase 1/2 (NCT05573126) Module A completed dose-finding with an optimal dose selected. NO validated human dose-response for muscle building exists, and none is provided here (harm-reduction policy: no protocols). Gray-market 'Testolone' products carry unverified label doses and purity, so any consumer 'dose' is unreliable.

How it compares

  • vs Testosterone / anabolic-androgenic steroids
    same: Both are AR agonists that engage the same receptor and suppress endogenous testosterone.
    different: Vosilasarm is non-steroidal, oral, not aromatized (no estrogen) and not 5α-reduced (no DHT); it is more tissue-selective in animals, but it still suppresses the HPG axis, shows a prominent liver-enzyme signal, and lacks the decades of human safety data testosterone has. Unlike TRT it has no approved indication.
  • vs Other SARMs (e.g., ostarine/enobosarm, ligandrol/LGD-4033)
    same: Shared class mechanism (AR agonism, tissue-selectivity claim), shared unresolved hepatotoxicity/cardiovascular concerns, WADA-prohibited since 2008, none approved.
    different: Vosilasarm/RAD140 has higher in-vitro AR potency (EC50 ~0.1 nM) and a long human half-life (~44.7 h); its liver-injury reports are relatively prominent. But no completed human trial shows it beats other SARMs for muscle gain, and enobosarm has larger, later-stage human trial programs.
  • vs 'EP0062' the oncology drug vs 'Testolone' the gray-market SARM
    same: Identical active compound.
    different: Nothing chemically — they are the SAME molecule (vosilasarm, CHEMBL1672635). The difference is context: EP0062 is a purity-controlled investigational drug given to monitored cancer patients; 'Testolone' is unregulated product of unknown purity used off-label by athletes.

History &amp; legal status

Discovered and developed by Radius Health (Miller et al 2011) as RAD140, aimed initially at muscle wasting/cachexia and hormone-receptor-positive breast cancer. The first-in-human Phase 1 (NCT03088527) ran 2017–2020 in ER+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer under Radius/later Stemline Therapeutics and was published (LoRusso et al 2022). Rights moved into the EP0062 program (Ellipses Pharma), now in a Phase 1/2 breast-cancer study (NCT05573126) with Module A completed. The INN 'vosilasarm' was assigned to formalize the name. Regulatory status: NOT approved for any indication in any jurisdiction. SARMs including RAD-140 are prohibited in sport (WADA Prohibited List, S1.2 other anabolic agents; banned since 2008) and are not lawful dietary-supplement ingredients; the U.S. FDA has warned consumers about SARM-containing products. It remains widely sold as a gray-market 'research chemical.'

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Whether the animal tissue-selectivity (muscle > prostate) and rodent muscle/bone anabolism translate to humans
  • Any efficacy for muscle growth or physical function in healthy humans — never tested in a completed trial
  • Depth, duration and reversibility of testosterone suppression specifically in men (the human trial was in postmenopausal women)
  • The precise molecular mechanism and true incidence of clinically significant liver injury (trial enzyme elevations were frequent; consumer DILI may be compounded by impurity/adulteration)
  • Long-term cardiovascular, lipid, QT and prostate safety
  • Clinical efficacy in ER+/AR+ breast cancer (Phase 1 showed modest activity; Phase 2 ongoing, results pending)
  • How gray-market 'Testolone' purity/dose compares to pharmaceutical-grade EP0062 material
  • Full human absorption/excretion mass-balance (only partially characterized publicly)

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Educational/harm-reduction only — not a recommendation to use, and no dosing is provided. For a clinician evaluating someone who reports taking 'RAD140/Testolone/vosilasarm': (1) LIVER — check ALT, AST, ALP and bilirubin, and have a low threshold to investigate jaundice, dark urine, pale stools, pruritus or right-upper-quadrant pain and to stop the compound; note that transaminase elevations were common even in the monitored clinical trial, and published consumer DILI cases resolved on cessation. (2) HORMONES (especially men) — total/free testosterone, LH, FSH, and (if fertility matters) a semen analysis, since HPG suppression is expected; counsel that recovery timing is uncertain. (3) CARDIOVASCULAR/METABOLIC — lipid panel (HDL can fall), blood pressure, and an ECG/QTc given the QT signal flagged in the clinical protocol, especially with other QT-prolonging drugs. (4) DRUG INTERACTIONS — probable CYP3A4 involvement means caution with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors/inducers. (5) PRODUCT RISK — counsel that gray-market SARMs are frequently mislabeled/adulterated, so lab abnormalities may reflect contaminants. (6) SPORT — advise athletes it is WADA-prohibited (banned since 2008) and will trigger a positive test. Escalate to hepatology for significant transaminase/bilirubin rise and to endocrinology for persistent hypogonadism.

Caveats

Biggest caveat: 'Vosilasarm' sounds like a brand-new cutting-edge drug, but it is literally RAD140/Testolone renamed — the community hype ('strongest SARM') and the 'pipeline compound' are the same molecule, and the human data are in breast-cancer patients, not athletes. Contrary to a common claim, human data DO exist: a completed Phase 1 is published, giving a human half-life (~44.7 h) and safety profile — and that profile is not reassuring for muscle-seekers, since liver-enzyme elevations were frequent even with pharmaceutical-grade drug and monitoring, and antitumor efficacy was modest. High test-tube potency (EC50 ~0.1 nM) does NOT equal proven superior muscle growth in humans; there is no human efficacy data for muscle-building and no head-to-head versus other SARMs or testosterone. The muscle/bone anabolic effect is best shown in rodents; primate data show a weaker lean-tissue signal than often stated, and human muscle-growth efficacy is untested. Testosterone suppression is expected in men but its depth/reversibility for this specific compound is not characterized (the human trial was in postmenopausal women). Gray-market 'Testolone' adds uncertain purity/adulteration on top of the intrinsic risks.
