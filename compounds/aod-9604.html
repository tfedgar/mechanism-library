AOD-9604

Main target: Adipocyte lipid-handling enzymes. Evidence is animal- and lab-only — no human trials yet.

EvidenceRodent / preclinical
ApprovalNot FDA-approved

A 15-amino-acid tail-end fragment of growth hormone, engineered to keep GH's fat-burning trick while ditching its blood-sugar baggage — but the human weight-loss promise was never delivered.

Rodent / preclinical 13 citations
Class
Synthetic peptide; modified C-terminal lipolytic fragment of human growth hormone (hGH 177-191 analog with an added N-terminal tyrosine)
Primary target
Adipocyte lipid-handling enzymes — stimulates hormone-sensitive lipase (HSL, fat breakdown) and inhibits acetyl-CoA carboxylase / lipogenic enzymes (fat storage)
Human status
Investigational / preclinical for almost everything claimed. The lipolytic and antilipogenic mechanism is well-characterized in rodent and ex-vivo human adipose tissue, but there is NO published peer-reviewed RCT demonstrating clinically meaningful weight loss or fat loss in living humans. Metabolic Pharmaceuticals ran Phase I/II obesity trials (~2001-2007); a 2004 review (PMID 15134286) confirms only that Phase IIa was underway. The program was discontinued for obesity — widely reported as failing to beat placebo on meaningful weight loss. It was later repositioned toward osteoarthritis/cartilage (rabbit data only, PMID 26275694). AOD-9604 holds a US GRAS self-affirmation as a food additive (a regulatory category, NOT an efficacy or safety endorsement for injection/fat loss). It is a WADA-prohibited substance in sport. Bottom line for viewers: the fat-loss claims rest on animal and test-tube data, not human outcome trials.

At a glance

  • In rodents and isolated (including human) fat tissue: increases lipolysis (fat breakdown) and decreases lipogenesis (fat storage), reducing body-weight gain and fat mass over weeks of dosing
  • Acutely raises energy expenditure and fat oxidation in mice — even in β3-AR knockout mice, showing the acute effect does not require β3-AR
  • Upregulates β3-AR expression in obese-mouse fat, restoring it toward lean levels, which may amplify lipolytic sensitivity over time
  • Unlike full growth hormone, did NOT cause insulin resistance or glucose intolerance in animal studies — the headline selling point of the fragment
  • No proven, clinically meaningful fat loss or weight loss in humans from any published controlled trial
  • Does not raise IGF-1 or act as a growth-promoting/anabolic agent — it is not 'GH in a vial'

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

6 steps1 human / established 4 rodent / preclinical 1 contested / speculative

  1. It's the fat-burning tail of growth hormone, isolated Established

    Full growth hormone (191 amino acids) has its fat-metabolism activity concentrated in a short stretch at the very end — residues 177-191. Researchers showed this synthetic 15-residue tail reproduces GH's anti-fat-storage (antilipogenic) action on its own. AOD-9604 is this fragment with an added tyrosine on the front for stability. So it's a deliberately stripped-down 'just the lipolytic part' molecule. AOD-9604-specific: PMID 11146367; β3-adrenergic/lipolysis PMID 11713213

  2. Inside the fat cell: turns up the breakdown enzyme, turns down the storage enzyme Evidence-rodent

    In isolated rat (and human) fat tissue, the fragment activates hormone-sensitive lipase — the enzyme that breaks stored triglyceride into free fatty acids and glycerol — and inhibits acetyl-CoA carboxylase, a key fat-building (lipogenic) enzyme. Net effect: the cell tips from storing fat toward releasing/burning it, mimicking what intact GH does to lipid handling. Ng et al. 2000, J Mol Endocrinol (PMID 11116208); Heffernan et al. 2000, Am J Physiol Endocrinol Metab (PMID 10950816)

  3. Burns more fat WITHOUT needing the β3 'fat-burning' receptor Evidence-rodent

    Forums often say AOD-9604 'works through the β3-adrenergic receptor.' The actual experiment shows the opposite for the acute effect: in mice genetically lacking β3-AR, the fragment STILL increased energy expenditure and fat oxidation. So the immediate fat-oxidation effect is not directly dependent on β3-AR — the precise molecular handshake it uses is not fully pinned down. Heffernan et al. 2001, Endocrinology (PMID 11713213)

  4. But it does crank UP β3-receptor numbers over time Evidence-rodent

    Where β3-AR matters is the longer game: chronic dosing raised β3-AR gene expression in obese-mouse fat back toward lean-mouse levels, and the chronic weight/lipolysis effect WAS lost in β3-AR knockout mice. So the model is — the fragment acutely burns fat by another route, and separately restores β3-AR expression, which then amplifies lipolytic sensitivity for the sustained effect. This is the nuance hype videos collapse into 'it's a β3 agonist' (it isn't). Heffernan et al. 2001, Endocrinology (PMID 11713213)

  5. The selling point: fat-burning minus the blood-sugar damage Evidence-rodent

    Real growth hormone causes insulin resistance and glucose intolerance — a major downside. The whole pitch for AOD-9604 is that, in animals, chronic treatment cut fat WITHOUT raising insulin resistance or impairing glucose tolerance (confirmed by euglycemic clamp). Mechanistically, the diabetogenic 'punch' of GH lives in a different part of the molecule, so this fragment leaves it behind. Ng et al. 2000, Horm Res (PMID 11146367); Ng et al. 2000, J Mol Endocrinol (PMID 11116208)

  6. Not anabolic, not 'GH lite' — and not proven in people Contested

    AOD-9604 does not raise IGF-1 and is not muscle-building. Critically, the entire chain above is rodent and test-tube work plus ex-vivo human fat samples — there is no published human trial showing real-world fat loss. The obesity drug program was run and then dropped; the regulatory 'GRAS' food-additive status is not evidence that injecting it burns fat. Treat human fat-loss claims as unproven. Wilding 2004, Curr Opin Investig Drugs (PMID 15134286); Heffernan et al. 2000 (PMID 10950816)

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. AOD-9604 is a 16-residue synthetic peptide: a tyrosine (Tyr) added to the N-terminus of the C-terminal lipolytic domain of human growth hormone, hGH 177-191 (sequence Tyr-Leu-Arg-Ile-Val-Gln-Cys-Arg-Ser-Val-Glu-Gly-Ser-Cys-Gly-Phe), MW ~1817 Da. ChEMBL lists it as CHEMBL6068615 / LAT-8881, molecule_type Protein, max clinical phase 2. CRITICAL BINDING FACT (Established, in vitro): AOD-9604 does NOT bind or compete at the growth hormone receptor (GHR) and does NOT induce GHR-dependent cell proliferation — in BaF-B03 cells transfected with hGH receptor it failed to displace 125I-hGH or drive proliferation (Heffernan 2001, PMID 11673763). It is therefore NOT a GH agonist and does not raise IG-1. It is ALSO NOT a direct β3-adrenergic receptor (β3-AR) agonist: in β3-AR knockout mice the chronic weight/lipolysis effect was abolished, yet AOD-9604 still acutely raised energy expenditure/fat oxidation (Heffernan 2001, PMID 11713213) — so β3-AR is required for the chronic anti-obesity phenotype but is not the primary receptor AOD-9604 occupies. No validated high-affinity receptor and NO published Ki/EC50/IC50 for any named receptor exists — the molecular target that initiates its signal is genuinely unidentified (Contested/Speculative). The frequent online claim that it 'binds β3-AR' is not supported by the knockout data.

Signalling cascade. Proposed cascade (mechanism is incompletely defined). 1) Lipolysis arm (Evidence-rodent/in vitro): AOD-9604 / its parent fragment AOD-9401 stimulate hormone-sensitive lipase (HSL) activity and trigger diacylglycerol release in isolated rat adipocytes, mimicking intact hGH (Ng 2000, PMID 11116208); the canonical downstream step is HSL/ATGL hydrolysis of triglyceride to glycerol + free fatty acids, and treated animals show raised plasma glycerol (an index of lipolysis) (Heffernan 2001, PMID 11673763). 2) Anti-lipogenesis arm (Evidence-rodent/in vitro): the fragment INHIBITS acetyl-CoA carboxylase (ACC), the rate-limiting lipogenic enzyme, reducing de-novo fatty-acid synthesis — the 177-191 sequence is reported as primarily ANTI-LIPOGENIC, with weaker direct lipolytic effect in some assays (Wu & Ng 1993, PMID 8358331; Ng 2000, PMID 11116208). 3) β3-AR upregulation arm (Evidence-rodent): chronic AOD-9604 restores suppressed β3-AR mRNA in obese-mouse fat toward lean levels; β3-AR is then required to sustain the lipolytic/thermogenic phenotype (β3-AR knockout abolishes the chronic effect) (Heffernan 2001, PMID 11713213). Net effect in rodents: increased fat oxidation, increased resting energy expenditure, smaller adipocytes, lower body-weight gain — WITHOUT the hyperglycemia/insulin resistance caused by full hGH.

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Investigated clinically as an ORAL tablet (sponsor Metabolic Pharmaceuticals/Calzada) and also studied as oral solution; gray-market/peptide-vendor use is overwhelmingly subcutaneous injection of reconstituted lyophilized powder. No approved formulation exists (no ester, no prodrug).
Absorption / bioavailability
Established in rodents that an ORAL dose is biologically active: oral AOD-9401/AOD-9604 reduced lipogenesis and weight gain in ob/ob mice and Zucker rats at ~500 µg/kg/day (Heffernan 2000, PMID 10950816; Ng 2000 PMID 11146367) — unusual for a peptide and the basis of the oral obesity program. Quantitative human oral bioavailability is not published; as a 16-mer peptide, systemic absorption is expected to be low and variable. Injected (SC) absorption parameters in humans are not formally published.
Half-life
Short. Parent AOD-9604 is rapidly degraded in serum/urine in vitro (Cox 2015, PMID 25208511). Plasma half-life is on the order of minutes (commonly cited ~30 min range) — peptide, not protein, kinetics; no rigorously published human t1/2 value (Contested).
Metabolism
Proteolytic, not CYP-mediated. In vitro incubation in human serum and urine generated six metabolites; the disulfide bond (Cys6-Cys13) is a key structural feature, and one internal fragment, CRSVEGSCG, is markedly more stable than the parent and other metabolites (Cox 2015, PMID 25208511). Degradation is by serum/tissue peptidases; the stable CRSVEGSCG metabolite is the anti-doping target marker.
Excretion
Renal — parent peptide and metabolites are detectable in urine (limit of detection ~50 pg/mL by LC-MS/MS); urinary CRSVEGSCG extends the detection window (Cox 2015, PMID 25208511).
Notes
No BBB penetration data; no ester/depot forms. Because it is rapidly cleared and not GHR-active, it does NOT raise serum IGF-1 and does NOT trigger the WADA hGH isoform immunoassay (Orlovius 2013, PMID 24124033) — but it IS independently detectable by targeted LC-MS/MS, so it is not 'undetectable'.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

Rodent dose-response is the only quantitative dataset. ANIMAL: Oral AOD-9604 ~500 µg/kg/day for 19 days in obese Zucker rats reduced body-weight GAIN by >50% (15.8 vs 35.6 g) and raised adipose lipolytic activity without impairing insulin sensitivity on euglycemic clamp (Ng 2000, PMID 11146367). In ob/ob mice, oral AOD-9401 (~analogous fragment) lowered weight gain from day 16, cut lipogenic activity, and acutely raised energy expenditure and fat oxidation (Heffernan 2000, PMID 10950816). Chronic AOD-9604 in Zucker rats shrank mean adipocyte diameter from ~110 to ~80 µm (Ng 2000, PMID 11116208). Intra-articular AOD9604 0.25 mg/knee weekly aided cartilage repair in a rabbit OA model — an unrelated off-target use (Kwon 2015, PMID 26275694). HUMAN: dose-response is essentially UNKNOWN/UNPROVEN for fat loss. The drug reached Phase 2 obesity trials (Wilding 2004, PMID 15134286; ChEMBL max_phase 2), and the sponsor publicly reported that the pivotal ~12-week obese-patient RCT did NOT achieve statistically significant weight loss versus placebo — but that pivotal trial appears to have NOT been published in PubMed (see key_unknowns). Commonly circulated injectable doses (~300 µg, e.g. ~250-500 µg) come from vendors/anecdote, NOT from any controlled human efficacy study. There is no validated human dose that produces a measurable fat-loss effect. Certainty: Evidence-rodent for the rodent numbers; Contested/absent for any human fat-loss dose-response.

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Intact human growth hormone / somatropin (the parent molecule)
    same: Both reproduce the lipolytic + anti-lipogenic effect on adipocytes (HSL stimulation, ACC inhibition, raised fat oxidation); AOD-9604 is literally hGH's C-terminal 177-191 domain plus an N-terminal Tyr.
    different: hGH acts through the GH receptor → JAK2/STAT5 → IGF-1, giving anabolism, organ/tissue growth, fluid retention AND diabetogenic insulin antagonism. AOD-9604 does NOT bind GHR, does NOT raise IGF-1, and (in rodents) does NOT cause hyperglycemia or insulin resistance — it keeps the fat effect, drops the growth and glucose effects, and loses the anabolic/recovery benefit.
  • vs hGH fragment 176-191 ('HGH frag 176-191')
    same: Nearly identical concept — a C-terminal GH lipolytic fragment marketed for fat loss; same proposed HSL/anti-lipogenic mechanism and same human-efficacy gap.
    different: AOD-9604 = 177-191 with a stabilizing N-terminal tyrosine added (a defined, patented, Phase-2 clinical molecule with a published metabolism/detection profile). 'Frag 176-191' as sold is the bare fragment without the engineered Tyr modification; vendor products are frequently mislabeled and the two are routinely conflated in fitness content.
  • vs Tesamorelin (Egrifta, a GHRH analog)
    same: Both are peptides marketed/used to reduce fat (tesamorelin specifically visceral fat in HIV lipodystrophy) and both sit in the GH-axis 'fat-loss peptide' category.
    different: Tesamorelin is an FDA-APPROVED GHRH-receptor agonist that works by raising endogenous GH and IGF-1 (so it carries GH-class effects: IGF-1 rise, fluid retention, glucose effects, and has proven human efficacy with RCT data). AOD-9604 acts downstream/peripherally with NO GH-axis stimulation, NO IGF-1 rise, and NO proven human fat-loss efficacy.
  • vs β3-adrenergic agonists (e.g. mirabegron)
    same: Both ultimately drive adipocyte lipolysis/thermogenesis via the β3-AR pathway, and AOD-9604's chronic effect REQUIRES functional β3-AR (knockout abolishes it).
    different: Mirabegron is a direct β3-AR agonist (defined receptor, defined EC50, approved for overactive bladder, studied for brown-fat thermogenesis). AOD-9604 is NOT a direct β3 agonist — it upregulates β3-AR expression and acts through an unidentified upstream target, so its receptor pharmacology is undefined.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • MK-677 adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-rodent
    On the lipolytic axis the two are redundant — both route fat mobilisation through GH's C-terminal lipolytic domain (AOD-9604 IS that fragment; MK-677 raises endogenous GH/IGF-1). The genuinely additive interaction is on GLUCOSE handling: AOD-9604's whole selling point is fat mobilisation WITHOUT insulin resistance (unlike full GH), whereas MK-677 reliably RAISES GH/IGF-1 and reduces insulin sensitivity / raises fasting glucose. Adding MK-677 therefore reintroduces exactly the diabetogenic GH effect AOD-9604 was engineered to leave behind — MK-677 supplies a metabolic side effect AOD lacks, rather than AOD-9604 opposing MK-677's action. Net: no clean-metabolic advantage from AOD's side is preserved once MK-677 is present. AOD-9604 monograph (no insulin resistance vs GH; PMID 11673763, PMID 11146367); MK-677 monograph (raises IGF-1 ~40-90%, raises fasting glucose / reduces insulin sensitivity) Watch: Fasting glucose, HbA1c and fasting insulin — MK-677 can raise them; also IGF-1 (MK-677-driven, not AOD). Watch for the appetite increase and water retention MK-677 adds on top.
  • Clenbuterol boosts ↑ Evidence-rodent
    Convergent adipocyte lipolysis. Clenbuterol is a β2-adrenergic agonist that drives catecholamine-style lipolysis and raises energy expenditure / fat oxidation. AOD-9604's chronic (rodent) effect UPREGULATES β3-AR expression in fat toward lean levels, and its sustained lipolytic effect REQUIRES functional β-adrenergic receptors (β3-AR knockout abolishes the chronic effect) — so it is not a direct β-agonist but adds a parallel HSL-driven fat-mobilisation signal and greater adrenergic-receptor availability alongside clenbuterol's direct agonism. Net: additive/potentiated lipolysis and thermogenesis, plus compounded catecholaminergic load. Note the additive-lipolysis half is rodent/mechanistic; the compounded adrenergic strain is the more concrete concern. AOD-9604 monograph (β3-AR upregulation; chronic effect β3-AR-dependent, NOT a direct agonist; PMID 11713213); clenbuterol monograph (β2-AR agonist, energy expenditure / fat oxidation) Watch: Resting heart rate, blood pressure, and serum potassium (clenbuterol causes hypokalemia and tachyarrhythmia); watch for tremor/palpitations. The compounded lipolytic drive also means more circulating free fatty acids to dispose of.
  • Ephedrine boosts ↑ Evidence-rodent
    Same adrenergic-lipolysis convergence as with clenbuterol. Ephedrine is a mixed-acting sympathomimetic (releases noradrenaline plus weak direct α/β agonism) that liberates free fatty acids from adipocytes and raises energy expenditure. AOD-9604 upregulates β3-AR (chronic, rodent) and its chronic lipolytic effect is β-adrenergic-receptor-dependent, so it can raise the receptor availability / parallel HSL signal that ephedrine's adrenergic drive then acts on — additive lipolysis and thermogenesis. AOD does NOT itself agonise β-receptors (knockout data), so it contributes receptor availability + a parallel HSL signal rather than duplicating ephedrine's direct/indirect agonism. AOD-9604 monograph (β3-AR upregulation, NOT a direct β3 agonist; PMID 11713213); ephedrine monograph (mixed α/β adrenergic FFA liberation, raised energy expenditure) Watch: Heart rate and blood pressure (ephedrine is sympathomimetic — palpitations, hypertension, insomnia); combined adrenergic load and elevated free fatty acids warrant caution.
  • Semaglutide boosts ↑ Evidence-rodent
    Additive fat loss through independent, non-overlapping mechanisms. Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist that reduces body weight mainly via appetite suppression, delayed gastric emptying and reduced energy intake (a central/gut mechanism). AOD-9604 acts peripherally on the adipocyte (HSL up, ACC down, fat oxidation). Because the two hit different nodes — intake reduction vs adipose fat mobilisation — their fat-loss effects can stack without sharing a receptor or enzyme. A relevant caveat: GLP-1 weight loss includes substantial lean-mass loss, which AOD does not counteract (it is non-anabolic, no IGF-1). AOD-9604 monograph (peripheral adipocyte mechanism, non-anabolic, no IGF-1); semaglutide monograph (GLP-1R agonist; appetite/intake reduction; substantial lean-mass loss) Watch: Body composition / lean mass (GLP-1 agents strip lean tissue — resistance training and protein intake matter); GI tolerance and, if relevant, glucose. AOD-9604 itself has no proven human fat-loss benefit to offset any added risk.
  • Tirzepatide boosts ↑ Evidence-rodent
    Same independent-mechanism additivity as with semaglutide. Tirzepatide is a GIP + GLP-1 receptor co-agonist driving weight loss centrally via appetite suppression and delayed gastric emptying. AOD-9604 works peripherally at the fat cell (HSL up, ACC down). Different receptors, different tissue — so the fat-loss effects are potentially additive rather than redundant. As with GLP-1/GIP agents, a share of weight lost is lean mass, which the non-anabolic AOD-9604 cannot preserve. AOD-9604 monograph (peripheral, non-anabolic); tirzepatide monograph (GIP+GLP-1 co-agonist; appetite suppression / delayed gastric emptying; large weight loss) Watch: Lean mass / body composition (dual agonists cause meaningful lean-mass loss), GI tolerance, and glucose. Combining does not add proven benefit from AOD's side.
  • Insulin works against Established
    Direct antagonism at the adipocyte lipolysis switch. AOD-9604 activates hormone-sensitive lipase to break down stored triglyceride. Insulin is the master ANTI-lipolytic hormone: it suppresses HSL and promotes lipogenesis and fat storage — the opposite of AOD's intracellular action. So exogenous insulin blunts the exact enzymatic step AOD-9604 turns on. The monograph highlights that AOD was engineered to avoid GH's insulin-antagonism, so it does not itself perturb insulin signalling — but exogenous insulin still opposes its lipolytic effect and carries its own acute hypoglycemia risk. AOD-9604 monograph (HSL activation / lipolysis; no insulin resistance unlike GH); insulin monograph (INSR → GLUT4/storage, anti-lipolytic, hypoglycemia) Watch: Blood glucose (exogenous insulin's acute hypoglycemia risk is the dominant hazard); recognise the two agents pull adipose metabolism in opposite directions (storage vs release).
  • T3 interacts Evidence-rodent
    Complementary supply-and-disposal of fatty acids. AOD-9604 tips adipocytes toward releasing free fatty acids and glycerol (HSL up, ACC down); those liberated fatty acids still have to be OXIDISED to be lost, or they are re-esterified back into fat. T3 acts on thyroid receptors (TRα/TRβ) to raise whole-body metabolic rate and hepatic/peripheral fatty-acid oxidation — providing the oxidative sink for the FFAs AOD mobilises. So T3 can enable AOD's mobilised fat to actually be burned rather than recycled; conversely T3 also drives muscle catabolism, a cost AOD does not carry. AOD-9604 monograph (HSL-driven FFA/glycerol release; fat oxidation needed for loss); T3 monograph (TRα/TRβ agonist raising metabolic rate / fatty-acid oxidation) Watch: TSH, free T4/T3, resting heart rate (T3 causes tachycardia, arrhythmia, bone/muscle loss and thyroid-axis suppression). This is a harm-reduction watch, not an endorsement to combine.
  • CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin overlaps (redundant) Evidence-rodent
    AOD-9604 IS the isolated C-terminal lipolytic domain of growth hormone (hGH 177-191 + N-terminal Tyr). CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin raise endogenous GH, and GH's own fat-burning action is concentrated in that exact fragment (HSL activation, acetyl-CoA-carboxylase inhibition, fat oxidation). So AOD-9604's fat-loss mechanism is a redundant SUBSET of what a GH pulse already delivers — the same lipolytic signal reached two ways. The non-redundant difference: the secretagogue combo ALSO raises IGF-1 and drives GH's anabolic/recovery and (at higher/chronic doses) insulin-resistance effects, none of which AOD-9604 produces (it does not bind GHR, does not raise IGF-1). AOD-9604 monograph (hGH 177-191 fragment; no GHR binding, no IGF-1 rise; PMID 11673763); CJC-1295+Ipamorelin monograph (GH/IGF-1 rise via GHRHR + GHS-R1a) Watch: Fasting glucose / HbA1c and IGF-1 (driven by the secretagogue, not AOD); note that adding AOD provides little mechanistic addition to GH-driven lipolysis while the GH arm carries the water-retention, carpal-tunnel and glucose signals to watch.
  • Tesamorelin overlaps (redundant) Evidence-rodent
    Both target adipose fat loss but at different points of the GH axis. Tesamorelin is a GHRH-receptor agonist that raises endogenous GH and IGF-1 — its lipolytic effect flows through the SAME GH C-terminal lipolytic domain that AOD-9604 physically is. Their fat-mobilising signals therefore overlap/duplicate. The key mechanistic contrast the monograph stresses: tesamorelin carries GH-class effects (IGF-1 rise, fluid retention, glucose effects) and has proven human visceral-fat efficacy, whereas AOD-9604 acts peripherally with NO IGF-1 rise, NO GH-axis stimulation, and no proven human fat-loss data. AOD-9604 monograph comparisons (vs tesamorelin: downstream/peripheral, no GH-axis, no IGF-1); PMID 11673763 Watch: IGF-1 and fasting glucose (tesamorelin-driven); an IGF-1 rise should be attributable to tesamorelin, not AOD — an AOD product that raises IGF-1 would signal adulteration.

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

"Origin: The lipolytic C-terminal domain of hGH (residues 177-191) was characterized at Monash University (Frank Ng and colleagues) from the 1970s-1990s; the diabetogenic/hyperglycemic activity of C-terminal hGH fragments was mapped first (Ng/Bornstein/Wade studies, late 1970s-80s), and the anti-lipogenic activity of synthetic 177-191 was shown by Wu & Ng 1993 (PMID 8358331). AOD-9604 (a stabilized Tyr-177-191 analog) was developed by Metabolic Pharmaceuticals Ltd (Australia; later Calzada/Calzada Ltd) as an ORAL anti-obesity drug. Clinical development: reached Phase 2 — Phase IIa trials underway by Feb 2002 (Wilding 2004, PMID 15134286; ChEMBL max_phase 2 as LAT-8881/CHEMBL6068615). The pivotal larger obesity RCT did not demonstrate statistically significant weight loss versus placebo, and Metabolic discontinued the obesity program (~2007); the molecule was later repurposed/relicensed (e.g. for osteoarthritis and other indications, and as LAT-8881 by Lateral Pharma). Regulatory status: NEVER approved as a drug by FDA, EMA, or TGA for any indication. In the US it has been the subject of FDA action — it is NOT an approved drug and FDA has stated it does not qualify as a legitimate compounding ingredient; a self-affirmed 'GRAS' food/cosmetic positioning was promoted by some marketers but it has no FDA drug approval. Anti-doping: BANNED by WADA (class S2, peptide hormones/growth factors) at all times, in and out of competition. It famously featured in the 2013 Australian Essendon AFL supplements scandal (ASADA). It evades the WADA hGH isoform immunoassay (Orlovius 2013, PMID 24124033) because it is not GHR-active, but is directly detectable by targeted LC-MS/MS via the stable CRSVEGSCG urinary metabolite (Cox 2015, PMID 25208511). Current status (2026): sold widely on the gray peptide market 'for research use only,' not legally marketable as a drug or dietary supplement in the US/EU; recent reviews classify it among unapproved direct-to-patient peptides with scarce human safety data (Mendias 2026, PMID 41966639)."

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • The pivotal human Phase 2 obesity RCT (the ~12-week, several-hundred-patient placebo-controlled trial that ended the obesity program) does not appear to be published in PubMed — its full design, n, effect sizes, and safety data are not in the peer-reviewed literature. Reviewers and the sponsor report it FAILED to show significant weight loss vs placebo, but the primary data are essentially unavailable. This is the single largest evidence gap.
  • What is the actual molecular target/receptor? AOD-9604 is not a GHR agonist and not a direct β3-AR agonist, yet its chronic effect needs β3-AR. The proximal binding partner that initiates HSL stimulation and ACC inhibition is unidentified — no Ki/EC50 exists for any receptor.
  • Does it produce ANY measurable fat loss in humans at gray-market injectable doses (~300 µg SC)? There is no controlled human body-composition data supporting the doses people actually use; rodent efficacy was oral at ~500 µg/kg, not the SC microgram doses sold online.
  • Human pharmacokinetics (oral bioavailability, SC absorption, half-life, tissue distribution) are not formally published — values circulating online are estimates.
  • Long-term human safety and immunogenicity are uncharacterized; whether repeated injection of impure peptide raises anti-drug antibodies or causes cumulative harm is unstudied.
  • Reproducibility of the off-target findings (cartilage repair in the rabbit OA model, PMID 26275694; orthopedic 'IGF-1/satellite-cell' claims in narrative reviews) — these mechanisms are not independently confirmed and may be mislabeled or placebo-amplified (Mendias 2026, PMID 41966639).

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only — NOT a usage endorsement and NO dosing guidance. (1) Product/source: because the biggest documented risk is contamination/mislabeling of gray-market vials (PMID 24976118; PMID 41966639), the most protective step is third-party lab verification of identity/sterility/endotoxin before any use. (2) Injection-site checks: watch for spreading redness, warmth, swelling, or abscess (infection) vs benign transient local irritation; seek care for signs of cellulitis. (3) Glucose: although rodent data show NO diabetogenic effect (unlike GH), human metabolic safety is unstudied — a baseline and periodic fasting glucose/HbA1c is reasonable, especially if combined with other agents. (4) IGF-1: expected to be UNCHANGED (no GHR activity) — an unexpectedly elevated IGF-1 would suggest the product is mislabeled/adulterated with a real GH secretagogue or hGH, a useful red flag. (5) Allergic/systemic reaction: monitor for rash, flushing, wheeze, or fever after injection (possible endotoxin or hypersensitivity). (6) Athletes: AOD-9604 is WADA-banned and detectable by LC-MS/MS (urinary CRSVEGSCG marker, PMID 25208511) — it will NOT show on the hGH isoform test but is independently testable, so it is a sanction risk. (7) General: since proven human benefit is absent, the key 'monitoring' judgment is that any adverse change has no efficacy upside to offset it. Persistent or systemic symptoms warrant stopping and clinical evaluation."

Caveats

HUMAN-DATA GAP IS THE HEADLINE: every fat-loss mechanism here comes from mice, Zucker rats, and isolated tissue. The closest human touchpoint is fat-tissue tested in a dish, not weight measured on a scale in a trial. No peer-reviewed RCT shows AOD-9604 produces meaningful weight or fat loss in living humans, and the obesity development program was discontinued. FORUM MYTH TO KILL: 'AOD-9604 is a β3-adrenergic agonist / works through β3-AR' — the knockout-mouse data explicitly show the acute effect does NOT require β3-AR; the receptor's role is upregulation over time, not direct agonism. 'GRAS status means it's proven safe/effective for fat loss' — false; GRAS is a US food-additive self-affirmation, unrelated to injectable efficacy. WADA-banned in sport. Most published 'AOD9604' literature since ~2013 is actually anti-doping detection methodology (how to catch it in urine/blood), not new efficacy data — easy to mistake for a body of clinical evidence. OA/joint claims rest on a single rabbit study (PMID 26275694). Mechanistic papers are heavily from one group (F.M. Ng / Monash / Metabolic Pharmaceuticals) with commercial interest — independent replication of the human-relevant claims is thin. None of this is medical advice.
