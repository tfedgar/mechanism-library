Ostarine

A pill that tries to flip the "build muscle" switch in muscle and bone while mostly leaving the prostate, skin and other tissues alone — the original "selective" androgen. Promising on paper, still not an approved drug anywhere, and real-world liver-injury reports keep stacking up.

Limited human data 26 citations
Class
Nonsteroidal selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM), aryl-propionamide chemotype
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR) — the same receptor testosterone and DHT use
Human status
Investigational only — NOT approved by FDA, EMA, or any regulator for any use, anywhere. It has the deepest human data of any SARM: multiple completed trials including a positive Phase II for lean mass in elderly/postmenopausal adults (PMID 22031847), a Phase II in cancer-related muscle wasting (PMID 23499390), the large Phase III POWER program in lung-cancer cachexia (PMID 27138015), and Phase II oncology trials in AR-positive breast cancer (PMID 38342115, 33141975). Despite hitting lean-mass endpoints, it repeatedly failed co-primary physical-FUNCTION endpoints, which sank its lead approval bids. Sold today only as a gray-market 'research chemical'/supplement adulterant — products are frequently mislabeled or contaminated. It is the single most-detected SARM in anti-doping testing (PMID 39063049).

At a glance

  • Increases lean body mass (human Phase II; modest)
  • Increases bone density / fracture healing (rodent)
  • Suppresses endogenous testosterone (HPG-axis shutdown)
  • Drug-induced liver injury reported (case reports)
  • Often fails to improve physical FUNCTION despite muscle gain (human)
  • No estrogenic or strong virilizing conversion (not aromatized, not 5a-reduced)
  • Most-detected SARM in doping control (WADA prohibited)

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Binds the androgen receptor directly Established

    Ostarine slots into the androgen receptor (AR) — the same docking site testosterone uses. It's a non-steroidal molecule, so it mimics an androgen's JOB without being shaped like a steroid. Think of it as a different-shaped key that still turns the same lock. PMID 35457222; corpus MoA card (PMID 14600402)

  2. Skips aromatization and 5-alpha-reduction Established

    Because it isn't a steroid, the body can't convert it to estrogen (no aromatization) and can't ramp it up into a stronger androgen in skin/prostate (no 5-alpha-reduction). That's the chemical reason it causes less of the estrogen-side and virilizing side effects you get from testosterone — the conversions simply don't happen. PMID 35457222

  3. Changes the receptor's shape, then its helpers Evidence-rodent

    When ostarine binds, it bends the AR into a slightly different shape than testosterone does. That altered shape decides WHICH helper proteins (coactivators) the receptor can grab. In muscle and bone, the right helpers are present and the receptor switches genes ON; in some other tissues the helper mix is different, so the same drug does much less. This 'differential coactivator recruitment' is the textbook explanation for tissue selectivity. PMID 23449329 (SARM S-101479); PMID 19846549

  4. Turns on muscle-building gene programs Evidence-rodent

    In muscle cells the activated AR moves into the nucleus and switches on genes that drive muscle-precursor cells to mature and grow (myogenic differentiation). In lab muscle cells and rodents this raises lean mass; it works through both classic gene-switching (genomic) and faster signaling (non-genomic) routes. PMID 35457222

  5. Builds bone too Evidence-rodent

    The same AR activation in bone-forming cells (osteoblasts) increases bone density and helps fractures heal in animal models — which is why it was studied for osteoporosis, not just muscle. PMID 31531719; PMID 29785666

  6. In humans: real but modest lean-mass gains Evidence-human

    In actual clinical trials, ostarine reliably ADDED lean body mass versus placebo — in healthy elderly/postmenopausal people and in cancer-cachexia patients. But the gains were modest, and crucially it often FAILED to translate into better physical FUNCTION (e.g. stair-climb power) in its big cancer trials. So 'more muscle on the scan' did not equal 'works as a drug'. (The Phase-3 POWER program met lean-mass but MISSED its co-primary stair-climb/physical-function endpoint.) PMID 22031847; PMID 23499390; PMID 27138015

  7. The 'selective = safe' promise is leaky Contested

    The marketed story is 'all the muscle, none of the downsides.' Reality: ostarine still suppresses your own testosterone, and a growing pile of case reports ties it to drug-induced liver injury (cholestatic hepatitis, jaundice) — exactly the steroid-like harm SARMs were supposed to avoid. Selectivity is partial and dose-dependent, not absolute. PMID 34368386; PMID 35655632; PMID 37871633

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Ostarine (enobosarm, S-22, GTx-024) is a nonsteroidal SELECTIVE AGONIST of the androgen receptor (AR; gene NR3C4, ChEMBL target CHEMBL1871; ChEMBL action type for the molecule = MODULATOR). It is an aryl-propionamide, chemically the S-enantiomer derived from the antiandrogen bicalutamide scaffold: (S)-N-(4-cyano-3-(trifluoromethyl)phenyl)-3-(4-cyanophenoxy)-2-hydroxy-2-methylpropanamide (C19H14F3N3O3, MW 389.3, single stereoisomer, ALogP ~3.2, QED 0.82, 0 Lipinski violations; ChEMBL CHEMBL1738889). It binds the AR ligand-binding domain (LBD) at the same orthosteric pocket that testosterone/DHT occupy. CERTAINTY ON AFFINITY: the corpus/ChEMBL and the abstracts I retrieved this run do NOT give a numeric Ki/Kd for ostarine; the foundational GTx aryl-propionamide medicinal-chemistry papers report SARMs in this series with AR binding Ki in the low-nanomolar range (roughly 1-10 nM, i.e. high affinity, lower than DHT) but I could not confirm an ostarine-specific Ki value from a source returned this run, so the exact number is Evidence-(not-confirmed-here). Functionally it is a partial/tissue-selective AGONIST: strong agonist in muscle and bone, weak agonist/near-neutral in prostate and skin/hair, which is the defining 'SARM' behaviour (Established that the behaviour is tissue-selective; the 'selective = safe' premise is leaky in practice). It is NOT aromatized to estrogen and NOT 5alpha-reduced to a more potent metabolite, unlike testosterone (Established).

Signalling cascade. Genomic pathway (classical): ostarine diffuses across the membrane (small lipophilic molecule) and binds the AR LBD. Ligand binding triggers a conformational change in helix 12 (the AF-2 surface) of the LBD, dissociation of heat-shock chaperones (HSP90/70), the intramolecular N-terminal/C-terminal (N/C) interaction, receptor dimerization, and nuclear translocation. The DNA-binding domain then docks on androgen-response elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer regions of androgen-responsive genes, and the AF-1/AF-2 surfaces recruit p160/SRC-family coactivators and the general transcriptional machinery to drive transcription. The TISSUE-SELECTIVITY hypothesis is that ostarine stabilises a distinct receptor conformation versus DHT, producing a different coactivator/corepressor recruitment profile that varies by the cofactor complement of each tissue (Contested/Speculative at the mechanistic detail level; the phenotype is Established). In skeletal muscle the downstream gene program includes induction of myogenin, MyoD and myosin heavy chain (MyH), driving satellite/myoblast proliferation and myogenic differentiation (Evidence-rodent/in vitro: Leciejewska 2022, PMID 35457222). Androgen-responsive muscle genes S-adenosylmethionine decarboxylase, myostatin and IGF-1Ea are modulated by ostarine in vivo (Evidence-rodent: Dubois 2015, PMID 26393303), and notably the anabolic effect is NOT confined to the satellite-cell AR — AR-positive muscle-resident fibroblasts (vimentin+) also mediate it, so ostarine 'does not selectively modulate AR in the satellite-cell lineage' (Evidence-rodent, PMID 26393303). Nongenomic (rapid) pathway: ostarine activates ERK1/2 (MAPK) signalling, and pharmacological AR blockade abolishes the proliferative/differentiation effect, confirming the cascade is AR-dependent and routes through ERK1/2 (Evidence-in vitro/rodent: PMID 35457222).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral (small-molecule, orally bioavailable; ChEMBL lists it as an oral development candidate). Recreationally dosed orally at ~10-25 mg/day (Evidence-human/forensic: corpus doping-analysis entries, PMIDs 32688441, 38467244).
Absorption / bioavailability
Rapid and essentially complete absorption with HIGH oral bioavailability in rats (Evidence-rodent: Kim 2013, PMID 24074268). Human absolute bioavailability number not given in sources retrieved this run, but oral dosing reliably produces detectable plasma/urine drug and pharmacodynamic lean-mass effects in humans (Evidence-human: Dalton 2011 PMID 22031847, Dobs 2013 PMID 23499390).
Half-life
Human plasma half-life is commonly cited as roughly ~24 h (supports once-daily dosing in the trials), but I could not confirm a precise human t1/2 figure from a source returned THIS run — treat the ~24 h as Evidence-(not-confirmed-here). In rats the radiolabel elimination half-life was 0.6 h (male) and 16.4 h (female), showing marked species/sex differences (Evidence-rodent: PMID 24074268). Urinary detectability after a single low/micro dose extends for days to weeks, consistent with a long human elimination tail (Evidence-human forensic: PMIDs 32959982, 38499138).
Metabolism
Hepatic phase I + phase II. Identified human metabolic routes: hydroxylation, ether cleavage, dealkylation, O-glucuronidation and sulfation; ten metabolites characterised from human hepatocytes and ostarine-positive urine (Evidence-human in vitro/in vivo: Taoussi 2024, PMID 39063049). In rats the major transformations were oxidation of the cyanophenol ring (metabolite M8), amide hydrolysis/conjugation (M3), and glucuronidation at the tertiary alcohol (M6) (Evidence-rodent: PMID 24074268). MECHANISTIC TOXICOLOGY NOTE: a metabolite, cyanophenol-sulfate, is proposed to participate in ostarine liver toxicity (Speculative/Evidence-in vitro: PMID 39063049).
Excretion
Predominantly fecal in rats (~70% of radioactivity in feces, 21-25% in urine within 48 h), with feces containing mostly unchanged drug (~49-65%) (Evidence-rodent: PMID 24074268). In humans, parent drug plus glucuronide/oxidative metabolites are recoverable in urine (and oral fluid), which is the basis of anti-doping detection (Evidence-human: PMIDs 39063049, 32959982, 38499138).
Notes
Not an ester and not a prodrug (it is the active S-enantiomer itself). Not aromatized and not 5alpha-reduced, so it generates no estrogenic or DHT-amplified metabolites by those routes (Established). Real-world recreational exposure is complicated by product contamination/mislabelling — supplements sold as 'ostarine' frequently contain other SARMs, steroids or stimulants (Evidence-human: PMID 39145153), and even micro-dose contamination can trigger a positive doping test (PMID 32959982).

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

HUMAN (the strongest data): In healthy elderly men and postmenopausal women (12 wk, n=120), ostarine produced DOSE-DEPENDENT, statistically significant gains in total lean body mass, with the 3 mg/day arm significant vs placebo (P<0.001) plus improved stair-climb physical function (P=0.013) and improved insulin resistance (P=0.013) (Evidence-human: Dalton 2011, PMID 22031847). In cancer patients (phase 2, n=159 safety / 100 evaluable, up to 113 days), BOTH 1 mg and 3 mg/day significantly increased lean body mass vs baseline (1 mg: median +1.5 kg, P=0.0012; 3 mg: median +1.0 kg, P=0.046) while placebo did not change (+0.02 kg, P=0.88) (Evidence-human: Dobs 2013, PMID 23499390). Note the effect plateaus — 3 mg was not clearly better than 1 mg for lean mass, and FUNCTIONAL endpoints (the POWER phase-3 program in cancer cachexia) did NOT robustly meet co-primary stair-climb-power endpoints, which is why the drug stalled at FDA (Evidence-human / regulatory: PMIDs 27138015, 33759397). In ER+/AR+ advanced breast cancer, ORAL 9 mg and 18 mg/day gave clinical benefit at 24 wk in ~29-32% (Evidence-human: Palmieri 2024, PMID 38342115) — confirming an even higher human dose range is tolerated in oncology. MAGNITUDE FRAMING for a lay audience: human lean-mass gains are MODEST (~1-1.5 kg over ~3 months), an order of magnitude smaller than supraphysiologic testosterone. RODENT: ostarine restores androgen-sensitive muscle (levator ani) weight after castration roughly as effectively as DHT, and 30 days of dosing raises muscle mass and myogenic gene expression (Evidence-rodent: PMIDs 26393303, 35457222); it also builds/heals bone in orchiectomized and ovariectomized rat models (Evidence-rodent: PMIDs 32876707, 29785666, 37378829). KEY UNKNOWN: there is NO validated human dose-response curve at the higher 'recreational' doses or for the long durations users actually take — human efficacy data top out around 3-18 mg/day in monitored trials, and supratherapeutic recreational use is essentially uncharacterised for benefit and harm (Established gap).

How it compares

  • vs Testosterone / anabolic-androgenic steroids (e.g., testosterone esters)
    same: Both are AR agonists driving muscle and bone anabolism through the same receptor and genomic ARE-transcription pathway; both suppress the HPG axis and can lower HDL and injure the liver.
    different: Testosterone is a steroid that IS aromatized to estradiol and 5alpha-reduced to DHT, hits AR in all tissues (prostate, skin, hair, RBCs) and is typically injected; ostarine is a nonsteroidal aryl-propionamide, is not aromatized/5alpha-reduced, is oral, and is comparatively muscle/bone-selective with much SMALLER human anabolic magnitude (~1-1.5 kg lean mass over 3 months vs the large gains from supraphysiologic testosterone).
  • vs Ligandrol (LGD-4033)
    same: Both nonsteroidal SARMs, oral, AR orthosteric agonists, marketed for lean mass, both cause cholestatic drug-induced liver injury and HPG suppression, both WADA-banned.
    different: LGD-4033 is a different (pyrrolidinyl-benzonitrile) chemotype, generally regarded as more potent/longer-acting per mg and used at lower recreational doses (~5-10 mg) than ostarine (10-25 mg); head-to-head human potency data are limited. Both appear in rodent muscle-building comparisons (e.g., PMID 33042018).
  • vs RAD-140 (testolone)
    same: Nonsteroidal SARM, oral, AR agonist for muscle, and a recurring cause of idiosyncratic drug-induced liver injury in case reports.
    different: RAD-140 is structurally distinct and is reported with notably severe DILI case reports (PMIDs 36561105, 36978171); contaminated 'RAD-140' products have also caused gynecomastia/hypogonadism via undisclosed steroids (PMID 39145153). Ostarine has the largest controlled human trial dataset of the three.
  • vs Andarine (S-4, GTx-007)
    same: Closely related aryl-propionamide SARM from the same GTx/Dalton medicinal-chemistry series; both detected together as abused SARMs in doping assays (PMID 31734960).
    different: Andarine is associated with a characteristic visual side effect (yellow-tinted vision) not typical of ostarine, and was largely abandoned for that and weaker selectivity; ostarine advanced further clinically.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Cardarine compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    This is the single documented real-world Cardarine stack harm. A published case report describes a man who bought Cardarine online and stacked it with the SARM ostarine and developed massive rhabdomyolysis (CK up to ~86,435 U/L) together with severe hepatocellular injury (AST ~2558 U/L); the dominant finding was the muscle breakdown, with liver enzyme rise partly attributable to it and to the co-used SARM. Ostarine (an androgen-receptor modulator) is independently an established cause of (often cholestatic) drug-induced liver injury in recreational users; Cardarine drives aggressive metabolic remodeling of myofibers and has its own human hepatotoxicity/rhabdomyolysis signal. Stacked, the muscle-breakdown and liver insults compound (causation cannot be cleanly split between the two). Cardarine + ostarine co-abuse: massive rhabdomyolysis + hepatocellular injury: Kintz 2021 PMID 34678947 (both monographs' side_effect_mechanisms / evidence_base). Ostarine-induced cholestatic DILI: ostarine monograph side_effect_mechanisms (multiple human case reports). Watch: Creatine kinase and renal function/urinalysis for myoglobinuria (severe myalgia with cola-colored urine is an emergency), plus ALT, AST, bilirubin (jaundice, dark urine, RUQ pain). Stop and seek care on any of these.
  • Ligandrol shares target Evidence-human
    Two non-steroidal SARMs competing for and co-activating the single androgen receptor — there is only one AR, so their anabolic effects are not truly independent but overlapping occupancy of the same pocket (Ligandrol binds with high apparent potency, Ki ~1 nM). Beyond redundancy at target, both independently suppress the HPTA and lower HDL, and both carry cholestatic DILI case reports, so combining them compounds axis suppression, the lipid hit, and liver risk rather than adding a distinct new mechanism. PMID 22459616 (LGD suppression/HDL); ostarine-mk-2866-enobosarm-gtx-024.json (AR agonist, HPTA suppression, HDL drop, cholestatic DILI) Watch: Total/free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG (axis suppression); fasting HDL/lipids; liver panel emphasising bilirubin (cholestatic pattern). Watch for symptoms of low testosterone (low libido, fatigue, testicular shrinkage).

History & legal status

"Ostarine originated in the early 2000s from the aryl-propionamide SARM program of James Dalton and colleagues (University of Tennessee), derived from the bicalutamide antiandrogen scaffold, and was developed clinically by GTx, Inc. (Memphis) as GTx-024/enobosarm/Ostarine; USAN 'enobosarm' assigned 2011 (ChEMBL CHEMBL1738889). It reached phase 2/3 for cancer cachexia (POWER trials in NSCLC) and was studied for sarcopenia, stress urinary incontinence, and AR+/ER+ breast cancer (PMIDs 27138015, 38342115, 33141975). It has NEVER been approved by the FDA or EMA; the POWER cachexia program increased lean mass but did not robustly meet the FDA's physical-function co-primary endpoint, blocking approval and fueling debate about cachexia trial endpoints (PMIDs 33759397, 27138015). Max clinical phase recorded is 3 (ChEMBL). Regulatory/legal status: the FDA issued public warnings (2017 onward) against SARMs in body-building products as unapproved drugs; ostarine is a PROHIBITED substance under the World Anti-Doping Agency code (class S1.2 'other anabolic agents') at all times, and is the single most common SARM in adverse analytical findings (~25% of S1.2 AAFs in 2021) (Evidence: corpus doping entries, PMIDs 38467244, 28137616). It is widely sold online and in supplements as a 'research chemical,' frequently adulterated or mislabeled (PMID 39145153). It is not a controlled substance in the same tier as classic steroids in all jurisdictions, but selling it for human consumption is illegal in the US, and the proposed US SARMs Control Act has sought to schedule SARMs."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No validated HUMAN dose-response or safety data at the higher/longer 'recreational' exposures people actually use — controlled human efficacy tops out at ~3-18 mg/day for weeks-to-months, leaving real-world use essentially uncharacterised.
  • The molecular basis of tissue selectivity (which AR conformation/coactivator complexes ostarine favors in muscle vs prostate vs liver) is not mechanistically resolved — and the selectivity is demonstrably leaky (liver, lipids, HPG axis).
  • Mechanism and predictors of the idiosyncratic cholestatic liver injury: is it the proposed cyanophenol-sulfate metabolite, host genetics, dose, or product contamination? (PMID 39063049 hypothesis is unproven.)
  • Cardiac risk in humans: rodent/in-vitro data show pro-fibrotic, sex-specific cardiotoxicity (PMID 37543162) but there are no human cardiovascular outcome data.
  • Long-term reversibility of HPG-axis suppression and fertility effects after extended ostarine use is not established in controlled human studies.
  • Whether enobosarm has a viable approved indication at all (breast cancer vs cachexia vs GLP-1-era lean-mass preservation) remains open after repeated late-stage setbacks (PMIDs 38342115, 40739991).

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"HARM-REDUCTION MONITORING (not an endorsement, no dosing guidance). LIVER: baseline and periodic liver panel — ALT, AST, ALP, total/direct bilirubin, GGT — because cholestatic drug-induced liver injury (jaundice, dark urine, pale stool, itch, RUQ pain) is the best-documented serious harm and ALT elevation occurred in ~7% of trial subjects; any jaundice or rising transaminases warrants stopping and medical review (PMIDs 35655632, 34368386, 37218811). LIPIDS: fasting lipid panel for HDL-C suppression and atherogenic shift (PMID 37218811). HORMONES: total testosterone, LH, FSH (and estradiol) to detect HPG-axis suppression/'shutdown'; in women watch for virilizing signs (voice change, hirsutism) (PMIDs 39145153, 26401842). CARDIAC: be alert to a theoretical pro-fibrotic cardiac signal from preclinical data — symptoms like exertional dyspnea, palpitations, or edema deserve evaluation (PMID 37543162). MUSCULOSKELETAL: counsel on tendon-injury risk given rapid strength gains (Achilles rupture reported) (PMID 37218811). GENERAL: hematocrit/hemoglobin (androgenic erythrocytosis), and recognize that product contamination/mislabelling means lab abnormalities may reflect undisclosed steroids/stimulants, not 'pure' ostarine (PMID 39145153). Persistent jaundice, severe abdominal pain, chest symptoms, or marked lab derangement should prompt immediate cessation and clinical care."

Caveats

Harm-reduction framing for the audience: (1) 'Selective = side-effect-free' is marketing, not reality — ostarine still shuts down your own testosterone and there is a real, growing case-report signal for drug-induced liver injury, including cholestatic jaundice (PMID 34368386, 35655632, 37871633). (2) It is unapproved and unregulated: independent lab analyses repeatedly find SARM products that contain the wrong compound, the wrong dose, or undisclosed extras (e.g. Cardarine contamination, PMID 34678947) — you cannot trust the label. (3) Most efficacy claims for muscle GROWTH in healthy lifters are extrapolated from clinical-population trials and rodent/cell work, not proven in young athletes; the human gains that exist are modest and didn't reliably improve function. (4) Don't confuse it with non-SARM 'SARM-bin' compounds: Cardarine is a PPARd agonist (different mechanism, carcinogenicity signal in rodents) and SR9009 is a Rev-erb agonist with terrible oral bioavailability — neither touches the androgen receptor.
