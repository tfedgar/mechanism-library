Library / Anabolic-androgenic steroids

Trenbolone

Main target: Androgen receptor. Backed by human data.

EvidenceHuman evidence
ApprovalNot FDA-approved

A 19-nortestosterone that hammers the androgen receptor harder than testosterone, can't be softened by aromatase or 5-alpha-reductase, and bleeds onto the progesterone and glucocorticoid receptors — which is why it builds muscle so aggressively yet brings its own distinct side-effect signature.

Strong human data 16 citations
Class
Synthetic anabolic-androgenic steroid (AAS); a 19-nortestosterone (estrane/"19-nor") derivative with a 4,9,11-triene structure
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR, gene NR3C4) — a nuclear hormone receptor / ligand-activated transcription factor
Human status
No human clinical use and no controlled human efficacy/safety trials. Mechanism is built from in vitro receptor-binding assays, rodent studies (rat, mouse), and bovine muscle-cell work; trenbolone acetate is FDA-approved only as a cattle growth implant. Human evidence is limited to anecdote and case reports of harm. Authors of the rat work explicitly framed it as a rationale for FUTURE preclinical study, not as established human therapy (PMID 21266670).

At a glance

  • Potent skeletal-muscle hypertrophy and strength gain — in castrated rats it raised androgen-sensitive muscle mass 35-40% above controls (Evidence-rodent, PMID 21266670)
  • Shifts muscle protein balance toward growth: suppresses muscle-wasting 'atrogenes' (MuRF1, atrogin-1) and raises anabolic IGF-1 and mechano-growth factor (Evidence-rodent, PMID 24928725)
  • Tissue-selective / SARM-like in rats: builds muscle and partially protects bone and reduces visceral fat at low doses WITHOUT enlarging the prostate or spiking hemoglobin — because it is not 5-alpha-reduced to a more potent prostate androgen (Evidence-rodent, PMID 21266670)
  • Does NOT convert to estrogen (non-aromatizing) — no estrogenic limb (Established, PMID 24928725 / 11252818)
  • Carries intrinsic progestogenic activity (binds PGR with affinity above progesterone) and tends to raise prolactin, driving gynecomastia and sexual dysfunction that estrogen blockers (aromatase inhibitors) do NOT fix (Established for the 19-nor class; corpus MoA cards: progesterone_receptor, prolactin_dopamine)
  • Binds the glucocorticoid receptor and competes with cortisol/dexamethasone in muscle, an additional anti-catabolic route (Evidence-rodent, PMID 24928725 GR-mRNA suppression; PMID 3871528 competitive binding)
  • Likely also acts non-genomically (rapid, transcription-independent) via a GPCR -> MMP -> EGFR/erbB2 -> IGF-1R -> Akt cascade shown in bovine muscle cells (Evidence-rodent/in vitro, PMID 27285910)

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

7 steps4 human / established 3 rodent / preclinical

  1. It enters the cell as the active 17-beta form Established

    Injectable trenbolone is an ester (acetate or enanthate) that is just a storage/timing shell; enzymes in the blood clip it off to release free 17-beta-trenbolone, the molecule that actually does the work. Being fat-soluble, it slips straight through the cell membrane. PMID 11252818

  2. It grabs the androgen receptor as hard as DHT Established

    Inside the cell it binds the androgen receptor (AR) with roughly the same strength as DHT, the body's most potent natural androgen — and far more avidly than testosterone. It also barely sticks to SHBG (the blood protein that mops up testosterone), so more of it stays free and active. PMID 11252818

  3. No off-switches: it can't be aromatized or 5-alpha-reduced Established

    Because it is a 19-nor steroid with a locked 4,9,11-triene structure, the enzyme aromatase can't turn it into estrogen, and 5-alpha-reductase can't modify it. Testosterone gets DIALED UP in the prostate (to DHT) and converted to estrogen in fat; trenbolone does neither, so its potency is the same everywhere and it skips the estrogenic limb. PMID 21266670

  4. The activated receptor goes to the nucleus and rewrites gene expression Evidence-rodent

    The trenbolone-AR complex changes shape, moves into the cell nucleus, and docks onto specific DNA sequences (androgen response elements), switching muscle genes on and off. In rat muscle this flips the balance toward growth: it shuts down the wasting genes MuRF1 and atrogin-1 and turns up the growth factors IGF-1 and mechano-growth factor. PMID 24928725

  5. It also blunts the muscle-wasting glucocorticoid signal Evidence-rodent

    Trenbolone binds the glucocorticoid receptor and lowers its expression in muscle, getting in the way of cortisol — the stress hormone that breaks muscle down. This anti-catabolic action stacks on top of the pro-growth AR effect to spare and build tissue. PMID 24928725

  6. A fast, non-genomic route adds to the growth signal Evidence-rodent

    Beyond the slow gene-transcription pathway, trenbolone appears to trigger a rapid membrane cascade — a G-protein-coupled receptor activating MMP enzymes that release EGF, firing the EGFR and IGF-1 receptors and the Akt growth pathway — which raises protein synthesis and lowers protein breakdown in muscle cells. PMID 27285910

  7. Its progesterone-receptor activity creates a separate side-effect channel Evidence-rodent (PGR binding shown; the prolactin/downstream effects in humans are Contested/anecdotal)

    As a 19-nor compound, trenbolone activates the progesterone receptor (it binds it even more tightly than progesterone does) and tends to raise prolactin. This progestin/prolactin route can cause gynecomastia and sexual dysfunction that are NOT driven by estrogen — which is why an aromatase inhibitor alone often fails to fix 'tren' gyno. PMID 11252818

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. Trenbolone's active 17beta-form (17beta-trenbolone / 17beta-TBOH) is a strong AR (NR3C4) agonist. Bauer et al. 2000 (rh-AR DHT-displacement assay) found 17beta-TBOH binds the human AR with affinity SIMILAR to DHT (the assay's reference high-affinity androgen), and Yarrow 2010 summarizes its AR affinity as ~3x that of testosterone [Established, human/in vitro: PMID 11252818, 20138077]. It is also a PR (PGR) agonist: Bauer found a slightly HIGHER affinity at the bovine progestin receptor than progesterone itself [Evidence-in vitro: PMID 11252818]; curated MoA confirms 19-nor steroids (nandrolone/trenbolone) have intrinsic progestogenic activity at PGR. It additionally engages the glucocorticoid receptor (GR), where in muscle it acts to OPPOSE glucocorticoid catabolism (functional antagonism of GR-driven wasting) [Evidence-rodent]. Affinity for SHBG is much LOWER than DHT, so a large free fraction circulates [Evidence-in vitro: PMID 11252818]. Crucially it is NOT a substrate for aromatase (no conversion to estradiol) nor for 5alpha-reductase (no conversion to a more potent androgen), unlike testosterone [Established: PMID 20138077, 21266670]. The 17alpha-epimer and trendione metabolites retain <5% of 17beta AR affinity [Evidence-in vitro: PMID 11252818].

Signalling cascade. Genomic route (dominant): lipophilic 17beta-TBOH diffuses across the membrane, binds the cytoplasmic AR ligand-binding domain, displaces HSP90 chaperones, the receptor homodimerizes, translocates to the nucleus, and binds androgen-response elements (AREs) to act as a transcription factor. In androgen-responsive muscle this UP-regulates anabolic genes IGF-1 and mechano-growth factor (MGF) and DOWN-regulates the atrogenes MuRF1 and atrogin-1 (FBXO32), net favoring protein accretion over breakdown; trenbolone suppressed atrogin-1 and GR mRNA MORE than testosterone in orchiectomized rat levator-ani/bulbocavernosus muscle [Evidence-rodent: PMID 24928725]. Anti-catabolic arm: by occupying/functionally opposing GR signalling in muscle, it blunts glucocorticoid-driven proteolysis. Non-genomic/rapid arm: AR-linked PI3K-Akt signalling — in human prostate (22Rv1) cells 17 TB drove AR-dependent proliferation via Akt/GSK3beta phosphorylation, up-regulating cyclin D2/CDK4 and cyclin E/CDK2 and down-regulating p27 (EC50 ~30 pM) [Evidence-in vitro: PMID 29421751]. Progestogenic arm: PGR agonism plus raised prolactin drives gynecomastia and sexual dysfunction independent of estradiol [Established/curated MoA].

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Parenteral (oil-depot intramuscular injection) as an ester prodrug; the original veterinary product is a subcutaneous ear implant of trenbolone acetate (TBA) in cattle. Trenbolone is essentially never used orally as the base (17alpha-methyltrenbolone/metribolone is the rare oral analog, and is markedly hepatotoxic).
Absorption / bioavailability
Esters (acetate – fast; enanthate – slow; hexahydrobenzylcarbonate/'parabolan' – slowest) are hydrolyzed by tissue/plasma esterases to liberate the active 17beta-trenbolone, giving sustained release from the oil depot. The base has negligible practical oral bioavailability; injectable depot bioavailability is high but not formally quantified in humans [Evidence-human gap].
Half-life
Driven by the ester, not the molecule: free 17beta-trenbolone is short-lived, while the depot half-life is roughly ~1-2 days for acetate up to ~5-10+ days for enanthate / hexahydrobenzylcarbonate (estimates extrapolated from ester class; no controlled human PK study of trenbolone esters exists) [Evidence-human gap / Speculative on exact numbers].
Metabolism
Hepatic. 17beta-trenbolone is interconverted to its 17alpha-epimer and oxidized at C17 to trendione (17-keto), both far weaker androgens (<5% AR affinity). Phase II conjugation (glucuronide/sulfate) precedes excretion. Metabolite profiling and doping assays detect epitrenbolone and related species by LC-MS/MS [Established: PMID 11252818, 19007801; cattle metabolite data PMID 25244153].
Excretion
Urinary and fecal as conjugated 17alpha-trenbolone, 17beta-trenbolone and trendione; in implanted cattle 17alpha-trenbolone dominates excreta and these metabolites are environmentally persistent (the basis for the large environmental-toxicology literature) [Evidence: PMID 18546691, 25244153, 16818248].
Notes
Prodrug ester design is the whole point: lipophilicity lets it cross the blood-brain barrier (CNS effects: insomnia, mood, hippocampal accumulation in rats) [Evidence-rodent: PMID 25461682]. No aromatization and no 5alpha-reduction means its metabolism REDUCES rather than amplifies potency in androgen-sensitive accessory tissues. Human pharmacokinetic data are essentially absent — trenbolone was never approved for human use, so all human PK is inferred.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

Rodent (best controlled data): in intact and orchiectomized F344 rats, graded trenbolone-enanthate (TREN) over 29 days increased androgen-sensitive levator-ani/bulbocavernosus muscle mass by 35-40% above sham at ALL doses, gave dose-dependent partial protection against orchiectomy-induced bone-mineral-density loss and visceral fat gain, and — critically — at the LOWEST doses kept prostate mass and hemoglobin near sham, whereas high-dose TREN and supraphysiologic testosterone raised prostate mass by 68% and 84% respectively. This demonstrates a genuine dose-dependent therapeutic window separating myotrophic from prostatic/erythrocytic effects in rats [Evidence-rodent: PMID 21266670]. Cattle: growth-promoting implants deliver on the order of ~200 mg TBA, producing measurable feed-efficiency/lean-gain effects [Evidence: PMID 25244153]. Fish/in vitro: AR-mediated endocrine effects occur at nanograms-per-liter and proliferative effects at picomolar (EC50 ~30 pM in 22Rv1) — i.e., extremely potent at the receptor [Evidence-in vitro/aquatic: PMID 12785594, 29421751]. HUMAN dose-response is UNKNOWN: there are no human efficacy or dose-finding trials; all human use is non-medical and self-reported, so the magnitude of muscle gain per unit dose, the threshold for side effects, and any human therapeutic window are not established [Evidence-human gap].

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Testosterone
    same: Both are AR agonists driving anabolic gene programs in muscle; both suppress the HPG axis.
    different: Testosterone aromatizes to estradiol and is 5alpha-reduced to more-potent DHT (amplifying both estrogenic and prostatic/androgenic effects). Trenbolone does NEITHER — its metabolism yields weaker products, giving ~3x higher AR affinity, no estrogenic load, and a wider muscle-vs-prostate window in rodents, but instead adds progestogenic/prolactin side effects testosterone lacks.
  • vs Nandrolone (19-nortestosterone / Deca)
    same: Both are 19-nor steroids with intrinsic PROGESTOGENIC activity at PGR and prolactin-mediated gyno/sexual side effects not fixed by AIs; both are non-aromatizing-dominant relative to testosterone.
    different: Nandrolone IS 5alpha-reduced (to weaker DHN) and aromatizes weakly; trenbolone's 4,9,11-triene structure blocks 5alpha-reduction and aromatization entirely, making it markedly more potent and more androgenic per dose, with stronger reported neuropsychiatric/sweating/insomnia effects.
  • vs Metribolone (R1881 / 17alpha-methyltrenbolone)
    same: Near-identical trienone core and very high AR affinity; metribolone is the standard radioligand for AR assays precisely because of this.
    different: The 17alpha-methyl group makes metribolone orally active but severely hepatotoxic, so it is essentially only a research ligand / rare oral, whereas trenbolone is used as injectable esters and is not 17-alkylated.
  • vs Selective androgen receptor modulators (e.g., enobosarm/ostarine)
    same: Both pursue the goal of muscle anabolism with reduced prostate/erythrocytic effect; trenbolone is sometimes described as having SARM-LIKE tissue selectivity in rodents.
    different: SARMs are non-steroidal, orally available, and engineered for tissue-selective AR conformations; trenbolone is a full steroidal AR agonist that also hits PR and GR, carries progestogenic and cardiovascular liabilities, and is far more potent and less selective in practice.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Andarine adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-rodent
    Both are AR agonists that suppress the HPG axis, so combined use deepens LH/FSH and endogenous-testosterone shutdown through the shared central mechanism. The overlap is specifically on AR occupancy and axis suppression; trenbolone's additional progesterone/glucocorticoid-receptor activity and renal strain are its own, not shared. andarine-s-4.json (AR agonist + HPG suppression, PMID 16099859, PMID 30503797); trenbolone.json (strong AR agonist, suppression, PGR/GR activity) Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone for combined suppression; separately track prolactin and kidney markers for trenbolone's own effects, and HDL for the shared androgenic lipid load.
  • Clenbuterol adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-rodent
    Cardiac remodeling from two directions. Clenbuterol drives a beta-adrenergic hypertrophic and pro-apoptotic program in the heart (cardiac apoptosis mediated through residual beta-1 agonism; hypertrophy via a fibroblast IGF-1 paracrine route), promoting left-ventricular hypertrophy and adverse remodeling on chronic misuse — this cardiac-hypertrophy limb is rodent/in-vitro grounded and was NOT detected over a 2-week human course. Androgens such as trenbolone independently promote pathological LV hypertrophy, fibrosis and impaired diastolic function via the androgen receptor (also rodent-grounded). Stacked in a 'cutting' context, the adrenergic cardiac load of clenbuterol adds to androgen-driven cardiac remodeling; a fatal forensic case documented LV hypertrophy in a user in whom clenbuterol plus multiple anabolic steroids including trenbolone were detected. Trenbolone additionally lacks aromatization and stresses the kidney, adding to overall cardiovascular strain. clenbuterol.json (beta-1 cardiac apoptosis PMID 15591297; fibroblast IGF-1 hypertrophy PMID 20577844; human LV mass unchanged at 2 wk PMID 40946331); trenbolone monograph (AR-driven cardiac strain, renal); PMID 31499423 (fatal LV hypertrophy, clenbuterol + AAS incl. trenbolone detected — human case, confounded) Watch: Blood pressure and resting heart rate; ECG and, where available, echocardiography for LV mass/wall thickness and function on chronic use; lipids and, for trenbolone, renal function. Chest pain, breathlessness or palpitations warrant stopping and cardiac evaluation.
  • Exemestane adds to a side effect ⚠ Established
    This is a wrong-tool / non-interaction that becomes a harm. Trenbolone does NOT aromatize, and its gynecomastia/sexual side effects are driven by progestogenic activity at PGR plus raised prolactin — NOT by estradiol — so exemestane (which acts only on aromatase) cannot address them; a dopamine agonist or SERM targets that pathway instead. Because exemestane has no beneficial target here, adding it to a non-aromatizing androgen simply suppresses whatever baseline estradiol exists, compounding estrogen-deprivation side effects (joint pain, low libido, bone loss). Trenbolone monograph mechanism_steps 3 (non-aromatizing) and 7 + side_effect_mechanisms (progestin/prolactin gyno not fixed by AIs); Prolactin/dopamine and Progesterone-receptor MoA cards; Aromatase MoA card (crashed E2 harms) Watch: Prolactin AND estradiol — if gyno/ED occurs on trenbolone with LOW or normal E2, prolactin/progestin is the cause and an AI will not help; suppressing already-low E2 with exemestane risks joint pain, low libido and bone loss.
  • GHRP-2 adds to a side effect ⚠ Established (19-nor prolactin/progestogenic effect; GHRP-2 prolactin rise in humans)
    Like nandrolone, trenbolone is a 19-nor with intrinsic progestogenic activity (binds the progesterone receptor with affinity above progesterone) that tends to raise prolactin. Stacked with GHRP-2's own non-selective prolactin bump, the two compound the prolactin load - additively worsening sexual dysfunction and prolactin-driven gynecomastia. Aromatase inhibitors do not address this because the driver is prolactin/progesterone, not estradiol. GHRP-2 monograph step 6 (prolactin rise); Trenbolone monograph (progestogenic, binds PGR above progesterone, raises prolactin, not AI-fixable) Watch: Prolactin; libido/erectile function; breast-tissue changes. A dopamine agonist targets only the prolactin arm, not trenbolone's progesterone-receptor activity.
  • Hexarelin adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    Two independent routes to the same problem — hyperprolactinemia. Hexarelin raises prolactin directly via pituitary GHS-R1a agonism (human-shown), while trenbolone is a 19-nortestosterone with intrinsic progestogenic activity that raises prolactin. Together they stack two prolactin-elevating mechanisms, worsening prolactin-driven effects (low libido, erectile dysfunction, gynecomastia) that an aromatase inhibitor will NOT fix because neither route is estrogen-mediated. Hexarelin monograph (prolactin rise, PMID 10404825/11238504); corpus PMID 15177700 (hexarelin raises prolactin); trenbolone monograph (19-nor progestogenic activity raises prolactin; AI does not fix) Watch: Serum prolactin; prolactin-related symptoms (libido/erectile changes, gynecomastia, galactorrhea). Estradiol control alone does not address this — the prolactin/progestin route is separate.
  • Masteron adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    Trenbolone is a strongly androgenic AR agonist, non-aromatizing, that also carries progestogenic (PGR) and prolactin-raising activity plus glucocorticoid-receptor binding; Masteron adds another potent non-aromatizing DHT-type androgen. Together they compound the non-aromatizing androgenic load (hair, skin, prostate, virilization) and the adverse lipid/cardiovascular strain both class-typically cause. CRITICAL correction: Masteron's 'anti-estrogen' reputation is AR-vs-ER crosstalk, NOT an aromatase inhibitor and NOT an anti-prolactin agent - so it does NOTHING for trenbolone's progesterone/prolactin-driven gyno or sexual dysfunction (that would require a dopamine agonist such as cabergoline, not Masteron). Masteron monograph steps 3 and 5, side_effect_mechanisms; Trenbolone monograph (progestogenic/prolactin, GR activity, non-aromatizing) Watch: HDL/LDL lipids and blood pressure (additive cardiovascular strain); prolactin if trenbolone-related sexual dysfunction or gyno signs appear (Masteron will NOT fix this); androgenic signs (hair, acne, prostate/PSA); virilization signs in women.
  • Methyltestosterone adds to a side effect ⚠ Speculative
    Both are non-aromatizing androgens (trenbolone is a 19-nor that does not aromatize; methyltestosterone acts as an aromatase inhibitor and does not raise serum estradiol), and both suppress the HPG axis. Stacked, neither supplies estrogen, so the pair can drive a combined low-estradiol state (impaired libido, erectile and joint/mood symptoms) while endogenous production is doubly shut down. This is a compounding effect, not a genuine opposition; the forum idea that methyltestosterone can 'supply estrogen' to trenbolone is unfounded because methyltestosterone is itself estrogen-lowering. Trenbolone's gynecomastia risk, when present, is progestin/prolactin-driven and is not offset by methyltestosterone.   • Cabergoline counteracts ↓ Contested
    Trenbolone is a non-aromatizing 19-nor that binds the progesterone receptor (more tightly than progesterone) and is reported to raise prolactin; elevated prolactin is thought to contribute to 'tren dick', low libido and gyno that estrogen blockers cannot fix. Cabergoline, a D2 agonist, lowers prolactin at the lactotroph and would counteract the prolactin arm of these effects. As with all 19-nors, it addresses only the prolactin arm; the progesterone-receptor arm remains untreated. Certainty is limited because trenbolone has no human clinical data at all and its prolactin elevation in humans is compound-specific-thin and largely anecdotal. cabergoline.json mechanism_steps#7; trenbolone monograph step 7 (PGR binding shown PMID 11252818; prolactin/downstream in humans Contested/anecdotal, no human trials); corpus MoA 'Prolactin & dopamine' Watch: Serum prolactin to confirm an actual elevation and to avoid over-suppression. If sexual dysfunction persists after prolactin normalizes, suspect the progestin (PGR) arm rather than prolactin. Because trenbolone itself is reported to be cardiotoxic/hypertensive and cabergoline can cause orthostatic hypotension, watch blood pressure and orthostatic symptoms; also monitor for impulse-control changes.
  • Enclomiphene works against Established
    Trenbolone is a strong androgen-receptor agonist that potently suppresses the HPG axis (GnRH→LH→FSH), collapsing endogenous testosterone and spermatogenesis. Enclomiphene raises central LH/FSH to drive the testis — so while trenbolone is present its suppressive feedback works directly against enclomiphene's axis-stimulating action, and enclomiphene cannot override active androgen suppression (it is used to attempt an HPTA restart only after such compounds clear). A wrinkle unique to trenbolone: it is progestogenic and tends to raise prolactin, and enclomiphene does nothing to counter that (prolactin control needs a dopamine agonist), so the progestin/prolactin-driven side-effect channel is not addressed by the SERM. Enclomiphene monograph (caveats: suppression cannot be overridden; mechanism_steps HPG drive); Trenbolone monograph (AR agonist, HPG suppression, progestogenic/prolactin, non-aromatizing); corpus MoA card 'HPG axis' Watch: LH/FSH (suppressed while trenbolone is active; should rise once it clears and enclomiphene drives the axis); total/free testosterone; prolactin (trenbolone-driven, not fixed by the SERM); estradiol; semen analysis for fertility recovery. Watch mood given both agents' CNS effects.
  • Insulin works against Contested
    Anabolic-androgenic steroids can impair glucose tolerance and induce insulin resistance at supraphysiologic doses; a case report describes new-onset diabetes (glucose ~1166 mg/dL) in a young bodybuilder using trenbolone plus GH plus testosterone. AAS-driven insulin resistance opposes insulin's glucose-lowering, shifting the effective insulin requirement upward, while insulin's own steep, unforgiving dose-response and lethal-low risk remain — chronic hyperglycemic drift set against acute crash risk. PMID 21558143 (case report: trenbolone + GH + testosterone new-onset diabetes; AAS decrease glucose tolerance / increase insulin resistance — confounded, single case); insulin monograph dose_response Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c for insulin-resistance drift; treat confusion/drowsiness as a possible insulin low regardless of glucose 'feel'. Use objective glucose monitoring, since AAS-induced resistance changes how much insulin effect is present at a given glucose.

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

"Developed in the 1960s; trenbolone acetate was commercialized for human muscle-wasting use in Europe as Parabolan (trenbolone hexahydrobenzylcarbonate, Negma, France) but that human formulation was discontinued (~1997). Its enduring, legal role is VETERINARY: trenbolone acetate is FDA-approved as a cattle growth-promotant ear implant (e.g., Finaplix, and combination implants such as Revalor/Synovex with estradiol) and is widely used in US beef production — the source of the large environmental-fate literature. It is NOT approved for human use anywhere. In the US it is a Schedule III controlled substance under the Anabolic Steroid Control Act; it is a WADA-prohibited substance in sport (detected by LC-MS/MS as trenbolone/epitrenbolone). The EU bans hormonal growth promoters in cattle, a long-standing point of trade dispute with the US."

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • No human pharmacokinetic data exist (half-life, bioavailability, clearance of any trenbolone ester in people are inferred, not measured).
  • Whether the SARM-like muscle-vs-prostate dose window seen in rats translates to humans at any dose is untested.
  • Trenbolone-specific human cardiovascular, renal, and hepatic risk is unquantified — current evidence is extrapolated from mixed-AAS cohorts.
  • The mechanism and reversibility of 'trensomnia,' night sweats, and acute neuropsychiatric effects are poorly defined; the rodent neurodegeneration/Abeta signal has unknown human relevance.
  • Relative contributions of PR agonism vs prolactin elevation to trenbolone's sexual/gyno side effects, and the best monitoring threshold, are not established.
  • Long-term consequences of chronic supraphysiologic AR + PR + GR co-modulation (vs testosterone's AR-dominant profile) on metabolism, mood, and fertility recovery are unknown.

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only — NOT a protocol. Bloodwork to watch over time: lipid panel (expect HDL suppression / adverse LDL shift), hematocrit/hemoglobin (polycythemia risk, though rodent data suggest trenbolone raises it less than testosterone), comprehensive metabolic panel with eGFR and cystatin C plus urine albumin/protein (glomerular injury signal — note creatinine is confounded by high muscle mass), liver enzymes (ALT/AST/GGT, especially relevant for any 17-alkylated trenbolone analog), and an endocrine panel: total/free testosterone, LH/FSH (HPG suppression), and notably PROLACTIN and progesterone-pathway markers given the progestogenic mechanism. Estradiol is less informative because trenbolone does not aromatize — a normal E2 with breast symptoms points to the prolactin/progestin route. Cardiac signs/screening: blood pressure, and given the human cohort data, awareness of left-ventricular and coronary risk with cumulative use. Clinical signs to track: gynecomastia/nipple changes, libido and erectile function, mood/aggression/sleep disturbance, edema, jaundice, reduced urine output or foamy urine, and chest symptoms. Anyone using should be encouraged to seek medical evaluation; this list is for awareness and clinician discussion, not endorsement."

Caveats

Direction/magnitude caveats for teaching accuracy: (1) The headline 'tissue-selective / SARM-like, prostate-sparing' finding is from castrated/intact RATS at controlled low doses (PMID 21266670) — at the HIGH dose even trenbolone enlarged the prostate (+68%), so 'prostate-safe' is dose-dependent and species-limited, NOT a human guarantee. (2) There are no human efficacy or safety trials; potency claims relative to testosterone come from receptor-binding and animal data, not human dosing. (3) The non-genomic GPCR->EGFR->IGF-1R cascade (PMID 27285910) was characterized in BOVINE satellite cells; its weight in human muscle is unproven. (4) PR/SHBG affinity figures (PMID 11252818) used bovine progestin receptor and recombinant human AR in vitro — good for direction, approximate for exact human numbers. (5) The progestin/prolactin -> gyno link is well established for the 19-nor class and supported by curated mechanism cards, but compound-specific human prolactin data for trenbolone itself is thin. (6) Known/реported harms (cardiotoxicity, neurotoxicity/aggression, nephrotoxicity, severe HPG-axis suppression, night sweats, 'tren cough') are largely anecdote/case-report level and were NOT the focus of this mechanism search. This is mechanism education only — not an endorsement, and explicitly not dosing or how-to guidance.
