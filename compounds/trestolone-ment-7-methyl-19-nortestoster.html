Trestolone

Main target: Androgen receptor. Backed by human data.

EvidenceHuman evidence
ApprovalNot FDA-approved

A lab-built androgen that hijacks the same intracellular receptor as testosterone but skips the prostate's amplifier enzyme — making it pound-for-pound far stronger, yet it still turns into an estrogen and still shuts down your own hormones.

Strong human data 18 citations
Class
Synthetic anabolic-androgenic steroid (AAS) — a 19-nortestosterone (nandrolone-family) derivative with a 7α-methyl group
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR) — a nuclear (ligand-activated transcription factor) receptor
Human status
Investigational only — NEVER approved by any regulator. Developed primarily by the Population Council as a male hormonal contraceptive and as a possible androgen-replacement therapy. Human data are limited to small Phase I/II-scale trials (implants/injections, tens of subjects): documented gonadotropin/sperm suppression (Noé 1999 PMID 10469681; Walton 2007 RCT PMID 17460095) and maintenance of libido/mood in hypogonadal men (Anderson 1999 RCT PMID 10522995). Development stalled in part due to short duration of action and dosing/delivery problems. No long-term human safety data (cardiovascular, prostate cancer, fertility recovery) exist. Today 'trestolone acetate' is sold by unregulated online research-chemical vendors and used off-label by bodybuilders — entirely outside any approved or monitored medical setting (Piper 2026 PMID 41407450). HARM-REDUCTION NOTE: human evidence is thin and short-term; this is teaching material, not medical guidance, and contains no dosing.

At a glance

  • Potent androgenic + anabolic gene activation via the androgen receptor (~10x testosterone in transactivation/anabolic assays) — Evidence-in vitro + historical anabolic data
  • Prostate-sparing RELATIVE to testosterone, because it resists 5-alpha-reductase amplification to DHT (not zero prostate effect) — Evidence-rodent/primate; residual in-vitro human effect
  • Aromatizes to 7α-methyl-estradiol, a potent estrogen → estrogenic side effects (water retention, gyno risk) are plausible; 'estrogen-free' is a myth — Evidence-in vitro
  • Strong suppression of LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone and sperm production (the contraceptive mechanism; also full natural-axis shutdown) — Evidence-human (small trials)
  • Maintains libido, erections, and mood like testosterone in hypogonadal men — Evidence-human (small RCT)
  • Lowers HDL ('good') cholesterol like other androgens — Evidence-human
  • NOT 17-alpha-alkylated and given by implant/injection in trials, so the classic oral-AAS liver toxicity is not the expected profile — Established (structure) / hepatotoxicity Speculative-for-MENT
  • Short-acting / hard to dose steadily; never approved; now sold by unregulated online vendors — Evidence-human PK + documented availability

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

7 steps6 human 1 established

  1. MENT enters the cell and binds the androgen receptor — harder than testosterone does Established (in vitro + binding data; human-relevant receptor)

    Trestolone is a small fat-soluble steroid, so it slips straight through the cell membrane. Inside, it latches onto the androgen receptor (AR), the same docking protein testosterone uses. The key difference: MENT grips the AR more tightly than testosterone, and that tighter grip is the main reason it punches above its weight. There is no separate 'MENT receptor' — it is borrowing testosterone's machinery, just more effectively. Kumar et al. 1999, J Steroid Biochem Mol Biol, PMID 10704910 (abstract + in vitro CAT-reporter and AR-binding data): 'the high potency of MENT is primarily related to its higher affinity to AR.'

  2. The activated receptor acts as a gene switch (the genomic mechanism) Established (mechanism class); MENT-specific transactivation: Evidence-in vitro

    Once MENT is bound, the AR changes shape, sheds its chaperone proteins, pairs up, and travels into the cell nucleus. There it sits on specific stretches of DNA (androgen-response elements) and switches muscle- and male-trait genes ON — telling the cell to make more protein. This 'bind receptor -> go to nucleus -> turn on genes' route is the classic genomic mechanism shared by all anabolic steroids. The muscle-building (anabolic) and masculinizing (androgenic) effects are the downstream result of this gene-transcription step, not a direct chemical action. García-Becerra et al. 2012, Reproduction, PMID 22065861: MENT was 'ten times more potent than testosterone and dihydrotestosterone in activating AR-driven gene expression' in transactivation assays; response blocked by antiandrogen (hydroxyflutamide) in Kumar 1999 PMID 10704910, confirming AR-specificity.

  3. It ignores the prostate's 5-alpha-reductase amplifier — the headline differentiator Evidence-rodent + primate for the sparing effect; in vitro human prostate cells show residual stimulation

    In the prostate and skin, testosterone normally gets converted by the enzyme 5-alpha-reductase into DHT, a much stronger androgen — which is why testosterone hits the prostate hard. MENT's 7α-methyl group blocks that conversion: it is NOT amplified into a stronger form in the prostate. So while MENT is super-potent at muscle, it does not get the local 'boost' in prostate tissue that testosterone does. This is the basis of its 'prostate-sparing' reputation. Caveat: 'spares the prostate' is relative, not zero — in cultured human prostate cells MENT still stimulated growth, just less than DHT. Kumar et al. 1992, Endocrinology, PMID 1597164 (not amplified in male reproductive tract; confirmed with 5α-reductase inhibitor); primate prostate-sparing: Cummings et al. 1998, JCEM, PMID 9851754; residual human prostate-cell growth: Mendoza et al. 2009, Int J Androl, PMID 18637152.

  4. It DOES aromatize — into a potent estrogen (so it is not 'estrogen-free') Evidence-in vitro (placental microsomes + receptor assays); human in-vivo magnitude not well quantified

    A common forum claim is that MENT can't convert to estrogen. That's wrong. The aromatase enzyme turns MENT into 7α-methyl-estradiol, which behaves as a strong estrogen — in lab tests roughly as potent as the body's own estradiol at the estrogen receptor. Practically this means estrogen-side effects (e.g., water retention, gynecomastia risk) are biologically plausible with MENT, contrary to the hype that it's a 'dry' compound. LaMorte et al. 1994, J Steroid Biochem Mol Biol, PMID 8142308 (aromatization to 7α-methyl-estradiol by human placental microsomes); 7α-methyl-E2 'potent estrogen activity via both ER subtypes with efficiency similar to natural E2': García-Becerra 2012, PMID 22065861.

  5. Because it is a strong androgen, it shuts down your own hormone axis (HPG suppression) Evidence-human (small implant trials in healthy/hypogonadal men)

    The brain (hypothalamus and pituitary) constantly samples blood androgen and estrogen levels. When a potent androgen like MENT floods the system, the brain reads 'plenty of hormone' and dials down its signals LH and FSH. LH normally tells the testes to make testosterone; FSH supports sperm production. With both suppressed, the testes go quiet: natural testosterone drops and sperm counts fall. This is exactly why it was developed as a male contraceptive — and exactly why it suppresses natural function in a fitness context. In men, MENT acetate implants demonstrably suppressed LH, FSH and endogenous testosterone. Noé et al. 1999, Hum Reprod, PMID 10469681 (subdermal MENT-acetate implants suppressed gonadotropins and testosterone in healthy men); Walton et al. 2007, J Androl, PMID 17460095 (RCT: rapid spermatogenic suppression w/ etonogestrel).

  6. Not a 17-alpha-alkylated oral — different liver/lipid story than oral AAS Established (structural fact); HDL drop: Evidence-human; broader oral-style hepatotoxicity: not demonstrated for MENT (largely Speculative/extrapolated)

    Unlike classic oral steroids (e.g., methyltestosterone, Dianabol, Anadrol), MENT is NOT 17-alpha-alkylated — the chemical trick that lets a steroid survive the liver as a pill but also causes the characteristic oral-steroid liver toxicity (cholestasis, enzyme elevations). MENT's potency comes from the 7α-methyl group instead, and it was delivered by implant/injection in trials, not as a liver-stressing oral. So the severe hepatotoxicity profile of 17-alkylated orals should NOT be assumed for MENT. That said: like other androgens it still lowered HDL ('good') cholesterol in human trials, so a negative lipid effect is real even without the 17-alkyl liver mechanism. Structure/class: Nieschlag et al. 2013, Contraception, PMID 23063338. HDL-C fell in men on MENT regimen: Walton et al. 2007, J Androl, PMID 17460095. (No 17α-alkyl group = the oral-AAS hepatotoxic mechanism does not apply.)

  7. Net effect: a high-potency androgen that maintains male function but with a short-acting, hard-to-dose profile Evidence-human (small trials) for efficacy; real-world unregulated use: documented but uncontrolled

    Putting it together: in hypogonadal men MENT maintained libido, erections and mood comparably to testosterone, confirming it is a genuine, fully functional androgen in humans — not just a lab curiosity. But it is rapidly cleared and was difficult to dose steadily (implants ran out of drug, and suppression wasn't sustained), which is part of why it never reached approval. Today it is sold by unregulated online vendors as 'trestolone acetate,' meaning real-world use is happening with human-grade evidence that is thin and entirely off-label. Anderson et al. 1999, JCEM, PMID 10522995 (maintains sexual behavior/mood in hypogonadal men; RCT crossover); availability via online distributors + human metabolite work: Piper et al. 2026, Drug Test Anal, PMID 41407450.

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. Trestolone/MENT (7α-methyl-19-nortestosterone) is a pure, potent AGONIST of the nuclear androgen receptor (AR), with NO clinically meaningful antagonist activity. CERTAINTY: Established (rodent/in-vitro receptor data; corroborated by human transactivation). KEY NUMBERS: In Kumar et al. (PMID 10704910), MENT bound rat AR with the HIGHEST affinity of all androgens tested, and its high in-vivo potency 'is primarily related to its higher affinity to AR' — binding affinity tracked bioactivity tightly (unlike DHT/19-NT, where metabolism distorts the relationship). In a human cell transactivation assay (García-Becerra, PMID 22065861), MENT was ~10x MORE POTENT than both testosterone (T) AND dihydrotestosterone (DHT) at activating AR-driven gene expression — this 10-fold figure is the most-cited potency number and is Evidence-human (HEK293/HeLa reporter cells). The classic Sundaram review (PMID 8489761) frames MENT as a 'considerably more potent' androgen than T. SELECTIVITY / OFF-TARGET: (1) Estrogen receptor — MENT itself binds ER WEAKLY: ED50 for displacing [3H]-E2 was 250 ng vs 70 pg for estradiol, a ~3,500-fold LOWER ER affinity (Beri, PMID 9879986, Evidence-rodent). HOWEVER its aromatized metabolite 7α-methyl-estradiol is a FULL, potent estrogen at both ERα and ERβ, with efficiency similar to natural E2 (PMID 22065861) — this is the mechanistic basis of MENT's strong estrogenic effects. (2) Progesterone receptor — MENT binds PR with affinity comparable to progesterone and is as efficient as progesterone at PR-driven transactivation (PMIDs 9879986, 22065861); clinically minor but real progestational activity. (3) SHBG — as a 19-nor/7α-methyl steroid, MENT binds SHBG poorly, so a high free fraction circulates. The decisive structural trick: the 7α-methyl group STERICALLY BLOCKS 5α-reductase, so MENT is NOT converted to a more potent DHT-like metabolite in prostate/skin/scalp — it acts on AR as the parent molecule everywhere (PMIDs 8489761, 17460095; Established). Note the contrast with the related 11β-methyl-19-NT, which 'avidly binds and activates human androgen AND progesterone receptors' (PMID 30252057) — the 19-nor backbone confers shared PR cross-reactivity across this family.

Signalling cascade. Classic genomic AR signalling (Established mechanism, corpus-grounded). 1) LIGAND BINDING: lipophilic MENT diffuses across the plasma membrane and binds the C-terminal ligand-binding domain (LBD) of cytoplasmic AR, inducing a conformational change. Because the 7α-methyl blocks 5α-reduction, the SAME molecule that enters the cell is the one that occupies AR in every tissue (no prostate-specific amplification step, unlike T→DHT). 2) CHAPERONE RELEASE: the unliganded AR is held in the cytoplasm by HSP90 and 14-3-3 chaperones; ligand binding triggers their dissociation, unmasking the nuclear localization signal. 3) NUCLEAR TRANSLOCATION + DIMERIZATION: AR translocates to the nucleus and homodimerizes. 4) DNA BINDING: the dimer's zinc-finger DNA-binding domain docks onto androgen response elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer regions of target genes. 5) COACTIVATOR RECRUITMENT + TRANSCRIPTION: the ligand-induced LBD surface recruits p160 coactivators (e.g., SRC-1) and the intrinsically disordered N-terminal domain mediates the bulk of transactivation, driving RNA-pol-II transcription of anabolic/androgenic genes (muscle protein synthesis, erythropoiesis via EPO, gonadotropin feedback genes). Because MENT also produces 7α-methyl-E2, a PARALLEL estrogen-receptor genomic cascade runs simultaneously in estrogen-target tissues (bone, breast, brain, hypothalamus). 6) NON-GENOMIC: 19-nortestosterone-class androgens can rapidly, transiently modulate kinase cascades (e.g., hippocampal MAPK, NMDA-GluN2B phosphorylation) in an AR-dependent, flutamide-blockable manner (Evidence-rodent; mechanistically plausible for MENT but not specifically demonstrated for it).

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Parenteral by design. As the free alcohol MENT it has a SHORT duration — development focused on (a) intramuscular micronized aqueous suspension (depot-like, used in early human PK), and (b) subdermal silastic IMPLANTS releasing the more lipophilic ester MENT ACETATE (trestolone acetate, CDB-903) for sustained 6-12 month delivery. Implant acetate is rapidly hydrolyzed on release, so MENT is the active circulating moiety. ORAL use is impractical for the parent (extensive first-pass) — this is why the related family member 11β-MNT was developed as an oral prodrug (dodecylcarbonate); no analogous approved oral MENT product exists. CERTAINTY: Established for IM/implant (human data).
Absorption / bioavailability
After single IM micronized aqueous doses of 2/4/8 mg in healthy men, peak serum MENT was reached ~1-2 h post-injection and was dose-dependent — notably NON-linear: doubling the dose raised peak concentration only ~60% (PMID 9194649, Evidence-human, n=18). Implants (e.g., 2x115 mg MENT-acetate) produced stable plasma MENT of ~1.3-1.4 nmol/L over 6 weeks (PMID 10522995). High free fraction due to low SHBG binding. Oral bioavailability of parent MENT: low (not a developed route).
Half-life
Short. Mean clearance after IM dosing was 1790 ± 140 L/day (PMID 9194649) — a high clearance rate consistent with a short circulating half-life of the free steroid (hours), which is precisely why sustained-release implants were required for therapeutic/contraceptive use. A specific terminal t½ in hours is not crisply reported in the human corpus (key_unknown).
Metabolism
Hepatic. In vitro human liver work (PMID 18992267, Evidence-human-tissue + rodent) identified three principal pathways: (i) 3-hydroxylation (human liver cytosol, 3α/3β-HSD), (ii) 16α-hydroxylation (human hepatic microsomes), and (iii) oxidation to 7α-methyl-19-norandrostenedione (human microsomes). Specific enzymes: in HUMANS, CYP3A4 plus 17β-HSD and 3-HSD; in rats, CYP2C11 and 3-HSD. SEPARATELY, MENT is a substrate for AROMATASE (CYP19A1), converting it to the potent estrogen 7α-methyl-estradiol — a metabolically important, clinically consequential pathway (PMIDs 22065861, 9879986). Reinvestigation of human urinary metabolism (PMID 41407450, 2026) detected ~50 candidate metabolites across unconjugated, glucuronidated and sulfated fractions, with two metabolites suitable as doping-control markers.
Excretion
Both urinary AND fecal. In castrated rats given (3)H-MENT, radioactivity was excreted roughly equally via feces and urine by ~30 h, with high/persistent radioactivity in liver and duodenum (enterohepatic handling) and specific uptake in prostate and seminal vesicles at 6 h (PMID 18992267, Evidence-rodent). Human urinary excretion confirmed (conjugated metabolites; PMID 41407450).
Notes
KEY PK takeaways: (1) The 7α-methyl group makes MENT RESISTANT to 5α-reductase — no DHT-like amplification in prostate/skin/scalp, the defining 'prostate-sparing' feature (PMIDs 8489761, 17460095). (2) MENT IS aromatized (to 7α-methyl-E2); it is NOT a non-aromatizing 19-nor like nandrolone in that respect — estrogenic activity is intrinsic, not avoidable by simply being 19-nor. (3) The acetate ester is a sustained-release/implant strategy, not a true oral prodrug. (4) Short native half-life mandates depot/implant delivery; bolus injection gives sharp peaks (PMID 9194649).

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

HUMAN dose-response is only partially characterized and NO efficacy dose-response for muscle/strength gain in athletes exists (key human-data gap). ANTIGONADOTROPIC (suppression of the HPG axis) human dose-response IS quantified: six daily IM injections of 1/2/4 mg MENT in healthy men suppressed testosterone, LH and FSH in a dose-dependent, statistically significant manner; the 4 mg/day dose produced a 74% fall in testosterone, 70% fall in LH, and 57% fall in FSH (PMID 9194649, Evidence-human, n=24). PEAK levels were dose-dependent but sublinear (doubling dose raised Cmax ~60%). CONTRACEPTIVE/REPLACEMENT (implant) dose-response: 2x115 mg MENT-acetate implants held plasma MENT at ~1.3-1.4 nmol/L and matched testosterone enanthate (200 mg IM q3wk) for sexual interest, erections (including NPT) and mood in hypogonadal men over 6 weeks (PMID 10522995, Evidence-human RCT crossover, n=20). In a contraceptive trial, 2 MENT implants + etonogestrel drove sperm to <1x10^6/mL by 12 weeks in 8/10 men — comparable initial suppression to testosterone — but suppression was NOT sustained because MENT release from the implants declined, illustrating that delivery (not intrinsic potency) was the limiting variable (PMID 17460095, Evidence-human RCT, n=29). POTENCY ANCHOR (animal/in-vitro): MENT is ~10x more potent than testosterone for AR transactivation (PMID 22065861) and historically reported ~10x more potent anabolically than testosterone in 1960s cancer-era animal work (cited in PMID 41407450) — meaning the active doses are far smaller than testosterone, but the human anabolic/myotrophic dose-response curve (mg → lean mass) has never been formally mapped. The aromatization-driven estrogenic effect is also dose-dependent and, per gram, MENT generates more estrogenic signal than equivalent testosterone because the 7α-methyl-E2 product is a full estrogen.

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Testosterone
    same: Both are AR agonists that suppress the HPG axis, support libido/erection/mood, raise hemoglobin, lower HDL, and ARE aromatized to an estrogen.
    different: MENT is ~10x more potent at AR (PMID 22065861) so active doses are far smaller; MENT is 5α-reductase RESISTANT so it does NOT amplify to a DHT-like metabolite in prostate/skin/scalp (PSA fell with MENT — 'prostate-sparing'; PMID 17460095); MENT's aromatized product (7α-methyl-E2) is a full potent estrogen, so per-gram estrogenicity is high; MENT is short-acting and needs implant/depot delivery.
  • vs Nandrolone (19-nortestosterone / 'deca')
    same: Both are 19-nor AAS sharing the nandrolone backbone, low SHBG binding, and progestational cross-reactivity at PR.
    different: Nandrolone IS a 5α-reductase substrate (reduced to the WEAKER 5α-dihydronandrolone, making it relatively prostate-mild by a DIFFERENT route), whereas MENT is reductase-RESISTANT (acts as parent everywhere). MENT is markedly more AR-potent. Both aromatize, but MENT's 7α-methyl-E2 is a notably strong estrogen.
  • vs 11β-methyl-19-nortestosterone (11β-MNT / 11β-MNTDC)
    same: Sister Population Council/NICHD 19-nor male-contraceptive candidate; potent dual AR + PR agonist; designed to suppress gonadotropins with reduced prostate stimulation.
    different: 11β-MNT was advanced as an ORAL prodrug (dodecylcarbonate / 11β-MNTDC) taken with food (PMID 30252057), whereas MENT was developed for IM/implant; 11β-MNT's 11β-methyl confers strong intrinsic progestational activity and it does not require 5α-reduction (PMID 20599615).
  • vs Dimethandrolone undecanoate (DMAU / 7α,11β-dimethyl-19-NT)
    same: Same research lineage; potent AR+PR agonist 19-nor androgen; shares the 7α-methyl 5α-reductase-blocking motif (prostate-sparing).
    different: DMAU is specifically NOT aromatizable (the 11β-methyl blocks recombinant human aromatase; PMID 18555683), so it produces little/no estrogen — the OPPOSITE of MENT, which aromatizes readily to a potent estrogen. DMAU is an oral undecanoate prodrug.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Nandrolone adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    Trestolone (MENT) is also a 19-nortestosterone and binds the progesterone receptor with affinity comparable to progesterone, so it shares nandrolone's progestogenic (PGR) gyno/sexual-dysfunction liability. Uniquely, trestolone aromatizes to a POTENT estrogen (7-alpha-methyl-estradiol, a full ER agonist), adding a strong estrogenic gyno driver on top. History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

"Origin: Synthesized at the Population Council (developers of nortestosterone-class steroids); its anabolic properties were already noted in 1960s cancer-research work, where MENT was reported ~10x more anabolically potent than testosterone (recounted in PMID 41407450). It carries multiple legacy research codes — CDB-903 / RU-27333 / NSC-69948 / NSC-142229 / U-15614 (ChEMBL CHEMBL452329, USAN 'trestolone' assigned 1971). Development focus: the Population Council/NICHD pursued MENT (as MENT-acetate implants, sometimes branded 'Mentr') chiefly as (a) a long-acting androgen-replacement therapy for hypogonadal men and (b) a component of a long-acting male hormonal contraceptive (paired with a progestin such as etonogestrel/Nestorone or an LHRH analogue) — PMIDs 8489761, 9194649, 10522995, 17460095. Regulatory status: NEVER approved for marketing for any indication anywhere; clinical development stalled (max clinical phase ~2 per ChEMBL), partly because implant delivery could not sustain adequate release (PMID 17460095) and the field shifted toward oral 19-nor prodrugs (11β-MNTDC, DMAU). Anti-doping: prohibited as an exogenous anabolic agent under the WADA Prohibited List (S1); 2026 work re-characterized its human urinary metabolites specifically to close a doping-detection gap, noting MENT is 'readily available via online distributors' (PMID 41407450). Current real-world status: an unapproved research chemical widely sold grey-market as 'trestolone acetate' for bodybuilding; not a legal medicine; in the US it falls under anabolic-steroid control (Schedule III class)."

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • No human anabolic dose-response curve: the mg-dose → lean-mass/strength relationship in men has never been formally measured (the 10x potency figure comes from receptor/transactivation and 1960s animal anabolic assays, not modern human body-composition trials).
  • Crisp human terminal half-life of free MENT is not clearly published — only a high clearance rate (1790 L/day) and the practical need for implants; exact t½ in hours is uncertain (PMID 9194649).
  • Magnitude and management of estrogenic load in real-world high-dose use: how much 7α-methyl-E2 is generated per mg at supraphysiologic doses, and whether aromatase inhibitors blunt it without sacrificing the bone/lipid benefits, is unquantified.
  • Long-term cardiovascular and prostate safety at bodybuilding-scale doses are unknown; clinical data exist only at modest replacement/contraceptive exposures.
  • Fertility-recovery kinetics after prolonged high-dose use are uncharacterized (contraceptive trials used low-dose, short-term exposure).
  • Whether MENT's PR (progestational) activity meaningfully contributes to mood, libido, or metabolic side effects in men at abuse doses is not established — PR cross-reactivity is documented in vitro (PMIDs 9879986, 22065861) but its clinical weight is unclear.

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (NOT dosing guidance). BLOODWORK: (1) Hematocrit/hemoglobin — androgens raise red-cell mass; watch for erythrocytosis/polycythemia (HDL fell and Hb rose in human MENT trials, PMID 17460095). (2) Lipid panel, especially HDL-C — expect HDL suppression and an atherogenic shift; track total/LDL/triglycerides too. (3) Estradiol — because MENT aromatizes to a potent estrogen (7α-methyl-E2), standard E2 immunoassays may UNDER-read the 7α-methyl metabolite, so interpret estrogen status by SYMPTOMS (gynecomastia, nipple tenderness, water retention/edema, blood pressure) as much as by a number. (4) LH, FSH and endogenous testosterone — will be suppressed; useful to gauge HPG shutdown and, after cessation, recovery (PMID 9194649). (5) PSA — expected to be LOWER than on testosterone (prostate-sparing), but still baseline + monitor (PMID 17460095). (6) Liver enzymes (ALT/AST, bilirubin, GGT) — MENT is not 17α-alkylated, but it is hepatically metabolized (CYP3A4/17β-HSD/3-HSD) and grey-market product purity is unverifiable (PMID 18992267). (7) Blood pressure and signs of fluid retention. CLINICAL SIGNS to watch: new/worsening gynecomastia, mood changes or aggression, loss of libido (a paradoxical sign of UNDER-dosing/insufficient free androgen, seen when MENT delivery waned, PMID 17460095), testicular atrophy, and fertility concerns. Because human safety data exist only at low replacement/contraceptive exposures, anyone using grey-market trestolone should treat all of the above as actively monitored, not assumed safe — and seek clinician oversight."}

Caveats

HUMAN-DATA GAP IS LARGE: every human trial was small (n in the tens), short, and aimed at contraception/replacement — not at the high-dose physique use seen in fitness circles. Long-term safety in humans is essentially unstudied. FORUM-HYPE CORRECTIONS: (1) 'MENT doesn't convert to estrogen' is FALSE — it aromatizes to a potent estrogen, 7α-methyl-estradiol (PMID 8142308, 22065861). (2) 'Prostate-safe' overstates it — it is prostate-SPARING relative to testosterone because it dodges 5α-reductase, but human prostate cells still respond to it (PMID 18637152). (3) The '10x' potency figure comes from animal anabolic assays and in-vitro AR transactivation (PMID 10704910, 22065861, 41407450) — it is NOT a clean 'use 1/10th the dose' instruction and shouldn't be read as a dosing rule. CERTAINTY MIX: the receptor/genomic mechanism and 5α-reductase resistance are well-established at the molecular level; the magnitude of estrogenic and prostate effects, and any high-dose toxicity, rest on in-vitro/animal data or are extrapolated. HEPATOTOXICITY: because MENT is not 17α-alkylated, the severe oral-AAS liver toxicity should not simply be assumed — but it still lowers HDL, and cardiovascular risk from suppressed natural hormones + altered lipids is a real concern. Backed by human data.
MENT enters the cell and binds the androgen receptor — harder than testosterone does. Trestolone is a small fat-soluble steroid, so it slips straight through the cell membrane. Inside, it latches onto the androgen receptor (AR), the same docking protein testosterone uses. The key difference: MENT grips the AR more tightly than testosterone, and that tighter grip is the main reason it punches above its weight. There is no separate 'MENT receptor' — it is borrowing testosterone's machinery, just more effectively. Kumar et al. 1999, J Steroid Biochem Mol Biol, PMID 10704910 (abstract + in vitro CAT-reporter and AR-binding data): 'the high potency of MENT is primarily related to its higher affinity to AR.'
