Setmelanotide

A brain-targeted MC4R agonist that lowers hunger AND raises calorie-burning — the first drug to repair a broken satiety circuit rather than just suppress appetite. Its dramatic effects are confined to rare melanocortin-pathway and hypothalamic obesity, not common obesity.

Limited human dataFDA-approved 16 citations
Class
Melanocortin-4 receptor (MC4R) agonist; synthetic cyclic octapeptide (disulfide-bridged), administered by subcutaneous injection
Primary target
Hypothalamic melanocortin-4 receptor (MC4R), a class A GPCR (canonically Gs-coupled, with setmelanotide showing Gq/11-biased signaling) that is the convergence point of the leptin–melanocortin energy-balance pathway
Human status
FDA-approved. First approval Nov 2020 for chronic weight management in patients ≥6 y with obesity due to biallelic POMC, PCSK1, or LEPR deficiency (First Approval review: Markham A, Drugs 2021, PMID 33638809). Approval expanded to Bardet-Biedl syndrome (2022, on the pivotal phase 3, PMID 36356613) and down to age ≥2 y (VENTURE trial, PMID 39549719). In 2024–2025 it was approved (US/EU) for acquired hypothalamic obesity, supported by a positive phase 2 trial (PMID 38697184; a larger phase 3, NCT05774756, is ongoing/has results). Evidence is strongest as an ESTABLISHED treatment within these rare MC4R-pathway indications; it is NOT approved for, and has not shown comparable efficacy in, common polygenic obesity.

At a glance

  • Reduces hunger / hyperphagia (large drops in patient-reported hunger scores in MC4R-pathway obesity; measured in small subsets)
  • Produces clinically meaningful weight and fat loss in genetically defined and acquired hypothalamic obesity (small, mostly open-label trials)
  • Increased resting energy expenditure (~6.4%, ~111 kcal/24h) in one short (~3-day) human calorimetry study — distinct from GLP-1 agents; long-term durability unknown
  • Shifted substrate use toward fat oxidation (lower respiratory quotient) in that same short study
  • Skin hyperpigmentation and darkening of moles/nevi (on-mechanism, via MC1R)
  • Injection-site reactions; nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (GI, common early)
  • Spontaneous penile erections in males; changes in sexual arousal (melanocortin class effect; in human labeling)
  • Efficacy is indication-specific: strong in rare MC4R-pathway/hypothalamic obesity, NOT established in common polygenic obesity

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Leptin signals fullness through POMC neurons Established

    When fat stores and a recent meal are adequate, leptin (and insulin) activate POMC (pro-opiomelanocortin) neurons in the hypothalamic arcuate nucleus. These neurons cleave POMC into alpha-MSH, the natural 'you're full' messenger. Setmelanotide is a synthetic stand-in for alpha-MSH — it works downstream of leptin, which is why it can help even when leptin signaling upstream is broken (e.g., LEPR deficiency). PMID 33684608 (Yeo et al., Mol Metab 2021); PMID 37365323 (Sweeney et al., Nat Rev Endocrinol 2023)

  2. Setmelanotide binds and activates MC4R in the hypothalamus Established

    The drug is a cyclic 8-amino-acid peptide that docks into MC4R on second-order neurons (notably in the paraventricular nucleus). Cryo-EM of the setmelanotide–MC4R–Gs complex (2.6 Å) shows it engages the receptor's orthosteric pocket much like alpha-MSH, using a calcium ion as a co-factor and displacing transmembrane helix 6 to switch the receptor 'on.' PMID 34561620 (Heyder et al., Cell Res 2021)

  3. Receptor activation triggers cAMP (and Gq-biased) signaling Established

    Active MC4R couples to Gs, raising intracellular cAMP; the cryo-EM work reports setmelanotide is biased toward Gq/11 signaling relative to the linear agonist NDP-alpha-MSH. This second-messenger cascade changes the firing of appetite-regulating neurons. In rare-obesity patients, restoring this signaling on a partly-functional MC4R pathway is what rescues the satiety defect. PMID 34561620 (Heyder et al., Cell Res 2021); PMID 36009013 (Reininghaus et al., Biomolecules 2022)

  4. Downstream circuits cut hunger and hyperphagia Established

    MC4R activation in the paraventricular nucleus suppresses the drive to eat. In the pivotal phase 3 trials, patients aged 12+ who continued into the withdrawal phase reported large drops in the 11-point 'most hunger' score — about -27% (POMC) and -44% (LEPR) — confirming the appetite effect is real in humans, not just rodents. (These hunger figures come from small subsets, n=7 each.) PMID 33137293 (Clément et al., Lancet Diabetes Endocrinol 2020)

  5. MC4R activation also raised resting energy expenditure in a short human study Evidence-human

    Unlike GLP-1 drugs (whose energy expenditure tends to fall as weight is lost — a metabolic-adaptation effect), central MC4R agonism increased calorie burning in one small, short human study. A randomized crossover room-calorimetry study of a continuous 1 mg/24h infusion of setmelanotide (as RM-493) over ~3 days raised resting energy expenditure ~6.4% (~111 kcal/24h, p=0.03) and shifted fuel use toward fat oxidation, with no rise in heart rate or blood pressure over that short exposure. Whether this REE boost persists over months–years is unknown. The sympathetic/central mechanism proposed for it is largely rodent-derived. PMID 25675384 (Chen et al., J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2015)

  6. Combined effect: weight and fat loss — but only in specific indications Established

    Lower intake (and, at least short-term, higher expenditure) produces meaningful weight loss in the target populations: ~80% (8/10) of POMC-deficient and ~45% (5/11) of LEPR-deficient patients lost ≥10% at ~1 year, and 32.3% of Bardet-Biedl patients ≥12y lost ≥10% at 52 weeks. In acquired hypothalamic obesity, a single small open-label phase 2 (n=18) reported a mean BMI reduction of ~15%. These are small, mostly single-arm/open-label trials. PMID 33137293 (POMC/LEPR); PMID 36356613 (BBS); PMID 38697184 (hypothalamic obesity, Evidence-human, single-arm phase 2)

  7. Off-target MC1R activation drives skin darkening Established

    MC4R's sister receptor MC1R on skin melanocytes controls pigment. Setmelanotide is not perfectly selective and also activates MC1R, stimulating melanin production. This is why skin hyperpigmentation and darkening/new moles are among the most common side effects — a direct, predictable consequence of hitting a related melanocortin receptor. At least one case report describes dysplastic nevi during treatment. PMID 34561620 (receptor engagement/selectivity); PMID 40836563 (dysplastic nevi case, LEPR deficiency)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Agonist at MC4R, a class A GPCR. Cryo-EM of the setmelanotide–MC4R–Gs complex (2.6 Å) shows the cyclic peptide occupying the orthosteric pocket similarly to endogenous alpha-MSH, using a calcium ion as a binding co-factor and displacing transmembrane helix 6 to open the intracellular G-protein interface. Relative to the linear agonist NDP-alpha-MSH, setmelanotide shows signaling bias toward Gq/11. It is a melanocortin-receptor agonist with a preference for MC4R but meaningful activity at MC1R (pigmentation) and some activity at other melanocortin receptors; the MC1R activity is the mechanistic basis of hyperpigmentation.

Signalling cascade. Active MC4R → Gs → adenylyl cyclase → ↑cAMP → PKA, plus Gq/11-biased → PLC/Ca2+ signaling. Net effect on paraventricular-nucleus neurons: suppressed appetite drive. A short human study also found increased resting energy expenditure, but the sympathetic/central mechanism proposed for that is rodent-derived. In a rat hypothalamic-injury model, setmelanotide restored CaMKK2/AMPK signaling and reactivated MC4R neurons (rodent evidence only).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Subcutaneous injection, once daily (abdominal SC in labeling).
Absorption / bioavailability
Absorbed after SC dosing; first-in-human multiple-dose PK/PD characterized in healthy obese volunteers (NCT02431442, phase 1).
Half-life
Approximately 11 hours (per FDA labeling), consistent with once-daily dosing.
Metabolism
Peptide; expected to be cleared by proteolytic degradation into amino acids/peptide fragments rather than by cytochrome-P450 metabolism, giving low expected risk of classic CYP drug–drug interactions. Poor blood–brain-barrier penetrance and modest metabolic stability are recognized liabilities driving next-generation analog design.
Excretion
Elimination of peptide fragments/parent; renal-impairment considerations per labeling.
Notes
Cyclic (disulfide-bridged) octapeptide; not orally bioavailable. Medicinal-chemistry programs are actively trying to improve BBB penetrance, solubility, and MC4R-vs-MC1R selectivity (e.g., carbocyclic analogs; solubility-enhancing modifications).

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

Trials titrate stepwise to a typical maximum of 3.0 mg/day, with lower ceilings in young children. In the RM-493 calorimetry study a continuous 1 mg/24h infusion raised resting energy expenditure ~6.4% (~111 kcal/24h). Higher/target maintenance doses produced the weight-loss responses in phase 3. Hyperpigmentation and GI effects are dose-/exposure-related, which is part of why dose is titrated. NOTE: this describes what trials did for mechanistic context only — it is NOT a dosing protocol.

How it compares

  • vs Semaglutide / tirzepatide (GLP-1 / GLP-1–GIP)
    same: Both reduce appetite and produce weight loss via central appetite circuits; both injectable
    different: Setmelanotide increased resting energy expenditure in a short human study, whereas GLP-1 agents' energy expenditure tends to fall with weight loss (metabolic adaptation); setmelanotide is narrowly indicated for rare MC4R-pathway/hypothalamic obesity, whereas GLP-1s are broadly effective in common obesity where setmelanotide is unproven
  • vs Melanotan II (unapproved tanning/aphrodisiac peptide)
    same: Both are melanocortin peptide agonists that darken skin and can cause spontaneous erections
    different: Setmelanotide is FDA-approved, MC4R-preferring, and rigorously studied; melanotan II is unregulated, nonselective, and not a legitimate obesity therapy — conflating them is a common and dangerous error
  • vs Leptin (metreleptin)
    same: Both act on the leptin–melanocortin axis to reduce food intake
    different: Leptin works upstream and fails when the receptor/downstream pathway is defective; setmelanotide acts downstream at MC4R, so it can work in LEPR deficiency where leptin cannot

History & legal status

Emerged from a ~25-year effort to drug the melanocortin pathway (development codes BIM-22493 / RM-493), advanced by Rhythm Pharmaceuticals. First FDA approval Nov 2020 for POMC, PCSK1, and LEPR deficiency obesity (age ≥6). Approved 2022 for Bardet-Biedl syndrome on the pivotal RCT. Indication expanded to age ≥2 (VENTURE) and, in 2024–2025, to acquired hypothalamic obesity. Yeo et al. frame it as a template for turning a single-gene discovery into an approved drug ('~25 years to transform from a single gene into an approved drug').

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Durability of the resting-energy-expenditure boost in humans over months–years (the key RCT was ~3 days, n=12).
  • Long-term dermatologic risk — whether MC1R-driven pigmentation/nevi changes carry any melanoma risk with chronic use (note: trials excluded patients with melanoma history/pre-melanoma lesions).
  • Whether the depression/suicidal-ideation labeling caution reflects a true drug effect or background risk in these populations (BBS-trial event was judged unrelated).
  • Real-world efficacy and safety outside tightly-selected trial populations, and in partial/heterozygous pathway variants (e.g., DAYBREAK, NCT04963231, n=164).
  • Cardiovascular safety over the long term (short studies showed no HR/BP rise, but MC4R has cardiovascular roles).
  • No meaningful role established in common polygenic obesity.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Monitor and document skin: full skin exam and mole/nevi mapping before starting and periodically during treatment (MC1R effect; a dysplastic-nevi case is reported) — dermatology referral for changing lesions. Track weight/BMI (BMI-Z in children) and hunger as efficacy signals; reassess if there is no meaningful response. Watch for GI intolerance (nausea/vomiting/diarrhea), injection-site reactions, and — in males — spontaneous erections. Assess mood/behavior given the labeled psychiatric caution. Because it acts centrally on a satiety circuit, response and side effects depend on the underlying genotype/etiology, so confirming an MC4R-pathway or hypothalamic indication is itself part of appropriate use. This is monitoring guidance for mechanism education only — NOT a dosing or treatment protocol, and no dosing regimen is provided.
