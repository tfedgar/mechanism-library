Turinabol

A 1960s East German oral steroid built for "lean, dry" gains without estrogen bloat — it can't aromatize, but the 17-alpha-methyl group that lets you swallow it is exactly what stresses the liver and crashes your HDL.

Human / approved 18 citations
Class
Oral 17-alpha-alkylated anabolic-androgenic steroid (AAS); a chlorinated derivative of methandrostenolone/Dianabol
Primary target
The androgen receptor (AR) — an intracellular ligand-activated transcription factor
Human status
Never an FDA-approved drug in the US. Developed by Jenapharm in East Germany in the 1960s and marketed as Oral-Turinabol; it was the central drug of the state-sponsored GDR doping program (State Plan 14.25), given — often without consent — to thousands of athletes including minors, producing documented virilization and other harms. Today it has no legitimate medical use, is a Schedule III controlled substance, and is banned in sport at all times by WADA. Human data are largely historical/observational (GDR program, case reports) plus modern controlled pharmacokinetic/anti-doping administration studies (e.g., a 5 mg single-dose human metabolite trial); there are no modern controlled efficacy/safety trials in athletes.

At a glance

  • Lean muscle and strength gains with little/no water retention or bloat ('dry' look) — because it does not aromatize to estrogen
  • No estrogenic side effects from the drug itself (no gyno/water from aromatization), unlike testosterone or Dianabol
  • Hepatotoxicity: raised liver enzymes, cholestasis (jaundice, dark urine), risk of peliosis hepatis and liver tumors at higher dose/longer duration — the cost of being an oral 17aa steroid
  • Atherogenic lipid shift: sharply lowered HDL and raised LDL/ApoB via hepatic lipase induction — a cardiovascular risk concern
  • Suppression of natural testosterone and fertility via HPG-axis shutdown (general AAS class effect)
  • Virilization risk in women (deepened voice, hair growth) — the GDR doping program produced documented, sometimes irreversible cases; androgenic effects can appear at relatively low doses
  • Long-detection-window metabolites: turinabol is famous in anti-doping because its long-term urinary metabolites stay detectable for weeks, leading to retroactive Olympic positives

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Three structural tweaks define the molecule Established

    Turinabol is testosterone with three edits: (a) a 17-alpha-METHYL group so it survives being swallowed, (b) a 1,2 double bond (the 'dehydro', shared with Dianabol), and (c) a 4-CHLORINE atom. Chemically it is 4-chloro-17beta-hydroxy-17alpha-methylandrosta-1,4-dien-3-one. Think of it as Dianabol with a chlorine bolted on — that chlorine is what kills the estrogen conversion. Confirmed structure across the chemistry literature. Turinabol (4-chloro, Δ1) is non-aromatizing by structure; class/ChEMBL pharmacology (vs Dianabol which aromatizes weakly)

  2. The 17-alpha-methyl group makes it work as a pill Established

    Normally, swallow a steroid and the liver destroys most of it on the first pass before it does anything. Adding a methyl group at the 17-alpha position physically blocks the liver enzymes from clearing it, so the drug survives into the bloodstream and stays active orally. This single trick — '17-alpha-alkylation' — is the entire reason oral steroids like turinabol, Dianabol, Anadrol and Winstrol exist. The catch comes in steps 5-6. PMID 27372877; PMID 29521706

  3. It binds the androgen receptor and flips on muscle-building genes Established

    Like all AAS, turinabol's real job is to act as a key for the androgen receptor (AR). The AR sits inactive inside the cell wrapped in chaperone (heat-shock) proteins. When the steroid binds, the chaperones fall off, two receptors pair up, move into the nucleus, and clamp onto specific DNA sites (androgen response elements). They recruit co-activators and switch on genes that increase muscle protein synthesis and reduce protein breakdown — the net result is more lean tissue and strength. This is the classic genomic nuclear-receptor mechanism shared by all androgens. PMID 10077001; PMID 27372877

  4. The 4-chlorine means it CANNOT aromatize to estrogen Established

    Testosterone and Dianabol get partly converted by the aromatase enzyme into estrogen, which drives water retention, bloating and gyno. Turinabol's 4-chloro substitution (plus the 1,2 double bond) blocks aromatase from acting on it — so there is essentially no estrogen conversion. That's why users describe the look as 'dry' and 'lean' rather than puffy. It also has relatively low binding to SHBG, the blood protein that mops up sex hormones, which raises the free, active fraction. Non-aromatizing status is well established; the SHBG detail is mechanistic inference from its class. PMID 14304134; PMID 26658226

  5. The same methyl group makes it liver-toxic Established

    Because the 17-alpha-methyl group forces the liver to process the drug slowly, it accumulates and stresses liver cells. Established harms from 17-alpha-alkylated orals include raised liver enzymes (ALT/AST/GGT), cholestasis (bile flow gets blocked — jaundice, dark urine), and at the severe end peliosis hepatis (blood-filled liver cavities) and liver tumors. The leading proposed cell-level cause is oxidative stress; the bile-transport disruption (BSEP/MRP2 pumps) is well documented. Effects are dose- and duration-dependent and usually reverse when the oral is stopped. Injectables are far less hepatotoxic. PMID 27372877; PMID 29521706; PMID 8527826

  6. It crashes HDL and raises LDL — the cardiovascular hit Established

    Oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS pass through the liver and strongly induce hepatic lipase, the enzyme that tears down HDL ('good') cholesterol. The signature result is a dramatic drop in HDL (can fall to single digits) and a rise in LDL/ApoB — an atherogenic, artery-clogging lipid shift that is much sharper with orals than with injectable testosterone. This is a cardiovascular risk driver independent of blood pressure. Statins/ezetimibe blunt LDL but the HDL crash mainly recovers only after stopping the drug. The lipoprotein effect of 17aa orals is established; hepatic lipase induction is the accepted mechanism. PMID 29521706; PMID 27372877

  7. It shuts down your own testosterone (HPG suppression) Evidence-human

    Any androgen this potent tells the brain (hypothalamus and pituitary) that there's plenty of hormone around, so the body throttles back its own signals (GnRH, then LH and FSH). Natural testosterone production and sperm output fall during use. This is a general, well-established AAS class effect; recovery timing varies and is not specific data for turinabol itself. PMID 29521706

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Turinabol (4-chloro-17α-methyl-17β-hydroxy-androsta-1,4-dien-3-one; 'dehydrochloromethyltestosterone', DHCMT) is an AGONIST at the intracellular androgen receptor (AR / NR3C4), the same nuclear-receptor target as testosterone and DHT (Established — AR is the shared target of testosterone, DHT, AAS and SARMs; corpus AR MoA card). NUMBERS — direct, high-quality affinity data for turinabol itself are essentially ABSENT (Evidence gap): the pivotal competitive-binding study of anabolic steroids (Saartok 1984, PMID 6539197) measured relative binding affinity (RBA) against [3H]methyltrienolone in rat/rabbit skeletal muscle and rat prostate AR, but did NOT include turinabol. It DID test turinabol's close structural parent, methanedienone (Dianabol; turinabol is its 4-chloro analog), which bound WEAKLY: RBA <0.05 relative to methyltrienolone (i.e. roughly 1/20th or less), in the same low tier as stanozolol and fluoxymesterone, and far below 19-nortestosterone, methenolone or testosterone. By structural inference turinabol is therefore expected to be a comparatively LOW-affinity AR agonist whose in-vivo potency depends heavily on full agonist efficacy and pharmacokinetics rather than tight binding (Contested/Speculative for turinabol specifically). SELECTIVITY/SHBG: turinabol is described as a non-aromatizing 17α-alkylated chlorinated AAS with low sex-hormone-binding-globulin (SHBG) binding; in Saartok's series the 17α-methyl A-ring-modified steroids (methanedienone, stanozolol, 17α-methyl-T) bound SHBG only weakly, consistent with turinabol circulating with relatively little SHBG sequestration (Evidence-in vitro by analogy). The 4-chloro and Δ1 (1,2-double-bond) modifications block 5α-reduction to a more potent DHT-like metabolite and block aromatization to estrogen, so turinabol cannot generate estradiol — it has no direct estrogen-receptor activity (Established by structure/metabolism data, PMIDs 17386713, 34089835).

Signalling cascade. Classic genomic nuclear-receptor cascade (Established for the AR class; corpus AR MoA card, supporting AR translocation/transcription literature PMIDs 39718068, 35723327): lipophilic turinabol diffuses across the plasma membrane and binds the ligand-binding domain of cytosolic AR, displacing heat-shock chaperones (HSP90/HSP70/FKBP). The ligand-bound receptor undergoes a conformational change enabling the intramolecular N-terminal/C-terminal (N/C) interaction that stabilizes the active receptor, then homodimerizes and translocates to the nucleus. There it binds androgen-response elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer regions and recruits coactivators and RNA polymerase II machinery (actin/myosin-VI-dependent transcriptional hub formation has been described, PMID 41443423), driving transcription of anabolic genes. Downstream ESTABLISHED tissue effects of AR activation: increased skeletal-muscle protein synthesis and hypertrophy, satellite-cell activation, stimulation of renal/hepatic erythropoietin with suppression of hepcidin (raising red-cell mass), sebaceous-gland stimulation, prostate growth, and negative feedback onto the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis. Because turinabol does not aromatize, the estrogen-receptor-mediated arm (water retention, gynecomastia, the cardioprotective HDL effect of estradiol) is largely absent — its phenotype is dominated by AR signaling alone (Evidence-human/Established by structure).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral (tablets). The compound was marketed as 'Oral-Turinabol' — oral administration is the defining feature (Established; PMIDs 818880, 6630400, 9216474).
Absorption / bioavailability
Orally bioavailable specifically BECAUSE of the 17α-methyl group, which sterically hinders hepatic 17β-hydroxyl oxidation and lets the molecule survive first-pass metabolism (Established mechanism for 17α-alkylated orals; corpus hepatotoxicity MoA card; PMID 29521706). No rigorous human absolute-bioavailability or Cmax/Tmax figures are published — quantitative human bioavailability is UNKNOWN (Evidence gap). Excretion data show rapid, extensive metabolism: in controlled human administration the parent drug is NOT detectable in urine and only ~15% is recovered as the three main hydroxylated metabolites (PMID 6630400).
Half-life
No well-characterized plasma elimination half-life is published for turinabol in humans (Evidence gap). Bodybuilding/anti-doping convention cites a SHORT active half-life (commonly stated as ~16 hours), but this is not anchored to a peer-reviewed pharmacokinetic study and should be treated as Speculative. What IS established is a long DETECTION window driven by minor 'long-term' metabolites (see notes), not a long pharmacodynamic half-life.
Metabolism
Extensive hepatic and extrahepatic cytochrome-P450 oxidation (Established; PMIDs 6630400, 17386713, 34089835, 29544993, 32860782). In humans the major early metabolites are 6β-hydroxy-turinabol, 6β,12-dihydroxy-turinabol and 6β,16-dihydroxy-turinabol, plus 17-epi-turinabol (PMID 6630400). Specific enzymes identified: human brain microsomal CYP46A1 (cholesterol 24-hydroxylase) selectively forms 16β-hydroxy-turinabol and is COMPETITIVELY INHIBITED by turinabol at its active site (PMID 34089835); CYP21A2 participates in forming the long-term metabolite 20βOH-NorDHCMT (PMID 29544993). The 17α-methyl group is retained through a Wagner–Meerwein/dehydration pathway that yields characteristic 17-hydroxymethyl-17-methyl-18-nor-Δ13 'long-term' metabolites (M3/M4; PMID 32860782). The 4-chloro substituent and the 1,2-double bond block both 5α-reduction and aromatization, so NO estrogenic or DHT-type active metabolites are formed (Established).
Excretion
Renal/urinary as conjugated and free hydroxylated metabolites; parent compound essentially undetectable in urine; recovery of the main metabolites ~15% (Established; PMIDs 6630400, 17386713). Long-term sulfo-/glucurono-conjugated metabolites (e.g. M3 / 20βOH-NorDHCMT) extend the anti-doping detection window to >22 days and, with modern LC-MS/MS, to many weeks/months after a single dose (Evidence-human; PMIDs 29544993, 34418529).
Notes
Turinabol is NOT a prodrug and carries no ester — it is the active molecule itself (oral, immediate). It readily crosses the blood–brain barrier (lipophilic steroid; demonstrated brain-enzyme CYP46A1 metabolism, PMID 34089835), and its competitive inhibition of brain cholesterol 24-hydroxylase is a flagged but unproven potential CNS concern (Evidence-in vitro; PMID 34089835). Its low water solubility was the subject of cyclodextrin-complexation work (PMID 35510437).

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

Human dose–response is essentially UNCHARACTERIZED in the modern peer-reviewed literature: there are no published randomized dose-ranging efficacy trials of turinabol in humans (Evidence gap). The richest human exposure data come from the declassified East German (GDR) State Plan 14.25 doping program (Franke & Berendonk 1997, PMID 9216474), where Oral-Turinabol was the principal androgen given to several thousand athletes per year, including minors and women; the documents describe clear, dose-related performance gains and dose-related virilization in females, but they are observational program records, not controlled dose–response data (Evidence-human, low certainty for magnitudes). Historical androgenic:anabolic (myotrophic) ratios from the classic rodent levator-ani/ventral-prostate bioassay are frequently cited as roughly 'anabolic ≈ testosterone, androgenic < testosterone' (turinabol marketed as a comparatively low-androgenic oral), but I could not retrieve the primary rodent assay paper in this search, so the specific numeric ratio remains UNVERIFIED here (Contested). Mechanistically, because turinabol is a relatively low-AFFINITY AR agonist (inferred from parent methanedienone RBA <0.05, PMID 6539197), meaningful anabolic effect in humans plausibly requires sustained AR occupancy (i.e. higher/repeated oral dosing) rather than the very low concentrations that saturate high-affinity agonists like methyltrienolone or DHT (Speculative extrapolation). Bottom line for the audience: the 'dose makes the effect' curve for turinabol in humans is NOT established by trial data — claims of specific mg-for-mg potency are extrapolations, and harm scales with dose and duration faster than any documented benefit.

How it compares

  • vs Methandrostenolone (Dianabol / methandienone, CHEMBL1418176)
    same: Direct structural parent — turinabol is literally 4-chloro-Dianabol; both are oral 17α-methyl androsta-1,4-dien-3-ones, both AR agonists, both relatively LOW-affinity (Dianabol parent RBA <0.05, PMID 6539197), both hepatotoxic 17α-alkylated orals
    different: The 4-chloro substituent BLOCKS aromatization, so turinabol produces NO estrogen — far less water retention, no gynecomastia, and no estrogenic HDL protection (so a 'drier' look but potentially harsher lipids). Dianabol aromatizes and causes pronounced water retention/estrogenic effects (Established by structure; PMIDs 17386713, 34089835; corpus Dianabol MoA card)
  • vs Oxandrolone (Anavar, CHEMBL1200436)
    same: Both oral 17α-alkylated, non-aromatizing AAS marketed as comparatively 'mild'/low-androgenic with low water retention; both AR agonists; both rely on PK/efficacy rather than high AR affinity (oxandrolone AR RBA was 'too low to determine' in Saartok 1984)
    different: Different ring chemistry (oxandrolone is a 2-oxa DHT-derivative; turinabol is a chlorinated testosterone/Δ1,4 derivative). Oxandrolone is the better-studied compound with actual clinical/FDA data; turinabol has essentially no modern clinical evidence base. Turinabol carries the unique GDR doping legacy and CYP46A1 brain interaction (PMID 34089835)
  • vs Stanozolol (Winstrol, CHEMBL2079587)
    same: Both oral 17α-alkylated, non-aromatizing AAS that bound AR weakly in Saartok 1984 (stanozolol RBA <0.05); both prized for 'lean/dry' physique effects and both hepatotoxic with the same BSEP/MRP2 cholestasis mechanism
    different: Stanozolol has a fused pyrazole A-ring; turinabol has a 4-chloro/Δ1 A-ring. Stanozolol has documented fatal cholestatic case reports (PMID 37948000) and stronger SHBG-lowering; turinabol's distinguishing feature is its extremely long-detection 18-nor long-term metabolites (PMIDs 29544993, 32860782)
  • vs Testosterone (endogenous reference)
    same: Same molecular target (AR agonist) and same downstream anabolic transcriptional cascade
    different: Testosterone is a HIGH-RBA AR ligand, aromatizes to estradiol, is 5α-reduced to potent DHT, and is not hepatotoxic when injected. Turinabol is low-affinity, non-aromatizing, non-5α-reducible, orally active, and hepatotoxic via 17α-alkylation — a fundamentally different risk/effect profile (PMID 6539197; corpus AR MoA card)

History & legal status

"Developed and patented by the East German pharmaceutical company VEB Jenapharm; marketed from 1965 as 'Oral-Turinabol' (chemical name dehydrochloromethyltestosterone / DHCMT). It became the central drug of the German Democratic Republic's state-sponsored doping program ('State Plan Research Theme 14.25'), administered from 1966 onward to several thousand athletes per year — including minors and women — to enhance performance, with documented serious side effects and deliberate detection-evasion strategies (Franke & Berendonk 1997, PMID 9216474). It was never approved by the US FDA. It is a Schedule III controlled substance in the United States (Anabolic Steroid Control Act) and a controlled/prescription-only or banned substance in most jurisdictions. It is prohibited at all times in sport by the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) and analogous bodies for animal sport (e.g. equine racing; PMID 17386713). Today turinabol has no legitimate therapeutic use and exists almost entirely as an illicit performance-enhancing drug; much of the modern peer-reviewed literature concerns anti-doping detection of its long-term metabolites (PMIDs 29544993, 32860782, 34418529)."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No direct measured AR binding affinity (Ki/RBA) or EC50 for turinabol itself exists — affinity is inferred only from its parent methanedienone (RBA <0.05, PMID 6539197). The true potency and full vs partial agonist efficacy of turinabol at the human AR is genuinely unknown.
  • No validated human pharmacokinetics: plasma half-life, Cmax/Tmax, and absolute oral bioavailability have never been rigorously published. The commonly cited '~16 h half-life' is not anchored to a peer-reviewed PK study.
  • No modern controlled dose–response or efficacy trials in humans — the magnitude of muscle/strength gain per dose, and the true androgenic:anabolic ratio, rest on uncontrolled GDR program records and unverified historical rodent assays.
  • Clinical significance of turinabol's competitive inhibition of brain CYP46A1 (cholesterol 24-hydroxylase) is unknown — whether chronic use perturbs brain cholesterol metabolism, memory or cognition in humans has never been studied (PMID 34089835).
  • Turinabol-SPECIFIC cardiovascular and hepatic outcome data are essentially absent; nearly all risk estimates are extrapolated from the broader 17α-alkylated AAS class rather than measured for turinabol.
  • Long-term and trans-generational health outcomes in the GDR-exposed cohort (especially women and adolescents dosed decades ago) remain incompletely characterized in the peer-reviewed literature.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only (no dosing guidance). LIVER: baseline and periodic ALT, AST, GGT and bilirubin; watch for jaundice, dark urine, pale stools, right-upper-quadrant pain — turinabol is a 17α-alkylated oral with established cholestatic hepatotoxicity (corpus hepatotoxicity MoA card; PMIDs 29521706, 27372877). LIPIDS/CARDIOVASCULAR: full lipid panel (HDL, LDL, total cholesterol, triglycerides) and blood pressure — non-aromatizing orals tend to crash HDL and raise LDL with no estrogenic buffering (PMID 21884791); consider longer-term cardiac/vascular assessment given coronary-calcification signals in chronic AAS users. HEMATOLOGY: hematocrit/hemoglobin for erythrocytosis (AR raises EPO, suppresses hepcidin; corpus AR MoA card). HPG/ENDOCRINE: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone and (in those trying to conceive) a semen analysis to track suppression and recovery; testicular atrophy is an expected sign of HPG shutdown (corpus HPG MoA card; PMID 21884791). ANDROGENIC SIGNS: monitor acne and scalp hair; in women monitor for virilization (voice deepening, hirsutism, clitoromegaly, menstrual change) — several of these are IRREVERSIBLE, so early recognition matters (PMID 9216474). The most effective mitigation is limiting/avoiding oral 17α-alkylated exposure altogether; liver-support supplements (e.g. TUDCA, NAC) have only LIMITED evidence (corpus hepatotoxicity MoA card). Any worrying value or symptom warrants evaluation by a clinician."

Caveats

Certainty labels: the AR genomic mechanism, non-aromatizing status, oral bioavailability via 17a-alkylation, hepatotoxicity, and the lipid (HDL/LDL) shift are ESTABLISHED for the 17aa oral class and for turinabol specifically where noted. The exact cell-level cause of liver toxicity (oxidative stress) is the leading HYPOTHESIS, not settled. The low-SHBG/free-fraction point is mechanistic inference from the drug class, not turinabol-specific measurement. HPG suppression and female virilization are well-supported as AAS class effects and from GDR-era observation, but most human turinabol data are historical/observational, not randomized — quantitative dose-response and recovery numbers should not be treated as precise. HARM-REDUCTION NOTE: this is a mechanistic teaching summary, not medical advice and not a usage or dosing guide. Liver-support supplements (TUDCA, NAC) have only LIMITED evidence; the real mitigation of oral-AAS harm is limiting/avoiding the oral. No dosing is provided by design.
