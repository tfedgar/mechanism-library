Clenbuterol

Main target: Beta-2 adrenergic receptor. Human evidence is still limited.

Works in humans?Limited human data
Approved?Not FDA-approved

A long-acting beta-2 agonist (an asthma-type drug) that tricks the body into the same "burn fat, spare muscle" state as adrenaline — which is exactly why bodybuilders abuse it and exactly why it can stress the heart.

Limited human data 19 citations
Class
Selective beta-2 adrenergic receptor agonist (sympathomimetic / "beta-agonist")
Primary target
Beta-2 adrenergic receptor (β2-AR), the dominant adrenergic receptor on skeletal muscle and on fat cells; a Gs-coupled GPCR
Human status
NOT approved for human use in the US, UK, and most of Europe. In some countries it is a veterinary/livestock drug and elsewhere a niche asthma medicine — but it is banned in food animals (residues have caused mass human poisonings) and is a WADA-prohibited substance in sport. Human evidence on physique effects is thin: a few small, short controlled studies (e.g. ~2 weeks in healthy men) confirm the metabolic effects but show only modest lean-mass change — far below the dramatic muscle gains seen in rodents that drive its reputation. Most of the muscle-growth and fiber-switching data are rodent/cell studies. It is used by bodybuilders and dieters as an off-label, illicit fat-loss aid, not as an approved therapy.

At a glance

  • Increased resting energy expenditure (~21%) and fat oxidation (~39%) in humans — the genuine 'fat burner' effect [PMID 31887249]
  • Muscle anabolic / anti-catabolic signaling via mTOR and beta-arrestin — large in rodents, modest in humans (~<1 kg lean mass over 2 weeks) [PMID 31887249, 30591079]
  • Improved glucose handling / metabolic reprogramming of muscle (shown in mice) [PMID 35013148]
  • Cardiovascular stimulation: raised heart rate, tremor, palpitations, arrhythmia risk, drop in blood potassium [PMID 31887249, 32169984]
  • Very long duration of action — effects (and side effects) persist for many hours after a single dose

Mechanism, step by step

6 steps5 human-evidenced 1 preclinical

  1. Clenbuterol mimics adrenaline at the beta-2 receptor Established

    Clenbuterol is shaped enough like your body's own adrenaline/noradrenaline that it latches onto the beta-2 adrenergic receptor and switches it on. The same receptor family is why asthma inhalers (which are beta-2 agonists) open the airways. Clenbuterol just does it for a very long time and all over the body. It prefers beta-2 over the heart's main beta-1 receptor — but 'prefers' is not 'only', which becomes the whole safety story. Established canonical β2-adrenergic (Gs→cAMP→PKA) pharmacology; ChEMBL CHEMBL49080 (ATC R03AC14)

  2. The receptor turns on the cAMP / PKA 'go' signal Established

    A switched-on beta-2 receptor activates a partner protein (Gs) that cranks up an internal messenger called cAMP, which in turn activates the enzyme PKA. cAMP/PKA is the cell's universal 'mobilize energy now' signal — the same cascade adrenaline uses when you're startled. This one cascade drives almost everything clenbuterol does. Established canonical β2-adrenergic (Gs→cAMP→PKA) pharmacology; ChEMBL CHEMBL49080 (ATC R03AC14)

  3. In fat and muscle: more heat and more fat burned (thermogenesis + fat oxidation) Evidence-human

    That energy signal raises resting metabolic rate and pushes cells to burn fat for fuel. In a controlled study of healthy young men, oral clenbuterol raised resting energy expenditure by about 21% and fat oxidation by about 39%. This is the real basis of the 'fat burner' reputation — the body genuinely idles hotter and burns more fat. PMID 31887249

  4. In muscle: a 'build and keep' signal via mTOR (anabolic / anti-catabolic) Contested (human anabolic effect small/inconsistent; mostly livestock + rodent data)

    In skeletal muscle the same beta-2 signaling switches on mTOR, the master switch for building muscle protein, and dials down muscle breakdown. In rodents this reliably produces real muscle growth (hypertrophy) and fiber-type shifts. In humans the muscle-building effect is much more modest: two weeks of oral clenbuterol added under ~1 kg of lean mass — real, but nowhere near the rodent results that fuel the hype. PMID 31887249; PMID 30591079

  5. A second, separate muscle pathway: beta-arrestin Evidence-rodent

    Beyond the cAMP route, the beta-2 receptor also signals through an adaptor protein called beta-arrestin-1, and this arm specifically drives the muscle-growth and contractility effects. This matters scientifically because it shows the muscle benefit and the dangerous cardiac/G-protein effects travel partly on different wires — the dream of separating them is why this receptor is still a drug-design target. PMID 30591079; PMID 32414934

  6. Why it hits the heart: beta-2 is not heart-silent, and clenbuterol lingers Established

    The heart is rich in beta-1 receptors but also carries beta-2, so a strong, long-lasting beta-2 push speeds heart rate, can trigger arrhythmias, and lowers blood potassium. Clenbuterol's very long half-life means one dose drives the receptor for many hours — you can't 'turn it off'. Real-world poisonings (including a pediatric case) show tremor, racing heart, chest pain and dangerous potassium drops. This cardiac risk is the main reason it is not approved for human use in most countries. PMID 31887249; PMID 32169984

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. TARGET / AFFINITY: Primary target is the beta2-adrenoceptor (ADRB2; ChEMBL210), a Gs-coupled GPCR (Established; ChEMBL get_mechanism CHEMBL49080, action_type AGONIST, direct_interaction=true). CRITICAL NUANCE ON 'SELECTIVITY': clenbuterol's beta2 preference is NOT primarily an affinity phenomenon. Autoradiographic binding work (Vos 1987, PMID 2886650) found clenbuterol has roughly EQUAL binding affinity for beta1 and beta2 receptors, yet selectively down-regulates only beta2 receptors on chronic dosing -- i.e. its selectivity is functional, not affinity-based (Evidence-rodent/in vitro). The definitive human-receptor study (Baker 2010, PMID 20590599; CHO-K1 cells stably expressing cloned human beta1/beta2/beta3, [3H]-CGP12177 binding + [3H]-cAMP accumulation) classifies clenbuterol as a beta2-selective agonist by virtue of SUBTYPE-SELECTIVE INTRINSIC EFFICACY (it activates beta2 far more effectively than beta1/beta3) rather than selective affinity (Evidence-in vitro, human receptors). ChEMBL labels it only 'moderately selective.' PRACTICAL CONSEQUENCE: because it still binds beta1 (cardiac), at the supratherapeutic doses people self-administer the beta1 'spillover' drives much of the cardiac toxicity -- and Burniston (PMID 15591297) showed clenbuterol-induced CARDIAC apoptosis is in fact mediated through beta1-AR, whereas its skeletal-muscle effects go through beta2-AR. Agonist (full/near-full at beta2); no meaningful antagonist activity. Numeric Ki/EC50 values vary by tissue/species and are not consistently reported for human receptors; affinity is in the low-nanomolar-to-submicromolar range across studies (Contested on exact numbers).

Signalling cascade. Canonical Gs cascade (Established; ChEMBL mechanism comment + textbook physiology). 1) Clenbuterol binds beta2AR -> receptor couples to stimulatory G-protein Gs -> 2) Gs-alpha activates ADENYLYL CYCLASE -> 3) rise in intracellular cAMP -> 4) activation of PROTEIN KINASE A (PKA). In AIRWAY smooth muscle (its licensed veterinary action) PKA opens K+ channels and down-regulates myosin light-chain kinase -> bronchodilation. In SKELETAL MUSCLE the anabolic limb is more complex and partly G-protein-INDEPENDENT: chronic beta2 stimulation drives hypertrophy that REQUIRES beta-arrestin-1 -- in beta-arrestin-1 knockout mice clenbuterol's hypertrophy and contractility gains are abolished (Kim 2018, PMID 30591079, Evidence-rodent). Downstream pro-hypertrophic signalling in muscle converges on mTOR/Akt and ribosomal protein S6: in HUMANS, a single 80 ug dose raised muscle phospho-mTOR(Ser2448) by 121% and PKA-substrate phosphorylation by 35% (Jessen 2020, PMID 31887249, Evidence-human, n=6); a 2-week RCT showed marked PKA activation and pS6(Ser235/236) (Hostrup 2025, PMID 40946331, Evidence-human). Rodent work shows a BIPHASIC growth program: IGF-1/IGF1R/Akt drives the early hypertrophic phase (days 3-7) and the negative regulator MYOSTATIN (via Smad2/3) rises later (day 21) to terminate growth (Abo 2012, PMID 22696074, Evidence-rodent). In adipose/muscle, cAMP/PKA also drives LIPOLYSIS and thermogenesis and metabolic reprogramming toward glucose utilisation via skeletal-muscle beta2/Gs (Meister 2022, PMID 35013148, Evidence-rodent).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral (the dominant route in human misuse; veterinary syrups/granules e.g. Ventipulmin are oral; some powders parenteral). ChEMBL flags the parent salt as not formally oral-approved for humans, reflecting its lack of human licensure in most countries rather than poor oral performance -- it is in fact well absorbed orally.
Absorption / bioavailability
Well absorbed after oral dosing. In healthy human volunteers (Yamamoto 1985, PMID 4045696), oral 20/40/80 ug gave dose-proportional peak plasma of ~0.1/0.2/0.35 ng/mL reached within ~2.5 h (Tmax), sustained >6 h. Plasma protein binding 89-98% (single 80 ug dose). Lipophilic (ChEMBL ALogP ~3; PSA 58 A^2; 0 Lipinski violations, QED 0.74) -> good membrane and CNS penetration. Evidence-human.
Half-life
Long: ~35 h in humans (Yamamoto 1985, PMID 4045696). This long half-life (vs minutes-to-hours for catecholamines) is why effects and side effects persist >24 h and why steady state is reached only after ~4 days of twice-daily dosing. Species-dependent: ~30 h rat, ~9 h rabbit. Evidence-human + Evidence-rodent.
Metabolism
Only partially metabolised; a large fraction is excreted unchanged. Hepatic metabolism (hydroxylation/oxidation and conjugation) yields several minor metabolites; specific human CYP isoform assignments are not well characterised in the primary literature reviewed here (Contested/under-characterised). The chloro-aniline ring makes it metabolically more stable than catecholamine beta-agonists (no catechol-O-methyltransferase/MAO substrate sites), contributing to the long half-life.
Excretion
Renal. ~20% of an oral dose is recovered as UNCHANGED drug in urine over 72 h in humans (Yamamoto 1985, PMID 4045696), which underpins urine-based anti-doping detection and the prolonged urinary detection window. Evidence-human.
Notes
Not an ester and NOT a prodrug (ChEMBL prodrug=0) -- it is the active molecule itself, so there is no 'kick-in' delay beyond absorption. Lipophilic and BBB-penetrant -> central effects (it was even trialled clinically as an antidepressant, Vos 1987). Long half-life + tissue persistence means dose stacking accumulates; this PK profile (slow on, slow off) is mechanistically central to its cardiac risk because beta-receptors stay tonically stimulated.

How it compares

  • vs Albuterol / salbutamol (short-acting beta2 agonist, SABA)
    same: Both beta2-agonist phenylethanolamine bronchodilators sharing the Gs/cAMP/PKA cascade; both have thermogenic/anti-catabolic potential and both are WADA-restricted in sport.
    different: Salbutamol is short-acting (half-life ~4-6 h vs clenbuterol's ~35 h), much less lipophilic/CNS-penetrant, has a wide therapeutic margin and is a licensed human inhaler. Clenbuterol's very long half-life means sustained receptor stimulation and accumulation -- the key reason its cardiac risk profile is worse at comparable receptor occupancy. Inhaled salbutamol is permitted up to set thresholds in sport; clenbuterol is banned outright at all times.
  • vs Salmeterol / formoterol (long-acting beta2 agonists, LABAs)
    same: Long-acting beta2 agonists with the same downstream cascade; like clenbuterol they show genuine beta2 affinity-selectivity.
    different: Baker 2010 (PMID 20590599) shows salmeterol/formoterol are beta2-selective by AFFINITY (binding), whereas clenbuterol is beta2-selective only by intrinsic EFFICACY while retaining near-equal beta1 BINDING -- so clenbuterol carries relatively more cardiac beta1 'spillover' risk at high doses. LABAs are licensed human asthma drugs (inhaled, lung-targeted, low systemic exposure); clenbuterol is taken systemically/orally for non-respiratory effects. Inhaled formoterol/salmeterol are permitted in sport within thresholds; clenbuterol is not.
  • vs Carvedilol (beta-arrestin-biased beta-blocker)
    same: Acts at the same beta2AR and, surprisingly, can engage the SAME beta-arrestin-1 pathway that mediates clenbuterol's muscle-strength effect.
    different: Carvedilol is classically an ANTAGONIST/inverse agonist (used in heart failure/hypertension) but functions as a beta-arrestin-BIASED ligand: it enhanced skeletal-muscle contractility via beta-arrestin-1 WITHOUT the G-protein-mediated cardiac arrhythmia risk and without hypertrophy (Kim 2020, PMID 32414934). It is the conceptual 'safer' counterpoint -- capturing a muscle benefit while avoiding the cAMP-driven cardiotoxicity that defines clenbuterol.
  • vs Anabolic-androgenic steroids (AAS)
    same: Frequently stacked with clenbuterol in physique culture for 'cutting'; both are WADA-prohibited and both can be present in contaminated/black-market vials (e.g. testosterone ampoule found to contain clenbuterol, PMID 29849287).
    different: Completely different mechanism -- AAS act on the nuclear androgen receptor to drive transcription-level muscle protein synthesis (large, durable hypertrophy in humans), whereas clenbuterol's beta2/cAMP anabolism is modest in humans (~0.9 kg lean in 2 weeks) and desensitises rapidly. Clenbuterol's main human-relevant effects are thermogenic/lipolytic, not the dramatic muscle-building seen in rodents.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Amycretin compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Amycretin raises resting heart rate modestly via the incretin class effect (amycretin-specific magnitude unquantified), and its GI side effects can cause vomiting/diarrhea with fluid and electrolyte loss. Clenbuterol is a beta-2 agonist that raises heart rate and drives hypokalemia (intracellular potassium shift). The tachycardia stacks, and amycretin-driven GI fluid/electrolyte loss can worsen clenbuterol's hypokalemia, a combined cardiac-strain and arrhythmia-risk concern. amycretin.json (class heart-rate rise, magnitude unquantified; GI vomiting/dehydration/electrolyte risk); clenbuterol.json (beta-2 agonist: tachycardia, arrhythmia risk, drop in blood potassium) Watch: Watch for palpitations, resting tachycardia, tremor and muscle cramps; monitor heart rate, blood pressure and serum potassium/electrolytes (especially during GI illness or dehydration).
  • DNP compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Two thermogenic agents acting through different mechanisms that converge on heat production and cardiac demand. DNP uncouples mitochondrial oxidative phosphorylation, dissipating the proton gradient as heat and forcing a compensatory rise in metabolic rate, oxygen demand and heart rate; clenbuterol independently raises resting energy expenditure (~+21%) and thermogenesis via beta-2/cAMP while directly stimulating the heart and lowering potassium. Combined, the extra heat load (DNP) plus adrenergic tachycardia and hypokalemia (clenbuterol) compound the risk of dangerous hyperthermia and tachyarrhythmia, with clenbuterol's long half-life meaning the adrenergic contribution cannot be quickly withdrawn if DNP-driven hyperthermia develops. clenbuterol.json (thermogenesis +21%, tachycardia, hypokalemia; PMID 31887249); dnp monograph (mitochondrial uncoupling -> heat, life-threatening hyperthermia) Watch: Core temperature and any sign of hyperthermia (profuse sweating, flushing, agitation) — this is the priority with DNP; plus heart rate, blood pressure, ECG, and serum potassium. Rising temperature or tachycardia warrants stopping and urgent medical review, not 'waiting it out' given clenbuterol's long persistence.
  • Ephedrine compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both are sympathomimetics that converge on the same adrenergic/cAMP output. Clenbuterol is a long-acting direct beta-2 agonist; ephedrine is a mixed-acting agent that releases endogenous noradrenaline AND weakly agonizes alpha-1/beta-1/beta-2. Stacked, they additively drive heart rate, contractility, blood pressure and tremor, and both push potassium into cells (beta-2/cAMP -> Na/K-ATPase), so their hypokalemia stacks on top of their tachycardia. Clenbuterol's ~35 h half-life means its adrenergic load persists for many hours, so adding ephedrine layers a second stimulant surge onto an already tonically stimulated adrenergic system. This is a real-world pairing (bodybuilders run 'ECA' during clen off-weeks), which is exactly why the additive cardiac/electrolyte load matters. clenbuterol.json (beta-2/cAMP, hypokalemia, 35 h half-life; PMID 23844963, 18072161); ephedrine.json (mixed alpha/beta sympathomimetic, beta2-driven hypokalemia, raised HR/BP) Watch: Resting heart rate and blood pressure (watch for sustained tachycardia and widened pulse pressure), palpitations, tremor, anxiety and insomnia; ECG for arrhythmia/SVT if symptomatic; serum potassium and magnesium. Anyone with hypertension, arrhythmia or prior cardiac disease is at substantially higher combined risk.
  • Halotestin compounds risk ⚠ Contested
    Two independent routes to low potassium and cardiovascular strain converge. Clenbuterol is a beta-2 agonist whose cAMP/PKA activation of Na+/K+-ATPase drives potassium into cells (hypokalemia) and causes tachycardia, widened pulse pressure and cardiac stress. Fluoxymesterone inhibits 11-beta-HSD2, letting cortisol illicitly activate the mineralocorticoid receptor ('apparent mineralocorticoid excess'), promoting renal sodium/water retention, potassium wasting, and a pressor effect. Stacked, the hypokalemia and blood-pressure/cardiac burdens add up, raising arrhythmia risk. Note the clenbuterol hypokalemia arm is well established in humans, but the Halotestin 11b-HSD2/mineralocorticoid arm is shown only in vitro (human enzyme) and inferred in-body — so the convergence is real but its clinical magnitude from the Halo side is unproven. Halotestin monograph step 8 & side_effect_mechanisms (11b-HSD2 inhibition -> mineralocorticoid excess, hypokalemia, hypertension - in vitro/inferred; PMID 22273746); clenbuterol monograph side_effect_mechanisms (beta2/PKA hypokalemia; tachycardia; PMID 23844963, 18072161) Watch: Serum potassium and sodium, blood pressure, resting heart rate; watch for palpitations, cramps, or ECG (ST/T-wave, QT) changes. Low potassium plus tachycardia is an arrhythmia risk warranting medical attention.
  • Insulin compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both drive potassium out of the blood into cells, so their hypokalemia is additive. Clenbuterol does this via beta-2/cAMP/PKA activation of the Na/K-ATPase; insulin does it by directly stimulating the same Na/K-ATPase and cellular glucose/K+ uptake (both are, in fact, used clinically to treat hyperkalemia). Layering insulin's intracellular potassium shift on top of clenbuterol's can produce deeper hypokalemia, which itself worsens arrhythmia risk from clenbuterol's adrenergic cardiac stimulation. There is also an opposing glucose axis worth noting (acute clenbuterol raises glucose via glycogenolysis/gluconeogenesis while insulin lowers it), making blood-glucose swings less predictable when both are present. clenbuterol.json (beta-2/cAMP Na/K-ATPase -> hypokalemia; acute hyperglycemia, PMID 31887249); insulin monograph (Na/K-ATPase K+ shift, glucose lowering, GLUT4) Watch: Serum potassium is the key shared watchpoint (additive hypokalemia); also blood glucose (opposing effects make it swing), and heart rate/ECG given hypokalemia plus adrenergic drive raises arrhythmia risk. Hypoglycemia warning signs from insulin can be blunted/confused by clenbuterol's tremor and palpitations.
  • MK-677 compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both worsen glucose handling by different routes, so their hyperglycemia/insulin-resistance effect compounds. Acute clenbuterol drives hepatic glycogenolysis/gluconeogenesis and lipolysis, raising glucose (~+30%), free fatty acids and a compensatory insulin surge. MK-677 raises GH/IGF-1, and GH causes insulin resistance and elevated fasting glucose. Run together, the beta-2-driven acute glucose rise sits on top of GH-induced insulin resistance, pushing glucose and insulin higher than either alone and stressing glycemic control. (Note the divergence with chronic low-dose beta-2 agonism, which improves glucose disposal in rodents — the compounding concern is the acute human effect.) clenbuterol.json (acute glucose +30%, FFA/insulin surge; PMID 31887249); mk-677 monograph (GH/IGF-1 -> insulin resistance, raised fasting glucose) Watch: Fasting glucose and, where available, HbA1c and fasting insulin; watch for symptoms of hyperglycemia. This matters most for anyone with impaired glucose tolerance or diabetes risk.
  • T3 compounds risk ⚠ Established
    T3 acts through cardiac TRalpha to raise heart rate, contractility and cardiac output (SERCA up, phospholamban down) and up-regulates cardiac beta-adrenergic receptors, sensitizing the heart to catecholamine/beta-2 drive. Clenbuterol supplies exactly that beta-adrenergic drive. So T3 both directly stimulates the heart AND primes it to respond more strongly to clenbuterol, producing additive tachycardia and arrhythmia risk. Thyrotoxic states also cause an intracellular potassium shift, which compounds clenbuterol's beta-2-mediated hypokalemia. Both also raise metabolic rate/thermogenesis, adding heat and cardiac demand. clenbuterol.json (beta-2 cardiac spillover, hypokalemia); t3 monograph (TRalpha cardiac stimulation, tachycardia/AF, PMID 25438971); thyrotoxic intracellular K+ shift/hypokalemia Watch: Resting and exertional heart rate, blood pressure, palpitations; ECG for atrial fibrillation/SVT and ST-T changes; serum potassium (additive hypokalemia risk) and, if clinically indicated, thyroid function. Watch for signs of thyrotoxicosis layered on adrenergic overdrive (tremor, heat intolerance, weakness).
  • Testosterone compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Shared cardiovascular strain plus a documented contamination hazard. On the mechanism side, testosterone raises hematocrit and, at supraphysiologic exposure, contributes to cardiac hypertrophy, adding to clenbuterol's beta-adrenergic cardiac load (raised heart rate, arrhythmia risk, and — on chronic misuse — LV hypertrophy). Distinctly, a documented case shows the co-exposure hazard: a man injecting product he believed to be testosterone presented with the classic beta-agonist toxidrome (tachycardia, hypokalemia, hyperglycemia, refractory hypotension treated with esmolol), and the unopened ampoule assayed as boldenone undecylenate plus clenbuterol (not testosterone). So the pair can interact both as an intentional AAS+clen stack (additive cardiac risk) and as unintended clenbuterol co-exposure from adulterated black-market injectables sold as testosterone. clenbuterol.json (cardiac hypertrophy/tachycardia; contamination note PMID 29849287); PMID 29849287 (ampoule sold as testosterone assayed as boldenone+clenbuterol -> beta-agonist toxicity); testosterone monograph (raises hematocrit, cardiac strain) Watch: Resting heart rate, blood pressure and ECG; hematocrit/hemoglobin (testosterone-driven erythrocytosis adds to cardiovascular risk); serum potassium and glucose. An unexplained beta-agonist toxidrome (tachycardia + hypokalemia + hyperglycemia + tremor) after using injectable androgens should prompt consideration of clenbuterol contamination.
  • Trenbolone compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-rodent
    Cardiac remodeling from two directions. Clenbuterol drives a beta-adrenergic hypertrophic and pro-apoptotic program in the heart (cardiac apoptosis mediated through residual beta-1 agonism; hypertrophy via a fibroblast IGF-1 paracrine route), promoting left-ventricular hypertrophy and adverse remodeling on chronic misuse — this cardiac-hypertrophy limb is rodent/in-vitro grounded and was NOT detected over a 2-week human course. Androgens such as trenbolone independently promote pathological LV hypertrophy, fibrosis and impaired diastolic function via the androgen receptor (also rodent-grounded). Stacked in a 'cutting' context, the adrenergic cardiac load of clenbuterol adds to androgen-driven cardiac remodeling; a fatal forensic case documented LV hypertrophy in a user in whom clenbuterol plus multiple anabolic steroids including trenbolone were detected. Trenbolone additionally lacks aromatization and stresses the kidney, adding to overall cardiovascular strain. clenbuterol.json (beta-1 cardiac apoptosis PMID 15591297; fibroblast IGF-1 hypertrophy PMID 20577844; human LV mass unchanged at 2 wk PMID 40946331); trenbolone monograph (AR-driven cardiac strain, renal); PMID 31499423 (fatal LV hypertrophy, clenbuterol + AAS incl. trenbolone detected — human case, confounded) Watch: Blood pressure and resting heart rate; ECG and, where available, echocardiography for LV mass/wall thickness and function on chronic use; lipids and, for trenbolone, renal function. Chest pain, breathlessness or palpitations warrant stopping and cardiac evaluation.
  • AOD-9604 potentiates ↑ Evidence-rodent
    Convergent adipocyte lipolysis. Clenbuterol is a β2-adrenergic agonist that drives catecholamine-style lipolysis and raises energy expenditure / fat oxidation. AOD-9604's chronic (rodent) effect UPREGULATES β3-AR expression in fat toward lean levels, and its sustained lipolytic effect REQUIRES functional β-adrenergic receptors (β3-AR knockout abolishes the chronic effect) — so it is not a direct β-agonist but adds a parallel HSL-driven fat-mobilisation signal and greater adrenergic-receptor availability alongside clenbuterol's direct agonism. Net: additive/potentiated lipolysis and thermogenesis, plus compounded catecholaminergic load. Note the additive-lipolysis half is rodent/mechanistic; the compounded adrenergic strain is the more concrete concern. AOD-9604 monograph (β3-AR upregulation; chronic effect β3-AR-dependent, NOT a direct agonist; PMID 11713213); clenbuterol monograph (β2-AR agonist, energy expenditure / fat oxidation) Watch: Resting heart rate, blood pressure, and serum potassium (clenbuterol causes hypokalemia and tachyarrhythmia); watch for tremor/palpitations. The compounded lipolytic drive also means more circulating free fatty acids to dispose of.
  • BPC-157 opposes Evidence-rodent
    Opposing effects on vascular tone and the cardiovascular system. BPC-157 is an endothelium-dependent, NO-mediated VASODILATOR, it relaxes vessels (confirmed in human internal mammary artery, abolished by the NOS blocker L-NAME) and helps maintain/normalize blood pressure via the eNOS/NO pathway. Clenbuterol is a beta-2 adrenergic agonist that drives sympathetic cardiovascular stimulation: tachycardia, palpitations, raised blood pressure, and cardiac strain. Their acute vascular directions oppose (NO-vasodilation vs adrenergic cardiovascular stimulation). This is opposition on vascular/cardiovascular tone, not a shared target. BPC-157 NO vasodilation/BP maintenance PMID 42123221 (human IMA), 33051481 (Src-Cav-1-eNOS, rodent), 23755725; Clenbuterol monograph (beta-2 agonist, tachycardia, hypertension, hypokalemia) Watch: Monitor blood pressure and heart rate (clenbuterol drives both up; BPC-157's NO effect leans the other way, so net effect is unpredictable). Clenbuterol independently causes hypokalemia and cardiac strain, check potassium and be alert to palpitations/arrhythmia. BPC-157's vascular data are largely rodent plus one ex-vivo human artery study.

History & legal status

Originated as a beta2-adrenergic bronchodilator (Boehringer Ingelheim; research code NAB 365), marketed for asthma/COPD under brand names such as Spiropent and Contraspasmin in some countries (Germany, parts of Europe, Latin America, China), and as a veterinary bronchodilator/tocolytic (e.g. Ventipulmin/Planipart for horses and cattle). It reached at most clinical Phase 2-equivalent status for human use in many jurisdictions (ChEMBL max_phase 2) and was even explored as an antidepressant (Vos 1987). It is NOT FDA-approved for humans in the United States and is unlicensed for human use across most of the world; in the US it is approved only for non-food-producing animals (e.g. horses). Its illicit use as a livestock growth promoter ('repartitioning agent' to add lean meat/reduce fat) led to human food-poisoning outbreaks from clenbuterol-contaminated meat/liver and to bans on use in food animals in the EU, US, China and elsewhere, with ongoing residue surveillance (e.g. bovine muscle/liver monitoring). In SPORT it is prohibited at all times by the World Anti-Doping Agency as an 'other anabolic agent' (same Prohibited-List category as anabolic steroids and SARMs); inadvertent positives from contaminated meat are a recognised anti-doping complication. In bodybuilding/fitness culture it is widely misused (oral, off-label, internet-sourced) for fat loss, despite the lack of approved human formulation and documented serious toxicity (Established; PMIDs 37062796, 23844963, 32151232, 24632537).

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Human anabolic dose-response is essentially unmapped: the only RCT used a single dose (80 ug/day) for 2 weeks. There are no controlled human data on higher doses, longer cycles, or whether the rapid beta2 desensitisation can be 'beaten' -- so the real human muscle/fat ceiling is unknown.
  • Human metabolism specifics: the exact CYP enzymes and quantitative metabolite pathways in humans are poorly characterised in the primary literature (most detailed metabolism work is in livestock for residue testing).
  • Whether the cardiac apoptosis/remodelling demonstrated in rodents (Burniston; via beta1) occurs to a clinically meaningful, cumulative degree in humans at misuse doses, and whether it is fully reversible, is not established -- human evidence is limited to acute case reports of injury, not longitudinal imaging/biopsy.
  • Reversibility and persistence of the oxidative-capacity/OXPHOS repression and VO2max drop seen in the RCT -- does it fully recover, and does repeated cycling cause cumulative loss?
  • Translatability of the beta-arrestin-1-biased mechanism: can a biased ligand (or carvedilol-like agent) deliver the muscle benefit without cAMP-driven cardiotoxicity in humans? Promising in mice, untested in people.
  • Long-term metabolic consequences: rodent data show chronic low-dose clenbuterol improves glucose handling, yet acute human dosing causes hyperglycaemia/insulin surges -- the net chronic metabolic effect in humans, and any dependence/withdrawal phenomena, are unknown.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Harm-reduction monitoring only (NOT a protocol and NOT an endorsement of use). CARDIAC is the priority: resting heart rate and blood pressure (watch for sustained tachycardia and widened pulse pressure); a baseline and on-symptom 12-lead ECG looking for ST/T-wave changes, QT prolongation and atrial fibrillation/SVT; and, if chest pain/palpitations/syncope occur, urgent evaluation with cardiac troponin (case reports document troponin/CPK rises and frank MI even in young users). BLOODS: serum POTASSIUM (beta2 agonists drive hypokalaemia, which compounds arrhythmia risk) and glucose (hyperglycaemia/insulin disturbance); consider magnesium. Track tremor, anxiety, insomnia, headache and palpitations as dose-related warning signs. Because the ~35 h half-life means effects and toxicity persist well beyond 24 h, any concerning sign warrants stopping and medical review rather than 'waiting it out.' Anyone with pre-existing cardiac disease, arrhythmia, hypertension, or on QT-prolonging or potassium-lowering drugs is at substantially higher risk. Black-market product is frequently mislabelled or contaminated (clenbuterol found in supposed testosterone vials), so unexpected beta-agonist toxidromes -- tachycardia + hypokalaemia + hyperglycaemia + tremor -- should prompt consideration of clenbuterol exposure. Watchpoints grounded in PMIDs 37062796, 23844963, 18072161, 29849287, 40032559, 40946331.

Caveats

Harm-reduction framing: clenbuterol's danger is not exotic — it is the predictable downside of over-revving the adrenaline system for hours you can't take back. The same beta-2/cAMP push that burns fat also strains the heart, and its long half-life means an accidental double-dose keeps driving the receptor with no off-switch, raising arrhythmia risk and dropping potassium. Documented human poisonings (including contaminated meat and a pediatric case) feature racing heart, chest pain, tremor and dangerous electrolyte shifts [PMID 32169984]. Be precise with your audience: (1) it is NOT a steroid or SARM and does not touch the androgen receptor; (2) the spectacular muscle-growth claims come mostly from rodents — the human muscle effect is modest [PMID 31887249]; (3) there is no monitoring of purity in underground supply, and counterfeit/overdosed product is a real poisoning route. This is mechanism education only, not dosing or how-to guidance.
