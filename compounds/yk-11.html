YK-11

Main target: Androgen receptor. Backed by human data.

EvidenceHuman evidence
ApprovalNot FDA-approved

A lab-made steroidal compound that switches on the androgen receptor in an unusual, partial way and ramps up follistatin to take the brakes (myostatin) off muscle growth — almost entirely studied in cells and rodents, never approved, with zero human trials.

Strong human data 18 citations
Class
Steroidal selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM); 19-norprogesterone-derived synthetic steroid
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR) — acts as a partial agonist that activates AR WITHOUT the normal N/C (amino/carboxyl-terminal) interaction
Human status
NOT approved by any regulator for any use. Zero registered human clinical trials (ClinicalTrials.gov lists none for YK-11/YK11). All mechanistic and efficacy evidence is in vitro (mouse/human cell lines) or in rodents. The ONLY human-derived data are anti-doping forensic studies that detect YK-11 in urine/blood/hair — these confirm people use it and that WADA bans it, but provide NO safety or efficacy data. It is sold illicitly as a 'research chemical'/supplement. Marketed as a SARM, but it is chemically a steroid (19-norprogesterone-derived), so 'selective/safe' branding is misleading. CRITICAL: YK-11 evidence is essentially ALL in-vitro (cell culture); there are no published rodent in-vivo or human trials.

At a glance

  • Promotes muscle-cell differentiation in vitro via follistatin-mediated myostatin inhibition (mouse cells)
  • Increases muscle master-regulator genes MyoD, Myf5, myogenin more than DHT (in vitro)
  • Stimulates bone-forming osteoblast proliferation/mineralization and bone-defect repair (rodent)
  • Triggers rapid non-genomic Akt signaling (in vitro)
  • Partial, gene-selective androgen activity — distinct gene set vs testosterone/DHT
  • Safety signal: induced oxidative stress and mitochondrial dysfunction in rat hippocampus (rodent)
  • No measured human efficacy — muscle/strength gains in people are unproven and anecdotal only

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

7 steps7 human

  1. Binds the androgen receptor as a partial agonist Evidence-human

    YK-11 docks into the same androgen receptor that testosterone and DHT use, but it only partially switches it on. In a reporter test it gave a weaker AR signal than DHT — it's a 'partial' agonist, not a full one. PMID 21372378 (in vitro, human AR reporter assay)

  2. Activates AR without N/C interaction Evidence-human

    Normally a full androgen makes the receptor fold so its two ends (the N-terminal and C-terminal) clasp together — that 'N/C interaction' drives strong gene activation. YK-11 skips this: it speeds the receptor into the nucleus but does NOT trigger the N/C clasp, and even blocks DHT from making it. This unusual folding is thought to be why it turns on a different, narrower set of genes than testosterone. PMID 21372378 (in vitro)

  3. Drives gene-selective output via different DNA binding and cofactors Evidence-human

    Because the receptor is shaped differently, it lands on DNA differently and recruits a different set of helper proteins (cofactors) than DHT does. This is the molecular reason its effects don't perfectly match a normal androgen — the basis of the 'selective' claim. PMID 36030969 (in vitro, MDA-MB-453 cells)

  4. Boosts follistatin in muscle cells Evidence-in vitro (human AR / muscle cell lines; NO in-vivo or human data)

    In mouse muscle precursor cells, YK-11 ramps up follistatin — a protein that mops up myostatin. Plain DHT does NOT do this. This follistatin step is the signature of YK-11. PMID 23995658 (in vitro, mouse C2C12 myoblasts)

  5. Follistatin blocks myostatin, releasing the muscle brake Evidence-in vitro (human AR / muscle cell lines; NO in-vivo or human data)

    Myostatin is the body's built-in 'stop growing' signal for muscle. By raising follistatin, YK-11 neutralizes myostatin, removing that brake. When researchers blocked follistatin with an antibody, YK-11's muscle-building effect disappeared — proving follistatin is the key link. PMID 23995658 (in vitro, mouse C2C12; anti-follistatin antibody reversed the effect)

  6. Turns up muscle-building master genes Evidence-in vitro (human AR / muscle cell lines; NO in-vivo or human data)

    With the brake off, YK-11 strongly raises the muscle-differentiation 'master switches' (MyoD, Myf5, myogenin) that turn precursor cells into mature muscle fibers — more strongly than DHT in these cell experiments. PMID 23995658 (in vitro, mouse cells)

  7. Also pushes bone cells and fires a fast non-genomic signal Evidence-in vitro (human AR / muscle cell lines; NO in-vivo or human data)

    Beyond muscle, YK-11 made mouse bone-forming cells proliferate and mineralize, and repaired bone defects in rodents. It also flips on a rapid 'non-genomic' Akt signal (a growth/survival pathway that acts in minutes, separate from gene transcription). PMID 29491216 (mouse MC3T3-E1 osteoblasts); PMID 39660819 (rodent BMSCs / bone defect repair)

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. YK-11 is a steroidal (gestrinone/19-norprogesterone-derived) compound that binds the intracellular androgen receptor (AR) and acts as a PARTIAL AGONIST (Established for AR partial agonism; Evidence-in vitro). No published equilibrium binding constant (Ki/Kd) or transactivation EC50 has been formally reported in peer-reviewed primary literature — the original characterization used an ARE-luciferase reporter and showed submaximal transactivation versus the full agonist DHT rather than a numeric affinity (PMID 21372378). Be explicit: any 'Ki' figures circulating on vendor/forum pages are NOT traceable to a primary source. The defining pharmacology is that YK-11 activates AR WITHOUT inducing the AR amino/carboxyl-terminal (N/C) interaction, and it actually PREVENTS DHT-mediated N/C interaction (PMID 21372378) — this is the molecular basis of its partial, gene-selective agonism. A 2025 in-silico docking/MD study reports favorable computed binding to both AR and 5-alpha-reductase type II, with hydrogen bonding at AR residue Arg752 and a C20 double bond conferring binding stability (Evidence-in silico, PMID 40757306; Arg752 contact also in PMID 37468001). Selectivity over classic steroid receptors (PR/GR/ER) is not rigorously quantified in humans; the 19-norprogesterone backbone raises plausible but unproven progestogenic cross-reactivity (Speculative).

Signalling cascade. Mechanism (Established that AR is the proximal target; downstream steps Evidence-in vitro/rodent): (1) YK-11 enters the cell and binds cytoplasmic AR, accelerating AR nuclear translocation comparably to DHT (PMID 21372378). (2) In the nucleus the AR N-terminal domain acts as a constitutive transactivator, but because the C17 orthoester/methoxyethylidene 'bulky' group sterically displaces AR helices 11/12, the N/C interaction does not form — yielding a DISTINCT DNA-binding and coactivator-recruitment pattern from DHT and therefore gene-SELECTIVE transcription (PMID 21372378; mechanistic detail via AR Gln902 mutagenesis and mammalian two-hybrid in PMID 36030969). (3) The signature anabolic node: in C2C12 myoblasts YK-11 (unlike DHT) induces FOLLISTATIN expression; follistatin is a secreted antagonist that sequesters myostatin/activin, so YK-11 amplifies myogenic regulatory factors (MyoD, Myf5, myogenin). Blocking follistatin with anti-Fst antibody reverses the anabolic effect, establishing follistatin as the causal mediator in vitro (PMID 23995658). (4) A parallel NON-GENOMIC arm: YK-11 increases phospho-Akt, driving osteoblast proliferation/mineralization in MC3T3-E1 cells, attenuated by AR antagonist — i.e., rapid AR-linked Akt signaling (PMID 29491216). (5) In vivo (septic mice), YK-11 lowered myostatin-pathway/inflammatory signaling (NF-kB, p-Smad2, pro-inflammatory cytokines) while raising follistatin (Evidence-rodent, PMID 33588136).

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Oral (taken as an unregulated research-chemical powder/capsule or liquid; no pharmaceutical formulation exists). Equine and rodent studies dose orally (PMID 36519889, 30379415).
Absorption / bioavailability
No human bioavailability, Cmax, Tmax or AUC data exist (human-data gap is total). Oral absorption is demonstrated only indirectly — urinary metabolites appear after oral dosing in humans and horses (PMID 30379415, 36519889). The orthoester moiety at the D-ring is chemically labile, predicting substantial first-pass/gut conversion.
Half-life
No validated human plasma half-life has been published (key unknown). Forum-quoted figures (~6-10 h) are NOT traceable to primary pharmacokinetic data. In a deuterated-YK11 human elimination study, intact parent YK-11 was NOT detectable in urine; unconjugated metabolites cleared within ~24 h while glucuronidated/sulfated metabolites persisted >48 h (PMID 30379415).
Metabolism
Extensive phase I and phase II conversion. The labile orthoester/methoxyethylidene group is hydrolyzed and the steroid nucleus is reduced; in humans 14 deuterated urinary metabolites were characterized, the two most useful being glucuronidated 5beta-19-nor-pregnane-3alpha,17beta,20-triol and 5beta-19-nor-pregnane-3alpha,17beta-diol-20-one (PMID 30379415). Reduction implies 5-beta/5-alpha reductase and hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase involvement; conjugation is via UGT (glucuronidation) and SULT (sulfation). Specific CYP isoenzymes have NOT been mapped (Evidence-human for metabolite identity; enzyme assignment partly inferred). Equine metabolism is broadly parallel (PMID 36519889).
Excretion
Renal/urinary as glucuronide and sulfate conjugates; little to no intact parent excreted (PMID 30379415, 36519889).
Notes
YK-11 is itself the active molecule (not a classic prodrug), but its anabolic action is mediated downstream by induced follistatin (PMID 23995658). It is NOT esterified. CNS exposure is a concern: PBPK modeling and rodent data indicate YK-11 crosses the blood-brain barrier and reaches the hippocampus (Evidence-rodent/in silico, PMID 38521455). The orthoester lability is the dominant PK theme and the reason no intact drug is recoverable in urine.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

Human dose-response is UNKNOWN — there are zero human pharmacology, efficacy or dose-finding studies and zero registered clinical trials (no YK-11 trials are registered on ClinicalTrials.gov). What is grounded: (1) In vitro, anabolic and follistatin-inducing effects in C2C12 myoblasts and osteogenic effects in MC3T3-E1 occur at micromolar concentrations on the order of ~1-10 uM (effect magnitudes equal or exceed DHT for myogenic markers and follistatin induction; PMID 23995658, 29491216) — these are CELL-CULTURE concentrations, not extrapolable to oral human doses. (2) Rodent toxicology: oral YK-11 at 0.35 g/kg in rats over 5 weeks produced measurable hippocampal oxidative stress and mitochondrial dysfunction (PMID 37468001) and memory/neuroinflammatory impairment (PMID 38521455) — note this is an extremely high mg/kg exposure and is a TOXICITY dose, not an efficacy dose. (3) Equine: 0.2 mg/kg/day x3 (hair study, PMID 39001560) and 50 mg/day x3 (metabolism, PMID 36519889) were used to generate detectable metabolites, not to measure anabolism. No species has a published muscle-growth dose-response curve. Real-world human use is self-reported in the low tens of milligrams orally, but NO efficacy or safety data support any specific human dose (explicit gap). This entry gives NO dosing protocol.

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Enobosarm (ostarine, MK-2866 / GTx-024)
    same: Both marketed as muscle-building SARMs sold on the gray market and both WADA-prohibited; both target AR for anabolic effect
    different: Enobosarm is NON-steroidal and is the most clinically advanced SARM, with multiple human RCTs (e.g., Phase 2 in cancer cachexia showing lean-mass gains, PMID 23499390; Phase 3 POWER program, PMID 27138015) and documented human DILI (PMID 35655632). YK-11 is STEROIDAL (19-norprogesterone-derived), has ZERO human trials, and uniquely works through follistatin induction rather than purely AR transactivation
  • vs RAD140 (testolone)
    same: Both potent gray-market 'SARMs' used for mass, both WADA-banned, both detectable as doping agents (YK-11 + RAD140 studied together in equine hair, PMID 39001560)
    different: RAD140 is non-steroidal with a cleaner AR-selective profile; YK-11 is steroidal, has the follistatin/myostatin-suppression mechanism RAD140 lacks, and its rodent neurotoxicity signal (PMID 37468001/38521455) is distinct
  • vs DHT (dihydrotestosterone, the endogenous full AR agonist comparator)
    same: Both bind AR, accelerate AR nuclear translocation and drive myogenic/osteogenic programs
    different: DHT is a FULL agonist that induces the AR N/C interaction; YK-11 is a PARTIAL agonist that does NOT induce N/C interaction and actively blocks DHT's N/C interaction (PMID 21372378), giving gene-selective transcription and follistatin induction that DHT does not produce (PMID 23995658)
  • vs Follistatin-directed / myostatin-inhibitor biologics (e.g., follistatin gene therapy, anti-myostatin antibodies)
    same: Share the downstream goal of relieving myostatin's brake on muscle (YK-11 raises endogenous follistatin)
    different: YK-11's myostatin effect is INDIRECT and modest (follistatin induced secondarily to AR signaling, shown in vitro/rodent only, PMID 23995658, 33588136), whereas dedicated biologics target the myostatin/activin pathway directly; YK-11 also carries full androgenic and HPG-suppressive liabilities those biologics do not

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

"YK-11 was first described in 2011 by Yuichiro Kanno and colleagues at Toho University, Japan, as a synthetic gestrinone/19-norprogesterone-derived steroid and characterized as a novel AR partial agonist / SARM (PMID 21372378), with the follistatin-mediated anabolic mechanism reported in 2013 (PMID 23995658). It has NEVER been an approved drug in any jurisdiction, never entered formal human clinical trials, and remains a research chemical. It emerged on the black/gray market around 2016-2018, where it was identified in seized products and sold by internet supplement vendors (PMID 30379415, 33934684). It is on the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) Prohibited List under S1.2 'Other Anabolic Agents'/SARMs (prohibited at all times), prompting development of dedicated doping-control detection methods (PMID 28440570, 30379415, 37946705) and equine-sport detection work (PMID 36519889, 39001560). In the US it is not an FDA-approved drug and not a legal dietary-supplement ingredient; the FDA has warned that SARMs are unapproved and illegally marketed, and the proposed SARMs Control Act would place SARMs under DEA scheduling. It is effectively banned for athletic use worldwide and sold only as an unregulated 'research/not-for-human-consumption' product."

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • No human pharmacokinetics: validated oral bioavailability, Cmax, and plasma half-life of YK-11 are entirely unknown (no intact parent even recovered in human urine; PMID 30379415).
  • No human efficacy or dose-response: there is no clinical evidence that YK-11 builds muscle or strength in humans, and no dose-finding data in any species for anabolism (only toxicity/metabolite-generation doses exist).
  • Quantitative AR pharmacology gap: no published Ki/Kd or transactivation EC50 for YK-11 at AR, and no rigorous selectivity profiling against PR/GR/ER despite its 19-nor progestin backbone.
  • Hepatotoxicity magnitude: whether YK-11 specifically causes DILI at human-relevant exposures (vs the documented SARM class effect) is uncharacterized — no YK-11-specific human liver-injury series exists.
  • Neurotoxicity translation: does the striking rodent hippocampal oxidative/apoptotic/memory toxicity at 0.35 g/kg (PMID 37468001, 38521455) occur at human-relevant doses, and is it reversible? Unknown.
  • 5-alpha-reductase interaction: the in-silico claim that YK-11 binds/inhibits 5aR-II (PMID 40757306, 38521455) is unconfirmed experimentally, leaving its true effect on local DHT and on androgenic side effects open.

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only (NOT an endorsement; no dosing given). Liver: baseline and periodic ALT, AST, GGT, alkaline phosphatase and total/direct bilirubin — SARM-class DILI can be cholestatic and may lag symptoms; jaundice, dark urine, pale stool, right-upper-quadrant pain, pruritus or nausea warrant immediate cessation and medical review (PMID 38059982, 35340496, 39328701). Hormonal axis: total/free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG and estradiol to detect HPG suppression; watch for low libido, erectile dysfunction, testicular shrinkage and mood/energy 'crash' after stopping. Lipids/cardiovascular: fasting lipid panel watching for HDL suppression and rising LDL; blood pressure. Hematology/renal: CBC (hematocrit), creatinine. Androgenic signs: acne, scalp hair shedding, and in women voice deepening/hirsutism/menstrual change (some virilization may be irreversible — stop at first sign). Neurological/cognitive: given rodent hippocampal toxicity, note new memory problems, mood disturbance or cognitive fog and seek evaluation (Evidence-rodent; human relevance unproven). Product-quality caveat: gray-market YK-11 is frequently mislabeled, underdosed, or adulterated with other SARMs/steroids (PMID 33934684), so any monitoring also has to account for unknown actual contents. None of this makes YK-11 safe — there is no human safety dataset."

Caveats

Harm-reduction and accuracy framing for a general audience: (1) UNAPPROVED & UNTESTED IN HUMANS — there is not a single human trial, so claimed muscle gains rest on cell/rodent data plus anecdote, and human risks are essentially uncharacterized. (2) IT'S A STEROID, NOT A 'CLEAN SARM' — YK-11 has a steroidal (19-norprogesterone) backbone, so the popular 'tissue-selective, side-effect-free' marketing is inaccurate; expect steroid-like and possible progestogenic behavior. (3) LIVER & HORMONAL CONCERNS ARE PLAUSIBLE BUT UNQUANTIFIED — anabolic-androgenic compounds commonly suppress natural testosterone and can stress the liver; YK-11 hepatotoxicity has not been formally measured in humans, which is a data gap, not a clean bill of health. Users widely report needing testosterone-suppression management — this is anecdote, not evidence. (4) NEUROTOXICITY SIGNAL — in rats, YK-11 caused oxidative stress and mitochondrial dysfunction in the hippocampus (PMID 37468001, 38521455). (5) Supplement products are unregulated: dose, purity, and even whether the bottle contains YK-11 at all are unverified. This is mechanism education only — not dosing, sourcing, or use guidance.
