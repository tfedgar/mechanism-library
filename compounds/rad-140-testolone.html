RAD-140

A lab-built molecule that flips the same "grow muscle" switch testosterone uses, but tries to leave the prostate and other organs mostly alone.

Limited human data 23 citations
Class
Nonsteroidal selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM)
Primary target
The androgen receptor (AR) — the same protein receptor that testosterone and DHT act on
Human status
Investigational, NOT an approved drug anywhere. The only formal human trial is a first-in-human Phase 1 study in ER+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer (PMID 34565686); efficacy/safety for muscle-building in healthy people has not been established in controlled human trials. Most muscle data are rodent/primate. Real-world human evidence is dominated by case reports of harm (liver injury, cardiac inflammation, hormonal suppression) from products sold as supplements. WADA-prohibited. Certainty on muscle benefit in humans: low. Harm-reduction note: this is sold unregulated, products are frequently mislabeled or contaminated, and documented serious toxicity is real.

At a glance

  • Builds lean muscle mass and increases bone density (shown in rodents/primates; the intended anabolic effect)
  • Designed to spare the prostate relative to testosterone — much weaker AR activation there in animal models
  • Does not aromatize to estrogen and is not 5-alpha-reduced to DHT (it is non-steroidal)
  • Suppresses the body's own testosterone production by shutting down the HPG axis
  • Human case reports: drug-induced liver injury (often cholestatic), myocarditis/myopericarditis, and gynecomastia/hypogonadism
  • Neuroprotective in cultured neurons and lesioned rat brain via ERK/MAPK signaling (preclinical only)
  • Banned by WADA as a performance-enhancing substance; detectable in anti-doping testing

Mechanism, step by step

  1. It plugs into the androgen receptor Established

    RAD-140 is a non-steroid molecule that slots into the same binding pocket on the androgen receptor (AR) that testosterone normally uses, and it binds tightly and specifically — so it acts like an artificial 'androgen key' for that one lock. PMID 24900290; PMID 28974548; PMID 30825507

  2. The receptor changes shape and wakes up Established

    Once RAD-140 is bound, the receptor folds into an active shape and drops the 'holding' proteins that normally keep it idle. Crucially, the exact shape RAD-140 induces is slightly different from the one testosterone induces, which matters for what happens next. PMID 18801930; PMID 30825507

  3. It cannot be converted to estrogen or to DHT Established

    Unlike testosterone, RAD-140 is not a steroid, so the body's aromatase enzyme cannot turn it into estrogen and 5-alpha-reductase cannot turn it into the stronger androgen DHT. Its effect comes only from directly activating the receptor — note this does NOT make estrogen side effects impossible, because shutting down your own testosterone can still unbalance hormones (real-world gynecomastia has been reported). PMID 24900290; PMID 39145153

  4. The activated receptor goes to the DNA and works as a gene switch Established

    The RAD-140-bound receptor moves into the cell nucleus, pairs up, and clamps onto specific 'androgen response element' spots on the DNA, where it turns some genes up and others down — this genomic step is the core of how androgens build tissue. PMID 18801930; PMID 28974548

  5. Tissue selectivity comes from which helper proteins get recruited Evidence-rodent

    Because RAD-140 holds the receptor in its own particular shape, in muscle and bone it recruits the coactivator proteins that drive strong gene activation, while in tissues like the prostate it recruits a weaker/different mix — so it behaves as a strong builder in muscle but only a weak activator in prostate. This 'selectivity' is a matter of degree, not an on/off guarantee. PMID 23449329; PMID 18801930; PMID 28974548

  6. Muscle and bone cells respond by building, plus a fast survival signal Evidence-rodent / early-phase (anabolic & tissue-selectivity claims rest on preclinical + Phase-1 oncology data, NOT muscle-building RCTs)

    The net result in target tissues is more protein synthesis and growth — increased lean muscle mass and bone in animal models. RAD-140 also fires a fast, non-gene 'survival' pathway (ERK/MAPK), which is how it protected nerve cells in rat studies. PMID 24900290; PMID 40680216; PMID 24428527

  7. It still signals through the receptor in places you do not want Evidence-human

    Because the target is the body's own androgen receptor, RAD-140 still has androgen-type effects elsewhere: it suppresses the brain's signal to make natural testosterone (HPG-axis shutdown), and human case reports link it to liver injury and heart-muscle inflammation — so 'selective' does not mean 'side-effect-free'. PMID 39145153; PMID 39328701; PMID 35233331; PMID 38864168

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. RAD-140 is a nonsteroidal selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM) of the oxadiazole class that binds the androgen receptor (AR) ligand-binding domain with high affinity and good steroid-receptor selectivity. In the discovery paper (Miller 2011, PMID 24900290) it bound human AR with Ki ~7 nM, somewhat tighter than testosterone (Ki ~29 nM) and comparable to DHT (Ki ~10 nM). Selectivity over other nuclear receptors is wide: progesterone receptor IC50 ~750 nM (vs progesterone 0.2 nM), with 'good selectivity' over estrogen and glucocorticoid receptors; functional EC50 in a C2C12 myo/osteoblast AR-transactivation assay was ~0.1 nM (DHT ~0.05 nM). It is an AR AGONIST in anabolic tissues (muscle, bone) but a weak/partial agonist — or even relative antagonist — in some reproductive and tumor contexts. Crucially RAD-140 has NO measurable aromatase substrate activity (it is not converted to estrogen) and is not 5-alpha-reduced, distinguishing it from testosterone. [Established/Evidence-in vitro] Certainty on the exact Ki is Evidence-in vitro (single-sponsor characterization; Radius Health).

Signalling cascade. Mechanistically RAD-140 works through the same AR machinery as endogenous androgens but with biased/tissue-selective output. GENOMIC pathway: lipophilic RAD-140 crosses the membrane and binds cytoplasmic AR, displacing heat-shock chaperones (HSP90/HSP70/HSP56), triggering a conformational change in the ligand-binding domain (helix 12 repositioning), receptor dimerization, and nuclear translocation. In the nucleus the AR dimer binds androgen-response elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer regions and recruits coactivators (SRC-1/2/3, p300/CBP, ARA70) to drive transcription of anabolic genes (e.g., IGF-1 axis, myostatin suppression, muscle structural/contractile genes). Tissue selectivity is thought to arise from ligand-specific receptor conformations that recruit DIFFERENT coregulator complexes depending on the cellular coactivator/corepressor milieu — so muscle/bone see strong agonism while prostate sees attenuated transcription. NON-GENOMIC pathway: RAD-140 also activates rapid membrane-proximal signaling; in hippocampal neurons it was as neuroprotective as testosterone via MAPK/ERK — it raised ERK phosphorylation and protection was abolished by the MEK inhibitor U0126 (PMID 24428527), consistent with AR-driven Src/Ras/Raf/MEK/ERK and PI3K/Akt cascades that support cell survival and protein synthesis. In AR+/ER+ breast cancer cells RAD-140 produces a DISTINCT program: AR activation that REPRESSES the estrogen pathway including the ESR1 gene, suppressing ER-driven proliferation (PMID 28974548).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral (tablet/capsule/liquid 'research chemical' forms on the gray market). Transdermal exposure has been characterized for doping purposes (PMID 40632609). Not an injectable/ester product — RAD-140 is the active molecule itself, not a prodrug.
Absorption / bioavailability
Orally bioavailable by design. Preclinical oral bioavailability: ~27-63% in rats and ~65-75% in cynomolgus monkeys (PMID 24900290). Human oral exposure confirmed in the Phase 1 trial (PMID 34565686) and in microdose excretion studies (PMID 35888790). [Evidence-rodent/primate for %F; Evidence-human for the fact of oral exposure]
Half-life
~44.7 hours in humans (terminal t1/2 from the Phase 1 oncology trial, PMID 34565686), which supported once-daily dosing. This long half-life means accumulation over days and a prolonged washout. [Evidence-human]
Metabolism
Hepatic. RAD-140 undergoes oxidative (phase I) metabolism — hydroxylation, O-demethylation/de-methoxylation and dechlorination of the chlorinated benzonitrile/oxadiazole scaffold — generating multiple metabolites identified in human urine (PMID 35888790) and in in vitro/equine systems (PMID 37421437, PMID 32853476). CYP-mediated oxidation is the dominant route; specific isoform mapping (e.g., CYP3A4) is characterized mainly in in vitro microsome/hepatocyte comparison work (PMID 37421437) rather than definitive human DDI studies. Microsomal half-life was >2 h across rat, monkey and human microsomes (PMID 24900290). [Evidence-human for metabolite identity; isoform attribution Evidence-in vitro]
Excretion
Renal/urinary elimination of metabolites and a small fraction of intact drug; the parent and at least six metabolites are detectable in urine, with elimination/detection windows extending days after even microdoses (PMID 35888790). [Evidence-human]
Notes
Not aromatized and not 5-alpha-reduced (no estrogenic or DHT metabolites), so estrogenic side effects are not driven by aromatization the way they are with testosterone. CNS penetrant (neuroprotective and AR-active in brain, PMID 24428527). Long t1/2 (~45 h) is clinically important: suppression and lab abnormalities can persist well beyond the last dose. Real-world gray-market product purity/identity is unreliable — products labeled RAD-140 are frequently mislabeled or co-formulated with other SARMs (e.g., LGD-4033) (PMID 33062783).

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

No validated therapeutic human dose-response exists; RAD-140 is not approved for any indication and human data come almost entirely from one oncology trial plus case reports. PRIMATE (cynomolgus, 28 days): doses 0.01, 0.1, 1.0 mg/kg produced a dose-graded body-weight gain, reaching >10% at 0.1 mg/kg (p<0.01); lean-mass increase trended with dose but did not reach significance (PMID 24900290) — establishing anabolic activity at sub-mg/kg doses with muscle stimulated at lower doses than prostate (the tissue-selectivity signal). RODENT: in castrated rats RAD-140 stimulated levator ani muscle at doses that largely spared prostate (Hershberger-type assay, PMID 24900290); 5 mg/kg in mice produced measurable muscle/health effects, some NEGATIVE in aging female mice (PMID 37758180, 40158703). HUMAN: the only formal dosing was 50, 100, 150 mg/day in postmenopausal metastatic breast-cancer patients; MTD was 100 mg/day, with dose-limiting hepatotoxicity (AST/ALT elevations) at higher exposure and clear AR target engagement (SHBG fell in 18/18, PSA rose in 16/20) (PMID 34565686). Recreational/bodybuilding doses (commonly cited as ~10-20 mg/day) have NEVER been studied for efficacy or dose-response in healthy people — the muscle-gain magnitude humans actually report is unquantified by controlled data. Bottom line: human anabolic dose-response in healthy adults is UNKNOWN; the only human dose-toxicity curve we have (oncology) shows liver injury rising with dose. [Evidence-primate/rodent for anabolism; Evidence-human only for target engagement and hepatotoxicity, in a non-athletic cancer population]

How it compares

  • vs Testosterone / anabolic-androgenic steroids (AAS)
    same: Both are AR agonists driving muscle/bone anabolism via the same genomic AR-ARE-coactivator machinery; both suppress the HPG axis and can cause cholestatic liver injury and HDL suppression.
    different: RAD-140 is nonsteroidal and tissue-selective (muscle/bone at doses sparing prostate), is NOT aromatized to estradiol and NOT 5-alpha-reduced to DHT, and is orally active without 17-alpha-alkylation. Testosterone has broad androgenic/virilizing and estrogenic effects; RAD-140's selectivity is real but only relative (PSA still rises). RAD-140 has far less human safety/efficacy data.
  • vs LGD-4033 (ligandrol)
    same: Both are nonsteroidal oral SARMs marketed for muscle gain; both suppress endogenous testosterone, lower HDL, and are implicated in DILI; both WADA-banned and frequently sold together (combo products).
    different: LGD-4033 has actual healthy-volunteer Phase 1 dose-ranging data (defined suppression and lean-mass signal at low mg doses); RAD-140's only human dosing is in cancer patients. RAD-140 has a notably long t1/2 (~45 h) and a distinct AR-mediated ESR1-repression mechanism exploited for breast cancer; LGD-4033 was not developed as an oncology agent.
  • vs Enobosarm (ostarine / MK-2866)
    same: Nonsteroidal SARM, oral, AR agonist with muscle/bone selectivity; WADA-prohibited; both reached clinical trials.
    different: Enobosarm is the most clinically advanced SARM (large Phase 2/3 programs in cancer cachexia and stress urinary incontinence, thousands of subjects) and is generally milder; RAD-140 appears more potent at the AR (Ki ~7 nM) and carries a stronger reported hepatotoxicity/cardiac case-signal. Enobosarm's human efficacy dataset dwarfs RAD-140's.
  • vs ACP-105 / GSK-2881078 / GLPG0492 (other nonsteroidal SARM candidates)
    same: Same class and intended muscle-selective AR-agonist profile; studied side-by-side in AR-binding/transactivation and doping-detection work.
    different: In comparative in vitro/in silico binding assays the SARMs show slightly different AR binding modes and potencies (e.g., GSK-2881078 EC50 ~4 nM in prostate-cell reporter); these candidates have their own (largely abandoned or differently-indicated) development paths, whereas RAD-140 uniquely advanced into a breast-cancer Phase 1.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • DHB compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both act through the androgen receptor and both suppress HDL cholesterol and the HPTA. RAD-140, as a SARM, raises hepatic lipase and suppresses HDL-C (PMID 35822201) - the same hepatic-lipase route by which non-aromatizing androgens like DHB depress HDL; combined, HDL falls further. Both also drive androgen-mediated negative feedback on GnRH/LH/FSH, so co-use deepens HPTA shutdown and slows recovery. Neither aromatizes, so there is no estrogenic buffer for lipids or libido. SARM hepatic-lipase/HDL suppression PMID 35822201; RAD-140 AR agonism + HPTA suppression (RAD-140 monograph); DHB HDL/HPTA class effect (DHB monograph side_effect_mechanisms) Watch: Fasting lipids (HDL-C/ApoB); LH, FSH, total testosterone and hypogonadal symptoms (libido, mood, testicular atrophy) to gauge combined suppression.
  • Exemestane compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    RAD-140 is an oral AR agonist that up-regulates hepatic lipase and lowers HDL (a class effect shared with anabolic steroids/SARMs). Exemestane's estrogen suppression independently removes estradiol's favorable influence on the lipid profile (its HDL-supporting effect). Stacking the two therefore compounds an unfavorable lipid shift through two separate mechanisms — direct AR-driven HDL suppression plus estrogen-deprivation lipid worsening. RAD-140 monograph side_effect_mechanisms (hepatic-lipase HDL suppression); Exemestane monograph caveats (U-shaped E2); Aromatase MoA card (crashed E2 worsens lipids) Watch: Full lipid panel (HDL, LDL/ApoB, TC:HDL ratio) — the additive HDL suppression plus estrogen-deprivation lipid worsening can exceed either alone. Keeping E2 in range (not crashed) limits exemestane's lipid contribution.
  • Halotestin compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both act through the androgen receptor, both suppress the HPG axis (low LH/FSH, suppressed endogenous testosterone), and both burden the liver and lipids. Fluoxymesterone drives AR-mediated negative feedback shutting down endogenous testosterone while crashing HDL via hepatic lipase and stressing the liver; RAD-140 is a strongly HPTA-suppressing SARM with human case reports of cholestatic drug-induced liver injury and hypogonadism/gynecomastia. Combined, they deepen gonadal-axis shutdown and add two hepatic/androgenic insults on top of Halotestin's marked hepatotoxicity and lipid harm. Halotestin monograph key_effects & side_effect_mechanisms (HPG suppression; hepatic-lipase HDL crash; hepatotoxicity); rad-140 monograph key_effects (HPG shutdown; case-report cholestatic DILI, hypogonadism, gynecomastia) Watch: LH/FSH, total/free testosterone (axis suppression), HDL/lipid panel, and liver enzymes/bilirubin; watch for testicular atrophy and low libido after cessation, and jaundice/pruritus (cholestatic DILI).
  • Ligandrol compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both are non-steroidal AR-agonist SARMs, so together they load the SAME androgen receptor and stack the same three off-target harms: (1) deeper hypothalamic/pituitary negative feedback = greater HPTA/testosterone shutdown; (2) additive HDL-cholesterol suppression via AR-driven changes in hepatic lipase/lipoprotein metabolism (a pro-atherogenic lipid shift both compounds produce alone); and (3) overlapping idiosyncratic cholestatic drug-induced liver injury — both carry repeated real-world DILI case reports, including combination LGD+RAD-140 products. Because RAD-140 has an even stronger reputation for suppression/hepatotoxicity, co-exposure raises the ceiling on all three. PMID 22459616 (LGD HDL/HPTA suppression, RCT); PMID 36561105, 33062783 (RAD-140 and RAD-140+LGD DILI case reports); rad-140-testolone.json; ligandrol-lgd-4033.json Watch: Fasting lipid panel (watch HDL falling); total/free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG for axis suppression; liver panel with special attention to total/direct BILIRUBIN plus ALT/AST/ALP/GGT, and signs of cholestasis (jaundice, dark urine, itching). Any jaundice or rising bilirubin warrants stopping and urgent medical review.
  • Enclomiphene opposes Evidence-human
    RAD-140 is a SARM (androgen-receptor agonist) that suppresses the HPG axis — its androgenic signal re-engages negative feedback and shuts down GnRH→LH→FSH and endogenous testosterone. Enclomiphene works in the opposite direction, blocking central ER to RAISE LH/FSH. While RAD-140 is present its suppression opposes enclomiphene's drive; enclomiphene is instead used to attempt recovery of the axis after RAD-140 is stopped, and it cannot override active SARM suppression. Enclomiphene monograph (caveats; HPG mechanism); RAD-140 monograph (HPG-axis shutdown; lowers HDL; DILI case reports); corpus MoA card 'HPG axis' Watch: LH/FSH and total/free testosterone to gauge suppression versus recovery (LH/FSH should rise once RAD-140 clears and enclomiphene acts). HDL/lipid panel (RAD-140 lowers HDL); liver enzymes given RAD-140's hepatotoxicity signal. Semen analysis if fertility is a concern.
  • Andarine shares target Evidence-rodent
    Both are non-steroidal SARMs occupying the same androgen receptor, so they compete for AR binding and their liabilities overlap on the same pathways rather than complementing each other: both suppress the HPG axis (lower LH/FSH), both lower HDL through hepatic AR effects, and both carry a SARM-class drug-induced-liver-injury signal (RAD-140 has published human DILI case reports). Layering them adds AR occupancy and hepatic/lipid burden through the identical mechanism. andarine-s-4.json (primary_target AR; HPG + HDL + DILI class effects, PMID 30503797); rad-140-testolone.json (AR agonist); PMID 36561105 (RAD-140 DILI) Watch: LH/FSH and total/free testosterone for combined suppression; HDL for the shared lipid shift; ALT/AST/GGT/ALP and bilirubin for the shared liver-injury signal.
  • Clomiphene citrate interacts Evidence-human
    RAD-140 is a suppressive androgen-receptor agonist: it activates ARs and thereby drives HPG negative feedback, shutting down endogenous LH/FSH and testosterone (HPTA suppression, confirmed in its monograph). Clomiphene is the SERM used to RESTART that suppressed axis afterward — its central ER antagonism only works once the suppressing SARM has cleared and the pituitary/testes can respond. The relationship is sequential/conditional: RAD-140 creates the suppression that clomiphene is later used to reverse, but while RAD-140 is still acting the axis stays suppressed and clomiphene cannot overcome the ongoing androgen feedback. This is the mechanistic basis of SERM-based recovery after SARMs. RAD-140 monograph (AR agonist, suppresses HPG axis; human case reports of hypogonadism) + clomiphene monograph caveats (upstream action, needs intact un-suppressed axis); PMC OA AAS/PCT review (SARMs/AAS suppress HPTA; SERMs incl. clomiphene used to restore) [corpus textbook]; case report PMID 12524089 Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone before and during recovery — rising LH/FSH indicates the axis is responding once the SARM clears; persistently flat gonadotropins suggest continued suppression or an axis that cannot respond. Baseline vs recovery bloodwork is the objective check.

History & legal status

RAD-140 (testolone) was discovered and developed by Radius Health, Inc. (Cambridge/Waltham, MA), first described in 2010-2011 (Miller et al., ACS Med Chem Lett, PMID 24900290) as an oral, tissue-selective anabolic SARM aimed at muscle-wasting/cachexia and later repositioned for AR+/ER+ metastatic breast cancer. It entered a first-in-human Phase 1 oncology trial (RAD1901-prefixed program; results published 2022, PMID 34565686), but RAD-140 is NOT FDA-approved for any indication and has no marketing authorization anywhere. Regulatory status: the U.S. FDA has issued public warnings that SARMs including RAD-140 are unapproved drugs and that body-building products containing them are illegally marketed and associated with serious harms (including liver injury); they are not legal dietary-supplement ingredients. In the U.S., the SARMs Control Act (introduced repeatedly in Congress) aims to schedule SARMs as controlled substances; as of this writing RAD-140 is not federally scheduled but is sold illicitly as a 'research chemical.' The World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) prohibits SARMs at all times (in- and out-of-competition) under class S1.2 (Other Anabolic Agents); RAD-140 is a documented cause of adverse analytical findings in athletes and racehorses, and substantial literature exists purely on its detection. Gray-market products are frequently mislabeled or adulterated with other SARMs. [Established for FDA/WADA status]

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Human anabolic dose-response in healthy adults is unknown — there are NO controlled efficacy trials in non-clinical users, so the actual muscle/strength gain magnitude and the dose at which it occurs are unquantified (all human dosing data come from metastatic breast-cancer patients).
  • Long-term safety is unknown — no data beyond short courses; cumulative hepatic, cardiovascular, prostate, and fertility consequences of months-to-years of use are uncharacterized.
  • The mechanism and predictors of the cardiac events (acute myocarditis/myopericarditis/heart failure) are unresolved — whether these are dose-dependent, idiosyncratic/immune-mediated, or confounded by contaminated/co-formulated products is unclear.
  • Definitive human enzymology of metabolism (which specific CYP isoforms dominate, and clinically meaningful drug-drug interactions) has not been established — current isoform attribution is largely from in vitro systems.
  • Full reversibility of HPG-axis suppression after extended real-world use (vs the short reversible cases reported) is not well defined, and the long ~45 h half-life prolongs recovery in ways not formally studied.
  • Whether RAD-140's 'tissue selectivity' meaningfully reduces real-world androgenic/prostate risk in humans is uncertain — PSA still rose in trial patients, so the selectivity may be narrower in vivo than preclinical framing suggests.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Harm-reduction monitoring (NOT an endorsement to use; no dosing guidance). Hepatic: baseline and periodic liver panel — AST, ALT, total/direct bilirubin, ALP, GGT — and watch for clinical DILI red flags (jaundice/scleral icterus, dark urine, pale stools, pruritus, right-upper-quadrant pain, unexplained fatigue/nausea); these warrant immediate cessation and medical review, since cholestatic injury is the dominant documented harm and can be severe. Endocrine: total/free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG, estradiol, and clinical signs of hypogonadism (low libido, erectile dysfunction, testicular shrinkage, mood change) and gynecomastia/edema; SHBG suppression is an early marker of AR engagement. Cardiovascular: blood pressure, a fasting lipid panel (especially HDL, which androgenic agents lower), and any chest pain, dyspnea, palpitations, syncope, or signs of heart failure — given case reports of myocarditis/myopericarditis/heart failure, new cardiac symptoms are an emergency. Prostate/reproductive: PSA in older men (RAD-140 raised PSA in trials). General: CBC and renal function (creatinine/eGFR), and hematocrit. Product-quality caveat: gray-market RAD-140 is frequently mislabeled or combined with other SARMs, so lab abnormalities cannot be assumed to reflect RAD-140 alone. The long half-life means abnormalities and suppression can persist for days-to-weeks after stopping, so re-check labs after a washout rather than assuming rapid normalization. Any concerning finding should prompt discontinuation and evaluation by a clinician.

Caveats

Tissue selectivity is relative and dose-dependent, not absolute — at higher exposure the prostate-sparing margin shrinks and androgenic/suppressive effects appear. The strongest muscle/bone and prostate-sparing data are from rodents and monkeys, not humans; human anabolic efficacy is essentially unproven in controlled trials. The non-genomic ERK/MAPK survival pathway and neuroprotection are preclinical. The breast-cancer mechanism (AR activation repressing the ER/ESR1 program) is a separate, cancer-specific action, not the muscle-building mechanism. Liver injury, myocarditis/myopericarditis, and HPG suppression in humans come from case reports (lower certainty than trials, but a consistent and concerning signal). Not FDA/EMA approved; WADA-banned. This is mechanism education, not endorsement, and contains no dosing guidance.
