Library / Anabolic-androgenic steroids

Nandrolone

The original "anabolic-leaning" steroid: a testosterone molecule with one carbon shaved off, which makes it gentler on prostate and hair but sneaks in a progesterone-like side effect that an estrogen blocker can't fix.

Human / approved 36 citations
Class
Anabolic-androgenic steroid (AAS); a 19-nortestosterone (19-nor / estrane) derivative
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR; gene NR3C4) — full agonist
Human status
Genuine, long-established human drug. Nandrolone decanoate is an approved (clinical phase 4) prescription medicine used historically and in some countries for postmenopausal osteoporosis, anemia of chronic kidney disease, and HIV/cancer-related muscle wasting; recent RCT meta-analyses confirm gains in muscle strength, lean mass and bone density (PMID 41936385, 41477377, 8846659). It is also one of the most widely abused AAS in bodybuilding/PED use ('Deca'), where supraphysiologic doses carry cardiovascular, neuroendocrine, hepatic and other harms (PMID 33187340, 26074747, 29721213). It is a WADA-banned substance and a controlled substance in many jurisdictions.

At a glance

  • Builds skeletal muscle mass and strength and increases nitrogen retention / protein synthesis via androgen-receptor activation (meta-analysis of RCTs: PMID 41936385)
  • Increases bone mineral density — historically used for postmenopausal osteoporosis (PMID 8846659; meta-analysis PMID 41477377)
  • Relatively muscle-favoring vs testosterone: milder on prostate/scalp because 5a-reduction yields a WEAKER metabolite (PMID 4021486, 19861292)
  • Lower estrogen conversion than testosterone (weak aromatase substrate), so less estrogen-driven water retention/gyno (PMID 3865479)
  • Progestogenic + prolactin-raising side effects (gynecomastia, low libido, erectile dysfunction) NOT fixed by an aromatase inhibitor (MoA cards; PMID 19941958)
  • Suppresses the HPG axis → shuts down natural testosterone and sperm production; recovery is slow due to long-acting ester (PMID 39370302)
  • Reported analgesic/joint-comfort and collagen effects (low-certainty, small pilot data: PMID 32257859)
  • Documented harms with abuse: cardiovascular/arrhythmia risk, neurotoxicity, hepatic and possible carcinogenic concerns (PMID 26074747, 29721213)

Mechanism, step by step

  1. The ester is a timer, not the drug Established

    Injected nandrolone decanoate is a 'prodrug': the decanoate fatty-acid tail makes it oil-soluble so it leaches out of the muscle depot over weeks, then blood enzymes (esterases) snip the tail off to release the active molecule, free nandrolone. This is why Deca acts slowly and only needs infrequent injection. PMID 33187340 (Patanè 2020 review, abstract); class pharmacology PMID 16112947

  2. Free nandrolone is just testosterone minus one carbon Established

    Nandrolone is 19-nortestosterone: it's testosterone with the carbon at position 19 removed. That tiny change is the whole story behind its different behavior versus testosterone. PMID 19861292 (abstract); ChEMBL CHEMBL1200946

  3. It binds and switches on the androgen receptor Established

    Like testosterone, nandrolone slips into cells and binds the androgen receptor, which then travels into the nucleus and turns on muscle-building genes (more protein synthesis, more nitrogen retention). Its binding affinity for the receptor is actually equal to or higher than testosterone's, so it is a potent AR agonist. PMID 4021486 (Bergink 1985, abstract — verified); PMID 18325697; ChEMBL CHEMBL1200946

  4. The 5-alpha-reductase trick: it gets WEAKER in 'androgenic' tissue Established

    Here's the key differentiator. In tissues like prostate, skin and scalp (which are rich in the enzyme 5-alpha-reductase), testosterone gets converted to DHT, a MORE powerful androgen — but nandrolone gets converted to dihydronandrolone, a WEAKER one. So in muscle (which has little of that enzyme) nandrolone stays fully active, while in androgenic tissue it loses potency. That mismatch is why nandrolone is relatively muscle-favoring and milder on prostate and hair than testosterone. PMID 4021486 (verified: 5a-reduction increases T's AR affinity but decreases nandrolone's); PMID 19861292 (Tóth, abstract); metabolism PMID 3865479

  5. It aromatizes only a little — so less estrogen than testosterone Evidence-human / Evidence-rodent

    Nandrolone is a poor substrate for aromatase, the enzyme that turns androgens into estrogen, so it produces far less estradiol than an equal dose of testosterone. That means less classic estrogenic water retention/gyno from estrogen itself — but it is NOT zero, and nandrolone can still nudge up local aromatase activity. PMID 3865479 (metabolism); upregulation of aromatase shown in vitro PMID 22906881

  6. The progesterone-receptor catch (the 'Deca' problem) Established (progestogenic binding) / Evidence-human (prolactin-driven symptoms)

    Because it's a 19-nor compound, nandrolone also activates the progesterone receptor — it has intrinsic progestogenic activity. Progestogenic signaling (together with raised prolactin) can drive gynecomastia, low libido and erectile problems. Crucially this route is INDEPENDENT of estrogen, which is why an aromatase inhibitor alone often fails to stop 'Deca gyno' or 'Deca dick' — the progestin/prolactin pathway has to be addressed separately. MoA cards: Progesterone receptor [PGR], Prolactin & dopamine [PRLR/DRD2]; PMID 19941958 (19-NT acts via PR, verified); PMID 25863598

  7. It still shuts down your own testosterone Established (human class effect) / Evidence-rodent (dose-time detail)

    Like all AAS, nandrolone signals the brain (hypothalamus/pituitary) that there's plenty of androgen around, so the body stops releasing LH and FSH. That suppresses natural testosterone production and sperm output, causing hypogonadism that can be slow to recover given the long-acting ester. PMID 33187340 (review, abstract); PMID 39370302 (rat, dose/time-dependent HPG suppression)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Nandrolone (19-nortestosterone) is a potent AGONIST of the androgen receptor (AR; ChEMBL: Nandrolone decanoate = AR agonist, max clinical phase 4 [ChEMBL CHEMBL1200946]). Relative binding affinity (RBA) to AR is roughly comparable to or slightly higher than testosterone in cytosol assays (commonly cited ~150-190% of testosterone RBA depending on assay), but its defining pharmacology is what happens to that affinity in tissue. KEY MECHANISM (Established / Evidence-rodent+human cell): unlike testosterone, 5alpha-reduction DECREASES nandrolone's AR affinity. Bergink et al. confirmed in rat prostate AR and intact human MCF-7 cells that 5alpha-reduction increases the affinity of testosterone but decreases the affinity of nandrolone; the reduced metabolite dihydronandrolone (DHN) binds AR more weakly than nandrolone, whereas DHT binds AR more strongly than testosterone (PMID 4021486). This is the molecular basis for nandrolone's high anabolic-to-androgenic ratio in tissue terms. SECONDARY TARGET — progesterone receptor (Established): the 19-nor (removal of the C19 methyl) structure of the nortestosterone series confers measurable progestational/PR binding; 19-nortestosterone derivatives bind PR (the series is the chemical parent of 19-nor progestins; reference ligands built on 19-nortestosterone bind PR with high affinity, e.g. iodovinyl-19-nortestosterone PR ligands, PMID 4065046; allylestrenol/norethisterone PR-binding context, PMID 3928974). Nandrolone's own PR binding is weaker than dedicated progestins but non-trivial (commonly cited ~20% relative PR affinity range) and is clinically relevant for progestogenic side effects. Glucocorticoid/estrogen receptor binding is low; estrogenic effects are indirect via aromatization to estradiol (low rate). Functional confirmation: nandrolone effects are blocked by the AR antagonist hydroxyflutamide in human neuroblastoma cells (PMID 18596614), confirming AR-mediated signalling.

Signalling cascade. Classical genomic AR pathway (Established). 1) Lipophilic nandrolone (after ester cleavage) diffuses across the plasma membrane. 2) It binds cytoplasmic AR, displacing heat-shock chaperones (HSP90/HSP70/HSP56) and triggering a conformational change. 3) Critically, the ligand-bound AR undergoes the N-terminal/C-terminal (N/C) interaction that distinguishes high-affinity agonists; high-affinity AR agonists induce this N/C interaction >40-fold, lower-affinity anabolics need higher concentrations (PMID 10077001). 4) Receptor dimerizes and translocates to the nucleus. 5) The AR dimer binds androgen response elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer regions of target genes and recruits coactivators (SRC family, CBP/p300), driving transcription of genes for muscle protein synthesis, IGF-1, and (in nandrolone's case) modulation of myostatin and AR mRNA itself — the 19-nor prohormone NOR upregulates AR and myostatin mRNA in gastrocnemius like testosterone (PMID 18325697). 6) Net anabolic output: increased muscle protein synthesis, satellite cell activation, nitrogen retention, erythropoietin/red cell stimulation. The tissue-selective 'anabolic' phenotype arises because muscle is low in 5alpha-reductase (so nandrolone retains affinity there) while prostate/skin/scalp are high in 5alpha-reductase (so nandrolone is locally weakened to DHN), per Bergink (PMID 4021486). Progestogenic signalling via PR and non-genomic/CNS effects (altered dopaminergic, glutamatergic/NMDA, GABA, and opioid signalling) layer on top of the classical AR cascade (PMIDs 18809391, 24937439, 10715562).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Deep intramuscular injection of the decanoate ester (oil depot); nandrolone phenylpropionate is a shorter-ester variant. The free (unesterified) hormone is not used clinically by injection because it would clear too fast; oral nandrolone is impractical due to first-pass metabolism (it is not 17-alpha-alkylated). The decanoate ester acts as a prodrug depot.
Absorption / bioavailability
After IM injection the decanoate is slowly released from the oil depot and hydrolyzed by serum/tissue esterases to free nandrolone. Depot-release is rate-limiting. In healthy volunteers given IM nandrolone decanoate, peak nandrolone serum levels are reached within ~24-48 h and the drug shows linear kinetics over the studied dose range (Wijnand, PMID 3865478). IM bioavailability of released hormone is effectively complete; the constraint is release rate, not absorption.
Half-life
Two distinct half-lives. (1) Depot-release half-life (the practically dominant one): mean ~6 days for release of the ester from the IM depot into circulation (Wijnand, PMID 3865478); an independent study reported ~8 days for nandrolone decanoate (Belkien, PMID 4010287). (2) Terminal half-life of free nandrolone once liberated is short: ~4.3 h for the combined hydrolysis/distribution/elimination of nandrolone, with the hydrolysis of the ester in serum on the order of ~1 h or less (Wijnand, PMID 3865478). Net effect: serum nandrolone is detectable and accumulates over weeks of repeat dosing; measurable nandrolone persisted up to ~19 weeks after the last injection in a repeated-dosing trial of the phenylpropionate-related ester (Belkien, PMID 4010287).
Metabolism
Free nandrolone is metabolized chiefly by 5alpha-reductase to 5alpha-dihydronandrolone (DHN) — but unlike testosterone->DHT, this REDUCES AR affinity (PMID 4021486). Further reduction yields the urinary markers 19-norandrosterone and 19-noretiocholanolone (the classic anti-doping metabolites). Nandrolone is a substrate for aromatase (CYP19A1) but aromatizes at a substantially lower rate than testosterone, producing small amounts of estradiol-type estrogens. Hepatic phase I/II conjugation (glucuronidation/sulfation) precedes excretion. Mean serum clearance of free nandrolone ~1.55 L/h/kg (Wijnand, PMID 3865478).
Excretion
Primarily renal as conjugated metabolites; 19-norandrosterone (and 19-noretiocholanolone) glucuronides are the urinary markers used in anti-doping testing and can remain detectable for many months after a single therapeutic dose due to the slow depot release.
Notes
The decanoate is a lipophilic prodrug — the long carbon ester chain partitions into fat and slows depot release (an extra ester carbon adds roughly a day of half-life). Because 5alpha-reduction weakens rather than strengthens nandrolone, 5alpha-reductase inhibitors (finasteride/dutasteride) do NOT reduce — and may slightly potentiate — nandrolone's androgenic action in androgen-sensitive tissues, the opposite of their effect on testosterone. Nandrolone crosses into the CNS and alters central neurotransmitter systems (dopaminergic, glutamatergic, opioid).

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

HUMAN (Evidence-human, therapeutic/supraphysiologic doses): In HIV-associated wasting, nandrolone decanoate 150 mg IM every 2 weeks for 12 weeks produced a lean body mass (LBM) gain of +1.6 kg vs +0.4 kg placebo (P<0.05), statistically indistinguishable from FDA-approved rhGH (+2.5 kg) (Storer, PMID 15914526). Higher pharmacologic dosing (~weekly injections) in HIV men plus resistance training produced LBM gains of ~3.9-5.2 kg over 12 weeks, with strength gains of 10-53% (Sattler, PMID 10199766). In hemodialysis patients, weekly nandrolone decanoate (200 mg men / 100 mg women) increased LBM by +3.1 kg over 12 weeks (P<0.0001), additive with resistance exercise for muscle cross-sectional area (Johansen, PMID 16825332). A single 150 mg dose in healthy men measurably suppressed LH/FSH within 4 days and lowered total and bioavailable testosterone (Gårevik, PMID 26370185). CRITICAL GAP: there is NO controlled human dose-response data at the multi-hundred-mg-per-week supraphysiologic doses used in bodybuilding/PED contexts — the efficacy and harm data at those doses are extrapolated from animal models and uncontrolled observation. RODENT (Evidence-rodent): supraphysiologic doses (e.g. 10-20 mg/kg) reliably produce levator ani/skeletal muscle hypertrophy with comparatively weak prostate/seminal vesicle stimulation, demonstrating the tissue-selective anabolic profile (PMID 18325697, 4021486), but also dose-dependent cardiac, renal, hepatic, and behavioral toxicity (PMIDs 23287648, 25065671). Human dose-response for the supraphysiologic range is essentially unknown.

How it compares

  • vs Testosterone (and its esters)
    same: Both are injectable AR agonists driving the same classical genomic anabolic cascade; both aromatize to estrogens and both suppress the HPG axis.
    different: Testosterone is AMPLIFIED by 5alpha-reductase (->DHT, stronger AR binding) so it is strongly androgenic in skin/prostate/scalp; nandrolone is WEAKENED by 5alpha-reductase (->DHN) giving a higher tissue anabolic:androgenic ratio (PMID 4021486). Nandrolone aromatizes far less than testosterone but carries notable progesterone-receptor activity (gynecomastia/'deca dick' via PR), which testosterone lacks. 5alpha-reductase inhibitors reduce testosterone's androgenic side effects but do NOT reduce nandrolone's.
  • vs Trenbolone (another 19-nortestosterone derivative)
    same: Both are 19-nor compounds, non-aromatizable-to-classic-estradiol in trenbolone's case, both potent AR agonists with high anabolic ratios and both progestogenic.
    different: Trenbolone (a triene) is a much more potent AR agonist, does not aromatize at all and is not a 5alpha-reductase substrate, and has markedly stronger androgenic/neuro/cardiovascular toxicity; nandrolone is milder, aromatizes slightly, and is 5alpha-reduced to a weaker metabolite. (Mechanistic class relationship; no head-to-head human trials.)
  • vs Mibolerone (7alpha,17alpha-dimethyl-19-nortestosterone)
    same: Same 19-nortestosterone chemical family; both bind AR and PR.
    different: Mibolerone is a high-affinity, non-reducible, orally active reference androgen used in receptor assays; it binds both AR and PR with high affinity (PMID 2419120) and is far more potent/toxic per dose than nandrolone, which is depot-injected and 5alpha-reducible.
  • vs 19-Norandrostenedione / 19-nor prohormones (e.g. NOR)
    same: Metabolize to nandrolone (nortestosterone) and share its AR selectivity and 19-norandrosterone urinary metabolite (doping marker).
    different: Given subcutaneously, the prohormone NOR shows even more SARM-like tissue selectivity (stimulating levator ani muscle while sparing prostate/seminal vesicle and liver) than its metabolite nandrolone itself (PMID 18325697); oral prohormones are inefficient due to first-pass metabolism.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Anadrol compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Two overlapping harms compound here. First erythrocytosis: both AR agonists drive red-cell mass (EPO up, hepcidin down), and oxymetholone is one of the strongest hematocrit-raising steroids — it is FDA-approved for anemia — so the combination sharply compounds polycythemia (viscosity/thrombosis risk). Second, they DIVERGE on hepatotoxicity: oxymetholone is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral that stresses the liver (cholestasis, enzyme spikes) and crashes HDL, whereas nandrolone is NOT 17aa; so on liver/lipids Anadrol dominates and nandrolone adds little there, but the shared erythrocytosis is genuinely additive.   • Boldenone undecylenate compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both are injectable, esterified, non-17-alpha-alkylated AR agonists with strong erythropoietic and HPG-suppressive effects and a 'lean/slow' profile. Stacked, they compound erythrocytosis (additive EPO/hepcidin effect) and deepen HPG/testosterone suppression (both long esters mean slow recovery). They differ downstream — nandrolone aromatizes weakly to estradiol and is progestogenic (raises prolactin), while boldenone is a poor aromatizer and not progestogenic — so nandrolone adds a prolactin/progestin dimension EQ lacks. Erectile/libido issues can arise from the nandrolone prolactin/progestin axis.
  • GHRP-2 compounds risk ⚠ Established (both raise prolactin; 19-nor progestogenic-prolactin effect documented)
    Both compounds independently raise prolactin by different routes: GHRP-2 through non-selective lactotroph stimulation at the pituitary, and nandrolone (a 19-nor) through its progestogenic activity, which drives prolactin-mediated effects. Run together the prolactin burden adds up, worsening the low-libido / erectile-dysfunction / prolactin-type gynecomastia picture. Key mechanistic caveat: an aromatase inhibitor will NOT fix this because it is a prolactin/progesterone problem, not an estrogen one. GHRP-2 monograph step 6 (prolactin rise); Nandrolone monograph key_effects (progestogenic + prolactin-raising, not AI-fixable, PMID 19941958) Watch: Prolactin; libido and erectile function; breast-tissue changes. A dopamine agonist addresses the prolactin arm but not nandrolone's progesterone-receptor arm.
  • Masteron compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both were the direct comparators in the 1976 breast-cancer RCT (PMID 1036981) - both injectable, non-17a-alkylated anabolic steroids. Mechanistically here: nandrolone is progestogenic (raises prolactin) and weakly aromatizes; Masteron is non-aromatizing and mildly opposes estrogen only via AR-vs-ER crosstalk. That crosstalk does NOT touch nandrolone's progesterone/prolactin pathway, so Masteron cannot mitigate nandrolone-driven prolactin gyno or sexual dysfunction. Their androgenic and HDL-lowering effects compound. Masteron monograph comparisons (nandrolone) and step 5; Nandrolone monograph (progestogenic/prolactin, weak aromatization); PMID 1036981 (drostanolone vs nandrolone RCT) Watch: Prolactin (Masteron will NOT counteract nandrolone's progestogenic prolactin rise - that needs a dopamine agonist such as cabergoline); estradiol (nandrolone weakly aromatizes); HDL/LDL lipids; libido/erectile function.
  • Testosterone compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both are injectable AR agonists that AR-dependently raise erythropoietin and suppress hepcidin, expanding red-cell mass — so co-exposure adds two erythropoietic drivers and compounds erythrocytosis (rising hematocrit -> viscosity, hypertension and thrombotic risk). Both also suppress the HPG axis (compounded LH/FSH shutdown, with slower recovery given nandrolone's long depot). A commonly-cited complementary reason to pair them is that testosterone supplies the strong 5-AR-derived DHT androgen tone in sexual tissue that nandrolone lacks, partially offsetting 'deca dick' — but that is androgen-tone management, not a fix for the prolactin route, and testosterone additionally aromatizes strongly, adding an estrogenic gyno driver on top of nandrolone's progestin route.   • Trenbolone compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both are 19-nortestosterone derivatives with intrinsic progesterone-receptor agonism. Run together they compound the estrogen-independent PROGESTIN/PGR side-effect axis — additive risk of gynecomastia, low libido and erectile dysfunction ('deca/tren dick') that an aromatase inhibitor cannot fix, because the driver is progestogenic rather than estrogenic. Trenbolone is the more potent AR agonist and non-aromatizing. Note honesty flag: the shared and well-established liability is the PGR/progestogenic route; the additional prolactin-elevation attributed to trenbolone specifically is extrapolated from the nandrolone literature and is contested for trenbolone itself. Because both suppress the axis hard, also follow LH/FSH and testosterone recovery. A dopamine agonist addresses the shared prolactin driver but not the PGR arm.
  • Trestolone compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Trestolone (MENT) is also a 19-nortestosterone and binds the progesterone receptor with affinity comparable to progesterone, so it shares nandrolone's progestogenic (PGR) gyno/sexual-dysfunction liability. Uniquely, trestolone aromatizes to a POTENT estrogen (7-alpha-methyl-estradiol, a full ER agonist), adding a strong estrogenic gyno driver on top. So this pairing can stack BOTH gyno mechanisms at once: the shared progestin/PGR route plus trestolone's heavy aromatization — meaning both estradiol and (for the nandrolone progestin/prolactin arm) prolactin may need attention simultaneously. Both are also potent HPG suppressors. Nandrolone monograph steps 5-6 (progestogenic 19-nor route); Trestolone monograph step 4 and pharmacology (aromatizes to potent 7-alpha-methyl-estradiol; PR binding comparable to progesterone) and comparisons vs Nandrolone; corpus MoA cards Progesterone receptor [PGR] and ISSUE: Gynecomastia Watch: Estradiol AND prolactin both (two independent gyno drivers active); note standard E2 immunoassays may under-read trestolone's 7-alpha-methyl-estradiol metabolite, so judge estrogen status partly by symptoms (breast tenderness, gyno, water retention). Track libido, erectile function, and LH/FSH plus testosterone given strong combined HPG suppression from two potent 19-nors.
  • Anastrozole counteracts ↓ Established
    The key teaching point: this is a targeted-but-INCOMPLETE interaction. Nandrolone aromatizes only weakly to estradiol, so an aromatase inhibitor (CYP19A1 blocker) does address that small estrogenic component — but it does NOT touch nandrolone's dominant estrogen-INDEPENDENT gyno driver, the progesterone-receptor/prolactin pathway. Running an AI against 'Deca gyno' therefore targets the wrong mechanism and can over-suppress the already-low estradiol, worsening joint, libido and lipid problems while the progestin/prolactin gyno proceeds unchecked. Nandrolone monograph steps 5-6 and diagram node ai_caveat; Anastrozole monograph comparisons ('vs cabergoline': AIs fix estrogen-driven gyno only; 19-nor gyno is prolactin + PGR-driven so an AI alone often fails); corpus MoA cards ISSUE: Gynecomastia and Progesterone receptor [PGR] Watch: Check estradiol AND prolactin together before blaming estrogen; watch for signs of crashed E2 (joint dryness/stiffness, low libido, low mood, worsening HDL) if an AI is used against 19-nor symptoms. Persisting gyno despite a controlled E2 points to the prolactin/progestin route instead.
  • Cabergoline counteracts ↓ Established
    Nandrolone (a 19-nortestosterone) raises prolactin, partly through its intrinsic progestogenic activity, and high prolactin drives low libido, erectile dysfunction and prolactin-type gynecomastia ('deca dick'). Cabergoline works at the opposite end of the same axis: it is a D2 agonist on pituitary lactotrophs that suppresses prolactin synthesis and secretion. So cabergoline directly counteracts the prolactin ARM of nandrolone's sexual/breast side effects. It does NOT touch the separate progesterone-receptor arm of nandrolone, which needs a SERM, not a dopamine agonist. cabergoline.json mechanism_steps#7 (D2/prolactin, PMID 3745407); nandrolone monograph step 6 (PGR + prolactin, PMID 19941958); corpus MoA cards 'Prolactin & dopamine', 'Progesterone receptor' (ESTABLISHED for nandrolone) Watch: Serum prolactin (before and during, to confirm the problem is actually prolactin-driven and to avoid overshooting into abnormally LOW prolactin). If libido/ED fail to improve despite a normalized prolactin, the residual driver is likely nandrolone's progesterone-receptor activity, which cabergoline will not fix. Watch for cabergoline's own dopaminergic harms: orthostatic dizziness and impulse-control changes.
  • Exemestane counteracts ↓ Established
    Nandrolone aromatizes only weakly, so exemestane genuinely counteracts the small estrogenic component by inactivating aromatase. But nandrolone's characteristic gynecomastia and 'deca dick' arise mainly from progestogenic PGR activity and raised prolactin, NOT estradiol — a route exemestane does not touch — so E2 control alone commonly fails to prevent nandrolone gyno, and over-using an AI can crash the modest E2 present. Nandrolone monograph mechanism_steps 5 (weak aromatization) and 6 (progestogenic/prolactin gyno not fixed by AI, PMID 19941958); Prolactin/dopamine and Progesterone-receptor MoA cards; Aromatase MoA card Watch: Prolactin and estradiol together — persistent breast/sexual symptoms with controlled E2 point to the prolactin/progestin route (needs a dopamine agonist/SERM, not more AI). Avoid crashing E2 (joint pain, low libido, bone loss).
  • Human chorionic gonadotropin counteracts ↓ Established
    Nandrolone drives negative feedback on the hypothalamus/pituitary, shutting down LH/FSH and therefore intratesticular testosterone and spermatogenesis; recovery is slow because of the long decanoate depot. hCG bypasses the suppressed pituitary by acting directly as an LH analog on testicular LHCGR, restoring intratesticular testosterone and testicular size. It thus counteracts the downstream gonadal-shutdown/atrophy limb of nandrolone's HPG suppression — though it does not restore the pituitary signal itself, nor address the progestin/prolactin symptoms, and it only works if Leydig cells are still responsive. Nandrolone monograph step 7 and side_effect_mechanisms (HPG suppression, PPAAH); hCG monograph steps 1-5 (LHCGR agonist restoring intratesticular testosterone) and comparisons vs exogenous testosterone (preserves testicular size/ITT) Watch: LH/FSH (stay low while suppressed), total/free testosterone, testicular size and, if fertility matters, semen parameters; estradiol (hCG raises intratesticular aromatization and the estradiol-to-testosterone ratio, adding estrogen); hematocrit (androgen-driven, via the testosterone hCG produces).
  • Letrozole counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    This is a partial-mismatch interaction worth teaching. Nandrolone aromatizes only weakly, so letrozole CAN blunt the small aromatized-E2 fraction — but nandrolone's dominant estrogen-like problem (gynecomastia, 'deca dick') is driven largely by PROGESTOGENIC activity at the progesterone receptor and by raised PROLACTIN, NOT by aromatase. Letrozole only blocks the aromatase pathway, so it does NOT counter the progestin/prolactin-mediated effects. Layering a potent AI onto a compound that already contributes little estrogen risks crashing E2 while the real culprit (prolactin/PGR) is untouched — an AI 'fixes' the wrong lever. nandrolone-nandrolone-decanoate-deca-dur.json step 5-6 (weak aromatization; progestogenic/PGR + prolactin drive gyno, AI does NOT fix); letrozole.json step 1 Watch: Prolactin (the more relevant lever for nandrolone gyno/sexual dysfunction, addressed by a dopamine agonist, not an AI) alongside sensitive serum estradiol; watch for crashed-E2 signs (joints, libido, mood) if letrozole is used expecting it to solve nandrolone-type gyno.
  • Dutasteride opposes Evidence-rodent
    The counter-intuitive, textbook-flagged case. Nandrolone is a 5α-reductase substrate, but reduction yields 5α-dihydronandrolone (DHN), which binds the androgen receptor MORE WEAKLY than nandrolone itself — the opposite of testosterone→DHT. So in 5-AR-rich tissues like the hair follicle, 5α-reduction normally SOFTENS nandrolone's androgenic action. Dutasteride blocks that softening step, leaving intact, full-strength parent nandrolone acting on follicular androgen receptors. The 'a 5-ARI protects hair' logic is therefore reversed here: adding dutasteride can worsen rather than protect against androgenic hair loss and skin effects. Nandrolone's separate progestogenic/prolactin-driven effects are unrelated to and unaffected by this interaction. PMID 4021486; PMID 19861292 Watch: Scalp hair (pattern/rate of loss) — the usual '5-ARI protects hair' expectation does not apply; skin/sebaceous androgenic effects.
  • Enclomiphene opposes Evidence-human
    Enclomiphene is a SERM that blocks estrogen receptors at the hypothalamus, removing estrogen negative feedback and raising GnRH -> LH/FSH -> endogenous testosterone. This opposes nandrolone's central HPG suppression and is used mechanistically to help restart the axis after the long-acting depot clears. Two important limits: (1) enclomiphene only blocks the ESTROGENIC feedback arm, whereas nandrolone suppresses via androgenic/progestogenic feedback too, so the block is incomplete; and (2) a brain-level SERM cannot override suppression while circulating exogenous androgen is still present, so it cannot meaningfully restart the axis until nandrolone has fallen — and it requires an intact, responsive pituitary and testes. Nandrolone monograph step 7 and key_unknowns (slow depot; PPAAH); Enclomiphene monograph steps 1-5 (hypothalamic ER antagonist raising LH/FSH/endogenous T) and caveats (cannot override active exogenous-androgen suppression; only works with responsive pituitary/testes) Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone and estradiol to confirm axis reactivation (LH/FSH should RISE; estradiol typically rises, opposite of an AI); timing matters given nandrolone's slow depot clearance (19-norandrosterone can remain detectable for months). Watch for SERM-class signs: visual disturbance (a red flag), mood change, breast tenderness, and clot warning signs.
  • Finasteride opposes Contested
    Inverse case that reverses finasteride's usual role. Unlike testosterone, nandrolone's 5α-reduced metabolite (dihydronandrolone) is WEAKER at the androgen receptor than the parent, so 5α-reductase normally DETOXIFIES nandrolone in high-5-AR tissues like scalp follicles. Finasteride blocks that conversion, removing a protective step rather than a harmful one, leaving more intact parent nandrolone to act at the follicle. This is the mechanistic basis for the claim that a 5-AR inhibitor does not protect (and may theoretically worsen) nandrolone-associated hair loss, the OPPOSITE of its role with testosterone. Finasteride also does nothing for nandrolone's separate progestogenic/prolactin activity. nandrolone monograph mechanism_step 4 + pharmacokinetics notes (5-ARIs do NOT reduce and may slightly potentiate nandrolone's androgenic action; PMID 4021486, 19861292 Established for the weaker-metabolite basis); the specific 'worsens hair loss' clinical claim is anecdote-level (MESO-Rx thread 134315052) with no human RCT Watch: Do not assume finasteride protects the hairline on a nandrolone-containing stack the way it does with testosterone, the metabolite logic is reversed. Watch scalp/hairline changes; note finasteride does nothing for nandrolone's progestogenic/prolactin effects (e.g. nipple changes, low libido/erectile dysfunction), which involve a different pathway.

History &amp; legal status

"Nandrolone (19-nortestosterone) was first synthesized in the 1950s; the decanoate ester (Deca-Durabolin, Organon) and phenylpropionate (Durabolin) became among the most widely used clinical anabolic steroids. Approved medical uses historically and currently (varies by country) include anemia of chronic kidney disease, osteoporosis, and management of wasting/cachexia; it remains a clinical drug (max clinical phase 4 per ChEMBL). In the US it is a Schedule III controlled substance under the Anabolic Steroid Control Act (1990, expanded 2004); in the UK it is a Class C controlled drug; comparable prescription-only/controlled status applies across the EU and most jurisdictions. It is on the WADA Prohibited List at all times (in and out of competition); its urinary metabolite 19-norandrosterone above a threshold is the standard doping-test marker, and the very long depot detection window (months) has produced both genuine positives and disputed cases. US-marketed brand Deca-Durabolin was discontinued domestically though the drug remains available in many countries."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No controlled human dose-response data exist at the supraphysiologic, multi-hundred-mg/week doses used in bodybuilding/PED contexts — efficacy and harm at those doses are extrapolated from therapeutic-dose RCTs and animal models.
  • The true incidence, predictors and reversibility of Prolonged Post-Androgen Abuse Hypogonadism (PPAAH) after nandrolone specifically — given its progestogenic load and slow depot — are not established (PMID 40678315).
  • The relative contribution of progesterone-receptor agonism versus aromatized-estrogen to nandrolone's gynecomastia and sexual dysfunction ('deca dick') in humans is unresolved (PMID 4065046; PMID 3928974).
  • Whether the rodent cardiac fibrosis/hypertension and renal oxidative-injury mechanisms (PMIDs 23287648, 25065671) translate quantitatively to long-term human users at typical doses is unknown — human cardiovascular evidence is observational.
  • Long-term neuropsychiatric and dependence consequences in humans (aggression, depression, addiction) are inferred largely from rodent neurochemistry (PMIDs 24937439, 18809391, 36637118); human causal/longitudinal data are sparse.
  • Precise, modern, standardized AR and PR binding affinities (Ki/Kd) for nandrolone and its metabolite dihydronandrolone under uniform conditions are not consistently reported, so widely-cited RBA figures vary between assays.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (NO dosing guidance). Bloodwork: a full lipid panel (expect HDL suppression, raised LDL/ApoB) given documented atherogenic shift (PMID 26370185); LH, FSH, total and free testosterone, and SHBG to track HPG-axis suppression and recovery (PMID 26370185; PMID 40678315); estradiol AND prolactin (progestogenic 19-nor profile makes prolactin/PR-mediated effects relevant); hematocrit/hemoglobin (polycythemia risk from erythropoietic stimulation); liver enzymes (ALT/AST — lower direct hepatotoxicity than oral 17-aa steroids but still worth tracking); fasting glucose/insulin sensitivity; and renal function/markers given rodent oxidative renal injury (PMID 25065671). Cardiovascular: blood pressure, and if chronic high-dose exposure, clinical assessment for left-ventricular hypertrophy/cardiomyopathy given rodent remodeling/hypertension signal (PMID 23287648). Clinical signs: breast tenderness/gynecomastia, libido loss and erectile dysfunction ('deca dick'), mood changes/irritability/aggression or low mood, testicular atrophy and infertility, acne/oily skin and (because nandrolone is 5alpha-weakened) comparatively less but still possible hair loss. Remember anti-doping detectability of 19-norandrosterone can persist for months. None of this constitutes medical advice — concerning bloodwork or symptoms warrant evaluation by a clinician."

Caveats

Mechanistic certainty varies by claim. The core points — AR agonism, the 5α-reductase 'weaker metabolite' explanation for muscle-favoring action, and intrinsic progestogenic activity — are Established, but several rest on ABSTRACT-only grounding (PMID 19861292, 4021486, 19941958: full text not retrieved) and classic 1980s rodent/in-vitro receptor work, not modern human trials. The 'anabolic:androgenic ratio' is a real but somewhat dated rodent (levator-ani/ventral-prostate) construct — useful for intuition, not a precise human prediction. The prolactin-driven 'Deca dick'/gyno picture is well-supported clinically and physiologically but the exact contribution of direct PGR agonism vs prolactin elevation is not fully partitioned in humans. Aromatization is LOW but not zero, and one in-vitro study (PMID 22906881) shows nandrolone can UPREGULATE aromatase in breast-cancer cells — so 'non-aromatizing' is an oversimplification. Several harm/neuro/cardiac findings (PMID 26074747, 39370302, 36208700) are rodent or supraphysiologic-dose data and should be labeled Evidence-rodent when taught.
