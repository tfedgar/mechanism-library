A brain-acting "desire switch" — instead of forcing blood into the penis like Viagra, PT-141 flips on the brain circuits that generate sexual wanting itself.

Limited human dataFDA-approved 27 citations
Class
Synthetic cyclic heptapeptide; non-selective melanocortin receptor agonist (an analogue of the natural hormone alpha-MSH)
Primary target
Melanocortin-4 receptor (MC4R), a Gs-coupled GPCR on neurons in the hypothalamus
Human status
Strongly established: PT-141 is a melanocortin (mainly MC4R) agonist and acts centrally — this is confirmed in humans, including a cryo-EM structure of bremelanotide bound to human MC4R (PMID 34433901). Human efficacy: erections in men in early PK/PD trials (PMID 14963471, 14999221, 15833522) and improved desire/arousal in women in Phase 3 RECONNECT trials, leading to FDA approval for HSDD (PMID 31599840, 31599847). IMPORTANT — the deeper circuit story (which exact hypothalamic neurons, the dopamine 'go' vs. inhibitory balance, oxytocin involvement, c-Fos activation, preoptic-area-to-penis wiring) is established largely in RODENTS (rats, hamsters) and not directly proven in the human brain (PMID 12851303, 15226502, 33455598, 39793696). Do not present the dopamine/oxytocin mechanism as a proven human fact — it is the best-supported model, extrapolated from animals.

At a glance

  • Increases sexual desire and arousal by acting centrally (in the brain), not on genital blood flow
  • Induces/facilitates penile erection in men, including without visual sexual stimulation and additively with sildenafil (Viagra)
  • Increases proceptive 'solicitation' (active seeking of sex) in female rodents — a desire effect, not just a reflex
  • FDA-approved (Vyleesi) for acquired, generalized hypoactive sexual desire disorder (HSDD) in premenopausal women
  • On-target side effects from the broader melanocortin system: transient blood pressure rise, nausea, flushing, headache, and skin darkening (hyperpigmentation) with repeated use

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

7 steps1 human 4 established 2 rodent / preclinical

  1. It's a copy of a natural brain hormone Established

    PT-141 is a synthetic analogue closely related to Melanotan II and is a NON-selective melanocortin agonist — so it can still cause skin darkening and facial flushing via MC1R. Its sexual effect comes from activating MC4R/MC3R in the brain, NOT from any loss of pigment activity. PMID 12851303; PMID 17584134

  2. It switches on melanocortin receptors in the brain, mainly MC4R Established

    PT-141 acts as an agonist (a turn-it-on key) at melanocortin receptors, especially MC4R and to a lesser extent MC3R. These receptors sit on neurons in the central nervous system, not in blood vessels — which is why this drug works on the brain rather than on plumbing. PMID 12851303; PMID 16839319; ChEMBL:CHEMBL4297533

  3. Activating MC4R fires up a Gs signal inside the neuron Established

    When PT-141 binds MC4R, the receptor couples to the Gs protein, which raises cyclic AMP (cAMP) inside the cell and makes the neuron more excitable. Cryo-EM structures actually captured bremelanotide bound inside the MC4R pocket driving this activation — so we know exactly how it docks. PMID 34433901

  4. This lights up arousal neurons in the hypothalamus Evidence-rodent

    In animals, systemic PT-141 activates neurons in the hypothalamus (shown by a spike in the activity marker c-Fos), concentrated in regions like the medial preoptic area that are wired — via tracing studies — directly to the erectile machinery of the penis. PMID 12851303; PMID 15226502

  5. It tips the brain's desire balance — boosting dopamine 'go' signals Evidence-rodent

    The leading model is that MC4R activation shifts the brain's pro-sexual vs. inhibitory balance, increasing dopamine signaling in reward/motivation circuits (and engaging oxytocin pathways). In female rats PT-141 selectively increased solicitation (actively seeking sex), not just reflexes — i.e. it raised wanting, not just mechanics. PMID 15226502; PMID 33455598; PMID 39793696

  6. In humans this shows up as more desire and, in men, more erections Evidence-human

    In men, intranasal and subcutaneous PT-141 produced measurable erections (RigiScan) even without visual stimulation, and added to sildenafil's effect. In premenopausal women it raised subjective desire and arousal — the basis for FDA approval in HSDD. The effect is real but modest in size, and it boosts desire rather than guaranteeing performance. PMID 14963471; PMID 15833522; PMID 31599840

  7. It acts on demand and fades — not a hormone you build up Established

    PT-141 is taken before anticipated activity and works over hours, then clears. Because melanocortin receptors also influence blood pressure and appetite, transient blood-pressure rises, nausea, and flushing are expected on-target effects of hitting this system, not random side reactions. PMID 27977473; PMID 35147466

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. PT-141 (bremelanotide) is a synthetic cyclic heptapeptide analogue of α-melanocyte-stimulating hormone (α-MSH) — a 7-residue cyclic core, Ac-Nle-cyclo[Asp-His-D-Phe-Arg-Trp-Lys]-OH (the same Ac-Nle-Asp-His-D-Phe-Arg-Trp-Lys pharmacophore family as melanotan-II/MT-II, but bremelanotide carries a free C-terminal carboxylic acid instead of MT-II's C-terminal amide). MW ~1025 Da (free base), formula C50H68N14O10 (ChEMBL CHEMBL2070241). It is a NON-SELECTIVE AGONIST across the melanocortin receptor family. Per the FDA Vyleesi label, functional/binding potency ranks MC1R > MC4R > MC3R > MC5R > MC2R — i.e. it is MOST potent at MC1R (skin/melanocyte, drives the pigmentation side effects) and MC4R (CNS, drives the therapeutic sexual effect), with MC3R and MC5R lower and essentially no meaningful MC2R (adrenal/ACTH receptor) activity [Established; FDA label 210557]. ChEMBL curation concurs: 'Binds MC1R, MC3R, MC4R and MC5R but most potent against MC1R and MC4R... MC1R & MC4R are most relevant at therapeutic doses' (CHEMBL4297533). All five MCRs are class A (rhodopsin-like) Gs-coupled GPCRs. IMPORTANT NUMBERS GAP: the public regulatory and literature sources establish the rank-order of potency but do NOT report a clean, consistent set of human MC4R/MC1R Ki or EC50 values [the precise nanomolar affinities are not reported — a data gap]. The receptor identity and Gs coupling are confirmed structurally: a 2021 cryo-EM study solved full-length human MC4R-Gs complexes bound to α-MSH, afamelanotide, bremelanotide (Vyleesi) and the small molecule THIQ, directly visualizing how the peptide agonist sits in the orthosteric pocket and engages the receptor [Established; PMID 34433901].

Signalling cascade. Step 1 — Receptor engagement: bremelanotide's D-Phe-Arg-Trp 'message' triad inserts into the orthosteric pocket of MC4R (and MC1R), with the conserved aromatic/basic residues stabilizing the active receptor conformation; cryo-EM shows the peptide drives the class-A activation rearrangement of transmembrane helix 6 [Established; PMID 34433901, 34561620]. Step 2 — G protein activation: active MC4R couples the heterotrimeric Gs protein, exchanging GDP for GTP on Gαs [Established; PMID 34433901, corpus MoA]. Step 3 — Second messenger: Gαs stimulates adenylate cyclase, raising intracellular cAMP, which activates protein kinase A (the canonical melanocortin-Gs-cAMP-PKA cascade; in MC1R-bearing melanocytes the same cAMP rise drives eumelanin synthesis, the basis of skin darkening) [Established; PMID 24807163 for MC1R-cAMP, PMID 34433901 for MC4R-Gs]. Step 4 — Neuronal output: at MC4R on hypothalamic neurons (concentrated in the medial preoptic area, mPOA), activation increases neuronal firing — shown in rodents as increased c-Fos immunoreactivity after systemic PT-141 [Evidence-rodent; PMID 12851303]. Step 5 — Pro-sexual circuit: the leading model is that MC4R activation in the mPOA increases downstream release of dopamine (an excitatory pro-desire neurotransmitter), tilting the central excitation/inhibition balance toward sexual desire and arousal; norepinephrine and serotonin/oxytocin circuits are also implicated [Evidence-rodent / Contested in humans; PMID 33455598]. The pro-erectile pathway is anatomically linked to genital autonomic outflow: pseudorabies virus injected into rat corpus cavernosum is taken up by neurons in the SAME hypothalamic region PT-141 activates, tracing a CNS-to-penis circuit [Evidence-rodent; PMID 12851303]. NOTE a key mechanistic dispute: a 2025 Syrian-hamster study found MC3R/MC4R mRNA in VTA dopamine neurons but bremelanotide did NOT change melanocortin-receptor expression in the mesolimbic system and did NOT enhance sexual reward (conditioned place preference), arguing the drug does NOT act via the classic VTA-nucleus accumbens reward circuit — so the precise pro-desire node remains unsettled [Evidence-rodent / Contested; PMID 39793696].

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Parenteral peptide — NOT orally bioavailable (it is a peptide, degraded in the gut). The approved product (Vyleesi) is a single-use subcutaneous autoinjector delivering 1.75 mg into the abdomen or thigh. Historically (Palatin's 2004 development-era studies) PT-141 was also given intranasally and by SC injection in ED trials [Established; FDA label; PMID 14963471, 14999221].
Absorption / bioavailability
After 1.75 mg SC, absorption is rapid and essentially complete: absolute bioavailability ~100%, median Tmax ~1.0 h (range 0.5-1.0 h), Cmax ~72.8 ng/mL, AUC ~276 h*ng/mL [Established; FDA Vyleesi label]. The earlier INTRANASAL formulation showed dose-proportional Cmax/AUC, faster Tmax ~0.5 h, but lower/variable absorption [Evidence-human; PMID 14963471].
Half-life
Terminal elimination half-life ~2.7 h (range 1.9-4.0 h) after SC 1.75 mg [Established; FDA label]. The early intranasal studies reported a similar short t1/2 of ~1.85-2.09 h [Evidence-human; PMID 14963471]. This short half-life underlies its on-demand, as-needed (not daily) use.
Metabolism
Metabolized by MULTIPLE HYDROLYSES of the amide bonds of the cyclic peptide backbone (i.e. peptidase/proteolytic cleavage of the ring), NOT by cytochrome-P450 oxidation — so classic CYP-mediated drug interactions are minimal [Established; FDA label]. No single named CYP enzyme is responsible; this is generic peptide proteolysis.
Excretion
Predominantly renal: ~64.8% of administered radioactivity recovered in urine and ~22.8% in feces [Established; FDA label]. Plasma clearance ~6.5 L/h. Renal impairment raises exposure ~1.2-2-fold (mild to severe); hepatic impairment raises AUC ~1.2-1.7-fold (mild to moderate) — relevant because higher peaks can worsen nausea and the transient pressor effect [Established; FDA label].
Notes
Volume of distribution ~25 L; plasma protein binding only ~21% (low) [Established; FDA label]. It is NOT an ester or prodrug — it is the active molecule itself (contrast with esterified anabolic steroids). BBB/CNS access: bremelanotide is a relatively small, partly cyclic peptide that reaches CNS melanocortin targets after systemic dosing (CNS c-Fos activation in rodents confirms functional central exposure), though it is not a freely brain-penetrant small molecule and the exact CNS-penetration fraction in humans is not well quantified [Evidence-rodent for CNS access; PMID 12851303]. Because it is a peptide cleared largely intact/by hydrolysis, it does not meaningfully induce or inhibit CYPs; the notable interactions are PK-via-gastric-motility rather than metabolic (see drug interactions).

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

HUMAN — female HSDD (approved use): The pivotal dose is 1.75 mg SC as-needed. A phase 2b dose-ranging study (0.75, 1.25, 1.75 mg) found the 1.75 mg dose was the one that hit statistical significance across all 7 patient-reported endpoints vs placebo (P<=.03), establishing 1.75 mg as the registration dose [Evidence-human; PMID 31277966]. At 1.75 mg in the phase 3 RECONNECT trials the absolute effect sizes were SMALL: desire (FSFI-D) increased ~0.30-0.42 points and distress (FSDS-DAO item 13) fell ~0.29-0.37 points vs placebo — statistically significant but modest, and independent re-analysis argues these are near-nil to small with questionable clinical meaningfulness [Evidence-human / Contested; PMID 31599840, 36809187]. HUMAN — male ED (never approved, development-era): clear dose-response. SC PT-141 0.3-10 mg produced statistically significant erections at doses >1.0 mg in healthy men (no visual sexual stimulation), and 4 mg and 6 mg both beat placebo in Viagra-inadequate-responders [Evidence-human; PMID 14999221]. Intranasal PT-141 required higher doses — significant erections only at >7 mg, first erection ~30 min [Evidence-human; PMID 14963471]. Low-dose intranasal PT-141 combined with sildenafil gave an enhanced (additive) erectile response [Evidence-human; PMID 15833522]. HUMAN — appetite/weight (off-target MC4R): in two phase 1 trials in obese women, repeated SC bremelanotide cut caloric intake by ~400 kcal/day and produced a ~1.3 kg greater weight loss over 16 days vs placebo (dose-dependent, more with twice-daily) — demonstrating the MC4R-satiety axis in humans [Evidence-human; PMID 35170192]. RODENT/PRIMATE: systemic PT-141 produces penile erections in rats and nonhuman primates and triggers female proceptive sexual behavior in rodent models, with c-Fos activation establishing central dose-dependent neuronal engagement [Evidence-rodent; PMID 12851303]. EXPLICIT GAP: there is NO modern, published human dose-response curve mapping bremelanotide dose to magnitude of DESIRE improvement beyond the narrow 0.75-1.75 mg HSDD window; the male-ED dose-response is from old (2004) small trials; and no validated human exposure-response model for libido magnitude exists in the public corpus.

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Melanotan-II (MT-II)
    same: Both are cyclic α-MSH heptapeptide analogues with the same Ac-Nle-...-D-Phe-Arg-Trp-Lys pharmacophore and non-selective melanocortin agonism (MC1R + MC3R/MC4R); MT-II is the direct chemical parent — PT-141 was developed FROM MT-II after MT-II's tanning trials unexpectedly produced erections/arousal.
    different: MT-II has a C-terminal AMIDE; bremelanotide is its ring-opened/des-amino metabolite analogue with a free C-terminal CARBOXYLIC ACID, intended to push the balance toward the pro-sexual MC4R effect and away from pigmentation. MT-II is an unapproved grey-market 'tanning'/libido peptide with documented serious harms in case reports — melanoma and changing nevi (PMID 21564053, 24355990), priapism (PMID 30796078), renal infarction (PMID 31953620), and rhabdomyolysis/systemic toxicity (PMID 23121206) — whereas bremelanotide is FDA-approved, dose-capped, and has a defined (if modest) benefit-risk profile. Bremelanotide still carries the MC1R pigmentation liability but its short half-life and intermittent dosing limit it.
  • vs Afamelanotide (Scenesse, NDP-α-MSH / [Nle4-D-Phe7]-α-MSH)
    same: Both are linear/cyclic α-MSH analogue melanocortin agonists; both were co-crystallized in the same 2021 MC4R-Gs cryo-EM study (PMID 34433901), confirming a shared peptide binding mode.
    different: Afamelanotide is deliberately developed for its MC1R/pigmentation effect — it is approved to increase pain-free light exposure in erythropoietic protoporphyria (a photoprotection indication) and is given as a subcutaneous implant, NOT for sexual function. Bremelanotide is developed for the CNS MC4R sexual-desire effect, with pigmentation as an unwanted off-target.
  • vs Setmelanotide (Imcivree)
    same: Both are cyclic melanocortin-receptor agonist peptides targeting MC4R; both engage the same Gs-cAMP cascade.
    different: Setmelanotide is comparatively MC4R-SELECTIVE and approved for rare genetic obesity (POMC, PCSK1, LEPR deficiency, Bardet-Biedl) — it exploits MC4R for SATIETY/weight loss, whereas bremelanotide is non-selective and used for sexual desire. Their overlap reveals bremelanotide's mechanism: bremelanotide also suppresses appetite via MC4R (the obesity phase 1 data), and both share MC1R-mediated hyperpigmentation as a class effect.
  • vs Flibanserin (Addyi) — the other FDA-approved female HSDD drug
    same: Both FDA-approved for acquired, generalized HSDD in premenopausal women; both produce only modest effect sizes and both have been criticized on benefit-risk grounds.
    different: Completely different mechanism: flibanserin is a daily ORAL serotonin 5-HT1A agonist / 5-HT2A antagonist (a CNS neurotransmitter modulator) that must be taken continuously and interacts dangerously with alcohol/CYP3A4; bremelanotide is an as-needed SUBCUTANEOUS melanocortin (MC4R) agonist taken before anticipated activity. PDE5 inhibitors (sildenafil) differ from both — they are peripheral vascular/cGMP agents for erection mechanics, not central desire.

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

"Origin: PT-141 (bremelanotide) is a second-generation derivative of melanotan-II (MT-II), an α-MSH analogue originally synthesized at the University of Arizona for skin tanning; MT-II's tanning trials unexpectedly caused spontaneous erections and arousal, which redirected development toward sexual dysfunction. Palatin Technologies developed PT-141, initially as an INTRANASAL product for male erectile dysfunction and female sexual dysfunction in the early-to-mid 2000s [PMID 14963471, 14999221, 12851303]. The intranasal ED program was halted partly over blood-pressure increases. Palatin pivoted to subcutaneous on-demand dosing for female HSDD; the phase 2b dose-ranging study (PMID 31277966) selected 1.75 mg, and the two phase 3 RECONNECT trials (PMID 31599840) supported approval. North American rights were out-licensed to AMAG Pharmaceuticals. The FDA APPROVED bremelanotide as Vyleesi on June 21, 2019 for premenopausal women with acquired, generalized HSDD — a subcutaneous autoinjector, on-demand, max 1 dose/24h and 8 doses/month [Established; FDA label; PMID 31429064]. It is explicitly NOT indicated for postmenopausal women, for men, or to enhance sexual performance, and is contraindicated in uncontrolled hypertension or known cardiovascular disease. ATC code G02CX05. Regulatory controversy: critics (and a Drug & Therapeutics Bulletin piece, PMID 34642243) argued the approval extended a questionable regulatory precedent set by flibanserin, given small effect sizes. LEGAL STATUS NUANCE for the audience: only the branded SUBCUTANEOUS 1.75 mg Vyleesi is FDA-approved. The vast 'PT-141' grey market (vials of lyophilized peptide sold as 'research chemicals,' often used nasally or injected at unvalidated doses) is NOT FDA-approved, is not quality-controlled, and falls outside the safety envelope of the approved product. Its chemical parent melanotan-II is an unapproved substance flagged by multiple regulators for serious adverse events."

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • Exact human binding constants: the public/regulatory record establishes the MC1R>MC4R>MC3R>MC5R>MC2R potency RANK but does not provide a clean, agreed set of human Ki/EC50 values for bremelanotide at each receptor — the precise affinities and the true MC4R-vs-MC1R selectivity margin remain inadequately quantified in the open literature.
  • Which CNS node actually drives desire: the dopamine/mPOA model (PMID 33455598) and the 2025 hamster data showing NO effect on the VTA-nucleus accumbens reward circuit and no enhancement of sexual reward (PMID 39793696) conflict — the precise neural circuit and neurotransmitter mediating the pro-desire effect in humans is unresolved.
  • Clinical meaningfulness of the effect: independent re-analysis (PMID 36809187) argues the RECONNECT benefits are nil-to-small on validated measures and that the outcome instruments may not be valid in HSDD — whether the statistically significant change is a real-world meaningful benefit is genuinely contested.
  • Long-term safety beyond ~18 months: the cumulative cardiovascular consequence of repeated transient pressor episodes, and whether focal hyperpigmentation fully reverses, are not established over years of use; the daily-dosing 38% hyperpigmentation signal raises questions for off-label high-frequency grey-market use.
  • Male efficacy and approval gap: PT-141 showed clear erectogenic dose-response in small 2004 male trials, yet was never approved for men and no modern phase 3 male data exist — the true efficacy/safety in men (heavily used off-label) is unquantified.
  • Grey-market PT-141 risk profile: nasal/injected unregulated 'PT-141' and its parent melanotan-II are linked in case reports to melanoma/changing nevi, priapism, renal infarction and rhabdomyolysis (PMIDs 21564053, 24355990, 30796078, 31953620, 23121206) — but whether bremelanotide itself, at higher-than-label frequency, carries these MC1R-melanoma or priapism risks is not characterized in controlled studies.

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (NOT a dosing protocol). Cardiovascular: because bremelanotide transiently RAISES blood pressure and lowers heart rate via MC4R, baseline and periodic BP checks are central — it is contraindicated in uncontrolled hypertension or known cardiovascular disease, so a documented BP history and well-controlled BP before/during use is the key signal to watch (PMID 27977473, FDA label). Watch for: a sustained rather than transient BP rise, palpitations, chest pain, or signs of a cardiovascular event — these are red flags to stop and seek care. Skin/melanocyte: inspect skin, face, breasts, and gums for new or darkening pigmentation, and crucially MONITOR MOLES/NEVI for change (asymmetry, border, color, size) — MC1R agonism drives melanin and the melanotan-class is associated in case reports with melanoma; any changing or new pigmented lesion warrants dermatology review. Frequent/daily use sharply increases hyperpigmentation risk (~38% with 8 daily doses) and may not reverse. GI/tolerability: nausea is very common (~40%) and is the main reason people stop; persistent vomiting risks dehydration. Drug interactions to flag: lowered absorption of ORAL naltrexone (loss of opioid blockade) and altered indomethacin levels — review concomitant oral meds (FDA label). Renal/hepatic: impairment raises drug exposure (and thus nausea and the pressor effect), so awareness of kidney/liver status is relevant. Priapism: an erection lasting >4 hours (a melanotan-class case-report risk, especially in men using it off-label) is a urological emergency. None of the above implies the compound should be used off-label; these are the bloodwork/vital-sign/skin signs a clinician would track if it is used."

Caveats

Magnitude honesty: in HSDD the clinical effect is statistically real but small, and independent re-analyses have challenged its clinical meaningfulness (PMID 33678061, 36809187, 34642243) — avoid hype like 'drastically improves' seen in marketing/social posts. Selectivity: PT-141 is NOT MC4R-selective; it also activates MC1R/MC3R/MC5R, which is why blood-pressure, appetite, and pigmentation effects occur — so 'it just targets the sex receptor' is wrong. The relative roles of MC4R vs MC3R in sexual behavior are still partly contested (PMID 17584130). Approved use is women with HSDD; use in men for ED/libido is off-label/investigational and largely based on older early-phase data. Black-market 'PT-141' is often co-sold with melanotan II and may be mislabeled (PMID 33245851). Contraindicated with uncontrolled hypertension/cardiovascular disease due to transient BP elevation (PMID 27977473). This is mechanism education, not medical or dosing advice.
