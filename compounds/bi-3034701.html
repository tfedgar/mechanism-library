BI 3034701

Main target: Simultaneous agonism of the GLP-1 receptor. Human evidence is still limited.

EvidenceLimited human data
ApprovalNot FDA-approved

Boehringer's first-in-class triple agonist that bolts the gut PYY/NPY2R satiety pathway onto a GLP-1/GIP backbone — a different third arm than retatrutide's glucagon.

Limited human data 7 citations
Class
Unimolecular triple receptor agonist peptide (GLP-1R / GIPR / NPY2R), long-acting, once-weekly subcutaneous — investigational anti-obesity agent
Primary target
Simultaneous agonism of the GLP-1 receptor (GLP-1R), the GIP receptor (GIPR), and the neuropeptide-Y2 receptor (NPY2R, the PYY3-36 pathway)
Human status
EMERGING / EARLY CLINICAL. BI 3034701 is a Boehringer Ingelheim investigational triple agonist. First-in-human Phase 1 (NCT06352437) — single ascending SC doses in healthy men (Part A) plus multiple rising SC doses in otherwise-healthy people with overweight/obesity (Part B), 125 participants, single site (Charite Research Organisation, Berlin) — started 10 Jun 2024 and was COMPLETED 7 Oct 2025. Its endpoints were purely safety/tolerability (drug-related treatment-emergent AEs) and pharmacokinetics (Part A AUC0-inf and Cmax; Part B steady-state AUCtau over a 168-hour interval and Cmax), NOT weight loss; no results are posted (has_results = false). A Phase 2, 42-week, randomized double-blind parallel-group placebo-controlled dose-finding trial in obesity/overweight without type 2 diabetes (NCT07662122; 7 arms = 6 active doses + placebo; 300 participants planned; ~57 global sites across the US, Canada, UK, Australia, New Zealand, China and South Korea) is NOT YET RECRUITING, with a registered start of 29 Jun 2026 and primary completion 4 Aug 2027; its primary endpoint is percent change in body weight from baseline at week 42. Bottom line: NO human efficacy data exist. Everything about weight-loss magnitude is currently extrapolation from mechanism and from rodent data on the NPY2R component (BI 1820237) plus human physiology of PYY3-36 and of the GLP-1/GIP class. It is years behind retatrutide and tirzepatide.

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

7 steps2 human 2 established 2 rodent / preclinical 1 contested / speculative

  1. GLP-1 receptor agonism (incretin backbone) Established

    One arm of the molecule mimics GLP-1, activating GLP-1 receptors in the pancreas (glucose-dependent insulin release), gut (slowed gastric emptying) and, crucially for weight, hindbrain and hypothalamic neurons that drive satiety and reduce food intake. This is the same mechanism that made semaglutide effective and is the best-validated anti-obesity pathway in humans. (This describes the GLP-1 class; no BI 3034701-specific human efficacy exists.) PMID 38916409 (J Endocrinol 2024 gut-hormone multi-agonist review); PMID 36750526 (tirzepatide SURPASS/SURMOUNT review)

  2. GIP receptor agonism (complementary incretin arm) Established

    A second arm activates the GIP receptor. Combined GIP + GLP-1 agonism (the tirzepatide mechanism) produces greater weight loss than GLP-1 alone in humans. GIP is thought to add insulinotropic and central/adipose effects, and preclinical work in mice, rats and shrews suggests GIPR agonism can blunt the nausea/emesis signaling that limits GLP-1 dosing — a tolerability-widening idea that is NOT yet confirmed for BI 3034701 or shown to translate to human nausea outcomes. PMID 36750526 (tirzepatide SURPASS/SURMOUNT review, human); PMID 34380697 (GIPR agonism attenuates GLP-1R-induced nausea/emesis, preclinical)

  3. NPY2R (PYY pathway) agonism — the novel third arm Evidence-human

    The differentiating arm mimics the gut hormone PYY3-36, which selectively activates the neuropeptide-Y2 receptor (NPY2R). PYY3-36 is released from intestinal L-cells after eating and is a physiological 'I'm full' signal; infused PYY3-36 reduces food intake in humans. BI 3034701 is designed to build long-acting NPY2R agonism into the same molecule as GLP-1/GIP — a mechanism no approved or late-stage competitor combines. The PYY/NPY2R satiety biology is human-validated; that BI 3034701 successfully delivers this in humans is a design claim, untested clinically. PMID 12954742 (PYY3-36 infusion in obese/lean humans, NEJM 2003); PMID 12167864 (PYY3-36 Y2 mechanism, Nature 2002); PMID 40619099 (BI 1820237 long-acting NPY2R agonist, Boehringer, Mol Metab 2025)

  4. NPY2R signaling suppresses appetite via the arcuate nucleus and vagus Evidence-rodent

    PYY3-36 acting on Y2 receptors inhibits NPY/AgRP 'hunger' neurons in the hypothalamic arcuate nucleus (Y2 is an auto-inhibitory receptor on these neurons), which disinhibits the anorexigenic POMC pathway; it also engages satiety signaling that slows gastric emptying. Net effect: reduced food intake — mechanistically distinct from, and complementary to, the incretin arms. The detailed arcuate circuitry is largely rodent-derived (including Y2r-null mice), with supporting human infusion data. PMID 12167864 (Batterham, Nature 2002, arcuate Y2 mechanism; rodent + human); PMID 40619099 (BI 1820237 reduces food intake and gastric emptying in mice)

  5. Synergy: NPY2R amplifies the incretin backbone rather than acting alone Evidence-rodent

    This is the core rationale, and it is rodent-derived. In Boehringer's own preclinical work, the long-acting NPY2R agonist BI 1820237 produced NO significant weight loss on its own in diet-induced obese mice, but combined with a GCGR/GLP-1R dual agonist (survodutide) it drove 22% weight loss vs 17% for the dual agonist alone (p<0.01), and a regression-based interaction analysis found it increased the dual agonist's efficacy by 265% at an ED50 of 11.7 nmol/kg. The bet behind BI 3034701 is that a single molecule delivering NPY2R + incretin agonism captures this synergy — but note the mouse data used a DIFFERENT backbone (GCGR/GLP-1, no GIP) and a SEPARATE molecule. PMID 40619099 (Augustin et al., Mol Metab 2025)

  6. Endogenous PYY is deficient in obesity — a rationale for supplementing it Evidence-human

    Fasting and post-meal PYY levels are lower in people with obesity, and fasting PYY correlates negatively with BMI (r = -0.84) — yet obese subjects are NOT resistant to PYY's appetite-suppressing effect (unlike leptin), with PYY3-36 infusion cutting buffet intake ~30% in obese and ~31% in lean subjects. This supports the idea that pharmacologically restoring/amplifying the PYY-NPY2R signal is a rational, human-validated satiety lever. PMID 12954742 (Batterham, NEJM 2003)

  7. Net integrated output: multi-pathway appetite suppression and weight loss (hypothesized) Speculative

    By hitting three complementary satiety/metabolic pathways at once, BI 3034701 is designed to lower food intake and body weight through partially independent mechanisms. Whether this translates to human weight loss — and whether it matches or beats GLP-1/GIP (tirzepatide) or GLP-1/GIP/glucagon (retatrutide) — is UNKNOWN and is exactly what the Phase 2 dose-finding trial (NCT07662122) is built to test. NCT07662122 (Phase 2 dose-finding, not yet recruiting); NCT06352437 (completed Phase 1, safety/PK)

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. Described (by sponsor/registry) as a unimolecular peptide engineered to agonize three GPCRs: GLP-1R and GIPR (class B secretin-family incretin receptors) plus NPY2R (a class A neuropeptide-Y2 receptor, the endogenous target of PYY3-36). The exact relative potency/balance at each receptor for BI 3034701 is not publicly disclosed. The NPY2R pharmacophore lineage is Boehringer's long-acting NPY2R agonist program exemplified by BI 1820237, characterized as a selective, long-acting Y2 agonist.

Signalling cascade. GLP-1R and GIPR couple to Gs -> adenylyl cyclase -> cAMP/PKA, driving glucose-dependent insulin secretion in beta-cells and satiety signaling centrally. NPY2R couples to Gi/o -> inhibits adenylyl cyclase, lowering cAMP; on arcuate NPY/AgRP neurons Y2 is an autoinhibitory receptor, so agonism suppresses orexigenic NPY/AgRP tone and disinhibits anorexigenic POMC neurons, plus it engages satiety signaling and slows gastric emptying (arcuate specifics largely rodent-derived).

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Subcutaneous injection (abdomen; self-injected in Phase 2); designed as a long-acting, once-weekly peptide. Phase 2 administers two injections per dosing.
Absorption / bioavailability
Not publicly reported. Phase 1 (NCT06352437) measured single-dose AUC0-inf and Cmax (Part A) and steady-state AUCtau over a 0-168-hour (1-week) interval plus Cmax (Part B), consistent with once-weekly dosing intent.
Half-life
Not publicly disclosed; the multiple-dose 1-week sampling interval in Phase 1 is consistent with a half-life compatible with weekly administration. Do not cite a specific number — none is published.
Metabolism
Not disclosed. As an engineered agonist peptide it is expected to undergo proteolytic/peptidase catabolism rather than CYP metabolism (class-typical), but no BI 3034701-specific data are published.
Excretion
Not reported (peptide catabolism to amino acids/peptide fragments expected by class analogy).
Notes
All PK specifics are pending; the completed Phase 1 was the PK/safety readout and results are not yet posted (has_results = false on ClinicalTrials.gov as of mid-2026).

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

No human dose-response data are available for BI 3034701. The Phase 2 trial (NCT07662122) is explicitly a dose-finding study with six active dose arms plus placebo (7 groups; >80% probability of active drug) over 42 weeks — this is where a human dose-response for body weight will first be established. In rodents, the NPY2R component BI 1820237 showed dose-dependent reductions in food intake and gastric emptying, and regression-based interaction analysis with survodutide gave an ED50 of 11.7 nmol/kg for the potentiation effect — but these are animal figures for a different (separate) molecule and do not translate to BI 3034701 human doses. For CONTEXT ONLY, retatrutide's human dose-response (a different-mechanism triple agonist) ran -7.2% at 1 mg up to -17.5% at 12 mg at 24 weeks (NEJM 2023 Phase 2).

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Retatrutide (LY3437943)
    same: Both are once-weekly SC unimolecular TRIPLE agonists sharing a GLP-1R + GIPR backbone for obesity.
    different: The THIRD arm differs fundamentally: BI 3034701 adds NPY2R (gut PYY/satiety pathway); retatrutide adds glucagon receptor (GCGR, boosting energy expenditure and hepatic effects). This is the headline differentiator. Retatrutide is far ahead (Phase 3; up to ~24% weight loss at 48 wk in Phase 2); BI 3034701 has zero published human efficacy.
  • vs Tirzepatide (GLP-1/GIP dual)
    same: Shares the exact GLP-1R + GIPR dual-incretin backbone.
    different: BI 3034701 adds a third (NPY2R) mechanism on top; tirzepatide is approved with 16.5-22.4% weight loss (SURMOUNT-1, 72 wk). BI 3034701's bet is that NPY2R synergy pushes beyond dual-incretin efficacy — unproven in humans.
  • vs Survodutide (GCGR/GLP-1, also Boehringer)
    same: Same sponsor (Boehringer Ingelheim); survodutide is the dual agonist that BI 1820237 (the NPY2R lineage) synergized with in mice.
    different: Survodutide has NO GIP and NO NPY2R; it is GLP-1/glucagon. BI 3034701 is GLP-1/GIP/NPY2R. The preclinical synergy was demonstrated with survodutide + a separate NPY2R agonist, not with BI 3034701's actual backbone.
  • vs PYY3-36 / standalone NPY2R agonists
    same: BI 3034701's third arm is the same NPY2R/PYY biology.
    different: Standalone PYY/NPY2R agonism historically underwhelms for weight loss (BI 1820237 alone gave no significant weight loss in DIO mice; native PYY3-36 is nausea-limited). BI 3034701's rationale is that co-packaging NPY2R with incretins unlocks synergy a standalone cannot.

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

Boehringer Ingelheim next-generation metabolic program. The NPY2R chemistry traces to Boehringer's long-acting Y2 agonist discovery work (BI 1820237, published Mol Metab 2025, PMID 40619099), developed alongside the GCGR/GLP-1 dual agonist survodutide. BI 3034701 is described as packaging NPY2R agonism into a GLP-1/GIP incretin backbone as a single triple-agonist molecule. First-in-human dosing began 10 Jun 2024 (NCT06352437) and completed 7 Oct 2025; the Phase 2 dose-finding obesity trial (NCT07662122) is registered with a start of 29 Jun 2026. No regulatory approval anywhere; investigational only; no INN/USAN generic name in public use yet (still an alphanumeric code).

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • Human efficacy: no weight-loss percentage exists for BI 3034701 — will Phase 2 (week-42 % body weight) match/beat tirzepatide or retatrutide?
  • Whether the rodent NPY2R-incretin synergy (shown with a DIFFERENT backbone, survodutide, and a separate molecule) reproduces in humans with a GIP-containing backbone
  • Relative receptor potency/balance across GLP-1R/GIPR/NPY2R (undisclosed) and how that shapes efficacy vs tolerability
  • Human PK/half-life confirming true once-weekly dosing (Phase 1 results not yet posted)
  • Tolerability: does adding NPY2R (which also slows gastric emptying) worsen GI AEs, or does the GIP arm offset nausea as rodent data suggest?
  • Cardiometabolic effects beyond weight (glucose, lipids, blood pressure, liver fat) — Phase 2 captures BP/waist but the broader profile is unknown
  • Durability and lean-mass preservation; effect in type 2 diabetes (excluded from current Phase 2)
  • Whether NPY2R agonism carries any off-target CNS or cardiovascular liabilities not seen with pure incretins

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Framed for harm-reduction/education, NOT a protocol. Because BI 3034701 is investigational and available only in trials, the only appropriate 'use' is trial participation under medical supervision. Parameters a clinician monitors for this drug class (by analogy — no BI 3034701-specific safety data yet): gastrointestinal tolerability (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation), especially during any dose escalation; hydration and signs of pancreatitis (severe persistent abdominal pain); resting heart rate and blood pressure (the Phase 2 tracks systolic and diastolic BP); rate of weight loss with attention to adequate nutrition and lean-mass preservation; injection-site reactions; and, given the NPY2R novelty, any unexpected CNS/mood or GI-motility effects. The Phase 2 protocol screens mood/suicidality (C-SSRS) at entry, consistent with class-wide vigilance. There are deliberately NO dosing instructions here; anyone considering this compound should do so only within the registered clinical trial (NCT07662122).

Caveats

HYPE-VS-DATA WARNING: Despite fitness/longevity chatter framing BI 3034701 as a 'retatrutide killer,' there is not a single published human weight-loss number for this compound as of mid-2026 — the Phase 2 that would generate one has not started (registered start 29 Jun 2026). The 'triple agonist' label is real but the third arm is DIFFERENT from retatrutide: BI 3034701 adds NPY2R (PYY pathway), whereas retatrutide adds glucagon (GCGR). They are not interchangeable and their side-effect profiles could differ substantially. The strongest efficacy signal (22% vs 17% weight loss; +265% potentiation, ED50 11.7 nmol/kg) comes from MICE and used the SEPARATE NPY2R agonist BI 1820237 combined with a GCGR/GLP-1R dual agonist (survodutide) — not from BI 3034701 itself and not from a GIP-containing backbone; the synergy claim is a rational hypothesis, not a demonstrated human result. NPY2R agonism alone did not significantly reduce weight in obese mice, so the whole value proposition rests on synergy that must still be proven in people. The idea that the GIP arm offsets GLP-1-type nausea rests on rodent/shrew data only. Expect GLP-1-class gastrointestinal adverse events (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation) and dose-dependent heart-rate increase as plausible risks by analogy to the class, not as measured BI 3034701 findings. This entry synthesizes published evidence for education only; it is not medical advice, and it deliberately contains no dosing or 'how to run it' information — the compound is not approved and is available only inside controlled clinical trials.
