OPK-88004

An oral, non-steroidal SARM that reliably adds lean mass and trims fat in men — but in the one well-run human trial it did NOT improve strength, physical performance, or sexual function, and its lead urology (BPH) program was terminated.

Human / approved 5 citations
Class
Non-steroidal selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM); cyclopenta[b]indole carbamate (C22H22N4O2, MW 374
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR, NR3C4; ChEMBL target CHEMBL1871) — intended tissue-selective agonist/modulator: anabolic in skeletal muscle while sparing the prostate
Human status
Investigational / pipeline — NOT approved for any indication anywhere. Originated at Eli Lilly as LY2452473 (studied for erectile dysfunction, alone and as the tadalafil combination LY900010, and later for BPH) and subsequently developed by OPKO Health as OPK-88004 (also coded TT-701). The best human efficacy evidence is a single NIH/NINR-funded, placebo-controlled Phase 2 RCT (n=114) in testosterone-deficient men after radical prostatectomy for low-grade, organ-confined prostate cancer (NCT02499497, sponsored by Dana-Farber Cancer Institute with the trial run through Brigham and Women's/Johns Hopkins; published Pencina et al. 2021, PMID 34019661). Its designated PRIMARY endpoint was a sexual-activity score (PDQ), which did NOT improve; body composition was a secondary endpoint. The trial showed dose-related lean-mass gain and fat loss with a reassuring short-term prostate-safety signal, but NO improvement in strength, physical performance, or sexual function. OPKO's own Phase 2 dose-ranging BPH trial (NCT03297398 / SAR-202, 15 mg vs 25 mg vs placebo × 16 weeks, primary endpoint = % change in serum PSA, planned n≈114) was TERMINATED. As of the 2024–2025 literature there is no Phase 3 program and no active approved use; several 2023–2025 papers concern only doping-control detection of the molecule (e.g. equine metabolism, PMID 36758220). SARMs including this one are prohibited in sport by WADA and are unapproved for human use; the molecule circulates illicitly as an unregulated 'research chemical' with no quality assurance.

At a glance

  • Dose-related increase in whole-body and appendicular lean mass (human RCT)
  • Significant reduction in percent body fat (human RCT)
  • Prostate-sparing at studied doses: no PSA recurrence and no erythrocytosis in prostatectomy survivors over 12 weeks
  • No improvement in muscle strength or physical performance despite lean-mass gain
  • No improvement in sexual desire, erectile function, mood, or fatigue
  • Suppresses HDL-cholesterol and ApoA1; raises hepatic lipase (class-typical oral-SARM lipid effect)
  • Lowers serum alkaline phosphatase; no change in dedicated bone turnover markers
  • Oral, once-daily; long parent half-life (~27 h); CYP3A4-metabolised

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Binds the androgen receptor directly Evidence-in vitro

    OPK-88004 is a small non-steroidal molecule (a cyclopenta[b]indole carbamate, not a steroid) that binds the androgen receptor (AR), the same receptor testosterone and DHT act on. ChEMBL curates it as an AR 'modulator' with direct_interaction = true and disease_efficacy = true. Note: this is a curated annotation supported by the class and the human ADME paper, not a published human receptor-binding assay for this exact molecule, so the molecule-specific binding evidence is best regarded as in vitro / curated. ChEMBL CHEMBL3542265 mechanism (target CHEMBL1871, AR); ref PMID 22961682

  2. Acts as a tissue-selective AR modulator rather than a pure androgen Contested

    The design premise of a SARM is conformational selectivity: on binding AR, the drug is meant to stabilise a receptor shape that recruits gene-activating co-regulators strongly in muscle but weakly (or with net antagonism) in prostate, so it builds muscle without driving prostate growth. For OPK-88004 this selectivity mechanism is INFERRED from its drug class and its clinical behaviour (lean-mass gain with no PSA recurrence) — there is no published human tissue-level co-activator mapping for this molecule, so the mechanism itself is not established for OPK-88004. Class mechanism reviewed in PMID 39285652; OPK-88004 prostate-sparing clinical behaviour PMID 34019661

  3. Activated AR translocates to the nucleus and drives an anabolic transcription program Established

    Ligand-bound AR moves into the nucleus, binds androgen-response elements in DNA, and switches on genes that increase muscle protein synthesis and satellite-cell activity — the same core pathway used by testosterone. This is canonical AR biology; for OPK-88004 specifically this downstream step is assumed from AR pharmacology and has not been separately mapped in humans for this molecule. Canonical AR signalling; OPK-88004 acts through AR per ChEMBL CHEMBL3542265 / PMID 22961682

  4. In muscle: increases lean body mass and reduces fat mass Evidence-human

    In androgen-deficient men after radical prostatectomy, 12 weeks of OPK-88004 produced a clear DOSE-RELATED rise in whole-body and appendicular (arm+leg) lean mass and a significantly greater drop in percent body fat versus placebo (all P<0.001). This is the strongest, most reproducible human effect and the basis for the 'lean-mass gain' headline. PMID 34019661 (RCT, n=114; NCT02499497)

  5. On bone turnover: lowers alkaline phosphatase, but bone markers unchanged Evidence-human

    OPK-88004 significantly lowered serum alkaline phosphatase (an enzyme influenced by bone/liver turnover), consistent with an androgenic signal, yet dedicated bone-formation/resorption markers did NOT differ from placebo over 12 weeks. So a bone benefit is not established for this compound in humans. PMID 34019661

  6. On the liver/lipids: raises hepatic lipase and suppresses HDL-cholesterol and ApoA1 Evidence-human

    Like other oral SARMs, OPK-88004 lowered HDL-cholesterol and ApoA1 and increased hepatic triacylglycerol lipase (HTGL). Mechanistically the HDL drop appears at least partly driven by faster HDL catabolism via hepatic lipase rather than a simple 'more atherogenic' shift — HDL particle size and cholesterol-efflux capacity were largely preserved. The cardiovascular meaning of this HDL suppression is genuinely unresolved. PMID 35822201

  7. Where the anabolic signal did NOT translate: no strength or performance gain Evidence-human

    Despite adding lean mass, OPK-88004 did NOT improve muscle strength, physical performance (stair-climb power, gait speed, loaded walk), sexual desire, erectile function, mood, or fatigue versus placebo over 12 weeks (P=0.73 across these outcomes). Added lean tissue on a DXA scan is not the same as functional strength — a critical honesty point that separates this compound's data from the hype. PMID 34019661

  8. Metabolised by CYP3A4 — vulnerable to drug interactions Established

    OPK-88004/LY2452473 is cleared almost entirely by metabolism (minimal unchanged drug in excreta), with CYP3A4 the largest single contributor to its depletion in vitro. This means strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g. ritonavir, itraconazole) or inducers can substantially change exposure — a real-world interaction and monitoring concern, reflected in trial exclusion criteria. PMID 22961682 (human [14C] mass-balance ADME; NCT01275157)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Ligand of the androgen receptor (AR / NR3C4; ChEMBL target CHEMBL1871). ChEMBL curates OPK-88004 (CHEMBL3542265; pref_name LY2452473) as an AR 'modulator' with direct_interaction = true and disease_efficacy = true (mechanism ref PMID 22961682). It is a non-steroidal cyclopenta[b]indole carbamate — chemically unrelated to testosterone — so it is not aromatised to estrogen and not 5α-reduced to DHT the way testosterone is. Calculated drug-likeness is favourable (QED 0.76, zero Rule-of-Five violations, MW 374.44, C22H22N4O2, cLogP 3.56). Note the direct-binding annotation is curated/class-based, not a published human binding assay for this molecule.

Signalling cascade. Canonical nuclear-receptor cascade: cytoplasmic AR binds OPK-88004 → receptor conformational change and co-regulator recruitment → nuclear translocation → binding to androgen-response elements → transcription of anabolic genes in skeletal muscle. Tissue selectivity (muscle-anabolic, prostate-sparing) is the defining class-level SARM property and is INFERRED for OPK-88004 from its clinical behaviour (lean-mass gain with no PSA recurrence) rather than from any published human co-activator mapping for this exact molecule.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral (once-daily dosing used in Phase 2). Rapidly absorbed.
Absorption / bioavailability
Tmax ~2–3 h for parent drug and total radioactivity after a single 15 mg [14C] oral dose in 6 healthy men (NCT01275157). A formulation/bioavailability study (NCT01401543, n=24) was also run by Lilly. Absolute oral bioavailability not reported in the retrieved sources.
Half-life
Terminal t½ ~27 h for parent LY2452473 and ~51 h for total radioactivity (supports once-daily dosing).
Metabolism
Extensively metabolised; metabolic clearance is the main elimination route (minimal unchanged drug in excreta). In vitro phenotyping indicated CYP3A4 is the largest single contributor to depletion. Multiple parallel pathways — major circulating metabolites S5 (acetylamine) and S12 (cyclopentene hydroxylation); largest excretory metabolite S10 (a diol). No single metabolic pathway accounts for >30% of the excreted dose.
Excretion
Near-complete recovery of the radioactive dose by 312 h: ~47.9% in urine, ~46.6% in feces.
Notes
CYP3A4-dominant clearance means strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (ritonavir, itraconazole, clarithromycin) or inducers (rifampicin) are expected to alter exposure — reflected in trial exclusion criteria that barred potent CYP3A4 modulators.

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

Human doses studied: 1, 5, and 15 mg/day in the prostatectomy-survivor trial (PMID 34019661 / NCT02499497), and 15 mg vs 25 mg/day in the terminated BPH trial (NCT03297398). Lean-mass increase and fat-mass decrease were explicitly DOSE-RELATED across 1–15 mg (P<0.001). Critically, the anabolic body-composition response did NOT bring a matching functional response at any dose (no strength/performance gain). HDL-C suppression is a class-consistent oral-SARM effect. NOTE: exact per-arm effect sizes are in the source papers/trial results; this library does not publish dosing protocols and none should be inferred from these figures.

How it compares

  • vs Testosterone / TRT
    same: Both act via the androgen receptor and increase lean mass / reduce fat.
    different: OPK-88004 is non-steroidal, prostate-sparing at studied doses, and did not cause erythrocytosis — attractive where prostate/hematocrit are concerns. BUT unlike testosterone it did NOT improve strength, sexual function, or mood in its trial, and it still suppresses HDL. Testosterone has decades of outcome data; OPK-88004 has one small 12-week study.
  • vs Enobosarm (GTx-024/ostarine)
    same: Both non-steroidal SARMs that raise lean mass and lower HDL-C; both show the recurring 'lean-mass-up but function-uncertain' pattern.
    different: Enobosarm has larger/longer cachexia and healthy-elderly datasets and has advanced to later-stage trials (incl. breast-cancer-related indications); OPK-88004's lead urology program was terminated and it has no Phase 3. Both are grey-market 'SARMs' but with different depth of evidence.
  • vs LGD-4033 (ligandrol) / RAD-140 (testolone)
    same: All non-steroidal AR agonists sold illicitly as 'research chemicals'; all raise regulatory/anti-doping and product-quality concerns.
    different: LGD-4033 and RAD-140 are the popular bodybuilding grey-market SARMs with more anecdote and known suppression/hepatotoxicity signals; OPK-88004 has cleaner published prostate-safety and human ADME/PK data but far less recreational use and no strength benefit shown. None are approved.
  • vs 5-alpha reductase inhibitors (finasteride/dutasteride) for BPH
    same: Both were explored for benign prostatic hyperplasia.
    different: Different mechanism entirely: 5-ARIs shrink prostate by lowering DHT; OPK-88004 was tested in BPH as an AR modulator with a PSA-based primary endpoint, and that program was terminated. Not interchangeable.

History &amp; legal status

Discovered/developed by Eli Lilly as LY2452473. Lilly explored it for erectile dysfunction — alone and as the fixed combination LY900010 (LY2452473 + tadalafil; Phase 2 NCT01160289, n=410) — and ran early human ADME (NCT01275157, [14C] mass balance, n=6) and formulation/bioavailability work (NCT01401543, n=24). The asset was later taken up by OPKO Health as OPK-88004 (also carrying the code TT-701). Its most cited efficacy trial was the NIH/NINR-funded academic Phase 2 in prostate-cancer survivors, sponsored by Dana-Farber Cancer Institute and run through Brigham and Women's / Johns Hopkins (NCT02499497, Pencina 2021, PMID 34019661). OPKO's own Phase 2 dose-ranging BPH trial (NCT03297398, SAR-202) was TERMINATED. There is no marketing approval in any jurisdiction. Since ~2020 much of the fresh literature is anti-doping/detection methodology (e.g. equine metabolism PMID 36758220 and urine LC-MS screening methods) — SARMs including this one are prohibited in sport by WADA and are unapproved for human use by regulators, yet circulate on the grey market as 'research chemicals.'

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Whether the DXA lean-mass gain ever converts to real strength or physical-function benefit with longer dosing — the 12-week trial said no.
  • Long-term prostate cancer safety: the reassuring short-term PSA/no-recurrence signal comes from a small 12-week study and cannot be generalised.
  • Cardiovascular meaning of the HDL-C/ApoA1 suppression — no CV-outcome trial exists; the mechanistic 'benign HDL catabolism' story is unproven at the outcome level.
  • Degree and reversibility of HPG-axis (LH/testosterone) suppression specific to OPK-88004.
  • Hepatotoxicity risk with chronic use (a class concern for oral SARMs) — not characterised beyond short-trial LFT monitoring.
  • Effects in populations never studied: healthy young athletes, women, and people using unverified illicit product.
  • Why the BPH program was terminated (efficacy, business, or safety) and whether any development will resume.
  • Fertility, bone strength (as opposed to bone markers), and mood effects over the long term.
  • Whether the inferred muscle-vs-prostate conformational selectivity actually holds at the tissue level in humans for this molecule (never directly mapped).

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Educational harm-reduction only — NOT a protocol, and this library gives no dosing guidance. If a clinician is monitoring someone exposed to this class, evidence points to watching: (1) LIPIDS — expect HDL-C and ApoA1 suppression; recheck a fasting lipid panel and place it in overall cardiovascular-risk context. (2) LIVER FUNCTION — baseline and periodic ALT/AST/bilirubin given the class hepatotoxicity concern; investigate jaundice, dark urine, or right-upper-quadrant pain. (3) PROSTATE — PSA and symptom review in older men, especially any prostate-cancer history; do not assume 'SARM = prostate-safe' outside the studied conditions. (4) HEMATOCRIT — androgens can raise red-cell mass (though erythrocytosis was not seen in the OPK-88004 trial). (5) HPG AXIS — LH/testosterone and, where relevant, semen parameters if suppression or fertility concerns arise. (6) DRUG INTERACTIONS — because clearance is CYP3A4-dependent, review co-medications and avoid combining with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors/inducers. (7) PRODUCT INTEGRITY — grey-market 'OPK-88004' is unregulated and frequently mislabelled or contaminated; none of the trial safety data apply to unverified product. Any red-flag symptom (chest pain, breathlessness, jaundice, urinary retention) warrants stopping and urgent medical review. Athletes: this is a WADA-prohibited substance.

Caveats

Read the headline carefully: the 'lean-mass and strength gains' framing is only half-true for this compound. In the one rigorous human trial, lean mass rose and fat fell in a dose-related way, but strength and physical-performance measures did NOT improve versus placebo — added DXA lean tissue did not become usable strength over 12 weeks. The compound also did not improve sexual function, despite Lilly's original interest in erectile dysfunction, and in fact the trial's designated primary endpoint was a sexual-activity score that was null. Evidence is thin and short: essentially one 12-week Phase 2 RCT (n=114) in a narrow population (older men, mean age 67.5, post-prostatectomy, androgen-deficient) — not healthy young lifters, not women, and not long enough to judge cancer safety, cardiovascular outcomes, or fertility. The prostate-safety signal (no PSA recurrence, no erythrocytosis) is genuinely encouraging but comes from a small, short study and cannot license testosterone-like use in men with prostate cancer outside trials. The lead urology (BPH) program was terminated, so real-world development has effectively stalled. Illicit 'SARM' products sold as OPK-88004 are unregulated: mislabelling, wrong compounds, and contamination are common across the grey-market SARM supply, and none of the human data above apply to unverified powders.
