Tesamorelin

Main target: The GHRH receptor. Backed by human data.

Works in humans?Human evidence
Approved?FDA-approved

A synthetic copy of your body's own "release growth hormone" signal — it tells the pituitary to make more of your own GH in natural pulses, and that extra GH preferentially burns off deep belly (visceral) fat and liver fat.

Human / approvedFDA-approved 17 citations
Class
Growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) analog / GHRH-receptor agonist (a "secretagogue" — it makes the body secrete its own GH rather than being GH itself)
Primary target
The GHRH receptor (GHRHR), a Gs-protein-coupled receptor on the somatotroph cells of the anterior pituitary gland
Human status
Human-validated for its core mechanism and its headline effect. Tesamorelin (Egrifta) is FDA-approved (2010) specifically to reduce excess visceral adipose tissue (VAT) in HIV patients with lipodystrophy — so essentially all the high-quality human data are in that population, not in healthy athletes or general obesity. Established in humans: it raises endogenous pulsatile GH and IGF-1 (IGF-1 +~108–181 ng/mL; Stanley 2011 PMID 20943777, Falutz 2010 PMID 20554713), reduces VAT by roughly 15–18% over 26–52 weeks (NEJM PMID 18057338; pooled phase 3 PMID 20554713), reduces liver fat / NAFLD (JAMA RCT PMID 25038357; Lancet HIV RCT PMID 31611038), and does this while largely sparing subcutaneous fat and, importantly, preserving insulin-stimulated glucose uptake in short-term study (PMID 20943777). NOT established: efficacy/safety for bodybuilding, anti-aging, or fat loss in healthy non-HIV people — that use is off-label and extrapolated, with no comparable RCT evidence. Effects reverse on discontinuation (VAT returns).

At a glance

  • Increases the body's own growth hormone secretion in a natural pulsatile pattern (boosts pulse amplitude and basal secretion without changing pulse frequency) — Evidence-human
  • Raises circulating IGF-1, the main downstream effector of GH (roughly +100 to +180 ng/mL) — Established
  • Selectively reduces visceral (deep abdominal) adipose tissue by ~15–18% over 6–12 months, while largely preserving subcutaneous fat — Established (in HIV lipodystrophy)
  • Reduces liver fat and can reverse NAFLD/steatosis — Evidence-human (HIV-associated NAFLD)
  • Improves lipid profile (lowers triglycerides) and reduces some inflammatory markers — Evidence-human
  • Short-term it appears to preserve insulin sensitivity, but GH excess can raise blood glucose, so glucose is monitored — Contested/Evidence-human
  • Effects are not permanent — visceral fat re-accumulates after stopping — Evidence-human

Mechanism, step by step

7 steps7 human-evidenced

  1. Mimics the brain's GH-release signal Established

    Tesamorelin is a lab-made, stabilized copy of GHRH — the hormone your hypothalamus normally sends to your pituitary to say 'release growth hormone.' The added chemical group lets it survive longer in the blood than natural GHRH, which breaks down within minutes. PMID 21283099 (Nat Rev Drug Discov); PMID 19243281

  2. Binds the GHRH receptor on pituitary somatotrophs Established

    It docks onto the GHRH receptor on the GH-producing cells (somatotrophs) of the anterior pituitary. This is a Gs-coupled receptor, so binding raises cyclic AMP inside the cell and switches on signaling cascades (including the MAPK pathway) that drive GH secretion. MoA corpus entry (Tesamorelin); PMID 19897610; PMID 25555516

  3. Triggers the body's OWN growth hormone — in natural pulses Evidence-human

    Crucially, it doesn't inject GH; it makes your pituitary release its own GH. Because it works through the normal signaling machinery, GH comes out in physiological pulses (bigger pulse peaks and higher baseline, but the same pulse rhythm), and the body's feedback brakes (like somatostatin) still apply — a key safety difference from injecting GH directly. PMID 20943777 (Stanley 2011, healthy men); PMID 25555516

  4. GH raises IGF-1, the main worker hormone Established

    The pulse of GH travels to the liver and other tissues and stimulates production of IGF-1 (insulin-like growth factor 1), which carries out many of GH's longer-term effects. In trials IGF-1 rose substantially (about +108 ng/mL in HIV patients, +181 µg/L in healthy men). PMID 20554713 (Falutz 2010); PMID 20943777

  5. GH/IGF-1 drive lipolysis, hitting visceral fat hardest Established

    Growth hormone is a potent activator of lipolysis (fat breakdown) and is especially active in visceral fat, which is rich in GH receptors. People with lots of visceral fat tend to have suppressed GH, so restoring GH pulses preferentially mobilizes and shrinks that deep abdominal depot rather than subcutaneous fat. PMID 25555516 (review of mechanism); PMID 18057338 (NEJM trial)

  6. Net result: visceral fat and liver fat fall Evidence-human

    Over months this produces a measurable ~15–18% drop in visceral adipose tissue and a reduction in liver fat, improving NAFLD and triglycerides. Liver studies also show tesamorelin shifts hepatic gene-expression and immune pathways consistent with less fat storage and less inflammation. PMID 25038357 (JAMA, liver fat RCT); PMID 31611038 (Lancet HIV, NAFLD RCT); PMID 32701508 (hepatic transcriptomics)

  7. Reversible, and dependent on a working pituitary Evidence-human

    Because the whole effect runs through your own GH axis, it only works if your pituitary can still make GH — and the benefits fade once you stop, with visceral fat re-accumulating. It is a throttle on your own system, not a permanent rewiring. PMID 20554713; PMID 25555516

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Tesamorelin (TH9507; sequence trans-3-hexenoyl-Tyr-Ala-Asp-Ala-Ile-Phe-Thr-Asn-Ser-Tyr-Arg-Lys-Val-Leu-Gly-Gln-Leu-Ser-Ala-Arg-Lys-Leu-Leu-Gln-Asp-Ile-Met-Ser-Arg-Gln-Gln-Gly-Glu-Ser-Asn-Gln-Glu-Arg-Gly-Ala-Arg-Ala-Arg-Leu) is a synthetic AGONIST analog of human GHRH(1-44). It is the full 44-amino-acid GHRH peptide N-terminally modified with a trans-3-hexenoic acid (hexenoyl) group, which sterically protects the Tyr1-Ala2 bond from dipeptidyl-peptidase IV cleavage and slows plasma degradation (Established; ChEMBL CHEMBL2111290 / TESAMORELIN ACETATE record confirms the 44-mer protein sequence and parenteral protein drug class). Target: pituitary-type GHRH receptor (pGHRH-R; gene GHRHR), a Class B1 (secretin-family) Gs-coupled GPCR on anterior-pituitary somatotrophs. It is a near-full agonist with affinity in the low-nanomolar range comparable to native GHRH; precise published Ki/EC50 values for tesamorelin specifically are NOT reported in the corpus, so exact affinity numbers are an evidence gap (Evidence-human for receptor identity and agonism; Speculative for an exact Ki). Selectivity: acts on GHRH-R, NOT on the ghrelin/GHS-R1a receptor used by GHRPs (ipamorelin, GHRP-6) — this is the key pharmacodynamic distinction from 'GH secretagogue' peptides (Established). It preserves the physiological GH negative-feedback loop: GH/IGF-1 and somatostatin still restrain the pituitary, so tesamorelin amplifies but does not abolish pulsatile GH secretion (Evidence-human; PMID 20943777 showed increased basal AND pulsatile overnight GH in healthy men).

Signalling cascade. GHRH-R engagement follows the Class B1 two-domain model: the peptide C-terminus docks the receptor extracellular domain and the N-terminal hexenoyl-Tyr1 inserts into the 7-transmembrane bundle, activating Gs. Gs-alpha stimulates adenylyl cyclase -> rising cAMP -> protein kinase A (PKA). PKA both (1) phosphorylates voltage-gated Ca2+ channels and CREB, driving Ca2+ influx that triggers exocytosis of stored GH secretory granules (acute pulse), and (2) via CREB and the pituitary transcription factor POU1F1/Pit-1 increases GH1 gene transcription and somatotroph proliferation (sustained). A parallel MAPK/ERK cascade is also activated downstream of the GHRH-R and contributes to somatotroph trophic signaling (Evidence-rodent/in vitro; corpus MoA card cites PMID 19897610, 21220406, 30538214 for MAPK involvement). Released GH acts on hepatic and peripheral GH receptors (JAK2/STAT5) to raise IGF-1, and on adipocyte GH receptors to activate lipolysis (hormone-sensitive lipase) preferentially in visceral adipose tissue; GH/IGF-1 together reduce hepatic de novo lipogenesis and triglyceride storage. Net pharmacodynamic readout: ~80-180% rise in serum IGF-1 and selective loss of visceral (not subcutaneous) fat. Because dosing is once-daily subcutaneous and the drug clears quickly, GH exposure stays pulsatile rather than the flat tonic elevation seen with exogenous recombinant GH — the proposed reason glucose tolerance is better preserved than with rhGH.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Subcutaneous injection only (abdominal). Supplied as a lyophilized powder (tesamorelin acetate) reconstituted before injection; not orally bioavailable (a peptide, degraded in gut). FDA-approved formulations: original EGRIFTA (2 mg/vial) and the more concentrated EGRIFTA SV / EGRIFTA WR (Established; ChEMBL: parenteral=true, oral=false).
Absorption / bioavailability
Absorbed from SC tissue with a rapid rise to peak; the dedicated PK/PD study (NCT02012556, single 2 mg SC then 14 daily doses in PWH) measured tesamorelin AUC, Cmax and Tmax plus the IGF-1 PD response. Absolute SC bioavailability is low and not precisely published in the corpus (peptide drugs of this size typically have modest, single-digit-to-low-tens % SC bioavailability) — treat exact %F as an evidence gap (Evidence-human that it is absorbed and pharmacodynamically active; Speculative on exact bioavailability).
Half-life
Very short parent half-life — on the order of minutes (roughly 25-40 min reported for the parent peptide in product labeling; the corpus PK trial quantifies it but the exact figure is not surfaced in retrieved text). The SHORT half-life is mechanistically important: it produces a transient GHRH-R stimulus and a pulsatile GH spike rather than sustained receptor occupancy. The pharmacodynamic effect (IGF-1 elevation) far outlasts the parent drug — IGF-1 rises over days-to-weeks and plateaus (Evidence-human; PMID 20943777, 18057338).
Metabolism
As a peptide it is cleared by ubiquitous proteolysis (peptidases/proteases) into amino acids and small fragments rather than by hepatic CYP450 enzymes; the N-terminal hexenoyl cap specifically blunts DPP-IV-mediated N-terminal cleavage, the main route that inactivates native GHRH. No clinically significant CYP-mediated drug-drug interactions are expected from the peptide itself (Evidence-human/Established for peptide class).
Excretion
Cleared by systemic proteolysis with renal handling of small peptide fragments and amino acids; no intact-drug biliary/renal excretion pathway of note (Evidence-human, peptide-class inference).
Notes
Not a prodrug and not albumin-conjugated — this is the key contrast with CJC-1295-DAC, which adds a maleimido-albumin-binding (DAC) tail to extend half-life to days. Tesamorelin's only modification is the hexenoyl cap for stability, so it keeps a short, GHRH-like kinetic profile. CNS: GHRH analogs can act in the brain (a related GHRH formulation crossed into CSF effects in the cognition trial PMID 22869065), but whether peripherally injected tesamorelin meaningfully crosses the blood-brain barrier is not established (Speculative). IGF-1 is the practical PK/PD surrogate used to gauge exposure and titrate clinically.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

HUMAN (the clinically validated species): The approved and almost-universally-studied dose is 2 mg SC once daily. At 2 mg/day, IGF-1 rises ~80% (NEJM PMID 18057338: +81.0% vs -5.0% placebo) to ~117% (cognition trial at 1 mg/day, PMID 22869065: +117%) and up to +181 ug/L absolute in healthy men (PMID 20943777). Visceral adipose tissue (VAT) falls ~15% at 26 weeks (NEJM: -15.2% vs +5.0% placebo) and ~18% by 52 weeks of continuous treatment (PMID 18690162, 20101189); a 2026 meta-analysis of 5 RCTs pooled a VAT reduction of -27.7 cm2 and a lean-body-mass gain of +1.4 kg (PMID 41545261). Hepatic fat fraction falls ~37% relative (-4.1% absolute) at 12 months in HIV-NAFLD (Lancet HIV PMID 31611038). Triglycerides drop ~50 mg/dL (NEJM). A formal multi-dose DOSE-RANGING study exists (early phase, PMID 16052083, doses up to 2 mg) and supported 2 mg as the efficacious dose; a 1 mg dose (cognition trial, T2D trial) still raised IGF-1 and reduced body fat but 2 mg is the marketed strength — so a fine-grained human dose-response curve above 2 mg is essentially UNKNOWN/untested. Effects are NOT durable: VAT and liver fat re-accumulate within weeks of stopping (PMID 20101189, 18690162) — this is a maintenance therapy, not a cure. RODENT/IN VITRO: GHRH-R agonism dose-dependently raises cAMP and GH release in pituitary cell systems, and GHRH agonists show tissue-trophic/lipolytic effects in animal models, but quantitative rodent dose-response specific to tesamorelin is not the basis of approval (Evidence-rodent/in vitro). Bottom line: human dose-response is well characterized only at the single 2 mg/day point; supraphysiologic 'more is better' dosing is unstudied and would be expected to push IGF-1 toward acromegalic territory.

How it compares

  • vs CJC-1295 (with DAC / long-acting GHRH analog)
    same: Both are synthetic GHRH(1-44/1-29)-based agonists at the same pituitary GHRH-R, raising pulsatile GH then IGF-1 via the Gs/cAMP/PKA pathway; both increase IGF-1 ~60-100%+.
    different: CJC-1295-DAC adds a drug-affinity-complex (albumin-binding maleimido) tail extending half-life to ~6-8 days, producing a sustained 'GH bleed' (more tonic, less pulsatile) and weekly dosing; tesamorelin is only stability-capped (hexenoyl), keeps a minutes-long half-life and daily SC dosing that preserves pulsatility. Tesamorelin is FDA-approved with the strongest human RCT evidence base; CJC-1295 is research-grade/unapproved with mostly biomarker (not outcome) human data.
  • vs Sermorelin (GHRH 1-29, 'GRF 1-29')
    same: Also a GHRH analog agonist at GHRH-R driving GH/IGF-1; preserves feedback and pulsatility.
    different: Sermorelin is the truncated 29-amino-acid active fragment without the N-terminal stabilizing cap, so it is even more rapidly degraded by DPP-IV (shorter, weaker), historically used as a pediatric GH-deficiency diagnostic/therapeutic (Geref, now largely discontinued). Tesamorelin is the full 44-mer, stabilized, more potent and durable per dose, and approved for an adult metabolic indication.
  • vs Ipamorelin / GHRP-6 (ghrelin-mimetic GH secretagogues, GHS-R1a agonists)
    same: End result overlaps — both raise pulsatile GH and IGF-1 and are used (off-label) to improve body composition.
    different: DIFFERENT RECEPTOR: GHRPs act on the ghrelin receptor GHS-R1a (Gq/PLC/IP3/Ca2+ pathway), not GHRH-R (Gs/cAMP). GHRPs can raise appetite, and older ones (GHRP-6/-2) raise cortisol and prolactin; ipamorelin is 'cleaner.' Because the pathways are independent, GHRH analogs like tesamorelin are synergistic with GHRPs at the somatotroph. Only tesamorelin is FDA-approved.
  • vs Recombinant human GH (somatropin)
    same: Both ultimately raise GH activity and IGF-1, reduce visceral fat, increase lean mass.
    different: rhGH delivers exogenous GH directly (bypassing the pituitary), giving tonic, non-pulsatile, feedback-independent exposure — associated with more insulin resistance, edema, arthralgia and acromegaly-like risk. Tesamorelin works upstream at the pituitary, retains negative feedback and pulsatility, and in trials showed a notably gentler glucose profile; it cannot raise GH in patients with no functional somatotrophs.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • GHK-Cu potentiates ↑ Evidence-human
    Tesamorelin (GHRH-R agonist) raises GH/IGF-1 (IGF-1 typically +100 to +180 ng/mL); the resulting IGF-1 elevation stimulates fibroblast collagen synthesis in skin and connective tissue, overlapping GHK-Cu's copper-driven collagen/GAG/elastin production. On the shared collagen/matrix endpoint the two are additive - Tesamorelin supplies the systemic IGF-1 growth signal, GHK-Cu the local copper-dependent remodeling. Convergent pathway, not shared receptor. PMID 21498100 (GH/IGF-1 and connective-tissue collagen synthesis); PMID 25281191 (IGF-1 collagen synthesis); PMID 18644225 (GHK-Cu matrix stimulation) Watch: Tesamorelin can raise fasting glucose and IGF-1 - monitor glucose/HbA1c and, if available, IGF-1. GHK-Cu adds only its own copper-related monitoring for systemic/injectable use.
  • GHRP-2 potentiates ↑ Established (class-level GHRH+GHRP synergy); specific pair not trialed
    Tesamorelin is a stabilized GHRH(1-44) analog on the GHRH receptor (Gs/cAMP). Paired with GHRP-2's ghrelin-receptor action (Gq/Ca2+), the two receptor pathways converge on the same somatotroph for a supra-additive GH pulse, with GHRP-2 additionally easing the somatostatin brake. Same complementary-receptor logic as any GHRH+GHRP pairing; tesamorelin brings strong GHRH-arm human data (visceral-fat and IGF-1 effects). GHRP-2 monograph step 4; Tesamorelin monograph (GHRHR agonist, pulsatile GH/IGF-1 rise) Watch: IGF-1; fasting glucose/HbA1c (both raise GH, which opposes insulin); fluid retention and joint symptoms.
  • GHRP-6 potentiates ↑ Evidence-human
    Tesamorelin is a stabilized GHRH analog acting on the GHRH receptor. Because GHRP-6 requires endogenous GHRH tone for a maximal pulse, a GHRH-R agonist provides the permissive co-signal, and the two separate receptor pathways (Gs vs Gq) summate at the pituitary to raise GH beyond either alone. Monograph step 3 + cascade (PMID 7883854, 9543138, 9893708); GHRH+GHRP synergy PMID 7745018 Watch: IGF-1 and fasting glucose/HbA1c (both tesamorelin and GH-axis stimulation can worsen glucose handling); watch for edema, arthralgia, paresthesias.
  • Hexarelin potentiates ↑ Evidence-human
    Same GHRP-plus-GHRH synergy as with sermorelin: tesamorelin is a stabilized GHRH analog acting at the GHRH receptor, a different pathway from hexarelin's GHS-R1a. Combining a GHRH-R agonist with a GHS-R1a agonist drives a supra-additive GH pulse (GHRP arm eases somatostatin tone; GHRH arm directly stimulates). The human synergy data are for GHRH(1-29) plus hexarelin; extending it to tesamorelin is a mechanism-class extrapolation since tesamorelin is a GHRH-R agonist. Hexarelin monograph mechanism step 2 (GHRH synergy, PMID 11238504); corpus PMID 8799695 (GHRH + hexarelin combined in humans); tesamorelin monograph (GHRH-R agonist) Watch: IGF-1 and signs of GH excess (edema, arthralgia, paresthesia); fasting glucose/HbA1c, since the larger GH surge accentuates insulin resistance.
  • CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin shares target Evidence-human
    Tesamorelin is a stabilized GHRH analog acting on the SAME pituitary GHRH receptor (Gs-coupled) as CJC-1295. Stacking it with CJC-1295 is redundant on the GHRH/Gs-cAMP arm and additively raises GH/IGF-1 through one shared receptor, amplifying the entire GHRH-class adverse cluster (arthralgia, myalgia, paresthesia/carpal tunnel, edema, injection-site reactions) rather than adding a complementary pathway the way Ipamorelin does. Tesamorelin monograph primary_target (GHRHR Gs-coupled agonist); PMID 41545261 (tesamorelin GHRH-analog class RCT/meta-analysis AE profile); CJC/Ipa comparison 'vs Tesamorelin' Watch: IGF-1 and the GHRH-class AE cluster (joint/muscle aches, paresthesia, peripheral edema, injection-site reactions); fasting glucose.
  • AOD-9604 redundant Evidence-rodent
    Both target adipose fat loss but at different points of the GH axis. Tesamorelin is a GHRH-receptor agonist that raises endogenous GH and IGF-1 — its lipolytic effect flows through the SAME GH C-terminal lipolytic domain that AOD-9604 physically is. Their fat-mobilising signals therefore overlap/duplicate. The key mechanistic contrast the monograph stresses: tesamorelin carries GH-class effects (IGF-1 rise, fluid retention, glucose effects) and has proven human visceral-fat efficacy, whereas AOD-9604 acts peripherally with NO IGF-1 rise, NO GH-axis stimulation, and no proven human fat-loss data. AOD-9604 monograph comparisons (vs tesamorelin: downstream/peripheral, no GH-axis, no IGF-1); PMID 11673763 Watch: IGF-1 and fasting glucose (tesamorelin-driven); an IGF-1 rise should be attributable to tesamorelin, not AOD — an AOD product that raises IGF-1 would signal adulteration.

"Developed by Theratechnologies (Montreal) under the research code TH9507; USAN 'tesamorelin' assigned 2007 (USAN stem '-relin' = GH-release-stimulating peptides). Originated as a stabilized full-length human GHRH(1-44) analog. Phase III program (NCT00123253, NCT00435136, NCT00608023) in HIV-associated abdominal lipodystrophy led to FDA approval in November 2010 as EGRIFTA (tesamorelin for injection) — to date the ONLY drug FDA-approved specifically to reduce excess visceral abdominal fat in HIV-associated lipodystrophy. A reformulated, more concentrated/stable version (EGRIFTA SV, later EGRIFTA WR) was subsequently approved to simplify reconstitution. The EU/EMA did NOT grant marketing authorization (the application was withdrawn), so it is not generally available in Europe. It is a prescription drug, not a controlled substance; however it is on the WADA Prohibited List (S2: peptide hormones / GH-releasing factors) and is banned in sport at all times. It is widely sold gray-market as a 'research peptide' for physique/anti-aging use, which is off-label and unregulated. Indication remains narrow (HIV lipodystrophy); HIV-NAFLD and aging/cognition uses are investigational only."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Long-term (multi-year) safety of chronic IGF-1 elevation in non-HIV physique/anti-aging users — including any cancer-promotion risk — is essentially unstudied; all pivotal data are in HIV patients over <=1 year.
  • Exact human pharmacology numbers are not published in accessible form: tesamorelin's GHRH-R binding affinity (Ki/EC50), absolute SC bioavailability, and precise parent half-life are gaps; IGF-1 is used as a surrogate instead.
  • Whether the liver-fat/fibrosis and immune-modulating benefits (Lancet HIV 2019; CID 2021) generalize to non-HIV NAFLD/MASH and translate into hard clinical outcomes (cirrhosis, HCC) is unknown — trials were small and biomarker/biopsy-based.
  • Dose-response above 2 mg/day is untested in humans, so the efficacy/safety trade-off of the higher doses used in unregulated settings is unknown and would be expected to risk acromegalic IGF-1 levels.
  • CNS penetration and durable cognitive benefit are unresolved: a related GHRH formulation improved executive function in MCI/older adults (Arch Neurol 2012), but whether peripherally dosed tesamorelin meaningfully reaches the brain and helps cognition long-term is unproven.
  • Effects are not durable after stopping (fat re-accumulates), but the optimal long-term maintenance strategy, and whether intermittent dosing preserves benefit while limiting IGF-1 exposure, has not been studied.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (no dosing guidance). Track serum IGF-1 as the primary exposure/safety surrogate — the goal in clinical use is to keep IGF-1 within the age-/sex-normal physiological range; persistently high IGF-1 signals overexposure and acromegaly-type risk. Glucose metabolism: fasting glucose and HbA1c (and ideally an OGTT or fasting insulin if risk factors), since GH is counter-regulatory — watch for new hyperglycemia or insulin resistance even though trials showed minimal net change. Watch clinically for the GH fluid-retention complex: peripheral edema, weight/fluid gain, joint pain (arthralgia), muscle pain, and carpal-tunnel symptoms (wrist/hand numbness, tingling) — these indicate excess GH/IGF-1 and warrant reassessment. Inspect injection sites for erythema, induration, or lipohypertrophy and rotate sites. Because IGF-1 is mitogenic, active or suspected malignancy is a contraindication and any new or growing mass should prompt evaluation and stopping. In HIV patients, monitoring also covers metabolic/lipid panel (triglycerides, cholesterol) which typically improve. Pregnancy is a contraindication. Any signs of acromegaly (enlarging hands/feet/jaw, coarsening features, new headaches/visual change) should trigger discontinuation and endocrinology review."

Caveats

TARGET/DIRECTION/MAGNITUDE check: Tesamorelin is a GHRH-receptor AGONIST that RAISES the body's own GH and IGF-1 (it is NOT exogenous GH and NOT a ghrelin/GHS-receptor agonist like ipamorelin — different receptor). The signature effect is a SELECTIVE ~15–18% reduction in visceral fat with subcutaneous fat largely preserved; do not overstate as general 'weight loss' (total body weight changes little). EVIDENCE POPULATION: Nearly all rigorous human data are in HIV-associated lipodystrophy — the only FDA-approved indication. Extrapolation to healthy lifters, anti-aging, or general obesity is Speculative-to-Evidence-rodent at best; there are no equivalent RCTs there. SAFETY/PHYSIOLOGY: Because it works through the natural GH axis, it requires a functional pituitary and its effects are REVERSIBLE (visceral fat returns after stopping). GH elevation can theoretically worsen glucose/insulin resistance — short-term human data suggested preserved insulin sensitivity (PMID 20943777) but glucose should be monitored, and this point is somewhat Contested. Standard GH-axis cautions apply: contraindicated with active malignancy (IGF-1 is mitogenic) and in pregnancy; can cause fluid retention/arthralgia and injection-site reactions.
