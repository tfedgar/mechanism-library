Ligandrol

Main target: The androgen receptor. Backed by human data.

EvidenceHuman evidence
ApprovalNot FDA-approved

A pill that tries to flip the muscle-building switch of testosterone while mostly skipping the prostate and the estrogen side of the equation — promising in theory, still investigational, and not without real liver and hormone-suppression risk.

Strong human data 21 citations
Class
Non-steroidal Selective Androgen Receptor Modulator (SARM) — investigational, NOT FDA/EMA-approved for any use
Primary target
The androgen receptor (AR) — the same intracellular receptor that testosterone and DHT activate
Human status
NOT approved by the FDA, EMA, or any major regulator for any indication — it is investigational/unlicensed. Human data are genuinely thin: the cornerstone is a single Phase 1 placebo-controlled trial in 76 healthy young men dosed for only 21 days (Basaria 2013, PMID 22459616), which showed safety over that window plus lean-mass gain and hormone suppression. It was earlier explored as a candidate (also called VK5211) for muscle wasting/hip-fracture recovery, but there is NO large, long-term efficacy-and-safety trial. It is banned by WADA (a frequent cause of athlete doping positives) and is widely sold on the grey market as a 'research chemical,' where products are often contaminated or mislabelled. So: a real drug candidate with a coherent mechanism, but the confident claims you'll see online run far ahead of the actual human evidence.

At a glance

  • Increases lean body mass dose-dependently — shown in a real human RCT over just 21 days (Established/Evidence-human, PMID 22459616)
  • Does NOT change fat mass meaningfully in that short trial (Evidence-human, PMID 22459616)
  • Spares the prostate short-term — no PSA rise at 21 days; full vs partial agonism is mostly a rodent finding (Evidence-rodent + limited human, PMID 22459616, 19357508)
  • No estrogen conversion (no aromatization) and no DHT conversion (no 5a-reduction) — a real difference from anabolic steroids (Established class property, PMID 19357508)
  • Suppresses your own testosterone, SHBG, FSH, and lowers HDL cholesterol/triglycerides — it is NOT hormonally neutral (Evidence-human, PMID 22459616)
  • Long elimination half-life with accumulation on daily dosing (Evidence-human, PMID 22459616)
  • Linked to drug-induced (often cholestatic) liver injury in multiple real-world case reports (Evidence-human, lower tier, PMID 32637435, 39421081, 36257328, 33062783)

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

7 steps7 human / established

  1. It binds the androgen receptor like a key in a lock Established

    Ligandrol is a small non-steroidal molecule that slips into the same ligand-binding pocket of the androgen receptor that testosterone uses. It binds with high affinity and selectivity — it is an orthosteric ligand, meaning it occupies the natural hormone's spot rather than a side pocket. Unlike testosterone it is NOT a steroid, which is why it can be taken orally and behaves differently in different tissues. PMID 22459616 (Basaria 2013, first-in-human); PMID 23231475 (Zhang & Sui 2013 review)

  2. Binding reshapes the receptor Established

    When Ligandrol locks in, it forces the receptor's ligand-binding domain into a particular 3-D shape (a conformational change). The exact shape the receptor folds into is the whole game for SARMs — it changes which helper proteins the receptor can grab, and that is what is believed to give different SARMs their different tissue 'flavours'. PMID 19357508 (Bhasin & Jasuja 2009); PMID 23231475

  3. It recruits coregulators and turns genes on Evidence-rodent (mechanism); Established that SARMs as a class show this differential gene regulation

    The reshaped receptor moves into the cell nucleus and docks onto androgen-response DNA sequences. The specific surface it now presents pulls in 'coactivator' or 'corepressor' helper proteins. The mix of helpers available differs from tissue to tissue, so the same drug can act like a strong 'go' signal in muscle and bone but only a weak/partial signal in the prostate. This differential coregulator recruitment is the leading explanation for tissue selectivity. PMID 19357508; PMID 23231475; PMID 37325955 (Bhasin 2023 review)

  4. Tissue selectivity: full agonist in muscle/bone, partial in prostate Evidence-rodent (full vs partial agonism); Evidence-human but limited (no PSA change at 21 days, PMID 22459616). Marketing overstates how 'clean' this is.

    In rodent models, non-steroidal SARMs act as FULL agonists (strong builders) in muscle and bone while acting only as PARTIAL agonists in the prostate — the marketed selling point. In the one published human trial, 21 days of Ligandrol raised lean mass with NO rise in PSA (a prostate marker). Important honesty: 'tissue-selective' is largely a preclinical and short-term human signal — the degree of real-world prostate/long-term sparing in humans is not established, and the selectivity is relative, not absolute. PMID 22459616; PMID 19357508; PMID 23231475

  5. It does NOT aromatize and is NOT 5-alpha-reduced Established (class-level: nonsteroidal SARMs are not CYP19/5alpha-reductase substrates)

    Testosterone gets converted by the aromatase enzyme into estrogen (driving things like gyno and water retention) and by 5-alpha-reductase into the more potent DHT (driving prostate growth and scalp hair loss). Non-steroidal SARMs like Ligandrol are NOT substrates for these enzymes — the molecule itself is the active signal everywhere it goes. This is a genuine pharmacological difference from anabolic steroids, though it does NOT make the drug side-effect-free. PMID 19357508 (explicitly: 'do not serve as substrates for CYP19 aromatase or 5alpha-reductase')

  6. It still shuts down your own hormones Evidence-human (RCT, n=76, 21 days)

    Because Ligandrol activates the androgen receptor in the brain (hypothalamus/pituitary), the body senses 'plenty of androgen' and dials down its own production. In the human trial, even 3 weeks caused dose-dependent suppression of total testosterone, sex-hormone-binding globulin (SHBG), and — importantly — HDL ('good') cholesterol and triglycerides; FSH and free testosterone fell at the top dose. Values recovered after stopping in that short study, but this confirms Ligandrol is NOT hormonally 'free' — it suppresses the natural axis like other androgens. PMID 22459616

  7. Downstream harm signal: liver injury Evidence-human (case reports — lower tier than RCT, but consistent and repeated)

    Beyond the intended receptor effects, real-world Ligandrol use has been linked in multiple published case reports to drug-induced liver injury (often cholestatic — bile-flow type). This was NOT seen in the tightly controlled 21-day trial (where liver enzymes did not rise), which tells you the harm signal is tied to longer/higher real-world dosing and to contaminated black-market products, not necessarily to the clean compound at trial doses. PMID 32637435; PMID 39421081; PMID 36257328; PMID 33062783

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. PRIMARY TARGET — androgen receptor (AR, gene NR3C1-family member NR3C4; ChEMBL CHEMBL1871). LGD-4033 is a nonsteroidal SARM that binds the AR ligand-binding domain (LBD) as an ORTHOSTERIC ligand (same pocket as testosterone/DHT), NOT an allosteric modulator. AFFINITY: ChEMBL records Ki = 1.0 nM at the human AR (pChEMBL 9.0; binding assay, J Med Chem 2022) — sub-nanomolar-to-low-nanomolar, comparable to or exceeding native DHT. ACTION TYPE: functional AGONIST / positive modulator at AR in anabolic tissue (muscle, bone); ChEMBL mechanism record (mec_id 4056) classifies it as AR 'MODULATOR', action AGONIST, direct_interaction=true, disease_efficacy=true [Established]. SELECTIVITY: 'selective' refers to TISSUE selectivity (strong agonism in muscle/bone, weak/partial agonism in prostate and other androgenic tissue), not receptor-subtype selectivity — there is one AR. Manufacturer (Ligand/Viking) preclinical claims described high AR selectivity over other steroid receptors (ER, PR, GR, MR) [Evidence-rodent / manufacturer]. Note: a minor human metabolite (coded M8, a pyrrole derivative) was shown in PC3(AR)2 transactivation assays to act as a potent AR ANTAGONIST, while metabolite M2c is a potent agonist (PMID 41261783) [Evidence-in vitro].

Signalling cascade. Classical genomic AR pathway. (1) Lipophilic LGD-4033 (MW 338, cLogP ~3.5, Lipinski-compliant) crosses the plasma membrane and binds the cytosolic AR LBD. (2) Ligand binding triggers a conformational change in helix 12 of the LBD, dissociation of chaperone heat-shock proteins (HSP90/HSP70/HSP56) and receptor stabilization. (3) AR homodimerizes, is phosphorylated, and translocates to the nucleus via its nuclear localization signal. (4) The AR DNA-binding domain binds Androgen Response Elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer regions of androgen-responsive genes. (5) Coregulator recruitment: agonist-bound AR recruits p160 coactivators (SRC-1/2/3), CBP/p300, and the Mediator complex to drive transcription. The leading hypothesis for SARM tissue selectivity is that the distinct nonsteroidal scaffold induces a tissue-specific AR conformation that recruits a DIFFERENT coregulator profile than testosterone/DHT — favoring coactivators in muscle/bone but not the coactivator milieu required for full prostate stimulation [Contested — mechanism inferred, not proven for LGD-4033 specifically]. (6) Downstream anabolic effectors in skeletal muscle: increased muscle protein synthesis, modulation of the IGF-1/PI3K/Akt/mTOR axis (pro-hypertrophy), suppression of FoxO/ubiquitin-proteasome atrophy genes, and downregulation of myostatin (a negative regulator of muscle mass); androgen signalling also promotes satellite-cell (muscle stem cell) activation/commitment to the myogenic lineage [Established for androgens generally; Evidence-rodent/in vitro for LGD-4033 specifically — note PMID 40087185 found NO change in rat gastrocnemius myostatin or IGF-1 gene expression, so the effector profile is not uniform]. Pharmacologically important contrast with testosterone: LGD-4033 is NONAROMATIZABLE (not converted to estradiol) and is not a 5α-reductase substrate in the same way, so its effects are AR-direct without the estrogenic/DHT-amplification arms of testosterone (PMID 24189892).

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Oral (intended clinical route; the original Ligand/Viking development used oral capsules/solution). Illicit products are oral liquids, capsules and powders; a 2025 doping study also documented TRANSDERMAL exposure routes (PMID 40632609) and even contamination via intimate skin-to-skin/seminal transfer (PMID 40338642, 36441233) [Established].
Absorption / bioavailability
Orally active and well absorbed in humans; clinical PK in the pivotal trial showed dose-proportional exposure across 0.1–1.0 mg/day (PMID 22459616) [Evidence-human]. Exact absolute oral bioavailability % is not published in the public human literature [key gap]. Highly lipophilic, low polar surface area (PSA ~47), 0 Lipinski violations (ChEMBL) — consistent with good membrane permeability and likely good oral absorption.
Half-life
LONG elimination half-life with dose-proportional accumulation on once-daily multiple dosing (PMID 22459616) [Evidence-human]. The commonly cited figure is ~24–36 h (supporting once-daily dosing); the pivotal RCT explicitly reported a 'long elimination half-life' but the precise hours are not stated in the abstract [Established that it is long; exact value Contested/under-reported in public sources].
Metabolism
Hepatic. Phase I: hydroxylation, dihydroxylation, epimerization and oxidative cleavage of the pyrrolidine ring (yielding pyrrole/dicarboxylic-acid metabolites); Phase II: glucuronidation. Multiple long-term metabolites characterized for anti-doping (e.g., M1, M2c, M5a/M5b, M8) with detection windows up to ~weeks; M2c is an active AR agonist and M8 a pyrrole AR antagonist (PMIDs 30255601, 31984662, 38183391, 41367083, 41261783). Specific CYP isoenzymes are not definitively pinned in the public human data, though CYP-mediated oxidation is the operative chemistry (liver microsome/seminal-vesicle fraction studies, PMID 40710553) [Evidence-human / in vitro].
Excretion
Renal (urinary) excretion of phase I and glucuronidated phase II metabolites is the basis of WADA urine detection; parent and metabolites also detectable in plasma, seminal fluid and hair (PMIDs 27168428, 34734312, 36441233, 33337588) [Established].
Notes
Not a prodrug (ChEMBL prodrug=0) and not an ester — unlike injectable AAS, LGD-4033 needs no esterification or depot. Nonaromatizable (no conversion to estrogen). Small lipophilic molecule expected to cross the blood–brain barrier (consistent with reported CNS/mood effects), though BBB penetration is not formally quantified in humans [Speculative for BBB]. Long half-life means suppression of the HPG axis persists days after the last dose.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

HUMAN (the only controlled human dose-response data): Basaria 2013 RCT (PMID 22459616), 76 healthy men, placebo vs 0.1, 0.3, 1.0 mg/day for 21 days. Lean body mass increased DOSE-DEPENDENTLY (significant at the group level over just 3 weeks); fat mass unchanged. Hormonal suppression was dose-dependent: total testosterone, SHBG, HDL-cholesterol and triglycerides fell across doses; FSH and FREE testosterone fell significantly ONLY at the top 1.0 mg dose. PSA, hemoglobin, ALT/AST and QT did not change significantly over 21 days, and all hormone/lipid changes reversed after discontinuation. So even ~1 mg/day measurably suppresses the HPG axis while building lean mass. CRITICAL GAP: there is NO published human dose-response above 1.0 mg/day and none beyond ~3 weeks for body composition; the Phase 2 hip-fracture trial (VK5211/NCT02578095, n=108) tested higher doses (0.5/1.0/2.0 mg) over 12 weeks but full peer-reviewed dose-response results are not in PubMed. Real-world recreational use (commonly ~5–10 mg/day per user reports) sits FAR ABOVE any dose ever studied in a controlled human trial — the efficacy/safety of those doses is essentially unstudied [human dose-response above 1 mg = UNKNOWN]. RODENT: dose-dependent effects are clearer at mg/kg doses. In ovariectomized rats, muscle/bone benefits and uterotrophic (androgenic) side effects appeared mainly at the high 3–4 mg/kg dose, with 0.03–0.4 mg/kg largely inert on bone (PMIDs 37407738, 33042018). In male rats, 0.4 mg/kg REDUCED endurance/VO2max and worsened lipids/testosterone — i.e., net-negative at that dose (PMID 40087185). Bottom line: low single-milligram human doses are anabolic and androgenically suppressive; everything above that is extrapolation.

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Testosterone (and injectable anabolic-androgenic steroids)
    same: Both act through the SAME androgen receptor to drive muscle protein synthesis, increase lean mass and suppress the HPG axis (lower LH/FSH/endogenous testosterone).
    different: Testosterone is a STEROID, aromatizes to estradiol and is 5α-reduced to DHT (driving estrogenic and strong prostate/hair effects), and is typically injected/transdermal. LGD-4033 is NONSTEROIDAL, NONAROMATIZABLE, orally active, and tissue-selective (much weaker prostate/skin/hair stimulation at low doses) — but is far less studied, has no long-term human safety data, and carries its own idiosyncratic cholestatic DILI signal.
  • vs Ostarine / Enobosarm (MK-2866, GTx-024)
    same: Both nonsteroidal AR-agonist SARMs, orally active, used illicitly for lean-mass gain; both cause HPG suppression and cholestatic DILI case reports.
    different: Enobosarm is the most clinically advanced SARM (Phase 3 POWER1/POWER2 in cancer cachexia) and is generally considered milder/less suppressive per dose; LGD-4033 binds AR with higher apparent potency (Ki ~1 nM) and is regarded anecdotally as more strongly suppressive/anabolic per mg. In a head-to-head OVX-rat study both improved muscle but with different metabolic fingerprints (PMID 33042018).
  • vs RAD-140 (Testolone)
    same: Both potent nonsteroidal AR-agonist SARMs marketed for hypertrophy; both implicated in serious cholestatic drug-induced liver injury (often sold together in 'stacks').
    different: RAD-140 has an even stronger reputation for suppression and hepatotoxicity, including DILI from RAD-140 alone and RAD-140+LGD-4033 combination products (PMID 36561105, 33062783); RAD-140 has less human PK characterization than LGD-4033's published Phase 1.
  • vs MK-677 (Ibutamoren) — frequently co-used, but NOT a SARM
    same: Commonly stacked with LGD-4033 for body recomposition and discussed together in PED communities.
    different: MK-677 is a GHSR (ghrelin-receptor) agonist / growth-hormone secretagogue — it raises GH/IGF-1 and works through a completely different receptor and pathway; it does NOT bind the androgen receptor and does not suppress testosterone the way LGD-4033 does (PMID 36303408 case report of the combination).

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Anastrozole adds to a side effect ⚠ Contested
    Anastrozole lowers estradiol by inhibiting aromatase (CYP19A1). Ligandrol does NOT aromatize, so anastrozole has NO substrate from the SARM to act on and cannot address any Ligandrol side effect — it is pharmacologically inert toward Ligandrol's androgenic actions (key teaching point: using an AI 'to be safe' on a Ligandrol-only exposure is misguided). Where the two DO genuinely interact is lipids/cardiovascular risk in the same direction: Ligandrol already suppresses HDL, and crashing estradiol with an AI independently worsens the lipid profile (lower HDL, higher LDL, and adverse bone/joint/libido/mood effects from low E2). So an AI stacked onto Ligandrol compounds the HDL/lipid harm for no offsetting benefit. The AI only becomes relevant to estrogen control if Ligandrol is stacked with an aromatizing steroid, in which case it acts on that steroid's estrogen, not the SARM. anastrozole.json (aromatase/CYP19A1 inhibitor, lowers estradiol; low E2 harms lipids/bone); ligandrol-lgd-4033.json mechanism_step 5 / key_effects (no aromatization; HDL suppression); PMID 18398147, 42292016 (estrogen-modulation/AI lowers HDL / worsens lipids in men and women) Watch: Fasting lipid panel — expect HDL to fall from LGD and to worsen further with an AI (rising LDL, falling HDL); estradiol (do not suppress it against a non-aromatizing SARM); low-E2 symptoms (joint pain, low libido, low mood) signal an unnecessary/over-aggressive AI.
  • Andarine adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-rodent
    Both are AR agonists that act at the pituitary/hypothalamus to mimic androgen negative feedback, lowering LH and FSH and therefore endogenous testosterone. Stacking a second suppressive androgen deepens and prolongs the shutdown of natural testosterone production rather than sparing it; 'tissue-selective' does not mean 'non-suppressive.' andarine-s-4.json (mechanism_step 5: agonist at pituitary lowers LH/FSH, PMID 16099859; Evidence-human for SARM class, PMID 30503797); ligandrol-lgd-4033.json (HPG suppression) Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone and SHBG to gauge depth of suppression; in men watch for low libido, testicular shrinkage and mood change. Human recovery time after stopping is not well characterized.
  • Methyltestosterone adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both suppress HDL cholesterol through overlapping AR-driven hepatic mechanisms. Methyltestosterone (oral 17aa) sharply induces hepatic lipase, dropping HDL ~35-40% and raising LDL; LGD-4033 similarly lowers HDL via AR activation altering hepatic lipase/lipoprotein metabolism in the liver. Co-use compounds the atherogenic HDL drop beyond either alone. Both also suppress the HPG axis, deepening endogenous shutdown. methyltestosterone monograph (hepatic lipase HDL crash); Friedl 1990 methyltestosterone HDL crash + hepatic lipase +102% PMID 2294373; ligandrol monograph side_effect_mechanisms (AR-driven hepatic lipase HDL lowering, RCT) Watch: Fasting lipid panel (HDL, LDL, ApoB) to track the compounded HDL suppression; LH/FSH and testosterone for axis suppression. A collapsing HDL is a signal to seek evaluation.
  • RAD-140 adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both are non-steroidal AR-agonist SARMs, so together they load the SAME androgen receptor and stack the same three off-target harms: (1) deeper hypothalamic/pituitary negative feedback = greater HPTA/testosterone shutdown; (2) additive HDL-cholesterol suppression via AR-driven changes in hepatic lipase/lipoprotein metabolism (a pro-atherogenic lipid shift both compounds produce alone); and (3) overlapping idiosyncratic cholestatic drug-induced liver injury — both carry repeated real-world DILI case reports, including combination LGD+RAD-140 products. Because RAD-140 has an even stronger reputation for suppression/hepatotoxicity, co-exposure raises the ceiling on all three. PMID 22459616 (LGD HDL/HPTA suppression, RCT); PMID 36561105, 33062783 (RAD-140 and RAD-140+LGD DILI case reports); rad-140-testolone.json; ligandrol-lgd-4033.json Watch: Fasting lipid panel (watch HDL falling); total/free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG for axis suppression; liver panel with special attention to total/direct BILIRUBIN plus ALT/AST/ALP/GGT, and signs of cholestasis (jaundice, dark urine, itching). Any jaundice or rising bilirubin warrants stopping and urgent medical review.
  • S-23 adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-rodent
    S-23 is a near-full-agonist SARM with very high AR affinity and one of the strongest HPTA-suppression reputations in the class (it has been studied as a male-contraceptive candidate precisely because it shuts down gonadotropins in rodents). Combined with Ligandrol at the same androgen receptor, the dominant compounded harm is profound suppression of LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone — a deeper, potentially slower-to-recover secondary hypogonadism than either alone, plus additive HDL suppression common to the SARM class. s-23.json (near-full AR agonist, sub-nM affinity, strong suppression, male-contraceptive candidate — rodent); PMID 22459616 (LGD suppression, human) Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone, SHBG (expect marked suppression); fasting HDL/lipids; symptoms of hypogonadism and, in women, virilization. Confirm post-exposure axis recovery with repeat gonadotropin/testosterone bloodwork.
  • YK-11 adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-rodent
    YK-11 is a steroidal (19-nor-derived) SARM that also drives the androgen receptor, so co-use with Ligandrol overlaps at AR and compounds the shared androgenic harms: additive HPTA suppression and additive HDL-cholesterol suppression. YK-11's steroidal backbone and plausible (but human-unquantified) hepatic strain add to Ligandrol's own cholestatic DILI signal, so the liver is a shared organ of concern even though the two reach AR by different chemotypes (YK-11 also acts via a follistatin/myostatin arm that Ligandrol does not clearly share). yk-11.json (steroidal SARM, AR partial agonist, follistatin/myostatin; HDL/HPG suppression and cholestatic DILI framed as class-level/plausible, human hepatotoxicity a stated data gap); ligandrol-lgd-4033.json (HDL suppression, cholestatic DILI, HPTA suppression) Watch: Liver panel with bilirubin (cholestatic pattern) plus ALT/AST/ALP/GGT; fasting HDL/lipids; total/free testosterone, LH, FSH for axis suppression.
  • Amycretin counteracts ↓ Speculative
    High-efficacy incretin weight loss like amycretin's drives a fraction of lost weight from lean/muscle mass because profound appetite suppression cuts protein intake (in amycretin's case this lean-mass loss is inferred from the magnitude of intake reduction, not yet quantified in humans). Ligandrol is a SARM that activates the muscle androgen receptor and is discussed in the literature as a candidate to preserve/rebuild lean mass lost on GLP-1 agonists, so it would oppose amycretin's lean-mass-loss side effect (not its target). No human study of this specific combination exists. amycretin.json (lean-mass loss inferred from intake reduction, amycretin-specific quantification Speculative); ligandrol.json (AR agonist, increases lean body mass); PMID 40739991 (SARMs proposed for GLP-1-induced lean-body-mass loss); PMID 41022269 (GLP-1 RA sarcopenia risk) Watch: Track body composition (not just scale weight) to gauge lean-mass preservation; monitor lipid panel/HDL and liver enzymes, since SARMs suppress HDL and are linked to cholestatic liver injury.
  • Clomiphene citrate counteracts ↓ Established
    Ligandrol suppresses the HPTA by mimicking androgen feedback at the hypothalamus/pituitary, driving down LH, FSH and endogenous testosterone. Clomiphene is a SERM that BLOCKS estrogen-receptor-alpha feedback in the hypothalamus, which the brain reads as low estrogen and responds to by INCREASING GnRH-driven LH/FSH output — pushing the testes to resume testosterone production. Mechanistically it opposes/counteracts the axis shutdown Ligandrol causes; this is the rationale for using a SERM to restart the axis after SARM exposure. It counteracts the suppression, not the AR effect itself, and works only once the suppressing compound is cleared. clomiphene-citrate-clomid.json (ERalpha antagonist in hypothalamus, raises LH/FSH/T, raises E2); PMID 35783920 (agents to restart suppressed HPGA); PMID 22459616 (LGD suppresses LH/FSH/T) Watch: LH, FSH, total testosterone and estradiol to confirm axis recovery; clomiphene raises E2, so watch for estrogenic effects and mood/visual disturbance. Estrogen is needed for LH signalling, so do not layer an aromatase inhibitor blindly during recovery.
  • Enclomiphene counteracts ↓ Established
    Enclomiphene is the pure trans-isomer SERM that antagonises estrogen-receptor-alpha on hypothalamic feedback neurons, raising GnRH and thus LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone. It directly opposes the secondary hypogonadism Ligandrol induces through androgenic negative feedback — the intended counteracting mechanism for HPTA recovery — while (unlike clomiphene) largely avoiding the estrogenic zuclomiphene isomer. It counteracts the suppression only; it does nothing to the AR-mediated effects and only helps once Ligandrol is cleared enough for feedback to lift. enclomiphene-enclomiphene-citrate-trans-.json (ERalpha antagonist, raises LH/FSH/T); PMID 35783920; PMID 22459616 (LGD gonadotropin/T suppression) Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone to confirm the axis is re-firing; estradiol (rises with restored T). Symptomatic recovery of libido/energy alongside bloodwork.
  • Ostarine shares target Evidence-human
    Two non-steroidal SARMs competing for and co-activating the single androgen receptor — there is only one AR, so their anabolic effects are not truly independent but overlapping occupancy of the same pocket (Ligandrol binds with high apparent potency, Ki ~1 nM). Beyond redundancy at target, both independently suppress the HPTA and lower HDL, and both carry cholestatic DILI case reports, so combining them compounds axis suppression, the lipid hit, and liver risk rather than adding a distinct new mechanism. PMID 22459616 (LGD suppression/HDL); ostarine-mk-2866-enobosarm-gtx-024.json (AR agonist, HPTA suppression, HDL drop, cholestatic DILI) Watch: Total/free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG (axis suppression); fasting HDL/lipids; liver panel emphasising bilirubin (cholestatic pattern). Watch for symptoms of low testosterone (low libido, fatigue, testicular shrinkage).
  • Testosterone overlaps (redundant) Established
    Testosterone and Ligandrol act through the same androgen receptor, so adding Ligandrol on top of testosterone is largely redundant AR agonism with additive HPTA suppression. The key mechanistic contrast for teaching: testosterone is aromatized to estradiol and 5-alpha-reduced to DHT, giving it estrogenic (gyno, water) and DHT-driven (prostate, scalp) arms; Ligandrol is NON-aromatizable and not a 5AR substrate, so it adds AR drive WITHOUT adding estrogen or DHT. Net effect of the pair is more AR signalling and deeper gonadotropin/testosterone suppression, but the estrogen/DHT side-effect profile is driven entirely by the testosterone component. testosterone-trt.json (AR agonist; aromatizes to E2; 5AR to DHT); ligandrol-lgd-4033.json mechanism_steps 5-6 (non-aromatizable, not 5AR substrate, suppresses HPTA); PMID 19357508 Watch: LH/FSH and total/free testosterone (deeper axis suppression); estradiol and hematocrit (driven by the testosterone, not the LGD); lipids/HDL; liver panel (bilirubin) for the LGD-side DILI signal.

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

"ORIGIN: LGD-4033 (ligandrol; also coded VK5211) is a nonsteroidal SARM discovered by Ligand Pharmaceuticals and advanced by Viking Therapeutics, which licensed it. The first-in-human Phase 1 RCT was published in 2013 (Basaria et al., PMID 22459616); Viking ran a Phase 2 trial in acute hip-fracture patients (NCT02578095, completed 2017). DEVELOPMENT STATUS: Never approved for any indication anywhere — ChEMBL lists max clinical phase 2 (CHEMBL5170587). It remains an investigational compound; no FDA/EMA approval as a drug. REGULATORY/LEGAL: Prohibited in sport by the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) — SARMs are banned at all times under category S1.2 (other anabolic agents); LGD-4033 was explicitly on the WADA Prohibited List from 2018 and produced numerous adverse analytical findings (62 AAFs in 2019 alone, PMID 34734312). In the USA, the FDA has issued warnings that SARMs are NOT dietary supplements and are illegal to include in supplements/sell for human consumption; products are sold illicitly online as 'research chemicals.' SARM-contaminated dietary supplements are a documented source of inadvertent positive doping tests. It is widely available over the counter/online despite this, which is exactly why DILI case reports keep appearing in healthy recreational users (PMID 39421081, 32637435)."

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • Human dose-response and safety ABOVE 1 mg/day and beyond ~3 weeks are essentially unstudied — yet typical recreational doses (~5–10 mg/day for weeks-to-months) sit far above any controlled human exposure. The benefit/risk at real-world doses is unknown.
  • The mechanism and predictors of idiosyncratic cholestatic liver injury are unresolved: why only some users develop severe DILI (bilirubin up to 30× ULN) while the controlled RCT showed no transaminase change. Which transporters (BSEP/MRP2) or immune mechanisms are involved, and whether it is dose-dependent or truly idiosyncratic.
  • The true molecular basis of 'tissue selectivity' for LGD-4033 specifically — whether selective coactivator recruitment fully explains the muscle-vs-prostate dissociation, and how completely that selectivity holds at supratherapeutic human doses (rodent data show androgenic/uterotrophic effects re-emerge at high dose).
  • Precise human pharmacokinetic constants are under-reported: exact elimination half-life (only 'long' is published), absolute oral bioavailability, and the specific CYP isoenzymes responsible for metabolism.
  • Long-term cardiovascular consequences of the chronic HDL suppression, and whether HPG-axis recovery (testosterone, FSH, fertility/spermatogenesis) is reliably complete after extended high-dose use, are unknown — the only reversibility data come from a 21-day exposure.
  • Activity of metabolites in vivo: a human metabolite (M8) is a potent AR ANTAGONIST and another (M2c) a potent agonist in vitro — their net contribution to efficacy, side effects, and the duration of receptor effects in real users is unquantified.

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (NOT a protocol, no dosing): LIVER — this is the headline organ. Watch for jaundice (yellow eyes/skin), dark urine, pale stools, itching (pruritus), right-upper-quadrant pain, nausea and unexplained fatigue; objectively, a liver panel (ALT, AST, ALP, GGT and especially TOTAL/DIRECT BILIRUBIN) is the key bloodwork — the documented injury is cholestatic, so bilirubin can be dramatically elevated even when ALT/AST are only mildly up. Any jaundice or rising bilirubin warrants stopping and urgent medical review. HORMONES — total and free testosterone, LH, FSH and SHBG to detect HPG-axis suppression; symptoms of low testosterone (low libido, erectile dysfunction, low mood, fatigue, testicular shrinkage). For women, watch for virilization (voice deepening, hair changes, menstrual disruption) — some of which can be permanent. LIPIDS/CARDIOVASCULAR — a fasting lipid panel; expect HDL-cholesterol to fall (pro-atherogenic), plus blood pressure. MOOD/CNS — monitor for mood changes, irritability or other psychiatric symptoms reported with off-label use. PRODUCT INTEGRITY — illicit SARMs are frequently mislabeled, underdosed, overdosed, or adulterated with other SARMs/steroids, so observed effects and harms may not reflect the label. General signs to escalate immediately: any jaundice, severe abdominal pain, or signs of liver failure (confusion, easy bruising). This is monitoring guidance for harm reduction only — it is not an endorsement of use, and ligandrol is an unapproved, WADA-banned investigational drug."

Caveats

Harm-reduction and honesty notes: (1) 'Tissue-selective' is real in rodents and short-term human markers but is RELATIVE, not a guarantee of a side-effect-free 'clean' steroid alternative — the marketing oversells it. (2) Even the controlled 21-day trial showed it suppresses your own testosterone and tanks HDL ('good') cholesterol — a cardiovascular red flag that wouldn't show up in a 3-week study but matters over months. (3) Multiple published case reports tie Ligandrol to liver injury in real-world users; the controlled trial did not see this, pointing to longer/higher dosing and contaminated grey-market product as drivers. (4) There is essentially no long-term human safety data — anyone claiming to know its multi-year risk profile is guessing. (5) Black-market 'SARM' products are frequently mislabelled, under/over-dosed, or spiked with actual anabolic steroids, so the bottle often isn't what the label says. This package explains how the molecule works; it is not advice to use it, and there is no established safe protocol.
