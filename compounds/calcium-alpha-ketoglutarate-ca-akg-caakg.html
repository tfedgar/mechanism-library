Calcium Alpha-Ketoglutarate

Main target: No single receptor. Human evidence is still limited.

EvidenceLimited human data
ApprovalNot FDA-approved

A TCA-cycle metabolite salt sold as a longevity supplement: strong rodent healthspan data and one eye-catching but uncontrolled human epigenetic-age study, now being tested in real RCTs — promising, but the human evidence is still thin and the hype has run ahead of the data.

Human status
Sold as a dietary supplement, not an approved drug — most visibly as Rejuvant (delayed-release Ca-AKG plus vitamins A and D). Direct human evidence for anti-aging effects is early and low-quality: the headline ~8-year epigenetic-age reduction (PMID 34847066) is a small (n=42), retrospective, single-arm, vitamin-confounded analysis by parties tied to the product; a larger 2026 cross-sectional cohort (PMID 42166733, n=4260) found a much smaller ~1.8-year signal, only for the delayed-release formulation, and still observational (healthy-user/recruitment bias). No completed placebo-controlled RCT has yet confirmed an epigenetic-age or clinical benefit. Several RCTs are running or recently completed (e.g., Singapore ABLE trial NCT05706389, Ca-AKG vs placebo, DNAm primary endpoint, ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING; Rejuvant safety/biomarker RCT NCT04821401, COMPLETED; a China Ca-AKG RCT NCT07114536, ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING; Stanford ocular open-label study NCT07269704, COMPLETED; CABG geroprotection RCT NCT07031128, RECRUITING), so higher-quality human data are coming. Best summary: mechanistically plausible, strong in rodents for healthspan, but the human anti-aging claims are hype-forward and not yet proven; rigorous mouse lifespan testing (ITP, PMID 41843349) was negative.

At a glance

  • Rodent: reduced frailty, improved healthspan, and modest median-lifespan gain when started late in life (one lab)
  • Rodent: lower chronic inflammation (higher IL-10, lower pro-inflammatory cytokines)
  • Human (uncontrolled): reported reduction in DNA-methylation 'biological age' — ~8 yr in the small retrospective study, ~1.8 yr in the larger cohort, delayed-release formulation only
  • Mechanistic: supplies co-substrate for TET/JmjC epigenetic demethylases; proposed partial ATP-synthase/mTOR inhibition mimicking dietary restriction (worms/cells; mammalian mTOR direction is unsettled)
  • Nitrogen buffering / amino-acid precursor; explored for exercise, bone, wound healing, ammonia handling
  • NOT established: robust lifespan extension (failed the rigorous multi-site mouse ITP), and no completed placebo-controlled human RCT confirming an epigenetic-age benefit yet

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

8 steps3 human / established 5 rodent / preclinical

  1. α-KG is a central TCA-cycle node and dioxygenase co-substrate Established

    Alpha-ketoglutarate (2-oxoglutarate) sits at the heart of the citric-acid cycle, linking sugar, fat and amino-acid metabolism. Beyond making energy, it is the required co-substrate that a whole family of enzymes (the 2-oxoglutarate-dependent dioxygenases) consume to do their jobs. This is textbook, established biochemistry — the supplement question is whether swallowing extra α-KG meaningfully changes any of this in people. PMID 34952764 (Gyanwali 2022, Trends Endocrinol Metab, review)

  2. Fuels TET and JmjC demethylases -> epigenetic remodeling Evidence-in vitro

    TET enzymes (which demethylate DNA) and JmjC/KDM enzymes (which demethylate histones) use α-KG as a co-substrate and release succinate. When α-KG is abundant relative to succinate/fumarate/2-HG, these enzymes run faster, nudging the cell toward a 'younger', more demethylated epigenetic state. This is the proposed link to 'epigenetic age' clocks. The enzyme chemistry is established; that oral Ca-AKG shifts these marks in adult human tissue is inferred, not directly shown. PMID 27476976 (TeSlaa 2016, Cell Metab); PMID 34563253 (Sekita 2021, Stem Cell Res Ther)

  3. May bind and partially inhibit ATP synthase, dampening TOR (worms/cells) Evidence-in vitro

    In worms and cultured mammalian cells, α-KG was reported to physically bind ATP synthase (complex V) and partially throttle it, lowering ATP, cutting oxygen consumption, reducing mTOR signaling and switching on autophagy — the classic 'α-KG mimics fasting' story. Important caveat: this was demonstrated in C. elegans and cell lines, and mammalian tissue data are mixed — dietary AKG has actually *activated* mTOR in skeletal muscle in a piglet model, so the direction of the mTOR effect is not settled in mammals, let alone aging humans. PMID 24828042 (Chin 2014, Nature, C. elegans + mammalian cells); contrast PMID 27385764 (Yao 2016, J Nutr, AKG activated mTOR in piglet muscle)

  4. Lowers chronic inflammation ('inflammaging') Evidence-rodent

    In aged mice, dietary CaAKG shifted immune signaling — notably raising the anti-inflammatory cytokine IL-10 and lowering systemic pro-inflammatory cytokines. Reducing this smoldering age-related inflammation is a plausible route to the healthspan benefits seen. Demonstrated in mice; human anti-inflammatory data are limited and preliminary. PMID 32877690 (Asadi Shahmirzadi 2020, Cell Metab)

  5. Compresses morbidity and modestly extends lifespan in mice Evidence-rodent

    When started in late-middle-aged C57BL/6 mice, CaAKG reduced frailty (better fur, gait, grip, activity) and compressed the sick period at end of life. Median lifespan rose modestly, with a larger, clearer effect on healthspan than on raw longevity. This is the strongest whole-animal evidence — but it is one lab, one mouse strain, and mostly a healthspan (not dramatic lifespan) story. PMID 32877690 (Asadi Shahmirzadi 2020, Cell Metab)

  6. Human study: apparent reduction in epigenetic (DNAm) age Evidence-human

    A retrospective, single-arm analysis of 42 people taking the Rejuvant formulation (delayed-release Ca-AKG + vitamins A/D) for ~7 months reported an average ~8-year drop in DNA-methylation 'biological age' (p=6.5e-12). It is genuinely intriguing but has no placebo group, no randomization, self-selected users, and mixes AKG with vitamins — so it cannot prove causation and the effect size is likely inflated. PMID 34847066 (Demidenko 2021, Aging (Albany NY), retrospective, uncontrolled, n=42)

  7. Larger real-world cohort: smaller, formulation-specific signal Evidence-human

    A 2026 cross-sectional cohort of 4,260 'health enthusiasts' found the delayed-release Ca-AKG+vitamin product ('Rejuvant') associated with ~1.8 years lower epigenetic age residual — far smaller than the 8 years first claimed, and only for the delayed-release formulation; plain AKG showed a small, non-significant effect. Consistent direction, much more modest magnitude, still observational (healthy-user/recruitment bias). PMID 42166733 (Pabis 2026, Aging Cell, cross-sectional, n=4260)

  8. Reality check: no lifespan extension in the rigorous mouse ITP Evidence-rodent

    The multi-site NIA Interventions Testing Program — the gold standard for aging drugs — tested alpha-ketoglutarate in genetically diverse UM-HET3 mice and found no significant lifespan extension in either sex. This tempers the strongest claims: healthspan/epigenetic effects may be real, but robust lifespan extension has not been replicated under the most rigorous conditions. PMID 41843349 (Korstanje/Miller 2026, GeroScience, ITP)

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. Alpha-ketoglutarate is not a receptor ligand but an enzyme co-substrate/allosteric modulator. It is the obligate 2-oxoglutarate co-substrate for the Fe(II)/2-OG dioxygenase superfamily: TET1-3 (5-methylcytosine hydroxylases, DNA demethylation), JmjC-domain KDM histone demethylases, and prolyl/asparaginyl hydroxylases (PHD1-3/FIH) that regulate HIF-1a stability, plus collagen prolyl-4-hydroxylase. These enzymes consume α-KG + O2 and produce succinate + CO2; competing metabolites succinate, fumarate and 2-hydroxyglutarate inhibit them, so the α-KG:succinate ratio is the functional lever. Separately, in C. elegans and cultured cells α-KG was reported to bind ATP synthase subunit beta (identified by DARTS) and partially inhibit complex V, reducing ATP and mTOR signaling; mammalian mTOR data are mixed. It is also a nitrogen acceptor (via glutamate dehydrogenase/transaminases) buffering ammonia.

Signalling cascade. Oral Ca-AKG -> intracellular α-KG (partly) -> (a) raises α-KG:succinate ratio -> faster TET/JmjC demethylation -> epigenetic remodeling toward a younger methylation profile; (b) in models, partial ATP-synthase inhibition -> lower ATP/O2 use -> altered mTOR + increased autophagy (proposed dietary-restriction mimetic; direction not settled in mammals); (c) immunomodulation -> higher IL-10, lower pro-inflammatory cytokines -> reduced inflammaging -> reduced frailty and compressed morbidity in mice.

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Oral (capsule/tablet); as calcium salt or delayed-release calcium salt (Rejuvant). AKG salts (e.g., ornithine-alpha-ketoglutarate) are also used parenterally in surgical/clinical-nutrition contexts.
Absorption / bioavailability
Rapidly and extensively metabolized in gut/liver; α-KG is avidly taken up and fed into the TCA cycle and transamination, so systemic bioavailability of intact α-KG after oral dosing is low and poorly quantified in humans. Endogenous plasma levels are low micromolar and rise with fasting/exercise. 'Delayed-release' formulations are marketed to improve delivery, and the human cohort signal was specific to the delayed-release product.
Half-life
Not well defined for oral supplementation in humans; endogenous α-KG turns over rapidly (minutes) as a core metabolite.
Metabolism
TCA-cycle oxidation to succinyl-CoA (via alpha-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase complex) and transamination to glutamate/glutamine; enters amino-acid and nitrogen metabolism. Not a xenobiotic — handled by core intermediary metabolism, not primarily CYP450.
Excretion
Metabolic disposal (CO2 via TCA oxidation, incorporation into amino acids). Small amounts of unchanged α-KG excreted renally in urine; urinary α-KG shifts with diet.
Notes
The calcium counter-ion contributes a non-trivial elemental calcium load (order of ~200 mg elemental Ca per 1 g Ca-AKG, formulation-dependent) — relevant to total daily calcium intake.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

No established anti-aging dose in humans and none provided here. The rodent healthspan study used dietary CaAKG (translation to humans is not direct). Human observational signals attach to specific commercial delayed-release Ca-AKG+vitamin products, and the reported epigenetic-age effect did not scale simply — plain AKG showed a smaller/non-significant signal than the delayed-release formulation, suggesting delivery/formulation matters more than raw dose. Running RCTs (e.g., ABLE NCT05706389, which uses 1 g/day Ca-AKG vs placebo) are designed to test whether it yields a real, placebo-controlled epigenetic-age or functional benefit. Harm-reduction framing only — not a protocol.

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Rapamycin
    same: Both are framed around reduced mTOR signaling and increased autophagy as a longevity mechanism
    different: Rapamycin has robust, replicated lifespan extension in the ITP across labs; Ca-AKG's mTOR effect is indirect (via ATP synthase in models), unsettled in mammals, and it failed to extend lifespan in the ITP
  • vs Metformin / dietary restriction mimetics
    same: Framed as a 'fasting mimetic'; endogenous α-KG rises with fasting and its lifespan effect overlaps with dietary restriction in worms
    different: α-KG is an endogenous metabolite/nutrient rather than a xenobiotic drug; DR-mimetic effect largely shown in invertebrates/cells, not confirmed in aging humans
  • vs NAD+ boosters (NMN/NR)
    same: Both are metabolite-based 'geroprotector' supplements marketed on surrogate biomarkers with heavy consumer hype ahead of hard clinical outcomes
    different: Different target: α-KG feeds epigenetic demethylases and TCA/mTOR; NAD+ precursors feed sirtuins/redox. Neither has proven human lifespan/clinical-outcome benefit
  • vs Plain (non-calcium / non-delayed-release) AKG
    same: Same active metabolite
    different: The human epigenetic-age signal attached specifically to delayed-release Ca-AKG+vitamin formulations; plain AKG showed a smaller, non-significant effect — formulation/delivery appears to matter

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

Alpha-ketoglutarate is a long-known TCA-cycle intermediate; its salts have been used clinically for decades (e.g., ornithine-alpha-ketoglutarate in surgical/burn nutrition and hyperammonemia; α-KG in cardioplegia and dialysis-related contexts). The longevity narrative was catalyzed by Chin et al. 2014 (Nature, C. elegans/ATP synthase) and Asadi Shahmirzadi et al. 2020 (Cell Metab, mouse healthspan). Ca-AKG is marketed in the US as a dietary supplement (DSHEA), not an FDA-approved drug; Rejuvant (Ponce de Leon Health) is the flagship delayed-release Ca-AKG+vitamins A/D product, popularized by the 2021 uncontrolled DNAm-age paper. It is not an approved therapeutic for aging anywhere; anti-aging claims are not FDA-evaluated. Multiple academic RCTs (Singapore/NUS, Stanford ocular, surgical geroprotection) are underway to generate controlled human data.

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • Whether oral Ca-AKG raises intracellular α-KG in adult human tissues enough to shift TET/JmjC activity in vivo
  • Whether the reported DNAm-age reductions reflect a real, causal, health-relevant change or artifacts of uncontrolled design, healthy-user bias, and the co-administered vitamins
  • Whether any human hard outcome (function, disease incidence, mortality) improves — currently only surrogate epigenetic markers
  • Which way mTOR actually moves with AKG in mammalian tissue (worm/cell data show inhibition; a piglet-muscle study showed mTOR activation) — the 'fasting-mimetic' mechanism is not settled outside invertebrates/cells
  • Why rigorous mouse lifespan testing (ITP) was negative while the single-lab mouse study was positive — dose, timing, strain, formulation?
  • Optimal formulation/delivery (delayed-release vs plain) and long-term safety of the chronic calcium load
  • Whether epigenetic-clock changes generalize across clocks/tissues or are clock-specific

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Harm-reduction, not a protocol: be aware that Ca-AKG adds elemental calcium (~200 mg per gram), so account for total daily calcium from all sources and be cautious with a history of kidney stones, hypercalcemia, or vascular calcification; periodic serum calcium and renal function are reasonable to review with a clinician if it is used chronically or stacked with other calcium/vitamin D. Watch for GI intolerance (nausea, loose stools). Recognize that any DNAm 'biological age' test result is a surrogate, prone to noise and marketing overinterpretation — do not treat a clock number as proof of added healthy years. People on other geroprotectors (rapamycin, metformin) or with cancer/proliferative conditions should discuss the theoretical HIF/epigenetic modulation with a clinician. Anyone considering it for a medical condition should seek individualized medical advice rather than self-treating.

Caveats

Human anti-aging evidence is weak and financially conflicted: the flagship study is uncontrolled, retrospective, and industry-linked, mixes Ca-AKG with vitamins A/D (which themselves affect methylation/health), and epigenetic clocks are surrogate markers, not proven to predict how long or how well a given person will actually live. The larger cohort shrank the effect ~4-5x and is still observational. The most rigorous mouse lifespan test (NIA ITP) found no benefit. The 'partial ATP-synthase/mTOR inhibition' mechanism is from C. elegans and cell lines and is not settled in mammals (dietary AKG has activated rather than inhibited mTOR in a piglet-muscle model), and oral α-KG is heavily metabolized on absorption, so how much intact α-KG reaches tissues in humans is genuinely uncertain. The calcium salt adds a meaningful calcium load (~200 mg elemental calcium per gram of Ca-AKG, formulation-dependent), which matters for people already high on calcium or with kidney-stone/vascular-calcification risk. This is not dosing or treatment advice.
