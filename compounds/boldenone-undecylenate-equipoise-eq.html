Boldenone undecylenate

Main target: The androgen receptor. Evidence is animal- and lab-only — no human trials yet.

A veterinary testosterone cousin with one tweaked double bond — it builds muscle and pumps up red blood cells like any androgen, but its "secret estrogen-killer" reputation is mostly gym folklore.

Human status
NOT approved for human use anywhere — boldenone undecylenate is a veterinary drug (Equipoise), licensed historically for horses. There is ZERO controlled human pharmacology: no human trials of its potency, aromatization rate, or safety. Everything 'known' about its human effects comes from (1) extrapolation from the androgen class, (2) rodent toxicology, (3) forensic/anti-doping metabolite studies, and (4) bodybuilding anecdote. At a glance

  • Muscle growth via androgen-receptor signaling (class effect; boldenone-specific potency never measured in humans)
  • Marked rise in red blood cells / hematocrit — EQ's most reliable and clinically important effect
  • Increased appetite is commonly reported anecdotally (no controlled human data)
  • Often lowers measured estradiol on a testosterone base — but via mundane chemistry, not a 'built-in aromatase inhibitor'
  • Suppresses the body's own testosterone production (HPG axis shutdown, class effect)

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

7 steps6 human / established 1 contested / speculative

  1. Slow-release depot Established

    The drug is boldenone bolted to a long fatty 'undecylenate' ester. Injected into muscle, it sits in an oily depot and trickles boldenone into the blood over weeks. The ester is just a timer — it gets snipped off in the body to release the active boldenone. This is the same trick used for testosterone enanthate or cypionate. Class pharmacology of esterified AAS; equine kinetics PMID 3235575

  2. Binds androgen receptor Established

    Free boldenone slips into cells and latches onto the androgen receptor — exactly the receptor testosterone activates. The receptor then travels to the cell nucleus, sits on DNA, and switches on genes. This is the shared engine of every anabolic steroid; boldenone is a full androgen, not a 'selective' designer molecule. AR-mediated AAS action, reviews PMID 41355050; PMID 32311143

  3. Builds muscle Established (androgen class) / Evidence-rodent (boldenone-specific)

    Switched-on androgen receptors crank up muscle protein synthesis, push muscle stem (satellite) cells to add nuclei to fibers, and reduce protein breakdown. Net result: bigger, stronger muscle. Boldenone is reputed to do this slowly and 'lean,' but no controlled human study has ever measured its potency — that profile is gym lore, not data. Testosterone anabolic mechanism PMID 41355050; satellite-cell role PMID 24166993

  4. Pumps red blood cells Established

    This is boldenone's signature effect. Androgens tell the kidney to make more erythropoietin (EPO) and suppress the iron-blocking hormone hepcidin, freeing up iron for the bone marrow. The marrow churns out red cells, so hematocrit (blood thickness) climbs. EQ is notorious for driving hematocrit high. Testosterone EPO/hepcidin RCT PMID 24158761; AR-driven erythropoiesis PMID 32311143; androgen-driven (not estrogen) PMID 19159688

  5. Aromatizes — but weakly Established (slow aromatization) / Contested ('50%' figure refuted)

    Like testosterone, boldenone can be converted by the aromatase enzyme into an estrogen, but its extra A-ring double bond makes it a POOR substrate, so it aromatizes more slowly. The famous '50% of testosterone' figure is unsourced folklore — no peer-reviewed number exists. Δ1 androgens do aromatize, just at reduced rates. Δ1 androgens aromatize slowly PMID 17656084; A-ring effects PMID 18555683

  6. Why E2 often reads low Contested (phenomenon, Low GRADE) / Speculative-to-Refuted (the 'horse estrogen' mechanism)

    Users on a testosterone base often see estradiol (E2) drop after adding EQ. The honest explanation is mundane: boldenone is a weak aromatizer, its metabolism leans toward estrone (E1) rather than potent E2, and any strong androgen turns the aromatase gene down. The viral 'EQ makes a horse estrogen in your brain that destroys aromatase' story is biochemically implausible and contradicted by human metabolite data. There is NO controlled human study — this rests on anecdote (GRADE Low). 17b-HSD E2/E1 equilibrium PMID 15288809; androgen-driven aromatase downregulation PMID 26199450; human metabolites are androgens only PMID 16308875; equilin is horse-specific PMID 164338

  7. Potent androgen metabolite + harms Established (erythrocytosis risk) / Evidence-rodent (organ oxidative damage)

    Boldenone's main reductive metabolite is 1-testosterone (DHB), itself a strong androgen — not an estrogen-blocker. The real costs are the androgen costs: thick blood (high hematocrit) raising clot/stroke/heart-attack risk, suppression of the body's own testosterone, and in animal studies oxidative damage to liver, kidney, testes and heart. Boldenone is NOT 17-alkylated, so it is not classically liver-toxic by that route. Human metabolites PMID 1591280, PMID 23090723; erythrocytosis risk PMID 39212549; rodent cardiotoxicity PMID 30116498; hepatorenal PMID 33986679; testicular PMID 33126548

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. Boldenone (the active drug; the undecylenate ester is a prodrug) is a full agonist of the nuclear androgen receptor (AR), the same receptor testosterone uses. Structurally it is testosterone with an added 1,2 (Δ1) double bond in the A-ring (androsta-1,4-dien-17β-ol-3-one), undecylenate-esterified at the 17β-OH for slow release (ChEMBL CHEMBL2106059; C30H44O3; MW 452.68; ALogP 7.51; USAN stem 'bol-', 1968). NO measured human AR Ki/Kd for boldenone exists in the indexed databases — ChEMBL returns no curated mechanism-of-action record and no AR Ki for this molecule, and classic relative-binding-affinity (RBA) tables in the literature center on testosterone/DHT/R1881 rather than boldenone. So the precise affinity is UNKNOWN [Evidence-in vitro / Established by class for AR agonism; affinity number not located]. By class, AR is a ~919-aa ligand-activated transcription factor; agonist binding to its C-terminal ligand-binding domain (LBD) is the initiating event [Established].

Signalling cascade. (1) The undecylenate ester is cleaved by plasma/tissue esterases to liberate free boldenone [Established — class pharmacology of esterified AAS]. (2) Lipophilic boldenone diffuses into target cells (muscle, marrow stroma, kidney, prostate, sebaceous gland) and binds cytoplasmic AR, displacing heat-shock chaperones (HSP90/HSP70/HSP56) — notably, boldenone exposure UP-regulates AR and HSP90 expression in rat liver/kidney by immunohistochemistry [Evidence-rodent; PMID 33986679]. (3) Ligand-bound AR homodimerizes, is phosphorylated, and translocates to the nucleus. (4) It binds androgen-response elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer DNA and recruits coactivators (e.g., SRC family, p160) to switch on transcription. (5) Anabolic output: increased muscle protein synthesis, satellite-cell activation/myonuclear accretion, and decreased proteolysis → net muscle accrual [Established for androgen class; boldenone-specific potency NEVER measured in humans]. (6) Erythropoietic output (boldenone's signature, established by class): AR signaling raises renal erythropoietin (EPO), suppresses hepatic hepcidin (HAMP) and raises erythroferrone (ERFE), freeing iron for the marrow → rising red-cell mass/hematocrit; this is androgen-driven and estrogen-independent [Established; testosterone RCT PMID 24158761; aromatase-deficient men PMID 19159688]. (7) HPG feedback: AR agonism at the hypothalamus/pituitary suppresses GnRH/LH/FSH → testicular shutdown [Established by class; rodent LH/FSH/testosterone declines PMID 33126548].

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Intramuscular oily depot injection only (veterinary 'Equipoise'); boldenone undecylenate is a 17β-undecylenate ester prodrug. Not formulated or approved for oral or human use. (Forensic human metabolism studies have used ORAL un-esterified boldenone for doping-control purposes — that is a research route, not the clinical EQ product.)
Absorption / bioavailability
Slow, sustained release from the IM oil depot governed by the C11 undecylenate ester; esterases cleave the ester to free boldenone. In horses (1.1 mg/kg IM), median absorption half-life ~8.5 h with Cmax ~1128 pg/mL [Evidence-animal; PMID 17348894]. No human bioavailability data exist [Speculative for humans].
Half-life
Effective half-life is ester- and species-dependent. In horses, plasma elimination half-life of boldenone after IM undecylenate was ~123 h (~5 days) [Evidence-animal; PMID 17348894]. Community/forum figures of a ~14-day 'half-life' are anecdotal and unverified [anecdote, not evidence]. Detection windows are long: boldenone/metabolites are detectable in cattle serum and urine for ≥120 days after IM undecylenate dosing [Evidence-animal; PMID 33970002, PMID 34850583].
Metabolism
Two arms. (a) Reductive/androgenic: free boldenone is reduced (5β-reductase predominating in humans) to 5β-androst-1-en-17β-ol-3-one (the major human urinary metabolite, 'BM1'), plus 5β-androst-1-ene-3α,17β-diol and related 3α-ol/17-one species; >95% excreted as stable glucuronide conjugates [Evidence-human; PMID 1591280, PMID 26319750]. 17β-HSD interconverts boldenone with boldione (ADD); the dominant reductive androgen metabolite is 1-testosterone (DHB), itself a potent androgen [Established]. (b) Oxidative/aromatase arm: boldenone is a POOR aromatase (CYP19A1) substrate because of the A-ring Δ1 bond — Δ1 androgens are aromatized by human aromatase but at reduced rates [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 17656084]. Minor metabolites ADD/ATD are mechanism-based ('suicide') aromatase inactivators in vitro [Established in vitro; supports the parsimonious 'EQ lowers E2' story].
Excretion
Renal, predominantly as glucuronide (and some sulfate) conjugates of the reduced metabolites; >95% of urinary metabolite mass is stable conjugate [Evidence-human; PMID 1591280]. 17β-boldenone sulfo-conjugate is a marker in cattle urine [Evidence-animal; PMID 19409402].
Notes
The undecylenate ester is purely a release timer (prodrug), not pharmacologically active itself. Boldenone is NOT 17α-alkylated, so it lacks the classic oral-17-alkyl hepatotoxic route. Lipophilicity (ALogP ~7.5) implies CNS penetrance by class, but no human brain-distribution data exist. The very long ester explains weeks-long persistence of effects (and suppressed-E2 'EQ hell' in forum reports) after the last dose [Evidence-animal half-life + anecdote].

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

Human dose-response is essentially UNKNOWN — there is ZERO controlled human pharmacology of boldenone undecylenate (no human potency, aromatization-rate, or safety trials). All human-use claims (the '50% of testosterone aromatization' figure, 'EQ lowers E2', 'EQ anxiety', 'lean slow gains') are extrapolation from the androgen class plus bodybuilding anecdote [Speculative/anecdote]. What IS dose-anchored comes from animals: in horses/ponies, IM boldenone undecylenate 1.1 mg/kg (or 0.55 mg/kg weekly) raised plasma testosterone above controls and altered testis histology/gonadotropins [Evidence-animal; PMID 16726297]; in beef bulls 1.1 mg/kg every 21 d raised average daily gain but increased sperm morphological abnormalities [Evidence-animal; PMID 16726958]. Rodent toxicology studies converge on ~5 mg/kg/week IM for 8 weeks producing measurable hepatorenal, testicular, and cardiac oxidative injury [Evidence-rodent; PMID 33986679, PMID 33126548, PMID 30116498]. These animal mg/kg figures do NOT translate to human dose recommendations and no human dose-effect magnitude can be stated.

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Testosterone
    same: Both are full AR agonists driving muscle protein synthesis and erythropoiesis; both are esterified for slow IM release; both suppress the HPG axis and aromatize to estrogen.
    different: Boldenone = testosterone + one A-ring Δ1 double bond. That bond makes it a POORER aromatase substrate (so net E2 often reads lower) and shifts metabolism toward 1-testosterone (DHB) rather than DHT. Testosterone is a licensed human drug with abundant controlled pharmacology; boldenone undecylenate is veterinary-only with ZERO human trials.
  • vs 1-Testosterone / DHB (dihydroboldenone)
    same: DHB is boldenone's major reductive (5α/5-ene) androgen metabolite; both act through AR; DHB carries much of boldenone's downstream androgenic signal.
    different: DHB is non-aromatizable (no estrogenic activity) and a more potent, 'harsher' androgen, whereas boldenone retains slow aromatization. So part of EQ's effect is really DHB acting — DHB is NOT an aromatase inhibitor, contradicting the 'built-in AI' folklore.
  • vs Boldione (ADD, androsta-1,4-diene-3,17-dione) and atamestane
    same: ADD is boldenone's 17-keto oxidative partner (via 17β-HSD); ADD and the drug atamestane (1-methyl-ADD) share boldenone's Δ1,4-dien-3-one A-ring; both ADD/ATD are mechanism-based ('suicide') aromatase inactivators in vitro.
    different: ADD/ATD are minor, low-concentration, largely urinary boldenone metabolites — atamestane is given as a dedicated 100–300 mg/day aromatase-inhibitor drug, so its proven human E2-lowering (PMID 8876703) does NOT prove the trace ADD/ATD from EQ meaningfully suppresses systemic aromatase in users (in-vitro→in-vivo gap).
  • vs Nandrolone (19-nortestosterone)
    same: Both are injectable, esterified, non-17α-alkylated AAS often described as 'lean/slow' gainers with strong erythropoietic effect and HPG suppression; neither is classically hepatotoxic by the 17-alkyl route.
    different: Nandrolone lacks C19 (19-nor) and is reduced by 5α-reductase to the WEAKER androgen DHN, and aromatizes to estradiol; boldenone keeps C19, is a poor aromatizer, and reduces to the STRONGER androgen DHB. Nandrolone has limited human clinical data; boldenone has essentially none.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Anadrol adds to a side effect ⚠ Established
    Both are AR agonists that strongly stimulate red-cell production; the curated erythropoiesis MoA card names boldenone AND oxymetholone specifically among the most potent hematocrit-raising androgens. Combining two of the strongest erythropoietic AAS compounds their effects on red-cell mass add, pushing hematocrit and blood viscosity higher. They differ in that Anadrol is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral carrying a hepatotoxicity route that non-alkylated injectable boldenone lacks. Corpus MoA card 'Erythropoiesis & iron' / 'ISSUE: Polycythemia' both name boldenone + oxymetholone; Anadrol monograph (17aa hepatotoxicity, step 6); EQ monograph step 4 Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin (this pairing is among the most likely to trigger erythrocytosis); blood pressure; signs of hyperviscosity (headache, flushing, visual change). Because Anadrol adds 17-alpha-alkyl hepatotoxicity that boldenone does not carry, also track ALT/AST/GGT/bilirubin.
  • DHB adds to a side effect ⚠ Established
    Both are androgen-receptor agonists that drive erythropoiesis (raise EPO, act on EPOR, suppress hepcidin, raise erythroferrone), so red-cell mass and hematocrit climb from two directions at once. This pathway does NOT require aromatization: aromatase-deficient men still show androgen-driven erythrocytosis (PMID 19159688), so DHB's non-aromatizing nature does not spare it. Boldenone is a classically strong hematocrit-raiser, and DHB is its 5-alpha-reduced sibling (dihydroboldenone) sharing the same delta-1 skeleton, so the two additively push polycythemia. Elevated hematocrit raises blood viscosity and clot/stroke/MI risk. Erythropoiesis & iron MoA card + PMID 24158761 (T raises EPO, lowers hepcidin); PMID 19159688 (erythropoiesis independent of aromatization); DHB = 5-alpha-boldenone per monograph comparison (Friedel 2006, PMID 16621347) Watch: Serial hematocrit/hemoglobin (and ferritin/EPO trend); watch for headaches, hypertension, ruddy complexion, or visual disturbance signaling hyperviscosity.
  • Enclomiphene adds to a side effect ⚠ Speculative
    Both can raise estradiol, so together they can compound estrogenic side-effect risk. Enclomiphene raises endogenous testosterone that is partly aromatized (lifting systemic E2) and does NOT block ER in breast/peripheral tissue, so its central ER antagonism won't neutralize peripheral E2. Boldenone can aromatize to estradiol, adding a second E2 source. Caveat: boldenone's aromatization is weak and its monograph notes it often LOWERS measured E2 on a testosterone base via mundane displacement chemistry, so the additive-estrogen effect is inconsistent rather than guaranteed. A separate, more reliable compounding concern is hematocrit — boldenone robustly raises red-cell mass. (Axis tension also applies: boldenone, an AR agonist, suppresses LH/FSH, opposing enclomiphene's central drive.) Enclomiphene monograph (side_effect_mechanisms: elevated estradiol; peripheral ER not blocked); Boldenone monograph (weak aromatization, 'estrogen-killer' reputation is folklore, often lowers measured E2; robustly raises hematocrit); corpus MoA card 'Estrogen receptors' Watch: Estradiol on a sensitive assay plus breast exam/symptoms (tenderness, swelling) for gynecomastia. Hematocrit/hemoglobin on a full blood count for boldenone-driven erythrocytosis. LH/FSH to see whether the androgen is suppressing the axis enclomiphene is trying to drive.
  • Masteron adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    Boldenone's most reliable clinical effect is a marked rise in red-cell mass/hematocrit, and Masteron adds its own class-androgen erythropoietic drive. Together they compound erythrocytosis and raise blood viscosity/thrombotic risk. Directional difference on estrogen: boldenone aromatizes (adds E2) while Masteron does not, so Masteron's receptor-level AR-vs-ER crosstalk at most partially offsets, and does NOT eliminate, boldenone's estrogenic potential. Masteron monograph monitoring (hematocrit); Boldenone monograph (marked hematocrit rise; aromatizes) Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin and CBC (additive polycythemia); blood pressure; estradiol (boldenone still aromatizes despite Masteron).
  • Nandrolone adds to a side effect ⚠ Established
    Both are injectable, esterified, non-17-alpha-alkylated AR agonists with strong erythropoietic and HPG-suppressive effects and a 'lean/slow' profile. Stacked, they compound erythrocytosis (additive EPO/hepcidin effect) and deepen HPG/testosterone suppression (both long esters mean slow recovery). They differ downstream — nandrolone aromatizes weakly to estradiol and is progestogenic (raises prolactin), while boldenone is a poor aromatizer and not progestogenic — so nandrolone adds a prolactin/progestin dimension EQ lacks. EQ monograph comparisons (vs nandrolone) + erythropoiesis MoA card; nandrolone monograph (weak aromatization, progestogenic/prolactin, 19-nor injectable) Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin (additive erythrocytosis); LH/FSH/testosterone (compounded suppression, slow recovery); prolactin and E2 (from the nandrolone side). Erectile/libido issues can arise from the nandrolone prolactin/progestin axis.
  • Testosterone adds to a side effect ⚠ Established
    Both are AR agonists that drive erythropoiesis by raising renal EPO and suppressing hepcidin (while raising erythroferrone), freeing iron for the marrow. The curated erythropoiesis MoA card names testosterone AND boldenone among the strongest hematocrit-raising androgens, so their erythropoietic signals add and hematocrit can climb faster and higher than with either alone (secondary erythrocytosis). Rising hematocrit thickens blood and raises clot/stroke/MI risk.
  • Anastrozole counteracts ↓ Contested
    Boldenone is aromatizable via CYP19A1, but its A-ring double bond (delta-1) shifts metabolism toward the 17-keto branch (estrone/E1) over estradiol (E2), and the androgen also downregulates aromatase, so its net systemic E2 impact is smaller and more variable than testosterone's (the common 'EQ raises E1, lowers E2' observation). Anastrozole blocks the same aromatase step, so it can lower whatever E2/E1 boldenone does generate, but because boldenone is a comparatively weak and atypical aromatase substrate, the AI's counteracting benefit is smaller and easier to over-shoot into an E2 crash than with testosterone. Note this is unproven in controlled human trials; support is mechanistic plus scaffold analogy (atamestane). boldenone-undecylenate-equipoise-eq.json (aromatizes; 'estrogen-killer' is folklore, key_effects); corpus example equipoise-boldenone-estradiol-mechanism.md (E1>E2 skew via 17b-HSD, AR-driven aromatase downregulation, GRADE Low-Moderate/unproven in trials; PMIDs 15288809, 26199450, 8876703); anastrozole.json mechanism_steps 1-4 Watch: Estradiol AND estrone if available (boldenone skews toward E1), interpreted against symptoms rather than a single number; be especially alert to over-suppression because the estrogen signal from boldenone is modest to begin with. Lipids and, chronically, bone density.
  • Exemestane counteracts ↓ Established
    Boldenone aromatizes, but its extra A-ring double bond makes it a POOR substrate, so it converts to estradiol slowly (the popular 'half the rate of testosterone' figure is unsourced folklore). Exemestane counteracts whatever estrogen boldenone does generate by irreversibly inactivating aromatase, lowering E2 formed from the boldenone substrate. Boldenone monograph mechanism_steps 5 (weak/slow aromatization; '50%' figure refuted, PMID 17656084) and step 4 (erythrocytosis); Exemestane monograph mechanism_steps 4; Aromatase MoA card Watch: Sensitive estradiol to avoid crashing E2; boldenone independently and strongly raises hematocrit (androgen-driven EPO/hepcidin effect), so monitor hematocrit/hemoglobin separately — exemestane does not affect that pathway.
  • Human chorionic gonadotropin counteracts ↓ Established
    Boldenone suppresses the HPG axis (lowers LH/FSH, intratesticular testosterone, spermatogenesis) via AR negative feedback. hCG acts directly on testicular LHCGR (an LH-receptor agonist) to restore intratesticular testosterone and testis volume, counteracting that suppression downstream. Trade-off: hCG stimulation also raises intratesticular aromatase activity, so estradiol can rise even while EQ tends to read low. EQ monograph side_effect_mechanisms (HPG suppression, PMID 33126548); hCG monograph (LHCGR agonist raises intratesticular T + E2 via aromatization) Watch: Testis size/symptoms; LH/FSH (will stay suppressed — hCG bypasses the pituitary rather than restarting it); and estradiol (hCG can raise E2). Watch E2 climb when weighing any AI decision.
  • Letrozole counteracts ↓ Contested
    Boldenone is a Delta-1 androgen that is a POOR aromatase substrate but still converts to some estrogen, so it can nudge E2 (its metabolism leans toward estrone). Letrozole blocks CYP19A1 and can lower that boldenone-derived estradiol. The corpus flags a caveat worth teaching: claims that boldenone 'self-suppresses' E2 like a built-in aromatase inhibitor are structural extrapolation and gym folklore, not demonstrated in humans (there is ZERO controlled human pharmacology of boldenone) — the real, deliberate handle on its E2 is aromatase inhibition. boldenone-undecylenate-equipoise-eq.json step 5-6 (weak Delta-1 aromatization, estrone shift; '50%' figure refuted; no controlled human study, GRADE Low); letrozole.json step 1 Watch: Sensitive serum estradiol (ideally paired with estrone to separate a true aromatase effect from the estrone shift); balance high-E2 signs against AI over-suppression. Boldenone independently raises hematocrit, so also monitor hematocrit/RBC — an issue letrozole does not address and that is unrelated to the E2 interaction.
  • Clomiphene citrate works against Evidence-human
    Boldenone suppresses the HPG axis (lowers LH/FSH and endogenous/intratesticular testosterone) via AR negative feedback. Clomiphene is a SERM that antagonizes estrogen feedback at the hypothalamus/pituitary to raise GnRH -> LH/FSH -> endogenous testosterone, driving the axis in the OPPOSITE direction to boldenone's shutdown (the basis for its use to restart the axis after AAS). A secondary caveat: because clomiphene RAISES estradiol (more testosterone gives aromatase more substrate, plus the zuclomiphene isomer is weakly estrogenic), it also moves estrogen opposite to an aromatase inhibitor, so a SERM and an AI pull estrogen against each other if both are layered on an EQ regimen. Clomiphene monograph (central ER antagonism raises LH/FSH/T, raises E2; PCT restart after AAS); EQ monograph side_effect_mechanisms (HPG suppression, PMID 33126548) Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone (should rise if clomiphene is opposing EQ's suppression) and estradiol (clomiphene pushes E2 up); do not stack a SERM and an AI blindly since they oppose on estrogen. Watch for visual disturbance and mood changes reported with SERMs.
  • Finasteride shares target Speculative
    Boldenone is a 5α-reductase substrate: its reductive metabolite is 1-testosterone (dihydroboldenone/DHB), a MORE potent androgen. Finasteride sits on the same enzyme node and would partially blunt the type-2 fraction of that conversion, lowering local production of the stronger metabolite, directionally analogous to how it lowers DHT from testosterone. This is inference from shared substrate biology only: boldenone's human metabolism actually leans on 5β-reduction, and there is no direct finasteride+boldenone data. Boldenone's separate aromatization to estradiol and its strong hematocrit-raising effect are pathways finasteride does not touch. boldenone monograph mechanism_step 7 + pharmacokinetics (5α/5-ene reduction to potent DHB; 5β-reductase predominates in humans); finasteride 5-AR substrate logic, monograph mechanism_steps 1-3, no direct combination data Watch: Even if finasteride blunts the 5α-reduced fraction, it does not affect boldenone's androgen-receptor drive or its aromatization/hematocrit effects. Watch hematocrit and estradiol independently, since finasteride offers no protection there.
  • Dutasteride overlaps (redundant) Established
    Dutasteride inhibits both 5-AR isoenzymes for near-total (~90-95%) DHT suppression, but boldenone is not a 5-AR substrate and its potent metabolite DHB is not produced by 5-AR, so dutasteride does not blunt boldenone's androgenic action. On an EQ-based regimen it only suppresses DHT from co-run testosterone; boldenone/DHB androgenic side effects proceed unaffected. (Dutasteride's own monograph notes muscle anabolism from testosterone is likewise unaffected by DHT blockade.) EQ monograph (non-5AR substrate; DHB metabolite); dutasteride monograph (dual isoenzyme block; anabolism DHT-independent) Watch: Do not expect dutasteride to mitigate boldenone-driven androgenic hair loss/acne/prostate effects; it acts only on the testosterone-to-DHT arm. Monitor DHT and androgenic symptoms accordingly.

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

"Developed by Ciba in the late 1950s–1960s as boldenone (USAN stem 'bol-' assigned 1968; ChEMBL synonyms BA-29038, NSC-79102, Parenabol, Vebonol, Equipoise). Originally trialed for human use (e.g., as Parenabol) but never gained durable human approval; it survived as the veterinary product Equipoise (boldenone undecylenate) for horses. Today it is NOT approved for human use in any major jurisdiction. In the United States it is a Schedule III controlled substance under the Anabolic Steroids Control Act; in the EU, use of boldenone/boldenone esters as growth promoters in food-producing animals is banned (Directive 96/22/EC), which is why much of the literature is forensic/residue work distinguishing exogenous EQ from endogenously occurring boldenone in cattle. The World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) prohibits boldenone and boldione as exogenous anabolic agents at all times in sport (with a special-handling comment for boldenone's possible endogenous origin). It has no current legitimate human medical indication."

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • What is boldenone's actual human AR binding affinity (Ki/Kd) and relative anabolic:androgenic ratio? No measured human number exists — ChEMBL has no curated AR mechanism/affinity record for it.
  • Does EQ genuinely lower net estradiol in humans, and by which route (poor aromatization vs 17β-HSD shift to estrone vs trace ADD/ATD aromatase inhibition vs immunoassay artifact)? No controlled human study with paired sensitive LC-MS estradiol + estrone before/after EQ has ever been done.
  • What fraction of boldenone is actually converted to its potent androgen metabolite 1-testosterone (DHB) versus the estrogen/aromatase arm in humans, and how much of EQ's clinical effect is really DHB?
  • Do the rodent cardiac (NOX2/NOX4), hepatorenal, and testicular oxidative injuries translate to humans at typical exposures, and are they reversible after the long ester clears?
  • What is the true human elimination half-life and detection window of boldenone undecylenate? Hard PK data are equine; the commonly cited '14-day half-life' is forum lore, not measured in humans.
  • Is the widely reported 'EQ anxiety' a real, boldenone-specific CNS effect, and if so via what mechanism? The viral ERβ 'horse-estrogen' explanation is refuted (and predicts the opposite direction), leaving the phenomenon unexplained.

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only — NOT dosing advice. (1) Hematocrit/hemoglobin and red-cell indices: EQ's signature risk is erythrocytosis; rising hematocrit thickens blood and raises clot/stroke/MI risk — watch for headaches, flushing, visual changes, hypertension; ferritin/EPO trends help characterize it. (2) Blood pressure: track for androgen- and viscosity-driven hypertension. (3) Lipids: full panel — AAS, and boldenone specifically in rodents, push LDL up and HDL down (atherogenic shift). (4) Liver and kidney panels (ALT, AST, bilirubin; creatinine, eGFR, urea, electrolytes): rodent data show oxidative hepatorenal injury even though boldenone is non-17α-alkylated. (5) HPG axis / endogenous testosterone: LH, FSH, total and free testosterone — expect suppression; watch for low-testosterone symptoms and slow recovery given the long ester. (6) Estradiol AND estrone, ideally by sensitive LC-MS (not just immunoassay): EQ commonly distorts measured estrogen; pairing E2 with E1 distinguishes a true aromatase effect from the 17β-HSD/estrone shift and from assay artifact. (7) Cardiac symptoms/markers and, where indicated, echocardiography: rodent NOX-driven cardiac remodeling is a flag for cardiovascular surveillance. (8) Androgenic skin/hair changes and, in women, signs of virilization (voice change, clitoromegaly, hirsutism) — some are irreversible, so early detection matters. (9) Mood/anxiety, given consistent (if unexplained) 'EQ anxiety' reports. Persistence note: because of the very long ester, abnormal values can take many weeks to normalize after cessation."

Caveats

Harm-reduction framing: EQ's standout risk is erythrocytosis — it pushes hematocrit high, thickening the blood and raising the risk of clots, stroke and heart attack (PMID 39212549; MoA-card established). Because of its very long ester, effects (including a suppressed estrogen state, 'EQ hell' in forum reports) persist for weeks after the last dose. Rodent studies show oxidative damage to liver, kidney, testes and heart (PMID 33986679, 33126548, 30116498) — suggestive, not proof of the same in humans. Unlike oral 17-alkylated steroids, boldenone is NOT 17-alpha-alkylated, so it does not carry that specific liver-toxicity route. Two viral mechanisms to debunk for the audience: (1) the '50% of testosterone aromatization' number is folklore with no source; (2) the claim that EQ becomes an equilin-like 'horse estrogen in your brain' that destroys aromatase is biosynthetically implausible in humans and contradicted by human metabolite data, which recover androgens only, no hidden estrogens (PMID 16308875, 164338). The defensible reason E2 can read low is simply that boldenone is a weak aromatizer whose metabolism leans toward estrone over estradiol, plus general androgen-driven aromatase downregulation. This is mechanism education, not medical or dosing advice.
