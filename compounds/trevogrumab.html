Trevogrumab

A Regeneron anti-myostatin antibody being tested to protect muscle while people lose weight on semaglutide — mechanistically sensible, but the human muscle-preservation data are not published yet.

Human status
PIPELINE / PHASE 2 — no approved indication, no published human efficacy data for muscle preservation. Trevogrumab (REGN1033) is a Regeneron investigational anti-myostatin antibody. Its current flagship study is COURAGE (NCT06299098), a randomized, double-blind Phase 2 trial of trevogrumab, with or without the anti-activin-A antibody garetosmab, added to semaglutide (Wegovy) in adults with obesity (n=1005 planned; Part A in healthy volunteers for safety, Parts B/C in obesity). Co-primary endpoints are percent change in total fat mass, total lean mass and body weight (Part B at week 26; Part C at week 52). Sponsor is Regeneron; start March 2024, primary completion ~May 2026, full completion ~Oct 2026; status is Active, not recruiting, with no results posted (verified on ClinicalTrials.gov). Earlier, REGN1033 was characterized preclinically for muscle wasting/sarcopenia but has no approval; historically, myostatin-pathway antibodies have added muscle mass in trials without reliably improving strength or physical function. Bottom line: mechanistically credible and in a well-designed obesity trial, but human muscle-preservation benefit is currently a hypothesis, not an established result.

At a glance

  • In rodents: increased muscle fiber size, muscle mass and grip/force; ~20% muscle-mass gain with post-developmental dosing (Evidence-rodent; PMID 26457176, PMID 27214824)
  • In rodents: protection against disuse-, glucocorticoid- and unloading-induced muscle atrophy, and faster recovery of lost muscle (Evidence-rodent; PMID 26457176)
  • Intended human effect (under test, unpublished): preserve/increase lean mass during semaglutide weight loss while preserving fat loss and total weight loss (Speculative; NCT06299098)
  • Class-level expectation (from the ActRII antibody bimagrumab, NOT trevogrumab): substantial fat-mass loss plus lean-mass GAIN and improved HbA1c — suggesting the pathway can shift body composition, though trevogrumab's own human body-composition data are pending (Evidence-human for bimagrumab; PMID 33439265)
  • Honest caveat: antibody blockade of myostatin in adults is a modest, ceiling-limited effect — not the 'double-muscle knockout' phenotype; and prior myostatin-pathway muscle drugs added mass without reliably improving strength/function (Contested; PMID 39340593)

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Myostatin is the brake on muscle Established

    Myostatin (GDF-8) is a TGF-beta-family protein made mainly by skeletal muscle that acts as a negative regulator, continuously limiting how large muscle can grow. Loss-of-function myostatin mutations cause dramatic muscle overgrowth in cattle, dogs, mice and rare humans; overexpression causes atrophy. This makes it an obvious target for building or protecting muscle. PMID 32911580 (Suh & Lee, J Bone Metab 2020 review), DOI 10.11005/jbm.2020.27.3.151

  2. Trevogrumab binds and neutralizes mature myostatin Established

    Trevogrumab (REGN1033) is a fully human monoclonal antibody that binds the mature, active form of myostatin with high specificity and potency, confirmed by surface-plasmon-resonance (Biacore) binding and cell-based Smad2/3 reporter assays. By coating the circulating growth factor, it stops it from reaching its receptor. PMID 26457176 (Latres et al., Skelet Muscle 2015), DOI 10.1186/s13395-015-0060-8

  3. ActRIIB signaling is interrupted Established

    Free myostatin normally docks onto activin type II receptors (ActRIIB/ActRIIA), which recruit the type I co-receptors ALK4/ALK5 and phosphorylate the intracellular messengers Smad2/3. Neutralizing myostatin lowers the amount of ligand available to trigger this receptor complex, so Smad2/3 activation in muscle falls. (The ActRIIB-ALK4/5 signaling axis in muscle wasting is characterized in cachexia models.) PMID 31285507 (Levolger et al., Sci Rep 2019, ActRIIB-ALK4/5 axis in muscle wasting), DOI 10.1038/s41598-019-46178-9; pathway review PMID 41954678 (Lisco et al., Rev Endocr Metab Disord 2026), DOI 10.1007/s11154-026-10033-w

  4. Reduced Smad2/3 signaling shifts muscle toward growth Evidence-rodent

    Smad2/3 signaling normally suppresses muscle protein synthesis and myoblast differentiation. Taking the brake off tips the balance toward myofibrillar protein synthesis and larger fibers. In mice, REGN1033 increased muscle fiber size, muscle mass and force production. PMID 26457176 (Latres et al., Skelet Muscle 2015), DOI 10.1186/s13395-015-0060-8

  5. Effect is muscle hypertrophy AND protection from atrophy Evidence-rodent

    In mice REGN1033 not only grew healthy muscle but also prevented muscle loss from immobilization, hindlimb unloading and glucocorticoid (dexamethasone) treatment, and improved recovery from pre-existing atrophy and exercise performance in aged mice. This anti-atrophy property is the rationale for pairing it with a weight-loss drug that drives catabolism. PMID 26457176 (Latres et al., Skelet Muscle 2015), DOI 10.1186/s13395-015-0060-8

  6. Post-developmental blockade is far milder than genetic knockout Evidence-rodent

    This is the key honesty point. Genetically myostatin-null mice have roughly TWICE normal muscle mass and broad proteomic remodeling (fiber-type switching). Giving REGN1033 to already-grown mice produced only ~20% more muscle mass with almost no proteomic change (0.7% of proteins altered, near the biological-noise floor). So antibody blockade in adults yields a smaller, qualitatively different effect than the myostatin-knockout physiology often invoked in hype. PMID 27214824 (Salzler et al., Proteomics 2016), DOI 10.1002/pmic.201600006

  7. In the weight-loss context: preserve lean mass, keep fat loss Speculative

    GLP-1 drugs like semaglutide cause large weight loss, but roughly a quarter of the weight lost is lean (muscle-containing) mass. The thesis of trevogrumab-plus-semaglutide is that neutralizing myostatin blunts the muscle-loss component while semaglutide keeps burning fat, improving the quality of weight loss. Whether this translates to meaningful human benefit is being tested but is NOT yet published. PMID 39719170 (Karakasis et al., Metabolism 2025, ~25% lean-mass loss on GLP-1), DOI 10.1016/j.metabol.2024.156113; human trevogrumab+semaglutide efficacy data pending (NCT06299098); pathway/positioning context PMID 41178728 (Diabetes Obes Metab 2026)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Fully human monoclonal antibody (research code REGN1033) that binds mature/active myostatin (GDF-8) with high specificity and potency. Specificity and potency were established by surface-plasmon-resonance (Biacore) binding and cell-based Smad2/3 signaling assays; the antibody is described as a specific, potent myostatin antagonist that does not depend on the broad receptor-blocking approach used by ActRII antibodies. The IgG subclass is not disclosed in the primary publication.

Signalling cascade. Myostatin normally signals via activin type II receptors (ActRIIB/ActRIIA) partnered with type I receptors ALK4/ALK5, driving Smad2/3 phosphorylation, which restrains muscle protein synthesis and myoblast differentiation. By sequestering the free mature ligand, trevogrumab lowers receptor occupancy and Smad2/3 activation, releasing the brake on muscle growth and increasing fiber size, mass and force in preclinical models. Because it targets the ligand rather than the receptor, it should spare receptor signaling by related ligands (e.g., activins, BMPs) more than a receptor-level blocker would — a specificity intended to reduce off-target effects.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Parenteral (injectable monoclonal antibody). In COURAGE it is given as a study injection alongside subcutaneous semaglutide; exact route/schedule are protocol-defined and not disclosed in a public protocol.
Absorption / bioavailability
As an ~150 kDa IgG antibody it is not orally bioavailable; systemic exposure is achieved by injection. No public human bioavailability figures for trevogrumab specifically.
Half-life
Not publicly reported for trevogrumab. Typical human IgG antibodies have half-lives on the order of ~2-4 weeks, consistent with intermittent dosing, but exact values await publication. COURAGE measures serum trevogrumab concentrations over time (up to 75 weeks) as a secondary endpoint.
Metabolism
Like other therapeutic IgG antibodies, expected to be cleared by proteolytic catabolism into peptides/amino acids and by target-mediated disposition (binding to its myostatin target), not by cytochrome-P450 metabolism; low expected risk of small-molecule drug-drug interactions.
Excretion
Expected to be catabolized to peptides/amino acids; negligible renal excretion of intact antibody (too large to be filtered), so unlikely to require renal/hepatic dose considerations of the kind small molecules do.
Notes
COURAGE also tracks total serum GDF-8 (myostatin) over time as a target-engagement pharmacodynamic marker (Part C secondary endpoint), and anti-drug antibody (ADA) incidence/magnitude for both trevogrumab and garetosmab. Human PK/PD numbers should be considered UNKNOWN/pending until trial publication.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

No public human dose-response curve for trevogrumab in the muscle-preservation setting. In mice, chronic REGN1033 dosing increased muscle mass, fiber size and force, and dosing produced a ~20% muscle-mass increase — notably a ceiling far below the ~2-fold mass of genetic myostatin knockouts, indicating post-developmental antibody blockade has an intrinsically limited maximal effect (PMID 26457176; PMID 27214824). COURAGE is designed partly to characterize the human dose/regimen (Parts A to B to C) and includes trevogrumab-alone and trevogrumab+garetosmab arms to probe whether adding activin-A blockade increases the body-composition effect. Any specific human doses are intentionally NOT provided here (harm-reduction: this is not a how-to).

How it compares

  • vs Bimagrumab (anti-ActRII receptor antibody)
    same: Both target the myostatin/activin-ActRII axis to shift body composition toward more muscle and less fat.
    different: Bimagrumab blocks the RECEPTOR (ActRII), so it also blocks activins and related ligands — broader effect (larger lean gain, notable fat loss). It is given by IV infusion. Trevogrumab neutralizes only the LIGAND myostatin — expected to be more selective/cleaner but likely a smaller-magnitude effect. Human body-composition numbers exist for bimagrumab (PMID 33439265) but NOT yet for trevogrumab.
  • vs Apitegromab / GYM329 (pro/latent-myostatin antibodies)
    same: All are myostatin-selective antibodies aiming to spare related TGF-beta ligands.
    different: Apitegromab and GYM329 bind the pro-/latent forms of myostatin (blocking its activation); GYM329 additionally 'sweeps' myostatin from plasma. Trevogrumab binds the already-mature active ligand. These target different points in myostatin's life cycle; apitegromab was developed mainly for neuromuscular disease (SMA) and is also now being tested for lean-mass preservation during incretin-induced weight loss.
  • vs Enobosarm (oral SARM) for GLP-1 muscle preservation
    same: Both are being tested to preserve lean mass during GLP-1 weight loss (a shared clinical goal).
    different: Enobosarm is a small-molecule androgen-receptor modulator (anabolic signaling), not a myostatin antibody; different mechanism, oral route, and different safety profile.
  • vs Semaglutide alone (the comparator context)
    same: Both are used for weight management; trevogrumab is an ADD-ON, not a standalone weight-loss drug.
    different: Semaglutide drives large weight loss but ~25% of it is lean mass (PMID 39719170); trevogrumab has no independent weight-loss effect and is intended purely to protect the muscle quality of that loss.

History & legal status

Trevogrumab originates as Regeneron's REGN1033, first characterized preclinically as a fully human anti-myostatin antibody in 2015-2016 (PMID 26457176; PMID 27214824). It was initially explored for muscle-wasting/sarcopenia contexts but has not reached approval — consistent with the broader myostatin field, where multiple antibodies increased muscle mass in trials without reliably improving strength or function, dampening enthusiasm for muscle-disease and aging indications. The GLP-1/obesity era revived the program: with semaglutide- and tirzepatide-driven weight loss shown to cost a substantial fraction of lean mass, Regeneron repositioned trevogrumab (and the anti-activin-A antibody garetosmab) as add-ons to preserve lean mass, launching the Phase 2 COURAGE trial (NCT06299098) in March 2024. Trevogrumab currently has NO regulatory approval anywhere and no approved indication; it is investigational only. Regulatory status hinges on COURAGE (primary completion ~2026) and any subsequent pivotal program.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Does trevogrumab actually preserve lean mass in humans during semaglutide weight loss, and by how much? (COURAGE co-primary endpoints, unpublished)
  • Does preserved lean mass translate into better STRENGTH, physical function, metabolic rate or long-term outcomes — or just DEXA-measured mass? (historic weak spot of the class)
  • Does adding garetosmab (anti-activin A) meaningfully increase the body-composition effect over trevogrumab alone, and at what safety cost?
  • Human PK: half-life, dosing interval, target-engagement (total GDF-8) dynamics, and ADA impact on efficacy — all currently unpublished
  • Human safety/tolerability profile specific to trevogrumab, including any cardiac, tendon, or off-target activin/BMP-related effects
  • The IgG subclass of trevogrumab (e.g., IgG1 vs IgG4) is not publicly confirmed, which is relevant to effector function and half-life expectations
  • Whether muscle preserved on-drug is retained after stopping, or lost on discontinuation (durability)
  • Effect in the populations excluded from COURAGE (diabetes, neuromuscular disease, HCM) — unknown and untested

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

This is investigational and not prescribable; the following are what a trial/clinician would MONITOR, not a usage protocol and not dosing guidance. Efficacy/body composition: DEXA-measured lean mass, fat mass and body weight (COURAGE's co-primaries), plus waist circumference and, ideally, functional measures (strength, gait/physical performance) since mass gain does not guarantee function. Target engagement/PK: serum trevogrumab levels and total serum GDF-8 (myostatin) over time. Immunogenicity: anti-drug antibodies. Safety labs/vitals per protocol: standard antibody safety monitoring, injection-site and hypersensitivity reactions, blood pressure (trial excludes poorly controlled hypertension), ECG/cardiac screening (trial excludes hypertrophic cardiomyopathy and ECG-defined LVH as a precaution), and lipid/metabolic panel (triglycerides, LDL-C, ApoB, total cholesterol are tracked as trial endpoints). Because it is combined with semaglutide, GLP-1-related GI tolerability and the usual GLP-1 monitoring also apply. No dosing, titration or 'how to run it' guidance is provided — by design.

Caveats

Evidence-tier honesty: The direct human efficacy data for trevogrumab preserving muscle during weight loss are NOT yet published — the mechanism steps are Established/Evidence-rodent, but the muscle-preservation-in-humans claim is Speculative pending COURAGE (NCT06299098). Do not conflate trevogrumab with bimagrumab: the '-20.5% fat, +3.6% lean, -0.76 HbA1c' numbers come from bimagrumab (an ActRII-receptor antibody given by IV infusion, PMID 33439265), a different drug hitting the receptor rather than the ligand; those figures cannot be assumed for trevogrumab. IgG subclass: the draft's claim that trevogrumab is specifically an 'IgG4' antibody is NOT supported by the primary publication (Latres 2015 describes it only as a fully human myostatin-blocking monoclonal antibody) and is not publicly confirmed — do not assert a specific subclass. Hype-vs-data: adult antibody blockade produces a modest ~20% muscle gain with minimal muscle remodeling — nothing like the ~2x 'double-muscled' knockout phenotype (PMID 27214824), and across the myostatin field, added mass has repeatedly failed to translate into strength or functional gains (PMID 39340593). Ligand specificity matters for side effects: broader activin/GDF/BMP inhibitors have caused off-target effects; a myostatin-selective antibody is expected to be cleaner, but this is inference, not proven for trevogrumab. Beware low-quality social-media claims (e.g., unsourced '51% of muscle loss preserved' figures circulating on X) — these are not corroborated by any published trial result. This is investigational: not approved, not available by prescription, and the trial explicitly excludes people with diabetes, prior/planned bariatric surgery, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy or ECG-defined LVH, poorly controlled hypertension, or neuromuscular disease.
