Epitalon

A four-amino-acid pineal-gland peptide that, in a dish, switches the telomerase gene back on and re-lengthens telomeres — sold as an "anti-aging" injectable, but with almost no real human evidence behind the hype.

Rodent / preclinical 22 citations
Class
Synthetic short peptide / "peptide bioregulator" (geroprotector)
Primary target
No classic cell-surface receptor
Human status
PRECLINICAL / unproven in humans. Not approved by the FDA, EMA, or any Western regulator; sold only as a grey-market 'research peptide,' typically as a self-injected powder of unverified purity. The strongest mechanistic data (telomerase reactivation, telomere lengthening) come entirely from cell-culture and rodent work. The most-cited 'human' result — the St. Petersburg/Kiev cohort reporting ~1.6-2.5x lower mortality in elderly people over 6-8 years (PMID 14523363, indexed as a clinical/randomized trial) — used Epithalamin, the bovine pineal EXTRACT, not synthetic Epitalon, was run by the same group that invented the compound (Khavinson/Morozov), and lacks modern blinding, placebo control, and independent replication. There are essentially no rigorous, independent, placebo-controlled human trials of injected synthetic Epitalon for any anti-aging outcome. Human safety data are minimal.

At a glance

  • Re-activates telomerase (hTERT) and lengthens telomeres in cultured human cells (in vitro)
  • Acts as a broad epigenetic gene regulator — turns on neuron/development genes in stem cells (in vitro)
  • Modulates the pineal gland and melatonin/circadian rhythm, mainly under stress (rodent)
  • Antioxidant: lowers free-radical damage markers in animal tissue (rodent)
  • Extends average lifespan and reduces spontaneous tumors in flies and rodents (rodent, largely one research group)
  • Marketed for sleep, 'longevity,' skin/anti-aging and immune support — these consumer claims are NOT backed by solid human trials

Mechanism, step by step

  1. A tiny peptide gets inside the cell and touches DNA Evidence-in vitro

    Epitalon is just four amino acids (Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly). Because it's so small and charged, it can slip into the cell and even the nucleus, where molecular-modeling and binding studies suggest it sticks to specific short DNA sequences and to histone proteins (the spools DNA wraps around) — behaving like a mini transcription-factor that helps pry chromatin open at certain genes. PMID 32019204 (Khavinson 2020, Molecules — histone H1/3, H1/6 binding by modeling); PMID 40141333 (review)

  2. It turns the telomerase gene (hTERT) back on Evidence-in vitro

    Most adult body cells keep their telomerase gene switched off, so their chromosome end-caps (telomeres) shorten a little with every division. When Epitalon is added to human cells in culture, it raises hTERT mRNA and restores telomerase enzyme activity — essentially re-activating a gene the cell had silenced. PMID 12937682 (Khavinson 2003, telomerase-negative human fibroblasts); PMID 40908429 (Al-Dulaimi 2025, Biogerontology — dose-dependent hTERT/telomerase up in normal cells)

  3. Telomeres get re-lengthened Evidence-in vitro

    With telomerase switched back on, the cells rebuild their telomeres — the protective DNA caps grow longer instead of shorter. A 2025 study saw dose-dependent telomere lengthening in normal human cells; in cancer cell lines the lengthening also happened via a backup mechanism called ALT (Alternative Lengthening of Telomeres). Longer telomeres are a lab marker of 'younger' cells, but lengthening telomeres is not the same as proving a person lives longer or healthier. PMID 40908429 (Al-Dulaimi 2025); PMID 12937682 (Khavinson 2003)

  4. It nudges genes for brain/nerve and pineal function Evidence-in vitro

    Beyond telomerase, Epitalon changes the activity of other genes. In human stem cells it raised neuron-differentiation markers (Nestin, GAP43, beta-Tubulin III, Doublecortin), and in animals it alters gene expression in heart and brain tissue — fitting the idea that it acts as a broad 'epigenetic' tuner of which genes are read, not a single-target drug. PMID 32019204 (Khavinson 2020); PMID 12360356 (gene-expression microarray, mouse heart)

  5. It influences the pineal gland and melatonin Evidence-rodent

    Because it was derived from a pineal extract, Epitalon is studied as a pineal regulator. In animals it modulates melatonin output and pineal secretion, but notably mostly under stress rather than in the normal resting state — suggesting it normalizes a disturbed rhythm rather than simply boosting melatonin across the board. PMID 12500171 (Sibarov 2002, stress-exposed rat pineal); PMID 40141333 (review of melatonin effects)

  6. Antioxidant / cell-protective effects Evidence-rodent

    Epitalon and its parent extract Epithalamin reduce markers of oxidative stress (free-radical damage to proteins and lipids) in animal tissues and protect cells from damage — one proposed route for its 'geroprotective' (aging-slowing) claims, alongside the telomere story. PMID 11335874 (Epithalamin, free-radical processes); PMID 23330089 (rat cortex/hippocampus, hypoxia)

  7. Claimed downstream payoff: slower aging, fewer tumors, longer life — mostly in flies/rodents Evidence-rodent

    In invertebrates and rodents, the pineal peptides extended average lifespan and lowered spontaneous tumor rates in several experiments. This is the headline 'anti-aging' result — but almost all of it comes from one Russian research group, in animals, and has not been independently reproduced at scale or convincingly shown in humans. PMID 9701766 (lifespan in flies/mice/rats); PMID 14501183 (SHR mice lifespan/tumors); PMID 12459848 (her-2/neu mice, breast tumors)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Epitalon = the linear synthetic tetrapeptide Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly (AEDG; H-Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly-OH), MW ~390 Da, derived from the amino-acid composition of the bovine pineal extract Epithalamin and later confirmed as a genuine constituent of the pineal polypeptide complex by LC-MS selective-reaction monitoring (Established for identity; PMID 29124531). CRITICAL: Epitalon has NO known cell-surface receptor, no classic agonist/antagonist target, and NO measured Ki/EC50/IC50 — there is no ChEMBL bioactivity record (confirmed: ChEMBL compound_search returned 0 hits this run). The proposed primary 'target' is intracellular DNA/chromatin itself. The signature mechanistic claim is direct sequence-specific minor/major-groove DNA binding: an in-silico complementary-binding model (Khavinson, Shataeva, Chernova) proposes AEDG recognizes the base block ATTTTC, which the authors report recurs in the promoter region of the telomerase (TERT) gene — i.e. the peptide is hypothesized to act 'like a transcription factor' to de-repress TERT (Evidence-in silico / Speculative; PMID 15990728). A second binding modality, supported by molecular-modeling, is histone binding: AEDG is modeled to preferentially bind linker histones H1/3 and H1/6 at His/Pro/Ser/Tyr/Arg/Lys-rich sites that contact DNA, providing an epigenetic (chromatin-decondensation) route rather than a sequence-specific one (Evidence-in silico; PMID 32019204). Affinities for either DNA or histone binding have NOT been quantified by biophysical methods (SPR/ITC/Kd absent) — the 2025 review explicitly states physico-chemical and structural data on the peptide remain 'quite limited' (PMID 40141333). Bottom line on binding: a small acidic tetrapeptide with NO validated high-affinity target and NO affinity constants; 'telomerase activator' is a downstream functional readout, not a measured receptor interaction.

Signalling cascade. Proposed cascade (label each step's certainty): (1) AEDG enters the cell and nucleus (mechanism of uptake undefined for a charged tetrapeptide — Speculative) → (2a) binds telomerase/TERT promoter DNA and/or (2b) binds linker histones H1, decondensing 'old' heterochromatin (Evidence-in silico + ex-vivo cytogenetics: differential scanning calorimetry/NOR-activation in lymphocytes of 75-88 yr olds shows deheterochromatinization; PMID 37042594) → (3) increased hTERT mRNA transcription and telomerase enzyme activity (Evidence-in vitro, human cells; PMID 40908429) → (4) telomere elongation; in normal cells via telomerase, and notably in cancer lines partly via telomerase-independent ALT (Alternative Lengthening of Telomeres) (Evidence-in vitro; PMID 40908429) → (5) broader transcriptional effects: AEDG raises neuronal-differentiation genes (Nestin, GAP43, βTubulin III, Doublecortin) 1.6-1.8x in human stem cells (Evidence-in vitro; PMID 32019204), alters IL-2 mRNA and thymocyte mitogenic activity, and modulates AChE/BuChE/telomerase enzyme activities (Evidence-rodent/in vitro, summarized; PMID 40141333). PARALLEL neuroendocrine arm: AEDG/Epithalamin restores pineal melatonin synthesis and normalizes circadian clock genes (Clock, Csnk1e down ~1.9-2.1x; Cry2 up ~2x) and increases urinary 6-sulfatoxymelatonin ~1.7x in middle-aged people with low melatonin (Evidence-human, small; PMID 33280326). ANTIOXIDANT arm (often attributed to Epithalamin, the parent extract, not pure AEDG): raises SOD/catalase, lowers lipid-peroxidation markers (Evidence-rodent + small human; PMID 11335874). The telomere/melatonin/antioxidant arms are not mechanistically unified; the field treats them as 'specific and nonspecific' effects (PMID 40141333).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
No oral/enteral data of value — as a tetrapeptide it is expected to be digested by gut peptidases, so human use is parenteral (subcutaneous injection) or intranasal in animal studies. Rodent work used s.c. injection (e.g. 1 µg/mouse) and intranasal instillation (PMID 17955380 intranasal → neocortical neuron activity changes; PMID 18856211 s.c. rats).
Absorption / bioavailability
UNKNOWN / unmeasured. There is no published human or animal bioavailability figure, no Cmax/Tmax, no AUC. The 2025 review explicitly flags the scarcity of physico-chemical/structural data (PMID 40141333). Any oral bioavailability is presumed near-zero due to proteolysis (mechanistic inference, not measured).
Half-life
UNKNOWN — no validated plasma half-life is published. Small linear peptides of this size are typically cleared within minutes by serum and tissue exo/endopeptidases, so an ultrashort half-life (order of minutes) is the mechanistic expectation, but NO direct PK measurement for AEDG exists. Treat any specific half-life figure circulating online as unsourced.
Metabolism
Presumed hydrolysis to free amino acids (Ala, Glu, Asp, Gly) by ubiquitous amino/carboxypeptidases and endopeptidases; the constituent amino acids enter normal metabolism. No specific CYP or named-enzyme pathway is relevant (peptide, not xenobiotic small molecule). No metabolite-ID study located.
Excretion
Not directly studied for AEDG. Constituent amino acids follow endogenous amino-acid handling; any intact peptide fraction would be expected to undergo renal filtration/peptidase degradation. No excretion-balance data published.
Notes
No ester/prodrug forms are characterized in the literature (vendor 'modified'/acetylated/amidated variants are not peer-reviewed). BBB penetration of an intact charged tetrapeptide is unproven; CNS effects after intranasal dosing (PMID 17955380) may reflect nose-to-brain or local routes rather than systemic BBB crossing. KEY HONEST CAVEAT: Epitalon is one of the most-discussed 'longevity peptides' yet has essentially NO formal PK package — the human-data gap here is severe and is the single biggest unknown for anyone using it.

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

Dose-response is poorly quantified and species-dependent; NO human dose-response curve exists for any hard outcome. RODENT lifespan/oncostatic work used very low absolute doses on intermittent schedules: 1.0 µg/mouse (~30-40 µg/kg) s.c. on 5 consecutive days each month produced a ~12-13% increase in maximum lifespan and reduced leukemia incidence in SHR mice, with NO change in mean lifespan and no effect on body weight/food intake (Evidence-rodent; PMID 14501183). In HER-2/neu transgenic mice, 1 µg/mouse for 5 days/month reduced cumulative mammary-tumor number/size and downregulated HER-2/neu mRNA ~3.7-fold (Evidence-rodent; PMID 12209581). In rats, 0.1 µg daily (5x/week) extended maximum lifespan only under disrupted light regimes, with little effect under standard light — i.e. effect size was context-dependent (Evidence-rodent; PMID 18856211). IN-VITRO telomere data are explicitly dose-dependent: epitalon produced dose-dependent telomere elongation and hTERT/telomerase upregulation in normal human cells (Evidence-in vitro; PMID 40908429). HUMAN signal: in PHA-stimulated lymphocytes from 11 men, AEDG incubation changed telomere length in 7/11 individuals — INCREASING length where baseline was short (by 18-156%) but DECREASING it where baseline was long, i.e. a 'normalization' rather than monotonic dose-response, in only 5/11 with a significant increase and 2/11 with a decrease (Evidence-human, very small n, no dose curve; PMID 31761987). For melatonin/circadian endpoints, AEDG raised urinary 6-SOMT ~1.7x in middle-aged people (Evidence-human, small; PMID 33280326). Explicit gap: human injectable doses, dose-effect thresholds, and ceiling effects for telomere/longevity outcomes are UNKNOWN; do not infer a human protocol from µg/kg mouse numbers.

How it compares

  • vs Epithalamin (parent pineal peptide extract)
    same: Epitalon (AEDG) was synthesized FROM Epithalamin's amino-acid composition and is a genuine constituent of the pineal polypeptide complex (PMID 29124531); both raise melatonin, act as antioxidants/geroprotectors, and most famous human 'longevity/mortality' data (the 266-person cohort) actually used Epithalamin, not pure AEDG.
    different: Epithalamin is a heterogeneous bovine-derived peptide MIXTURE (animal-sourced, batch-variable); Epitalon is a single defined synthetic tetrapeptide. Attributing Epithalamin's clinical results to pure Epitalon is an inferential leap the user should know about.
  • vs Thymalin (thymic peptide bioregulator)
    same: Same Khavinson 'bioregulator' class and same flagship 266-person trial; combined Thymalin+Epithalamin gave the largest mortality reduction; both claimed geroprotective.
    different: Thymalin is thymus-derived and primarily immune-restorative; Epitalon is pineal/telomere/circadian-oriented. Different source tissue and different proposed primary axis.
  • vs TA-65 (cycloastragenol, plant-derived telomerase activator)
    same: Both marketed as telomerase activators aiming to lengthen/maintain telomeres for 'anti-aging'; both share the same theoretical telomerase-vs-cancer safety tension.
    different: TA-65 is an orally available small-molecule triterpenoid (not a peptide, no injection); it has more commercial human telomere data but is also controversial. Epitalon is an injectable peptide acting via proposed direct DNA/chromatin binding rather than a small-molecule signaling route.
  • vs Melatonin
    same: Mechanistically overlapping downstream endpoints — Epitalon's circadian/antioxidant effects are largely mediated by RESTORING endogenous melatonin synthesis; both improve circadian gene expression and oxidative markers.
    different: Melatonin is an endogenous hormone with extensive human PK/safety data and oral bioavailability; Epitalon is an unapproved research peptide with essentially no PK package. Much of Epitalon's 'benefit' may simply be upstream melatonin restoration.

History &amp; legal status

Origin: Developed by Prof. Vladimir Khavinson and colleagues at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology (Russia), emerging from Soviet-era 'peptide bioregulator' research begun in the 1970s on the bovine pineal extract Epithalamin. Epitalon (AEDG) was designed as the synthetic tetrapeptide matching Epithalamin's amino-acid signature; it was later confirmed by mass spectrometry to be a natural component of the pineal polypeptide complex (PMID 29124531). The overwhelming majority of evidence originates from this single Russian research lineage (Khavinson/Anisimov), which is a notable independence/conflict-of-interest limitation. Regulatory status: Epitalon is NOT approved by the FDA, EMA, or MHRA as a drug for any indication; it has no marketing authorization anywhere as a defined synthetic-peptide pharmaceutical. (Epithalamin, the parent extract, has historical use within the Russian/CIS bioregulator framework.) It has NO ChEMBL drug record (confirmed this run). In Western markets it is sold gray-market as a 'research chemical / not for human consumption' and is not a dietary supplement under FDA rules; in sport, injectable peptides of this type fall under anti-doping scrutiny and unapproved-substance prohibitions. There is no recognized pharmacopeial monograph, so product identity/purity is unverified.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Does Epitalon actually bind the human TERT promoter or histone H1 in vivo, with what affinity? The DNA/chromatin-binding mechanism is entirely in-silico/modeled — no biophysical Kd, no ChIP confirmation in living human cells (PMID 15990728, 32019204).
  • What is the human pharmacokinetics? There is no published bioavailability, half-life, metabolism, or excretion data for AEDG — a glaring gap for a compound in widespread self-injection use (PMID 40141333).
  • Net cancer risk: in vitro it activates telomerase AND ALT in cancer lines (PMID 40908429), yet rodent studies show REDUCED spontaneous tumors (PMID 14501183, 12209581). The true human oncogenic-vs-oncostatic balance is unresolved.
  • Do the famous human mortality benefits belong to Epitalon or to the heterogeneous parent extract Epithalamin? The 266-person trial used Epithalamin, and nearly all human data come from one group with no independent replication (PMID 14523363).
  • Is telomere 'normalization' (lengthening short / shortening long telomeres, PMID 31761987) a real homeostatic effect or assay noise in tiny samples — and does it translate to any clinical endpoint?
  • Long-term human safety, reproductive toxicity, and behavior in people with active or occult malignancy are completely uncharacterized.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Harm-reduction monitoring only (NOT a protocol, and no dosing implied): (1) Because the headline theoretical risk is telomerase/ALT-driven tumor promotion, anyone considering this should have age-appropriate cancer screening current and AVOID use entirely with any known or suspected active/prior malignancy — discuss with an oncologist. (2) Baseline and periodic CBC with differential (the rodent data center on hematologic/leukemia outcomes; watch for unexplained cytopenias or persistent lymphocytosis). (3) Given immune-modulating signals (IL-2, IFN-γ, thymocytes), watch for new autoimmune-type symptoms, persistent fevers, or recurrent infections. (4) Circadian/melatonin effects — track sleep quality and any new sleep-phase disruption. (5) Injection-site monitoring: redness, swelling, induration, abscess, or systemic signs of infection from non-sterile research-grade product; product sourcing/sterility is the most concrete real hazard. (6) If used despite the gaps, third-party purity/identity testing of the vial matters because there is no pharmacopeial standard. Overarching: there is no validated biomarker proving benefit (telomere-length testing is noisy and not clinically actionable here), so 'monitoring' is primarily for harm detection, not efficacy confirmation. This is informational harm-reduction, not medical advice or endorsement of use.

Caveats

MASSIVE human-data gap: telomerase/telomere effects are in vitro; lifespan/anti-tumor effects are in animals. Treat 'it lengthens your telomeres and reverses aging' as a lab finding extrapolated by marketers, not a proven human benefit. SOURCE BIAS: a large share of the entire literature — including the human cohort — originates from one group (Vladimir Khavinson and the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology), which has a commercial/intellectual stake; independent replication is sparse, though a 2025 UK group (Brunel, PMID 40908429) did reproduce telomerase/telomere effects in cells. The famous 'Kiev study' studied the EXTRACT (Epithalamin), not the synthetic tetrapeptide. THEORETICAL CANCER RISK: anything that reactivates telomerase raises a legitimate concern about helping pre-cancerous cells survive — the same 2025 study showed Epitalon also drove telomere lengthening in cancer cells via ALT; animal studies paradoxically report fewer tumors, so the net effect is genuinely unsettled. PRODUCT QUALITY: grey-market peptides vary in purity, dose, and sterility, adding real-world risk independent of the molecule itself. Oral forms are likely degraded in the gut. Mechanism details (exact DNA/histone binding) rest on modeling and in-vitro work, not definitive structural proof. Retraction check (live): PMIDs 12937682, 14523363, and 40908429 are NOT retracted and carry no expression of concern as of this run.
