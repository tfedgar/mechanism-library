S-40503

A prototype bone-selective SARM from Kaken: in rats it built cortical bone as strongly as DHT while sparing the prostate — but it never generated published human efficacy data.

Rodent / preclinical 7 citations
Class
Nonsteroidal selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM), tetrahydroquinoline (cyclopenta[c]quinoline / THQ) chemotype
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR, NR3C4) — tissue-selective agonist with a bone- and muscle-anabolic, prostate-sparing profile
Human status
Preclinical / research compound only. All published efficacy and mechanistic data are in rats (plus in-vitro binding work). S-40503 was developed by Kaken Pharmaceutical (Japan) as an osteoporosis lead and is frequently cited as a prototype bone-selective SARM, but there is no published evidence of completed human efficacy trials, no registered trials on ClinicalTrials.gov, no approval anywhere, and no published human pharmacokinetic or safety data. It is documented in the anti-doping literature — Thevis et al. developed a plasma detection method describing it as being at an early stage of clinical trials (PMID 19280612) — which indicates human-relevant interest and possible early human exposure, but does NOT constitute published clinical efficacy or safety evidence and cannot be verified against any trial registry. Like all SARMs it is a WADA-prohibited substance (SARMs added to the Prohibited List in 2008) and is not an approved medicine; any presence in consumer 'SARM' products would be unregulated and unverified.

At a glance

  • Increased femoral and tibial cortical bone mineral density in orchiectomized and ovariectomized rats — comparable to DHT (rodent)
  • Increased periosteal mineral apposition rate = direct new bone formation, an anabolic rather than purely anti-resorptive action (rodent)
  • Increased cortical bone biomechanical strength where estrogen did not (rodent)
  • Increased levator ani muscle weight comparable to DHT (rodent) — muscle anabolism
  • No prostate enlargement at any dose tested, versus ~1.5-fold enlargement with DHT (rodent) — the defining tissue-selectivity
  • Bone effect preserved in immobilized rats independent of muscle — direct drug-on-bone action (rodent)
  • No published human efficacy, safety, or pharmacokinetic outcome data — human profile is essentially unknown
  • Banned by WADA as part of the SARM class; has appeared in doping-control / black-market surveillance

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Selective binding to the androgen receptor Evidence-in vitro

    S-40503 is a small nonsteroidal molecule that slots into the ligand-binding pocket of the androgen receptor (AR). In receptor-binding assays it bound AR with nanomolar affinity and showed clear preference for AR over other nuclear receptors it was screened against — so its actions are expected to run mainly through AR rather than estrogen/progesterone/glucocorticoid signaling. PMID 14600402 (Hanada 2003, Biol Pharm Bull)

  2. Ligand locks the receptor in its 'on' shape Evidence-in vitro

    For the related Kaken cyclopenta-quinoline (CN-THQ) series, crystallography and docking showed the ligand forces a large conformational change at Trp741 and forms a hydrogen bond to helix 12 of the AR, which is what pins the receptor into its active (agonist) shape rather than an inactive/antagonist shape. This is the structural basis for the scaffold behaving as an AR agonist. Shown for the optimized long-tail THQ lead compound in the same chemical family as S-40503, not with S-40503 itself. PMID 24273094 (Nagata 2014, ChemMedChem)

  3. Activated AR turns on androgen-responsive genes Evidence-in vitro

    The activated receptor moves to DNA and switches on androgen-responsive genes — the same transcriptional program natural androgens like DHT use to drive tissue growth. For this THQ chemotype, strong AR agonist activity was confirmed in AR transcriptional (reporter/cell-based) assays. This step is inferred for S-40503 from its AR-agonist pharmacology plus the shared scaffold; it was not directly reported gene-by-gene for S-40503. PMID 24273094 (Nagata 2014); mechanism generalized from PMID 18801930 (Narayanan 2008)

  4. Tissue selectivity from differential coregulator/pathway use Contested

    Why the same receptor builds bone and muscle but spares the prostate is the central SARM puzzle. The prevailing explanation — worked out for a different nonsteroidal SARM (the aryl-propionamide S-22), not for S-40503 — is that SARM-bound AR adopts a slightly different surface than DHT-bound AR, recruiting a different mix of coactivator/corepressor proteins and engaging both genomic and rapid nongenomic (kinase) signaling differently across tissues. The net result is proposed to be a strong anabolic signal in bone/muscle and a weak one in prostate. For S-40503 this is an unproven mechanism applied purely by analogy; it has not been demonstrated for this compound. PMID 18801930 (Narayanan 2008, Mol Endocrinol, S-22)

  5. Direct anabolic action on bone-forming cells Evidence-rodent

    In orchiectomized and ovariectomized rats S-40503 raised femoral bone mineral density and increased the periosteal mineral apposition rate — a direct readout of osteoblasts laying down new bone on the outer bone surface. Critically, in immobilized (non-loaded) orchiectomized rats it still increased tibial cortical BMD without changing surrounding muscle, showing the bone effect is a direct drug-on-bone action, not just a secondary consequence of bigger muscles. PMID 14600402 (Hanada 2003)

  6. Anabolic in muscle, near-silent in prostate Evidence-rodent

    At doses that increased levator ani (a pelvic/'anabolic' muscle) as much as DHT, S-40503 did NOT enlarge the prostate at any dose tested, whereas DHT raised prostate weight ~1.5-fold. Treating normal rats also produced no prostate enlargement. This muscle-and-bone-yes / prostate-no split is the defining SARM feature and the whole point of the class. PMID 14600402 (Hanada 2003)

  7. Bone-building, not just anti-resorptive Evidence-rodent

    Unlike estrogen (an anti-resorptive that mainly stops bone loss), S-40503 increased both bone density AND biomechanical strength of cortical bone in ovariectomized rats, and estrogen did not increase strength in the same experiment. This positions the compound as an anabolic (bone-forming) agent, closer in behavior to teriparatide than to bisphosphonates — the same 'increases bone size/content' signature later reported for its sister THQ SARM S-101479. PMID 14600402 (Hanada 2003); corroborated by PMID 22791158 (Furuya 2012, S-101479)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Direct, selective agonist ligand of the androgen receptor (AR/NR3C4). In competitive nuclear-receptor binding assays S-40503 bound AR with nanomolar affinity and preferentially over other nuclear receptors screened, identifying AR as its primary molecular target (PMID 14600402). It is a nonsteroidal small molecule built on a tetrahydroquinoline / cyclopenta[c]quinoline scaffold; a closely related optimized long-tail THQ analog was co-crystallized with AR, where the ligand induces a large Trp741 conformational change and hydrogen-bonds to helix 12 to stabilize the agonist conformation (PMID 24273094).

Signalling cascade. AR agonism → androgen-responsive gene transcription plus rapid nongenomic kinase signaling → tissue-selective anabolic output. In bone the downstream readout is increased osteoblast-driven periosteal mineral apposition and cortical bone accrual; in muscle, increased levator ani mass; in prostate the anabolic output is minimal, producing the bone/muscle-yes, prostate-no split (PMID 14600402). The genomic-vs-nongenomic and differential coregulator-recruitment framework that is invoked to explain this selectivity was characterized for the nonsteroidal SARM S-22 versus DHT (PMID 18801930) and is applied to S-40503 by analogy, not by direct demonstration.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral (intended); rodent systemic dosing
Absorption / bioavailability
Not published for S-40503 in humans. The THQ program broadly aimed for oral bioavailability with acceptable ADME; specific oral-bioavailability figures reported in the Kaken program relate to optimized analogs rather than S-40503 itself (PMID 24273094).
Half-life
Not established in humans; no published human PK.
Metabolism
Human metabolism not characterized in the primary literature. Detection-oriented mass-spectrometry work fragmented the parent molecule for doping control but did not establish a human metabolic pathway (PMID 19280612).
Excretion
Not characterized. Anti-doping method development targeted the circulating parent drug in plasma specifically because metabolism/renal-elimination data were limited (PMID 19280612).
Notes
The near-total absence of published human PK is itself a key limitation — dosing behavior, accumulation, and drug interactions in humans are unknown.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

In rats, S-40503 produced dose-dependent gains in bone mineral density and levator ani muscle weight that reached magnitudes comparable to DHT, while showing essentially flat, non-elevated prostate weight across the dose range tested — i.e., a wide separation between the anabolic dose-response (bone/muscle) and the androgenic/virilizing dose-response (prostate) (PMID 14600402). This dose separation is the quantitative signature of tissue selectivity. No human dose-response data exist, and rodent dose separations have historically not translated cleanly to human therapeutic windows for SARMs. NO human dosing guidance is provided here.

How it compares

  • vs DHT (5α-dihydrotestosterone)
    same: Comparable magnitude of bone density and levator ani muscle gains in rats; both non-aromatizable AR agonists
    different: S-40503 does not enlarge the prostate at anabolic doses whereas DHT raises prostate weight ~1.5-fold — the core selectivity advantage (PMID 14600402)
  • vs Estrogen (anti-resorptive comparator in the same study)
    same: Both can influence bone density in OVX rats
    different: S-40503 increased cortical bone strength and drove new bone formation (anabolic); estrogen did not increase strength in the same experiment — S-40503 behaves as an anabolic, not just an anti-resorptive (PMID 14600402)
  • vs Andarine (S-4) — arylpropionamide SARM tested in bone
    same: Both nonsteroidal SARMs with rodent cortical-bone anabolic and bone-strength benefits in osteopenic models
    different: Different chemotype (arylpropionamide vs tetrahydroquinoline) and different developer (GTx vs Kaken); S-4 bone data come from Kearbey et al. (PMID 19728047)
  • vs S-101479 (sibling Kaken THQ SARM)
    same: Same tetrahydroquinoline platform, nM AR binding, bone-anabolic with periosteal cortical bone formation
    different: S-101479 was studied more explicitly in female (OVX) models and in combination with standard osteoporosis drugs, showing additive bone benefit (PMID 22791158)
  • vs Enobosarm / RAD140 (clinically advanced SARMs)
    same: Same broad goal — prostate-sparing anabolism via AR
    different: Enobosarm and RAD140 reached human trials with published clinical data; S-40503 has no published human efficacy trials, making it a preclinical prototype rather than a clinical candidate

History & legal status

S-40503 was generated by the Central Research Laboratories of Kaken Pharmaceutical Co. (Kyoto, Japan) and reported in 2003 (Hanada et al., PMID 14600402) as an early prototype demonstrating that a nonsteroidal AR ligand could reproduce androgen's bone/muscle anabolism while sparing the prostate — a proof-of-concept for using SARMs in osteoporosis (especially male/hypogonadal and postmenopausal bone loss). Kaken continued the tetrahydroquinoline program, producing S-101479 (PMID 22791158) and structurally optimized long-tail THQ leads with defined AR crystal structures (PMID 24273094). By 2009 the anti-doping community had it on their radar: Thevis et al. built a plasma detection assay and noted it was in early clinical development (PMID 19280612). Regulatory status: never approved anywhere; no SARM is approved for the osteoporosis/anabolic indications discussed. As a SARM it is prohibited at all times by the World Anti-Doping Agency (SARMs class, added to the Prohibited List in 2008), and detection methods place it in the doping-surveillance literature (PMID 28137616). In consumer/'research chemical' markets S-40503 is far less prominent than ostarine or RAD140, and any such product is unregulated and unverified.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Whether any human efficacy data (bone density, fracture risk, body composition) exist at all — none are published and no trials are registered
  • Human pharmacokinetics: oral bioavailability, half-life, metabolism, and elimination in humans are uncharacterized
  • Human safety: hepatotoxicity, HDL/lipid effects, cardiovascular signals, and HPG-axis suppression/fertility impact have not been measured for S-40503 specifically
  • Whether the rodent prostate-sparing translates to no prostate or other androgen-sensitive-tissue effects in humans
  • Whether the Trp741/helix-12 and coregulator-selectivity mechanism (shown for sibling/other SARMs) actually governs S-40503 in human tissue
  • Long-term skeletal outcomes: whether bone gained is durable and fracture-protective rather than just densitometrically higher
  • Reason the program did not advance to visible clinical development (efficacy, safety, commercial, or strategic) is not documented in the literature

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

This is harm-reduction information, not an endorsement of use; S-40503 is not an approved drug and is banned in sport. If someone has been exposed to a product labeled S-40503, the class-relevant things a clinician would monitor are: (1) hormonal axis — total/free testosterone, LH, FSH (SARMs suppress endogenous testosterone, and recovery can be slow); (2) liver function — ALT, AST, bilirubin at baseline and periodically, since oral SARMs have caused transaminase elevations and drug-induced liver injury; (3) lipids — a fasting panel, watching for HDL reduction; (4) hematocrit/hemoglobin, as androgens can raise red cell mass; (5) in men, symptoms of testicular atrophy/low libido on withdrawal, and in women any signs of virilization (voice change, hirsutism, which may be irreversible). Because product identity and purity are unverified, monitoring should assume the actual contents may differ from the label. Any concerning symptom (jaundice, dark urine, right-upper-quadrant pain, chest pain, marked mood change) warrants stopping and prompt medical review. No dosing or cycling guidance is provided.

Caveats

Evidence maturity is the dominant caveat. The strong, specific claims (bone-selective anabolism, DHT-comparable BMD gains, prostate sparing) come essentially from a single early rodent paper (Hanada 2003, PMID 14600402) plus supporting work on sibling THQ compounds (S-101479: PMID 22791158; the optimized long-tail THQ lead: PMID 24273094). Do not read rat femoral-BMD numbers as predictive of human bone or body-composition outcomes — SARMs as a class have repeatedly shown smaller and more ambiguous effects in humans than in rodents, and no human efficacy data exist for S-40503 specifically. The tissue-selectivity mechanism (Trp741/helix-12 conformational lock, differential coregulator recruitment, genomic vs nongenomic signaling) is best-characterized for a DIFFERENT SARM (the aryl-propionamide S-22, PMID 18801930) and, at the structural level, for an optimized THQ analog (PMID 24273094); for S-40503 it is applied by chemical analogy and has not been directly demonstrated — hence it is flagged as Contested rather than established for this compound. 'Prostate-sparing in rats' is NOT a guarantee of no androgenic harm in humans: SARMs in human trials have consistently suppressed the HPG axis (lowering endogenous testosterone, LH, FSH) and lowered HDL cholesterol, and some (e.g., in enobosarm and other SARM trials) raised liver enzymes — none of which is captured by the rodent prostate readout. There is essentially no human safety dataset for S-40503 to characterize hepatotoxicity, lipid, cardiovascular, or fertility effects. Finally, material sold online as 'S-40503' is unverified: identity, purity, dose, and contamination are unknown, and the compound is banned in sport.
