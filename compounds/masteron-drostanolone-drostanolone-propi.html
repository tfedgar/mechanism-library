Library / Anabolic-androgenic steroids

Masteron

A DHT with a built-in lock: it can't turn into estrogen, so it gives "dry," hard androgenic effects with no water bloat - and decades ago doctors used it to fight breast cancer.

Limited human data 25 citations
Class
Injectable anabolic-androgenic steroid (AAS); a synthetic derivative of dihydrotestosterone (DHT)
Primary target
The androgen receptor (AR)
Human status
Mixed/obsolete. Drostanolone propionate WAS a genuinely approved human drug (brand names Masteril, Masterid, Drolban) used from the 1960s-1980s as a hormonal therapy for advanced/metastatic breast cancer in women - so real human clinical data exist, but they are old, from cancer patients (not athletes), and concern efficacy/tolerability for tumors rather than performance. It was withdrawn/abandoned for that indication because tamoxifen and aromatase inhibitors proved more effective with fewer virilizing effects. Today it has NO current medical approval for any indication and is essentially only used non-medically as a performance/physique drug. There are NO modern controlled human trials of drostanolone for muscle-building - that use is unapproved, unmonitored, and the dosing/effects claimed by users are not validated by clinical evidence. It is a controlled substance (Schedule III in the US).

At a glance

  • Lean, 'dry,' hard muscle look with no estrogen-driven water retention - the trait it is famous for in bodybuilding
  • No gynecomastia / no estrogenic bloat because it cannot aromatize into estrogen
  • Mild-to-moderate anabolic muscle/strength gain; relatively weak anabolic compared with testosterone, often used as an 'add-on'
  • Strong, direct androgenic side-effect profile in susceptible people: accelerated male-pattern hair loss, acne, prostate stimulation
  • Pronounced virilization risk in women (voice deepening, facial hair, clitoral enlargement) - some effects irreversible
  • Suppresses the body's own testosterone production (HPG axis shutdown), like all AAS
  • Cardiovascular strain: lowers HDL ('good') cholesterol, can raise blood pressure
  • Historical therapeutic effect: tumor regression in some advanced breast-cancer patients via androgen opposing estrogen signaling

Mechanism, step by step

  1. It is essentially a 'tweaked DHT' Established

    Drostanolone is dihydrotestosterone (DHT - the body's strongest natural androgen) with a small methyl group added at the 2-position and a fatty-acid ester bolted on so it releases slowly after injection. So it acts like a long-acting, slightly tougher version of DHT. PMID 32245263 (Molecules 2020); PMID 1606052 (J Steroid Biochem Mol Biol 1992)

  2. It switches on the androgen receptor Established

    Like all AAS, it diffuses into cells and binds the androgen receptor. The activated receptor moves to the DNA and turns up genes that build muscle protein and drive male (androgenic) traits. This is the core anabolic + androgenic effect shared by the whole class. PMID 29521706 (Curr Sports Med Rep 2018, AAS review); PMID 576855 (Eur J Cancer 1977, AR binding)

  3. It CANNOT become estrogen (no aromatization) Established

    Most testosterone-based steroids can be converted by the aromatase enzyme into estrogen, causing water retention and gyno. Drostanolone's A-ring is already saturated (it's DHT-based) and the 2-methyl group blocks aromatase, so it makes essentially zero estrogen. This is why it's prized as a 'dry,' non-bloating compound. This is a chemical fact, not just lore. PMID 32245263 (Molecules 2020, structure); PMID 37669128 (Biol Reprod 2023, aromatization & 5a-reductase resistance concepts)

  4. It does NOT need (and resists) 5-alpha-reductase Established

    Testosterone gets amplified into DHT by 5-alpha-reductase in skin, scalp and prostate. Drostanolone is ALREADY a 5-alpha-reduced steroid, so it can't be converted further and isn't blunted by finasteride-type drugs. Its androgenic action in those tissues (scalp, prostate, skin) is therefore direct - part of why hair loss and acne are characteristic androgenic risks for susceptible users. PMID 20599615 (J Steroid Biochem Mol Biol 2010, role of 5a-reduction in androgen potency); PMID 32716491 (Endocrinology 2020, 5a-reductase biology)

  5. The 'anti-estrogen' reputation = androgen opposing estrogen, not blocking it Evidence-human

    Marketing calls Masteron 'anti-estrogenic.' What's real: activating the androgen receptor pushes back on estrogen-receptor signaling - in breast tissue the AR can directly suppress what the estrogen receptor does. That AR-vs-ER crosstalk is the modern explanation for why it was historically used against breast cancer. It is NOT an aromatase inhibitor and NOT a SERM like tamoxifen; it works through the androgen pathway, not by blocking estrogen directly. PMID 31698248 (iScience 2019, AR is a non-canonical inhibitor of ER); PMID 18515094 (J Steroid Biochem Mol Biol 2008, synthetic androgen antiproliferative in breast cancer cells)

  6. It was a real breast-cancer drug Established

    Long before bodybuilding, drostanolone propionate (Masteril/Masterid) was given to women with advanced/metastatic breast cancer from the 1960s-80s. Androgens were a recognized hormonal therapy; trials compared it against other androgens. It worked partly by the AR-opposes-ER effect above. It was later abandoned for breast cancer because tamoxifen and aromatase inhibitors were more effective with fewer virilizing side effects. PMID 13920749 (Cancer 1962); PMID 1036981 (Clin Oncol 1976); PMID 1243528 (Arch Geschwulstforsch 1975)

  7. Why it's 'safer on the liver' but not harmless Evidence-human

    Because it's injected and NOT 17-alpha-alkylated, it skips the liver-stressing oral pathway that makes pills like superdrol/stanozolol hepatotoxic. But it still raises blood pressure risk, crashes good cholesterol (HDL), suppresses natural testosterone, and drives androgenic effects (hair loss, acne, prostate, virilization in women). One lab study also flags that drostanolone can inhibit a liver bile-acid enzyme (AKR1D1), a possible cholestasis route - so 'liver-friendly' is relative, not absolute. PMID 27372877 (Med Hypotheses 2016, 17a-alkyl AAS hepatotoxicity); PMID 37451653 (Toxicol Lett 2023, AAS incl. drostanolone inhibit AKR1D1 - in vitro/in silico, Evidence-rodent/in vitro)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Masteron's active molecule is drostanolone (dromostanolone) = 2α-methyl-5α-dihydrotestosterone (2α-methyl-DHT); the propionate/enanthate ester is a 17β-OH prodrug that must be cleaved before activity. CONFIRMED IDENTITY: the parent is literally a 2α-methylated DHT molecule (PMID 32245263 names drostanolone propionate '2α-Methyl-4,5α-dihydrotestosterone 17β-propionate'; ChEMBL CHEMBL1582 parent / CHEMBL1201048 propionate, molecular_formula C20H32O2 parent, flagged prodrug=1). MECHANISM CLASS: it is a direct AGONIST at the nuclear androgen receptor (AR, gene AR/NR3C4). Because it is ALREADY a 5α-reduced (DHT-type) molecule, it is NOT a substrate for 5α-reductase (no further activation/deactivation by SRD5A1/2) and CANNOT be aromatized to an estrogen — the saturated A-ring plus 2α-methyl group blocks the aromatase reaction that requires a 3-keto/Δ4 substrate [Established: structural class; PMID 41898445 for the general SAR of 5α-reduced/2-substituted androgens]. AFFINITY NUMBERS: a validated human Ki/EC50 for drostanolone itself at AR was NOT found in this run (corpus/ChEMBL/PubMed returned no curated AR Ki for drostanolone) — this is a genuine data gap. As a frame of reference, DHT (the unmethylated parent scaffold) binds AR with very high affinity (Kd ~0.2–1 nM, the most potent endogenous AR agonist; PMID 31753699, 32202088). Drostanolone is generally described as a moderate-affinity AR agonist with the classic 'high androgenic relative to anabolic, modest absolute potency' DHT-derivative profile, but published quantitative affinity for the drostanolone molecule specifically is sparse. SHBG: like other 5α-reduced non-aromatizable androgens it is expected to bind sex-hormone-binding globulin and may displace more potent androgens — frequently invoked as the basis of Masteron's 'free-testosterone-raising / hardening' reputation — but a measured drostanolone–SHBG affinity was NOT located this run (Speculative/Contested for the magnitude).

Signalling cascade. Classical genomic AR pathway (Established for AR agonists generally; drostanolone-specific steps inferred from class): (1) Esterase cleavage in blood/tissue releases free drostanolone from the propionate/enanthate ester. (2) The lipophilic steroid (ALogP of the propionate ~5.17, ChEMBL) diffuses across the plasma membrane. (3) It binds the ligand-binding domain (LBD) of cytoplasmic AR, triggering dissociation of heat-shock chaperones (HSP90/HSP70/HSP56) and a conformational change that folds helix 12 over the LBD. (4) This exposes nuclear-localization signals; AR undergoes an intramolecular N-terminal/C-terminal (N/C) interaction and homodimerizes. (5) The AR dimer translocates to the nucleus and binds androgen-response elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer regions of target genes. (6) Coactivators (SRC/p160 family, CBP/p300, with histone-acetyltransferase activity) and the mediator complex are recruited, driving transcription of anabolic genes (e.g. those increasing myonuclear protein synthesis, satellite-cell activity, IGF-1 axis; in muscle DHT-type androgens modulate myostatin/follistatin and Pax7 — PMID 18434429, 23995658). (7) In skeletal muscle the net effect is increased fractional protein synthesis and reduced protein breakdown; in androgenic tissue (skin, prostate, hair follicle) the SAME receptor drives sebum, prostate growth and follicle miniaturization. NON-GENOMIC arm (Evidence-rodent/in vitro): androgens also trigger rapid membrane-initiated signalling — Src/PI3K/Akt and MAPK/ERK kinase cascades and Ca2+ flux — within minutes, independent of transcription (reviewed PMID 41898445). The therapeutically relevant feature of drostanolone is 'anabolic-androgenic dissociation' being only modest: as a DHT derivative it carries substantial androgenic (skin/hair/prostate) signalling, NOT a clean SARM-like tissue selectivity.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Intramuscular injection only (oil depot). Two common esters: drostanolone propionate (Masteron, the classic form) and drostanolone enanthate. The 17β-ester is a depot prodrug — not orally used (no 17α-alkylation, so it would be hepatically cleared if swallowed). ChEMBL lists the propionate as parenteral, prodrug=1, first approval 1982.
Absorption / bioavailability
After IM injection the esterified steroid leaves the oil depot by diffusion and lymphatic drainage of interstitial fluid; absorption rate is governed by ester chain length (longer ester = slower release). Systemic bioavailability of the released parent androgen by the IM route is high (Established for IM androgen esters generally; PMC AAS review). Oral bioavailability is effectively nil due to first-pass metabolism (not used orally).
Half-life
Ester-dependent. Drostanolone PROPIONATE: short — the 3-carbon propionate ester prolongs effective half-life to roughly ~1 day (the AAS pharmacology review states a propionate ester extends half-life to ~1.0 day; corpus PMC review), so it requires frequent injection (every 1–3 days in practice). Drostanolone ENANTHATE: the 7-carbon enanthate ester gives a substantially longer release (the same review gives ~4.2 days for an enanthate ester on a comparable steroid), allowing less frequent dosing. These are ESTER half-lives/release rates, not the half-life of free drostanolone, which is short once liberated [Evidence-human for the ester principle; drostanolone-specific PK trials were NOT found this run].
Metabolism
Two-step. (1) Ubiquitous serum and tissue ESTERASES hydrolyze the 17β-ester to liberate free drostanolone (Established). (2) Free drostanolone is then metabolized further — it is already 5α-reduced so is NOT a 5α-reductase substrate, and it CANNOT be aromatized. Documented oxidative/reductive transformations (microbial-biotransformation model, PMID 29326586) include hydroxylations and 3-keto reduction to 2α-methyl-3α/3α,17β-diol metabolites and oxidation of the 17β-OH; in humans hepatic phase-I (likely CYP-mediated hydroxylation) and 3α-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase-type reduction generate the urinary metabolites used in doping control. Exact human CYP isoform mapping for drostanolone was NOT located this run (gap).
Excretion
Renal — as conjugated (glucuronide/sulfate) phase-II metabolites of drostanolone and its 2α-methyl-androstane diol/dione metabolites in urine (basis of WADA/anti-doping detection). Quantitative human clearance/excretion fractions were NOT found this run.
Notes
Key distinguishing PK/PD features: (a) PRODRUG ester — activity depends on esterase cleavage; (b) NON-AROMATIZABLE — no estradiol generated, so no estrogenic water retention/gynecomastia driven by this drug itself; (c) 5α-reduced ALREADY — cannot be 'protected' by 5α-reductase inhibitors (finasteride/dutasteride do NOT blunt its androgenic skin/hair effects, unlike testosterone); (d) high lipophilicity (ALogP ~5.17) consistent with depot behavior and presumed CNS access, though BBB-specific data were not retrieved; (e) the propionate is the rate-limiting, short-acting form — 'Masteron' colloquially means the propionate.

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

HUMAN therapeutic dose-response (the only formal clinical data): in advanced/metastatic breast carcinoma drostanolone propionate was dosed at roughly 100 mg IM three times weekly (~300 mg/week), producing objective tumor regression in a minority of patients (response rates on the order of ~20–30% of treated women in mid-20th-century series; e.g. Seay 1972, PMID 5067664; comparative RCT vs nandrolone decanoate, PMID 1036981). This is the ONLY rigorous human dose-response evidence and it is for an oncologic, not performance, endpoint. PERFORMANCE/physique dose-response in humans is UNKNOWN from controlled trials — there are NO randomized dose-finding studies of drostanolone for muscle/strength or 'hardening'; all such dose-response claims are anecdotal (forum-level) and cannot be quantified here. RODENT: the classic non-aromatizable-androgen assays (Hershberger-type) place 2α-methyl-DHT/drostanolone as androgenic with modest anabolic-to-androgenic separation; a specific cited rodent example — drostanolone propionate 40 mg/kg 5×/week for 6 weeks had NO effect on carcinogen-induced rat mammary tumors at that dose, while LOWER doses (6–12 mg/kg/week) of related DHT analogs caused tumor regression and a very high androgen dose stimulated growth — i.e. a biphasic, dose-dependent androgen effect (textbook PMC review on androgens in rodent mammary carcinogenesis). NET: human anabolic dose-response is genuinely unestablished; only oncologic (~300 mg/wk) and rodent ranges are grounded.

How it compares

  • vs Stanolone / DHT (the unmethylated parent, ChEMBL CHEMBL27769)
    same: Both are 5α-reduced, non-aromatizable AR agonists with strong androgenic character; both act through identical AR genomic/non-genomic signalling.
    different: Drostanolone carries a 2α-methyl group that confers metabolic stability (resists 3α-HSD inactivation better than DHT) and allows depot esterification for injectable use, giving it usable systemic activity; bare DHT is rapidly inactivated and far less practical as an injectable anabolic.
  • vs Stanozolol (Winstrol, another DHT-derived AAS)
    same: Both DHT-derived, non-aromatizable, used for 'cutting/hardening', both worsen HDL/lipids and are androgenic to skin/hair.
    different: Stanozolol is 17α-alkylated and orally active but hepatotoxic (cholestasis/peliosis risk); drostanolone is 17β-esterified, injectable, and NOT 17α-alkylated, so it lacks the same direct hepatotoxicity profile. Stanozolol also markedly lowers SHBG.
  • vs Testosterone (and its esters)
    same: Both are AR agonists driving muscle anabolism and HPG-axis suppression.
    different: Testosterone IS aromatized to estradiol (gynecomastia, water retention, but HDL-protective estrogen) and IS 5α-reduced to DHT; drostanolone is neither aromatized nor a 5α-reductase substrate, so it produces no estrogenic effects, can't be modulated by AIs or 5-ARIs, and tends to hit HDL harder while never causing estrogenic gyno on its own.
  • vs Nandrolone decanoate (Deca-Durabolin, a 19-nor AAS) — the direct comparator in the 1976 breast-cancer RCT
    same: Both injectable, esterified, non-17α-alkylated anabolic steroids used clinically for breast carcinoma palliation (compared head-to-head in PMID 1036981).
    different: Nandrolone is a 19-nortestosterone that IS aromatizable (weakly) and is 5α-reduced to the WEAKER dihydronandrolone (so it is relatively less androgenic in skin/prostate); drostanolone is a non-aromatizable DHT derivative that is comparatively more androgenic. Nandrolone is also notorious for progestogenic activity and sexual dysfunction, which drostanolone lacks.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Anadrol compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both are AR agonists that stimulate erythropoiesis and raise red-cell mass. Anadrol is notorious for strongly raising hematocrit (it is FDA-approved for anemia, boosting EPO and suppressing hepcidin); Masteron raises red-cell mass as a class androgen effect. Run together the erythrocytosis is additive, pushing hematocrit/hemoglobin higher and increasing blood viscosity and clot/stroke risk. Both also suppress HDL, so the atherogenic lipid hit compounds as well. (Distinct hepatic routes: Anadrol is 17a-alkylated and markedly hepatotoxic; Masteron is injectable and much less so, so hepatotoxicity does not simply add.) Masteron monograph monitoring (hematocrit) and side_effect_mechanisms (lipids); Anadrol monograph (strongly raises hematocrit, suppressed HDL, hepatotoxicity) Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin and full blood count (polycythemia raises thrombotic risk); blood pressure; HDL/LDL lipid panel for the combined dyslipidemia; liver enzymes (mainly for Anadrol's 17a-alkyl hepatotoxicity).
  • Anastrozole compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Anastrozole inhibits aromatase (CYP19A1) to lower estradiol. Masteron contributes no aromatizable substrate at all, and its AR-vs-ER crosstalk pushes estrogen SIGNALING in the same (downward) direction as the AI - so the two do NOT oppose each other; instead their estrogen-lowering effects compound. On a Masteron-dominant regimen there is little drug-derived estrogen for an AI to remove, so adding/over-titrating an AI risks driving the small remaining (mostly testosterone-derived or baseline) estradiol below the healthy range. Because estradiol has a U-shaped risk curve, a low-E2 state stacks joint pain, worse lipids and bone/mood harm on top of Masteron's own HDL crash. (Masteron's crosstalk is receptor-level, not enzyme inhibition, so it does not add to anastrozole's mechanism directly - but the low-E2 downside does compound.) Masteron monograph step 3 (no aromatization); Aromatase MoA card (low-E2 harms, U-shaped E2 risk) Watch: Sensitive estradiol to catch over-suppression; HDL/LDL lipid panel (both low E2 and Masteron independently worsen HDL); joint symptoms, libido and mood as low-estrogen red flags.
  • Boldenone undecylenate compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Boldenone's most reliable clinical effect is a marked rise in red-cell mass/hematocrit, and Masteron adds its own class-androgen erythropoietic drive. Together they compound erythrocytosis and raise blood viscosity/thrombotic risk. Directional difference on estrogen: boldenone aromatizes (adds E2) while Masteron does not, so Masteron's receptor-level AR-vs-ER crosstalk at most partially offsets, and does NOT eliminate, boldenone's estrogenic potential. Masteron monograph monitoring (hematocrit); Boldenone monograph (marked hematocrit rise; aromatizes) Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin and CBC (additive polycythemia); blood pressure; estradiol (boldenone still aromatizes despite Masteron).
  • Exemestane compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Exemestane is a steroidal (suicide) aromatase inhibitor that lowers estradiol. As with the other AIs, Masteron adds no aromatizable substrate and its AR-vs-ER crosstalk lowers estrogen signaling in the same direction - so the two compound rather than oppose. On a Masteron-dominant regimen there is little drug-derived estrogen for exemestane to remove, so over-suppression toward crashed E2 becomes the dominant risk, and low estradiol compounds Masteron's independent HDL-lowering to worsen the lipid profile. Masteron's receptor-level anti-estrogen effect is a separate mechanism and does not add to enzyme inhibition. Masteron monograph step 3; Aromatase MoA card (steroidal AI, U-shaped E2 risk) Watch: Sensitive estradiol (guard against over-suppression); lipid panel (low E2 plus Masteron both hit HDL); joint, libido, mood and bone symptoms of estrogen deficiency.
  • Letrozole compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Letrozole is the most potent aromatase inhibitor (>98% E2 suppression). Since Masteron cannot aromatize and adds no estrogen substrate, and its AR-vs-ER crosstalk lowers estrogen signaling in the same direction as the AI, pairing a strong AI like letrozole with a Masteron-heavy regimen compounds estrogen suppression and creates high risk of an estradiol crash - there is simply little estrogen to spare. Crashed estradiol then compounds Masteron's own HDL-lowering, worsening the atherogenic lipid shift. Note this is NOT an opposing interaction: Masteron's receptor-level 'anti-estrogen' effect and the AI's enzyme blockade both reduce estrogen action and cannot substitute for one another. Masteron monograph step 3; Aromatase MoA card (letrozole >98% suppression, U-shaped risk) Watch: Sensitive estradiol (letrozole crashes E2 hard); full lipid panel; watch for joint dryness, low libido, low mood and bone symptoms signalling estrogen deficiency.
  • Nandrolone compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both were the direct comparators in the 1976 breast-cancer RCT (PMID 1036981) - both injectable, non-17a-alkylated anabolic steroids. Mechanistically here: nandrolone is progestogenic (raises prolactin) and weakly aromatizes; Masteron is non-aromatizing and mildly opposes estrogen only via AR-vs-ER crosstalk. That crosstalk does NOT touch nandrolone's progesterone/prolactin pathway, so Masteron cannot mitigate nandrolone-driven prolactin gyno or sexual dysfunction. Their androgenic and HDL-lowering effects compound. Masteron monograph comparisons (nandrolone) and step 5; Nandrolone monograph (progestogenic/prolactin, weak aromatization); PMID 1036981 (drostanolone vs nandrolone RCT) Watch: Prolactin (Masteron will NOT counteract nandrolone's progestogenic prolactin rise - that needs a dopamine agonist such as cabergoline); estradiol (nandrolone weakly aromatizes); HDL/LDL lipids; libido/erectile function.
  • Oxandrolone compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Oxandrolone and Masteron are both non-aromatizing, DHT-type (already 5-alpha-reduced) androgens that lower HDL and raise LDL. Because neither generates HDL-protective estrogen, their dyslipidemic effects add together, producing a more atherogenic lipid profile than either alone. This is a lipid/cardiovascular compounding, independent of oxandrolone's separate (comparatively mild) 17a-alkyl hepatic concern. Masteron monograph side_effect_mechanisms (HDL); Oxandrolone monograph (non-aromatizing, lowers HDL, already 5a-reduced) Watch: HDL-C, LDL-C and triglycerides (additive atherogenic shift); blood pressure; periodic liver enzymes given oxandrolone's mild hepatotoxicity.
  • Stanozolol compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both are DHT-derived, non-aromatizing androgens that characteristically suppress HDL cholesterol and raise LDL, partly because no HDL-protective estrogen is generated. Stacked, the atherogenic lipid shift is additive and can drive HDL very low; both also share androgenic drying/scalp/skin effects. (Distinct hepatic routes: stanozolol is 17a-alkylated and hepatotoxic; Masteron is injectable and much less so, so hepatotoxicity does not simply add. Stanozolol additionally strongly suppresses SHBG, which Masteron does not clearly do.) Masteron monograph side_effect_mechanisms (HDL crash in non-aromatizing androgens); Stanozolol monograph (non-aromatizing, adverse lipids, SHBG suppression) Watch: Fasting HDL-C, LDL-C, triglycerides (expect a sharp additive HDL drop); blood pressure; liver panel (mainly for stanozolol's 17a-alkyl hepatotoxicity).
  • Trenbolone compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Trenbolone is a strongly androgenic AR agonist, non-aromatizing, that also carries progestogenic (PGR) and prolactin-raising activity plus glucocorticoid-receptor binding; Masteron adds another potent non-aromatizing DHT-type androgen. Together they compound the non-aromatizing androgenic load (hair, skin, prostate, virilization) and the adverse lipid/cardiovascular strain both class-typically cause. CRITICAL correction: Masteron's 'anti-estrogen' reputation is AR-vs-ER crosstalk, NOT an aromatase inhibitor and NOT an anti-prolactin agent - so it does NOTHING for trenbolone's progesterone/prolactin-driven gyno or sexual dysfunction (that would require a dopamine agonist such as cabergoline, not Masteron). Masteron monograph steps 3 and 5, side_effect_mechanisms; Trenbolone monograph (progestogenic/prolactin, GR activity, non-aromatizing) Watch: HDL/LDL lipids and blood pressure (additive cardiovascular strain); prolactin if trenbolone-related sexual dysfunction or gyno signs appear (Masteron will NOT fix this); androgenic signs (hair, acne, prostate/PSA); virilization signs in women.
  • Testosterone counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    Testosterone aromatizes to estradiol and can raise it into gynecomastia/water-retention territory; Masteron is non-aromatizing (its A-ring is already 5-alpha-reduced/saturated, so it generates zero estrogen). Additionally, activating the androgen receptor produces AR-vs-ER crosstalk that can suppress estrogen-receptor signaling in breast tissue, which is the documented basis of Masteron's historical anti-breast-cancer use. This is why Masteron is popularly said to 'dry out' a testosterone base. CRITICAL correction: this is receptor-level crosstalk, NOT aromatase inhibition and NOT a SERM effect, so Masteron does NOT lower serum estradiol and cannot substitute for estrogen-control drugs - a common inaccurate claim. Whether the crosstalk meaningfully reduces systemic water retention (vs just the breast-tissue antiproliferative effect that is actually documented) is not established. Masteron monograph steps 3 and 5 (PMID 31698248 AR non-canonical ER inhibitor; PMID 18515094); note step 5 mechanism is preclinical/translational Watch: Estradiol (sensitive assay) plus breast tenderness/swelling; do NOT assume Masteron 'covers' estrogen management. Also watch hematocrit and HDL, since both compounds push those the same (adverse) direction.
  • Dutasteride redundant Established
    Dutasteride inhibits both 5-alpha-reductase type 1 and 2 for near-total DHT suppression, acting on the testosterone-to-DHT conversion step. Masteron is a pre-formed 5-alpha-reduced androgen, so dutasteride cannot reduce Masteron or blunt its direct androgenic effects on scalp, prostate and skin. The compounds do not meaningfully interact via Masteron's own action; dutasteride only affects DHT arising from any co-run aromatizable androgen. This corrects the common belief that a 5-ARI makes Masteron 'hair-safe.' Masteron monograph step 4; Hair-loss MoA card Watch: Hair/scalp and prostate/PSA (no benefit expected against Masteron's direct androgenic drive); do NOT rely on a 5-ARI to make Masteron 'hair-safe.'
  • Finasteride redundant Established
    Finasteride blocks 5-alpha-reductase type 2 to stop testosterone becoming DHT, reducing DHT-driven hair loss and prostate stimulation. Masteron IS ALREADY a 5-alpha-reduced steroid (2a-methyl-DHT), so it is not a substrate for the enzyme; its androgenic scalp/prostate/skin action is delivered directly to the androgen receptor. Finasteride therefore does NOT blunt Masteron's hair loss, acne or prostate effects - a widely misunderstood point and a key harm-reduction correction. Finasteride still lowers DHT made from any co-administered testosterone, but it offers no protection against Masteron itself. Masteron monograph step 4 and side_effect_mechanisms (alopecia); Hair-loss MoA card (5-ARI mechanism) Watch: Scalp recession and acne (expect NO protection from a 5-ARI against Masteron's own androgenic effects); PSA/prostate symptoms; recognise finasteride only helps with testosterone-derived DHT, not Masteron.
  • Proviron redundant Contested
    Proviron (mesterolone, 1a-methyl-DHT) and Masteron (2a-methyl-DHT) are both DHT-derived, non-aromatizing, already-5-alpha-reduced androgens marketed as mild 'anti-estrogens' that bind SHBG. Their mechanisms overlap heavily: neither aromatizes, both are 5-alpha-reduced (so finasteride does not blunt them), both are claimed to displace testosterone from SHBG to raise free-T, and both mildly oppose estrogen via the androgen receptor rather than by blocking aromatase. Stacking them is largely redundant on the estrogen/SHBG axis while adding androgenic side-effect load. Note the SHBG free-T claim itself is Contested - a measured drostanolone-SHBG affinity was never established. Masteron monograph pharmacology + key_unknowns (SHBG free-T unquantified, Contested/Speculative); Proviron monograph (DHT-derived, non-aromatizing, binds SHBG) Watch: SHBG and free/total testosterone (overlapping, hard-to-titrate effect of uncertain magnitude); androgenic side effects (hair, acne, prostate) which add rather than cancel.

History &amp; legal status

"Drostanolone (2α-methyl-DHT) was developed in the late 1950s–early 1960s as a synthetic androgen; the USAN stem-year for the propionate is 1963 (ChEMBL). It was introduced therapeutically as an ANTINEOPLASTIC hormonal agent for advanced/metastatic breast carcinoma in women, marketed under trade names Masteron, Masteril, Masterid, Permastril, and (US, Eli Lilly) Drolban; NCI code NSC-12198. ChEMBL records dromostanolone propionate at max_phase 4 with a first_approval of 1982 (it was a licensed parenteral drug). Clinical use was largely abandoned from the 1970s–1980s as tamoxifen and modern endocrine/chemo regimens superseded androgen therapy for breast cancer (its virilizing side effects made it a poor choice once alternatives existed). It then persisted essentially only as a non-medical performance/physique drug ('Masteron'). REGULATORY STATUS TODAY: as an anabolic-androgenic steroid it is a controlled substance — Schedule III in the United States (Anabolic Steroids Control Act) and a prohibited substance in sport at all times under the WADA Prohibited List (class S1 anabolic agents); it has no current mainstream therapeutic indication and is not a legitimately marketed pharmaceutical in most countries. Material sold as 'Masteron' today is overwhelmingly from unregulated/underground sources."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No validated human AR binding affinity (Ki/EC50) for the drostanolone molecule itself was found this run — its quantitative receptor potency relative to DHT/testosterone is not rigorously established in the accessible literature.
  • Human pharmacokinetics of drostanolone (free-drug half-life, clearance, volume of distribution, ester-specific release curves) have not been formally characterized in modern PK studies; current half-life figures are inferred from ester chemistry, not measured for this drug.
  • The widely repeated claim that drostanolone meaningfully raises free testosterone by displacing it from SHBG lacks a measured human SHBG-binding affinity or controlled free-T data — magnitude is essentially unquantified (Contested/Speculative).
  • There is NO controlled human dose-response for any performance, body-composition, or 'hardening/cosmetic' endpoint — all such effects rest on anecdote, not trials.
  • Drostanolone-specific long-term cardiovascular, prostate, and hepatic outcome data do not exist; risk is extrapolated from the AAS class and from DHT-axis biology, not from studies of this molecule.
  • The exact human hepatic enzymes (specific CYP isoforms, HSD reductases) responsible for drostanolone phase-I metabolism are not mapped — current metabolite structures come from microbial-biotransformation and doping-control models rather than human hepatocyte studies.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only — NOT a usage endorsement and NO dosing guidance. Bloodwork/signs a clinician would track in someone exposed to a DHT-derived androgen like this: (1) LIPID PANEL — fasting HDL-C, LDL-C, total cholesterol, triglycerides; non-aromatizing DHT-type androgens characteristically crush HDL and raise LDL, so watch for a sharply atherogenic shift. (2) BLOOD PRESSURE and CARDIAC signs — periodic BP; clinical attention to exertional symptoms; in chronic exposure, echocardiographic assessment of LV wall thickness/diastolic function is what the literature flags (PMID 35460300). (3) HPG AXIS / HORMONES — total and free testosterone, LH, FSH, estradiol (to document suppression); SHBG. (4) HEMATOCRIT / HEMOGLOBIN / CBC — androgens raise red-cell mass (polycythemia raises clot/stroke risk). (5) PROSTATE — PSA and urinary symptoms in men, especially older men, given direct prostatic AR stimulation; a baseline before any androgen exposure is standard. (6) LIVER PANEL (ALT/AST, bilirubin, GGT) — drostanolone is 17β-esterified (not 17α-alkylated) so direct hepatotoxicity is LOWER than oral alkylated steroids, but monitoring remains prudent. (7) VIRILIZATION SIGNS in women — voice change, hirsutism, clitoromegaly, menstrual disruption are red flags and voice/genital changes can be permanent; (8) SKIN/HAIR — acne flare and scalp recession (note: 5α-reductase inhibitors will NOT prevent its hair/skin effects because it is already 5α-reduced). Any concerning result warrants discontinuation and evaluation by a qualified clinician."

Caveats

Harm-reduction and accuracy notes for the audience: (1) 'Anti-estrogen' is marketing shorthand - drostanolone is NOT an aromatase inhibitor or a SERM. It doesn't block estrogen directly; activating the androgen receptor opposes estrogen-receptor signaling. Do not treat it as a substitute for estrogen-control drugs in a cycle - that's a common and inaccurate claim. (2) 'Dry/no bloat' is true and chemically real (no aromatization), but it does NOT mean 'safe' - HDL crash, blood-pressure rise, testosterone suppression, hair loss, acne and prostate effects all still happen. (3) It is one of the more virilizing compounds for women because it's a potent, already-5a-reduced androgen that finasteride won't blunt; some virilization is permanent. (4) 'Liver-safe' is relative: it is injectable and not 17a-alkylated, so it lacks the classic oral-steroid liver toxicity, but a 2023 lab study shows it can inhibit the liver enzyme AKR1D1 (a potential cholestasis pathway) - that's in-vitro/in-silico evidence, not proof of harm in users, but 'liver-friendly' overstates it. (5) The strong historical human data are from breast-cancer patients, not athletes - do not read cancer-trial tolerability as evidence that performance use is safe. (6) Black-market product is frequently counterfeit or mislabeled (NMR/adulteration studies confirm this), adding dosing and contamination risk. This is mechanism education only - not dosing, sourcing, or how-to guidance."
