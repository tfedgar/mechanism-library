MK-677

Main target: Growth hormone secretagogue receptor type 1a. Backed by human data.

EvidenceHuman evidence
ApprovalNot FDA-approved

An oral pill that tricks your body's "hunger hormone" switch into telling the brain to pump out growth hormone — raising GH and IGF-1 on a natural-looking daily rhythm, without being a steroid or touching the androgen receptor at all.

Strong human data 34 citations
Class
Orally-active nonpeptide growth hormone secretagogue / ghrelin receptor (GHSR-1a) full agonist — not a steroid, not a SARM
Primary target
Growth hormone secretagogue receptor type 1a (GHSR-1a) — the "ghrelin receptor," a Gq/11-coupled GPCR on pituitary somatotrophs and hypothalamic neurons
Human status
Investigational only — never approved for any indication. Developed by Merck in the 1990s and tested in numerous human RCTs (GH-deficient children, frail elderly, hip-fracture recovery, Alzheimer's, dialysis patients, healthy older adults). It reliably raises GH and IGF-1 (target engagement confirmed) but failed to show meaningful clinical benefit on function/disease endpoints, and a phase IIb hip-fracture trial was stopped early over a congestive-heart-failure safety signal (PMID 21067829). Not an approved drug; sold only as a gray-market 'research chemical'/fitness compound and banned in sport (WADA). Studied in humans = Evidence-human for hormonal effects; clinical efficacy = largely negative.

At a glance

  • Raises pulsatile growth hormone (GH) secretion while preserving the natural GH rhythm and feedback loops (unlike injected HGH)
  • Increases serum IGF-1 by roughly 40–90% in humans, sustained with daily dosing
  • Increases lean (fat-free) body mass
  • Strongly stimulates appetite and causes water retention (GHSR-1a is the hunger receptor)
  • Increases bone-turnover markers
  • Downside: raises fasting blood glucose and reduces insulin sensitivity (GH opposes insulin)
  • Did NOT clearly improve muscle strength or physical function in the longest human RCT despite more lean mass
  • Reported liver injury (hepatotoxicity case report) and a heart-failure safety signal that halted one trial

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

7 steps7 human / established

  1. Mimics ghrelin at its receptor Established

    MK-677 is a synthetic molecule that binds GHSR-1a, the same receptor your stomach's hunger hormone ghrelin uses. Cryo-EM structures show ibutamoren sitting in the ghrelin-binding pocket and flipping the receptor into its active shape. It behaves as a simple full agonist, not a partial one — it switches the receptor fully on. PMID 34737341 (Nat Commun 2021, cryo-EM structure); PMID 15905359 (Mol Endocrinol 2005)

  2. Fires the GPCR second-messenger cascade Established

    An activated GHSR-1a is a G-protein coupled receptor: it triggers phospholipase C, which raises inositol phosphates and floods the cell with calcium, and engages the PKC/ERK pathway. That intracellular calcium spike is the trigger that makes hormone-secreting cells release their cargo. PMID 15905359 (Mol Endocrinol 2005); PMC review: GHSR intracellular signaling

  3. Pushes the pituitary AND quiets the brake Established

    MK-677 acts at two places at once. In the pituitary it directly tells somatotroph cells to release growth hormone. In the hypothalamus it activates GHRH neurons and works against somatostatin — the body's natural 'GH brake.' Pushing the accelerator while easing the brake is why it raises GH more cleanly than older drugs. PMID 9535057 (J Neuroendocrinol 1998, arcuate nucleus activation); PMID 15261841 (Best Pract Res Clin Endocrinol Metab 2004); PMID 11452249 (Clin Pharmacol Ther 2001)

  4. GH rises in pulses, preserving the natural rhythm Evidence-human

    Crucially, MK-677 boosts the size of your own GH pulses rather than flooding the body with flat, constant GH the way an injection would. The brain's feedback loops stay intact, so the body doesn't fully shut its own production down — a key difference from injecting synthetic HGH. PMID 15261841 (Best Pract Res Clin Endocrinol Metab 2004); PMID 18981485 (Ann Intern Med 2008)

  5. Liver converts the GH signal into IGF-1 Established

    The pulsed GH travels to the liver and tells it to make insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF-1), the hormone that actually drives most of GH's tissue-building effects. In humans MK-677 reliably raises IGF-1 by roughly 40-90% and keeps it elevated with daily dosing — and this IGF-1 rise depends on the pituitary, confirming GH is the middleman. PMID 18981485 (Ann Intern Med 2008, ~50-90% IGF-1 rise); PMID 28340044 (Nephrol Dial Transplant 2018, 65% rise vs placebo); PMID 10098487 (Endocrinology 1999, pituitary-dependence)

  6. Higher GH/IGF-1 shifts body composition and appetite Evidence-human

    Sustained GH and IGF-1 increase lean (fat-free) mass and bone-turnover markers, and because GHSR-1a is the hunger receptor, MK-677 also strongly stimulates appetite — driving the food intake and water retention users notice. In the year-long trial lean mass rose but strength and function did not clearly improve. PMID 18981485 (Ann Intern Med 2008, lean mass); PMID 9467534 (J Clin Endocrinol Metab 1998, anti-catabolic); PMID 10404019 (J Bone Miner Res 1999, bone turnover)

  7. The trade-off: raises blood sugar and insulin resistance Evidence-human

    Because GH opposes insulin, MK-677 raises fasting blood glucose and reduces insulin sensitivity — a consistent, real metabolic downside. Liver injury has also been reported. This is the central harm-reduction point: the same axis that builds tissue also worsens glucose control. PMID 18981485 (Ann Intern Med 2008, raised fasting glucose/insulin resistance); PMID 40675653 (BMJ Case Rep 2025, hepatotoxicity)

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. MK-677 (ibutamoren; ibutamoren mesylate = MK-0677; L-163,191; ChEMBL13817; MW 528.7 free base, 624.8 mesylate; C27H36N4O5S) is an orally active NONPEPTIDE growth-hormone secretagogue that acts as a SIMPLE FULL AGONIST at the growth hormone secretagogue receptor type 1a (GHS-R1a), the receptor for the endogenous peptide ghrelin. PRIMARY TARGET — GHS-R1a, a class-A (rhodopsin-like) Gq/11-coupled GPCR (366 aa, 7TM). AFFINITY (Established/Evidence-in vitro): binding affinity Kd/Ki ≈ 6.5 nM at human GHS-R1a, with functional potency 0.2–1.4 nM across all downstream signalling readouts (calcium mobilization, inositol-phosphate turnover, CRE- and SRE-driven transcription, arrestin mobilization) — i.e. it activates every assayed pathway with similar high potency [PMID 15905359]. In the original Merck medicinal-chemistry characterization it released GH from rat pituitary cells with EC50 = 1.3 nM and was orally active in dogs at doses as low as 0.125 mg/kg [PMID 7624358]. SELECTIVITY: At ~1 mg/kg oral in dogs it released GH WITHOUT significant changes in aldosterone, LH, thyroxine or prolactin, and only modest cortisol increases — i.e. relatively GH-selective among pituitary axes [PMID 7624358]. AGONIST CLASS NUANCE (Evidence-in vitro): in a comparative receptor-dimer study MK-677 behaved as a NEUTRAL agonist — a simple agonist that, unlike L-692,429 (positive allosteric modulator) or GHRP-6 (negative allosteric modulator), did not allosterically shift the potency of co-administered ghrelin [PMID 15905359]. GHS-R1a also has notable constitutive (ligand-independent) activity (~50% of maximal), relevant to its tonic signalling [textbook: GHSR signalling review].

Signalling cascade. STEP 1 — Receptor activation: MK-677 binds the orthosteric pocket of GHS-R1a on pituitary somatotrophs and on hypothalamic neurons. STEP 2 — Gq/11–PLCβ: the receptor couples to Gαq/11, activating phospholipase C-β, which cleaves PIP2 into IP3 and DAG. IP3 mobilizes intracellular Ca2+ from the ER and DAG activates protein kinase C (notably the PKCε isoform). This PLC/IP3/[Ca2+]i pathway is the best-characterized GHS-R1a route and is the proximate trigger for Ca2+-dependent GH exocytosis from somatotrophs [PMID 15905359; textbook GHSR signalling review]. STEP 3 — ERK1/2 / MAPK: the PLC/PKCε arm converges on ERK1/2 (p42/p44 MAPK) activation, mediating proliferative/anti-apoptotic and gene-transcription effects (CRE/SRE reporters activated in vitro) [PMID 15905359; textbook]. STEP 4 — Synergy with GHRH: on the somatotroph, GHS-R1a activation amplifies GHRH-receptor (Gs/cAMP/PKA) signalling, so MK-677 increases the AMPLITUDE of endogenous GH pulses rather than creating new ones — pulsatility and pulse number are preserved [PMID 8954023; PMID 8768828; textbook]. It also functionally suppresses somatostatin tone. STEP 5 — Hypothalamic circuit: systemically, MK-677/ghrelin mimetics activate orexigenic NPY/AgRP neurons in the arcuate nucleus, driving the appetite (orexigenic) signal that parallels the GH effect [PMID 39813130; PMID 9535057]. STEP 6 — Downstream endocrine output: pulsatile GH acts on hepatic GH receptors to raise IGF-1 and IGFBP-3; the IGF-1 rise requires intact pituitary GH (it is pituitary-mediated, not a direct hepatic effect) [PMID 10098487]. Net effect: elevated 24-h GH, IGF-1, and IGFBP-3 with preserved pulsatility.

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Oral, once daily — this is the defining PK advantage over peptide secretagogues (GHRP-6, hexarelin, ghrelin), which require injection. Studied/used as the mesylate salt (MK-0677). Bedtime dosing is common in trials because it augments the natural nocturnal GH pulse [PMID 8768828; PMID 9349662].
Absorption / bioavailability
Orally bioavailable in humans, dogs and rats — a single oral dose produces a clear GH/IGF-1 response, and the molecule was specifically engineered for oral activity (Lipinski: 1 rule-of-5 violation, ALogP 1.77, PSA 122 Å2, QED 0.54) [PMID 7624358; ChEMBL13817 calculated properties]. NOTE (Speculative/data-gap): a precise human oral bioavailability percentage is NOT established in the public peer-reviewed literature; commonly cited fitness-media figures are not traceable to a primary human PK study.
Half-life
Functionally LONG: a single oral dose elevates GH in a physiologic pattern and IGF-1 stays elevated over a full 24-h dosing interval, supporting once-daily dosing [PMID 9238854 (corpus); PMID 8954023]. A frequently cited terminal half-life of ~4–6 hours appears in secondary/regulatory summaries, but a definitive human t½ value with a primary citation is NOT robustly documented in the indexed peer-reviewed corpus (label Evidence-human: durable 24-h pharmacodynamic effect is established; the exact t½ number is a data gap).
Metabolism
Hepatic. Detailed enzyme mapping comes largely from EQUINE/doping-control work (Evidence-in vitro / animal): liver-microsome and in-vivo studies in horses identified multiple oxidative/hydrolytic metabolites, consistent with CYP-mediated phase-I metabolism [PMID 34542924; PMID 35302297; PMID 35716382]. Human CYP isoform assignment (e.g. CYP3A4) is plausible from the chemotype but is NOT firmly pinned to a primary human PK paper in this corpus (data gap).
Excretion
Parent drug and metabolites are recoverable in urine (and hair) after oral dosing, the basis for anti-doping detection assays in horses and humans [PMID 36354265; PMID 35302297; PMID 40882886]. Quantitative human urinary/biliary recovery fractions are not detailed in the indexed primary literature.
Notes
MK-677 is NOT a prodrug, NOT an ester, and NOT a steroid or hormone itself — it is a small-molecule receptor agonist that raises the body's OWN GH/IGF-1, distinguishing it pharmacokinetically and mechanistically from injected rhGH or peptide GHRPs. Whether meaningful CNS penetration occurs is uncertain: in dogs, GH-secretagogue dosing did NOT change CSF IGF-1 or GH, arguing against a strong direct central IGF-1 effect [PMID 10226793], yet ghrelin mimetics clearly engage hypothalamic neurons after systemic dosing [PMID 9535057; PMID 39813130]. A practical caution: counterfeit/grey-market 'MK-677' supplements have been found adulterated with SARMs (RAD-140, LGD-4033), cardarine and even exogenous steroids/GH [PMID 39145153].

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

HUMAN (Evidence-human, all once-daily oral): Dose-ranging in healthy elderly (2/10/25 mg) showed dose-dependent GH rise; 25 mg/day raised mean 24-h GH ~97% and restored IGF-1 into the young-adult range (141→219 µg/L at 2 wk, 265 at 4 wk) [PMID 8954023]. In young men, 5 and 25 mg increased IGF-1 dose-dependently with preserved GH pulsatility [PMID 8768828]. The pivotal long-term dose is 25 mg/day: in healthy older adults this raised GH/IGF-1 to young-adult levels and increased fat-free mass +1.1 kg vs −0.5 kg placebo over 12 months [PMID 18981485]. In GH-deficient adults, 10 vs 50 mg gave graded IGF-1 rises (+52% vs +79%) — note responsiveness tracked with residual baseline GH/IGF-1, i.e. the more deficient the pituitary, the weaker the response [PMID 9329386]. In GH-deficient children, 0.2 vs 0.8 mg/kg/day produced dose-related GH/IGF-1 increases but with high inter-individual variability (some non-responders) [PMID 11452249]. IMPORTANT GAP: there is NO modern dose-response study in young healthy athletes at the doses used recreationally; the recreational 'sweet spot' (commonly cited ~10–25 mg) is extrapolated from these older clinical 25-mg studies, not from athlete-specific dose-finding. ANIMAL: dogs respond orally from 0.125 mg/kg [PMID 7624358]; beagles show sustained IGF-1 elevation on repeat dosing [PMID 9071975]; rats given 4 mg/kg for 6 wk showed increased IGF-1 and somatic/skeletal growth (tibia, epiphyseal-plate width) [PMID 30450851]. Magnitude caveat: IGF-1 responses are large and reproducible, but downstream FUNCTIONAL gains (strength, function) did NOT scale with the IGF-1 rise in controlled trials [PMID 18981485; PMID 21067829].

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Recombinant human GH (somatropin)
    same: Both raise circulating GH activity and IGF-1 and produce anabolic body-composition shifts (↑fat-free mass), and share GH-driven side effects (insulin resistance, edema).
    different: MK-677 is an ORAL small molecule that raises the body's OWN pulsatile GH (preserving feedback and pulsatility), whereas rhGH is an injected hormone giving non-pulsatile exposure that overrides hypothalamic feedback. MK-677's effect is capped by pituitary reserve (weaker in severely GH-deficient patients) [PMID 9329386]; rhGH is not. MK-677 additionally triggers appetite via ghrelin signalling, which rhGH does not [PMID 18981485; PMID 39813130].
  • vs Ghrelin / peptide GHRPs (GHRP-6, hexarelin, ipamorelin)
    same: All are GHS-R1a agonists acting through the same Gq/PLC/Ca2+ somatotroph pathway to release GH, and all stimulate appetite to varying degrees.
    different: MK-677 is NONPEPTIDE and ORALLY active with a long once-daily duration, versus the injectable, short-acting peptides. Pharmacodynamically MK-677 is a NEUTRAL agonist of ghrelin signalling, whereas GHRP-6 acts as a negative allosteric modulator and L-692,429 as a positive one [PMID 15905359]. Ipamorelin is marketed as 'cleaner' (less cortisol/prolactin spillover) but lacks oral bioavailability and long-term human RCT data.
  • vs GHRH analogs (e.g. CJC-1295, sermorelin, tesamorelin)
    same: Both ultimately raise GH/IGF-1 and lean mass; can be synergistic because GHS-R1a agonism amplifies GHRH-receptor cAMP signalling on the somatotroph (the two arms potentiate each other) [textbook GHSR review].
    different: Different receptor and signalling: GHRH analogs act on the Gs/cAMP/PKA GHRH receptor; MK-677 acts on the Gq ghrelin receptor and additionally drives hunger. MK-677 is oral; most GHRH analogs are injected. Tesamorelin (a GHRH analog) is FDA-approved (HIV lipodystrophy); MK-677 has NO approval.
  • vs LUM-201 / other oral GHS-R1a agonists
    same: Same drug class (oral nonpeptide GH secretagogues) and same receptor target, pursued for GH deficiency.
    different: Newer agents like LUM-201 are being clinically developed (e.g. pediatric GHD) with reported GH responses exceeding standard secretagogues, whereas MK-677/ibutamoren was discontinued by Merck and never approved [PMID 35354138].

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • AOD-9604 adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-rodent
    On the lipolytic axis the two are redundant — both route fat mobilisation through GH's C-terminal lipolytic domain (AOD-9604 IS that fragment; MK-677 raises endogenous GH/IGF-1). The genuinely additive interaction is on GLUCOSE handling: AOD-9604's whole selling point is fat mobilisation WITHOUT insulin resistance (unlike full GH), whereas MK-677 reliably RAISES GH/IGF-1 and reduces insulin sensitivity / raises fasting glucose. Adding MK-677 therefore reintroduces exactly the diabetogenic GH effect AOD-9604 was engineered to leave behind — MK-677 supplies a metabolic side effect AOD lacks, rather than AOD-9604 opposing MK-677's action. Net: no clean-metabolic advantage from AOD's side is preserved once MK-677 is present. AOD-9604 monograph (no insulin resistance vs GH; PMID 11673763, PMID 11146367); MK-677 monograph (raises IGF-1 ~40-90%, raises fasting glucose / reduces insulin sensitivity) Watch: Fasting glucose, HbA1c and fasting insulin — MK-677 can raise them; also IGF-1 (MK-677-driven, not AOD). Watch for the appetite increase and water retention MK-677 adds on top.
  • Anadrol adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    MK-677 raises GH/IGF-1 and causes fluid retention/edema plus insulin resistance and elevated glucose. Anadrol independently causes marked water retention ('bloat') and raises blood pressure. Run together, the two water-retention mechanisms compound - additive edema and BP elevation - while their glucose effects diverge (MK-677 worsens insulin resistance, Anadrol tends to improve sensitivity), making net glucose harder to predict. Anadrol monograph mechanism_step 7 + side_effect_mechanisms (marked water retention, hypertension); MK-677 monograph key_effects (GH/IGF-1 up, insulin resistance/raised glucose, water retention) Watch: Blood pressure and signs of fluid overload (edema, weight spikes, breathlessness); fasting glucose/HbA1c given MK-677's insulin-resistance effect.
  • Cardarine adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    Two pressures on glucose handling collide. MK-677 raises GH/IGF-1, and chronic GH elevation is counter-regulatory to insulin — it promotes lipolysis, raises free fatty acids, and induces peripheral/hepatic insulin resistance (the glucose–fatty-acid / Randle-cycle direction), producing measurably higher fasting glucose and reduced insulin sensitivity in human studies. Cardarine independently shifts muscle fuel preference from glucose to fat. Layered together, both agents push muscle away from disposing of glucose, which can compound glucose intolerance / worsened insulin sensitivity even though neither is a 'sugar' drug per se. MK-677 → GH-driven FFA rise + insulin resistance / raised fasting glucose (12-mo RCT + OGTT data): MK-677 monograph side_effect_mechanisms (PMID 18981485; 9467542; 8954023). Cardarine glucose→fat fuel switch: monograph mechanism_steps 2; Risérus 2008 PMID 18024853. Watch: Fasting glucose, fasting insulin, HbA1c, and an OGTT if symptomatic; watch for signs of insulin resistance (rising fasting glucose, energy dips). MK-677 also causes fluid retention and raised prolactin worth tracking, but the glucose axis is the shared mechanistic concern here.
  • Clenbuterol adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    Both worsen glucose handling by different routes, so their hyperglycemia/insulin-resistance effect compounds. Acute clenbuterol drives hepatic glycogenolysis/gluconeogenesis and lipolysis, raising glucose (~+30%), free fatty acids and a compensatory insulin surge. MK-677 raises GH/IGF-1, and GH causes insulin resistance and elevated fasting glucose. Run together, the beta-2-driven acute glucose rise sits on top of GH-induced insulin resistance, pushing glucose and insulin higher than either alone and stressing glycemic control. (Note the divergence with chronic low-dose beta-2 agonism, which improves glucose disposal in rodents — the compounding concern is the acute human effect.) clenbuterol.json (acute glucose +30%, FFA/insulin surge; PMID 31887249); mk-677 monograph (GH/IGF-1 -> insulin resistance, raised fasting glucose) Watch: Fasting glucose and, where available, HbA1c and fasting insulin; watch for symptoms of hyperglycemia. This matters most for anyone with impaired glucose tolerance or diabetes risk.
  • DNP adds to a side effect ⚠ Speculative
    MK-677 raises GH and IGF-1, and because GH opposes insulin it consistently raises fasting glucose and reduces insulin sensitivity (an Established human downside). DNP creates the opposite acute demand - a hypermetabolic, glucose-consuming, glycolysis-dependent state in which glucose support is protective. The two push glucose handling in opposing directions (MK-677 impairs glucose disposal and raises glucose; DNP burns through it), destabilising glycaemic control and making blood-glucose behaviour during a DNP hypermetabolic episode less predictable. This is a glucose-axis complication (opposing effects producing an unstable, hard-to-interpret picture) rather than a shared-target interaction; the clinical magnitude is uncertain. MK-677 monograph (raised fasting glucose/insulin resistance Evidence-human; PMID 18981485) + DNP monograph (hypermetabolic glucose demand, glucose supplementation as supportive care; PMID 41487961) Watch: Blood glucose (both MK-677-driven fasting hyperglycaemia/insulin resistance and the risk of rapid glucose consumption during a DNP hypermetabolic state - watch for swinging, hard-to-interpret glucose). MK-677 fluid retention plus DNP dehydration is also worth tracking.
  • Dianabol adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    MK-677 raises GH/IGF-1 and characteristically causes fluid retention and insulin resistance. Dianabol independently drives marked sodium/water retention (the Hervey trials showed disproportionate fluid/electrolyte gain) plus estrogenic bloat. Stacked, the two compound edema and blood-pressure elevation through different routes (GH-mediated vs androgen/estrogen-mediated fluid retention). The glucose disturbance is largely one-directional - MK-677's GH-induced insulin resistance - worth monitoring on its own since Dbol does not clearly drive it; the two are complementary rather than sharing a mechanism, so neither should be assumed benign together. Dbol monograph side_effect_mechanisms (fluid/electrolyte retention, BP; Hervey PMID 61389/7018798); mk-677-ibutamoren-ibutamoren-mesylate-mk.json (raises GH/IGF-1, fluid retention, raised fasting glucose/insulin resistance PMID 18981485) Watch: Blood pressure and visible edema (two additive fluid-retention drivers); fasting glucose/HbA1c for GH-induced insulin resistance; estradiol for the androgen/estrogen fluid component.
  • Dutasteride adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    Two independent routes to worsened glucose handling converge. Dual 5α-reductase inhibition is uniquely (vs finasteride) linked to reduced insulin-stimulated glucose disposal, increased body fat and hepatic lipid accumulation — a type-1-5AR peripheral effect shown in controlled human clamp studies. MK-677 raises GH/IGF-1, and GH excess drives insulin resistance and higher fasting glucose. Stacked, both push insulin sensitivity in the same wrong direction and both promote hepatic/ectopic lipid, compounding the risk of impaired glucose tolerance. PMID 24823464; PMID 26574953 Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c; fasting insulin/insulin-resistance markers; liver enzymes and any signs of fatty-liver progression; lipid panel.
  • Ephedrine adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    Ephedrine acutely raises blood glucose (beta-adrenergic stimulation of glycogenolysis/lipolysis and counter-regulatory catecholamine action; documented in human dosing studies). MK-677, by chronically elevating GH/IGF-1 via GHSR-1a, induces insulin resistance and raises fasting glucose in humans. The two push hyperglycemia and impaired glucose handling from different directions at once (acute sympathetic hyperglycemia on top of a GH-driven insulin-resistant background), compounding the metabolic-glucose burden. Both component glucose effects are human-documented; the compounding is a mechanistic inference. PMID 12672771 Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c, and symptoms of hyperglycemia (thirst, polyuria); this axis is the shared readout to watch when combining a glucose-raising sympathomimetic with a GH secretagogue.
  • Methyltestosterone adds to a side effect ⚠ Evidence-human
    MK-677 (GHSR-1a agonist) raises GH/IGF-1, which induces insulin resistance and raises fasting glucose. Methyltestosterone independently reduces whole-body insulin-stimulated glucose uptake (demonstrated directly in humans, ~33% drop). Stacked, the GH-driven and androgen-driven insulin resistance compound, worsening glucose tolerance more than either alone. Diamond 1998 methyltestosterone reduced glucose uptake PMID 9851788; methyltestosterone monograph side_effect_mechanisms (insulin resistance); MK-677 monograph (GH-induced insulin resistance) Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c, fasting insulin/HOMA-IR; symptoms of hyperglycemia. Watch for lethargy or increased hunger from MK-677.
  • BPC-157 boosts ↑ Evidence-rodent
    Same GHR-sensitization logic as with injectable secretagogues, but oral and sustained: BPC-157 raises GHR expression on connective-tissue repair cells, while MK-677 (oral GHS-R1a/ghrelin-receptor agonist) chronically elevates GH and IGF-1, supplying persistent ligand for the up-regulated receptor and plausibly amplifying the GH/IGF-1-driven collagen-synthesis arm of repair. The interaction is on GH SIGNALING, not a shared receptor: BPC-157 changes receptor density, MK-677 changes hormone level. BPC-157 GHR up-regulation PMID 25415472; MK-677 monograph (GHSR-1a raises GH/IGF-1, insulin resistance) Watch: MK-677 notably worsens insulin sensitivity and raises fasting glucose, monitor glucose/HbA1c and watch for edema and increased appetite. Elevated IGF-1 plus pro-angiogenic BPC-157 both argue for current cancer screening. The GHR-upregulation evidence is rodent/in-vitro; treat the potentiation as mechanistically plausible, not proven in humans.
  • GHK-Cu boosts ↑ Evidence-human
    MK-677 (GHS-R1a full agonist) raises GH and, importantly, IGF-1 by roughly 40-90% in humans; the GH/IGF-1 axis is a direct stimulus to collagen synthesis in tendon, muscle and skin fibroblasts (human GH-receptor blockade lowers muscle-tendon collagen synthesis; IGF-1 raises collagen output in engineered human tendon). GHK-Cu independently pushes fibroblasts to make collagen/elastin/GAG. So MK-677's systemic IGF-1 rise can potentiate GHK-Cu's local matrix-building on the same fibroblast/collagen endpoint. This is convergent pathway logic, not a shared receptor. PMID 21498100 (GH/IGF-1 blockade lowers muscle-tendon collagen synthesis in humans); PMID 25281191 (IGF-1 enhances collagen synthesis in engineered human tendon); PMID 18644225 (GHK-Cu fibroblast collagen/GAG stimulation) Watch: MK-677 causes insulin resistance, raises fasting glucose/HbA1c and can cause fluid retention - watch glucose/HbA1c and edema. GHK-Cu adds only its own copper caution (serum copper/ceruloplasmin) with injectable gray-market use.
  • Cabergoline counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    MK-677 is an oral GHS-R1a agonist that is relatively GH-selective but still produces modest, usually transient prolactin (and small cortisol) increases via lactotroph/HPA spillover. Cabergoline lowers prolactin via pituitary D2 agonism, so it can counteract a symptomatic MK-677 prolactin rise — but the magnitude of MK-677's prolactin effect is smaller and less consistent than the injectable GHRPs (e.g. PRL +23% within the normal range; several studies found no chronic change), so the interaction is weaker and often clinically unnecessary. cabergoline.json mechanism_steps#7 + key_unknowns (MK-677 prolactin uncertain); mk-677 monograph side_effect 'Transient rise in cortisol/prolactin' (PMID 18981485; PMID 9467542) Watch: Serum prolactin to decide whether an MK-677 prolactin rise is real and symptomatic (galactorrhea, mood/libido change) before adding a dopamine agonist, and to avoid over-suppression. Because MK-677 also worsens insulin sensitivity and cabergoline has a mild glucose-lowering signal, fasting glucose/HbA1c monitoring is reasonable on the combination.
  • Metformin counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    These push blood glucose in opposite directions. MK-677 raises GH/IGF-1, and GH is a counter-regulatory hormone that antagonizes insulin (promotes lipolysis, raises free fatty acids, induces hepatic/peripheral insulin resistance via post-receptor signalling); in human RCTs MK-677 consistently raised fasting glucose (~5 mg/dL) and reduced insulin sensitivity. Metformin does the reverse: it suppresses hepatic gluconeogenesis and improves insulin sensitivity. Mechanistically metformin can blunt (counteract) the hyperglycemia/insulin-resistance MK-677 induces, which is why an insulin-sensitizer is the physiological counter to a GH secretagogue's main metabolic downside. mk-677...json (GH-induced insulin resistance; 12-month RCT fasting glucose +5 mg/dL, reduced insulin sensitivity, Evidence-human); metformin.json (improves insulin sensitivity, suppresses gluconeogenesis) Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c; fasting insulin / HOMA-IR or OGTT for insulin resistance with long-term GH-secretagogue use; watch for new acanthosis nigricans, excessive thirst/urination. No dosing guidance.
  • 5-Amino-1MQ works against Evidence-rodent
    MK-677 (a GHSR-1a agonist that raises GH/IGF-1) reliably impairs glucose homeostasis in humans: it raises fasting glucose and reduces insulin sensitivity (a classic GH-driven insulin-resistance effect confirmed across RCTs). 5-Amino-1MQ is proposed to work in the opposite metabolic direction: by raising NAD+ and activating SIRT1-type programs it is expected to favor insulin sensitivity and fatty-acid oxidation (NNMT overexpression associates with insulin resistance, and inhibiting it is proposed to improve it). So on the glucose/insulin axis 5-Amino-1MQ partially opposes MK-677's diabetogenic tendency, while on body composition the two are complementary (GH-driven lean mass + NNMT-driven fat loss). IMPORTANT caveat on the opposition's strength: the 5-Amino-1MQ insulin-sensitizing direction is rodent/in vitro only, and Nnmt-knockout mice did NOT actually improve glucose tolerance (Brachs 2019), and human adipose NNMT rises during weight loss, so the counter-force may be weaker or more context-dependent than the clean rodent story implies. The insulin-resistance axis is the load-bearing edge to monitor. MK-677 raises fasting glucose/impairs OGTT and insulin sensitivity (human RCTs, PMID 9467542); 5-Amino-1MQ insulin-sensitivity direction from NNMT metabolic-target data (PMID 38919254, 24717514, rodent/in vitro), with the counter-nuance that Nnmt-KO did not improve glucose tolerance (PMID 30552109). Net partial-opposition inferred. Watch: Fasting glucose, fasting insulin, and OGTT/HbA1c: MK-677 reliably worsens glucose tolerance in humans, so track whether the combination trends toward hyperglycemia; watch for polyuria/thirst as insulin-resistance signs.
  • Amycretin works against Evidence-human
    Amycretin IMPROVES insulin sensitivity and lowers fasting glucose. MK-677 (a ghrelin-receptor/GH secretagogue) raises GH/IGF-1 and is documented to impair glucose homeostasis, causing insulin resistance and higher fasting glucose. The two push glucose metabolism in OPPOSITE directions, so MK-677 partly counteracts amycretin's glycemic benefit; conversely amycretin may mask MK-677's glucose-worsening on labs while the underlying insulin resistance persists. amycretin.json (improved insulin sensitivity, lower fasting glucose); mk-677.json (raises fasting glucose, reduces insulin sensitivity; GH opposes insulin) Watch: Monitor fasting glucose, HbA1c and fasting insulin to detect MK-677-driven glucose impairment that amycretin's benefit could be hiding; watch the net glycemic effect rather than assuming amycretin fully offsets it.
  • Cagrilintide works against Contested
    MK-677 is a ghrelin-receptor (GHSR-1a) full agonist that activates orexigenic NPY/AgRP neurons in the hypothalamic arcuate nucleus to drive hunger — the endogenous 'eat' signal. Cagrilintide's amylin/CTR signal drives satiety through the hindbrain. They push appetite in directly opposite directions, so MK-677-induced hunger works against cagrilintide's food-intake suppression (and cagrilintide's satiety may blunt the appetite MK-677 stimulates). Both individual mechanisms are established; the net cancellation is inferred, not directly measured together. monograph mk-677 (GHSR-1a full agonist, hunger); cagrilintide.json (hindbrain satiety); corpus MoA ghrelin/amylin Watch: Track net appetite/food intake and body-weight trajectory (the two effects partly cancel). Separately, MK-677 raises GH/IGF-1 and can worsen insulin resistance/fasting glucose — monitor fasting glucose/HbA1c independent of the appetite tug-of-war.
  • Insulin works against Evidence-human
    MK-677 is an oral ghrelin-receptor (GHSR-1a) agonist that sustainedly raises GH and IGF-1. As an on-target consequence of chronic GH elevation it raises fasting glucose and reduces insulin sensitivity (in a 12-month human RCT fasting glucose rose ~5 mg/dL and insulin sensitivity fell). This opposes insulin's glucose-lowering on the shared glucose-disposal axis: the GH-driven resistance blunts insulin's effect and biases glucose upward, so exogenous insulin's net action becomes a moving target against MK-677's chronic hyperglycemic drift. MK-677 monograph side_effect_mechanisms / key_effects (human RCT: raised fasting glucose, reduced insulin sensitivity); insulin monograph key_effects PMID 11914108 Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c (expect fasting glucose to drift up on MK-677); an oral glucose tolerance test if available. Track glucose objectively and treat confusion/drowsiness as a possible insulin low regardless, since warning symptoms lag brain impairment.
  • MOTS-c works against Evidence-human
    Antagonistic on the insulin-sensitivity axis. MOTS-c improves insulin sensitivity and glucose uptake (rodent-grounded), whereas MK-677 (via sustained GH/IGF-1 elevation) is documented in humans to RAISE fasting glucose and REDUCE insulin sensitivity. The GH-driven insulin resistance from MK-677 works against MOTS-c's core intended metabolic benefit, so the two pull glucose control in opposite directions. PMID 18981485 (Nass 2008, Ann Intern Med - MK-677 2-yr trial, raised glucose/reduced insulin sensitivity); PMID 8954023 (Chapman 1996 - MK-677 GH/IGF-1 axis); PMID 25738459 (MOTS-c insulin sensitization, rodent) Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c (watch for the GH-induced rise from MK-677 blunting any MOTS-c insulin-sensitizing effect); HOMA-IR if tracking insulin sensitivity.
  • CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin overlaps (redundant) Established
    MK-677 is an orally-active non-peptide agonist of the SAME receptor Ipamorelin hits, GHS-R1a (the ghrelin receptor), signaling through the same Gq/PLC/PKC/Ca2+ cascade. Adding it to the CJC/Ipamorelin stack is largely redundant on the ghrelin-receptor arm rather than additive on a new pathway, and because MK-677 gives a long, sustained (once-daily oral) IGF-1 elevation, combining it compounds the GH-class liabilities (greater cumulative IGF-1, more fluid retention, a bigger hit to insulin sensitivity) with no new mechanism recruited. MK-677 monograph primary_target (GHS-R1a full agonist); PMID 16582936 (GHS-R1a PLC/PKC/ERK cascade); CJC-1295+Ipamorelin monograph comparison 'vs MK-677' Watch: IGF-1 (cumulative over-exposure above age-adjusted range), fasting glucose/HbA1c (MK-677 sustained exposure is notably insulin-desensitizing), and signs of edema/carpal-tunnel from additive GH excess.
  • GHRP-2 overlaps (redundant) Established (identical GHS-R1a target); MK-677 glucose effect Evidence-human
    MK-677 is an orally-active nonpeptide full agonist at the SAME receptor GHRP-2 uses (GHS-R1a). Because MK-677 gives sustained 24-h receptor occupancy while GHRP-2 gives sharp pulses, adding pulsatile GHRP-2 on top of continuous MK-677 offers little extra GH (the receptor is already driven) but compounds the shared downsides: additive appetite/food intake and, critically, additive GH-induced insulin resistance and rising fasting glucose, which MK-677 alone reliably causes in humans. GHRP-2 monograph (GHS-R1a agonist); MK-677 monograph (raised fasting glucose/reduced insulin sensitivity, Evidence-human) Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c (compounded GH-driven insulin resistance is the key hazard); appetite/weight; IGF-1 to gauge whether the added peptide is doing anything beyond MK-677 alone.
  • GHRP-6 overlaps (redundant) Established
    MK-677 is a non-peptide GHS-R1a full agonist acting on the exact same ghrelin receptor as GHRP-6. Running both does not add a new pathway — they co-occupy one receptor, and continuous GHS-R1a occupancy drives beta-arrestin desensitization/internalization, so the combined GH response is blunted rather than doubled. Their GH-axis, appetite, water-retention and insulin-resistance liabilities also stack on the same mechanism. Monograph comparisons (same receptor GHS-R1a; MK-677 balanced full agonist, long half-life); biased agonism + beta-arrestin desensitization PMID 15905359 Watch: IGF-1 (combined ceiling and desensitization), fasting glucose/insulin/HbA1c (both raise insulin resistance via GH), appetite/weight; note MK-677's long half-life sustains exposure and favors desensitization.
  • Hexarelin overlaps (redundant) Established
    Both are full agonists at the very same receptor, GHS-R1a (the ghrelin receptor), driving the same pituitary somatotroph pathway to raise GH/IGF-1 — so stacking them is largely redundant rather than additive on the GH-releasing target. Chronic co-agonism of one receptor also raises the risk of GHS-R1a desensitization, so the GH output per dose can wane, and both share a GH-driven insulin-resistance liability. (Unlike hexarelin, MK-677 is comparatively GH-selective and does not meaningfully raise prolactin, so the cortisol/prolactin concern here is hexarelin's, not a compounding of the two.) Hexarelin monograph (GHS-R1a primary target; desensitization risk, Contested for chronic use); MK-677 monograph (GHS-R1a full agonist; relatively GH-selective, insulin resistance) Watch: IGF-1 as the integrated GH readout (watch for a plateau or fall despite continued use, suggesting desensitization); fasting glucose/HbA1c since both can worsen insulin sensitivity; appetite/weight and fluid retention (MK-677 is notable for both).

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

"ORIGIN: Designed and synthesized at Merck Research Laboratories in the early-mid 1990s as L-163,191 / MK-0677, the lead oral nonpeptide GH secretagogue from a medicinal-chemistry program that built on earlier nonpeptide L-692,429; first fully characterized in Patchett et al. 1995 (PNAS) [PMID 7624358]. The USAN 'ibutamoren mesilate' carries the '-moren' stem (non-peptidic growth hormone secretagogues), assigned 1997 [ChEMBL]. CLINICAL DEVELOPMENT: Merck ran an extensive human program through the late 1990s–2000s for age-related GH decline/sarcopenia, frailty/hip-fracture recovery, GH-deficient children and adults, catabolic states, obesity and Alzheimer's disease [PMID 8954023; PMID 18981485; PMID 21067829; PMID 11452249; PMID 19015485]. APPROVAL STATUS: NEVER approved for any indication by FDA, EMA, or other major regulator. Development stalled because pivotal trials showed robust biomarker (IGF-1) engagement but failed primary clinical/functional endpoints (Alzheimer's, hip-fracture function) and surfaced a congestive-heart-failure safety signal in the elderly hip-fracture trial [PMID 19015485; PMID 21067829]. Max clinical phase reached = Phase 2/IIb (ChEMBL max_phase 2). CURRENT LEGAL/REGULATORY STATUS: It is NOT an approved medicine and is NOT a dietary-supplement ingredient — it is an unapproved investigational drug. It is PROHIBITED in sport: WADA bans growth-hormone secretagogues, and MK-677 is specifically targeted by anti-doping detection assays (human and equine hair/urine testing) [PMID 36354265; PMID 40882886; PMID 35716382]. It is widely sold grey-market as a 'research chemical'/SARM-adjacent product, with documented adulteration and quality problems [PMID 39145153; PMID 40675653]."

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • Does the durable IGF-1 elevation translate into any HARD clinical benefit in healthy users? Controlled trials repeatedly showed strong IGF-1 engagement but NO gains in strength/physical function (elderly) and NO disease modification (Alzheimer's) — so whether the lean-mass increase is functionally meaningful, or largely water/non-contractile, is unresolved [PMID 18981485; PMID 19015485; PMID 21067829].
  • What is the real long-term cardiovascular risk? The CHF safety signal that halted the hip-fracture trial is unexplained — is it pure fluid overload, a direct cardiac GH/IGF-1 effect, or confounded by a frail elderly population? No data in young athletes [PMID 21067829].
  • Precise human pharmacokinetics — definitive oral bioavailability %, terminal half-life, and the specific human CYP enzymes responsible — are not clearly established in the primary peer-reviewed literature (most enzyme data are equine).
  • Long-term metabolic safety: chronic insulin resistance and raised fasting glucose are consistent across trials, but whether multi-year recreational use causes frank type-2 diabetes (especially in predisposed users) has never been studied [PMID 18981485; PMID 9467542].
  • Is sustained supraphysiologic IGF-1 oncologically safe? IGF-1 is mitogenic and epidemiologically linked to some cancers; no long-term carcinogenicity/cancer-incidence data exist for chronic MK-677 use in humans (genuine open question, not a demonstrated harm).
  • How much of marketed 'MK-677' is actually MK-677, and at what dose? Documented adulteration with SARMs/steroids/GH means real-world risk profiles may reflect contaminants, not the molecule — a quality-control unknown that confounds nearly all anecdotal reports [PMID 39145153; PMID 40675653].

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (NOT dosing advice; framed for someone who has decided to use or is evaluating use): GLUCOSE/METABOLIC — fasting glucose, fasting insulin, HbA1c, and ideally an OGTT, before and periodically during use; impaired glucose tolerance and reduced insulin sensitivity are the most consistent, expected effects [PMID 18981485; PMID 9467542; PMID 8954023]. Anyone pre-diabetic, diabetic, or with metabolic syndrome should regard this as a major red flag. IGF-1 — serum IGF-1 (and IGFBP-3) to confirm engagement and avoid sustained supraphysiologic levels well above the age-adjusted reference range [PMID 18981485; PMID 19015485]. FLUID/CARDIAC — watch for new lower-extremity edema, rapid weight gain (much of it can be water), exertional breathlessness, or signs of heart failure; the elderly CHF signal means anyone with cardiac risk should be especially cautious and seek evaluation for these symptoms [PMID 21067829; PMID 18981485]. LIVER — baseline and periodic liver function tests (AST/ALT); a published case of reversible transaminitis warrants monitoring, particularly given grey-market product quality [PMID 40675653]. OTHER HORMONES — cortisol and prolactin may rise transiently; persistent symptoms (galactorrhea, mood/skin changes) merit checking [PMID 18981485; PMID 9467542]. PRODUCT INTEGRITY — because adulteration with SARMs/steroids is documented, unexplained gynecomastia, testicular shrinkage, or hypogonadal symptoms should prompt a hormone panel (LH, FSH, total/free testosterone, estradiol) and suspicion of contaminants, not just MK-677 itself [PMID 39145153]. ACROMEGALIC FEATURES — joint pain, soft-tissue swelling, carpal-tunnel-type symptoms, and jaw/feature changes are theoretical GH-excess concerns with chronic use and should trigger reassessment. None of this constitutes a protocol or endorsement; it is what a clinician would track to catch harm early."

Caveats

CERTAINTY LABELS: Receptor identity, full-agonist behavior, and the GPCR signaling cascade are Established (cryo-EM + pharmacology). The GH→IGF-1 rise and the glucose/insulin-resistance trade-off are Evidence-human (multiple RCTs). Lean-mass gain without strength/function gain is Evidence-human (single 1-year RCT, healthy elderly — PMID 18981485). DIRECTION/MAGNITUDE: IGF-1 rise is large and consistent (~40–90%); GH rise is pulse-amplitude (not flat), distinguishing it from exogenous HGH. ACCURACY GUARDRAILS for the teaching audience: (1) MK-677 is frequently mis-grouped with SARMs/steroids — it has zero androgen-receptor activity; the LGD-4033+MK-677 anecdotes in social media conflate two unrelated mechanisms. (2) 'More GH/IGF-1' did not translate to strength or clinical benefit in trials — raised lean mass is partly water. (3) The metabolic downside (insulin resistance, higher blood sugar) is real and is the inseparable flip side of chronically elevating the GH axis. (4) Long-term safety is NOT established in healthy/athletic users; CHF signal and hepatotoxicity are documented. Not approved anywhere. This is mechanism education, not endorsement or dosing guidance.
