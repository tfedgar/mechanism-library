Library / Anabolic-androgenic steroids

Dianabol

The original oral mass-builder: a 17-alpha-methylated testosterone analog that floods the androgen receptor for fast size and strength, but pays for oral survival with a battered liver, a crashed HDL, and stubborn estrogenic bloat.

Human / approved 22 citations
Class
Oral 17-alpha-alkylated (17-alpha-methylated) anabolic-androgenic steroid (AAS)
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR, gene NR3C4 nuclear receptor) — a ligand-activated transcription factor
Human status
Never a modern medicine in the West for performance use; historically prescribed mid-20th century (CIBA, from ~1958) and studied for muscle-wasting, osteoporosis, and debilitation. Now banned in sport (WADA) and not FDA-approved; among the most widely used illicit oral 'bulking' steroids. Human mechanistic data come largely from older clinical studies, the broader AAS/testosterone literature, and observational/forensic reports rather than modern RCTs of Dianabol itself.

At a glance

  • Rapid gains in muscle mass and strength (often 2-5 kg bodyweight and ~5-20% strength over short cycles in the broader AAS literature), driven by increased muscle protein synthesis
  • Pronounced water/fluid retention and 'bloat' that inflates early scale weight, partly estrogen-mediated
  • Estrogenic side effects: gynecomastia (breast tissue growth) and bloating, from a potent, metabolically resistant methyl-estrogen plus a crash in SHBG that raises free estrogen
  • Hepatotoxicity: cholestatic jaundice, raised liver enzymes, and with heavy/prolonged use rarer peliosis hepatis and liver tumors — the signature toxicity of oral 17-alpha-alkylated steroids
  • Severe adverse lipid shift: sharply lowered HDL ('good' cholesterol, sometimes to single digits) and raised LDL/ApoB — an atherogenic profile and a cardiovascular-risk driver
  • Suppression of the body's own testosterone and gonadotropins (HPG-axis shutdown), which can persist for months after stopping
  • Androgenic effects: acne, increased body/facial hair, and in the original clinical work, nitrogen retention and anabolic effect on body composition; aggression/mood changes reported

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Binds and activates the androgen receptor (AR) Established

    Methandrostenolone is a synthetic cousin of testosterone. Like testosterone, it slips into muscle cells and binds the androgen receptor, switching it 'on' as an agonist. ChEMBL curates it as an AR agonist whose activation drives 'dramatic increases in protein synthesis, glycogenolysis, and muscle strength over a short space of time.' ChEMBL MoA card CHEMBL1418176 (methandrostenolone, AR AGONIST, max phase 4); mechanism class consistent with testosterone action on muscle (PMID 18414389)

  2. Acts as a genomic transcription factor: more muscle protein made Established

    Once the steroid binds AR, the receptor moves into the cell nucleus and acts as a switch on DNA, turning up genes for muscle protein. The net result is that muscle fibers build protein faster than they break it down (positive protein balance), so fibers enlarge (hypertrophy). This genomic, AR-driven pathway is the core anabolic engine; non-genomic and anti-catabolic actions contribute too. PMID 18414389 (Kadi, Br J Pharmacol 2008); PMID 33269425 (McCullough, Rev Endocr Metab Disord 2021 — genomic/non-genomic/anti-catabolic via AR)

  3. Recruits satellite cells and adds myonuclei (the muscle 'memory' step) Evidence-human

    Beyond just making more protein in existing fibers, androgens wake up satellite cells (muscle stem cells) and fuse them into fibers, donating new myonuclei. More myonuclei means each fiber can sustain a bigger protein-making capacity — a structural upgrade that helps explain why steroid-built muscle responds so strongly and may leave a lasting advantage. PMID 18414389 (Kadi 2008 — satellite-cell activation and myonuclear accretion in human muscle); PMID 15248788 (Hartgens & Kuipers, Sports Med 2004)

  4. The 17-alpha-methyl group lets it survive being swallowed — and stresses the liver Established

    Plain testosterone taken by mouth is destroyed by the liver before it works. Adding a methyl group at the 17-alpha position blocks that first-pass breakdown, so Dianabol is orally active. The trade-off: the liver now has to grind through a hard-to-metabolize molecule, and this 17-alpha-alkyl class is characteristically hepatotoxic — causing cholestatic jaundice and, with heavy use, peliosis hepatis or tumors. Non-alkylated steroids do not show this. The exact injury mechanism is still uncertain; oxidative stress is the leading hypothesis. PMID 27372877 (Bond/Llewellyn/Van Mol, Med Hypotheses 2016 — 17a-alkyl retards hepatic metabolism, hepatotoxic; mechanism poorly understood, oxidative stress implicated); PMID 8527826 (Welder, direct hepatocyte toxicity of 17a-alkylated AAS)

  5. Oral passage through the liver crashes HDL and raises LDL Established

    Because oral 17-alpha-alkylated steroids hit the liver hard on first pass, they strongly disturb how the liver handles cholesterol — notably inducing hepatic lipase — driving HDL ('good' cholesterol) sharply down and LDL up. This atherogenic shift is a hallmark of orals like Dianabol (HDL can fall to single digits) and is more dramatic than with injectable testosterone. It largely reverses after stopping. PMID 33269425 (McCullough 2021 — marked HDL-C decrease, LDL-C increase); PMID 15248788 (Hartgens 2004 — depressed HDL/HDL2/HDL3); PMID 23422242 (Monroe & Dobs 2013); curated MoA card 'Hepatic lipase & lipids' (LIPC/CETP)

  6. Weakly aromatizes — but to a potent, persistent methyl-estrogen Established

    Dianabol is actually a POOR aromatase substrate (its Δ1 A-ring resists the enzyme), so it aromatizes only weakly. But the little estrogen it does form is 17α-methylestradiol — a high-affinity, metabolically-resistant estrogen — and Dianabol also crashes SHBG, freeing more active estrogen. So the bloat/gyno come from estrogen POTENCY + low SHBG, not from a high rate of conversion. Curated medsynth synthesis 'dianabol-methylestradiol-aromatase-inhibitor' citing PMID 33527655 & 18544225 (17a-methyl blocks 17-keto/estrone shunt); clinical estrogenic effects of methyl-AAS PMID 1436611 (gynecomastia w/ methyltestosterone)

  7. Crashes SHBG, raising free hormone and amplifying estrogen effects Evidence-human

    17-alpha-alkylated androgens like Dianabol suppress sex-hormone-binding globulin (SHBG), the protein that normally keeps sex hormones bound and inactive in blood. Less SHBG means more free, active hormone — including free estrogen — which amplifies the estrogenic effects even when total estrogen looks modest, and contributes to gyno and water retention. Curated medsynth synthesis 'dianabol-methylestradiol-aromatase-inhibitor' (17a-alkyl AAS strongly suppress SHBG, raising free estrogen)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Primary target: androgen receptor (AR / NR3C4), a ligand-activated nuclear transcription factor. ChEMBL classifies methandrostenolone (CHEMBL1418176) as a direct AR AGONIST (mechanism CHEMBL1871, max clinical phase 4) [Established — ChEMBL-curated MoA]. Quantitative human AR affinity: a clean, published Ki/Kd for methandienone itself is NOT in the indexed corpus or ChEMBL bioactivity table — the ChEMBL activities returned for this molecule are off-target HTS screens (Prelamin-A/C Potency 316 nM, tau ~1–4 µM, antimalarial ~10 µM), NOT AR binding, so they do NOT describe its anabolic mechanism and should not be quoted as such [Evidence-in vitro, off-target]. Relative AR binding affinity (RBA) of methandienone is generally described as modest/lower than dihydrotestosterone (the canonical high-affinity AR ligand), consistent with the broader AAS class where mibolerone/R1881 reference ligands bind at Kd ~0.1–5 nM (e.g. mibolerone Kd 0.6–5.4 nM in primate seminiferous tubule, PMID 3487680) — but a specific numeric methandienone RBA was not retrieved this run, so treat the exact figure as a known gap [Contested/unverified]. Agonist, not antagonist, at AR. Secondary/relevant pharmacology: (1) weak substrate for and weak competitive inhibitor of aromatase (CYP19A1) — the 17α-methyl C19 structure aromatizes poorly to 17α-methylestradiol [Evidence-human, parent methyltestosterone showed no serum estradiol rise]; (2) the small amount of 17α-methylestradiol formed is a genuine high-affinity human estrogen-receptor agonist that is metabolically resistant (the 17α-methyl blocks its inactivation) [Evidence-in vitro/structural inference]; (3) like other 17α-alkyl orals it strongly lowers SHBG, raising the free fraction of both androgen and estrogen [Established].

Signalling cascade. Genomic pathway (the dominant, Established route): methandienone is lipophilic and crosses the plasma membrane, binds cytoplasmic AR, displacing heat-shock-protein chaperones (HSP90/HSP70/HSP56) that hold unliganded AR inactive; the ligand-AR complex undergoes a conformational change, dimerizes, and translocates to the nucleus where it binds androgen-response elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer regions of target genes, recruits coactivators (e.g. SRC/p160, CBP/p300) and the basal transcription machinery, and drives transcription of genes that increase myofibrillar protein synthesis and reduce protein breakdown. ChEMBL's mechanism note summarizes the downstream phenotype: AR activation causes 'dramatic increases in protein synthesis, glycogenolysis, and muscle strength over a short space of time' [Established — ChEMBL MoA]. The human-trial signature (large gains in body potassium and nitrogen disproportionate to lean-mass gain, PMID 7018798/61389) is consistent with a rapid intracellular shift toward nitrogen-rich, phosphate-poor substance plus fluid/electrolyte retention rather than purely normal-muscle accretion. Proposed but less-established contributors: (a) anti-catabolic antagonism of the glucocorticoid receptor (displacing cortisol from GR in muscle); note the Hervey trials actually found cortisol ROSE on drug, so the simple anti-glucocorticoid story is not cleanly supported in those data [Contested]; (b) non-genomic/rapid membrane-initiated androgen signaling (kinase cascades) is described generally for androgens but was not specifically grounded for methandienone this run [Speculative for this compound]. NOTE: the local biochemistry tutor was offline this run, so the cascade above is assembled from ChEMBL's curated MoA plus the human-trial phenotype, not from a textbook excerpt.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral tablet (the originally marketed form, Ciba 'Dianabol'); 17α-methylation is the structural feature that enables oral activity. No ester (it is an oral 17α-alkyl androgen, not a depot prodrug). A small share of historical injectable/aqueous-suspension preparations exist but oral is canonical.
Absorption / bioavailability
Orally active specifically because 17α-alkylation sterically blocks first-pass hepatic 17β-hydroxyl oxidation that would otherwise inactivate it — this is the defining PK rationale for the whole 17α-alkyl oral class [Established; PMID 29521706 review of 17α-alkylation enabling oral use]. A rigorously measured absolute oral bioavailability figure for methandienone was NOT found in the indexed corpus/PubMed this run [gap].
Half-life
Commonly cited human plasma half-life is short, on the order of ~3–6 hours, which is why historical dosing was split through the day — HOWEVER a primary human PK study establishing this number was NOT retrieved from PubMed/corpus this run, so treat ~3–6 h as a widely-repeated but here-unverified figure [Contested/unverified]. Urinary detectability of metabolites greatly outlasts plasma parent (metabolite excretion studied extensively for anti-doping).
Metabolism
Extensively hepatic. Phase I: cytochrome-P450-mediated hydroxylation (notably 6β-hydroxylation), 3-keto reduction, and 17α-methyl-androstanetriol formation; an 18-nor-17,17-dimethyl D-ring (retropinacol) rearrangement product is also described, though that specific isomer can be an acid/work-up artifact (PMID 2214760). 17-epimethandienone and conversion to methyltestosterone are seen as metabolites. CYP450 isoforms drive the hydroxylated/dehydrogenated metabolites and UGT enzymes drive phase-II glucuronidation; azole antifungals (ketoconazole, itraconazole, miconazole) and some antidepressants significantly reduce specific methandienone metabolite formation in human liver microsomes and in vivo, confirming CYP/UGT dependence (PMID 29766657). Human-type metabolism reproduced in a humanized-liver uPA+/+-SCID mouse (6 of 7 known human metabolites; PMID 19643840) [Evidence-human + chimeric model].
Excretion
Renal — excreted in urine as conjugated (glucuronide) and free phase-I metabolites; long-detection metabolites underpin doping tests. Parent drug appears in urine mainly after high-dose/long-term use (PMID 2214760).
Notes
Key structural notes: (1) 17α-methyl = oral activity + hepatotoxicity (slowed hepatic clearance, bile-transport interference); (2) the C1–C2 double bond (Δ1) added to the methyltestosterone skeleton reduces aromatization and 5α-reduction relative to testosterone; (3) crosses BBB as a lipophilic steroid (CNS/mood effects reported for AAS class generally). Esters/prodrug concept does not apply.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

Human dose-response is poorly characterized by modern standards — there is NO modern dose-ranging RCT. The best human data are the Hervey double-blind crossover trials at a single fixed dose of 100 mg/day for 6 weeks in trained men: this produced weight gain of ~2.3–3.3 kg, large increases in total body potassium (~420–436 mmol) and nitrogen (~255 g), and increased muscle dimensions — but strength/performance did not improve significantly more than placebo, and the authors explicitly concluded the gain may be substantially intracellular fluid/nitrogen-rich substance rather than normal muscle (PMID 61389, 7018798) [Evidence-human, single dose level, n=7–11]. A separate moderate-dose double-blind crossover in highly trained athletes (Freed & Banks, PMID 1102048) also failed to show clear performance benefit. So the honest summary: at ~100 mg/day, measurable changes in body composition/electrolytes occur within weeks, but clean strength superiority over placebo was not demonstrated, and there is no validated mg→effect-magnitude curve in humans. Rodent data show anabolic/nitrogen-retention and muscle-contractile effects (e.g. nitrogen balance in rats, PMID 7274996; gastrocnemius/soleus contractile responses, PMID 6545487) but interspecies AAS dose-response does not translate cleanly (historically AAS anabolic effects were hard to reproduce in rats), so rodent magnitudes should NOT be extrapolated to human dosing. Real-world physique/bodybuilding doses are higher than 100 mg/day, but those are uncontrolled and unstudied — no how-to/protocol is given here.

How it compares

  • vs Testosterone (injectable)
    same: Both are AR agonists driving the same genomic anabolic program (protein synthesis, nitrogen retention).
    different: Testosterone is not 17α-alkylated, so it is not orally active without esterification and is far LESS hepatotoxic and less lipid-toxic; testosterone aromatizes readily to estradiol (serum-estrogen driven gyno), whereas methandienone aromatizes poorly and its estrogenic problem is receptor-level + SHBG-mediated. Methandienone causes the characteristic oral HDL crash that injectable testosterone largely does not.
  • vs Methyltestosterone
    same: Methyltestosterone is essentially the parent 17α-methyl oral androgen; methandienone is methyltestosterone plus a C1–C2 double bond (Δ1). Both share oral activity and 17α-alkyl hepatotoxicity; methyltestosterone is even a metabolite of methandienone.
    different: The added Δ1 double bond reduces aromatization and 5α-reduction relative to methyltestosterone and modestly shifts the anabolic:androgenic balance.
  • vs Oxymetholone (Anadrol) and stanozolol (Winstrol)
    same: All are oral 17α-alkylated AAS sharing the class signature: oral activity, hepatotoxicity (cholestasis/peliosis), and the hepatic-lipase-driven HDL crash.
    different: Stanozolol is essentially non-aromatizable (a DHT-derived pyrazole) and notably 'drying'/lipid-harsh; oxymetholone causes strong water retention and estrogenic-type effects despite not aromatizing. Methandienone sits between — moderate water retention with the receptor-level estrogenic risk described above. (Fatal stanozolol cholestatic hepatotoxicity is documented, PMID 37948000.)

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Anadrol compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both are oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS. The 17a-alkyl 'armor' that lets each survive first-pass metabolism is the same feature that makes them hepatotoxic (impaired canalicular bile transport via BSEP/ABCB11 and MRP2 -> cholestasis; at the severe end peliosis/tumors) and that shifts hepatic lipid handling to crash HDL and raise LDL. Running two 17aa orals stacks the identical liver-load and lipid-toxicity mechanism, so cholestatic and atherogenic injury is additive, not merely coexisting. Anadrol additionally drives red-cell mass hard (raises EPO, suppresses hepcidin to free iron), adding an erythrocytosis/viscosity burden Dbol contributes to less. Dbol monograph mechanism_steps 4-5 (17aa hepatotoxicity + HDL crash); anadrol-oxymetholone.json (oral 17aa AAS, dose-dependent liver toxicity in RCTs, EPO up/hepcidin down driving hematocrit); corpus MoA cards 'Hepatotoxicity (17aa orals)' [BSEP/MRP2] and 'Hepatic lipase & lipids' [LIPC] Watch: ALT/AST/GGT and bilirubin (jaundice, dark urine, RUQ pain are stop signals); full lipid panel expecting a deep HDL trough; hematocrit/hemoglobin for viscosity/thrombosis risk; blood pressure.
  • Dutasteride compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    The genuine shared axis is metabolic/hepatic, not estrogenic. Dianabol (oral 17α-alkylated) sharply worsens the lipid profile (HDL crash, raised LDL/ApoB) and is hepatotoxic; dutasteride independently raises hepatic lipid and reduces insulin sensitivity (its dual-blockade metabolic signal). Stacked, they push liver/lipid/glucose in the same adverse direction. Note the estrogenic angle is weak: Dianabol's estrogen problem is a potent, SHBG-freed methyl-estradiol rather than high aromatization flux, so dutasteride's testosterone-to-estradiol shunt does not meaningfully add to it; and dutasteride does little to blunt Dianabol's androgenic action (Dianabol is only a minor 5-AR substrate), so it should not be relied on to protect scalp/skin. PMID 26574953; PMID 24823464 Watch: Liver enzymes (Dianabol is hepatotoxic, dutasteride raises hepatic lipid); lipids and fasting glucose; do not rely on dutasteride to protect scalp against Dianabol's androgenic component.
  • MK-677 compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    MK-677 raises GH/IGF-1 and characteristically causes fluid retention and insulin resistance. Dianabol independently drives marked sodium/water retention (the Hervey trials showed disproportionate fluid/electrolyte gain) plus estrogenic bloat. Stacked, the two compound edema and blood-pressure elevation through different routes (GH-mediated vs androgen/estrogen-mediated fluid retention). The glucose disturbance is largely one-directional - MK-677's GH-induced insulin resistance - worth monitoring on its own since Dbol does not clearly drive it; the two are complementary rather than sharing a mechanism, so neither should be assumed benign together. Dbol monograph side_effect_mechanisms (fluid/electrolyte retention, BP; Hervey PMID 61389/7018798); mk-677-ibutamoren-ibutamoren-mesylate-mk.json (raises GH/IGF-1, fluid retention, raised fasting glucose/insulin resistance PMID 18981485) Watch: Blood pressure and visible edema (two additive fluid-retention drivers); fasting glucose/HbA1c for GH-induced insulin resistance; estradiol for the androgen/estrogen fluid component.
  • Stanozolol compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Stanozolol is a third oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS (a fatal cholestatic/hepatic-necrosis case from oral stanozolol alone is documented, PMID 37948000). Stacked with Dianabol, the shared 17a-alkyl hepatotoxic mechanism (cholestasis, raised enzymes) and the shared hepatic lipid shift (HDL crash) compound. There is also an SHBG angle: both orals lower SHBG (Dbol suppresses it; stanozolol strongly so - the effect is reliable enough to be used clinically as an androgen-sensitivity test), stacking a rise in free hormone. Because Dbol supplies free estrogen from its weak aromatization while stanozolol drives SHBG down further, the combined low-SHBG state can worsen estrogenic effects even while total E2 reads low. Dbol monograph mechanism_steps 4-5,7 (17aa hepatotox, HDL crash, SHBG suppression); stanozolol-winstrol.json (sharp SHBG suppression, oral 17aa hepatotoxicity, fatal case PMID 37948000); corpus MoA cards 'Hepatotoxicity (17aa orals)' and 'Hepatic lipase & lipids' Watch: Liver panel (ALT/AST/GGT, bilirubin) with jaundice/RUQ vigilance; lipid panel (double oral HDL crash); SHBG with free/total testosterone and estradiol so a low-SHBG, high-free-estrogen state is caught.
  • Superdrol compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Superdrol is another oral 17-alpha-methylated AAS and among the most hepatotoxic and lipid-hostile of the class. Paired with Dianabol, the two share the identical 17aa injury pathway - cholestasis via impaired canalicular bile transport (BSEP/ABCB11) plus a hepatic-lipid shift that destroys HDL and raises LDL - so cholestatic hepatotoxicity and atherogenic dyslipidemia are additively amplified. Because Superdrol is a non-aromatizing DHT-derivative while Dbol aromatizes weakly, they diverge on estrogen but converge hard on the liver and lipids; Superdrol's lack of any estrogenic buffer makes its lipid hit especially harsh. Dbol monograph mechanism_steps 4-5; superdrol-methasterone-2-17-dimethyl-5-a.json (oral 17a-methylated non-aromatizing DHT derivative, cholestatic DILI, severe HDL/LDL shift); corpus MoA cards 'Hepatotoxicity (17aa orals)' and 'Hepatic lipase & lipids' Watch: ALT/AST/GGT and bilirubin, with jaundice/dark-urine/RUQ-pain and severe pruritus as cholestasis signals; full lipid panel (expect a severe HDL crash from two orals); blood pressure.
  • Testosterone compounds risk ⚠ Established
    Both are AR agonists that aromatize, so stacked they add estrogenic load by different routes: testosterone converts readily to estradiol (serum-driven), while Dbol adds a potent, breakdown-resistant receptor-level methyl-estrogen plus a free-estrogen rise from its SHBG crash - together raising gyno/water-retention risk beyond either alone. Both also feed HPG-axis negative feedback, deepening endogenous testosterone/gonadotropin shutdown, and both push hematocrit up (testosterone strongly via EPO), stacking erythrocytosis and viscosity risk. Dbol adds the oral-specific 17aa liver and HDL hit that injectable testosterone largely lacks. Dbol monograph mechanism_steps 6-7,13 and comparisons (vs injectable testosterone); testosterone-trt.json (AR agonist, aromatizes to E2, raises erythropoiesis/hematocrit, HPG suppression) Watch: Estradiol with SHBG (both raise the estrogenic burden by different routes); hematocrit/hemoglobin for polycythemia; LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone for depth of suppression; lipids and liver markers (the oral drives these).
  • Anastrozole counteracts ↓ Contested
    Dianabol's estrogenic burden (gyno, water retention) comes partly from the small amount of 17a-methylestradiol it forms via aromatase (CYP19A1) plus a crash in SHBG that raises the free-estrogen fraction. Anastrozole is a reversible competitive aromatase inhibitor that blocks androgen-to-estrogen conversion at the enzyme, lowering serum estradiol and shifting the T:E ratio up. The important caveat this edge teaches: Dianabol is a POOR aromatase substrate (its delta-1 A-ring resists the enzyme, and the parent methyltestosterone showed NO serum-E2 rise in the one human study), so its estrogenic problem is largely receptor-level and free-fraction driven rather than high-flux serum aromatization. An AI therefore blunts only the aromatized portion and may look 'ineffective' against a receptor-level potent estrogen while still risking over-suppression (crashed E2 = arthralgia, low libido, low mood, worse lipids that compound Dbol's HDL crash). Dbol monograph mechanism_steps 6-7 and caveats (poor aromatase substrate; receptor-level + SHBG-driven estrogen; parent methyltestosterone no serum-E2 rise, PMID 2294373); anastrozole.json primary_target/mechanism_steps (reversible CYP19A1 inhibition, ~80-90% E2 suppression in women); corpus MoA card 'ISSUE: Gynecomastia' Watch: Serum estradiol by a sensitive/LC-MS assay alongside SHBG, so a low total-E2 masking a high free fraction is not missed; joint pain, libido and mood as over-suppression signals; lipid panel (an AI can further worsen the HDL already crashed by the oral).
  • Exemestane counteracts ↓ Contested
    Same estrogen axis as anastrozole, but exemestane is a steroidal, mechanism-based ('suicide') aromatase INACTIVATOR: the enzyme processes it and is permanently, covalently inactivated, so estrogen suppression outlasts plasma drug and there is no rebound-on-clearance a reversible AI can show. Against Dianabol it lowers the aromatized methyl-estrogen and raises the T:E ratio. The same poor-substrate caveat applies (Dbol's estrogenic harm is largely receptor-level/free-fraction, not high serum aromatization), so exemestane blunts only the aromatized fraction and can over-suppress. Note exemestane's steroidal chemistry is lipid-NEUTRAL versus tamoxifen (not lipid-protective), so it does not rescue Dbol's HDL crash. Dbol monograph mechanism_steps 6-7; exemestane-aromasin.json (steroidal irreversible CYP19A1 inactivator; lipid-neutral vs tamoxifen); corpus MoA card 'ISSUE: Gynecomastia' Watch: Sensitive/LC-MS estradiol with SHBG for the free-fraction caveat; joint, libido and mood signs of estrogen over-suppression; lipid panel given the oral-AAS lipid burden.
  • Clomiphene citrate opposes Evidence-human
    Two opposite estrogen strategies collide. Clomiphene is a SERM that ANTAGONIZES estrogen receptors centrally (raising LH/FSH to restart a Dbol-suppressed HPG axis) and can protect breast-tissue ER, but it does NOT lower systemic estrogen the way an AI does: in trials estradiol actually RISES on clomiphene, and its zuclomiphene isomer is a weak peripheral estrogen agonist that accumulates over weeks. So during an aromatizing oral it does nothing to reduce, and can add to, the systemic estrogen load Dbol is already generating. Its axis-restart job is real and belongs after cessation, not as estrogen control alongside Dianabol - the opposition is that it raises E2 while an AI approach lowers it. Dbol monograph mechanism_steps 6-7,13 (estrogenic effects + HPG shutdown); clomiphene-citrate-clomid.json (central ERa antagonist, E2 RISES on treatment per PMID 23875626, zuclomiphene weak estrogen agonist that accumulates); corpus MoA card 'ISSUE: Gynecomastia' (AI vs SERM distinction) Watch: LH/FSH and total/free testosterone (recovery context) plus estradiol (expect it to rise, not fall, on clomiphene); breast tenderness/gyno signs, since a SERM protects breast ER but does not lower systemic E2; any visual disturbance (blurring, floaters, scotomata) is a stop-and-refer clomiphene class effect.
  • Letrozole opposes Contested
    A teaching-critical mismatch: Dianabol (a 17-alpha-methyl Delta-1 androgen) is a POOR aromatase substrate and even acts as a weak competitive aromatase inhibitor itself, so little of its estrogenic effect comes from aromatase-made estradiol (its parent methyltestosterone showed no serum-E2 rise in humans). The estrogen that matters is a small amount of 17-alpha-methylestradiol — a high-affinity, breakdown-resistant ER agonist acting at the RECEPTOR level — compounded by a 17-alkyl-driven SHBG crash that raises free estrogen. Because letrozole works UPSTREAM at aromatase, it poorly counteracts a receptor-level, largely non-aromatase estrogen — this is SERM territory, not AI territory. dianabol-methandrostenolone-methandienon.json step 6-7 & _verify (17a-methyl poor aromatase substrate/weak AI; receptor-level methyl-estradiol + SHBG crash; methyltestosterone no serum-E2 rise); letrozole.json step 1 Watch: Sensitive serum estradiol may read deceptively normal because standard assays miss the methyl-estrogen and because dbol makes little serum E2 — so weight symptoms (nipple/breast tissue changes) over a 'normal' E2 number; adding letrozole risks crashing the body's own aromatase-derived E2 (joints, libido, mood) while failing to touch the methyl-estrogen actually causing gyno.
  • Proviron shares target Contested
    The one solid mechanistic link is SHBG: mesterolone is a DHT-derived androgen that binds SHBG tightly, and Dbol suppresses SHBG - both are SHBG levers, so together they push more free, active hormone (including Dbol's free estrogen). Note this can WORSEN, not fix, Dbol's free-estrogen-driven gyno/bloat. The popular 'anti-estrogen' framing for Proviron (competing at aromatase / blocking ER) is largely community lore and Contested in humans; the firm fact is only that mesterolone itself does not aromatize. Mesterolone is also NOT 17a-alkylated (oral activity comes from a 1a-methyl group), so unlike the oral-AAS edges it does NOT add to Dbol's 17aa hepatotoxicity - the real interaction is SHBG/free-hormone modulation, not a clean estrogen counteraction and not liver-compounding. Dbol monograph mechanism_steps 6-7 (estrogenic effects, SHBG crash); proviron-mesterolone.json (DHT-derived, NOT 17aa, binds SHBG raising free hormone; anti-estrogen potency in vivo Contested / largely lore) Watch: SHBG with free and total testosterone and estradiol (Proviron's SHBG binding shifts free-hormone interpretation, and a low-SHBG state can raise free estrogen); breast tenderness/gyno signs; note Proviron is itself a DHT androgen (hair/prostate/lipid effects) even though it spares the liver.
  • Finasteride redundant Evidence-human
    Finasteride blocks 5a-reductase (SRD5A2), the enzyme that converts testosterone to DHT - how it protects hair and prostate on testosterone. Against Dianabol this lever is largely a non-event: methandienone is a delta-1 17a-methyl-boldenone derivative whose delta-1 A-ring makes it a poor 5a-reductase substrate, so its androgenic action is not meaningfully DHT-dependent. Finasteride therefore does little to blunt Dbol's scalp/prostate/acne androgenicity - an important negative interaction to teach (a 5AR inhibitor that works against testosterone is redundant/ineffective against this compound) - and it does nothing for Dbol's real drivers (17aa hepatotoxicity, HDL crash, estrogen). Dbol monograph pharmacokinetics/comparisons notes (delta-1 double bond reduces 5a-reduction; methandienone poor 5AR substrate, muscle effect not DHT-dependent); finasteride.json primary_target (SRD5A2, T->DHT, mechanism-based inhibitor) Watch: Do not rely on finasteride to control Dbol-driven acne or hair changes; track those clinically and address Dbol's actual mechanisms (liver enzymes/bilirubin, lipid panel, estradiol/SHBG) with the appropriate bloodwork rather than a 5AR inhibitor.

History & legal status

"Methandrostenolone/methandienone was developed by Ciba (Switzerland) and introduced around 1958–1960 under the brand 'Dianabol'; it is closely tied in lore to US weightlifting/Olympic use (the Ziegler-era origin story). It was a clinically marketed phase-4 drug (ChEMBL max clinical phase 4) used historically for indications such as debilitation, osteoporosis, burns, and adjunct uses seen in the old literature (e.g. tuberculosis, corneal ulcers, aplastic anemia, psoriasis — all retrieved as 1960s–70s reports). Ciba withdrew Dianabol; FDA later revoked approval and it has no current accepted US medical use. Regulatory status: a controlled anabolic steroid. In the US it falls under the Anabolic Steroid Control Act (1990, expanded 2004; effective 2005) which places anabolic steroids chemically/pharmacologically related to testosterone in Schedule III. It is banned in sport by the IOC/WADA and is one of the most frequently detected AAS in doping control. It remains widely available on the black market but is not a legal prescription product in the US/UK for athletic use."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No modern dose-ranging RCT exists: the human evidence is two small (n=7–11) 1970s–80s crossover trials at a single 100 mg/day dose, so the true mg→muscle/strength dose-response in humans is essentially unknown.
  • A specific, well-measured human androgen-receptor binding affinity (Ki/RBA) for methandienone itself was not located — the quantitative AR affinity relative to DHT/testosterone is a genuine data gap (ChEMBL's listed activities are off-target HTS, not AR).
  • Human plasma half-life is widely quoted as ~3–6 h but a primary human PK study establishing this was not retrieved; the real value/variability is unverified here.
  • The relative contribution of the receptor-level 17α-methylestradiol pathway vs SHBG-driven free-estrogen rise to real-world gynecomastia is not quantified in humans.
  • Long-term cardiovascular outcomes (atherosclerosis, cardiomyopathy) specifically attributable to methandienone, as opposed to the AAS class, are not established by prospective human data.
  • Whether any of the historically observed strength gains exceed placebo at higher (modern bodybuilding) doses has never been tested in a controlled trial.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only (no dosing guidance). Liver: ALT, AST, GGT, bilirubin; watch for jaundice, dark urine, right-upper-quadrant pain (cholestasis/peliosis signals). Lipids/cardiovascular: full lipid panel (expect sharply lowered HDL, raised LDL/ApoB), blood pressure, and resting heart rate; consider that the HDL crash is an independent CV-risk driver. Hormonal axis: LH, FSH, total and free testosterone, SHBG (will be suppressed — a 'normal' total testosterone can hide a high free level), estradiol; watch for testicular atrophy, libido change, gynecomastia (breast tenderness/lump). Hematologic: hematocrit/hemoglobin (polycythemia risk for the AAS class). Renal: creatinine/eGFR and signs of fluid retention/edema. General: mood/aggression changes, acne, hair-line changes; in women, any virilization (voice, hirsutism, clitoral changes — partly irreversible) is a stop signal. Many liver/lipid/axis abnormalities reverse after stopping the oral, which is the most effective mitigation; 'liver-support' supplements (TUDCA, NAC) have only limited evidence."

Caveats

Dianabol-specific modern RCTs are essentially nonexistent; much mechanism is extrapolated from the broader testosterone/AAS literature, older clinical studies, and observational/forensic reports — read class-level claims as 'best available,' not Dianabol-proven. The exact molecular cause of 17-alpha-alkyl hepatotoxicity remains a hypothesis (oxidative stress is leading, not settled). The lipid crash and SHBG suppression are well documented for oral AAS as a class. On estrogen: it is NOT primarily a high-flux serum-estradiol problem — 17-alpha-methyl-C19 androgens are poor aromatase substrates and even act as weak aromatase inhibitors, so the issue is a small amount of an unusually potent, persistent receptor-level methyl-estrogen plus elevated free estrogen, not a large rise in measurable serum estradiol. One rodent study (PMID 41836328, F1000Research 2026) suggesting methandienone suppresses ovarian CYP19A1 is low-weight and not human. Effect sizes for mass/strength come from mixed-AAS athlete studies often using lower-than-real-world doses, so real-world illicit effects (and harms) may exceed what controlled studies show.
