Kisspeptin

The brain's "ignition key" for the reproductive system: a hypothalamic peptide that switches on the hormone cascade controlling puberty, fertility, and sex hormones.

Limited human data 22 citations
Class
Endogenous neuropeptide / KISS1R (GPR54) agonist; investigational reproductive-axis stimulant (peptide therapeutic, not an approved drug)
Primary target
KISS1R (also called GPR54) — a Gq/11-coupled G-protein-coupled receptor expressed mainly on GnRH neurons in the hypothalamus
Human status
Investigational / not an approved drug. Kisspeptin (and analogs like kisspeptin-10/-54, also called metastin 45-54) has substantial human data from academic clinical trials (multiple Phase 1-2, e.g. NCT00914823, NCT04648969, NCT05633966, NCT07224438; one registry record labeled Phase 3), mainly in reproductive disorders: hypothalamic amenorrhea, hypogonadotropic hypogonadism, hyperprolactinemic amenorrhea, and as a safer ovulation trigger in IVF. The core finding — that kisspeptin stimulates LH/FSH in humans — is robust and replicated. No kisspeptin product is FDA/EMA-approved for any indication; psychosexual/libido uses remain explicitly 'under investigation.' Sold as a 'research peptide' online with no quality, dosing, or safety oversight.

At a glance

  • Stimulates release of LH and FSH from the pituitary (the core, well-replicated human effect)
  • Downstream rise in testosterone (men) and estrogen/progesterone (women)
  • Can trigger or support ovulation / egg maturation in women (under clinical study for fertility and hypothalamic amenorrhea)
  • Essential trigger for puberty — loss-of-function KISS1R mutations cause failure to enter puberty
  • Modulates sexual/emotional brain processing (modest, mixed human data; not an anxiety drug)
  • Effect blunts under continuous dosing (receptor desensitization) — pulsatile delivery preserves response

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Kisspeptin binds KISS1R on GnRH neurons Established

    Kisspeptin (the protein product of the KISS1 gene) latches onto its dedicated receptor, KISS1R (GPR54), which sits primarily on the surface of GnRH neurons in the hypothalamus. This is the receptor that, when broken by mutation in humans, causes failure to go through puberty — proving the system is essential. PMID 17323132 (human neuroendocrine physiology review); PMID 25765628 (KISS1R review)

  2. KISS1R fires a Gq/11 -> PLC -> calcium signal that excites the neuron Established

    KISS1R is a Gq/11-coupled GPCR. Activation turns on phospholipase C (PLC), which makes IP3 and DAG; IP3 releases calcium from internal stores and DAG/PKC shuts certain potassium channels. The net effect is the GnRH neuron depolarizes (gets electrically excited) and sustains firing. (The Gq/PLC/IP3/Ca2+/PKC cascade is the textbook output of this receptor family; the kisspeptin-specific excitation of GnRH neurons is well documented.) PMID 25765628 (KISS1R hallmarks); PMID 23550004 (Kisspeptin excitation of GnRH neurons); corpus GPCR/PLC textbook (Biochemistry Free & Easy)

  3. Arcuate KNDy neurons act as the GnRH 'pulse generator' Established

    A special cluster of kisspeptin neurons in the arcuate nucleus also makes neurokinin B (NKB) and dynorphin — hence 'KNDy' neurons. NKB acts as the accelerator and dynorphin as the brake, and together they make the kisspeptin neurons fire in rhythmic bursts. This rhythm is what drives GnRH to come out in pulses rather than a steady stream — the timing the rest of the system needs. PMID 41423875 (KNDy as GnRH pulse generator); PMID 42309751, PMID 42309752 (GnRH pulse generator reviews)

  4. Pulsatile GnRH drives the pituitary to release LH and FSH Established

    GnRH travels the short distance to the anterior pituitary and tells it to secrete the two gonadotropins: luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH). Crucially this only works if GnRH arrives in pulses — classic monkey studies showed steady (continuous) GnRH actually shuts the pituitary down, while pulses keep it responsive. PMID 17323132; PMID 42309751 (pulsatile vs continuous GnRH)

  5. LH and FSH act on the gonads to make sex hormones and gametes Established

    In the bloodstream LH and FSH reach the testes or ovaries. There they drive production of testosterone (men) or estrogen and progesterone (women) and support sperm production or egg maturation and ovulation. This is the bottom of the chain — the actual reproductive output of switching on kisspeptin. PMID 17323132; PMID 35837314 (HPG axis review)

  6. In humans, giving kisspeptin raises LH/FSH — but route and timing matter Evidence-human

    Injected kisspeptin (IV or under the skin) reliably raises LH and downstream hormones in healthy people and in patients with certain forms of hypogonadism, and even intranasal kisspeptin can do it. But a continuous infusion blunts the response over time (the receptor desensitizes), which is why pulsatile dosing is used clinically. So it is not simply 'more kisspeptin = more hormones forever.' PMID 40215751 (intranasal, EBioMedicine 2025); PMID 42230485 (continuous infusion desensitization in men); PMID 40036336 (controlled human study, n=95)

  7. Kisspeptin also touches sexual/emotional brain circuits Evidence-human

    Beyond hormones, kisspeptin receptors exist in limbic brain regions, and human studies suggest kisspeptin can modulate processing of sexual and emotional cues — part of why it is being explored for low desire. Honesty check: a careful controlled human study found kisspeptin did NOT change anxiety, and the dramatic 'libido booster' claims circulating on peptide social media run well ahead of the modest, mixed human evidence. PMID 40036336 (no effect on anxiety); PMID 40519205 (sexual-medicine review: role still 'under investigation')

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Kisspeptins are the endogenous ligands of KISS1R (GPR54; UniProt Q969F8, ChEMBL CHEMBL5413), a class-A rhodopsin-family GPCR. The KISS1 gene product is a 54-amino-acid peptide (kisspeptin-54 / 'metastin'); proteolysis yields shorter isoforms KP-14, KP-13 and KP-10 that share the C-terminal amidated decapeptide (...Tyr-Asn-Trp-Asn-Ser-Phe-Gly-Leu-Arg-Phe-NH2), which is the minimal sequence needed for full receptor activation [Established; PMID 11387329, 18193176]. SELECTIVITY/AGONISM: all natural isoforms are FULL AGONISTS at KISS1R with broadly similar in-vitro potency; the receptor is essentially selective for kisspeptins (it was de-orphanized in 2001 by screening KiSS-1-derived peptides) [Established; PMID 11387329]. AFFINITY NUMBERS: the de-orphanization work described 'high-potency agonism indicative of a cognate ligand' for KiSS-1 peptides at AXOR12/KISS1R; literature-reported potencies for kisspeptin-10 cluster in the low-nanomolar range (EC50/IC50 ~ 1-5 nM for calcium mobilization and ~ sub-nanomolar to low-nM binding affinity in heterologous assays), but I did not retrieve a single canonical Ki value this run, so treat exact figures as Evidence-in vitro/Contested rather than a settled number [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 11387329]. Several peptide and one small-molecule KISS1R ANTAGONISTS (e.g. peptide 234/kisspeptin-234 series) have been developed as research/therapeutic tools [Evidence-rodent; PMID 23550006].

Signalling cascade. KISS1R couples predominantly to Gq/11. Agonist binding activates phospholipase C-beta (PLCbeta), hydrolyzing PIP2 into IP3 and diacylglycerol (DAG). IP3 mobilizes intracellular Ca2+ from ER stores; DAG plus Ca2+ activate protein kinase C (PKC). The Gq/Ca2+/PKC arm also drives arachidonic-acid release and a robust MAPK/ERK1/2 (and to a lesser extent p38) phosphorylation cascade — ChEMBL/Reactome annotate KISS1R under 'G alpha (q) signalling events' and 'Peptide ligand-binding receptors' [Established; PMID 11387329, 19770291]. FUNCTIONAL OUTPUT IN GnRH NEURONS: kisspeptin acts directly on KISS1R-expressing GnRH neurons to produce a slow, near-complete and long-lasting membrane DEPOLARIZATION and sustained action-potential firing (the canonical effect is mediated largely through opening of canonical transient-receptor-potential (TRPC, notably TRPC4) cation channels downstream of Gq/PLC, with inhibition of K+ conductances contributing). Direct excitation of GnRH neurons by a kisspeptin analog was confirmed by brain-slice electrophysiology and was absent in KISS1R-knockout mice [Evidence-rodent; PMID 27245315, 19770291]. This depolarization triggers pulsatile GnRH release into the hypophyseal portal system; GnRH then drives pituitary LH and FSH secretion and, downstream, gonadal testosterone/estradiol. The arcuate KNDy neurons (co-expressing Kisspeptin, Neurokinin B and Dynorphin) constitute the GnRH 'pulse generator': NKB/NK3R signalling synchronizes/excites and dynorphin/kappa-opioid signalling restrains the kisspeptin output [Established; PMID 19770291, 17323132].

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Peptide — NOT orally bioavailable (degraded by gastrointestinal proteases). In humans it has been given by intravenous infusion or subcutaneous bolus injection in research settings; no approved oral/transdermal product exists [Established; PMID 17635940, 36287566].
Absorption / bioavailability
Oral bioavailability is effectively nil (peptide). Subcutaneous and intravenous routes both produce rapid, reliable rises in plasma LH within minutes to ~30-45 min, confirming good parenteral absorption of KP-54 after SC injection [Evidence-human; PMID 17635940, 19820030].
Half-life
Plasma half-life is short and isoform-dependent: in mice KP-54 ~32 min vs KP-10 ~4 min; the longer KP-54 half-life is a major reason KP-54 produces a more sustained LH rise and is the isoform used in most human trials [Evidence-rodent for the exact minutes; the KP-54 > KP-10 durability is corroborated in humans; PMID 28464043]. Human KP-54 elimination is also rapid (on the order of tens of minutes), consistent with a single SC bolus giving a transient (a few hours) gonadotropin response [Evidence-human; PMID 19820030].
Metabolism
Proteolytic degradation by peptidases/proteases (the labile C-terminal Arg-Phe-NH2 motif and internal bonds are cleaved); no cytochrome-P450 involvement. Engineered analogs deliberately add triazole peptidomimetic bonds and albumin-binding motifs to resist proteolysis and slow clearance, dramatically extending action — direct evidence that proteolysis is the dominant elimination route [Evidence-rodent; PMID 27245315, 28464043].
Excretion
Cleared by peptide catabolism plus renal filtration/clearance of the small peptide and its fragments; analog design that slows RENAL clearance (albumin binding) markedly prolongs half-life, implicating renal elimination [Evidence-rodent; PMID 27245315].
Notes
Blood-brain-barrier handling differs by isoform and matters mechanistically: peripherally given KP-54 (but not KP-10) activates c-FOS in GnRH neurons behind the BBB, suggesting KP-54 can reach central GnRH neurons whereas KP-10 may act mainly at more accessible sites — a PK/distribution difference, not just clearance [Evidence-rodent; PMID 28464043]. CRITICAL PHARMACODYNAMIC CAVEAT: KISS1R desensitizes (tachyphylaxis) with continuous or repeated dosing — chronic twice-daily SC KP-54 in women largely abolished the LH/FSH response by day 14, so kisspeptin behaves very differently as a single pulse vs sustained exposure [Evidence-human; PMID 19820030]. No ester/prodrug forms exist for the native peptide; 'long-acting' versions are the synthetic analogs above [Evidence-rodent; PMID 27245315].

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

HUMAN DOSE-RESPONSE (KP-54, the best-characterized isoform): In healthy women, single SC KP-54 boluses across 0.2-6.4 nmol/kg produced a DOSE-DEPENDENT increase in mean LH; response magnitude was strongly cycle-phase dependent (mean LH rise over baseline: ~0.12 IU/L follicular vs ~20.6 IU/L preovulatory vs ~2.2 IU/L luteal at 0.4 nmol/kg), showing the same dose can give a near-zero or a large effect depending on background sex-steroid milieu [Evidence-human; PMID 17635940]. As an IVF ovulation TRIGGER, single SC KP-54 at 1.6-12.8 nmol/kg induced oocyte maturation in ~95% of women with a generally dose-dependent rise in mature-egg yield (highest oocyte yield at 12.8 nmol/kg) [Evidence-human; PMID 25036713, 26192876]. For brain/behavioral effects, a 75-minute IV infusion of KP-54 at 1 nmol/kg/h modulated sexual-processing brain activity (and increased penile tumescence by up to ~56% over placebo in men) without measurable adverse effects [Evidence-human; PMID 36287566, 36735255]. ANIMAL DATA extend dose-response to ovulation induction and pubertal advancement (e.g., a long-acting analog at ~0.3 nmol/mouse/day advanced puberty; ~15 nmol/ewe induced synchronized ovulation) [Evidence-rodent; PMID 27245315]. WHAT IS UNKNOWN: there is NO established human dose-response for chronic/repeated dosing aimed at sustained testosterone elevation — because the axis desensitizes with continuous exposure, more is not simply better, and no validated multi-day human protocol or maximally effective single dose for a testosterone-raising indication has been defined [Evidence-human; PMID 19820030]. KP-10 is markedly less effective than KP-54 after peripheral dosing at matched amounts because of its ~4-min half-life and poorer central access [Evidence-rodent; PMID 28464043].

How it compares

  • vs GnRH / GnRH agonists (e.g., leuprolide, triptorelin) and the GnRH 'trigger' used in IVF
    same: All ultimately drive pituitary LH/FSH; like GnRH agonists, kisspeptin can be used to trigger an LH surge for egg maturation and shows the same continuous-exposure desensitization principle
    different: Kisspeptin acts one step UPSTREAM, on GnRH neurons themselves (requires an intact GnRH system), whereas GnRH agonists act directly on pituitary GnRH receptors. Kisspeptin's trigger produces a shorter, more physiological endogenous surge and is associated with lower OHSS risk; GnRH agonists given chronically cause profound suppression (medical castration) used in prostate cancer/precocious puberty
  • vs Neurokinin B (NKB) / NK3R agonists and antagonists (e.g., the KNDy-neuron co-transmitter system)
    same: NKB is co-expressed with kisspeptin and dynorphin in the same arcuate KNDy neurons that form the GnRH pulse generator; NKB signaling helps drive kisspeptin output, so both modulate the same pulse-generator circuit
    different: NKB acts via NK3R to synchronize/excite KNDy neurons (an upstream autoregulatory loop), while kisspeptin is the final neuropeptide output onto GnRH neurons. NK3R ANTAGONISTS suppress the axis (used for hot flushes/PCOS research), the opposite direction from kisspeptin agonism
  • vs hCG (the standard IVF ovulation trigger)
    same: Both are used to trigger final oocyte maturation in IVF
    different: hCG is a long-acting LH-receptor agonist that saturates the ovary for days, raising OHSS risk; kisspeptin triggers the woman's own transient LH surge with markedly lower OHSS incidence (no moderate/severe/critical OHSS in high-risk women)
  • vs Long-acting synthetic kisspeptin analogs (e.g., Decourt 2016 'C6', triazole/albumin-binding peptides)
    same: Same KISS1R target, same GnRH-neuron excitation and ovulation/puberty effects; inactive without KISS1R
    different: Engineered for protease resistance and slowed renal clearance, giving far longer half-life and 'dramatically enhanced' pharmacodynamics vs native KP-10/KP-54 — but sustained exposure raises the desensitization concern

History &amp; legal status

"The KISS1 gene was identified in 1996 as a melanoma metastasis-SUPPRESSOR (named after Hershey's Kisses, where it was discovered, in Pennsylvania). In 2001 several groups independently de-orphanized the receptor GPR54/AXOR12/hOT7T175, showing KiSS-1-derived peptides ('metastin'/kisspeptins) are its high-potency cognate agonists [PMID 11387329]. In 2003 loss-of-function GPR54/KISS1R mutations were shown to cause normosmic hypogonadotropic hypogonadism (absent puberty) in humans and mice, establishing kisspeptin as a master gatekeeper of puberty and reproduction [PMID 16731583, 18193176]. First human administration (IV KP-54 to healthy men, Imperial College London/Dhillo group) followed in ~2005, with female and IVF studies through the 2010s [PMID 17323132, 25036713]. REGULATORY STATUS: kisspeptin is NOT an approved drug anywhere — it remains investigational, used only in registered clinical trials (e.g., IVF triggering, HSDD); there is no FDA/EMA marketing authorization. It is a research-grade peptide, not a controlled substance, but it is also not a legal therapeutic product; 'research chemical'/grey-market sale for human use is unapproved and unregulated. It is not a banned/scheduled drug per se, though as an HPG-axis stimulant it would fall under anti-doping scrutiny in the spirit of hormone-modulator rules if used to manipulate the endocrine system [Established/regulatory]."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • What is the true human dose-response and maximally effective single dose for a TESTOSTERONE-raising indication in men, and can any intermittent/pulsatile regimen sustain elevated testosterone without triggering KISS1R desensitization? (Chronic dosing causes tachyphylaxis; no validated multi-day human protocol exists.)
  • Exact human binding affinity (Ki) and receptor-occupancy/EC50 values for KP-54 vs KP-10 vs analogs — published figures vary and a single canonical human number is not well established.
  • Long-term safety of repeated kisspeptin/KISS1R agonism, including any effect on KISS1R-expressing tumors (KISS1 is a metastasis suppressor) — essentially no long-term human data.
  • The precise ion-channel and intracellular mechanism of the sustained GnRH-neuron depolarization in humans (TRPC4 vs other conductances; relative roles of Ca2+/PKC vs ERK) — established mainly in rodent electrophysiology.
  • How much KP-54 (and analogs) actually crosses the human blood-brain barrier to reach GnRH neurons, and whether central vs peripheral KISS1R drives the behavioral/sexual effects seen in the HSDD trials.
  • Whether the acute fMRI/behavioral effects in HSDD translate into durable real-world improvement in sexual function with a practical (repeatable) dosing schedule, given desensitization.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"HARM-REDUCTION MONITORING (no dosing/protocol guidance — observation only). Bloodwork that reflects the on-target axis: serum LH, FSH, total/free testosterone (men) or estradiol and LH (women) to track HPG-axis activation and to detect the expected loss of response over time (tachyphylaxis) — a flat or falling response to repeated exposure is the key red flag that the receptor has desensitized [Evidence-human; PMID 19820030]. In anyone using it around fertility/cycles, watch for signs of an unintended LH surge/ovulation or menstrual-cycle disruption, since kisspeptin most potently triggers LH in the preovulatory phase [Evidence-human; PMID 17635940]. If used (off-label, unapproved) near assisted reproduction, OHSS surveillance principles apply, though kisspeptin's signal is toward LOWER OHSS risk than hCG [Evidence-human; PMID 26192876]. General prudence: monitor for any acute infusion-type symptoms (flushing/warmth, blood pressure changes) given vascular KISS1R expression — note controlled human RCTs reported no significant adverse effects at the doses studied [Evidence-human; PMID 36287566, 36735255]. Because the compound is investigational and grey-market product purity is unverified, source/identity and contamination are practical safety concerns. None of this is medical advice — anyone considering endocrine manipulation should involve a clinician."

Caveats

CERTAINTY LABELS: The receptor binding, signaling, KNDy pulse generator, and HPG cascade are Established (human genetics + decades of neuroendocrine work). That kisspeptin INJECTION raises LH/FSH in people is Evidence-human and well-replicated. Effects on libido/sexual brain processing are Evidence-human but MODEST and MIXED — a controlled human study (PMID 40036336) found no anxiety effect, and reviews still call psychosexual use 'under investigation' (PMID 40519205). HUMAN-DATA GAP: most mechanistic detail beyond 'raises LH' comes from rodents/livestock (gilts, mice, rabbits); long-term safety, optimal dosing, and chronic self-administration in healthy fitness users are essentially unstudied. PHARMACOLOGY TRAP: continuous/high exposure DESENSITIZES the receptor and can blunt or even suppress the response (PMID 42230485, 42309751) — 'more is better' is wrong; the clinical effect depends on pulsatile dosing. HYPE FLAG: peptide-seller social posts (commercial Twitter accounts in the corpus) market kisspeptin-10 as a natural testosterone/fertility/libido booster 'without shutting down natural rhythm' — that framing oversells thin human evidence and ignores desensitization and the lack of approval or quality control. Short peptide half-life and injection/intranasal-only delivery also limit real-world use. Not medical advice.
