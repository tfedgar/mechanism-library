Library / Anabolic-androgenic steroids

Primobolan

A mild, non-aromatizing DHT-derived anabolic steroid: lean-tissue gains with low estrogen, but with classic androgen risks and surprisingly thin modern human data.

Human / approved 29 citations
Class
Anabolic-androgenic steroid (AAS), DHT-derived, non-aromatizing
Primary target
Nuclear androgen receptor (AR / gene NR3C4)
Human status
Investigational/legacy by modern standards. Methenolone was developed by Schering in the early 1960s and used clinically for decades for anemias (aplastic/refractory anemia, MDS), wasting/underweight states, osteoporosis, burns, and breast cancer (PMID 14251577, 367090, 4623546, 4952912, 38623532, 39113433). It is NOT FDA-approved and is withdrawn or unavailable in most Western markets; it remains a controlled substance and a WADA-banned doping agent (extensive metabolite/detection literature: PMID 2242348, 26259657, 36843443). Human mechanistic data are old, sparse, and largely indirect (case series, anemia trials, doping metabolite studies); there are essentially no modern controlled trials characterizing its muscle effects in healthy adults. Most claims about its tissue 'mildness' and selectivity are extrapolated from its DHT-derived, non-aromatizing chemistry and bodybuilding anecdote, NOT from rigorous human pharmacology.

At a glance

  • Lean/anabolic muscle gains via AR-driven protein synthesis (modest 'mild' reputation)
  • No aromatization to estrogen -> low water retention, low gynecomastia risk
  • Stimulates red blood cell production (used historically for anemia)
  • Suppresses endogenous testosterone / HPG axis
  • Androgenic effects possible: acne, male-pattern hair loss, virilization in women
  • Adverse lipid changes and potential cardiovascular/cardiac risk as an AAS
  • Enanthate (injectable) is non-17-alkylated -> lower direct liver toxicity than oral 17-alkylated steroids

Mechanism, step by step

  1. It's a DHT cousin, not a testosterone clone Established

    Methenolone is built on the 5alpha (dihydrotestosterone-like) skeleton with an extra 1-methyl group and a double bond at position 1. Chemically it is 17beta-acetoxy(or hydroxy)-1-methyl-5alpha-androst-1-en-3-one. Because it is already 5alpha-reduced, it is not a substrate for the aromatase enzyme, so it cannot convert to estrogen. PMID 2242348; PMID 14451915

  2. Binds the androgen receptor Established

    Like all anabolic steroids, methenolone works by entering cells and binding the intracellular androgen receptor (the same receptor testosterone and DHT use). Its anabolic signal depends on this nuclear AR — in animal models androgen self-administration and anabolic effects require a functional AR. PMID 7460859

  3. Switches on muscle genes Established

    Once bound, the receptor-steroid complex moves to the cell nucleus and acts as a transcription factor, turning on genes that increase protein synthesis and reduce protein breakdown in muscle. This is the general AAS mechanism that drives nitrogen retention and lean-mass gain. PMID 33269425; PMID 24166993

  4. Low estrogen, low water Evidence-human

    Because it can't aromatize, methenolone doesn't raise estradiol the way testosterone does. In practice that means little estrogen-driven water retention and a low gynecomastia risk — the basis of its reputation as a 'dry, mild' compound. Non-aromatizing androgens also don't elevate prolactin in humans. PMID 2242348; MESO-Rx community ref 134316236 (anecdote, lower credibility)

  5. Stimulates red blood cell production Evidence-human

    A well-documented androgen effect: methenolone boosts erythropoiesis (red blood cell formation), which is why it was used clinically for aplastic and refractory anemias. Animal work shows it acts on erythropoietin-responsive bone marrow cells. PMID 367090; PMID 4623546; PMID 4418513

  6. Resists breakdown but still stresses the body Evidence-human

    The injectable enanthate ester is NOT 17-alpha-alkylated, so it lacks the strong liver toxicity of oral 17-alkylated steroids; the oral acetate form survives first-pass somewhat differently and only ~1-2% of an oral dose is excreted unchanged, implying heavy first-pass metabolism. Like other AAS it can still raise harmful cholesterol patterns and suppress the body's own testosterone. PMID 2242348; PMID 7215163

  7. Androgenic side effects remain Established

    Being DHT-derived does NOT make it side-effect-free. As an AR agonist it can still cause androgenic effects (acne, hair loss in predisposed people, virilization in women) and suppresses the natural hormone axis. AAS as a class are linked to cardiac remodeling and, in misuse, serious cardiovascular harm. PMID 33269425; PMID 28432434

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Methenolone (1-methyl-5α-androst-1-en-17β-ol-3-one) is a 5α-reduced (DHT-skeleton) AAS that acts as an AGONIST at the nuclear androgen receptor (AR/NR3C4) — the same single-receptor mechanism as testosterone/DHT (Established; ChEMBL CHEMBL2106880; mechanistic class confirmed PMID 14050545). The best hard binding data come from Saartok 1984 (PMID 6539197), a rat/rabbit competition assay vs [3H]-methyltrienolone (R1881): RANK ORDER of relative binding affinity (RBA) at the muscle/prostate AR was R1881 > nandrolone > METHENOLONE > testosterone > 1α-methyl-DHT. So methenolone's intrinsic AR affinity is GREATER than testosterone's and exceeds DHT-in-muscle (DHT's muscle RBA is artifactually low because muscle 3α-HSD rapidly reduces DHT's 3-keto group to inactive 3α/3β-diols; methenolone resists this, Evidence-rodent). Because the assay is normalized to R1881=100, no single Ki in nM is reported for methenolone in this run — an explicit numeric gap. SHBG binding: methenolone binds human SHBG only WEAKLY (ordered 1α-methyl-DHT >> DHT > T > 3β-adiol > 3α-adiol = 17α-methyl-T > METHENOLONE > methandienone > stanozolol; PMID 6539197) — i.e. it carries relatively little 'free-testosterone-liberating' SHBG-displacement effect compared with the strong SHBG binders. Selectivity: classed as relatively 'anabolic-selective' (the old Schering myotrophic:androgenic ~ favourable), but Saartok found the muscle-AR:prostate-AR RBA ratio for AAS clustered near unity (0.4-1.7), arguing the muscle-vs-prostate 'selectivity' of AAS is NOT explained by differential AR affinity (Contested mechanism for the classic anabolic:androgenic dissociation). No meaningful progesterone-receptor or estrogen-receptor agonism; NON-aromatizable (the 1-methyl/1-ene A-ring blocks aromatase), so no estrogenic signalling (Established).

Signalling cascade. Genomic AR cascade (Established, generic to AR agonists; methenolone-specific steps inferred): lipophilic methenolone diffuses across the plasma membrane and binds cytoplasmic AR; ligand binding triggers dissociation of the HSP90/HSP70/HSP56 chaperone complex, a conformational change in the ligand-binding domain, intramolecular N/C-terminal (N/C) interaction, receptor phosphorylation and dimerization. The ligand-AR dimer translocates to the nucleus, binds androgen-response elements (AREs) in promoter/enhancer DNA via the two-zinc-finger DNA-binding domain, and recruits coactivators (p160/SRC family, CBP/p300) to drive transcription. In skeletal muscle the net transcriptional output increases protein synthesis, nitrogen retention and glycogen storage and is anti-catabolic — methenolone specifically was shown in early rodent work to raise muscle protein/ATP/glycogen and exert an anti-catabolic action (Evidence-rodent; PMID 14050545, PMID 14218826). A second, key downstream axis is ERYTHROPOIESIS: androgens acting through the DNA-BINDING activity of the AR in NON-hematopoietic cells increase renal/hepatic erythropoietin (EPO) transcription and SUPPRESS hepcidin (raising iron availability), expanding red-cell mass; the DNA-binding dependence was proven in AR-DNA-binding-domain-mutant (ARΔZF2) mice where 5α-DHT lost all erythroid activity (Evidence-rodent, PMID 32311143), and testosterone's hepcidin suppression/iron mobilization was confirmed in a human RCT (Evidence-human, PMID 31894236). This EPO/hepcidin pathway is the mechanistic basis for methenolone's historical use in refractory anemias (PMID 367090, 4418513).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Two clinically used esters/forms: methenolone ACETATE (oral tablets, historically 'Oral Primobolan'; also injectable in some markets; ChEMBL CHEMBL2106880, ATC A14AA04) and methenolone ENANTHATE (intramuscular oil depot, 'Primobolan Depot'; ChEMBL CHEMBL2106949). The parent active drug is methenolone (free 17β-ol).
Absorption / bioavailability
Methenolone is NOT 17α-alkylated; it instead carries a 1-methyl/1-ene A-ring modification that confers oral activity by slowing hepatic inactivation, but oral bioavailability is modest and first-pass metabolism is extensive. In a human study, after a 50 mg ORAL dose of methenolone acetate, the unchanged parent methenolone recovered in urine accounted for only ~1.63% of the ingested dose (Evidence-human; PMID 2242348) — consistent with heavy first-pass biotransformation and the practical need for relatively large/frequent oral dosing. The enanthate ester (IM depot) bypasses first-pass and is released slowly from the oil depot (Established for ester-depot PK; ester-specific human PK numbers for methenolone not retrieved this run — a gap).
Half-life
Oral methenolone acetate is short-acting: the major urinary metabolite remained detectable for ~5 days after a single 10 mg oral dose and parent methenolone was detectable in urine up to ~90 h after 50 mg oral (Evidence-human; PMID 6339822, PMID 2242348). The injected enanthate has a much longer effective half-life (depot release over roughly 1-2 weeks, ester-class inference; exact t1/2 not quantified in retrieved sources — explicit gap).
Metabolism
Hepatic. After deacetylation of the acetate to methenolone, Phase I metabolism proceeds by oxidation of the 17β-hydroxyl, reduction of A-ring substituents, and hydroxylation at C6 and C16; ≥12 urinary metabolites identified, the MAJOR one being 3α-hydroxy-1-methylen-5α-androstan-17-one (Evidence-human; PMID 2242348, PMID 6339822). Phase II conjugation is predominantly glucuronidation, with a distinctive sulfate fraction enriched in 16α-/16β-hydroxy isomers (PMID 2242348); sulfo- vs gluco-conjugate patterns are exploited in doping control (PMID 26259657, PMID 36843443). Specific CYP isoforms were not named in retrieved sources (gap), though hepatic CYP-mediated hydroxylation is implied.
Excretion
Renal/urinary as glucuronide and sulfate conjugates plus minor free steroid; only ~1-2% excreted as unchanged parent (PMID 2242348). Long urinary detectability of metabolites underpins WADA doping tests (PMID 6339822, PMID 11820291 hair analysis).
Notes
Key structural facts for the audience: (1) NON-aromatizable — the 1-ene/1-methyl A-ring blocks aromatase, so no estrogen, no estrogenic gyno/water retention. (2) NOT 17α-alkylated — so it largely AVOIDS the classic 17aa oral hepatotoxicity profile (cholestasis/peliosis); strikingly, the curated French hepatotoxicity database in ChEMBL records ZERO cytolytic/cholestatic/severe-hepatitis references and 0% trial frequency of acute or moderate hepatotoxicity for methenolone acetate across 6 indexed references (Evidence-human/curated; ChEMBL CHEMBL2106880, Gastroenterol Clin Biol 2004). (3) As a 5α-reduced DHT-derivative it is already '5α-reduced' and is not a substrate for further potentiation by 5α-reductase, so finasteride/dutasteride do not blunt it. (4) Acetate (oral) is short-acting; enanthate (IM) is a long-acting depot prodrug requiring ester cleavage to liberate active methenolone.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

HUMAN dose-response for physique/performance endpoints is essentially UNCHARACTERIZED in controlled trials — a major, explicit evidence gap. The human literature is old therapeutic dosing, not graded performance curves. Documented human dosing/effect points: (a) Anemia — in 19 refractory-anemia patients, methenolone (Primobolan) produced partial remissions in pancytopenia (3/6), bicytopenia (2/4), myelofibrosis (1/4) with 'negligible' side effects, but did NOT prolong survival (Evidence-human, uncontrolled; PMID 367090). (b) Pediatric/underweight — methenolone acetate/enanthate increased body weight in underweight premature infants/children (Evidence-human, old/observational; PMID 14251577). (c) Metabolic PK anchor — a single 50 mg oral dose gives measurable systemic exposure but only ~1.6% urinary parent recovery (PMID 2242348), implying that oral physique doses must be relatively high/frequent to overcome first-pass loss. (d) Adverse dose-response — in 28 menopausal breast-cancer women, methenolone enanthate induced type II hyperlipoproteinemia in 12/28 with NO clear relationship between cholesterol elevation and methenolone dose (Evidence-human; PMID 7215163), i.e. lipid harm was not cleanly dose-graded. RODENT dose-response: methenolone raised body weight, muscle protein, ATP and glycogen and was anti-catabolic in mice/rats (Evidence-rodent; PMID 14050545, PMID 14218826, PMID 5358568 muscular-dystrophy model). Bottom line: in animals methenolone is a genuine anabolic/anti-catabolic agent with positive nitrogen balance; in humans the magnitude of muscle/strength gain at any given milligram dose has never been quantified in a controlled study, and real-world potency is widely regarded as mild — but that 'mild' label rests on anecdote, not graded human data.

How it compares

  • vs Testosterone
    same: Both are AR agonists driving the identical genomic muscle-anabolic and EPO/erythropoietic cascade; both suppress the HPTA.
    different: Methenolone is non-aromatizable (no estrogen, no estrogenic gyno/water retention) whereas testosterone aromatizes to estradiol; methenolone has HIGHER intrinsic AR affinity than testosterone (PMID 6539197) yet is clinically milder/less potent for mass; methenolone binds SHBG weakly; methenolone is orally usable (acetate) without 17α-alkylation, testosterone is not orally effective unesterified.
  • vs Drostanolone (Masteron)
    same: Both are non-aromatizing 5α-reduced DHT-derived AAS used for lean/'quality' physique effects with low estrogenic activity and relatively low hepatotoxicity (non-17aa); both already 5α-reduced so unaffected by finasteride.
    different: Drostanolone carries a 2α-methyl group (anti-estrogenic, firmer feel); methenolone has the 1-methyl/1-ene A-ring. Anecdotally methenolone is reported MORE anabolic than masteron in males despite masteron's higher AR affinity — a proposed but UNPROVEN mechanism (Speculative; corpus YouTube source, not evidence).
  • vs Nandrolone (19-nortestosterone)
    same: Both bind the AR more avidly than testosterone (PMID 6539197 rank: nandrolone > methenolone > T); both used therapeutically for anemia/wasting.
    different: Nandrolone IS aromatizable (to weak estrogen) and is a progesterone-receptor agonist (progestogenic side effects, 'deca' issues); methenolone is non-aromatizing and non-progestogenic. Nandrolone is markedly more potent for mass.
  • vs Stanozolol (Winstrol) and other 17α-alkylated orals
    same: Both are non-aromatizing and used for lean/cutting goals; both DHT-family.
    different: Stanozolol is 17α-alkylated → hepatotoxic (cholestasis, HDL crash) and has VERY LOW AR affinity (RBA <0.05, PMID 6539197), acting largely via non-AR/SHBG mechanisms; methenolone is non-17aa (much lower liver toxicity, ChEMBL near-zero signal) and has HIGH AR affinity. Mechanistically quite different despite similar 'cutting' positioning.

History & legal status

"Developed by Schering (West Germany) and Squibb (USA, codes SH-567/SQ-16496 for the acetate, SH-601/SQ-16374 for the enanthate); USAN assigned 1964-1966 (ChEMBL). Marketed as Primobolan (oral acetate) and Primobolan Depot (IM enanthate). Mid-20th-century therapeutic uses included aplastic/refractory anemias (PMID 367090, 7467606), advanced/metastatic breast cancer as endocrine therapy (PMID 4952912, 1224996), post-operative/catabolic states and underweight premature infants and children (PMID 13893391, 14251577, 14218826), and adjunct in corticosteroid-treated rheumatoid arthritis (PMID 5855252). With the rise of modern oncology and EPO/transfusion medicine, therapeutic use faded; it remains available pharmaceutically in some countries (e.g. historically Bayer/Schering markets) but is largely off the mainstream market. Regulatory status: in the USA methenolone is a Schedule III controlled substance under the Anabolic Steroids Control Act (a federally controlled anabolic steroid); it is a prohibited substance in sport at all times under the WADA Code (extensive doping-control metabolite/hair methods exist — PMID 6339822, 11820291, 26259657, 36843443). In the UK it is a Class C controlled drug. ChEMBL lists max clinical phase 2 for the acetate. No retraction/safety-withdrawal flag found for the compound (ChEMBL withdrawn_flag = false)."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No controlled human dose-response data for muscle mass, strength, or body-composition endpoints — the widely repeated claim that Primobolan is 'mild' rests on anecdote, not graded clinical measurement.
  • No directly measured human AR Ki/Kd in nM for methenolone — affinity is known only as a relative (RBA vs R1881) figure in animal tissue (PMID 6539197); absolute human potency at the receptor is unquantified.
  • Specific human CYP isoenzymes responsible for the C6/C16 hydroxylation and 17-oxidation of methenolone are not identified in the retrieved literature.
  • Quantitative human pharmacokinetics of the enanthate depot (release half-life, time-to-peak, accumulation) were not found — depot kinetics are inferred from ester-class behavior only.
  • Whether methenolone's reportedly favourable anabolic-to-androgenic profile reflects a true tissue-selective mechanism or merely lower overall potency is unresolved — Saartok 1984 found AR affinity does NOT explain anabolic:androgenic dissociation, leaving the mechanism of 'selectivity' open.
  • Long-term cardiovascular outcome data (atherosclerosis, MACE) specific to methenolone are absent; harm is extrapolated from AAS-class lipid (PMID 7215163) and cardiac-remodeling (PMID 28432434) data, plus a single in-treatment MI case.
  • Reversibility timelines for HPTA recovery and for any virilization after methenolone specifically have not been characterized.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only (no dosing guidance). Bloodwork to watch: (1) LIPID PANEL — HDL-C, LDL-C/ApoB, total cholesterol; methenolone can induce type II hyperlipoproteinemia that reverses on cessation (PMID 7215163), so falling HDL and rising LDL are expected red flags. (2) HEMATOCRIT/HEMOGLOBIN/RBC — for erythrocytosis from EPO upregulation + hepcidin suppression (PMID 32311143, 31894236); rising hematocrit raises thrombotic risk. (3) HPTA panel — total/free testosterone, LH, FSH (suppression expected), plus a fertility check (semen analysis) if relevant. (4) Estradiol — expected LOW/normal since non-aromatizing; a crashed estradiol when stacked with aromatizing compounds plus an AI is a watch-point. (5) LIVER markers (ALT/AST/GGT, bilirubin) — methenolone is non-17aa with a near-absent hepatotoxicity signal (ChEMBL), so marked enzyme elevation should prompt looking at OTHER stack components or causes. (6) Blood pressure and, with chronic use, cardiac structure (echocardiogram) given AAS-class LV remodeling (PMID 28432434). (7) PSA/prostate and androgenic skin/hair changes; in women, watch for any virilization signs (voice change, hirsutism, clitoromegaly — potentially irreversible, PMID 367090). General signs to monitor: chest pain/dyspnea (CV/thrombosis), unusual fatigue or libido loss (HPTA suppression), worsening acne or scalp hair loss. Baseline-then-periodic testing is the principle; abnormal trends warrant medical review and reassessment of use."

Caveats

Harm-reduction and accuracy notes: (1) 'Mild' does not mean 'safe.' Methenolone still binds the androgen receptor, suppresses your own testosterone, and can cause acne, hair loss, and (in women) irreversible virilization. (2) Its low-estrogen, 'dry' profile is real chemistry (it can't aromatize), but this also removes some of testosterone's cardioprotective estrogen and AAS as a class are tied to adverse cholesterol shifts and cardiac remodeling (PMID 33269425, 28432434). (3) The injectable enanthate is NOT 17-alpha-alkylated, so it is gentler on the liver than oral 17-alkylated orals — but it is still a foreign androgen with systemic risks; the oral acetate is heavily first-pass metabolized (only ~1-2% excreted unchanged) and historically required relatively large oral doses. (4) Human evidence is OLD and THIN: most data are 1960s-1990s anemia/wasting trials and doping-detection metabolite studies, not modern muscle-physiology RCTs in healthy people; claims of unique tissue-selectivity are largely marketing/anecdote, not SARM-style selectivity. (5) It is unapproved in most markets, a controlled substance, and WADA-banned; black-market product carries purity/dosing/contamination risks. This is educational mechanism information, NOT medical advice and explicitly NOT dosing or how-to guidance. Lower-credibility forum (MESO-Rx) material is flagged as anecdote and kept separate from peer-reviewed claims.
