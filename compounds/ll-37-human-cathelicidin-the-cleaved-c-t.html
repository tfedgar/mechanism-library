LL-37

Your body's own broad-spectrum "defense peptide" — it punches holes in bacteria AND acts as an alarm bell that calls in immune cells. Powerful but double-edged: the same peptide that heals wounds can drive psoriasis and rosacea when it goes haywire.

Limited human data 33 citations
Class
Endogenous host-defense / antimicrobial peptide (cathelicidin family)
Primary target
Two distinct target types: (1) anionic microbial membranes (physical disruption — no single protein receptor); (2) host G-protein-coupled receptor FPR2/FPRL1 (formyl peptide receptor 2) for chemotaxis/immune signaling
Human status
Established as a real, endogenous human peptide with well-characterized biology — this is NOT a hype research peptide with no human data. Human evidence is strong for what it IS and where it's expressed (neutrophils, epithelial cells, skin, airway), its FPR2 chemotaxis (human cells, De Yang 2000), and its causal role in human DISEASE (psoriasis self-DNA/RNA sensing, rosacea, atopic dermatitis, cancer — Established/Evidence-human). The therapeutic/regenerative claims (wound healing, angiogenesis) rest mainly on in-vitro human-cell work plus rodent in-vivo models (Evidence-rodent / Evidence-in vitro) — no approved LL-37 drug, and injecting/supplementing exogenous LL-37 in humans is NOT established or safe-by-default given its pro-inflammatory, double-edged biology. Vitamin D3 is the major upstream regulator of its expression (Evidence-human).

At a glance

  • Direct microbial killing: physically disrupts bacterial (and some fungal/enveloped-viral) membranes by selectively inserting into negatively-charged lipids — kills without a specific protein receptor, so resistance is harder to evolve
  • Immune-cell recruitment (chemotaxis): acts through FPR2/FPRL1 to pull neutrophils, monocytes, and T cells to sites of infection/injury — bridges innate and adaptive immunity
  • Endotoxin (LPS) neutralization: binds and sequesters bacterial LPS, dampening septic/inflammatory overdrive (pleiotropic, lung-relevant)
  • TLR modulation: tunes Toll-like receptor signaling up or down depending on context — can blunt LPS responses yet AMPLIFY responses to self-DNA/RNA
  • Wound healing & angiogenesis: stimulates re-epithelialization, keratinocyte/endothelial proliferation, and new blood-vessel formation (mostly in-vitro human cells + rodent models)
  • DOUBLE-EDGED autoimmunity driver: complexes with self-DNA/RNA (e.g., in NETs) to trigger plasmacytoid dendritic cells → type-I interferon → drives PSORIASIS; abnormally processed LL-37 drives ROSACEA inflammation/vascularity
  • Context-dependent in cancer: promotes some tumors (ovarian, lung, breast) but suppresses others (colon, gastric) — receptor-expression-dependent

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Made on demand, then cut loose Established

    LL-37 is the active business end of a larger precursor protein called hCAP18, made by neutrophils and epithelial cells (skin, gut, airway). An enzyme snips off the inactive 'cathelin' part to release the active 37-amino-acid peptide. Its production is switched UP by vitamin D3 — which is why vitamin D status ties into innate skin/airway immunity. Dombrowski 2010, Arch Dermatol Res, PMID 20221619; De Yang 2000, J Exp Med, PMID 11015447

  2. It's a positively-charged peptide that's drawn to bacteria, not your cells Evidence-in vitro

    LL-37 is cationic (positively charged) and amphipathic (has both water-loving and fat-loving sides). Bacterial membranes are studded with NEGATIVELY charged lipids (like phosphatidylglycerol), while your own cell membranes show mostly neutral lipids outward. This charge mismatch is the selectivity trick: LL-37 readily inserts into and disrupts bacterial-type membranes but largely leaves human membranes alone. Neville 2006, Biophys J, PMID 16299073

  3. It tears open the microbe's membrane Evidence-in vitro

    Once attracted, LL-37 packs into the bacterial membrane and disorders its lipid packing — thinning and rupturing the membrane so the cell leaks and dies. Because this is physical demolition rather than hitting one specific protein, microbes find it harder to evolve resistance (a key reason AMPs are studied as antibiotic alternatives). Neville 2006, Biophys J, PMID 16299073; Zanetti 2004, J Leukoc Biol, PMID 12960280

  4. It also rings an alarm bell — calling in immune cells via FPR2 Evidence-human

    Beyond killing microbes directly, LL-37 acts like a distress flare. It binds the receptor FPR2/FPRL1 on human neutrophils, monocytes, and T cells, triggering calcium signaling and chemotaxis (cell movement toward the signal). This recruits reinforcements to the infection site, linking the fast innate response to the slower adaptive one. De Yang 2000, J Exp Med, PMID 11015447; Kim 2017, J Immunol, PMID 27974458

  5. It mops up bacterial endotoxin (LPS) and re-tunes inflammation Evidence-in vitro

    LL-37 binds LPS — the inflammatory molecule on Gram-negative bacteria that can drive sepsis — and neutralizes it, calming that danger signal. More broadly it modulates Toll-like receptors: it can DAMPEN responses to LPS yet AMPLIFY responses to nucleic acids. So it's not simply 'anti-inflammatory' — it shifts the immune dial depending on what it's bound to. Golec 2007, Ann Agric Environ Med, PMID 17655171; Scheenstra 2020, Front Immunol, PMID 32582207; Coorens 2017, Sci Rep, PMID 28102367

  6. It helps wounds close — promoting healing and new blood vessels Evidence-rodent

    At injured tissue, LL-37 pushes skin and lining cells to multiply and migrate (re-epithelialization) and stimulates endothelial cells to form new blood vessels (angiogenesis). In rodent wound models, applying LL-37 increased vascularization and wound closure. This is the 'regenerative' side that gets peptide-community attention — but most of the data are in-vitro human cells and rodents, not controlled human trials. Ramos 2011, Peptides, PMID 21693141; Wu 2010, Acta Pharmacol Sin, PMID 20676121; Zanetti 2004, J Leukoc Biol, PMID 12960280

  7. The dark side: when LL-37 binds your own DNA/RNA it drives disease Evidence-human

    The same alarm-bell power turns harmful when LL-37 complexes with the body's OWN DNA/RNA (released from dying cells or in neutrophil extracellular traps). These complexes activate plasmacytoid dendritic cells to pump out type-I interferon — a core engine of PSORIASIS. Abnormally processed LL-37 also drives ROSACEA's inflammation and redness, and it has tumor-promoting OR tumor-suppressing roles depending on the cancer. This is why 'more LL-37' is not automatically good. Takahashi 2020, Int J Mol Sci, PMID 32947991; Yang 2024, Front Immunol, PMID 39351216; Piktel 2016, Arch Immunol Ther Exp, PMID 26395996

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. FOUND sources for binding/receptors (cite from these; numbers to be extracted at synthesis): PMID 11015447 (De Yang, J Exp Med 2000) — original demonstration LL-37 uses FPRL1/FPR2 as receptor to chemoattract neutrophils, monocytes, T cells + induce Ca2+ flux (cross-desensitized by FPRL1 agonist). PMID 22069323 (Subramanian, J Biol Chem 2011) — identifies MRGPRX2 (MrgX2) as a second GPCR for LL-37 on human mast cells; notably resistant to phosphorylation/desensitization/internalization. PMID 19307752 (Lee, Exp Mol Med 2009) — LL-37 via FPRL1 inhibits SAA-induced IL-8, blocks ERK/p38 MAPK (context-dependent anti-inflammatory arm). PMID 27974458 (Kim, J Immunol 2017) — FPR2 signaling on follicular DCs drives CXCL13/BAFF, B-cell activation. PMID 15879124 (Kurosaka, J Immunol 2005) — mouse ortholog CRAMP uses FPRL1/mFPR2, adjuvant activity (rodent bridge).

Signalling cascade. FOUND mechanism/cascade reviews: PMID 40063262 (Svensson & Nilsson, Inflamm Res 2025, REVIEW) — multi-mechanism update: membrane receptor binding, Ca2+ import, dual pro/anti-inflammatory, endotoxin (LPS) neutralization, cytotoxic at 1-10 uM. PMID 42095322 (Liu, Immun Inflamm Dis 2026, REVIEW) — CAMP gene product; concentration/receptor/microenvironment-dependent switch protective->pathogenic. PMID 38972467 — SC absorption followed two-phase kinetic model; lung redistribution after IV.

PMID 38972467 — IV: rapid blood clearance, largely hepatic; quantitative t1/2 in full text (extract at synthesis). NOTE: no dedicated human PK/half-life study found — flag human gap.
Metabolism
FOUND proteolysis source: PMID 19029324 (Stromstedt, Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2009) — protease cleavage of LL-37-derived peptide by human neutrophil elastase, S. aureus aureolysin, V8 protease, P. aeruginosa elastase; basis for rapid degradation. Reviews PMID 42095322, 40869425 note rapid protease degradation as key translational obstacle.
Excretion
PMID 38972467 — IV cleared mainly by liver; SC cleared predominantly renally; uptake in lymph nodes/spleen.
Notes
PMID 40869425 (Voronko, Int J Mol Sci 2025, REVIEW) — analog/truncation/nanocarrier strategies to extend half-life and cut cytotoxicity (esters/prodrug-adjacent context). NOTE: human exogenous-LL-37 PK is essentially absent; all PK is rodent/in vitro.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

FOUND quantitative concentration anchors: PMID 40063262 (Svensson & Nilsson, Inflamm Res 2025) — cytotoxic to many human cell types at 1-10 uM; psoriatic lesions reach ~300 uM; gingival crevicular fluid in periodontitis ~1 uM (in vivo human concentration data, not administered-dose data). PMID 22069323 — sustained Ca2+/degranulation thresholds in mast cells. PMID 29091929 (Mihailovic, PLoS One 2017) — rodent dose-divergence: low-dose CRAMP immunization REDUCED atherosclerosis, high-dose EXACERBATED it (clear non-monotonic dose-response, mouse). EXPLICIT GAP: no human administered dose-response data found — state clearly.

How it compares

  • vs Mouse CRAMP (Camp/cathelin-related antimicrobial peptide)
    same: FOUND PMID 15879124, 29091929 — sole rodent cathelicidin, ortholog of hCAP18/LL-37, uses FPRL1/mFPR2, chemotactic + adjuvant, can act as self-antigen.
    different: Different sequence; the standard animal-model surrogate — translational caveat (mouse data <-> human).
  • vs KR-12 / truncated LL-37 analogs
    same: FOUND PMID 39334990, 40869425 — KR-12 is smallest active fragment of LL-37; retains antimicrobial/immune activity, vitamin-D/sunlight inducible.
    different: Smaller, lower toxicity, narrower spectrum, greater proteolytic resistance — engineered-around LL-37's cytotoxicity/instability.
  • vs Human beta-defensins / RNase 7 (alarmins)
    same: FOUND PMID 31978413, 42011032 — co-acting innate AMPs; hBD2 and LL-37 both promote TLR9 self-DNA sensing by pDCs.
    different: PMID 31978413 — RNase 7 (not LL-37/hBD2) drives keratinocyte IP-10/IFN-b self-DNA response; distinct receptor wiring; PMID 42011032 network analysis separates cathelicidin/alpha-defensin axis from S100A8/A9.

History & legal status

FOUND framing sources: Corpus MoA-gen 'LL-37' + PMID 40063262 — only human cathelicidin, product of CAMP gene -> hCAP18 precursor, cleaved C-terminally to 37-residue LL-37. PMID 16497887 (Liu, Science 2006) — vitamin-D/TLR origin of induction biology. PMID 40869425, 42095322 (reviews) — therapeutic candidate status, NO approvals; clinical translation blocked by cytotoxicity, rapid protease degradation, cost. NOTE: regulatory/legal status (no FDA approval; investigational/analog stage) to be stated explicitly at synthesis — no approval document found because none exists.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Human exogenous-administration pharmacokinetics (half-life, bioavailability by route) — only mouse SPECT/CT data found (PMID 38972467); human PK essentially unknown.
  • Whether therapeutic LL-37 dosing in humans could trigger or worsen psoriasis-type autoimmunity given its confirmed T-cell autoantigen role (PMID 25470744) — no human safety data.
  • The protective-vs-pathogenic concentration switch in humans: reviews (PMID 42095322, 40063262) describe context-dependence but the in vivo threshold in humans is undefined.
  • Relative in vivo contribution of FPR2 vs MRGPRX2 vs membrane effects to clinical outcomes — receptor pharmacology is in vitro (PMID 11015447, 22069323).
  • Dose-response shape in humans: rodent data are non-monotonic (PMID 29091929) but no human dose-response exists.
  • Long-term safety/immunogenicity of LL-37 or analogs in vivo — reviews flag this as unresolved (PMID 40869425).

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

FOUND harm-reduction signals to watch (from grounded mechanisms; NO dosing): Skin — new/worsening psoriasiform plaques, erythema, rosacea-like flares (PMID 25470744, 31465746, 31402919). Allergic/pseudoallergic — flushing, wheal/itch, mast-cell-type reactions via MRGPRX2 (PMID 22069323). Inflammatory markers — given dual pro-inflammatory arm and sepsis/COVID network role, watch systemic inflammation context (PMID 42011032). Injection-site cytotoxicity/necrosis at high local concentration (PMID 40063262, 1-10 uM cytotoxic, 300 uM in lesional skin). Cardiovascular caution flagged by rodent atherosclerosis self-antigen data (PMID 29091929). State that no validated human monitoring protocol exists.

CONTENT CAVEATS to carry into the teaching package: (1) Human-data gap on EXOGENOUS use — there is robust human biology for endogenous LL-37, but NO approved drug and essentially no controlled human trials of injected/topical LL-37 as a therapeutic; regenerative/anti-aging 'peptide-stack' framing is extrapolation from in-vitro + rodent work. (2) Double-edged: pro-healing AND pro-autoimmune (psoriasis/rosacea) — never present as uniformly beneficial; 'boosting' it is not self-evidently safe. (3) Concentration-dependent: protective at physiological levels, cytotoxic/pro-inflammatory at high levels; dose framing matters. (4) Cancer effect is tissue-specific (promotes ovarian/lung/breast, suppresses colon/gastric). (5) Much membrane mechanism is model-membrane biophysics (monolayers), not live human tissue. (6) FORUM HYPE WARNING: fitness/peptide communities promote 'LL-37' vials for healing/anti-microbial benefit with no human efficacy/safety RCTs — flag this explicitly.
