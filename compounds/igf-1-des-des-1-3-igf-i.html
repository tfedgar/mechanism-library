IGF-1 DES

A truncated cousin of IGF-1 that dodges its own off-switch proteins, so milligram-for-milligram it hits the IGF-1 receptor harder — but the "inject-it-for-local-muscle-growth" claim lives almost entirely in rodents and bro-science, not human data.

Rodent / preclinical 20 citations
Class
Truncated insulin-like growth factor-1 analogue / IGF-1 receptor agonist peptide (a single-chain ~67-amino-acid growth-factor variant; not a steroid, not a hormone you swallow)
Primary target
The type 1 IGF-1 receptor (IGF-1R), a receptor tyrosine kinase — the SAME receptor normal IGF-1 uses
Human status
No published human trials test IGF-1 DES for muscle hypertrophy or performance; its use in bodybuilding is entirely off-label/non-clinical and unmonitored. The potency, IGFBP-escape, and growth findings come from biochemistry and animal models (mice, pigs, marmosets, cell culture). Native rhIGF-1 has been studied in humans for other conditions, but that does NOT transfer to the truncated DES variant for local muscle growth. The signature forum claim — that injecting it produces growth specifically in the injected muscle — has no human evidence and is undercut by animal data showing rapid clearance and body-wide distribution. Mechanism-based safety concerns (hypoglycemia from insulin-like action; chronic activation of a universal 'grow/survive' receptor) are real but not characterized in human DES users. This is a teaching/mechanism summary, not medical or dosing advice.

At a glance

  • Activates the IGF-1 receptor -> PI3K/Akt/mTOR -> increased muscle protein synthesis (Established mechanism in muscle cells)
  • Suppresses FoxO-driven muscle-breakdown genes (atrogenes), tilting balance toward growth (Established)
  • Escapes IGF-binding proteins, making it more available and more potent per dose than native IGF-1 (Evidence-rodent/pig/primate)
  • Promotes satellite-cell proliferation and local muscle regeneration (Evidence-rodent)
  • Insulin-like blood-sugar lowering — potentially stronger and more prolonged than native IGF-1 (Evidence-rodent/primate)
  • Claimed 'inject-it-here, grow-it-here' local hypertrophy in lifters is UNPROVEN in humans (Speculative)

Mechanism, step by step

  1. It's IGF-1 with the front end chopped off Established

    IGF-1 DES is identical to natural IGF-1 except it's missing the first three amino acids at one end (glycine-proline-glutamate, hence 'des-1-3'). This isn't a lab invention only — the body itself makes small amounts of this truncated form when an acid protease clips circulating IGF-1. So 'DES' is a real, naturally occurring variant, not a wholly synthetic drug. PMID 8930132 (Ballard 1996, review); PMID 7988428 (Yamamoto & Murphy 1994, serum acid protease generates des(1-3)IGF-I)

  2. That missing tip lets it escape the proteins that normally leash IGF-1 Established

    In the body, most IGF-1 is not free — it's bound up by IGF-binding proteins (IGFBPs) that act like handcuffs, keeping it inactive and controlling where and when it works. Removing those three amino acids cripples IGF-1's grip on the IGFBPs. Classic experiments showed the binding proteins block normal IGF-1 and IGF-2 but essentially CAN'T block des(1-3)IGF-I. So DES walks around 'unhandcuffed.' PMID 2539101 (Ross 1989, IGFBPs inhibit IGF-1/2 but not des(1-3)IGF-I); PMID 1383276 (Adashi 1992); PMID 7680515 (Lindgren 1993, IGFBP-1 potently inhibited by des(1-3))

  3. Free and unleashed, it's a more potent IGF-1R agonist per microgram Evidence-rodent

    Because it isn't sequestered by binding proteins, more of the injected dose reaches the IGF-1 receptor in active form. In animal models — dwarf (lit/lit) mice, pigs and marmoset monkeys — des(1-3)IGF-I produced stronger and longer growth and metabolic effects than the same amount of normal IGF-1. So its 'extra potency' is really about availability, not a different receptor. PMID 2280209 (Gillespie 1990, enhanced potency in lit/lit mice); PMID 9415072 (Tomas 1997, IGFBP-poor IGF-1 variants more potent/prolonged in pigs and marmosets)

  4. At the cell, it flips the same anabolic master switches as IGF-1 Established

    Once it binds the IGF-1 receptor, the downstream wiring is ordinary IGF-1 biology: it turns on the PI3K -> Akt -> mTOR pathway, which ramps up muscle protein synthesis. The same Akt signal also shuts off FoxO transcription factors, which normally switch on 'muscle-breakdown' genes (the atrogenes / E3 ubiquitin ligases). Net effect in muscle cells: build more, break down less. PMID 32858949 (Yoshida & Delafontaine 2020, mechanism review); PMID 15550386 (Latres 2005, IGF-1 inversely regulates atrophy genes via PI3K/Akt/mTOR); PMID 20593312 (Glass 2010, PI3K in muscle hypertrophy/atrophy)

  5. It also feeds muscle repair cells (satellite cells) and local regeneration Evidence-rodent

    IGF-1 signaling drives muscle stem (satellite) cell proliferation and helps injured muscle rebuild. Locally delivered IGF-1 accelerates regeneration and dampens the inflammation that otherwise stalls repair. This is the biological seed of the bodybuilding idea that IGF-1 DES could cause growth right where it's injected — by acting locally before it's cleared. PMID 17264161 (Pelosi 2007, local mIGF-1 accelerates muscle regeneration, mouse); PMID 32858949 (Yoshida & Delafontaine 2020, satellite-cell/anabolic review)

  6. The 'site-specific local growth' selling point is a forum claim, not human evidence Speculative

    The popular pitch is: inject IGF-1 DES into a lagging muscle, its short window of high local activity makes THAT muscle grow. There are NO human trials testing local hypertrophy from injected IGF-1 DES. Animal data show DES is cleared from plasma fast and distributes through the body, so the 'stays put and only grows the injected muscle' story is unproven and biologically shaky. Treat it as Speculative in humans. PMID 2005410 (Ballard 1991, plasma clearance/tissue distribution of des(1-3)IGF-I in rats); no human hypertrophy trials located (PubMed, total query n=756; none in humans for muscle growth)

  7. Same receptor as insulin's cousin = real hypoglycemia and growth-signal risks Evidence-rodent

    IGF-1 and IGF-1 DES partly mimic insulin and can drop blood sugar; because DES is more available, that effect can be stronger and longer. IGF-1R signaling is also a general 'grow and survive' signal active in many tissues — a reason chronically pushing it raises theoretical concerns (e.g., promoting growth of existing abnormal cells). This is mechanism-based caution, not a dosing or how-to statement. PMID 9415072 (Tomas 1997, prolonged hypoglycaemic action of IGFBP-poor variants in pigs/marmosets); PMID 14710353 (Dupont 2003, IGF axis in cell-cycle progression)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. IGF-1 DES (DES(1-3)IGF-I) is human IGF-I lacking the N-terminal tripeptide Gly-Pro-Glu (residues 1-3); the critical change is loss of Glu-3. TARGET / AFFINITY: It is a full AGONIST at the type 1 IGF receptor (IGF-1R, a transmembrane receptor tyrosine kinase). Its IGF-1R affinity is essentially IDENTICAL to intact IGF-I: in HT29-D4 colon-carcinoma cells, both IGF-I and DES(1-3)IGF-I bound the type-1 receptor with KD ~5x10^-10 M (~0.5 nM) [Established/in vitro, PMID 1281142]; in rat osteoblast IGF-1R studies, DES was bound as well as or marginally better than IGF-I at 0.2 nM [Evidence-rodent/in vitro, PMID 1647310]. THE DEFINING PROPERTY is collapse of IGF-binding-protein (IGFBP) affinity: deleting Glu-3 removes a key contact for IGFBPs, so DES binds IGFBPs ~10- to 100-fold more weakly. It was ~3-fold more potent than intact IGF-I (and ~10-fold more potent than insulin) at suppressing IGFBP-1 in HepG2 medium [PMID 7680515]; in calvarial cultures DES bound IGFBPs ~100-fold less well than IGF-I [PMID 1647310]; purified IGFBPs failed to inhibit DES bioactivity while strongly inhibiting IGF-I/IGF-II [PMID 2539101]. DES also weakly crosses to the insulin receptor (as does IGF-I, low affinity) and, like IGF-I, has negligible affinity for the IGF-II/mannose-6-phosphate receptor. NET: same receptor affinity as IGF-I, but far less sequestration by IGFBPs, so much higher FREE/bioavailable fraction reaching IGF-1R; this is why it is ~10-fold more potent than IGF-I on cultured cells [Established, review PMID 8930132].

Signalling cascade. Agonist binding to the IGF-1R extracellular alpha-subunits triggers a conformational change and trans-autophosphorylation of tyrosines in the beta-subunit intracellular kinase domain (activation-loop Y1131/1135/1136). The activated kinase phosphorylates docking adaptors IRS-1/IRS-2 and Shc. Two principal arms follow: (1) PI3K/AKT/mTOR - IRS-1 phosphotyrosines recruit the p85 subunit of class-IA PI3K, generating PIP3, which recruits PDK1 and AKT; AKT activates mTORC1 (via inhibition of TSC1/2 and PRAS40) driving cap-dependent translation (p70S6K, 4E-BP1) and protein synthesis, and inhibits FOXO and the proteasomal atrophy program (suppressing MuRF1/atrogin-1), i.e. simultaneously increasing protein synthesis and decreasing protein breakdown - the hallmark dual anabolic action seen with DES in L6 myoblasts [PMID 1378742] and rat muscle [PMID 1371669]. AKT also activates GSK3 inhibition and glucose uptake (insulin-like metabolic effects). (2) RAS/RAF/MEK/ERK (MAPK) - Shc/Grb2/SOS activate Ras, driving the mitogenic/proliferative and differentiation program (cyclin D, cell-cycle entry). Because DES delivers the SAME IGF-1R signal as IGF-I but with far less IGFBP buffering, the cascade is engaged at lower nominal concentrations and for the locally available free peptide. [Cascade = Established canonical IGF-1R biology; DES-specific functional readouts Evidence-rodent/in vitro, PMID 1378742, 1281142, 2539101]

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Parenteral only (peptide; orally degraded). In animal studies given by subcutaneous injection or continuous SC osmotic minipump, or IV for kinetic tracer work. No approved human formulation. A research transdermal cell-penetrating-peptide delivery of DES(1-3)IGF-I has been demonstrated in vitro/preclinically but is not a clinical product [Evidence-in vitro, PMID 35760980].
Absorption / bioavailability
Not orally bioavailable (peptide hydrolysed in gut). After SC administration absorption is rapid for a ~7 kDa peptide; no validated human bioavailability figures exist. Because DES does not bind IGFBPs, the absorbed peptide circulates largely as FREE peptide rather than in 150 kDa IGFBP-3/ALS ternary complexes [Evidence-rodent, PMID 2005410].
Half-life
Markedly SHORTER than IGF-I because the IGFBP reservoir that prolongs IGF-I survival is bypassed. In rats, total plasma clearance of DES(1-3)IGF-I was 4.59 mL/min/kg vs 1.20 for IGF-I and 1.34 for IGF-II - i.e. ~3.5-4x faster clearance; steady-state volume of distribution was larger for DES (461 mL/kg vs 167 for IGF-I) [Evidence-rodent, PMID 2005410]. For context, IGFBP-3/ALS binding normally extends intact IGF-I half-life from minutes (free) to ~16 h (bound) [textbook/Established]; DES, being unbound, sits at the short (minutes-scale) end. No human half-life data exist.
Metabolism
As a small protein, catabolised by receptor-mediated internalisation/degradation and by proteolysis; the kidney is the dominant site of uptake/clearance (radiolabel accumulated in kidney far more than other organs for all IGF peptides, and most for DES) [Evidence-rodent, PMID 2005410]. No specific CYP/enzyme pathway (not a small-molecule substrate).
Excretion
Renal handling dominates - kidney showed the greatest tracer accumulation; free (non-IGFBP-bound) peptide is filtered/taken up renally more readily than ternary-complex-bound IGF-I, consistent with the higher clearance and larger Vd [Evidence-rodent, PMID 2005410].
Notes
DES is NOT an ester or prodrug - it is a naturally occurring truncated IGF-I found in bovine colostrum, human brain and porcine uterus, generated by post-translational cleavage [PMID 8930132]. The pharmacology is driven entirely by its low IGFBP affinity (high free fraction, fast clearance, larger Vd, more tissue/gut delivery). DES distributed into gut, skin, brain and adrenals MORE than IGF-I (rank order DES>IGF-I>IGF-II), mirroring the inverse of IGFBP binding - the authors proposed IGFBPs restrain transfer of growth factor to tissue [PMID 2005410]. Human BBB penetration of exogenous DES is not characterised; the brain accumulation seen was a rodent tracer finding.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

Human dose-response is UNKNOWN - there are no published human pharmacodynamic or dose-finding studies of DES(1-3)IGF-I; all dose-response data are rodent. RODENT GROWTH (GH-deficient lit/lit mice): 30 ug/day IGF-I, 30 ug/day DES, or 3 ug/day DES for 3 weeks all increased body length substantially vs controls, whereas 3 ug/day IGF-I did NOT - i.e. DES at 1/10th the dose matched IGF-I, demonstrating ~10-fold higher in-vivo potency; the low DES dose selectively raised kidney and heart weight, higher doses raised liver/lung/stomach [Evidence-rodent, PMID 2280209]. CATABOLIC-STATE ANABOLISM (dexamethasone-treated rats, SC osmotic pump, 7 days): IGF-I dose-dependently reversed steroid-induced wasting (highest dose 695 ug/day gave +6 g body weight vs -19 g in dex-only and +18 g pair-fed); DES(1-3)IGF-I and LR3-IGF-I were ~2.5-fold more potent than IGF-I, increased gut weight up to 45%, raised muscle protein synthesis and lowered breakdown (3-methylhistidine excretion fell from 83.5 to 65.1 umol/kg/7d at top IGF-I dose) [Evidence-rodent, PMID 1371669]. CELL CULTURE: DES is generally ~10-fold more potent than IGF-I at stimulating hypertrophy/proliferation, the gap appearing specifically in IGFBP-secreting cells and vanishing in IGFBP-free chick fibroblasts [PMID 8930132, 1378742, 2539101]. Effective in-vitro concentrations were low-nanomolar (e.g. GH secretion from pituitary cells max at 0.1 ug/L for DES vs 1 ug/L IGF-I; IGFBP-1 suppression 3-10 ug/L) [PMID 1715381, 7680515]. The species/route gap is large: human effective doses, ceiling effects, and safety thresholds are NOT established.

How it compares

  • vs IGF-I (mecasermin, recombinant human IGF-I)
    same: Identical IGF-1R agonist with essentially the same receptor affinity (~0.5 nM) and the same downstream PI3K/AKT/mTOR + Ras/MAPK cascade and anabolic/metabolic effects.
    different: DES lacks N-terminal Gly-Pro-Glu (esp. Glu-3), so binds IGFBPs ~10-100x more weakly -> higher free fraction, ~10-fold higher cell potency and ~2.5-fold higher in-vivo anabolic potency, but ~3.5-4x faster clearance and shorter half-life. IGF-I is the only one with FDA approval (mecasermin, severe primary IGF-I deficiency); DES is not a medicine.
  • vs LR3-IGF-I (Long R3 IGF-I)
    same: Both are IGFBP-evading IGF-I analogues with greatly enhanced potency in IGFBP-rich settings; in dexamethasone rats both were ~2.5x more potent than IGF-I.
    different: LR3 adds a 13-aa N-terminal extension AND Arg3 substitution; notably LR3 binds the IGF-1R ~3-fold LESS well than IGF-I yet is still more potent in vivo because IGFBP escape dominates. LR3 has a longer half-life than DES (the extension/Arg3 confers more stability), whereas DES is shorter-acting. [PMID 1371669, 1378742]
  • vs IGF-II
    same: Acts on IGF-1R and is mitogenic; in the same rodent clearance study behaved similarly to IGF-I (clearance 1.34 vs 1.20 mL/min/kg).
    different: IGF-II binds IGFBPs even more avidly than IGF-I (so is more sequestered/less potent free), and binds the IGF-II/M6P receptor (a clearance/scavenger receptor) which IGF-I and DES do not. DES is the deliberately IGFBP-stripped opposite extreme. [PMID 2005410, 2539101]
  • vs Insulin
    same: Shares the AKT/metabolic (glucose-lowering) arm; IGF-I peptides have low-affinity insulin-receptor cross-reactivity.
    different: Insulin acts mainly via the insulin receptor for acute metabolism; DES acts via IGF-1R for sustained growth/anabolism. DES was ~10-fold more potent than insulin at suppressing IGFBP-1 in HepG2 cells, reflecting different receptor engagement. [PMID 7680515]

History & legal status

"DES(1-3)IGF-I is a NATURALLY occurring truncated form of human IGF-I (Gly-Pro-Glu deleted from the N-terminus), first isolated from bovine colostrum, and also found in human brain and porcine uterus - i.e. a physiological post-translational cleavage product, not a designed drug [PMID 8930132]. It was characterised in the late 1980s-1990s largely by the Adelaide/CSIRO group (Ballard, Francis, Read, Tomas, Wallace) as a research tool to dissect IGF-I vs IGFBP biology and as a candidate for catabolic-wasting and gut-growth indications. It can be made recombinantly in E. coli (often as fusion-protein analogues) [PMID 1378742]. REGULATORY: DES(1-3)IGF-I has NO drug approval in any jurisdiction - no FDA/EMA marketing authorisation, no completed human efficacy trials. (By contrast, full-length recombinant IGF-I, mecasermin/Increlex, is FDA-approved for severe primary IGF-I deficiency, and mecasermin rinfabate combined IGF-I with IGFBP-3.) DES is sold only as a grey-market 'research chemical'/peptide in the fitness/bodybuilding space, not as a regulated medicine; quality, identity and sterility of such material are unverified. In sport, IGF-1 and its analogues/releasing factors are PROHIBITED at all times under the WADA Prohibited List (class S2, growth factors), so use is an anti-doping violation. Legal status as a human therapeutic: unapproved/illegal to market for human use in major regulators."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No human pharmacokinetics or pharmacodynamics: half-life, bioavailability, effective dose, and ceiling effects in humans are entirely uncharacterised - all quantitative data are rodent or in-vitro.
  • Long-term neoplastic risk of chronic unbuffered IGF-1R agonism in humans is unquantified; mechanistic plausibility and IGF-I epidemiology exist, but no DES-specific human cancer data.
  • Whether the rodent organ-overgrowth pattern (kidney/heart/gut/liver) translates to humans, and at what cumulative exposure cardiac or renal remodelling becomes fixed vs reversible.
  • Real-world grey-market product identity/purity: whether vials sold as 'IGF-1 DES' actually contain correctly folded DES(1-3)IGF-I, full-length IGF-I, LR3, or degraded/contaminated peptide - no independent verification.
  • Tissue selectivity in humans: the rodent finding that low IGFBP binding shifts biodistribution toward gut/skin/brain is unconfirmed in humans, including any CNS exposure.
  • Interaction with endogenous GH/IGF-I axis and IGFBP-3/ALS dynamics during repeated dosing in humans (degree and reversibility of feedback suppression).

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (NOT a how-to; describes signs/labs a clinician would watch). METABOLIC: blood glucose - IGF-1R/insulin-like signalling can cause hypoglycaemia, especially early after a dose; watch for shakiness, sweating, confusion, palpitations. GROWTH/STRUCTURAL: signs of soft-tissue/acromegaly-like change (jaw/hand/foot enlargement, coarsening features, joint pain), and symptoms suggesting visceral organ enlargement (the rodent kidney/heart/liver/gut overgrowth signal) - clinically followed with periodic echocardiography/cardiac symptoms (exertional dyspnoea, oedema) and renal function (creatinine/eGFR) if exposure is ongoing. FLUID: oedema/weight gain and blood pressure (class fluid-retention effect of IGF-I peptides). ENDOCRINE: IGF-1 axis labs - serum IGF-1, IGFBP-3, and GH may be deranged (feedback suppression of GH); thyroid and a full metabolic panel are reasonable. NEOPLASTIC VIGILANCE: given pro-proliferative signalling, attention to any new or growing lumps, GI symptoms, and age-appropriate cancer screening; not a reason for casual use. INJECTION SITE: redness/swelling/abscess (sterility of grey-market peptides is unverified). Any of these warrants stopping and seeking medical evaluation. There is no established safe monitoring-defined dose - the safest course is not to use an unapproved IGF-1R agonist with no human safety data."

CRITICAL EVIDENCE-GAP flags for the synthesizer: (1) The des(1-3)-specific literature is old (mostly 1989-2001) and largely about renal failure, mammary gland, and assay biochemistry — NOT muscle. (2) Steps 4-5 lean on GENERIC IGF-1 muscle reviews (PMID 32858949) extrapolated to DES; DES-specific muscle-hypertrophy papers were not found. Label this extrapolation explicitly. (3) ZERO human muscle/hypertrophy data for DES — never present animal potency as a human effect. (4) 'Local hypertrophy' is a forum/marketing claim; PMID 2005410 (rat clearance/distribution) argues against site-confinement. (5) Hepatotoxicity/lipid/17-alpha-alkylation cautions in the seed brief apply to ORAL AAS, NOT to IGF-1 DES — do not conflate; DES is an injectable peptide with a different risk profile (hypoglycemia, growth-signal concerns).
