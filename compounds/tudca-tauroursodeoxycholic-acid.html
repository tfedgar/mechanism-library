TUDCA

A hydrophilic bile acid that props up bile flow and calms ER stress — the mechanistically-right, evidence-thin choice for cholestatic oral-steroid liver strain.

Human / approved 9 citations
Class
Hydrophilic bile acid / chemical chaperone (hepatoprotectant / anti-cholestatic)
Primary target
Chemical chaperone reducing ER stress/UPR; anti-cholestatic via Ca2+/PKC-driven bile-transporter (BSEP/MRP2) activation
Human status
Real drug with genuine human data, but the bodybuilding use is off-label extrapolation. TUDCA is an approved prescription hepatobiliary drug in China and parts of Europe with decades of use for cholestatic liver disease; UDCA (its parent) is FDA-approved for primary biliary cholangitis. Human RCT evidence exists for improved insulin sensitivity (n=20, PMID 20522594) and for the AMX0035 fixed combination in ALS (CENTAUR, Phase 2, n=137, PMID 32877582 / NCT03127514). CRITICAL HONESTY: there is NO placebo-controlled human trial showing TUDCA prevents or reverses 17aa oral-steroid liver injury; that use rests on mechanism plus rodent cholestasis models. The curated corpus card is blunt — liver-support supplements (TUDCA, NAC) have LIMITED evidence and 'avoiding/limiting orals is the real mitigation.' Regulatory reality check: AMX0035 (Relyvrio/Albrioza), pairing TUDCA with sodium phenylbutyrate, won FDA/Health Canada approval largely on CENTAUR, then the confirmatory Phase 3 PHOENIX trial (NCT05021536, 664 patients) failed its primary endpoint and Amylyx withdrew it from the US and Canadian markets in 2024 — a cautionary example of a positive small trial not holding up.

At a glance

  • Restores bile flow in cholestasis by stimulating canalicular bile transporters (BSEP/MRP2) via Ca2+/PKC signaling (rodent/cell data)
  • Shifts the bile-acid pool toward a less toxic, hydrophilic composition, reducing detergent injury to hepatocytes
  • Acts as a chemical chaperone: reduces ER stress and dampens the UPR, cutting ER-stress-driven apoptosis (mostly preclinical)
  • Mechanistically matched to the CHOLESTATIC (not purely oxidative) injury pattern of 17aa oral steroids — the rationale for on-cycle use
  • Improved hepatic and muscle insulin sensitivity ~30% in a small human RCT (best human evidence, metabolic not hepatic)
  • Modestly slowed functional decline in ALS as half of the AMX0035 combination in Phase 2 CENTAUR — a result NOT confirmed in the larger Phase 3 PHOENIX trial
  • NOT demonstrated in any human trial to prevent or reverse anabolic-steroid liver injury — the popular use is extrapolated, not proven

Mechanism, step by step

  1. A gentle, water-loving bile acid enters the bile-acid pool Established

    TUDCA is the taurine-conjugated form of UDCA — a hydrophilic bile acid. Unlike the harsh, detergent-like bile acids the liver makes on its own, TUDCA does not solubilise cell membranes. Taken orally it is well absorbed, joins the enterohepatic bile-acid pool, and shifts the pool toward a less toxic, more hydrophilic composition. In humans it is partly deconjugated to UDCA and reconjugated, with less conversion to hydrophobic metabolites than UDCA itself. PMID 31757001; PMID 9918905

  2. It acts as a chemical chaperone, easing ER stress Evidence-rodent

    Misfolded proteins accumulating in the endoplasmic reticulum trigger the unfolded protein response (UPR). TUDCA behaves as a chemical chaperone — it helps proteins fold and blunts the UPR (lowering BiP/GRP78, PERK phosphorylation, XBP1 splicing). Most of this evidence is in vitro and in animal models. PMID 31757001; PMID 21778463

  3. Reduced ER stress cuts the pro-death (apoptotic) signal Evidence-rodent

    Sustained ER stress drives apoptosis through CHOP and caspase-12/caspase-3. By calming the UPR, TUDCA suppresses these pathways. Demonstrated in rodent acute pancreatitis (less caspase-12/3 activation, less acinar damage) and in primary rat hepatocytes exposed to toxic bile salts. PMID 21778463; PMID 9537871

  4. It stimulates bile secretion — the anti-cholestatic action Evidence-rodent

    Separately from chaperoning, TUDCA is directly anti-cholestatic. In hepatocytes it mobilises intracellular calcium and activates PKC, promoting insertion of canalicular export pumps (BSEP/ABCB11, MRP2) into the membrane, restoring bile flow. PKC inhibitors abolish this protection; the PKA inhibitor does not. PMID 12077103

  5. This is exactly the injury pattern of 17-alpha-alkylated oral steroids Established

    Oral AAS are 17-alpha-alkylated to survive first-pass metabolism, which makes them hepatotoxic in a specifically CHOLESTATIC way: impaired bile transport via BSEP/ABCB11 and MRP2, raising enzymes and bilirubin and, at the severe end, jaundice, peliosis hepatis, adenoma and rarely fatal necrosis. TUDCA's bile-transport rescue maps onto this pattern — the rationale for on-cycle use. corpus:hepatotoxicity_17aa_orals; PMID 37948000

  6. In an estrogen-cholestasis model, TUDCA protected where UDCA did not Evidence-rodent

    In ethinylestradiol-treated rats, TUDCA prevented rises in serum bile acids, bilirubin, ALP and GGT and preserved liver ultrastructure while UDCA did not — a rodent signal that the taurine-conjugated form has anti-cholestatic advantages. It remains a rodent result, not a human oral-steroid trial. PMID 7548801

  7. In humans, the best-controlled data are for insulin sensitivity, not liver protection Evidence-human

    The strongest human RCT for TUDCA is metabolic, not hepatoprotective: in 20 obese adults, 4 weeks of 1,750 mg/day improved hepatic and muscle insulin sensitivity ~30% versus placebo, with increased muscle insulin signaling — but WITHOUT measurable change in tissue ER-stress markers. No comparable trial shows TUDCA prevents oral-steroid liver injury. PMID 20522594

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. TUDCA has no single high-affinity receptor driving its main effects. Its dominant actions are physicochemical/signaling: (1) as a hydrophilic bile acid it is a weak, non-membrane-disrupting detergent that dilutes toxic hydrophobic bile acids; (2) it acts as a chemical chaperone, physically stabilising protein folding in the ER and blunting the UPR (reduced BiP/GRP78, PERK phosphorylation, XBP1 splicing, CHOP); (3) as an anti-cholestatic agent it triggers hepatocyte Ca2+ mobilisation and PKC activation, which promotes membrane insertion/activity of the canalicular export pumps BSEP (ABCB11) and MRP2. It is additionally reported to signal through the membrane bile-acid receptor TGR5/GPBAR1 and to modulate the nuclear receptor FXR, which may contribute to its metabolic and anti-inflammatory effects (mechanistic/preclinical).

Signalling cascade. Two parallel cascades. Anti-cholestatic: TUDCA -> intracellular Ca2+ rise -> PKC activation -> transporter (BSEP/MRP2) insertion -> restored bile secretion -> clearance of retained toxic bile acids -> less hepatocyte injury (PKC inhibitors abolish this; the PKA inhibitor does not). Cytoprotective/chaperone: TUDCA -> reduced ER stress/UPR -> lower CHOP and caspase-12/caspase-3 activation -> reduced apoptosis. The two converge on 'fewer hepatocytes injured/killed under cholestatic or ER-stress load.'

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral (capsules/powder as supplement; oral or IV as drug in some countries).
Absorption / bioavailability
Absorbed across the small intestine; in PBC patients TUDCA was better absorbed than oral UDCA and produced higher biliary UDCA enrichment (32.6% vs 29.2%), with less conversion to hydrophobic metabolites. Exact human oral bioavailability is not cleanly defined because it is deconjugated/reconjugated in transit.
Half-life
Not a single clean value — governed by enterohepatic cycling rather than simple plasma clearance; effective residence is prolonged by recirculation.
Metabolism
Undergoes enterohepatic recirculation. Partially deconjugated by gut bacteria to UDCA, then reconjugated (partly with glycine); undergoes reduced biotransformation to more hydrophobic species (e.g. lithocholate) than UDCA does — a favourable safety feature. Effectively TUDCA and UDCA interconvert and share a common pool.
Excretion
Fecal excretion is the major elimination route for both TUDCA and UDCA; a minor fraction appears in urine. Ursodeoxycholate accounted for ~8% of total fecal bile acids during TUDCA dosing vs ~23% during UDCA dosing.
Notes
Because it feeds a shared UDCA/TUDCA pool, effects overlap substantially with UDCA; the practical PK advantage claimed for TUDCA is better absorption and less hydrophobic biotransformation.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

Human dosing anchors come from trials, not from steroid-support practice, and this entry gives NO cycle protocol. Reference points: the insulin-sensitivity RCT used 1,750 mg/day for 4 weeks (PMID 20522594); the PBC PK study used 750 mg/day for 2 months (PMID 9918905); the ALS AMX0035 combination delivered 1 g/day of taurursodiol (TUDCA) alongside sodium phenylbutyrate (PMID 32877582). Anti-cholestatic and chaperone effects appear dose-dependent in preclinical models. The key caveat: no human dose has been shown to prevent oral-steroid liver injury, so any 'liver-support dose' is unvalidated for that purpose. Higher intake mainly increases the risk of the dose-limiting side effect, diarrhea.

How it compares

  • vs UDCA (ursodeoxycholic acid, the parent drug)
    same: Both feed a shared hydrophilic bile-acid pool and are anti-cholestatic; both are used for PBC
    different: TUDCA is the taurine conjugate — better absorbed, with less conversion to hydrophobic/toxic metabolites (PMID 9918905), and in the estrogen-cholestasis rat model TUDCA protected where UDCA failed (PMID 7548801). Practically similar benefits; TUDCA may have a modest PK edge.
  • vs NAC (N-acetylcysteine)
    same: Both are popular 'on-cycle liver support' supplements with LIMITED human evidence for that use
    different: NAC targets OXIDATIVE stress/glutathione; TUDCA targets CHOLESTATIC injury and ER stress. Since 17aa oral injury is prominently cholestatic, TUDCA's mechanism is a well-matched rationale — but neither is proven in steroid users.
  • vs Sodium phenylbutyrate (its AMX0035 partner)
    same: Both reduce cellular stress; combined in the approved-then-withdrawn ALS drug
    different: Phenylbutyrate is an ammonia scavenger / HDAC inhibitor that also reduces ER stress; TUDCA is a bile acid / chaperone. Their combination's Phase 3 failure (PHOENIX) is a caution about over-reading TUDCA's small positive trials.
  • vs Milk thistle / silymarin
    same: Both marketed as hepatoprotectants with modest, contested clinical evidence
    different: Silymarin is antioxidant/anti-fibrotic with weak trial data; TUDCA has a defined anti-cholestatic bile-transport mechanism and a stronger mechanistic fit for oral-steroid cholestasis, plus better human RCT data (in insulin sensitivity/ALS, not liver-support).

History & legal status

TUDCA occurs naturally in bile (famously enriched in bear bile, used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries). Its parent UDCA is FDA-approved for primary biliary cholangitis and gallstone dissolution. TUDCA itself is a prescription hepatobiliary drug in China and available in parts of Europe; in the US it is sold only as a dietary supplement, not an approved drug. The highest-profile regulatory arc is AMX0035 (Relyvrio in the US, Albrioza in Canada), a fixed combination of sodium phenylbutyrate + taurursodiol (TUDCA): approved by the FDA (Sept 2022) and Health Canada largely on the small CENTAUR Phase 2 (NCT03127514), then the confirmatory Phase 3 PHOENIX trial (NCT05021536, 664 patients) failed to meet its primary endpoint, and Amylyx voluntarily withdrew the product from the US and Canadian markets in 2024. This sequence is the single most important context for judging TUDCA's evidence base: a mechanistically attractive agent with a positive small trial that did not survive a large confirmatory one.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Whether TUDCA actually prevents or reduces 17aa oral-steroid liver injury in humans — never tested in a controlled trial in steroid users.
  • The right dose, timing and duration for any 'liver-support' use — all current dosing is borrowed from unrelated indications.
  • How much of TUDCA's benefit is its own vs simply acting through the shared UDCA pool.
  • Whether the ~30% insulin-sensitivity improvement (PMID 20522594) is durable, clinically meaningful, or reproducible in larger trials — and why it occurred without measurable change in ER-stress markers.
  • Long-term safety of chronic high-dose supplement TUDCA in otherwise healthy athletes.
  • Whether TUDCA masks (rather than prevents) ongoing injury by normalising liver panels while damage continues.
  • Real-world purity/dose accuracy of US supplement-grade product.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

This is monitoring guidance, not a protocol and not medical advice. For anyone using hepatotoxic 17aa orals, the corpus is explicit that the real mitigation is limiting/avoiding orals — TUDCA is not a substitute. Sensible things to MONITOR (with a clinician): a full liver panel including ALT, AST, GGT, alkaline phosphatase, and crucially total/direct BILIRUBIN (cholestatic injury shows as rising bilirubin/GGT/ALP, sometimes before ALT), plus watching for clinical red flags of cholestasis — jaundice, dark urine, pale stool, itching (pruritus), and right-upper-quadrant discomfort; any of these warrants stopping the offending oral and seeking care. Because TUDCA can lower enzymes and improve bile flow, do not interpret a normalised panel on TUDCA as proof of safety — trend the numbers over time and against symptoms. Separately monitor the harms TUDCA does NOT address: a lipid panel (HDL, LDL/ApoB), blood pressure, and hematocrit/hemoglobin. Watch for TUDCA's own dose-limiting side effect (diarrhea) as a signal to lower intake.

Caveats

TUDCA is mechanistically the RIGHT tool for the RIGHT problem (cholestatic, bile-transport injury from orals) but the human evidence for that specific use is essentially absent — it is inferred from rodent cholestasis models and cell studies, not from any trial in steroid users. Do not treat it as a license to run hepatotoxic orals: the corpus is explicit that limiting/avoiding 17aa orals is the actual mitigation, not a supplement. Two mechanisms are often conflated in marketing — the anti-cholestatic bile-transport action and the ER-stress chaperone action are distinct, and neither is proven to protect against AAS in humans. TUDCA does little for the OTHER big oral-steroid harms (crashed HDL, raised LDL/ApoB, blood pressure, hematocrit), so a 'clean' liver panel on TUDCA does not mean a safe cycle. It can also mask rising enzymes/bilirubin, giving false reassurance while injury continues. Supplement-grade product purity and dose accuracy are unregulated in the US. The AMX0035 story (approval largely on a small trial, reversal after a large one) is a direct warning against over-reading TUDCA's thin evidence base. This is mechanism education, not medical advice or a protocol.
