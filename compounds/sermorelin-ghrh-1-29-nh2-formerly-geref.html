Sermorelin

A trimmed-down copy of your body's own "make growth hormone" signal — it tells the pituitary to release its own GH in natural pulses, instead of injecting GH directly.

Human / approved 23 citations
Class
Growth hormone secretagogue — specifically a GHRH (growth hormone-releasing hormone) analog / GHRH receptor agonist
Primary target
Pituitary-type GHRH receptor (pGHRH-R) on anterior-pituitary somatotroph cells — a Class B1 G protein-coupled receptor
Human status
Human data exist and are decent for its NARROW historical uses — not for the uses it's hyped for. Sermorelin (brand Geref) was an FDA-approved agent: (1) as a diagnostic provocative test for GH deficiency (single IV dose), and (2) for treating idiopathic GH deficiency in children, where it raised height velocity over 12-36 months — though head-to-head it underperformed injected somatropin [PMID 18031173]. Its manufacturing was DISCONTINUED in the US (~2008), so today's product is compounded, not a branded FDA drug. For the adult 'anti-aging,' body-composition, sleep, and recovery claims that drive its popularity in the fitness/peptide world, human evidence is thin: small or retrospective studies show it reliably raises GH and IGF-1 [PMID 9360512 RCT; PMID 28830317 retrospective], but there are NO large, long-term RCTs showing it improves muscle, fat loss, longevity, or hard health outcomes in healthy adults. Both human studies cited were checked live and are not retracted.

At a glance

  • Raises endogenous, pulsatile growth hormone secretion (~70-107% increase in integrated 12-h GH in an elderly RCT) — Evidence-human [PMID 9360512]
  • Raises serum IGF-1, the downstream marker of GH action (e.g., ~160 to ~239 ng/mL in hypogonadal men) — Evidence-human [PMID 28830317; PMID 9360512]
  • Increased height velocity / catch-up growth in some children with idiopathic GH deficiency over 12-36 months — Evidence-human, but weaker than injected GH (somatropin) head-to-head — [PMID 18031173]
  • Used as a diagnostic provocative test: a single IV 1 mcg/kg dose probes whether the pituitary can release GH — Established clinical use [PMID 18031173]
  • Immune-modulating signals (more B cells, T-cell activation) reported in one aging RCT — Evidence-human, single small study, needs replication [PMID 9360512]
  • Preserves natural feedback regulation vs. exogenous HGH (mechanism-level claim) — Evidence-human reasoning [PMID 18046908]
  • Common side effects: transient facial flushing and injection-site pain; generally well tolerated short-term — Evidence-human [PMID 18031173]

Mechanism, step by step

  1. It's the working tip of natural GHRH Established

    Your hypothalamus makes GHRH, a 44-amino-acid hormone. Sermorelin is just the first 29 amino acids (with an amide cap) — and that's the shortest fragment that keeps the FULL biological punch of natural GHRH. So it isn't a foreign drug; it's a copy of the active end of a hormone you already make. PMID 18031173 (Prakash & Goa, BioDrugs 1999); PMID 9513600 (Cervini, J Med Chem 1998)

  2. It docks onto the GHRH receptor on pituitary cells Established

    Sermorelin binds the pituitary GHRH receptor (a GPCR) on somatotrophs — the GH-making cells of the anterior pituitary. It binds in two steps: its tail grabs the receptor's outer domain to hold on, and its head slots into the membrane-spanning core to flip the switch 'on.' Corpus MoA: Sermorelin [PMID 39207455, 25752763, 30373845, 39266057]

  3. It switches on the cAMP/PKA pathway inside the cell Established

    A flipped-on GHRH receptor activates the enzyme adenylyl cyclase, which pumps out the second messenger cAMP. cAMP turns on protein kinase A (PKA), which acts as a molecular 'tagger' that triggers the cell to dump its stored growth-hormone granules. (This is a different switchboard from ghrelin-type secretagogues, which work through calcium/IP3.) Corpus tutor over PMC OA reviews on GH-secretagogue signaling; PMID 9667794 (Annals of Medicine 1998)

  4. The pituitary releases its OWN growth hormone — in pulses Evidence-human

    The result is a burst of GH secreted from the pituitary into the blood. Crucially this is pulsatile, like your natural nighttime GH rhythm — not the flat, sustained level you get from injecting GH. In a human RCT, a sermorelin-type GHRH analog raised 12-hour integrated GH secretion by ~70-107% in older adults. PMID 9360512 (Khorram, J Clin Endocrinol Metab 1997, RCT); PMID 18031173

  5. GH drives the liver to make IGF-1 Evidence-human

    That GH travels to the liver (and other tissues), which responds by making IGF-1 (insulin-like growth factor 1) — over half of your circulating IGF-1 comes from the liver. IGF-1 is the longer-acting messenger that carries out much of GH's tissue-building and metabolic work. In hypogonadal men, GHRP/sermorelin therapy raised serum IGF-1 from ~160 to ~239 ng/mL. PMID 28830317 (Sigalos, Am J Mens Health 2017, retrospective); PMID 9360512 (IGF-1 +28% in RCT)

  6. Your own feedback brakes stay intact (the key safety idea) Evidence-human

    Because sermorelin acts UPSTREAM at the pituitary, the body's natural brake — somatostatin and IGF-1 negative feedback — still works. If GH/IGF-1 climb too high, the brake limits further release. Injecting GH directly bypasses that brake entirely. This is the central pitch for why a GHRH analog is theoretically 'more physiologic' than HGH — but note it's a mechanism argument, not proof of better real-world safety. PMID 18046908 (Walker, Clin Interv Aging 2006); PMID 18031173

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Sermorelin is the synthetic 29-amino-acid N-terminal fragment of human GHRH (GRF 1-29-NH2, supplied as the acetate salt; ChEMBL CHEMBL1201490, structure_type SEQ, USAN '-morelin' stem). It is a full AGONIST at the pituitary-type growth-hormone-releasing-hormone receptor (pGHRH-R / GHRHR; ChEMBL target CHEMBL2032), a Class B1 (secretin-family) Gs-coupled GPCR expressed on anterior-pituitary somatotrophs [Established; ChEMBL get_mechanism mec_id 756 AGONIST direct_interaction=true; PMID 39207455, 25752763]. The 1-29 fragment retains essentially the full intrinsic GH-releasing potency of the parent 1-40/1-44 GHRH: in humans GHRH(1-29)-NH2 has similar molar potency to GHRH(1-40) and GHRH(1-44) [Evidence-human; PMID 2866496]. Selectivity: at GH-releasing doses it does NOT change PRL, TSH, LH, FSH, ACTH, insulin, glucagon, glucose, cortisol or thyroid hormones, i.e. it is functionally selective for the somatotroph GHRH-R rather than other pituitary axes [Evidence-human; PMID 2866496]. Published quantitative Kd/EC50 affinity numbers for sermorelin at human GHRH-R were NOT found in the tools queried this run (human-data gap on exact Ki/EC50). Note a species difference: the D-Ala2 'superagonist' analog is ~2x more potent than native 1-29 in humans (PMID 2866496) but far more so in rat, because rat vs human somatotroph GHRH-R interact differently with these peptides [Evidence-human/rodent; PMID 2546126, 2866496]. Sermorelin also binds extrahypothalamic GHRH-R splice variants, principally SV1 [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 19897610, 32156725].

Signalling cascade. Two-step binding model: the C-terminal region of the peptide docks the receptor extracellular domain and the N-terminus (residues 1-29 retain the critical N-terminal pharmacophore) engages the seven-transmembrane bundle to switch the receptor on [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 39207455]. Canonical somatotroph cascade: receptor activates Gs-alpha -> adenylate cyclase -> rise in cAMP -> activation of protein kinase A (PKA). PKA promotes opening of voltage-gated Ca2+ channels and a rise in intracellular [Ca2+]i, which triggers exocytosis of stored growth hormone (acute secretion). cAMP/PKA also phosphorylates CREB, driving transcription via the pituitary-specific transcription factor Pit-1 (POU1F1), which both up-regulates the GH gene and the GHRH receptor itself, sustaining somatotroph GH synthesis and somatotroph proliferation/trophic maintenance [Established for the GHRH/cAMP axis; Evidence-in vitro for proliferation: forskolin reproduces and somatostatin opposes the GRF mitogenic effect, implicating cAMP — PMID 3018748; cascade synthesis PMID 39470866, 25489106]. Released GH acts on hepatic and peripheral GH receptors to raise IGF-1, the main downstream effector of growth and anabolism [Established; PMID 39470866]. Separately, at the extrahypothalamic SV1 splice-variant receptor, GHRH/analog binding activates the MAPK (ERK1/2) and PI3K-Akt pathways, raising PCNA and proliferation — the basis of the GHRH-axis 'growth-factor' activity studied in cancer (relevant to the receptor family, not an intended pituitary action) [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 19897610, 32156725].

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Parenteral peptide — subcutaneous injection is the clinical/most-studied route; intravenous used in diagnostic/PK studies; intranasal studied but poorly absorbed. Not orally bioavailable (peptide is digested). Supplied as sermorelin acetate, lyophilized for reconstitution (ChEMBL: parenteral=true, oral=false, first_approval 1990) [Established; ChEMBL compound_search; PMID 8329825, 2546126].
Absorption / bioavailability
After SC injection, GH-releasing bioactivity is reproducible and roughly equivalent to native 1-29 (SC 3 ug/kg gives comparable peak GH and AUC to the D-Tyr1,D-Ala2 analog) [Evidence-human; PMID 2546126]. Intranasal absorption is LOW: bioavailability only ~3-5%, so ~50 ug/kg intranasally is needed to approximate 1 ug/kg IV — illustrating poor mucosal uptake of the bare peptide [Evidence-human; PMID 8329825].
Half-life
Very short. In humans, sermorelin/GHRH(1-29)-NH2 is rapidly eliminated from plasma after IV injection; commonly cited plasma half-life is only a few minutes (single-digit minutes), although the downstream GH elevation it triggers persists ~3 hours [Evidence-human; PMID 8329825]. In rat matrices the parent peptide disappears with apparent half-lives of 18+/-4 min in serum and 13+/-3 min in liver homogenate [Evidence-rodent; PMID 1437711]. Exact validated human terminal t1/2 in minutes was not given numerically in the tools this run beyond 'rapidly eliminated' (human-data gap on a precise number).
Metabolism
Proteolytic, not CYP-mediated. The rate-limiting cleavage is by dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-4/DPP-IV) at the Ala2-Asp3 bond, generating the inactive sermorelin(3-29)-NH2 metabolite — the same vulnerability all native GHRH(1-29) analogs share and the reason longer-acting analogs add a position-2 substitution (e.g. tesamorelin, CJC-1295) [Established/Evidence-in vitro; PMID 1437711 (DPP-IV site identified), PMID 34665524 (sermorelin(3-29)-NH2 confirmed as in-vitro metabolite)]. Additional trypsin-like (Arg11-Arg12, Arg20-Lys21) and chymotrypsin-like (Tyr10-Arg11, Tyr18-Ala19) cleavages occur in serum and liver, yielding fragments such as (1-20), (3-20) and (1-18) [Evidence-rodent; PMID 1437711]. Nineteen in-vitro metabolites were catalogued for WADA detection work [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 34665524].
Excretion
Cleared as small peptide fragments; intact parent and metabolites appear at very low concentration in urine with rapid renal clearance, which is why anti-doping detection is analytically difficult [Evidence-human/in vitro; PMID 41138283, 26879649, 34665524]. Quantitative renal/biliary excretion fractions were not reported in the tools this run.
Notes
No ester/prodrug form — sermorelin is the active peptide itself (ChEMBL prodrug=0). Pulsatile, physiologic mode of action: because it works one step upstream (drives the pituitary rather than replacing GH), GH output stays subject to negative feedback from somatostatin and IGF-1, limiting supraphysiologic GH and preserving the nocturnal pulse — repeated intranasal dosing did not suppress overnight GH secretion [Evidence-human; PMID 8329825]. BBB: as a hydrophilic peptide, CNS penetration of exogenous sermorelin is expected to be poor; no validated human BBB-penetration data were found this run (knowledge gap).

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

HUMAN (acute, IV): a clear monotonic dose-response. The lowest dose tested, 0.25 ug/kg IV, already produces significant GH release; maximal GH release (mean GH peaks ~90 mU/L) is reached at about 1-2 ug/kg IV, with little further gain above that — i.e. the somatotroph response saturates around 1-2 ug/kg [Evidence-human; PMID 8329825]. An earlier IV dose-ranging study (0.015-0.5 ug/kg) showed a graded rise in integrated GH at every step, confirming dose-dependence in the low microgram/kg range, peak GH at 15-30 min [Evidence-human; PMID 2866496]. HUMAN (SC): a submaximal 3 ug/kg SC bolus gives a robust GH peak and AUC [Evidence-human; PMID 2546126]. HUMAN (intranasal): dose-dependent but ~50x weaker — ~50 ug/kg intranasal ≈ 1 ug/kg IV due to 3-5% bioavailability [Evidence-human; PMID 8329825]. HUMAN (chronic): nightly SC GRF(1-29) 5 ug/kg for 6 months accelerated growth velocity in short children with normal GH testing, without down-regulating the acute GH response or 24-h GH profile [Evidence-human; PMID 2907992]; nightly SC 10 ug/kg of the [Nle27] analog for 16 weeks in elderly raised 12-h integrated GH by 70-107% and IGF-1 by ~28% [Evidence-human; PMID 9360512]. SPECIES NOTE: 'superagonist' position-2 analogs are markedly more potent than native 1-29 in RAT but only ~2x in humans — rodent potency does NOT translate to humans [Evidence-human vs rodent; PMID 2546126, 2866496]. EXPLICIT GAP: there is no modern, formal human dose-response study of chronic sermorelin for body-composition/anti-aging endpoints with hard outcomes; sustained-effect dose-response for muscle/fat in adults is essentially unknown.

How it compares

  • vs Tesamorelin (Egrifta) — stabilized GHRH(1-44) analog
    same: Same target (pituitary GHRH-R agonist), same upstream/pulsatile mechanism raising GH then IGF-1; same DPP-IV vulnerability problem that drove its design
    different: Tesamorelin is a trans-3-hexenoyl-modified GHRH(1-44) with an N-terminal protecting group conferring resistance to DPP-IV and a longer effective action; it is FDA-approved (2010) for HIV-associated lipodystrophy with RCT evidence of visceral-fat reduction. Sermorelin is the bare 1-29 fragment, shorter-acting, and was approved for pediatric GH-deficiency diagnosis/therapy, not body composition [Evidence-human; PMID 18057338, 18690162, 41545261]
  • vs CJC-1295 (Modified GRF 1-29 / with-DAC)
    same: Also a GHRH(1-29)-based agonist at the same GHRH-R with the same Gs-cAMP cascade
    different: CJC-1295 adds position-2/15/27 substitutions to resist DPP-IV (mod-GRF, t1/2 ~25-30 min) and, in the DAC form, an albumin-binding maleimide that extends half-life to ~6-8 days, converting pulsatile GH release into a sustained 'bleed' — losing the physiologic pulsatility that sermorelin preserves. CJC-1295 is NOT an approved drug (research/gray-market) whereas sermorelin was FDA-approved [Established/Evidence-mixed; corpus MoA-gen CJC-1295; web medsbase]
  • vs Ibutamoren / MK-677 and ghrelin-mimetic GHRPs (e.g. ipamorelin)
    same: End result overlaps — increased pulsatile GH/IGF-1; often discussed together with sermorelin in the PED context
    different: Different RECEPTOR: these act at the growth-hormone-secretagogue receptor GHSR-1a (ghrelin receptor) via Gq/PLC/PKC/Ca2+, not the GHRH-R/Gs/cAMP pathway. They synergize with GHRH agonists (the two receptors potentiate each other at the somatotroph), and MK-677 is orally active with a long half-life — neither true of sermorelin [Established; corpus MoA-gen MK-677; PMID 9543138]

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • GHRP-2 potentiates ↑ Established (GHRH+GHRP synergy)
    Sermorelin is a GHRH(1-29) analog acting on the pituitary GHRH receptor (Gs-coupled, raises cAMP). GHRP-2 acts on the distinct ghrelin receptor GHS-R1a (Gq-coupled). Because GHRH presses the accelerator while GHRP-2 works a third lever and partially overrides the somatostatin brake, the two produce a much larger combined GH pulse than either alone - the classic GHRH+GHRP complementary-pathway potentiation, not merely additive. GHRP-2 monograph step 4 (PMID 9285939; PMID 9186261); Sermorelin monograph (GHRH-R agonist, PMID 9360512) Watch: IGF-1 (on-target amplification); fasting glucose and insulin sensitivity (GH is counter-regulatory); signs of GH excess (edema, arthralgia, paresthesia).
  • GHRP-6 potentiates ↑ Evidence-human
    Sermorelin is GHRH(1-29), a pure GHRH-receptor agonist. GHRP-6's GH output is gated by available GHRH and is weak/absent when the hypothalamo-pituitary link is bypassed; adding exogenous GHRH restores and amplifies the pulse. The GHRH-R (Gs/cAMP) and GHS-R1a (Gq/Ca2+) pathways converge at the somatotroph, giving supra-additive GH release — the canonical GHRH+GHRP synergy. Monograph step 3 + cascade (PMID 7883854, 9543138, 9893708); GHRH+GHRP synergy PMID 7745018 Watch: IGF-1 trend for the amplified GH signal; fasting glucose/HbA1c for GH-driven insulin resistance; symptoms of fluid retention or joint discomfort from GH/IGF-1 excess.
  • Hexarelin potentiates ↑ Evidence-human
    Hexarelin acts at GHS-R1a while sermorelin is a GHRH-receptor agonist — two independent, complementary triggers on the same pituitary somatotroph. GHRP-plus-GHRH is the classic synergistic pairing: together they produce a markedly larger GH pulse than the sum of either alone, because the GHRP arm also blunts somatostatin tone. This synergy is human-demonstrated with hexarelin plus GHRH(1-29). Hexarelin monograph mechanism step 2 (GHRH synergy, PMID 11238504); corpus PMID 8799695 (combined GHRH + hexarelin on GH secretion in humans) Watch: IGF-1 and symptoms of GH excess from the amplified pulse — fluid retention/edema, joint pain/arthralgia, carpal-tunnel-type paresthesia; fasting glucose (larger GH pulses mean more insulin resistance).
  • CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin shares target Established
    Sermorelin is GHRH(1-29) acting on the SAME GHRH receptor as CJC-1295 (both are GHRH(1-29) analogs on the Gs/cAMP/PKA somatotroph pathway). Adding it to the CJC arm is redundant: same receptor, same second messenger, except Sermorelin is rapidly degraded by DPP-4 and very short-acting whereas CJC-1295 is DPP-4-resistant (and, with DAC, multi-day). They occupy one receptor rather than recruiting a second, so the combination adds no mechanism beyond what CJC-1295 already provides on that arm. Sermorelin monograph primary_target (pituitary GHRH-R agonist, GHRH(1-29)-NH2); CJC-1295 monograph step 1 (DPP-4-resistant GHRH(1-29) analog on GHRHR) Watch: IGF-1 (over-stimulation of the GHRH arm) and the usual GH-excess signs (edema, arthralgia); no new monitoring beyond the GHRH class.

History & legal status

"Origin: GHRH (growth-hormone-releasing factor, somatocrinin) was isolated in 1982 (Brazeau/Ling/Guillemin; PMID 6130528) and the minimal fully active fragment GHRH(1-29)-NH2 was synthesized as sermorelin. USAN assigned 1989 (-relin / -morelin stem = 'GH-release-stimulating peptides'). Approvals: FDA-approved 1990 (per ChEMBL first_approval 1990; max_phase 4) and marketed as Geref (Serono) for (a) diagnostic assessment of pituitary GH secretory capacity and (b) treatment of idiopathic GH deficiency / growth failure in children with an intact pituitary; ATC codes H01AC04 (pituitary/hypothalamic hormones) and V04CD03 (diagnostic). Withdrawal: Geref was DISCONTINUED from the US market in the mid-2000s (around 2008) — a commercial/manufacturing decision, not a safety withdrawal (ChEMBL withdrawn_flag=false, black_box_warning=false). Current status: no longer a branded FDA product; available in the US only via compounding pharmacies (prescription, off-label, e.g. 'anti-aging'/GH-support), an unapproved-use gray area. Anti-doping: GHRH and all its synthetic analogs (sermorelin, tesamorelin, CJC-1295) are PROHIBITED at all times by WADA (class S2, peptide hormones/growth factors); detection is analytically hard due to low urinary levels and rapid clearance [Established; PMID 34665524, 41138283, 26879649]."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No precise, modern validated human terminal plasma half-life (in minutes) or full clearance/excretion fractions for sermorelin were located this run — the human PK is characterized qualitatively ('rapidly eliminated', GH elevated ~3 h) rather than with a definitive t1/2 number.
  • Exact human GHRH-R binding affinity (Kd/Ki) and EC50 for sermorelin are not established in the retrieved literature; potency is described only relative to native GHRH.
  • No long-term controlled human trials of sermorelin for adult body composition, muscle, strength, sleep or 'anti-aging' hard outcomes — the popular use case is essentially unstudied at modern evidentiary standards; existing chronic data are small and surrogate-endpoint (GH/IGF-1) only.
  • Whether chronic agonism of the GHRH-R/SV1 growth-factor axis (and the accompanying IGF-1 rise) carries any real proliferative/oncologic risk in humans is unresolved — the only signal is in-vitro/rodent and comes from the antagonist (anticancer) literature.
  • Real-world safety/quality of compounded sermorelin (purity, dose accuracy, immunogenicity, contaminants) is undocumented — efficacy/safety data are from pharmaceutical Geref, not gray-market product.
  • Magnitude and durability of GH/IGF-1 response across age, sex, adiposity and dosing schedules (and how much pulsatility-preservation matters clinically) are not quantified in adults.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (NOT a dosing guide; flags what to watch, not how to use): (1) IGF-1 — the key safety surrogate for downstream GH effect; watch for IGF-1 drifting above the age/sex reference range, which signals excess GH-axis stimulation. (2) Fasting glucose, HbA1c and fasting insulin — GH/IGF-1 elevation can reduce insulin sensitivity; monitor for hyperglycemia/new insulin resistance. (3) Symptoms of GH excess — new fluid retention/edema, joint aches (arthralgia), carpal-tunnel-type hand numbness/tingling, headache; these should prompt re-evaluation. (4) Injection sites — inspect for redness, swelling, persistent nodules or signs of infection; rotate sites. (5) Vasomotor symptoms — flushing/warmth or headache around dosing (usually transient). (6) Thyroid panel (TSH, free T4) — GH-axis stimulation can unmask reduced T4-to-T3 or alter thyroid status in some people. (7) Baseline clinical caution for active or high-risk malignancy given the theoretical GHRH-R/IGF-1 proliferative axis — a reason to involve a clinician before use. (8) Because product is typically compounded/gray-market, source quality and any allergic/hypersensitivity reactions are an independent thing to watch. Any abnormal IGF-1, glucose dysregulation, or GH-excess symptoms warrants stopping and clinician review. This is monitoring guidance only, not an endorsement or protocol."

Caveats

HYPE vs. EVIDENCE: Sermorelin reliably moves the biomarkers (GH up, IGF-1 up) — that part is real and human-proven. But 'moves a biomarker' is NOT 'improves muscle, fat, recovery, or healthspan.' Those downstream benefits in healthy adults are essentially unproven by rigorous trials; forum claims run far ahead of the data (Speculative for anti-aging/performance). MECHANISM ≠ SAFETY: the 'feedback stays intact, so it's safer than HGH' argument is a logical mechanism story, not a demonstrated long-term safety result. THE IGF-1 / CANCER FLAG: chronically pushing the GH-IGF-1 axis up is biologically double-edged — IGF-1 is a growth signal, and extrahypothalamic GHRH/IGF-1 signaling is implicated as a growth factor in several cancers (the corpus and PubMed show GHRH-receptor splice variants like SV1 driving tumor growth; GHRH ANTAGONISTS are being studied AS anti-cancer agents) [PMID 32156725; PMID 35322894; corpus MoA]. That doesn't prove sermorelin causes cancer, but it's the reason long-term safety is genuinely unknown and not just unmeasured. REGULATORY: it's a compounded peptide, banned in sport (WADA-prohibited growth-hormone secretagogue), and not an FDA-approved consumer anti-aging therapy. NOT MEDICAL ADVICE: this explains mechanism for teaching, it is not a recommendation to use it.
