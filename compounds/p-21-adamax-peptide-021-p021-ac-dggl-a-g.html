P-21

A CNTF-derived tetrapeptide that boosts hippocampal neurogenesis and BDNF in rodents — genuinely interesting preclinical biology, but zero human data and now sold gray-market intranasally on the strength of mouse studies alone.

Rodent / preclinical 11 citations
Class
CNTF-derived neurogenic/neurotrophic tetrapeptide mimetic (adamantylated peptide) — investigational, preclinical
Primary target
Mimics the CNTF active region while inhibiting LIF/CNTF (gp130–JAK/STAT) detrimental signaling; upregulates hippocampal neurogenesis and BDNF, lowering GSK-3β activity and tau hyperphosphorylation (all rodent/in vitro)
Human status
Preclinical only. P021 was discovered in the Khalid Iqbal laboratory at the New York State Institute for Basic Research in Developmental Disabilities (NYSIBR/IBR, Staten Island) and its commercial development is associated with Phanes Biotech Inc. (Malvern, PA). Every study to date is in vitro or in rodents (mouse, rat) — Alzheimer's (3xTg-AD), Down syndrome (Ts65Dn), traumatic brain injury, cognitive aging, and CDKL5 deficiency models. A ClinicalTrials.gov search for P021/Adamax/P-21 returned zero relevant human trials; there are no published human pharmacokinetic, safety, dose-finding, or efficacy data, and no regulatory approval anywhere. It is not an approved drug and is sold only as gray-market 'research'/nootropic material. The 2024 CDKL5 study (Mottolese, PMID 39592934, which lists Phanes Biotech and NYSIBR affiliations) is a cautionary data point: in vitro rescue did NOT reproduce in vivo (no BDNF increase, no neuroanatomical benefit, only limited behavioral benefit), underscoring how model- and context-dependent the biology is.

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Designed as a small drug-like fragment of CNTF Established

    P021 (Peptide 021) is a tetrapeptide, Ac-DGGL(A)G-NH2, derived by epitope-mapping the most active region of human ciliary neurotrophic factor (CNTF). An adamantylated glycine ('A', the 'Adamax' part) was added to the C-terminus to improve blood-brain-barrier crossing and resist breakdown by exopeptidases, making it 'druggable.' Its precursor Peptide 6/6c was the original hit. Blanchard 2010, PMID 20952820; Baazaoui & Iqbal 2017, PMID 28655344; Kazim & Iqbal 2016 review, PMID 27400746

  2. Captures CNTF's benefit while avoiding its toxic signaling Evidence-rodent

    Full CNTF has neurotrophic activity but causes anorexia, muscle loss, cramps and hyperalgesia in humans. P021 is described as reproducing the neurogenic/neurotrophic effect of the CNTF active region while inhibiting leukemia inhibitory factor (LIF) signaling — the shared gp130/JAK-STAT arm thought to drive those adverse effects. This 'mimic-CNTF-but-block-LIF' framing is the developers' mechanistic model, demonstrated functionally in rodents rather than by resolved receptor-binding data. Kazim & Iqbal 2016 review, PMID 27400746; Blanchard 2010, PMID 20952820

  3. Increases adult hippocampal (dentate gyrus) neurogenesis Evidence-rodent

    In adult mice, chronic P021/Peptide 6c increased commitment, differentiation and survival of newborn progenitor cells in the dentate gyrus (doublecortin, NeuN, BrdU), and these new neurons functionally integrated (raised c-fos, MAP2, synaptophysin). This is the core claimed effect. In the TBI study the precursor Peptide 6 raised newborn neurons by ~80%. Blanchard 2010, PMID 20952820; Chohan 2015 (TBI), PMID 25255260

  4. Raises BDNF expression Evidence-rodent

    Across multiple rodent AD/DS/TBI models, P021 increased brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), the growth factor central to synaptic plasticity and neuron survival, presented as the key node linking P021 to downstream effects. Importantly, one 2024 study (CDKL5-deficient mice) FAILED to raise BDNF in vivo despite in vitro rescue, showing the effect is not universal across disease models. Kazim 2014, PMID 25046994; Wei 2021, PMID 34057082; Mottolese 2024 (negative in vivo), PMID 39592934

  5. BDNF lowers GSK-3β activity, reducing tau hyperphosphorylation Evidence-rodent

    In AD-model mice, higher BDNF was associated with decreased glycogen synthase kinase-3β (GSK-3β) activity — a major tau kinase — which reduced abnormal tau hyperphosphorylation at AD-associated sites. The authors describe this as the 'probable' pathway. This is a disease-model (tauopathy) mechanism, not a demonstrated nootropic effect in healthy people. Kazim 2014, PMID 25046994; Kazim & Iqbal 2016 review, PMID 27400746

  6. Activates plasticity signaling cascades and restores synaptic proteins Evidence-rodent

    P021 increased phosphorylated CREB (pCREB) and engaged PLC/PKC, MEK/ERK and PI3K/Akt cascades, and restored deficits in postsynaptic proteins such as PSD-95, improving dendritic and synaptic density in injured or diseased rodent brain. Wei 2021, PMID 34057082; Wei 2020, PMID 32854771; Chohan 2015, PMID 25255260

  7. Translates to cognitive/memory improvement in rodent models Evidence-rodent

    The downstream readout is improved hippocampus-dependent memory (spatial reference and novel-object tasks) in normal mice, aged rats, and models of Alzheimer's, Down syndrome and traumatic brain injury. All memory benefit to date is in animals; none has been shown in humans. Blanchard 2010, PMID 20952820; Bolognin 2014 (aging), PMID 24702821; Kazim 2017 (Down syndrome), PMID 28368015

  8. Marketed nootropic claims outrun the data Speculative

    Vendors position intranasal P-21/Adamax as a next-gen Cerebrolysin/Dihexa alternative for neurogenesis. There are NO human pharmacokinetic, safety or efficacy studies, no clinical trials, and no controlled data on intranasal dosing in people. Extrapolating chronic oral-diet mouse results to human intranasal self-dosing is speculative. No human trials found (ClinicalTrials.gov query for P021/Adamax/P-21 returned 0 relevant neurotrophic trials); all cited evidence is preclinical

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. P021 is a C-terminally adamantylated tetrapeptide (Ac-DGGL[A]G-NH2) derived by epitope mapping of human CNTF; its precursor is Peptide 6/6c. It is described as reproducing the neurotrophic activity of the CNTF active region while inhibiting the LIF signaling pathway (the shared gp130/JAK-STAT arm implicated in CNTF's adverse effects). Precise receptor-level binding (specific receptor subunits, affinities/Ki/EC50) has not been resolved in public target-binding databases — there is no ChEMBL compound record (confirmed 0 hits), so 'target' here is functional/pathway-level rather than a validated single-receptor interaction.

Signalling cascade. Functional cascade (all rodent/in vitro): CNTF-region mimicry + LIF-pathway inhibition → increased BDNF → (a) increased pCREB with downstream PLC/PKC, MEK/ERK and PI3K/Akt activation driving synaptic-protein expression and dentate gyrus neurogenesis; (b) decreased GSK-3β activity → reduced tau hyperphosphorylation. Net: enhanced neurogenesis, synaptic/dendritic repair, and improved hippocampus-dependent memory.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Preclinical studies used chronic ORAL administration (drug-in-diet) or subcutaneous depot delivery; the founding Peptide 6/6c work used subcutaneous depot. The marketed nootropic route is INTRANASAL, which has never been formally characterized for P021.
Absorption / bioavailability
The adamantyl (adamantylated glycine) modification and C-terminal amidation were added specifically to increase blood-brain-barrier permeability and resist exopeptidase degradation, making the peptide orally 'druggable' in rodents. Quantitative oral bioavailability figures and human absorption data are not published.
Half-life
Not established in humans; the peptide backbone is short but stabilized by terminal modifications. No published human half-life.
Metabolism
Designed to resist exopeptidase cleavage via terminal N-acetylation/C-amidation and adamantylation; detailed metabolic pathway/CYP data not published.
Excretion
Not characterized in the public literature.
Notes
There are NO human pharmacokinetic data of any kind — no Cmax, Tmax, AUC, half-life, brain penetration, or intranasal absorption studies in people. All PK inferences are from rodent dosing designed for oral/subcutaneous delivery.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

No human dose-response exists. In rodents, effects were seen with chronic dosing (e.g., 50 nmol/day subcutaneously over 30 days in the TBI study; drug-in-diet over weeks-to-months in AD/aging models). Because the mechanism is neurogenic/neurotrophic and cumulative (newborn-neuron survival and BDNF-driven plasticity build over weeks), rodent benefits were tied to prolonged administration rather than acute dosing. No threshold, ceiling, or intranasal human dose-response is known, and animal nmol/day figures do NOT translate to a human protocol.

How it compares

  • vs Cerebrolysin
    same: Both are peptide-based neurotrophic agents aiming to mimic endogenous neurotrophic support and converge on PI3K/Akt-type trophic signaling.
    different: Cerebrolysin is a porcine-brain-derived multi-peptide mixture given IV/IM with decades of human clinical trials (stroke/dementia); P021 is a single defined synthetic tetrapeptide with ZERO human data. The 'alternative' framing overstates P021's readiness.
  • vs Dihexa
    same: Both marketed to the nootropic community as pro-neuroplasticity peptides based largely on preclinical data.
    different: Dihexa is an angiotensin IV-derived HGF/c-Met potentiator acting on synaptogenesis; P021 is a CNTF-derived tetrapeptide acting via LIF inhibition and BDNF/neurogenesis. Different targets, different mechanisms; both lack human efficacy/safety data.
  • vs Native CNTF (Axokine)
    same: P021 is derived from CNTF's active region and aims to keep its neurotrophic benefit.
    different: Recombinant CNTF/Axokine failed clinically partly due to side effects (anorexia, muscle loss) and immunogenicity; P021 was specifically engineered to inhibit the LIF arm blamed for those effects and to be small/BBB-permeable/orally active — but this advantage is demonstrated only in rodents.
  • vs BDNF-raising interventions generally (e.g., exercise)
    same: Converges on increased BDNF and hippocampal neurogenesis as the effector node.
    different: Exercise and lifestyle raise BDNF through physiological, self-limiting, well-characterized routes with proven human safety; P021 is an unstudied exogenous peptide with no human safety profile.

History & legal status

P021 (Peptide 021) originated in the laboratory of Khalid Iqbal at the New York State Institute for Basic Research in Developmental Disabilities (NYSIBR/IBR, Staten Island). The lineage began with Peptide 6/6c reported in 2010 (Blanchard et al., PMID 20952820); the adamantylated, druggable P021 was then advanced through a decade of rodent AD, Down syndrome, TBI, aging and CDKL5 studies (2014–2024). Commercial development is associated with Phanes Biotech Inc. (Malvern, PA), whose affiliation appears on the 2024 CDKL5 paper (PMID 39592934). As of this writing it has NO regulatory approval, and a ClinicalTrials.gov search for P021/Adamax/P-21 returns zero relevant human trials. Its appearance as intranasal 'P-21 / Adamax' in the nootropic/peptide gray market is a consumer/vendor phenomenon, not a sanctioned clinical program.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Whether P021 does anything measurable in humans — no PK, safety, or efficacy data exist.
  • Whether the marketed intranasal route delivers meaningful, safe brain exposure (never studied for this peptide).
  • The precise receptor-level mechanism — which receptors/subunits it binds and with what affinity (no ChEMBL/binding data).
  • Why the BDNF/neurogenesis effect engages in AD/DS/TBI models but failed in vivo in the CDKL5 model — i.e., in which biological contexts it actually works.
  • Long-term safety of chronic neurogenic/neurotrophic stimulation, including any proliferative/oncologic risk.
  • Independent replication outside the originating lab and its collaborators.
  • Product identity, purity, sterility and endotoxin status of gray-market material.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

This is harm-reduction context, NOT a recommendation to use P021 (there is no established safe human use, and no dosing guidance is provided). For anyone who has already taken gray-market intranasal peptides and is monitoring their health, reasonable things to watch and discuss with a clinician include: (1) local nasal/sinus symptoms — irritation, congestion, bleeding, or signs of infection at the administration site; (2) systemic hypersensitivity/immune reactions (rash, swelling, fever) — unregulated peptides carry endotoxin/immunogenicity risk; (3) new or changing neurological symptoms (headache, mood changes, seizures) given the compound acts on brain plasticity pathways; (4) any new or unusual lumps, growths, or unexplained systemic symptoms, given the theoretical proliferative concern of chronic neurotrophic signaling; (5) baseline and follow-up clinical review if combining with other experimental neuroactive peptides. Because there are no validated biomarkers or human safety data, the most important 'monitoring' step is to recognize that efficacy and safety in humans are simply unknown, to source-verify any material, and to involve a qualified clinician rather than self-experimenting. No dosing protocol is provided.

Caveats

The entire evidence base is preclinical and dominated by a single originating laboratory (Iqbal/NYSIBR) and its collaborators, which raises independent-replication concerns; most work uses chronic oral/diet or subcutaneous dosing in genetically engineered rodents, not intranasal dosing in healthy adult humans. There are NO human data — no pharmacokinetics, no safety, no efficacy, no trials. The marketed intranasal route has never been characterized in people; intranasal absorption, brain delivery, and systemic exposure are unknown for this compound. The BDNF effect is not universal: a 2024 in vivo CDKL5 study (PMID 39592934) found no BDNF increase and no neuroanatomical benefit despite in vitro rescue, so the 'neurogenesis engine' does not fire in every context. Boosting neurogenesis, BDNF and cell survival is a double-edged sword — the same pathways (PI3K/Akt, gp130/STAT3, sustained trophic signaling) are implicated in tumor biology, so an unmonitored long-term neurogenic/neurotrophic agent carries a theoretical proliferative/oncologic concern that has not been studied for P021. 'Mimics CNTF but blocks LIF's bad effects' is the developers' functional framing; the precise receptor-level pharmacology (which receptor subunits it binds, and with what affinity) is not resolved by classic target-binding data (no ChEMBL compound record). Gray-market material has no purity, sterility, identity or endotoxin guarantees; intranasal peptide use adds infection and local-tissue risks. This is a mechanism/education entry, NOT an endorsement or a use recommendation.
