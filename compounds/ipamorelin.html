Ipamorelin

The prototype "clean" ghrelin-receptor agonist: a pentapeptide that fires a short, natural-looking GH pulse without dragging up cortisol or prolactin — but its human evidence is a pair of failed gut-motility trials, not body-composition data.

Rodent / preclinical 8 citations
Class
Selective ghrelin-receptor (GHS-R1a) agonist / growth-hormone secretagogue (GHRP-class pentapeptide)
Primary target
GHS-R1a (ghrelin receptor) on anterior-pituitary somatotrophs; evokes a pulsatile GH release with minimal ACTH/cortisol or prolactin co-secretion
Human status
Investigational; never approved for any indication and abandoned as a drug candidate. Ipamorelin was developed at Novo Nordisk (Denmark) in the 1990s, then licensed to Helsinn Therapeutics, who ran the only substantial human program: two placebo-controlled phase 2 trials for postoperative ileus / GI recovery — NCT00672074 (117 patients, published as Beck et al. 2014, PMID 25331030) and NCT01280344 (320 patients enrolled, dose-finding, completed 2014, no results posted). In the published trial ipamorelin (0.03 mg/kg IV twice daily) was well tolerated but did NOT significantly beat placebo on median time to first tolerated meal (25.3 vs 32.6 h, p=0.15), and the program was discontinued. Crucially, there are NO published human randomized trials of ipamorelin for muscle gain, fat loss, anti-aging, or body composition — the fitness/longevity use case rests on animal GH-release data plus general GH/IGF-1 human biology and anecdote. It is sold only as a 'research chemical'/compounded peptide, is not an approved medicine, and is a WADA-prohibited substance (S2 peptide hormones / growth factors / GH secretagogues) in sport.

At a glance

  • Short, pulsatile release of the body's own growth hormone from the pituitary (Evidence-rodent/porcine; direct human GH-release data are thin)
  • Inferred downstream rise in IGF-1 driving lean-tissue synthesis, collagen turnover and lipolysis (extrapolated from GH/IGF-1 human biology, NOT from any ipamorelin body-composition trial)
  • 'Clean' selectivity — negligible cortisol/ACTH and prolactin elevation vs older GHRPs (Evidence-rodent/porcine; not confirmed in humans)
  • Prokinetic / gut-motility effects via the ghrelin receptor (Evidence-rodent; FAILED to beat placebo in two human ileus/GI-recovery trials)
  • Anti-catabolic (muscle strength) and bone-formation effects in glucocorticoid-treated rats (Evidence-rodent)
  • Commonly reported human anecdotal effects: water retention, hand tingling/carpal-tunnel-like symptoms, transient hunger, reported better sleep — mostly generic GH-excess signs, not ipamorelin-specific trial data (Speculative/Contested)

Mechanism, step by step

  1. A pentapeptide built to hit one receptor Established

    Ipamorelin is a synthetic 5-amino-acid peptide (Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2). It was engineered from the older GHRP-1 scaffold by deleting the central Ala-Trp dipeptide, which stripped away the off-target ACTH/cortisol-releasing activity while keeping GH-releasing potency. It is NOT a form of growth hormone itself — it tells your own pituitary to release GH. Raun 1998, PMID 9849822

  2. Binds GHS-R1a, the ghrelin receptor Established

    It acts as an agonist at GHS-R1a, the same G-protein-coupled receptor that the stomach hormone ghrelin uses. Pharmacology with GHRP vs GHRH antagonists shows ipamorelin works through the GHRP/ghrelin-receptor pathway, not the separate GHRH receptor. This is a distinct 'lever' from GHRH analogs like CJC-1295 or sermorelin. Raun 1998, PMID 9849822

  3. Turns on Gq signaling inside the somatotroph Established

    GHS-R1a is a Gq/G11-coupled receptor. Activation drives phospholipase-C, IP3/DAG and a rise in intracellular calcium (plus PKC), which triggers the somatotroph to dump its stored growth-hormone granules. GHS-R1a also amplifies GHRH-driven cAMP signaling via PKC, which is why GHRPs and GHRH analogs are synergistic. Note: this signaling is characterized mainly for ghrelin/GHS at the receptor generally, not specifically re-demonstrated for ipamorelin. Bresciani 2019, PMID 31491959; Camiña 2003, PMID 14610293

  4. Releases a short, pulse-like burst of GH Evidence-rodent

    In rat pituitary cells and in living rats and pigs, ipamorelin releases GH with potency and efficacy similar to GHRP-6. Because the peptide is cleared quickly, the GH rise is a discrete pulse rather than a flat, sustained elevation — argued to be closer to the body's natural GH bursts than exogenous HGH. Direct human GH-release pharmacodynamic data are sparse; the pulsatility claim rests mainly on animal work plus general secretagogue physiology. Raun 1998, PMID 9849822

  5. The 'clean' part: minimal cortisol and prolactin Evidence-rodent

    This is ipamorelin's defining feature. In pigs it released GH as strongly as GHRP-6 but did NOT raise ACTH or cortisol above the levels seen with GHRH alone — even at doses more than 200x the GH-releasing dose — and did not move prolactin, FSH, LH or TSH. Older GHRPs (GHRP-2/-6) meaningfully raise ACTH/cortisol; ipamorelin's selectivity is why it became the preferred GHRP. This selectivity is animal (porcine/rodent) data; whether it fully holds in humans at used doses is untested. Raun 1998, PMID 9849822

  6. A likely second site of action in the stomach Evidence-rodent

    Part of a GHRP's GH effect appears to come from outside the pituitary: radiolabeled GHS accumulate in the acid-secreting stomach, and removing GI tissue blunts the GH response to GHRP-6 by 60-70%. This suggests GHRPs may also work by releasing endogenous ghrelin — one reason this drug class also has gut-motility (prokinetic) effects. This resection experiment used GHRP-6, not ipamorelin directly. Ahnfelt-Rønne 2001, PMID 11322495

  7. Downstream: GH raises IGF-1 and drives the anabolic effects Contested

    A GH pulse acts on the liver and peripheral tissues to raise IGF-1. GH/IGF-1 together promote lean-tissue and collagen synthesis and lipolysis (fat breakdown) — this is well-established human endocrinology. But essentially all of ipamorelin's hoped-for physique/recovery effects are INFERRED downstream GH/IGF-1 effects; they have not been demonstrated in any human ipamorelin trial. So they carry the same theoretical benefits — and the same risks — as raising GH by any route. Bresciani 2019, PMID 31491959; acromegaly GH/IGF-1 human data PMID 18349062

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Agonist at GHS-R1a (the ghrelin/growth-hormone-secretagogue receptor type 1a), a class-A GPCR. Ipamorelin is a pentapeptide, Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2, derived from the GHRP-1 scaffold by removing the central Ala-Trp dipeptide. In primary rat pituitary cells it released GH with EC50 ≈ 1.3 nmol/L and Emax ≈ 85% (vs GHRP-6 EC50 ≈ 2.2 nmol/L, 100%). Its defining property is functional selectivity for GH release with essentially no ACTH/cortisol or prolactin co-release in animal models.

Signalling cascade. GHS-R1a couples primarily to Gαq/11 → phospholipase-C-β → IP3/DAG → intracellular Ca2+ mobilization and PKC, driving Ca2+-dependent exocytosis of stored GH granules from somatotrophs. GHS-R1a also potentiates GHRH-receptor cAMP signaling via a PKC-dependent mechanism (basis for GHRP + GHRH synergy) and functionally opposes hypothalamic somatostatin tone. A peripheral component may involve gastric GHS-R1a and release of endogenous ghrelin (shown for GHRP-6, inferred for the class).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Subcutaneous or intravenous injection (peptide; not orally bioavailable — degraded by GI proteases). Human IV dosing in the ileus trials was weight-based (0.03 mg/kg).
Absorption / bioavailability
Negligible oral bioavailability. Injected peptide is absorbed and distributed rapidly; no reliable published human oral formulation.
Half-life
Short — on the order of ~2 hours cited in secondary/manufacturer sources; the exact human half-life is not well documented in peer-reviewed literature. The pharmacodynamic GH pulse is brief (minutes to ~2 hours), consistent with rapid clearance. TREAT PRECISE PK NUMBERS AS LOW-CERTAINTY.
Metabolism
Proteolytic cleavage by peptidases in plasma and tissues, as for other small peptides; not a cytochrome-P450 substrate, so classic CYP drug interactions are not expected.
Excretion
Peptide fragments/amino acids handled by normal proteolytic and renal pathways; specific human excretion data not well characterized.
Notes
Because clearance is fast and the GH response is pulsatile, ipamorelin is often paired in grey-market practice with a longer-acting GHRH analog (CJC-1295 with or without DAC) to combine a primed somatotroph pool with a release trigger. All human PK detail should be considered sparse and partly derived from non-peer-reviewed sources.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

In animals ipamorelin shows a clear dose-dependent GH release: in anesthetized rats ED50 ≈ 80 nmol/kg (Emax ≈ 1545 ng/mL GH); in conscious pigs ED50 ≈ 2.3 nmol/kg (Raun 1998). The selectivity is dose-robust in animals — no ACTH/cortisol release even at >200x the GH ED50. In the human ileus trial a fixed 0.03 mg/kg IV twice-daily dose was tolerated but not efficacious for the gut endpoint. There is NO validated human dose-response for body-composition or 'anti-aging' endpoints, and this monograph gives no dosing protocol. Note desensitization: sustained GHS-R1a agonism can attenuate responses over time (homologous desensitization is documented for the receptor), and endogenous negative feedback (IGF-1, somatostatin) constrains how much GH any secretagogue can push.

How it compares

  • vs GHRP-2 / GHRP-6 / hexarelin (older GHRPs)
    same: All are GHS-R1a agonists that trigger a GH pulse and can raise appetite; all are injectable peptides, none approved for physique use.
    different: Same receptor (GHS-R1a) and similar GH-releasing potency, but ipamorelin is markedly more SELECTIVE — the older GHRPs meaningfully raise cortisol/ACTH (and prolactin), whereas ipamorelin does not in animal studies. This 'clean' profile is its entire selling point.
  • vs CJC-1295 / sermorelin / tesamorelin (GHRH analogs)
    same: Both are secretagogues that raise your OWN GH/IGF-1 rather than injecting HGH, and both preserve pulsatility better than exogenous GH. (Note: tesamorelin is the one FDA-approved agent here, for HIV lipodystrophy.)
    different: Different receptor and mechanism: GHRH analogs bind the GHRH receptor (Gs/cAMP) to prime and build the releasable GH pool; ipamorelin binds the ghrelin receptor (Gq/Ca2+) to trigger release and blunt somatostatin. Hence the popular CJC-1295 + ipamorelin stack — complementary levers argued to give a supra-additive GH pulse.
  • vs Exogenous HGH (somatropin)
    same: Both ultimately act through the GH/IGF-1 axis and share GH-excess risks (insulin resistance, edema, tissue growth).
    different: HGH delivers a fixed, non-pulsatile dose of the hormone; ipamorelin evokes a smaller, self-limited, feedback-constrained pulse of endogenous GH — lower ceiling but arguably more physiologic and self-limiting.
  • vs Anamorelin (clinically developed GHS-R1a agonist)
    same: Both are selective ghrelin-receptor agonists acting on the same target with appetite/GH effects.
    different: Anamorelin is an orally active, selective GHS-R1a agonist that reached phase 3 and is approved in Japan (ADLUMIZ) for cancer cachexia; ipamorelin is injectable and was abandoned. Anamorelin thus has the human efficacy/approval evidence ipamorelin lacks.

History & legal status

Discovered and characterized at Novo Nordisk (Denmark) in the mid-1990s and reported by Raun et al. in 1998 as 'the first selective GH secretagogue' — a milestone that separated GH release from HPA-axis and prolactin activation. Rights later passed to Helsinn Therapeutics, which pursued postoperative ileus / GI-recovery indications, running two placebo-controlled phase 2 trials (NCT00672074, 117 patients, published 2014; and NCT01280344, 320 patients, completed 2014, unpublished). Neither established efficacy and the program was discontinued; ipamorelin has NEVER been approved by the FDA, EMA or any regulator for any indication. It is not a compendial/pharmaceutical product and is marketed only as a 'research chemical' or via compounding channels (subject to tightening restrictions on compounded peptides). It is banned in sport under WADA (S2 peptide hormones / growth factors / GH secretagogues).

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Whether ipamorelin produces any meaningful body-composition, strength, recovery, or 'anti-aging' benefit in humans — there are zero published RCTs for these uses.
  • The true human pharmacokinetics (half-life, bioavailability by route) — peer-reviewed human PK data are sparse; common '~2 h half-life' figures come from secondary sources.
  • Whether the animal 'clean' selectivity (no cortisol/prolactin) fully translates to humans at the doses people actually use.
  • Long-term safety of chronic, repeated GH pulses in healthy adults — including insulin sensitivity and the theoretical tumor-promotion risk of sustained IGF-1 elevation.
  • Degree and durability of GHS-R1a desensitization with ongoing use.
  • Real-world product identity/purity/sterility, since supply is grey-market and unregulated.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Harm-reduction monitoring only (NOT an endorsement to use, and NOT a protocol). Because ipamorelin acts through the GH/IGF-1 axis, the things worth watching are the signs of GH excess and metabolic disturbance: fasting glucose and HbA1c (GH is diabetogenic); IGF-1 relative to an age-appropriate reference range rather than a supraphysiologic level; and clinical signs such as carpal-tunnel-like tingling/numbness, joint pain, and edema, which can indicate GH excess. People with any personal or family history of active or prior cancer, diabetes/pre-diabetes, or heart disease warrant particular caution given proliferative and insulin-antagonistic GH/IGF-1 effects. Because it is a ghrelin agonist, appetite changes may occur. Product-quality vigilance matters: grey-market peptides carry identity/purity/sterility risk (injection-site infection, endotoxin exposure). Anyone considering this should involve a qualified clinician, and competitive athletes should know it is WADA-prohibited. Seek medical care for persistent numbness, vision changes, severe headache, or symptoms of high blood sugar. This section deliberately gives no doses, frequencies, titration, or 'cycle' guidance.

Caveats

The single most important caveat: ipamorelin's reputation is built on strong PRECLINICAL selectivity data (Raun 1998, rat/pig) plus extrapolation from human GH/IGF-1 biology, NOT on human outcome trials. The only human efficacy trials were for gut motility/GI recovery and both FAILED to beat placebo. No human data show it builds muscle, cuts fat, or slows aging. 'Raises your own GH pulse' is documented in animals, but the magnitude, durability, and clinical relevance in healthy adults are not established in humans. Because the effects people want are downstream GH/IGF-1 effects, ipamorelin carries the same theoretical risks as raising GH by any means — insulin resistance / higher blood glucose, fluid retention, carpal-tunnel-like symptoms, and, with chronic excess, acromegaly-like tissue and organ growth plus possible promotion of pre-existing tumors (proliferative IGF-1 signaling). Product-quality risk is real: grey-market 'research' peptides vary in purity/identity/sterility and are not pharmaceutical-grade. GHS-R1a agonism can also increase appetite. This monograph is mechanism education and harm-reduction only — it is not medical advice and contains no dosing protocol.
