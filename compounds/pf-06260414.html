PF-06260414

A Pfizer non-steroidal SARM that cleared a first-in-human Phase 1 trial with clean short-term tolerability but clear signals of testosterone/SHBG/HDL suppression and liver-enzyme (ALT) rises — then went no further.

Limited human data 9 citations
Class
Non-steroidal selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM); an isoquinoline-carbonitrile cyclic-sulfamide small molecule (Pfizer development candidate)
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR, gene NR3C4) — a tissue-selective agonist intended to favor muscle/bone over prostate/skin (selectivity demonstrated only for the SARM class in animals, not for this molecule in humans)
Human status
Human data exist but are limited to a single completed Phase 1 first-in-human study (NCT02070939; Bhattacharya et al., Clin Ther 2016, PMID 27085586): 72 healthy men (Western + a Japanese cohort), randomized double-blind placebo-controlled single- and multiple-ascending-dose design (single doses 1–400 mg; multiple doses 3–100 mg BID; plus a 60 mg once-daily cohort and a 30 mg BID Japanese cohort, n≈8 per cohort, n=7 Japanese). It established safety, PK and endocrine PD only — no efficacy (muscle, strength, body composition) endpoint was tested and no disease population was studied. A second Phase 1 relative-bioavailability/food-effect study of a spray-dried-dispersion solid vs nanosuspension formulation (NCT02393807) was WITHDRAWN with zero enrollment. There are no Phase 2+ trials; Pfizer did not advance the compound. It is one of only about half a dozen SARMs with any published human PK/PD data, which is why it appears in SARM systematic reviews (PMID 39285652), but it is an abandoned pipeline candidate — not an approved or marketed drug, and not approved for any use anywhere.

At a glance

  • Dose-dependent suppression of the body's own testosterone (HPG-axis shutdown) — the clearest human pharmacodynamic effect in Phase 1 (max at 100 mg BID)
  • Lowering of sex-hormone-binding globulin (SHBG) and of HDL ('good') cholesterol — on-target androgenic lab changes seen in the trial
  • Rise in liver enzyme ALT (most frequent adverse event, 7 subjects) — a human liver safety signal
  • Intended muscle/lean-mass anabolism — expected from the SARM class but NOT measured for this compound in any species reported
  • Prostate sparing is the design goal (PSA was monitored) but tissue selectivity was never confirmed in people
  • Ethnic PK difference: Japanese subjects had ~80-100% higher exposure (Cmax +98.6%, AUCτ +79.5%) than Western subjects at matched doses, yet similar HPG-axis hormonal effects

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Binds the androgen receptor as an oral non-steroidal ligand Established

    PF-06260414 is a small synthetic molecule (not a steroid) intended to occupy the same ligand pocket testosterone and DHT use on the androgen receptor (AR). Because it is non-steroidal it is not aromatized to estrogen or 5-alpha-reduced to DHT the way testosterone is. Its ChEMBL profile (formula C14H14N4O2S, MW ~302.4, QED 0.86, zero Lipinski violations) is that of a drug-like small molecule. Note: it was dosed ORALLY in the Phase 1 trial, but ChEMBL does NOT flag it as oral (all route flags are unset), so 'oral' is established from the clinical study, not from ChEMBL. ChEMBL CHEMBL4297476 (InChIKey ZKAVFOXYJCREBQ-SNVBAGLBSA-N); oral dosing per Bhattacharya 2016 PMID 27085586

  2. AR binding drives receptor activation and nuclear translocation Established

    When an agonist binds AR, the receptor changes shape, sheds chaperone proteins, moves into the nucleus, and binds DNA at androgen-response elements to switch anabolic genes on. This canonical AR signaling cascade is shared by testosterone, anabolic steroids and SARMs and is established AR biology. That PF-06260414 specifically acts as an AR agonist is INFERRED from its SARM classification and its downstream human endocrine effects (testosterone/SHBG/HDL suppression), not from a published human receptor-occupancy or binding-affinity study — none exists for this molecule. Androgen receptor MoA card (corpus, curated); AR–GnRH repression PMID 22877652

  3. In muscle, AR activation promotes anabolic gene transcription (the intended effect) Evidence-rodent

    The therapeutic goal of a SARM is to switch on muscle- and bone-building gene programs. For the SARM class this raises lean body mass in humans, and analog non-steroidal SARMs increase lean mass in rodents and monkeys. For PF-06260414 specifically, muscle/lean-mass efficacy was NEVER measured in any species reported here — its only human trial was a Phase 1 safety/PK/PD study in healthy men that tracked hormones and safety labs, not body composition. So the anabolic step is a class/animal extrapolation, not a demonstrated property of this drug. SARM class review PMID 39285652; analog SARM-2f (Takeda) in monkeys PMID 32030892

  4. Tissue selectivity is designed to spare the prostate — unproven for this molecule in humans Evidence-rodent

    SARMs are engineered so the same receptor produces strong effects in muscle/bone and weaker effects in prostate and skin, thought to arise from tissue-specific co-regulator recruitment and the absence of DHT amplification. The Phase 1 trial monitored PSA as a safety endpoint, but true muscle-vs-prostate selectivity for PF-06260414 was never established in humans. Class selectivity rests on rodent Hershberger-type assays (for related benzonitrile SARMs, e.g. Takeda's series). Aikawa 2017 PMID 28454849 (Takeda benzonitrile SARM series); Androgen receptor MoA card (corpus)

  5. AR activation feeds back on the brain to suppress the body's own testosterone (HPG-axis shutdown) Evidence-human

    An AR agonist signals the hypothalamus and pituitary that androgen levels are 'sufficient,' lowering LH/FSH and the testes' own testosterone output. In the Phase 1 trial, multiple-dose PF-06260414 (up to 100 mg twice daily) produced the largest placebo-corrected drops in total testosterone and sex-hormone-binding globulin (SHBG) — a direct, dose-dependent HUMAN signal of HPG-axis suppression, the same 'shutdown' seen with steroids and other SARMs. Bhattacharya 2016 PMID 27085586; AR–GnRH repression mechanism PMID 22877652

  6. Oral androgenic signaling and first-pass liver exposure perturb liver enzymes and lipids Evidence-human

    Oral androgen-active compounds are handled by the liver and can raise liver enzymes and lower HDL ('good') cholesterol. In the Phase 1 trial the single most common adverse event was a rise in ALT (a liver enzyme), reported in 7 subjects, and HDL was among the most sensitive lab values to modulation. HDL suppression is a recognized on-target androgenic effect; the ALT rise is a real human safety signal, though its precise mechanism (hepatocellular vs cholestatic, as documented for other oral SARMs) was not characterized for this molecule. Bhattacharya 2016 PMID 27085586; SARM/ostarine DILI PMID 34368386, PMID 35340496

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Non-steroidal small-molecule agonist of the androgen receptor (AR, NR3C4). Molecular formula C14H14N4O2S, MW ~302.4; the structure (SMILES C[C@@H]1CNS(=O)(=O)N(c2ccc3c(C#N)nccc3c2)C1) is an isoquinoline-carbonitrile linked to a cyclic sulfamide (methyl-substituted 1,2,6-thiadiazinane 1,1-dioxide), NOT a simple benzonitrile sulfonamide (the nitrile sits on the isoquinoline ring). Single defined stereocenter (chirality=1), ALogP ~1.4, PSA ~86, QED 0.86, zero Lipinski violations, ChEMBL max_phase 1 (CHEMBL4297476, InChIKey ZKAVFOXYJCREBQ-SNVBAGLBSA-N). As a non-steroidal ligand it is not a substrate for aromatase or 5-alpha-reductase, so it does not generate estrogenic or DHT metabolites. No published human AR binding-affinity (Ki) or receptor-occupancy value exists; ChEMBL holds no curated mechanism-of-action record and no route/dosed-ingredient flags for it, consistent with an early-stage candidate.

Signalling cascade. Canonical AR pathway: cytoplasmic AR–chaperone complex → agonist binding → conformational change and nuclear translocation → dimerization and binding to androgen-response elements → recruitment of tissue-specific co-activators/co-repressors → transcription of anabolic (muscle/bone) genes, plus negative feedback that represses hypothalamic GnRH and pituitary LH/FSH. SARM 'selectivity' is attributed to tissue-specific co-regulator milieus and the absence of DHT amplification in skin/prostate.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral. The Phase 1 trial (NCT02070939) dosed it orally as single doses 1–400 mg and multiple doses up to 100 mg BID. (ChEMBL does not carry an 'oral' route flag for this compound; the route is established from the trial.)
Absorption / bioavailability
Fast absorption with median Tmax ~1–2 h. A dedicated relative-bioavailability/food-effect study of a spray-dried-dispersion solid vs nanosuspension formulation at 30 mg (NCT02393807) was planned but WITHDRAWN with zero enrollment, so formulation bioavailability and food effect were never reported.
Half-life
Mean terminal t½ approximately 6.9–12.8 h across cohorts — compatible with once- or twice-daily dosing.
Metabolism
Not fully characterized in the public literature. An equine in-vitro liver-microsome study (PMID 34714606) reported the first identification of PF-06260414 phase I metabolites (five metabolites) for doping-control purposes; human metabolic pathway, responsible CYP enzymes, and drug–drug-interaction profile are not published.
Excretion
Urinary PK (amount excreted unchanged, renal clearance) was a specified trial endpoint; quantitative values are not detailed in the abstract-level record.
Notes
PK was dose-proportional and time-independent (linear). Notable ethnic difference: at matched doses, Japanese subjects had Cmax ~98.6% and AUCτ ~79.5% higher than Western subjects, yet showed similar HPG-axis hormone modulation — an exposure difference without a proportional PD difference.

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

Single doses spanned 1–400 mg and multiple doses 3–100 mg BID (n≈8/cohort), with additional 60 mg QD and 30 mg BID (Japanese) arms. PK was dose-proportional. Endocrine PD was dose-dependent: in single-dose cohorts, HPG hormone changes were similar to placebo (little acute effect), whereas the maximum placebo-corrected suppression of total testosterone and SHBG occurred in the highest multiple-dose regimen (100 mg BID). So repeated dosing at the top of the tested range is where on-target androgenic feedback becomes clearly measurable. No efficacy dose-response (muscle/strength) exists because efficacy was not studied.

How it compares

  • vs Enobosarm (ostarine, GTx-024/MK-2866)
    same: Non-steroidal AR agonist; same HPG/HDL/liver liability pattern
    different: Enobosarm reached Phase 3 with published efficacy (lean mass, some functional endpoints) and documented real-world drug-induced liver injury cases; PF-06260414 has only Phase 1 PK/PD and never had efficacy tested. Both share testosterone suppression, HDL lowering and liver-enzyme signals.
  • vs Ligandrol (LGD-4033)
    same: Both suppress endogenous testosterone and HDL; both flagged for liver-toxicity concern
    different: LGD-4033 has human RCT lean-mass data and marked dose-dependent HDL/testosterone suppression, and is heavily used illicitly; PF-06260414 is far less studied and not widely diverted, with a distinct Pfizer chemotype.
  • vs Testosterone / anabolic-androgenic steroids
    same: Both are AR agonists that shut down endogenous testosterone and can lower HDL
    different: PF-06260414 is non-steroidal, not aromatized to estrogen or reduced to DHT, and intended to spare prostate/skin; steroids act broadly on all AR tissues and add estrogenic/DHT effects. But PF still suppresses the HPG axis like steroids.
  • vs Other pipeline SARMs generally
    same: Shared class pharmacology and the persistent inability to fully divorce anabolism from HPG suppression and lipid/liver effects
    different: PF-06260414 is notable mainly as one of the ~6 SARMs with any human PK/PD data, yet it was quietly discontinued after Phase 1 with no disclosed efficacy program — a common fate for SARM candidates.

History &amp; legal status

Developed by Pfizer as a non-steroidal SARM clinical candidate. First-in-human Phase 1 (NCT02070939) ran Feb 2014–Mar 2015 at Pfizer's New Haven Clinical Research Unit and was published in Clinical Therapeutics in 2016 (Bhattacharya et al.). A companion formulation/food-effect Phase 1 (NCT02393807) was withdrawn without enrolling. No Phase 2 or later trials were registered or published; Pfizer did not advance the compound and there is no approval for any indication in any jurisdiction. It has since appeared mainly in doping-control/analytical-chemistry literature (e.g. equine metabolite characterization, PMID 34714606) reflecting concern about SARM diversion into sport, and in SARM systematic reviews as one of the few clinically characterized members of the class. Like all SARMs, it is not an approved drug or dietary supplement; the U.S. FDA has warned against SARMs in consumer products, and PF-06260414 sold online is unregulated.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Efficacy: does it actually increase muscle mass, strength or physical function in humans? Never tested in any species reported.
  • True tissue selectivity in humans — muscle/bone benefit vs prostate/skin risk — is unproven (only PSA safety monitoring exists).
  • Nature and dose/duration dependence of the ALT elevation: transient adaptive rise vs genuine hepatotoxicity? Injury pattern uncharacterized.
  • Magnitude and reversibility of HPG-axis suppression with longer exposure, and fertility recovery time.
  • Long-term cardiovascular consequences of sustained HDL lowering.
  • Behavior at supraphysiologic 'bodybuilding' doses far above the 100 mg BID ceiling tested.
  • Human metabolism, responsible CYP enzymes, and drug–drug interactions (only in-vitro equine metabolite data exist).
  • Reason for program discontinuation (efficacy, safety margin, strategic, or hepatic signal) is not publicly disclosed.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Educational harm-reduction only — not an endorsement of use, and NOT a protocol; there is no approved or safe way to take this compound. Given the Phase 1 signals, the biologically relevant things a clinician would watch are: (1) Liver — ALT/AST and bilirubin, since an ALT rise was the most common trial adverse event and other oral SARMs have caused clinically significant liver injury; jaundice, dark urine, or unexplained fatigue warrant urgent medical evaluation. (2) Lipids — a fasting lipid panel, expecting HDL to fall. (3) Endocrine axis — total/free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG and estradiol, since suppression of endogenous testosterone is expected and recovery after stopping is uncharacterized. (4) Prostate/hematology — PSA and hematocrit/hemoglobin, both androgen-sensitive and both monitored as trial safety endpoints. Any material obtained outside a clinical trial cannot be assumed to contain PF-06260414 at the labeled identity or purity, which compounds every risk above. The appropriate action for anyone experiencing symptoms is to stop and consult a clinician — not to 'manage' labs while continuing.

Caveats

This is a discontinued pipeline compound with a thin evidence base — treat every anabolic/performance claim as unproven in humans. Efficacy (muscle mass, strength, function) was NEVER tested for PF-06260414 in people; the only human study measured safety, PK and hormones in healthy men over roughly 6 weeks. 'Tissue selectivity' (muscle over prostate) is a design goal supported by rodent assays for the SARM class (including Takeda benzonitrile analogs, not this exact Pfizer molecule), not a demonstrated human property of PF-06260414. Even in that short, well-controlled trial the drug clearly suppressed the body's own testosterone and SHBG, lowered HDL, and its most common adverse event was a rise in the liver enzyme ALT — the same androgenic and hepatic signals that have caused documented liver injury with other oral SARMs sold illicitly. Long-term safety, cardiovascular risk, fertility recovery after HPG suppression, and behavior at supraphysiologic 'bodybuilding' doses are entirely unknown. PF-06260414 is not approved, is not a supplement, and any material sold online under this name is unregulated and unverified for identity/purity.
