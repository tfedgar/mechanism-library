Menotropins

The dual-hormone fertility ancillary: hMG supplies the FSH signal that hCG (LH-only) cannot, driving quantitatively fuller spermatogenesis when restarting the testis.

Human / approvedFDA-approved 13 citations
Class
Purified urinary gonadotropin preparation with combined LH-like and FSH activity (injectable protein/glycoprotein hormone)
Primary target
Two gonadal G-protein-coupled receptors: the LHCGR (LH/hCG receptor) on testicular Leydig cells -> intratesticular testosterone; and the FSHR (FSH receptor) on Sertoli cells -> spermatogenic support
Human status
Established human drug (FDA/EMA-approved gonadotropin, first approval 1975; ChEMBL max_phase 4). For MALE fertility it is used within a well-documented human evidence base for hypogonadotropic hypogonadism: a 2024/2025 meta-analysis of 41 studies / 1,673 men (PMID 39445789), a randomized controlled trial comparing hCG/hMG with pulsatile GnRH (Lin 2019 PMID 31374027), a randomized adolescent study (Liu 2021 / NCT02880280 PMID 33813517), an active Phase 4 hCG-vs-hCG/hMG RCT (NCT03687606), and long-standing mechanistic human studies (Schaison 1993 PMID 8263139; Depenbusch 2002 PMID 12444893; Ley 1985 PMID 3928676). Direct evidence that the FSH component adds quantitative value over hCG alone is solid (Muir 2025). The specific fitness/PED use — restarting the axis after anabolic-steroid-induced suppression ('PCT') — is off-label and largely EXTRAPOLATED from the hypogonadotropic-hypogonadism literature; there is little dedicated randomized trial evidence for the post-steroid scenario specifically.

At a glance

  • Raises intratesticular AND serum testosterone via the LH/hCG (Leydig) arm — the same core effect as hCG
  • Adds the FSH signal hCG lacks: drives Sertoli-cell support and inhibin B/AMH output (the FSH-specific spermatogenic-capacity markers)
  • Induces/increases sperm production more fully than hCG alone (higher sperm output, more men crossing sperm thresholds) — a QUANTITATIVE benefit shown in meta-analysis
  • Increases testicular volume — but note this rises with gonadotropin therapy generally (hCG alone raises it comparably); it is not a clean FSH-only readout
  • Used to restart fertility in hypogonadotropic hypogonadism and (off-label, extrapolated) after androgen-induced HPG suppression
  • Best data are for INDUCTION of spermatogenesis; hCG alone can often MAINTAIN it once primed (though counts drift down without FSH)
  • Effect size is smaller and slower in congenital/never-primed testes than in acquired/post-suppression cases

Mechanism, step by step

7 steps6 human-evidenced 1 preclinical

  1. What hMG actually is (LH + FSH bioactivity in one vial) Established

    Menotropins is a protein product extracted and purified from the urine of postmenopausal women, standardised to contain roughly equal international units of FSH activity and LH activity. In highly purified modern versions (Menopur / HP-hMG), the LH-like bioactivity is contributed mainly by co-purified human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG), which acts on the same receptor as LH. ChEMBL lists it as an approved parenteral protein drug (first approval 1975; FDA description 'Menotropins (FSH;LH)'; USAN 1967). This dual composition is the whole point: one injection delivers both gonadotropin signals. ChEMBL CHEMBL2108427 (Menotropins, max_phase 4, first_approval 1975, USAN 1967, FDA synonym 'MENOTROPINS (FSH;LH)')

  2. LH arm: stimulates Leydig cells to make intratesticular testosterone Established

    The LH/hCG activity in hMG binds LHCGR on Leydig cells, raising intratesticular testosterone to the very high local concentrations (reported at roughly 100-fold above blood levels) that the seminiferous tubules need. This is the same signal hCG provides on its own, and it alone can restore serum testosterone and often initiate some sperm production. In a classic series of hypogonadotropic men, hCG alone normalised serum testosterone in essentially all treated men and produced sperm in a minority. Ley & Leonard 1985, J Clin Endocrinol Metab, PMID 3928676; Schaison 1993 PMID 8263139; intratesticular-T ~100x serum per review PMID 24047626

  3. FSH arm: the piece hCG cannot supply — Sertoli-cell support Evidence-rodent

    The FSH activity in hMG binds FSHR on Sertoli cells, driving the Sertoli-cell program (support of the germ-cell population, GDNF/growth-factor signalling in the spermatogonial-stem-cell niche, and inhibin B output) that helps determine how MANY sperm the testis can make. This FSH signal is exactly what LH-only hCG monotherapy is missing. The mechanistic detail here is drawn from in-vitro (mouse) and animal (avian/rooster) models plus knockout-mouse reviews, so the molecular specifics are non-human; the human-level consequence is captured in steps 4-6. Sertoli mechanism: PMID 40141344 (in vitro, mouse); PMID 41576440 (avian/rooster, in vivo + in vitro); knockout-mouse + human review PMID 28100869

  4. Both signals together drive quantitatively fuller spermatogenesis Evidence-human

    In men with complete gonadotropin deficiency, giving LH activity plus FSH (hMG + hCG) reliably induces spermatogenesis, while FSH plus exogenous testosterone fails — testosterone cannot substitute for the LH/intratesticular-testosterone signal. A 2024/2025 meta-analysis of 41 studies (1,673 men) found combined hCG/FSH produced significantly higher mean sperm output and more men reaching every sperm-concentration threshold than hCG alone — the direct human evidence that adding the FSH component matters quantitatively. Muir 2025 meta-analysis, Clin Endocrinol, PMID 39445789; Schaison 1993 PMID 8263139

  5. Inhibin B/AMH track the FSH signal; testicular volume rises with therapy generally Evidence-human

    FSH drives Sertoli-cell products such as inhibin B and seminal AMH, which rise specifically when the FSH arm is added and correlate with sperm density and testicular volume — so these are the more FSH-specific readouts. Testicular volume also increases during gonadotropin therapy, but honestly it is NOT a clean FSH-only marker: in the meta-analysis, testicular-volume gains were similar whether men got hCG alone or hCG/FSH, and were mainly lower in congenital HH (reflecting how much baseline Sertoli-cell mass there is to recruit) rather than tracking the FSH arm per se. Seminal AMH/inhibin B FSH-marker data (rFSH+hCG) PMID 18343809; testicular-volume equivalence between hCG and hCG/FSH PMID 39445789

  6. Once primed, LH-only can partly maintain — but FSH is needed for full quantity Evidence-human

    After spermatogenesis is induced with hMG+hCG (or GnRH), hCG alone can qualitatively maintain sperm production in most men for extended periods, but sperm counts drift downward over ~12 months and one man became azoospermic — showing FSH is required for quantitatively normal spermatogenesis, not just to switch it on. This is the mechanistic basis for the common 'induce with FSH-containing hMG, maintain with hCG' logic. Depenbusch 2002, Eur J Endocrinol, PMID 12444893

  7. Clinical setting: restarting a suppressed or never-started HPG axis Evidence-human

    hMG is used where the pituitary cannot deliver its own FSH/LH — congenital/idiopathic hypogonadotropic hypogonadism, Kallmann syndrome, hypopituitarism — and by analogy in the fitness world for restarting a testis suppressed by exogenous androgens. Human studies (e.g. hCG/hMG in adolescents, NCT02880280 PMID 33813517; hCG/hMG vs pulsatile GnRH RCT PMID 31374027) confirm the combined regimen raises testosterone, penile/testicular growth and, over 6-24 months, sperm output. Note the anabolic-steroid 'PCT' use is off-label and largely extrapolated from the HH literature, not from dedicated post-steroid trials. Liu 2021, Chin Med J, PMID 33813517 (NCT02880280); Lin 2019 RCT PMID 31374027

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Two distinct class-A GPCRs. The LH/hCG activity binds LHCGR (LH/choriogonadotropin receptor) on Leydig cells; the FSH activity binds FSHR on Sertoli cells. Both are heavily glycosylated dimeric glycoprotein hormones sharing a common alpha-subunit with a hormone-specific beta-subunit; glycosylation (sialylation) governs circulating half-life and bioactivity. In highly-purified menotropins (Menopur / HP-hMG) the LH-receptor agonism is delivered predominantly by co-purified urinary hCG rather than by pituitary LH.

Signalling cascade. Receptor occupancy -> Gs -> adenylyl cyclase -> cAMP -> PKA (FSHR can also recruit beta-arrestin/ERK signalling). In Leydig cells this upregulates steroidogenic machinery (StAR, CYP11A1, CYP17) to raise intratesticular testosterone; in Sertoli cells it drives the support program (androgen-binding protein, GDNF, inhibin B, structural support of differentiating germ cells). Note the Sertoli-cell downstream detail is characterised mainly in animal/in-vitro systems.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Parenteral only — intramuscular or subcutaneous injection (self-administered SC is feasible, e.g. Jones & Darne PMID 8435901). Not orally bioavailable (large glycoprotein).
Absorption / bioavailability
Slow absorption from an injection depot; SC and IM both effective for inducing testicular growth/spermatogenesis in HH. Being a glycoprotein, there is no small-molecule first-pass-metabolism concept.
Half-life
FSH activity has a relatively long circulating half-life (on the order of ~24-36 h for urinary FSH), driven by its high sialic-acid content; the hCG-derived LH activity is even longer-lived than pituitary LH (hCG terminal half-life ~24-36 h). These figures are literature-typical for urinary gonadotropins, are formulation-dependent, and are NOT derived from the male-HH fertility trials.
Metabolism
Cleared as a glycoprotein — hepatic/renal uptake and desialylation-mediated clearance rather than CYP metabolism.
Excretion
Renal and hepatic clearance of the intact/partially-degraded glycoprotein; historically these hormones were RECOVERED from urine, underscoring renal handling.
Notes
Because it is urine-derived and standardized by BIOACTIVITY (IU) rather than by mass, potency per vial is defined functionally. Half-life numbers here are literature-typical/approximate and formulation-dependent, not read off the male-HH trials.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

Dose-response is documented as an IU-of-FSH-activity relationship rather than mg (no dosing schedule is provided here by design). Human data establish: (a) the FSH component adds quantitatively to spermatogenesis — combined hCG/FSH beat hCG alone on mean sperm output and on the fraction of men reaching >0, >1, >5, >10, >20 M/mL thresholds (Muir meta-analysis, 1,673 men, PMID 39445789); (b) response is time-dependent and slow — a median of ~18 months of gonadotropin treatment to the reported sperm concentrations, with sperm often appearing around 6-11 months (Lin RCT: hCG/hMG sperm initiation ~10.9 months; Liu CHH cohort: median ~15 months); (c) baseline biology caps the ceiling — congenital HH yields lower mean sperm output than hypopituitary/mixed cohorts, and smaller basal testes and cryptorchidism history predict slower response.

How it compares

  • vs hCG (LH-only) monotherapy
    same: Both raise intratesticular and serum testosterone via LHCGR; both can be used to restart/support the testis; both grow testicular volume; both are injectable.
    different: hCG supplies ONLY the LH signal — no FSH. hMG adds FSH bioactivity, giving quantitatively fuller spermatogenesis (higher sperm output, more men crossing fertility-relevant thresholds), which matters most for INDUCTION and never-primed testes. Testosterone and testicular-volume responses are similar between the two — the FSH benefit is specifically on sperm quantity. Practical pattern: FSH-containing hMG for induction, hCG often sufficient for maintenance.
  • vs Recombinant FSH (follitropin alfa/beta)
    same: Both deliver the FSH signal to Sertoli cells; both are used with hCG to induce spermatogenesis with broadly comparable efficacy in HH.
    different: rFSH is pure FSH with defined mass/composition and no LH activity (so it is paired with hCG), and avoids urine-derived contaminants/immunogenicity concerns; hMG bundles LH+FSH activity in one vial (LH activity from co-purified hCG in Menopur/HP-hMG) and is standardized by bioactivity, not mass. rFSH is generally costlier.
  • vs Pulsatile GnRH pump
    same: Both aim to restore downstream FSH+LH signalling and induce spermatogenesis in hypogonadotropic men.
    different: The GnRH pump works ONE level upstream — it needs an intact pituitary to secrete the patient's own FSH/LH in physiologic pulses, so it only helps hypothalamic (not pituitary) defects. In head-to-head data it produced sperm faster and at a higher rate (Lin 2019: 53% vs 25%) but requires a wearable pump. hMG/hCG works even when the pituitary itself is the problem.
  • vs SERMs (clomiphene/enclomiphene) and testosterone (TRT)
    same:
    different: SERMs raise the body's OWN LH/FSH by blocking hypothalamic estrogen feedback — useless if the pituitary can't respond, and they do not supply exogenous FSH. TRT provides androgen but SUPPRESSES the axis and impairs fertility (exogenous testosterone cannot replace LH-driven intratesticular testosterone for spermatogenesis, per Schaison 1993). hMG is chosen precisely when the gonadotropin signal — especially the FSH arm — must be delivered directly.

History & legal status

Human menopausal gonadotropin was one of the earliest gonadotropin therapeutics — Pergonal (menotropins) reached approval in 1975 (ChEMBL first_approval 1975; USAN 'menotropins' 1967). It is extracted from the urine of postmenopausal women, historically at industrial scale. It is FDA/EMA-approved (ChEMBL max_phase 4) with the FDA description 'Menotropins (FSH;LH)'; trade names include Menopur, Pergonal, Repronex, Humegon, Merional and Normegon. Its principal LABELLED indication set is female infertility/ovulation induction and, for males, stimulation of spermatogenesis in hypogonadotropic hypogonadism (ChEMBL indication Hypogonadism, MeSH D007006, ref NCT01084265). Over time the field moved toward highly-purified urinary products (Menopur / HP-hMG — LH activity largely from co-purified hCG) and recombinant FSH; hMG-based male-fertility trials remain active (e.g. NCT02880280; the Phase 4 hCG-vs-hCG/hMG RCT NCT03687606).

Key unknowns / open questions

  • How well the hypogonadotropic-hypogonadism data transfer to the specific fitness/PED scenario of restarting an axis suppressed by exogenous androgens — dedicated randomized 'post-steroid' trials are largely absent.
  • Optimal relative contribution and timing of the FSH arm vs the LH/hCG arm for the fastest, fullest sperm recovery in an otherwise-healthy suppressed man.
  • Whether highly-purified urinary hMG (hCG-derived LH activity) and recombinant FSH+hCG differ meaningfully in real-world male-fertility outcomes.
  • Long-term durability of sperm output and whether early testicular volume / inhibin B / seminal AMH reliably predict eventual fertility in the restart setting.
  • Whether prior long-term high-dose anabolic-steroid exposure changes Sertoli-cell responsiveness compared with never-treated hypogonadotropic men.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

What to MONITOR (physician-guided; not a protocol, no doses given): semen analysis for sperm concentration/motility as the definitive efficacy readout (expect months, not weeks); serum total testosterone (to confirm the LH/hCG arm is active) and estradiol (aromatization -> gynecomastia risk); hematocrit/hemoglobin for erythrocytosis from rising testosterone; inhibin B (and where available seminal/serum AMH) as the FSH-specific Sertoli-cell/spermatogenic-capacity markers; testicular volume as a general (non-FSH-specific) sign of testicular response; LH/FSH to confirm the exogenous signal and, in restart contexts, recovery of the endogenous axis; injection sites for local reactions; and clinical estrogenic/androgenic effects (gynecomastia, acne, mood). Negative prognostic flags to recognize early: cryptorchidism history, very small baseline testes, congenital/never-primed HH, and multiple pituitary deficits — these predict a slower/incomplete response.

Caveats

This monograph explains mechanism and evidence and describes what to MONITOR — it is not a protocol and contains no dosing or 'how to run it' guidance. Key honesty points: (1) The headline claim 'FSH does what hCG cannot' is real but nuanced — hCG ALONE initiates and even maintains spermatogenesis in a meaningful fraction of men; the FSH component's proven benefit is QUANTITATIVE (higher sperm output, more men reaching fertility-relevant sperm thresholds), most important for INDUCTION and in never-primed testes. (2) Testicular volume is a weak proxy for 'the FSH arm working' — the Muir 2025 meta-analysis found testicular-volume gains were similar with hCG alone vs hCG/FSH; inhibin B / seminal AMH are the more FSH-specific markers. (3) The Sertoli-cell molecular detail (GDNF, niche) is largely animal/in-vitro (mouse in-vitro, rooster, knockout mice), not human — the human evidence is at the level of sperm output, not molecular pathway. (4) Nearly all high-quality human data are in hypogonadotropic hypogonadism (pituitary can't make gonadotropins), NOT in otherwise-healthy men restarting after androgen use — the PCT application is inference, not direct trial data. (5) 'hMG' is not one fixed molecule: older products are cruder urinary extracts; modern highly-purified products (Menopur / HP-hMG) derive their LH activity mainly from co-purified hCG, so batch/product composition matters. (6) Response is slow (often 6-24 months) and incomplete in congenital HH, cryptorchidism, or very small baseline testes. (7) Product is human-urine-derived (theoretical protein-contaminant/immunogenicity considerations that recombinant FSH avoids). None of this is medical advice; fertility restoration should be physician-supervised with semen analysis.
