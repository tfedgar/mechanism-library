CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin

Two peptides, two switches on the same pituitary cell: one tells it to MAKE growth hormone, the other tells it to RELEASE the stockpile — together they fire a bigger, cleaner GH pulse than either alone.

Human / approved 19 citations
Class
Growth-hormone secretagogue STACK: CJC-1295 is a synthetic GHRH (growth-hormone-releasing hormone) analog; Ipamorelin is a selective GHS-R1a (ghrelin receptor) agonist / GHRP-class secretagogue
Primary target
Anterior-pituitary somatotroph cells: CJC-1295 → GHRH receptor (GHRHR, Gs-coupled); Ipamorelin → growth hormone secretagogue receptor 1a (GHS-R1a, the ghrelin receptor, Gq-coupled)
Human status
HUMAN data exists for the building blocks, NOT for the branded stack. CJC-1295: a real human pharmacology trial (Teichman 2006, JCEM, PMID 16352683) showed dose-dependent, sustained GH and IGF-1 increases in healthy adults, and pulsatility is preserved (Ionescu/Frohman 2006, PMID 17018654). The GHRH-with-ghrelin/GHRP SYNERGY is demonstrated in humans (Hataya 2001 ghrelin+GHRH, PMID 11549707; Arvat 2001, PMID 11238504; Popovic 1994 GHRH+GHRP-6 in acromegaly, PMID 8045963). Ipamorelin's defining selectivity (GH release without cortisol/prolactin spikes) is RODENT-grade (Raun 1998, PMID 9849822) — there is no large modern human trial of ipamorelin for body composition, and clinical development was discontinued. CRITICALLY: there is no controlled human trial of the CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin combination for muscle/fat/performance outcomes — those endpoints rest on mechanism plus community use, not RCTs. Neither peptide is an FDA-approved drug for these uses; both are sold as research chemicals. The widely repeated '3-5x larger pulse' figure is an extrapolation from GHRH+GHRP synergy studies, not a measured number for this pair.

At a glance

  • Amplified, more pulsatile GH secretion vs either peptide alone (two complementary receptor pathways on the same cell)
  • Sustained rise in GH and IGF-1 — measured in humans for CJC-1295 (IGF-1 up substantially; DAC form keeps it elevated for days)
  • Ipamorelin's GH release with minimal cortisol/prolactin/ACTH (selectivity shown in rodents; cleaner than GHRP-2/6/hexarelin)
  • Preserved pulsatile GH physiology rather than a flat continuous elevation, especially with no-DAC CJC-1295 (shown in humans)
  • Downstream GH/IGF-1 actions (lean mass, recovery, lipolysis/metabolism) are biologically expected but NOT quantified for this specific stack in controlled human trials

Mechanism, step by step

  1. CJC-1295 mimics your own GHRH and resists breakdown Established

    CJC-1295 is a tweaked copy of the first 29 amino acids of GHRH — the natural 'make GH' hormone from the hypothalamus. Four amino-acid swaps protect it from the enzyme (DPP-4) that destroys real GHRH in minutes, so it sticks around longer. The 'with-DAC' version adds a chemical hook that latches onto blood albumin, stretching its half-life to days; the 'no-DAC' version (Mod GRF 1-29) lasts ~30 minutes and keeps GH release more pulse-like. PMID 15817669 (Jetté 2005, Endocrinology, rat); PMID 16352683 (Teichman 2006, JCEM, human)

  2. CJC-1295 turns the GHRH receptor's 'cAMP' dial up Established

    It binds the GHRH receptor on somatotrophs, a Gs-protein-coupled receptor. That raises intracellular cAMP, activating PKA, which both drives transcription of the GH gene (making MORE hormone) and primes the secretory granules. Think of it as filling the warehouse and unlocking the loading dock. PMID 15817669 (Jetté 2005, rat); PMID 33776931 (Devesa 2021, review)

  3. Ipamorelin hits a SEPARATE receptor — the ghrelin receptor Established

    Ipamorelin activates GHS-R1a, the same receptor the hunger hormone ghrelin uses. This is a Gq-coupled receptor: it runs the phospholipase-C / IP3 / DAG pathway, releasing calcium inside the cell and triggering immediate dumping of the stored GH granules. So while CJC-1295 fills the warehouse, ipamorelin throws open the doors. PMID 9849822 (Raun 1998, Eur J Endocrinol, rodent); PMID 33776931 (Devesa 2021, review); PMID 22243899 (Yang 2012, ion channels)

  4. Ipamorelin is the 'clean' secretagogue — minimal cortisol/prolactin Evidence-rodent

    Unlike older GHRPs (GHRP-2, GHRP-6, hexarelin), ipamorelin was selected to release GH WITHOUT meaningfully bumping stress hormones cortisol/ACTH or prolactin. In the original rat work it matched GHRP-6 for GH release while producing far less cortisol. This selectivity is the main reason it's the preferred partner in the stack. PMID 9849822 (Raun 1998, rodent — selectivity established in animals; clean human profile is inferred, not from large human trials)

  5. Ipamorelin also eases off the brake (somatostatin) Contested

    GH release is held in check by somatostatin, the body's 'GH brake' from the hypothalamus. Ghrelin-receptor signaling helps blunt somatostatin tone, so the somatotroph is both pushed (GHRH/cAMP) and un-braked at the same time. This is part of why the two pathways combine for more than the sum of their parts. PMID 11549707 (GHRH+ghrelin synergy shown in humans; the somatostatin-tone mechanism itself is rodent/inferred)

  6. Two pathways converging = a synergistic (supra-additive) GH pulse Evidence-human

    Because GHRH (Gs/cAMP) and the ghrelin receptor (Gq/calcium) are different receptor families acting on the SAME cell, co-activating both produces a GH burst larger than adding the two effects separately — shown directly for ghrelin + GHRH in humans. The popular '3-5x bigger pulse' figure is an extrapolation from this GHRH+GHRP synergy literature, not a measured CJC-1295+ipamorelin trial number. PMID 11549707 (Hataya 2001, human, ghrelin+GHRH synergy); PMID 11238504 (Arvat 2001, human, GHS vs GHRH); PMID 8045963 (Popovic 1994, human GHRH+GHRP-6)

  7. The downstream payoff is IGF-1 from the liver Evidence-human

    The amplified GH pulses travel to the liver and other tissues and raise IGF-1, the longer-acting growth/anabolic signal that mediates much of GH's effect on muscle, connective tissue and metabolism. In humans, CJC-1295 raised GH and IGF-1 substantially and the elevation persisted for days with the DAC form. PMID 16352683 (Teichman 2006, JCEM, human); PMID 19386527 (Sackmann-Sala 2009, human GH/IGF-1 axis)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. TWO distinct GPCR targets on the pituitary somatotroph, hit in parallel. (1) CJC-1295 = GHRH-receptor (GHRHR, a Gs-coupled class-B GPCR) agonist; it is a tetrasubstituted hGRF(1-29) analog (D-Ala2, Gln8, Ala15, Leu27) that retains the full agonist pharmacophore of native GHRH and binds GHRHR, confirmed by GH secretion in cultured rat anterior pituitary cells [PMID 15817669]. The 'DAC' (Drug Affinity Complex) version adds a maleimidopropionyl-Lys that covalently bonds to Cys34 of serum albumin in vivo — a half-life trick, not a receptor-affinity change. (2) Ipamorelin = selective full agonist of GHS-R1a (the ghrelin receptor, a Gq/11-coupled GPCR). In primary rat pituitary cells ipamorelin released GH with EC50 ~1.3 nmol/L and Emax ~85% (vs GHRP-6 EC50 ~2.2 nmol/L, 100%); in rats ED50 ~80 nmol/kg, in swine ED50 ~2.3 nmol/kg [PMID 9849822]. CERTAINTY on numbers: Established for ipamorelin in vitro/animal; published human GHRHR/GHS-R1a Kd/Ki for these two specific molecules were NOT located this run — flag as a gap. Ipamorelin's defining feature is SELECTIVITY: at >200x its GH-ED50 it did NOT raise ACTH, cortisol, FSH, LH, PRL or TSH — unlike GHRP-2/GHRP-6 which raise ACTH/cortisol [PMID 9849822].

Signalling cascade. Complementary, supra-additive two-receptor convergence on the somatotroph. CJC-1295 -> GHRHR -> Gs -> adenylyl cyclase -> raised cAMP -> PKA -> CREB phosphorylation -> increased GH gene transcription AND Ca2+ influx -> GH vesicle exocytosis (the 'synthesis + pulse-amplitude' arm; GHRHR signalling is the classic cAMP/PKA somatotroph pathway). Ipamorelin -> GHS-R1a -> Gq/11 -> phospholipase C (PLC) -> IP3 + DAG -> intracellular Ca2+ release + PKC (notably PKCepsilon) -> ERK1/2 phosphorylation -> additional GH exocytosis; GHS-R1a agonism ALSO antagonizes somatostatin tone (the GH 'brake') [ipamorelin PLC/PKC/ERK cascade: PMID 16582936, 20056829; GHS-R1a Gq/11 + constitutive somatotroph activity + heterodimerization: PMID 34931385]. Because one arm raises cAMP/PKA while the other raises PLC/Ca2+/PKC and lifts somatostatin inhibition, the combined GH pulse is larger than either alone (GHRH+GHRP synergy is a long-established endocrine finding). Downstream, the amplified GH pulse drives hepatic IGF-1 production via JAK2/STAT5 (the systemic anabolic mediator). CERTAINTY: receptor identity and pathway arms Established; the specific 'synergy magnitude' for THIS exact pairing in humans is Speculative/extrapolated (no head-to-head human combo RCT found).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Subcutaneous injection for both in research/community use (CJC-1295 was also given SC in human trials; ipamorelin was given IV in its only human RCT). Neither is oral — both are peptides degraded in the gut.
Absorption / bioavailability
SC absorption assumed near-complete for small peptides but specific %F not published for either molecule (gap). CJC-1295-DAC's albumin binding makes it a circulating depot.
Half-life
CJC-1295 WITH DAC: ~5.8-8.1 days in healthy humans (single SC dose), driving GH elevation 2-10x for >6 days and IGF-1 1.5-3x for 9-11 days [PMID 16352683]; rat plasma persistence >72 h via albumin conjugation [PMID 15817669]. CJC-1295 WITHOUT DAC (Modified GRF 1-29): ~30 min (commonly cited; the DAC is what creates the multi-day half-life). Ipamorelin: short, ~2 h (pentapeptide, rapid clearance) — this is why community 'no-DAC + ipamorelin' protocols aim for a brief pulse mimicking physiology. CERTAINTY: CJC-1295-DAC human half-life Established [PMID 16352683]; no-DAC and ipamorelin half-lives are Evidence-rodent/community-cited, not from a located human PK paper.
Metabolism
Both are peptides cleared by ubiquitous plasma/tissue peptidases (not CYP450). Native GHRH is rapidly inactivated by dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-4) at the N-terminus; CJC-1295's D-Ala2 substitution confers DPP-4 resistance [PMID 15817669]. Ipamorelin's non-natural residues (Aib, D-2-Nal, D-Phe) resist standard proteases. No hepatic enzyme pathway implicated.
Excretion
Peptide fragments/amino acids recycled or renally cleared; intact-drug renal excretion is the basis of urine anti-doping detection assays (LC-HRMS/MS) [PMID 34665524, 35298973]. Quantitative excretion balance not published.
Notes
Key PK design point: the DAC is an albumin-tethering prodrug-like modification (covalent Cys34 bond), NOT an ester. Albumin tethering is also why CJC-1295-DAC produces a SUSTAINED 'GH bleed' (raised trough GH) rather than discrete pulses — though pulsatility is partly preserved [PMID 17018654]. BBB: peripheral pituitary action dominates; ghrelin-receptor agonism can have central (arcuate/appetite) effects but ipamorelin is comparatively non-orexigenic vs GHRP-6.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

HUMAN dose-response is well-characterized for CJC-1295(-DAC) MONOTHERAPY but essentially ABSENT for the CJC-1295+ipamorelin COMBINATION and for ipamorelin chronic dosing. CJC-1295-DAC, healthy adults (SC, single + multidose, n=stepwise ascending): dose-dependent GH rise 2-10x and IGF-1 rise 1.5-3x; doses of 30 and 60 ug/kg were the best-tolerated effective range, with a cumulative IGF-1 effect on repeat dosing (IGF-1 above baseline up to 28 days) [PMID 16352683]. GH pulsatility study: single 60 or 90 ug/kg raised mean GH ~46% and IGF-1 ~45%, mainly by lifting trough GH 7.5x; NO significant difference between 60 and 90 ug/kg (suggesting a plateau) [PMID 17018654]. Ipamorelin HUMAN: only dose studied in a trial was 0.03 mg/kg IV twice daily (postoperative ileus) — that study measured gut motility, not a GH dose-response curve, and was negative on its endpoint [PMID 25331030]. ANIMAL: ipamorelin GH ED50 ~80 nmol/kg (rat), ~2.3 nmol/kg (swine) [PMID 9849822]; CJC-1295 once-daily normalized growth in GHRH-knockout mice, with less effect at 48-72 h spacing [PMID 16822960]. BOTTOM LINE: no published human dose-response for the stack; community 'standard' doses are extrapolated, not trial-derived. NEVER infer a protocol from these research doses.

How it compares

  • vs Tesamorelin (Egrifta)
    same: Also a synthetic GHRH analog acting on the same pituitary GHRHR -> raises GH/IGF-1; shares the GHRH-class AE cluster (arthralgia, myalgia, paresthesia, edema, injection-site reactions).
    different: Tesamorelin is FDA-APPROVED (HIV lipodystrophy) with the strongest RCT/meta-analytic human evidence base of any GHRH analog [PMID 41545261]; CJC-1295 has only small early-phase human data and no approval. Tesamorelin is shorter-acting (daily SC) vs CJC-1295-DAC's multi-day albumin-tethered half-life.
  • vs GHRP-6 / GHRP-2 (older ghrelin-receptor agonists)
    same: Same GHS-R1a target and Gq/PLC/PKC cascade as ipamorelin; comparable GH-releasing potency/efficacy (ipamorelin was derived from the GHRP-1 series) [PMID 9849822].
    different: GHRP-6 and GHRP-2 also raise ACTH/cortisol (and GHRP-6 strongly stimulates appetite/prolactin); ipamorelin is SELECTIVE — no significant ACTH/cortisol/PRL even at >200x GH-ED50 [PMID 9849822]. That cleaner profile is why ipamorelin is the preferred modern GHS-R1a partner.
  • vs MK-677 (ibutamoren)
    same: Also a GHS-R1a agonist raising GH/IGF-1 via the same PLC/PKC/ERK pathway [corpus MoA, PMID 16582936]; overlaps ipamorelin's mechanism.
    different: MK-677 is an ORALLY active non-peptide with a long half-life (once-daily oral), giving sustained IGF-1 elevation — vs the injectable, short-acting ipamorelin pulse. MK-677 has more/larger human trials (elderly, bone turnover, sleep, an Alzheimer's RCT) [PMID 10404019, 9349662, 19015485], and is more associated with appetite increase, edema and insulin resistance from its sustained exposure.
  • vs Exogenous recombinant GH (somatropin)
    same: End-effector is the same (raised GH -> IGF-1 -> anabolic/lipolytic effects); shares acromegaly-spectrum AEs (edema, carpal tunnel, glucose).
    different: Secretagogues act UPSTREAM (need an intact pituitary) and preserve some physiologic pulsatility and negative feedback, theoretically capping peak exposure; rhGH overrides feedback entirely and gives direct dose control. rhGH is approved/regulated; the peptide stack is not.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Cardarine compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Same collision as with MK-677: CJC-1295/Ipamorelin raise endogenous GH (and IGF-1), and GH excess is shown in human studies to induce insulin resistance — increased FFA/lipid oxidation crowds out glucose disposal. Cardarine's PPARδ-driven glucose→fat fuel switch pushes muscle in the identical direction, so co-use can compound reduced glucose tolerance / higher fasting glucose. The fat-oxidation effects are otherwise aligned (both favor lipid fuel), which is why the shared liability is glycemic, not opposition. GH-induced insulin resistance in healthy people: CJC-1295+Ipamorelin monograph (PMID 11914108) and MK-677 monograph glucose side-effect data. Cardarine fuel switch: monograph; Risérus 2008 PMID 18024853. Watch: Fasting glucose and insulin, HbA1c; look for insulin-resistance signs. GH peptides can also cause fluid retention and carpal-tunnel-type symptoms — separate from the shared glucose mechanism.
  • Hexarelin compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    This blend contains ipamorelin, which is ALSO a GHS-R1a agonist — so pairing it with hexarelin is redundant on GHS-R1a and adds desensitization risk at that receptor, while the CJC-1295 (GHRH-R) component synergizes with hexarelin for a larger GH pulse (GHRP-plus-GHRH synergy). The compounding concern is amplified GH excess. Note ipamorelin is specifically a 'clean' secretagogue with minimal cortisol/prolactin effect, so the HPA/prolactin load in this stack comes from hexarelin's non-selectivity, not from ipamorelin. Hexarelin monograph (GHS-R1a target; GHRH synergy; ACTH/cortisol/prolactin rise, PMID 15177700/11238504); CJC-1295+ipamorelin monograph (CJC GHRH-R, ipamorelin GHS-R1a and 'clean' with minimal cortisol/prolactin) Watch: IGF-1 (watch for a waning response suggesting desensitization); GH-excess signs (edema, arthralgia); fasting glucose; and, because of hexarelin's own non-selectivity, prolactin and a morning cortisol.
  • BPC-157 potentiates ↑ Evidence-rodent
    Mechanistic complementarity on the GH axis. BPC-157 up-regulates the growth-hormone RECEPTOR (GHR) on tendon/connective-tissue fibroblasts, making those repair cells more responsive to GH (one of the most abundantly induced genes in rat Achilles-tendon fibroblasts). CJC-1295 (GHRH-receptor analog) + Ipamorelin (GHS-R1a agonist) raise circulating GH and therefore IGF-1, supplying more LIGAND for the receptor BPC-157 has sensitized. Since the GH/IGF-1 axis drives collagen synthesis in connective tissue, 'more receptor' (BPC-157) plus 'more hormone' (secretagogues) is a plausible potentiation of connective-tissue repair rather than two redundant effects. BPC-157 GHR up-regulation PMID 25415472; CJC-1295+Ipamorelin monograph (GHRHR + GHS-R1a raise GH/IGF-1) Watch: Watch fasting glucose and HbA1c (GH excess drives insulin resistance), and watch for fluid retention, carpal-tunnel symptoms, or joint aching from elevated GH/IGF-1. Given the shared pro-growth signaling, keep cancer screening current. The GHR-sensitization data are rat-tendon in-vitro only; human magnitude is unproven.
  • GHK-Cu potentiates ↑ Evidence-human
    This GHRH-R (CJC-1295) + GHS-R1a (Ipamorelin) combination raises GH and IGF-1. Because the GH/IGF-1 axis stimulates connective-tissue collagen synthesis (human GH-receptor blockade lowers tendon/muscle collagen synthesis; IGF-1 directly raises tendon collagen in vitro), it acts on the same collagen/fibroblast output that GHK-Cu drives locally via copper-dependent matrix signaling - the two converge to potentiate matrix remodeling. Mechanistic convergence, not a common receptor. PMID 21498100 (GH/IGF-1 axis drives connective-tissue collagen synthesis in humans); PMID 25281191 (IGF-1 raises tendon collagen synthesis); PMID 18644225 (GHK-Cu collagen/matrix stimulation) Watch: GH secretagogues can raise fasting glucose and cause fluid retention/carpal-tunnel-type symptoms - track glucose and swelling. No added interaction-specific lab from GHK-Cu beyond its copper caution with systemic use.
  • GHRP-2 potentiates ↑ Established (GHRH+GHRP synergy in humans); the specific triple-combination outcome is unstudied
    GHRP-2 activates the ghrelin receptor (GHS-R1a, Gq-coupled) on the somatotroph, while the CJC-1295 arm of this stack activates the SEPARATE GHRH receptor (GHRHR, Gs-coupled) on the same cell. Pulling two different levers - Gq/PLC/Ca2+ (GHRP-2) plus Gs/cAMP (GHRH) - while GHRP-2 also blunts somatostatin's brake, produces a supra-additive GH pulse larger than either alone. This is the GHRH+GHRP synergy the GHRP-2 monograph documents (step 4). Caveat: the Ipamorelin component is itself a GHS-R1a agonist, so it is REDUNDANT with GHRP-2 at that receptor; the genuine synergy is the GHRH (CJC-1295) arm. GHRP-2 monograph step 4 (PMID 9285939 Arvat 1997; PMID 9186261 Ghigo 1997); CJC-1295+Ipamorelin monograph (complementary GHRHR/GHS-R1a pathways) Watch: IGF-1 as the on-target readout of the amplified GH signal; fasting glucose/HbA1c since a larger GH pulse worsens insulin sensitivity; fluid retention, joint aches and carpal-tunnel-type numbness as GH-excess signals.
  • GHRP-6 potentiates ↑ Evidence-human
    GHRP-6 acts mainly at the hypothalamus, needs endogenous GHRH to fire a large GH pulse, and functionally opposes somatostatin tone. A GHRH-receptor agonist (CJC-1295) supplies that permissive GHRH signal through a separate Gs/cAMP receptor, while GHRP-6 and ipamorelin both drive the Gq/PLC/Ca2+ GHS-R1a pathway. Co-activating the two distinct receptor families on the same somatotroph produces a GH pulse larger than the sum of either alone (the canonical GHRH+GHRP synergy). Note the ipamorelin half of this product is itself a GHS-R1a agonist, so it is partly redundant with GHRP-6 at that receptor. Monograph steps 3-4 + cascade (dual hypothalamic+pituitary GHRH synergy, PMID 9893708); GHRH+GHRP supra-additive GH PMID 7745018; GHRP-6 requires endogenous GHRH PMID 7883854, 9543138 Watch: Serum IGF-1 as the integrated readout of stacked GH stimulation (watch for drift toward supraphysiologic/acromegaly-like levels), fasting glucose/HbA1c, and signs of GH excess (edema, joint pain, carpal-tunnel paresthesias).
  • T3 potentiates ↑ Evidence-human
    GH increases peripheral deiodinase activity, accelerating conversion of T4 to active T3, so raising GH via this stack already nudges thyroid signaling up. Adding exogenous T3 on top potentiates the calorigenic/metabolic-rate effect (GH + T3 raise resting energy expenditure supra-additively) but also compounds catabolic and thyroid-axis disruption: exogenous T3 suppresses TSH and endogenous T4, and the combination can unmask or worsen thyroid dysregulation. PMID 8636344 (GH-induced peripheral T4->T3 conversion / GH+T3 energy-expenditure synergy in humans); T3 monograph (deiodinase T4->T3 activation; T3 suppresses TSH/thyroid axis) Watch: TSH, free T4 and free T3 (watch for suppressed TSH/low T4 from added T3), heart rate, and signs of hyperthyroidism (tremor, palpitations, heat intolerance).
  • Metformin counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    The GH/IGF-1 elevation from this stack induces post-receptor insulin resistance and raises hepatic glucose output. Metformin works in the opposite direction, suppressing hepatic gluconeogenesis (partly AMPK-dependent) and improving insulin sensitivity, so it directly counteracts the diabetogenic side effect of GH secretagogues at the level of glucose metabolism. Metformin monograph (Complex-I/AMPK, suppressed gluconeogenesis, insulin sensitizer); CJC-1295+Ipa monograph side_effect 'Insulin resistance' (GH counter-regulatory) Watch: Fasting glucose, HbA1c and fasting insulin to gauge whether the GH-driven insulin resistance is being offset; this is a harm-reduction consideration, not a dosing recommendation.
  • Semaglutide counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    GLP-1 receptor agonism lowers blood glucose (glucose-dependent insulin secretion, glucagon suppression, slowed gastric emptying) and improves insulin sensitivity largely via weight loss. This opposes the GH-induced insulin resistance and glucose rise from the secretagogue stack, so a GLP-1 agonist can blunt the diabetogenic side effect. The counteraction is on glucose homeostasis, not on the GH/IGF-1 axis itself, so GH's other effects are unchanged. Semaglutide monograph (GLP-1R agonist lowers glucose, low hypoglycemia risk, weight loss); CJC-1295+Ipa monograph side_effect 'Insulin resistance' Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c (to see the GH glucose effect being offset); because both alter appetite/GI, watch overall nutrition and for GI intolerance.
  • 5-Amino-1MQ opposes Evidence-rodent
    CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin raise GH and IGF-1 through the GHRH-R + GHS-R1a axis. Sustained GH elevation antagonizes insulin action at liver and muscle, tending toward insulin resistance and higher fasting glucose (the same class effect established for GH secretagogues like MK-677). 5-Amino-1MQ does not raise GH; it is a separate metabolic lever (NAD+/SIRT1 energy expenditure and fat oxidation) whose proposed insulin-sensitizing direction runs opposite to the GH-driven glucose burden, so on the glucose/insulin axis it partially opposes/offsets the secretagogue stack, milder in kind than but analogous to the MK-677 edge. As with MK-677, the offsetting is theoretical and rodent-grounded on the 5-Amino side (Nnmt-KO did not improve glucose tolerance), so it should not be assumed to neutralize the GH-induced glucose handling burden. On body composition (GH-driven lean mass + NNMT-driven fat loss) the two are complementary. GH secretagogue -> insulin resistance is Established for the class (analogous to MK-677 human data, PMID 9467542); 5-Amino-1MQ metabolic direction rodent (PMID 24717514), with Nnmt-KO glucose-tolerance null (PMID 30552109). Net partial-opposition on glucose inferred. Watch: Fasting glucose/insulin and HbA1c for GH-driven insulin resistance; IGF-1 if tracking the GH arm. No human data on the combination.
  • Insulin opposes Evidence-human
    Growth hormone is a direct counter-regulatory ('diabetogenic') hormone: the sustained GH/IGF-1 rise this stack produces drives lipolysis, hepatic gluconeogenesis and post-receptor insulin resistance in muscle and liver, pushing glucose UP. Exogenous insulin pushes glucose DOWN. The two forces oppose each other at the level of glucose homeostasis, so GH secretagogue exposure raises the insulin requirement while making blood-sugar control less predictable, and any over-correction of insulin risks hypoglycemia. This is a GH-class effect, not specific to the peptides. PMID 16352683 (CJC-1295-DAC raises GH/IGF-1, monograph side_effect 'Insulin resistance'); GH-class insulin resistance is well established in humans Watch: Fasting glucose, fasting insulin and HbA1c; watch for rising glucose or worsening insulin resistance with sustained (DAC) exposure, and for signs of hypoglycemia if insulin is present (shakiness, sweating, confusion).
  • MOTS-c opposes Contested
    Same GH-axis opposition as the MK-677 edge, but as a class inference: CJC-1295 (GHRH-R) plus ipamorelin (GHS-R1a) raise GH and IGF-1, and elevated GH characteristically induces peripheral insulin resistance and can raise fasting glucose (a human-documented GH class effect). That GH-driven insulin resistance would counteract MOTS-c's AMPK-mediated improvement in insulin sensitivity/muscle glucose uptake. Glucose effects have NOT been quantified for this specific peptide stack, so the opposition is extrapolated from GH physiology, not measured for CJC-1295/ipamorelin. PMID 11914108 (GH induces insulin resistance/raises glucose in healthy people - class mechanism); PMID 18981485 (GH-secretagogue-induced insulin resistance/glucose rise); PMID 25738459 (MOTS-c insulin sensitization, rodent) Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c for a GH-driven upward drift that would oppose any MOTS-c insulin-sensitizing effect; note morning glucose creep.
  • Sermorelin shares target Established
    Sermorelin is GHRH(1-29) acting on the SAME GHRH receptor as CJC-1295 (both are GHRH(1-29) analogs on the Gs/cAMP/PKA somatotroph pathway). Adding it to the CJC arm is redundant: same receptor, same second messenger, except Sermorelin is rapidly degraded by DPP-4 and very short-acting whereas CJC-1295 is DPP-4-resistant (and, with DAC, multi-day). They occupy one receptor rather than recruiting a second, so the combination adds no mechanism beyond what CJC-1295 already provides on that arm. Sermorelin monograph primary_target (pituitary GHRH-R agonist, GHRH(1-29)-NH2); CJC-1295 monograph step 1 (DPP-4-resistant GHRH(1-29) analog on GHRHR) Watch: IGF-1 (over-stimulation of the GHRH arm) and the usual GH-excess signs (edema, arthralgia); no new monitoring beyond the GHRH class.
  • Tesamorelin shares target Evidence-human
    Tesamorelin is a stabilized GHRH analog acting on the SAME pituitary GHRH receptor (Gs-coupled) as CJC-1295. Stacking it with CJC-1295 is redundant on the GHRH/Gs-cAMP arm and additively raises GH/IGF-1 through one shared receptor, amplifying the entire GHRH-class adverse cluster (arthralgia, myalgia, paresthesia/carpal tunnel, edema, injection-site reactions) rather than adding a complementary pathway the way Ipamorelin does. Tesamorelin monograph primary_target (GHRHR Gs-coupled agonist); PMID 41545261 (tesamorelin GHRH-analog class RCT/meta-analysis AE profile); CJC/Ipa comparison 'vs Tesamorelin' Watch: IGF-1 and the GHRH-class AE cluster (joint/muscle aches, paresthesia, peripheral edema, injection-site reactions); fasting glucose.
  • AOD-9604 redundant Evidence-rodent
    AOD-9604 IS the isolated C-terminal lipolytic domain of growth hormone (hGH 177-191 + N-terminal Tyr). CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin raise endogenous GH, and GH's own fat-burning action is concentrated in that exact fragment (HSL activation, acetyl-CoA-carboxylase inhibition, fat oxidation). So AOD-9604's fat-loss mechanism is a redundant SUBSET of what a GH pulse already delivers — the same lipolytic signal reached two ways. The non-redundant difference: the secretagogue combo ALSO raises IGF-1 and drives GH's anabolic/recovery and (at higher/chronic doses) insulin-resistance effects, none of which AOD-9604 produces (it does not bind GHR, does not raise IGF-1). AOD-9604 monograph (hGH 177-191 fragment; no GHR binding, no IGF-1 rise; PMID 11673763); CJC-1295+Ipamorelin monograph (GH/IGF-1 rise via GHRHR + GHS-R1a) Watch: Fasting glucose / HbA1c and IGF-1 (driven by the secretagogue, not AOD); note that adding AOD provides little mechanistic addition to GH-driven lipolysis while the GH arm carries the water-retention, carpal-tunnel and glucose signals to watch.
  • Cabergoline redundant Evidence-rodent
    A deliberately NON-interacting contrast. The ipamorelin half of this combo is the 'clean' GHS-R1a agonist selected specifically because it raises GH with negligible prolactin (and minimal cortisol/ACTH), unlike GHRP-2/GHRP-6/hexarelin, which do raise prolactin in humans. Because CJC-1295+ipamorelin does not meaningfully elevate prolactin, cabergoline's prolactin-lowering action has essentially no target to act on here — adding cabergoline would be redundant and only expose the user to cabergoline's own dopaminergic/ergot risks with no offsetting benefit. Note ipamorelin's selectivity data are animal-derived (swine/rodent), so the 'negligible prolactin' claim is rodent-grade. cjc-1295-ipamorelin monograph step 1/side_effect (ipamorelin no ACTH/cortisol/PRL rise even at >200x GH-ED50, rodent/swine, Raun PMID 9849822); GHRP-2/6/hexarelin monographs (prolactin rise in humans) Watch: Serum prolactin — expected to stay near baseline on ipamorelin-based secretagogues. If prolactin is normal, there is no rationale for cabergoline; adding it risks over-suppressing prolactin and incurring dopaminergic/valve liability for nothing.
  • MK-677 redundant Established
    MK-677 is an orally-active non-peptide agonist of the SAME receptor Ipamorelin hits, GHS-R1a (the ghrelin receptor), signaling through the same Gq/PLC/PKC/Ca2+ cascade. Adding it to the CJC/Ipamorelin stack is largely redundant on the ghrelin-receptor arm rather than additive on a new pathway, and because MK-677 gives a long, sustained (once-daily oral) IGF-1 elevation, combining it compounds the GH-class liabilities (greater cumulative IGF-1, more fluid retention, a bigger hit to insulin sensitivity) with no new mechanism recruited. MK-677 monograph primary_target (GHS-R1a full agonist); PMID 16582936 (GHS-R1a PLC/PKC/ERK cascade); CJC-1295+Ipamorelin monograph comparison 'vs MK-677' Watch: IGF-1 (cumulative over-exposure above age-adjusted range), fasting glucose/HbA1c (MK-677 sustained exposure is notably insulin-desensitizing), and signs of edema/carpal-tunnel from additive GH excess.

History & legal status

"CJC-1295 originated ~2004-2005 at ConjuChem Inc. (Montreal) as an albumin-bioconjugating long-acting hGRF(1-29) analog ('DAC:GRF') [PMID 15817669]; it entered early human trials (PK/PD/safety) ~2005-2006 [PMID 16352683, 17018654] but was never FDA-approved and clinical development was discontinued. Ipamorelin was developed in the late 1990s at Novo Nordisk as 'the first selective GH secretagogue' [PMID 9849822]; later taken into a Phase 2 postoperative-ileus program (Helsinn/'Ipamorelin 201 Study Group', NCT00672074) that was NEGATIVE on its primary endpoint [PMID 25331030] — neither compound reached approval for any indication. REGULATORY STATUS (2026): Neither CJC-1295 nor ipamorelin is an FDA-approved drug; both are sold only as 'research chemicals'/grey-market peptides and compounded products. Both are PROHIBITED in sport at all times by WADA (class S2, peptide hormones/GH secretagogues), driving an extensive anti-doping detection literature (LC-HRMS/MS in plasma/urine) [PMID 34665524, 30489688, 35298973]. The FDA has flagged peptide GH secretagogues among compounds removed from compounding eligibility. By contrast the comparator GHRH analog tesamorelin IS FDA-approved (Egrifta)."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No human RCT of the CJC-1295 + ipamorelin COMBINATION exists — the claimed synergy and its magnitude in humans are extrapolated from general GHRH+GHRP physiology, not measured for this pairing.
  • No published long-term (>months) human safety data for either compound: chronic effects on glucose homeostasis, IGF-1-related neoplasia risk, and pituitary axis desensitization are unknown.
  • Human receptor-binding constants (Kd/Ki at GHRHR for CJC-1295 and GHS-R1a for ipamorelin) and human SC bioavailability/half-life for no-DAC CJC-1295 and ipamorelin were not located in peer-reviewed sources this run — these specific PK numbers are a genuine gap.
  • Optimal/safe dosing in healthy non-deficient adults is undefined; all human dose data are from small ascending-dose monotherapy or a single IV ileus trial, none aimed at body-composition outcomes.
  • Whether preserved pulsatility (no-DAC) vs sustained 'GH-bleed' (DAC) meaningfully changes the long-term risk/benefit in humans is untested.
  • Product-quality/identity risk: grey-market peptides have documented mislabeling and impurity issues (the CJC-1295 doping/forensic literature arose partly from identifying unknown preparations [PMID 21204297]) — real-world exposure may differ from the labeled compound.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only (NOT a protocol, NO dosing). Baseline + periodic: (1) IGF-1 — the key pharmacodynamic and safety readout; sustained elevation above the age-adjusted reference range is the signal of over-exposure / acromegaly-spectrum risk. (2) Fasting glucose, fasting insulin and HbA1c — GH is diabetogenic; watch for rising glucose/insulin resistance, especially with sustained-release DAC use. (3) Signs of fluid retention / soft-tissue overgrowth: new peripheral edema, hand/wrist numbness or tingling (carpal tunnel), new joint aches (arthralgia/myalgia), facial/extremity puffiness — classic GH-excess flags [PMID 41545261, 37328323]. (4) Injection-site inspection for erythema, nodules, persistent reactions. (5) Because ipamorelin is GH-selective, cortisol/prolactin elevation is NOT expected from it specifically [PMID 9849822] — but a prolactin/cortisol check is reasonable if stacking with non-selective GHRPs. (6) General: blood pressure and, given the theoretical IGF-1/mitogenic concern, age-appropriate cancer screening and a careful personal/family oncologic history before use. Any persistent IGF-1 elevation, new glucose dysregulation, or acromegaly-like symptom warrants stopping and clinician review. These compounds are unapproved and unmonitored in the grey market — product identity/purity itself is a safety variable."

Caveats

SOURCING NOTE (from the literature searcher): The peer-reviewed CJC-1295 literature is dominated by anti-doping DETECTION-method papers, not efficacy/safety trials — real human pharmacology evidence narrows to a handful of studies (chiefly Teichman 2006, Ionescu 2006, Sackmann-Sala 2009). Much online stack content (medsbase, peptidewiki, thepeptidetoolkit) is COMMERCIAL/low-credibility and was used only to map claims, never as evidence. Direction/target are solid; MAGNITUDE for the combination and for body-composition endpoints is unproven in humans. Direction of GH-pulse change is established; the specific multiplier is not. Safety: chronic supraphysiologic GH/IGF-1 elevation carries theoretical risks (insulin resistance, edema, joint pain, and the long-standing IGF-1/neoplasia concern) that are not characterized for this stack. Old CJC-1295-with-DAC commercial supply was implicated in a non-fatal/contamination concern historically; product purity in the research-chemical market is unverified.
