Eloralintide

Lilly's selective, long-acting amylin-receptor agonist posting up to ~20% weight loss as a once-weekly monotherapy in Phase 2 — a landmark for the amylin class, but the pivotal Phase 3 data are not yet in.

Limited human data 9 citations
Class
Selective amylin (AMY1R-preferring) receptor agonist; long-acting analog peptide, once-weekly subcutaneous
Primary target
Amylin receptors — heterodimers of the calcitonin receptor (CTR) plus receptor-activity-modifying proteins (RAMP1/2/3 → AMY1R/AMY2R/AMY3R)
Human status
PIPELINE / investigational — not approved by FDA, EMA, or any regulator; not available by prescription. Human evidence to date: (1) Phase 1 single-ascending-dose in healthy volunteers (part of NCT05295940; PMID 41109426), (2) Phase 1 12-week multiple-ascending-dose in obesity/overweight (part of NCT05295940 program; PMID 41559929), showing −2.6% to −11.3% weight loss with minimal GI events, and (3) a pivotal 48-week Phase 2 (NCT06230523, PMID 41207310, Lancet, N=263) showing up to −20% weight loss. A large Phase 3 ENLIGHTEN program launched late 2025/early 2026 — obesity without T2D (ENLIGHTEN-1, NCT07321886, N≈1980), with T2D (ENLIGHTEN-2, NCT07282600), obstructive sleep apnea (ENLIGHTEN-3, NCT07369011), osteoarthritis knee pain (ENLIGHTEN-4, NCT07353931), and add-on to a weekly incretin (ENLIGHTEN-6, NCT07392190) — with primary completions projected 2028. Bottom line: the efficacy signal is genuinely striking and best-in-amylin-class as monotherapy, but it rests on one Phase 2; long-term efficacy, durability, cardiovascular/organ outcomes, and real-world tolerability are all UNPROVEN pending Phase 3.

At a glance

  • Dose-dependent body-weight reduction: mean −9% (1 mg) up to −20% (9 mg and 6-9 mg escalation) vs −0.4% placebo at 48 weeks (Phase 2, N=263)
  • Reduced appetite / earlier satiation and lower food intake (the core mechanism)
  • Weight lost is preferentially fat mass in preclinical (DIO-rat) models; human body-composition data not yet reported
  • Once-weekly subcutaneous dosing (long half-life; PK stated to support weekly injection)
  • Gastrointestinal side effects are dose-dependent: low at Phase 1 doses, but nausea reached ~64% at the 6 mg fixed dose and 33-54% at higher-dose arms in Phase 2
  • Positioned as monotherapy AND as an add-on/combination partner to incretins (tirzepatide) — combination efficacy data still unreported
  • Potentially gentler GI/aversion profile than non-selective amylin agonists (rodent-supported; human head-to-head comparison unproven)

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Mimics the gut/pancreas satiety hormone amylin Established

    Native amylin is a 37-amino-acid peptide co-secreted with insulin from pancreatic beta-cells at meals; its main job is to signal fullness (satiation), slow the stomach, and blunt post-meal glucagon. Eloralintide is an engineered, non-aggregating amylin analog designed to do this same job but with a long half-life allowing once-weekly injection. Native amylin itself is unusable as a drug because it aggregates (amyloid) and is cleared within minutes. PMID 26071095 (Hay 2015, Pharmacol Rev); PMID 41747885 (Bailey 2026, Peptides)

  2. Binds amylin receptors with a bias toward human AMY1R over the calcitonin receptor Evidence-in vitro

    Amylin receptors are built from the calcitonin receptor (CTR) plus a RAMP accessory protein, giving three subtypes (AMY1R, AMY2R, AMY3R). In Lilly's own cell assays eloralintide preferentially activated the human AMY1R — about 12-fold more potently than the plain calcitonin receptor and ~11-fold more than AMY3R. This is the marketing thesis: older amylin drugs (cagrilintide, pramlintide) hit CTR/AMY3R more broadly, and Lilly's bet is that a cleaner AMY1R signal keeps the appetite benefit while trimming aversive side effects. Caveat: this selectivity is a human-receptor property; in rat cells both AMY1R and AMY3R were activated more potently than CTR. PMID 41109426 (Briere 2025, Mol Metab)

  3. Acts on hindbrain appetite circuits (area postrema / NTS) to drive satiation Evidence-rodent

    Amylin and its analogs act mainly in the brain — at circumventricular sites in the hindbrain (the area postrema and nucleus tractus solitarius) and downstream at the parabrachial nucleus and hypothalamus. Calcitonin-receptor-expressing (CALCR) neurons there register the signal and suppress food intake. This is where 'I feel full sooner and eat less' comes from. Nearly all of this circuit mapping is rodent/non-human-primate work; the human read-out is indirect (reduced appetite and food intake). PMID 39426704 (Hankir 2025, Biochimie); PMID 26071095 (Hay 2015, Pharmacol Rev)

  4. Reduces food intake and slows gastric emptying → energy deficit → fat-mass loss Evidence-rodent

    Downstream of the brainstem signal, amylin agonists lower calorie intake and modestly slow gastric emptying, producing a sustained energy deficit. In diet-induced obese rats eloralintide dose-dependently cut food intake and body weight, and the weight lost was primarily fat mass. Gastric slowing is generally thought to be milder/less central to amylin's effect than with GLP-1 drugs — a proposed (not proven) reason for a gentler nausea profile. PMID 41109426 (Briere 2025, Mol Metab); PMID 26071095 (Hay 2015, Pharmacol Rev)

  5. AMY1R-selectivity appears to lower the aversive/nausea signal (preclinically) Evidence-rodent

    In lean rats, eloralintide caused significantly less conditioned taste avoidance (a rodent proxy for malaise/aversion) than cagrilintide, a non-selective amylin agonist. The interpretation is that dialing down calcitonin-receptor and AMY3R activation reduces the 'this makes me feel sick' component while keeping the appetite effect. This is a plausible, testable hypothesis — but 'less taste avoidance in rats' is not the same as 'less nausea in people,' and the human tolerability picture is clearly dose-dependent (see side effects). PMID 41109426 (Briere 2025, Mol Metab)

  6. May restore leptin sensitivity and synergize with incretins (mechanistic rationale) Speculative

    Amylin functionally interacts with leptin and can re-sensitize leptin signaling in obese rodents, and amylin agonists synergize with GLP-1 drugs (the cagrilintide+semaglutide 'CagriSema' precedent). This is the rationale for Lilly testing eloralintide alongside tirzepatide. For eloralintide specifically, human synergy remains a hypothesis — the combination trials (e.g., NCT06345066, NCT06603571) are ongoing or completed-but-unpublished, with no efficacy results reported. PMID 26071095 (Hay 2015, Pharmacol Rev); PMID 39426704 (Hankir 2025, Biochimie); NCT06345066, NCT06603571 (Lilly combination trials)

  7. Net clinical result: dose-dependent weight loss up to ~20% over 48 weeks Evidence-human

    In the Phase 2 trial the mechanism translated into real weight loss: mean −20% at the top doses (9 mg and 6-9 mg escalation) versus −0.4% on placebo at 48 weeks, in a dose-dependent staircase (−9% at 1 mg up to −20%). This is the headline that makes eloralintide a landmark for the amylin class — as monotherapy it rivals GLP-1 monotherapy magnitudes. Caveat: this rests on a single 263-person Phase 2; Phase 3 confirmation is pending. PMID 41207310 (Billings 2025, Lancet; NCT06230523)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Long-acting synthetic amylin analog engineered for non-aggregation and extended half-life. Agonist at the amylin receptor family — heterodimers of the calcitonin receptor (CTR) with receptor-activity-modifying proteins RAMP1/2/3 forming AMY1R/AMY2R/AMY3R. In Lilly cell-based assays it preferentially activated human AMY1R: ~12-fold greater potency than at the bare CTR and ~11-fold greater than at AMY3R. Note the species difference: in rat cells both AMY1R and AMY3R were activated more potently than CTR, so the 'AMY1R-selective' descriptor is specifically a human-receptor property.

Signalling cascade. Amylin receptors are class B G-protein-coupled receptors coupling primarily to Gs → adenylyl cyclase → cAMP → PKA in CALCR-expressing neurons of the hindbrain (area postrema, nucleus tractus solitarius). Neuronal activation propagates to the lateral parabrachial nucleus and hypothalamic nuclei to suppress food intake; a functional interaction with leptin signaling is described (preclinical). Net physiology: satiation, reduced meal size, modest slowing of gastric emptying, suppression of postprandial glucagon.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Subcutaneous injection, once weekly.
Absorption / bioavailability
SC absorption consistent with a long-acting peptide; exposure (AUCτ,ss and Cmax) was approximately dose-proportional across the Phase 1 multiple-ascending-dose range (dose-normalised geometric-mean ratios ~1.1 and ~1.0 respectively). Dedicated relative-bioavailability data are in a Phase 1 study (NCT06916065).
Half-life
Long enough to support once-weekly dosing; the PK profile was explicitly stated to support weekly administration. Exact terminal half-life not emphasized in the public abstracts.
Metabolism
As a peptide, expected proteolytic/peptidase catabolism to amino acids rather than CYP-mediated metabolism; CYP-based drug-drug-interaction risk is expected to be low (formal characterization pending). In-vitro metabolic profiling of related weight-loss amylin agonists (pramlintide, cagrilintide, KBP-066 — not eloralintide itself) has been done in the anti-doping context (PMID 41702251).
Excretion
Peptide clearance; dedicated organ-impairment PK studies are underway — severe renal impairment/ESRD (NCT07426380) and mild-to-severe hepatic impairment (NCT07401862) — meaning renal/hepatic dosing implications are NOT yet established.
Notes
PK characterized across Phase 1 single- and multiple-ascending-dose studies (NCT05295940) plus ethnicity-bridging studies in Japanese (NCT06297616, with tirzepatide) and Chinese (NCT06916091) participants. No dose escalation was needed to reach target exposure in the Phase 1 MAD, though Phase 2 tested both fixed and escalating schemes for tolerability.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

Clear monotonic dose-response for weight loss over 48 weeks (Phase 2, NCT06230523): mean percent change in body weight −9% (1 mg), −12% (3 mg), −18% (6 mg), −20% (9 mg), −20% (6-9 mg escalation), −16% (3-9 mg escalation) vs −0.4% placebo. Tolerability was also dose-dependent and, revealingly, non-monotonic: nausea was 11% (1 mg), 13% (3 mg), 64% (6 mg fixed), 33% (9 mg), 54% (6-9 mg), 25% (3-9 mg) vs 14% placebo — the 3-9 mg gradual-escalation arm achieved −16% with markedly lower nausea (25%) than the 6 mg fixed arm (64%), illustrating that dose-escalation/titration blunts GI side effects. Fatigue was likewise dose-dependent (up to 43% at 9 mg, 46% at 6-9 mg vs 12% placebo). Phase 1 MAD showed −2.6% to −11.3% weight loss at 12 weeks with minimal GI events at the doses tested. NO DOSING PROTOCOL is provided here — these figures describe the trial's fixed randomized arms for evidence context only.

How it compares

  • vs Cagrilintide (Novo Nordisk, non-selective amylin/DACRA)
    same: Both long-acting weekly amylin-receptor agonists for obesity; both act via hindbrain CALCR neurons.
    different: Cagrilintide is non-selective (hits CTR/AMY3R more broadly); eloralintide is human-AMY1R-preferring and caused less conditioned taste avoidance than cagrilintide in rats — the basis for a claimed tolerability edge (unproven head-to-head in humans). Cagrilintide's lead role is in the CagriSema combo with semaglutide.
  • vs Pramlintide (first-gen amylin analog, approved)
    same: Amylin-based mechanism; central satiation.
    different: Pramlintide is short-acting (multiple daily injections), used as an insulin adjunct in diabetes with modest weight effect; eloralintide is weekly and posts far larger monotherapy weight loss.
  • vs Semaglutide / tirzepatide (GLP-1 / GLP-1+GIP incretins)
    same: All produce central appetite suppression and clinically meaningful weight loss via weekly SC dosing.
    different: Different receptor family (incretins vs amylin). Eloralintide's ~20% Phase 2 monotherapy figure is in the tirzepatide range and above semaglutide — but that's cross-trial, not randomized. Lilly is also testing eloralintide combined with tirzepatide, betting on additive/synergistic loss. GI-side-effect mechanism overlaps (area postrema); amylin may be gentler per the selectivity thesis (unproven).
  • vs Amycretin (Novo, GLP-1+amylin unimolecular)
    same: Both next-gen agents engaging amylin biology for large weight loss.
    different: Amycretin is a dual GLP-1/amylin single molecule (numerically largest NMA weight loss, ~24% high-dose) whereas eloralintide is a pure selective amylin agonist; different side-effect trade-offs and both early-stage.

History & legal status

Developed by Eli Lilly (code LY3841136; INN eloralintide). First-in-human single-ascending-dose study began March 2022 (NCT05295940, N=148 across healthy Part A and overweight Part B). Phase 1 MAD and combination-with-tirzepatide studies (NCT06297616, NCT06345066, NCT06603571, NCT06916065, NCT06916091) followed through 2024-2025. The pivotal 48-week Phase 2 (NCT06230523, under master protocol NCT06143956) ran Feb 2024-Aug 2025 and was published in The Lancet (Dec 6, 2025). A broad Phase 3 ENLIGHTEN program launched late 2025/early 2026 spanning obesity without T2D (ENLIGHTEN-1, NCT07321886, N≈1980), with T2D (ENLIGHTEN-2, NCT07282600), obstructive sleep apnea (ENLIGHTEN-3, NCT07369011), osteoarthritis knee pain (ENLIGHTEN-4, NCT07353931), and add-on to a weekly incretin (ENLIGHTEN-6, NCT07392190), plus organ-impairment and mechanism studies — primary completions projected 2028. NO regulatory approval anywhere as of mid-2026; all use is investigational.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Phase 3 confirmation: does the ~20% monotherapy weight loss hold in large, diverse, longer trials? (data not before 2028)
  • Durability and weight-regain after stopping — no long-term or maintenance data.
  • Cardiovascular, renal, and other hard organ-outcome effects — untested (incretins have CV-outcome data; eloralintide has none yet).
  • Real-world tolerability and discontinuation rates at effective doses, and the best titration to keep nausea low while reaching efficacy.
  • Body composition in humans: how much lean/muscle vs fat is lost, and muscle-preservation strategies.
  • Whether the AMY1R-selectivity yields a clinically meaningful tolerability advantage vs cagrilintide (no head-to-head).
  • Combination efficacy/safety with tirzepatide and other agents (trials ongoing or completed-but-unpublished).
  • PK and dosing in renal/hepatic impairment (dedicated studies in progress).
  • Effects on bone, and on glucose metabolism/insulin sensitivity (dedicated Phase 1 mechanism study NCT07665879 planned), plus rare/idiosyncratic adverse events.
  • Immunogenicity of the peptide over chronic weekly dosing.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

For evidence/harm-reduction context only — NOT a protocol and NOT medical advice; this drug is investigational and not legally available by prescription. If it were used under clinical supervision, the sensible things to WATCH would be: (1) GI tolerability — nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and appetite/hydration status, since these are dose-dependent and the main reason people struggle; (2) rate and magnitude of weight loss with attention to adequate protein intake and resistance exercise to protect lean mass; (3) fatigue and functional status; (4) in anyone with diabetes on other glucose-lowering drugs, hypoglycemia risk from combined appetite suppression/reduced intake; (5) renal and hepatic status, given PK in impairment is not yet defined; (6) bone health over long-term use (class-level uncertainty). GREY-MARKET/PED WARNING: 'research-chemical' amylin peptides sold online are unregulated, of unverified identity/purity/sterility, and carry injection and contamination risks entirely separate from the drug's pharmacology; amylin-receptor agonists are also under anti-doping surveillance. Anyone considering metabolic therapy should do so with an approved agent under a clinician, not via unregulated sources.

Caveats

HYPE-VS-DATA: The ~20% figure is real and impressive but comes from ONE 48-week Phase 2 of 263 people (PMID 41207310); it is not yet confirmed in Phase 3, has no long-term (>1 yr) or cardiovascular-outcomes data, and no head-to-head vs tirzepatide/semaglutide. Cross-trial comparisons (e.g., the network meta-analysis ranking high-dose eloralintide −18.01% [P-score 0.89], behind high-dose amycretin −23.95% and near CagriSema −17.18%) are LOW-CERTAINTY, INDIRECT, and NOT randomized comparisons; the authors themselves flag the data as sparse and preliminary (PMID 42175595). SELECTIVITY CLAIM: The 'clean AMY1R-selective = less nausea' story is grounded in in-vitro human-receptor ratios and rodent taste-avoidance data (PMID 41109426); in humans, nausea was clearly dose-dependent and reached ~64% at the 6 mg fixed-dose arm — so 'selective' does NOT mean side-effect-free, it means potentially better-tolerated per unit efficacy, which is still clinically unproven and has no head-to-head vs cagrilintide. EVIDENCE TIERING: mechanism steps 3-6 rest largely on rodent/in-vitro data; only the weight-loss and tolerability read-outs are human. PED/FITNESS CONTEXT: This is unapproved, not available legally by prescription; 'research-chemical'/grey-market amylin peptides are unregulated, of unknown identity/purity, and carry real risk. Amylin-receptor agonists are on the anti-doping radar (metabolic-profiling/detection work for weight-loss amylin agonists in preventive doping research, PMID 41702251). NOT medical advice; no dosing guidance is provided.
