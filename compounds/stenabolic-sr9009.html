Main target: REV-ERBα and REV-ERBβ. Evidence is animal- and lab-only — no human trials yet.

EvidenceRodent / preclinical

A "circadian clock" drug sold as an exercise-in-a-pill — but it's an unapproved research chemical with zero human trials, and a chunk of what people credit to it may not even involve its supposed target.

Rodent / preclinical 19 citations
Class
Synthetic REV-ERB agonist (a circadian-clock nuclear-receptor ligand)
Primary target
REV-ERBα and REV-ERBβ (genes NR1D1 / NR1D2) — nuclear receptors that form the negative arm of the body clock and act as transcriptional repressors
Human status
Unapproved and investigational. SR9009 is NOT an approved drug anywhere, is not in clinical use, and to date has essentially no published human efficacy or safety trials — the entire evidence base is cell-culture and rodent work. It is banned by WADA (S4 class) and has been detected as an undeclared ingredient in products sold to consumers as "SARMs" (JAMA 2017, PMID 29183075), so what is in a given bottle is frequently not what the label says. It is also widely reported to have very poor oral bioavailability, meaning the doses people swallow likely deliver little active drug — note: this PK claim is repeated everywhere but no dedicated published human/animal pharmacokinetic paper has been identified, so treat it as not-well-published rather than firmly established.

At a glance

  • Rodent studies: increased whole-body energy expenditure and reduced fat mass in diet-induced obese mice (Solt 2012, PMID 22460951) — Evidence-rodent
  • Rodent studies: improved running/exercise capacity and more muscle mitochondria via the LKB1-AMPK-SIRT1-PGC1α pathway (Woldt 2013, PMID 23852339) — Evidence-rodent
  • Lowered blood lipids and glucose in obese mice (Solt 2012) — Evidence-rodent
  • Wake-promoting / sleep-architecture effects, acting partly through REV-ERBβ (Amador 2018, PMID 29355503) — Evidence-rodent
  • A meaningful fraction of its cellular effects occur even when REV-ERB is genetically deleted — i.e. off-target, not 'clean' (Dierickx 2019, PMID 31127047) — Established (in cells)
  • Marketed human benefits (fat loss, endurance, 'cardio in a pill'): no human trial support — Speculative in humans

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

7 steps2 established 4 rodent / preclinical 1 contested / speculative

  1. It targets the body clock, not a hormone receptor Established

    Every cell runs a roughly 24-hour clock built from a loop of proteins that switch genes on and off. REV-ERBα and REV-ERBβ are part of that loop — they act like an 'off switch' (a repressor) for clock and metabolic genes. SR9009 is a synthetic molecule designed to bind REV-ERB and turn that off-switch UP. This is a fundamentally different lever from steroids or SARMs, which work on the androgen receptor. Solt 2012 Nature, PMID 22460951

  2. Switching on REV-ERB rewires metabolic genes Evidence-rodent

    When SR9009 activates REV-ERB in liver, muscle and fat, it changes the daily on/off pattern of dozens of metabolism genes. In obese mice this raised energy expenditure (they burned more) and cut fat mass, blood fats and blood sugar. This is the original 'metabolic benefit' result — and it is in mice, not people. Solt 2012 Nature, PMID 22460951

  3. In muscle, it pushes mitochondria — the 'endurance' story Evidence-rodent

    The exercise-mimetic reputation comes from one key muscle study. REV-ERBα turns out to be highly active in oxidative (endurance-type) muscle. Boosting it increased the number and function of mitochondria — the cell's aerobic power plants — and mice could run longer. It does this by flipping on the LKB1-AMPK-SIRT1-PGC1alpha pathway, the same master switch real exercise uses for endurance adaptation. Woldt 2013 Nat Med, PMID 23852339

  4. More mitochondria + less self-cleanup = higher aerobic capacity Evidence-rodent

    Two things combine: REV-ERB activation builds more mitochondria AND dials down autophagy, the process that clears worn-out mitochondria. Net result in the rodent model is more aerobic machinery in the muscle, which is why it's hyped as 'cardio in a pill.' Important: this was muscle-level biology in mice; it has never been shown to translate to trained humans. Woldt 2013 Nat Med, PMID 23852339

  5. It also messes with the sleep/wake clock Evidence-rodent

    Because REV-ERB is a core clock gene, SR9009 doesn't just touch metabolism — it shifts sleep and wakefulness too. In mice it acts as a wake-promoter, and that effect runs partly through REV-ERBβ. This is a reminder that you can't cleanly hit 'fat burning' without nudging the whole circadian system. Amador 2018 Biochem Pharmacol, PMID 29355503

  6. The big asterisk: a lot of it isn't even REV-ERB Established

    This is the most important and least-marketed finding. When researchers genetically deleted BOTH REV-ERBα and REV-ERBβ and then gave cells SR9009, the drug STILL changed metabolism, gene activity and cell viability. That means a meaningful share of SR9009's effects are off-target — they happen through pathways other than its supposed receptor. So you cannot assume 'SR9009 effect = REV-ERB effect,' and the off-target actions are poorly mapped and not safety-characterized. Dierickx 2019 PNAS, PMID 31127047

  7. What actually reaches you is the real-world problem Contested

    Even setting biology aside: SR9009 is widely reported to be poorly absorbed when swallowed, so oral doses may deliver little active drug. And independent testing found SR9009 turning up as an undeclared ingredient in products sold as 'SARMs,' with labels frequently wrong about what — and how much — is inside. Combined with zero human safety data, that means real-world use is essentially an uncontrolled experiment. JAMA 2017, PMID 29183075

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. SR9009 (a tri-substituted pyrrole, ethyl ester of 'SR9011-like' scaffold; the parent acid SR9009 has a chlorobenzyl/thiophene-methyl amine head) is a SYNTHETIC AGONIST of the heme-binding nuclear receptors REV-ERBalpha (NR1D1) and REV-ERBbeta (NR1D2). It is a DUAL/non-isoform-selective agonist — it activates both alpha and beta, and the related compound SR9011 is more potent (Evidence-in vitro / Established for dual activity; Solt 2012, Nature, PMID 22460951). Reported cell-based potency: EC50 in the low-micromolar-to-high-nanomolar range (Solt 2012 reported SR9009 EC50 ~670 nM for REV-ERBalpha and ~800 nM for REV-ERBbeta in cotransfection/repression assays; these are functional EC50 values, NOT equilibrium Ki, and should be cited as such — Evidence-in vitro). REV-ERBs are constitutive transcriptional REPRESSORS; their endogenous ligand is HEME, which binds the ligand-binding domain and stabilizes recruitment of the NCoR/HDAC3 corepressor complex. SR9009 is an AGONIST in the sense that it mimics/enhances this repressive activity — i.e., a REV-ERB 'agonist' DRIVES MORE gene repression, the opposite directionality of a typical activating receptor agonist. CRITICAL CAVEAT (Established, human cell + KO models): SR9009 has documented REV-ERB-INDEPENDENT effects — it alters viability, metabolism and transcription in cells genetically lacking BOTH REV-ERBalpha and beta (Dierickx 2019, PNAS, PMID 31127047), and kills prostate-cancer cells via an LXRalpha/FOXM1 pathway independent of REV-ERBs (PMID 36357378). So measured cellular effects cannot be assumed to be 'REV-ERB activation' — a major interpretive limitation for the whole evidence base.

Signalling cascade. Mechanistically: (1) SR9009 binds the REV-ERB ligand-binding domain at/near the heme pocket and promotes recruitment of the NCoR1–HDAC3 corepressor complex to REV-ERB response elements (RORE/RevDR2 motifs). (2) This SUPPRESSES transcription of REV-ERB target genes — most famously the core-clock activator BMAL1 (ARNTL), reinforcing circadian repression, plus a wide metabolic gene set. In Solt 2012 (PMID 22460951), in-vivo dosing altered the circadian pattern of clock and metabolic genes in hypothalamus, liver, skeletal muscle and adipose and INCREASED ENERGY EXPENDITURE in mice. (3) In skeletal muscle the relevant axis (from REV-ERBalpha loss-of-function work, Woldt 2013, Nat Med, PMID 23852339): REV-ERBalpha positively supports the LKB1–AMPK–SIRT1–PGC-1alpha (PPARGC1A) signaling pathway. REV-ERBalpha deficiency DEACTIVATES this pathway, lowers mitochondrial biogenesis and INCREASES autophagy/mitophagy, reducing oxidative capacity and exercise tolerance; conversely overexpression or pharmacological activation INCREASED mitochondrial number and exercise capacity — this is the molecular basis of the 'exercise-mimetic'/'endurance' marketing. (4) Metabolic outputs in animals: suppression of de-novo lipogenesis genes, suppression of cholesterol-biosynthesis genes (REV-ERB directly binds and represses HMG-CoA reductase and other cholesterologenic genes, and indirectly via SREBF2; Sitaula 2017, PMID 28213272), reduced plasma cholesterol/triglycerides, reduced fat mass in diet-induced-obese mice (Solt 2012). (5) Anti-inflammatory output: REV-ERB activation shifts macrophages from M1 toward M2 and represses NF-kB/NLRP3-driven cytokines (Sitaula 2015 atherosclerosis, PMID 25800870). NOTE: an unknown fraction of these readouts may be REV-ERB-independent per PMID 31127047.

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Research compound; NOT an approved drug and never formulated as a registered pharmaceutical. Black-market 'Stenabolic' is sold as an oral liquid/capsule for athletic/PED use. In the foundational rodent studies it was given by INTRAPERITONEAL INJECTION (e.g. 100 mg/kg in Solt 2012), precisely because oral exposure is poor.
Absorption / bioavailability
POOR oral bioavailability and poor pharmacokinetics are the defining limitation — repeatedly described in the primary literature and the reason it was dosed IP/SC in animals (Established / Evidence-rodent; consistent across Solt 2012 and subsequent work). The original Scripps/Burris characterization noted SR9009/SR9011 have suboptimal PK with low oral exposure and short systemic persistence, which is why orally bioavailable successor REV-ERB ligands (e.g. SR12418 and related) were later pursued. Practical implication: the human oral dose-response is essentially unknown and oral 'Stenabolic' likely delivers little intact active compound (Speculative for humans).
Half-life
Short systemic half-life (order of a few hours in rodents; precise validated human half-life NOT established — Evidence-rodent / Contested for exact value). Short duration of action is part of the 'poor PK' descriptor and is why animal protocols used frequent/high dosing.
Metabolism
Extensively HEPATICALLY metabolized. In human liver microsomes SR9009 is biotransformed to ~8–13 phase-I metabolites via hydroxylation, N-/O-dealkylation, and oxidation, with the DE-ALKYLATED metabolites most abundant; principal CYP isoenzymes are CYP3A4, CYP3A5, CYP2C19 and CYP2D6 (Established in vitro; Mazzarino 2018, Drug Test Anal, PMID 30395700; Geldof 2016, PMID 27706103). Phase-II GLUCURONIDATION follows. CYP2D6 and CYP2C19 GENETIC POLYMORPHISMS measurably alter the metabolite profile, and CYP3A inhibitors (ketoconazole, itraconazole, fluoxetine, nefazodone, paroxetine, etc.) significantly change metabolism — a real drug-interaction surface (PMID 30395700).
Excretion
Renal excretion of phase-I/II metabolites; the ester-cleaved carboxylic-acid metabolite and dealkylated/glucuronidated species are the doping-control markers. Parent SR9009 is largely converted before excretion (Evidence-in vitro / Established for marker identification; PMID 27706103, PMID 30395700; equine in-vivo metabolite confirmation PMID 34224639).
Notes
SR9009 is itself an ESTER; ester hydrolysis to the corresponding carboxylic acid is a major route and a key doping marker. BBB penetration: SR9009 reaches brain/CNS sufficiently to alter hypothalamic clock-gene expression and sleep/wake and to show effects in stroke and Alzheimer mouse models — so it does cross into the CNS in rodents (Evidence-rodent; e.g. PMID 30973894, PMID 39557376). Not a prodrug in the therapeutic sense; not esterified for depot like AAS.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

DOSE-RESPONSE IS ALMOST ENTIRELY RODENT, by injection. Foundational efficacy doses: Solt 2012 (PMID 22460951) used ~100 mg/kg/day IP in mice and saw altered circadian/metabolic gene rhythms, increased energy expenditure, and in diet-induced-obese mice reduced fat mass with improved dyslipidemia and hyperglycemia. Woldt 2013 (PMID 23852339) showed pharmacological REV-ERB activation in vivo increased running/exercise capacity in mice (the source of the 'endurance' claim). Atherosclerosis suppression (Sitaula 2015, PMID 25800870): 100 mg/kg for 7 weeks significantly reduced plaque size in LDLR-null mice. A CHRONIC LOW-DOSE arm (10 mg/kg/day, Yang 2025, PMID 39800061) reduced light-induced weight gain, white-fat mass and insulin resistance but had NO significant effect on overall energy homeostasis and only partial molecular effects — i.e. lower doses give attenuated, narrower effects. Cancer cytotoxicity (Sulli 2018, Nature, PMID 29320480) occurs at micromolar concentrations in vitro and tumor-suppressive doses in vivo. HUMAN DOSE-RESPONSE: UNKNOWN. There are no human pharmacokinetic or efficacy trials; no validated human dose produces a defined effect magnitude. Because oral bioavailability is poor, the relationship between an ingested 'Stenabolic' dose and any systemic effect in humans cannot be specified from evidence (Speculative). NO dosing protocol is provided here.

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs SR9011 (sister REV-ERB agonist, same Scripps/Burris series)
    same: Same chemotype and dual REV-ERBalpha/beta agonism; same circadian/metabolic and anticancer effects; same poor PK; same off-target autophagy/lipogenesis actions and doping-marker workup.
    different: SR9011 is generally more potent in vitro and produces a larger metabolite set (~14 vs ~8); both share the same fundamental oral-bioavailability problem. SR9009 is the one that reached the consumer/PED market under 'Stenabolic'.
  • vs GW501516 (Cardarine, PPARdelta agonist — the other 'endurance' research chemical)
    same: Both marketed together as oral 'exercise-mimetics'/endurance enhancers, both prohibited by WADA, both non-approved research chemicals sold on the gray market, both act on metabolic/oxidative gene programs (PGC-1alpha-linked).
    different: Different target entirely: Cardarine is a PPARdelta AGONIST (transcriptional activator) with reasonable oral bioavailability, whereas SR9009 hits circadian REV-ERB nuclear receptors as a repression-enhancer and is poorly oral. GW501516 carries a specific, serious carcinogenicity signal (multi-organ tumors in rodent tox studies that halted development) — a hazard not characterized the same way for SR9009; conversely SR9009 has documented receptor-independent off-target activity that Cardarine does not share.
  • vs SLU-PP-332 / SLU-PP-915 (ERRalpha/beta/gamma pan-agonist exercise mimetics)
    same: Same therapeutic concept — synthetic nuclear-receptor agonists that boost mitochondrial biogenesis, fatty-acid oxidation and aerobic capacity ('exercise in a pill'); both prohibited as metabolic modulators by WADA.
    different: Target ERR orphan nuclear receptors (estrogen-related receptors) rather than REV-ERB; newer SLU-PP-915 was specifically engineered for ORAL bioavailability, directly addressing the limitation that cripples SR9009. They are activators of mitochondrial/PGC-1alpha programs via a distinct receptor (PMID 36988910, PMID 41421047).
  • vs Heme (the endogenous REV-ERB ligand)
    same: Both occupy the REV-ERB ligand-binding pocket and promote the receptor's repressive (corepressor-recruiting) activity.
    different: Heme is the natural, physiologically regulated ligand whose action is tightly REV-ERB-dependent; SR9009 is a synthetic mimic that additionally exerts significant REV-ERB-INDEPENDENT effects (PMID 31127047), so it is not a clean surrogate for heme/REV-ERB biology.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Cardarine overlaps (redundant) Evidence-rodent
    Both are non-hormonal 'exercise-mimetic' metabolic modulators that converge on the same downstream program: driving skeletal-muscle fatty-acid oxidation and remodeling toward oxidative (fatigue-resistant) fibers. Cardarine does this by switching on PPARδ; SR9009 does it through the REV-ERB circadian-clock nuclear receptors (and, notably, partly through REV-ERB-INDEPENDENT effects on metabolism). Because they hit different receptors but push the same fat-oxidation/oxidative-fiber endpoint, stacking is largely redundant on the intended effect while summing each compound's separate liabilities — and both are WADA-banned metabolic modulators. Neither has human safety data at these exposures. Cardarine PPARδ/oxidative-fiber program: Narkar 2008 PMID 18674809; monograph mechanism_steps. SR9009 REV-ERB and REV-ERB-independent metabolic effects: SR9009 monograph primary_target/mechanism_steps. Watch: Fasting lipid panel (both lower triglycerides — an additive drop is plausible), fasting glucose/insulin, and general well-being; there is no validated blood test for the theoretical oncologic signals of either agent, so track any new/unexplained symptoms rather than assuming the combination is 'safe'.

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

"Origin: discovered as part of a synthetic REV-ERB ligand program led by Thomas Burris and Theodore Kamenecka at The Scripps Research Institute (Florida); SR9009 and SR9011 were first reported as in-vivo-active synthetic REV-ERB agonists in Solt et al., Nature 2012 (PMID 22460951), as tools to probe circadian/metabolic biology. The 2013 Woldt Nature Medicine muscle/exercise paper (PMID 23852339) seeded the 'exercise mimetic / endurance enhancer' reputation that drove gray-market adoption under the name 'Stenabolic'. REGULATORY STATUS: NOT approved by FDA, EMA or any regulator for any indication; never entered formal human clinical trials as a marketed drug; it is a research chemical. It is NOT a SARM (it is a REV-ERB ligand) despite being sold alongside SARMs. It is explicitly PROHIBITED in sport: the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) bans REV-ERB agonists / metabolic modulators (S4 'Hormone and Metabolic Modulators' category alongside agents like GW501516/Cardarine), and certified reference materials and detection methods for SR9009 doping control have been developed (PMID 30129998, PMID 27706103, PMID 30395700, equine work PMID 34224639). Sale as a dietary supplement/'research only' product is not lawful for human consumption in the US/EU; products are frequently mislabeled (JAMA, PMID 29183075). Equine racing authorities likewise screen for it."

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • What fraction of SR9009's biological effects are genuine REV-ERB activation versus REV-ERB-independent off-target activity? Dierickx 2019 (PMID 31127047) proved the off-target component exists but did not quantify how much of the metabolic/endurance/anticancer phenotype is on- vs off-target.
  • Does ORAL SR9009 produce ANY meaningful systemic exposure or endurance effect in humans? Given its poor oral bioavailability and short half-life and the absence of human PK studies, whether ingested 'Stenabolic' does anything at all in people is genuinely unknown.
  • Validated human pharmacokinetics — actual oral bioavailability %, plasma half-life, and active-metabolite exposure — have never been measured in living humans (only human-microsome in-vitro data exist).
  • Long-term safety of chronic REV-ERB/clock suppression in humans: no data on endocrine, immune, glucose/lipid, or oncologic consequences of sustained use.
  • Reproducibility/translation of the rodent endurance effect to humans: the exercise-capacity gain is rodent-only, partly from genetic overexpression, and confounded by the off-target caveat — it has not been confirmed in any human trial.
  • Tissue-specific and dose-specific safety window: cancer studies report selective tumor-cell lethality with little normal-tissue toxicity in mice, but whether a safe-versus-cytotoxic dose boundary exists for healthy humans is uncharacterized.

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only (NOT an endorsement; no dosing guidance). Reasonable signals to watch and discuss with a clinician: (1) LIPID PANEL — REV-ERB agonism strongly suppresses cholesterol/lipid synthesis in animals, so total/LDL cholesterol and triglycerides could drop; track for unexpectedly low cholesterol as well as abnormal values. (2) FASTING GLUCOSE / HbA1c and insulin sensitivity — given documented effects on glucose handling and insulin resistance in rodents. (3) LIVER FUNCTION TESTS (ALT/AST, bilirubin) — heavy hepatic CYP metabolism and unknown product purity warrant baseline and periodic LFTs. (4) SLEEP and circadian symptoms — insomnia, fragmented sleep, daytime drowsiness or disrupted sleep–wake timing, since the drug directly manipulates the clock. (5) DRUG-INTERACTION review — flag concurrent CYP3A4/2D6/2C19 substrates and inhibitors (azole antifungals, several SSRIs/nefazodone) that can alter exposure. (6) PRODUCT-QUALITY awareness — because black-market 'Stenabolic' is frequently mislabeled or adulterated (JAMA PMID 29183075), unexplained symptoms may reflect an undeclared compound, not SR9009; third-party assay of any product is the single highest-yield risk-reduction step. (7) General: any new cardiovascular, neurological, or constitutional symptoms warrant stopping and clinical evaluation. There is NO established human-safety bloodwork protocol for this compound — all monitoring is precautionary and extrapolated from rodent mechanism."

Caveats

Harm-reduction and honesty framing for the audience: (1) There are no human clinical trials of SR9009 for fat loss, endurance, or anything else — every benefit in this package is from mice or cell dishes, and rodent metabolic results routinely fail to translate to humans. (2) It is unapproved and WADA-banned (athletes will test positive). (3) The 'clean targeted clock drug' pitch is undermined by Dierickx 2019: a real chunk of its activity is off-target and uncharacterized, so long-term safety is genuinely unknown. (4) Poor oral bioavailability is widely claimed — meaning swallowed product may do little, which paradoxically can push people to higher/sketchier dosing. (5) Grey-market supply is unreliable: JAMA testing shows products labeled SARMs containing SR9009, wrong doses, or other undeclared drugs. Bottom line to teach: SR9009 is a fascinating circadian-biology research tool, NOT a validated human supplement, and 'exercise in a pill' is a marketing claim that mouse data does not earn in humans. No dosing or sourcing guidance.

Evidence is animal- and lab-only — no human trials yet.
It targets the body clock, not a hormone receptor. Every cell runs a roughly 24-hour clock built from a loop of proteins that switch genes on and off. REV-ERBα and REV-ERBβ are part of that loop — they act like an 'off switch' (a repressor) for clock and metabolic genes. SR9009 is a synthetic molecule designed to bind REV-ERB and turn that off-switch UP. This is a fundamentally different lever from steroids or SARMs, which work on the androgen receptor.
