Clomiphene citrate

A pill that tricks your brain into thinking estrogen is low, so the brain cranks up the natural signal to make more testosterone — without shutting your testicles down.

Human / approvedFDA-approved 20 citations
Class
Selective estrogen receptor modulator (SERM), triphenylethylene family
Primary target
Estrogen receptor alpha (ESR1 / ER-alpha) — ChEMBL target CHEMBL206 — in the hypothalamus and pituitary
Human status
Evidence-human / widely used. Clomiphene citrate is FDA-approved for female ovulation induction; its use for male hypogonadism and male infertility is off-label but well-studied, with multiple RCTs and several systematic reviews/meta-analyses (e.g., Hohl 2025 PMID 41066380; Huijben 2022 PMID 34933414). The purified enclomiphene isomer reached Phase 3 (ChEMBL max_phase 3) but is not broadly approved. In the PED/bodybuilding world it is a mainstay of post-cycle therapy (PCT) to restart the natural axis after anabolic-steroid-induced shutdown.

At a glance

  • Raises LH and FSH by blocking estrogen feedback in the brain (central, not at the testes)
  • Increases endogenous testosterone, often roughly doubling levels into the normal range in hypogonadal men (Evidence-human)
  • Preserves intratesticular testosterone, testicular size, and sperm production — the key difference from testosterone replacement therapy, which suppresses them (Evidence-human)
  • Oral, once-daily; cheap; used off-label for male hypogonadism/infertility and in PCT to restart the axis after AAS
  • Mixed estrogenic profile from the slow-clearing zuclomiphene isomer; reported issues include mood changes and visual disturbances (Contested/Evidence-human)
  • Can lower IGF-1 in some men (Evidence-human; Mogar N et al. J Endocr Soc 2025, PMID 40421427)

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Normal feedback loop (the baseline) Established

    Your testosterone system runs on a thermostat called the HPG axis: the hypothalamus releases GnRH in pulses, which tells the pituitary to release LH and FSH, which tell the testicles' Leydig cells to make testosterone and support sperm. Some testosterone is converted to estrogen (estradiol), and that estrogen feeds back to the brain to throttle the whole loop down — keeping levels in check. Corpus MoA card 'HPG axis'; Ide V et al. Int J Mol Sci 2020, PMID 33375030

  2. Clomiphene blocks the estrogen sensor in the brain Established

    Clomiphene binds to estrogen receptor alpha in the hypothalamus and pituitary and acts as an antagonist there — it sits in the receptor without switching it on, so the brain can no longer 'feel' the estrogen that normally tells it to slow down. ChEMBL mechanism mec_id 7688 (ER-alpha modulator, CHEMBL206, direct interaction); Wheeler KM et al. Sex Med Rev 2019, PMID 30522888

  3. The brain thinks estrogen is low and ramps up GnRH Established

    With the estrogen brake released, the hypothalamus increases the size and frequency of its GnRH pulses, because it perceives a shortage of estrogen feedback. Wu YC, Sung WW. Pharmaceuticals 2024, PMID 39338395; Corpus MoA card 'Estrogen receptors'

  4. Pituitary releases more LH and FSH Established

    The stronger GnRH signal drives the pituitary to secrete more luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) into the bloodstream. Wheeler KM et al. Sex Med Rev 2019, PMID 30522888; trial NCT00697814

  5. Testicles make more testosterone — and keep working Evidence-human

    LH tells the testicular Leydig cells to produce more testosterone, while FSH supports the Sertoli cells and sperm production. Because the signal comes from the man's own brain (not injected testosterone), the testicles stay switched on — so intratesticular testosterone and fertility are preserved, unlike with TRT which shuts the axis down. Wiehle RD et al. Fertil Steril 2014 (enclomiphene RCT vs topical T), PMID 25044085; Micol LA et al. Curr Pharm Des 2021, PMID 32329682

  6. Two isomers behave differently Evidence-human

    Clomiphene is a mix: enclomiphene (the trans-isomer) is the potent, shorter-lived antagonist that raises LH/FSH and testosterone; zuclomiphene (the cis-isomer) is weakly estrogenic and clears very slowly, so it accumulates over weeks of dosing and is blamed for some estrogen-like side effects. This is why purified enclomiphene was developed. Helo S et al. BJU Int 2017 (serum enclomiphene vs zuclomiphene on chronic CC), PMID 27511863; Rodriguez KM et al. Expert Opin Pharmacother 2016, PMID 27337642

  7. Net effect and magnitude Evidence-human

    In hypogonadal men, clomiphene typically raises total testosterone substantially (often roughly doubling it into the normal range) along with rises in LH and FSH; meta-analyses confirm it increases testosterone versus placebo while maintaining sperm parameters, positioning it as a fertility-sparing alternative to testosterone replacement. Hohl A et al. Arch Endocrinol Metab 2025 RCT meta-analysis, PMID 41066380; Huijben M et al. Andrology 2022 meta-analysis, PMID 34933414

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Clomiphene citrate (Clomid; research codes MER-41/MRL-41; ChEMBL3185958; freebase C26H28ClNO, MW ~406; citrate salt MW ~598; ALogP ~6.6, highly lipophilic) is a first-generation triphenylethylene SERM. Commercial 'clomiphene' is a ~50:50 (historically ~38:62) racemic mixture of two geometric isomers with OPPOSITE pharmacology at estrogen receptors: ENCLOMIPHENE (E-isomer, zalclomiphene's counterpart) is the potent ESTROGEN ANTAGONIST that drives the HPG-stimulating effect, while ZUCLOMIPHENE (Z-isomer) is a long-acting ESTROGEN AGONIST. Target = estrogen receptor (predominantly ERalpha/ESR1 in hypothalamus and pituitary; also binds ERbeta/ESR2). ChEMBL curates the mechanism as 'Estrogen receptor alpha modulator, action type MODULATOR' (Established). Quantitative human ER Ki/EC50 values for the separated isomers are NOT well established in public binding databases (ChEMBL returned no Ki/Kd records for this entry; treat specific affinity numbers as a genuine data gap). Relative binding affinity (RBA): in a classic ERalpha/ERbeta competition panel, clomifene bound BOTH subtypes but with LOW affinity relative to estradiol — ranking far below 4-OH-tamoxifen and estradiol itself (clomifene clustered near estriol/2-OH-estradiol at the weak end), i.e. it is a comparatively weak, non-subtype-selective ligand whose clinical effect depends on being given chronically and on tissue context rather than on high intrinsic affinity (Evidence-in vitro, Kuiper 1997).

Signalling cascade. Mechanism is INDIRECT and centrally driven (Established). In the hypothalamus, circulating estradiol (aromatized from testosterone) normally binds ERalpha on/around kisspeptin (KNDy) and GnRH-regulating neurons, providing NEGATIVE FEEDBACK that restrains GnRH pulse frequency. Enclomiphene occupies hypothalamic ERalpha and acts as an ANTAGONIST, blocking estradiol's genomic feedback signal (ER no longer drives the estrogen-response-element transcriptional program that suppresses GnRH drive). The brain 'reads' this as low estrogen, so GnRH pulsatile secretion INCREASES. Increased GnRH pulses stimulate pituitary gonadotropes (also disinhibited by ER antagonism there) to release more LH and FSH. LH drives testicular Leydig-cell testosterone synthesis (via LHCGR -> cAMP/PKA -> StAR -> steroidogenesis); FSH supports Sertoli cells and spermatogenesis. Because the drive is endogenous and upstream, intratesticular testosterone and sperm production are PRESERVED (unlike exogenous testosterone, which suppresses the axis). The zuclomiphene isomer simultaneously exerts weak peripheral/central estrogenic AGONISM and, due to its very long residence, accumulates with repeated dosing — this partially offsets and complicates the 'clean antagonist' picture and is the rationale for developing purified enclomiphene. (Established for the antagonist-feedback loop; Evidence-human for net LH/FSH/T rise; mechanistic kisspeptin detail is Evidence-rodent/inferred.)

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral (only marketed form; tablets, citrate salt). No ester/prodrug — clomiphene is not a prodrug (ChEMBL prodrug=0); it is administered as the active racemate.
Absorption / bioavailability
Readily absorbed orally; quantitative absolute bioavailability in humans is not well characterized (Evidence-human, incomplete). Highly lipophilic (ALogP ~6.6), which drives extensive tissue distribution and enterohepatic recirculation. Tmax differs by isomer: in PCOS patients after a single 50 mg oral dose, enclomiphene Tmax ~3 h vs zuclomiphene Tmax ~7 h; single-dose Cmax ~15 ng/mL for each isomer, but AUC was ~20x larger for zuclomiphene (Zu AUC0-456h ~1289 ng/mL·h vs En AUC0-72h ~65 ng/mL·h) reflecting Zu's far slower elimination (Evidence-human, Ghobadi 2009 PMID 19033451).
Half-life
Markedly isomer-divergent. Enclomiphene is comparatively short-lived (cleared within days; in men, enclomiphene effects on LH/T persisted ~1 week after stopping — Wiehle 2013). Zuclomiphene has a VERY long terminal half-life (on the order of weeks; classic estimates ~1 month) with a 'flat terminal phase' such that conventional compartmental half-life could not be reliably fit (Evidence-human, Ghobadi 2009). Consequence: with repeated/cyclic dosing, zuclomiphene ACCUMULATES — day-3 zuclomiphene was undetectable in cycle 1 but rose progressively across ~3 consecutive monthly cycles then plateaued, combined En+Zu peaking ~100 nmol/L (Evidence-human, Young 1999 PMID 10202872).
Metabolism
Hepatic. Clomiphene undergoes oxidative/CYP-mediated metabolism (CYP enzymes including CYP2D6/CYP3A-type oxidation and N-deethylation are implicated) and is subject to enterohepatic recirculation; specific isozyme attribution is incompletely mapped in the public literature (Evidence-human, partial — treat exact CYP assignments as uncertain).
Excretion
Primarily biliary/fecal elimination with enterohepatic recycling; renal contribution is minor. The long zuclomiphene tail reflects deep lipophilic tissue sequestration and slow release rather than rapid renal clearance (Evidence-human, Ghobadi 2009).
Notes
No BBB problem for intended action — clomiphene readily reaches hypothalamic/pituitary ER (its central action is the therapeutic mechanism). The key PK story is the two-isomer split: enclomiphene = the short, antagonist 'active' driver; zuclomiphene = long-lived, accumulating, weakly estrogen-agonist 'contaminant' implicated in lingering and estrogenic side effects. This isomer asymmetry is the entire rationale for purified enclomiphene (Androxal) development.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

HUMAN (men, hypogonadism — best data): In a randomized phase II PK/PD study of secondary-hypogonadal men, oral enclomiphene citrate at 6.25, 12.5, and 25 mg/day all raised total testosterone, with 25 mg/day giving mean Day-42 morning TT 604 +/- 160 ng/dL — comparable to transdermal testosterone gel (500 +/- 278 ng/dL) but, unlike gel, with INCREASED LH and FSH (gel suppressed them) (Evidence-human, Wiehle 2013 PMID 23875626). A phase II RCT confirmed enclomiphene normalized TT and LH while preserving sperm counts, whereas T-gel suppressed gonadotropins and risked oligospermia (Evidence-human, Wiehle 2014 PMID 25044085). Across RCTs, pooled SERM therapy (clomiphene/enclomiphene) raised TT by a weighted mean ~+274 ng/dL, LH ~+4.7 IU/L, FSH ~+4.6 IU/L vs placebo (Evidence-human, meta-analysis Hohl 2025 PMID 41066380). For racemic clomiphene in men, typical studied exposures cluster in a low daily-to-alternate-day oral range, but a rigorous human DOSE-RESPONSE curve for racemic clomiphene in men is NOT well established (most male data are observational/uncontrolled) — this is a genuine gap. WOMEN (ovulation induction, the original indication): doses were titrated 50–150 mg/day for 5 days per cycle to the minimum effective ovulation-inducing dose; combined isomer plasma peaked ~100 nmol/L in practice (Evidence-human, Young 1999 PMID 10202872). Do not infer dosing protocols from these figures — magnitudes are reported only to characterize the exposure–effect relationship.

How it compares

  • vs Enclomiphene citrate (Androxal) — the purified trans-isomer
    same: Same molecular target (hypothalamic/pituitary ERalpha antagonism), same net effect: raise GnRH -> LH/FSH -> endogenous testosterone while preserving spermatogenesis; both oral SERMs.
    different: Enclomiphene is the SINGLE active antagonist isomer with short half-life and no long-lived estrogen-agonist contaminant; racemic clomiphene also delivers ZUCLOMIPHENE (long half-life, accumulating, weak estrogen AGONIST) thought to drive lingering/estrogenic side effects. Enclomiphene was developed specifically to give a cleaner, more predictable HPG-stimulating profile.
  • vs Tamoxifen (also a triphenylethylene SERM)
    same: Same SERM class and chemotype; both antagonize ER in some tissues and can be used off-label to stimulate the male HPG axis and to treat/prevent gynecomastia.
    different: Tamoxifen is a PRODRUG activated to 4-OH-tamoxifen/endoxifen (much higher ER affinity than clomiphene; CYP2D6-dependent activation), has a well-defined breast-tissue antagonism profile and oncology indication; clomiphene's defining feature is its two-isomer mix and primary use in ovulation induction / male HPG stimulation.
  • vs Aromatase inhibitors (e.g., anastrozole, letrozole)
    same: Both can raise endogenous testosterone by removing estrogenic negative feedback on the HPG axis; both used off-label for male hypogonadism/fertility.
    different: Different mechanism POINT: AIs cut estrogen SYNTHESIS (block aromatase, lowering systemic E2) whereas clomiphene blocks the estrogen RECEPTOR centrally while E2 actually RISES. AIs lower E2 (risking bone/lipid effects from estrogen deficiency); clomiphene preserves/raises E2.
  • vs hCG (human chorionic gonadotropin)
    same: Both used to raise endogenous testosterone and preserve testicular function/fertility versus exogenous testosterone.
    different: hCG acts DOWNSTREAM as an LH-receptor agonist directly on Leydig cells (bypasses the hypothalamus/pituitary), is injectable, and does not raise FSH; clomiphene acts UPSTREAM (central ER antagonism), is oral, and raises both LH and FSH. In meta-analysis, SERM (and SERM+hCG) gave higher LH/FSH than hCG alone.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Andarine counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    Same axis-opposing mechanism as enclomiphene: clomiphene antagonizes hypothalamic/pituitary estrogen receptors, lifts negative feedback, and raises LH/FSH to restore the endogenous testosterone that andarine's pituitary AR agonism had suppressed. It works in the opposite direction on the same LH/FSH -> testosterone circuit. andarine-s-4.json (HPG suppression); clomiphene-citrate-clomid.json (ERalpha antagonist restarts LH/FSH; visual side effect its own class effect); PMID 26496621 Watch: LH, FSH, total testosterone and estradiol to confirm recovery. Separately, clomiphene has its own documented visual side effects (blurring, floaters, scotomata) that are a stop-and-refer signal and are mechanistically distinct from andarine's separate, anecdote-only visual reports. Harm-reduction context, not a dosing or PCT instruction.
  • Ligandrol counteracts ↓ Established
    Ligandrol suppresses the HPTA by mimicking androgen feedback at the hypothalamus/pituitary, driving down LH, FSH and endogenous testosterone. Clomiphene is a SERM that BLOCKS estrogen-receptor-alpha feedback in the hypothalamus, which the brain reads as low estrogen and responds to by INCREASING GnRH-driven LH/FSH output — pushing the testes to resume testosterone production. Mechanistically it opposes/counteracts the axis shutdown Ligandrol causes; this is the rationale for using a SERM to restart the axis after SARM exposure. It counteracts the suppression, not the AR effect itself, and works only once the suppressing compound is cleared. clomiphene-citrate-clomid.json (ERalpha antagonist in hypothalamus, raises LH/FSH/T, raises E2); PMID 35783920 (agents to restart suppressed HPGA); PMID 22459616 (LGD suppresses LH/FSH/T) Watch: LH, FSH, total testosterone and estradiol to confirm axis recovery; clomiphene raises E2, so watch for estrogenic effects and mood/visual disturbance. Estrogen is needed for LH signalling, so do not layer an aromatase inhibitor blindly during recovery.
  • Anastrozole opposes Evidence-human
    These two push estradiol in OPPOSITE directions on the same testosterone->E2 axis. Clomiphene blocks the brain's estrogen receptor, raising LH/FSH and testosterone; the extra testosterone is aromatized, so on clomiphene serum E2 typically RISES (documented in clomiphene/enclomiphene trials). Anastrozole instead blocks aromatase (CYP19A1), cutting E2 SYNTHESIS, so it LOWERS E2. Layered together, anastrozole counteracts the E2 rise clomiphene produces. They share a directional goal of raising the testosterone:estrogen ratio (clomiphene centrally, anastrozole by removing E2 feedback), but their effect on estradiol itself is antagonistic — and crashing E2 with an AI on top removes the very estrogen signal that supports bone, lipids, libido and mood. Clomiphene monograph (E2 rises on clomiphene) + anastrozole monograph (CYP19A1 inhibition lowers E2 ~80-90%); MoA card 'Aromatase [CYP19A1]' (U-shaped E2 risk); head-to-head clomiphene vs anastrozole in hypogonadal men, PMID 26176805 Watch: Track estradiol (sensitive assay) alongside total/free testosterone and LH/FSH. Watch for signs of crashed estrogen when both are used — joint pain/dryness, low libido, low mood, worsening lipids — since E2 has a U-shaped risk curve and 'more AI = safer' is wrong.
  • Boldenone undecylenate opposes Evidence-human
    Boldenone suppresses the HPG axis (lowers LH/FSH and endogenous/intratesticular testosterone) via AR negative feedback. Clomiphene is a SERM that antagonizes estrogen feedback at the hypothalamus/pituitary to raise GnRH -> LH/FSH -> endogenous testosterone, driving the axis in the OPPOSITE direction to boldenone's shutdown (the basis for its use to restart the axis after AAS). A secondary caveat: because clomiphene RAISES estradiol (more testosterone gives aromatase more substrate, plus the zuclomiphene isomer is weakly estrogenic), it also moves estrogen opposite to an aromatase inhibitor, so a SERM and an AI pull estrogen against each other if both are layered on an EQ regimen. Clomiphene monograph (central ER antagonism raises LH/FSH/T, raises E2; PCT restart after AAS); EQ monograph side_effect_mechanisms (HPG suppression, PMID 33126548) Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone (should rise if clomiphene is opposing EQ's suppression) and estradiol (clomiphene pushes E2 up); do not stack a SERM and an AI blindly since they oppose on estrogen. Watch for visual disturbance and mood changes reported with SERMs.
  • Dianabol opposes Evidence-human
    Two opposite estrogen strategies collide. Clomiphene is a SERM that ANTAGONIZES estrogen receptors centrally (raising LH/FSH to restart a Dbol-suppressed HPG axis) and can protect breast-tissue ER, but it does NOT lower systemic estrogen the way an AI does: in trials estradiol actually RISES on clomiphene, and its zuclomiphene isomer is a weak peripheral estrogen agonist that accumulates over weeks. So during an aromatizing oral it does nothing to reduce, and can add to, the systemic estrogen load Dbol is already generating. Its axis-restart job is real and belongs after cessation, not as estrogen control alongside Dianabol - the opposition is that it raises E2 while an AI approach lowers it. Dbol monograph mechanism_steps 6-7,13 (estrogenic effects + HPG shutdown); clomiphene-citrate-clomid.json (central ERa antagonist, E2 RISES on treatment per PMID 23875626, zuclomiphene weak estrogen agonist that accumulates); corpus MoA card 'ISSUE: Gynecomastia' (AI vs SERM distinction) Watch: LH/FSH and total/free testosterone (recovery context) plus estradiol (expect it to rise, not fall, on clomiphene); breast tenderness/gyno signs, since a SERM protects breast ER but does not lower systemic E2; any visual disturbance (blurring, floaters, scotomata) is a stop-and-refer clomiphene class effect.
  • Exemestane opposes Established
    Clomiphene is a SERM that antagonizes hypothalamic/pituitary ERα, lifting negative feedback to raise LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone — and because more testosterone means more aromatizable substrate, it characteristically RAISES estradiol. Exemestane drives E2 in the opposite direction by irreversibly destroying aromatase, so the two act antagonistically on estrogen. Beyond the direct opposition, an SERM axis-restart partly relies on adequate estrogen signaling for bone/lipids, so adding an AI can blunt the very estrogen the strategy depends on and make over-correction easy. Clomiphene monograph mechanism_steps (ERα antagonism → ↑LH/FSH → ↑T; T→E2); Exemestane monograph mechanism_steps 8; ped-ancillaries reference (crashing E2 during recovery harms lipids/bone); Aromatase MoA card Watch: Estradiol plus LH/FSH and total testosterone; because both drugs move E2 in opposite directions, over-suppression is easy — confirm E2 is genuinely elevated before combining, and watch libido, mood and bone-relevant symptoms.
  • Letrozole opposes Established
    These move serum estradiol in OPPOSITE directions. Clomiphene is a SERM that blocks estrogen receptors in the hypothalamus/pituitary, releasing negative feedback so LH/FSH and testosterone rise — and the extra testosterone aromatizes, so clomiphene RAISES circulating E2 (and its zuclomiphene isomer is itself weakly estrogenic). Letrozole instead LOWERS E2 by blocking its synthesis. Both raise the testosterone-to-estrogen ratio and both lift HPG drive, but their effect on the estradiol level itself is antagonistic — a key point since crashing E2 removes exactly the estrogen a SERM leaves circulating for bone/lipids/libido. clomiphene-citrate-clomid.json side_effect 'gynecomastia' (E2 rises on clomiphene, PMID 23875626) & comparison vs AIs; letrozole.json expand.comparisons (SERMs 'can even raise' E2) Watch: Sensitive serum estradiol plus LH/FSH and total/free testosterone to see the net; watch for crashed-E2 symptoms (joint pain, low libido, mood) if an AI is layered onto a SERM's already-favorable T:E2 ratio, since the combination can over-suppress estrogen.
  • Testosterone opposes Established
    These work against each other on the HPG axis. Clomiphene's entire mechanism is to DISINHIBIT the brain so it drives its OWN LH/FSH and testosterone. Exogenous testosterone (and its aromatized estradiol) does the opposite — it feeds negative feedback back onto the hypothalamus/pituitary, SUPPRESSING GnRH/LH/FSH and shutting the testes down. While exogenous T is present, its estrogen occupies the feedback loop clomiphene is trying to blind the brain to, and it adds direct androgen feedback the SERM cannot block. Clomiphene works UPSTREAM and only if the pituitary/testes are intact and not overridden — so on active TRT it cannot 'restart' the axis; it is instead used AFTER T is stopped, to reverse steroid-induced hypogonadism. Clomiphene monograph caveats/mechanism_steps (works upstream, needs intact un-overridden axis) + testosterone monograph (exogenous T suppresses HPG axis, lowers LH/FSH); case report of clomiphene reversing steroid-induced hypogonadism after cessation, PMID 12524089 Watch: LH and FSH are the tell-tale labs: on suppressive testosterone they stay low despite clomiphene (axis overridden); a genuine SERM-driven restart shows LH/FSH climbing once exogenous androgen has cleared. Track total/free testosterone and estradiol; confirm the axis is actually responding before assuming clomiphene is 'working'.
  • RAD-140 interacts Evidence-human
    RAD-140 is a suppressive androgen-receptor agonist: it activates ARs and thereby drives HPG negative feedback, shutting down endogenous LH/FSH and testosterone (HPTA suppression, confirmed in its monograph). Clomiphene is the SERM used to RESTART that suppressed axis afterward — its central ER antagonism only works once the suppressing SARM has cleared and the pituitary/testes can respond. The relationship is sequential/conditional: RAD-140 creates the suppression that clomiphene is later used to reverse, but while RAD-140 is still acting the axis stays suppressed and clomiphene cannot overcome the ongoing androgen feedback. This is the mechanistic basis of SERM-based recovery after SARMs. RAD-140 monograph (AR agonist, suppresses HPG axis; human case reports of hypogonadism) + clomiphene monograph caveats (upstream action, needs intact un-suppressed axis); PMC OA AAS/PCT review (SARMs/AAS suppress HPTA; SERMs incl. clomiphene used to restore) [corpus textbook]; case report PMID 12524089 Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone before and during recovery — rising LH/FSH indicates the axis is responding once the SARM clears; persistently flat gonadotropins suggest continued suppression or an axis that cannot respond. Baseline vs recovery bloodwork is the objective check.
  • S-23 interacts Evidence-rodent
    S-23 is a high-affinity androgen-receptor agonist noted for STRONG HPTA suppression — it drives androgen negative feedback that switches off endogenous LH/FSH and testosterone (studied as a male-contraceptive concept for exactly this shutdown). Clomiphene is the upstream SERM used to restart the axis after such suppression; it can only raise LH/FSH/T once the suppressing androgen is gone and the pituitary/testes are free to respond. While S-23 is present, the axis stays clamped and clomiphene's central disinhibition is overridden by the ongoing androgen feedback. Note: S-23's suppression is characterized in rodents (no human data), while the clomiphene-restart side is human — hence the mixed certainty. S-23 monograph (strong AR agonist, strong HPG suppression, male-contraceptive candidate — Evidence-rodent) + clomiphene monograph caveats (needs intact, un-suppressed axis); PMC OA AAS/PCT review (androgen suppression of HPTA; SERM restart) [corpus textbook] Watch: LH/FSH and total/free testosterone across cessation and recovery — gonadotropins should climb as S-23 clears if the axis is intact; flat LH/FSH signals continued suppression. Objective before/after bloodwork rather than symptoms alone.
  • Cabergoline redundant Established
    Complementary, NON-overlapping pairing that does not share a target: clomiphene (a SERM) antagonizes estrogen receptor alpha in the hypothalamus/pituitary, raising LH/FSH/testosterone and blocking estrogen at breast tissue (the estrogenic and HPTA arms); cabergoline lowers prolactin via pituitary D2. Together they can cover the estrogen + prolactin arms of 19-nor gyno, but they act on different receptors and outputs and neither potentiates or opposes the other pharmacologically. A key interpretation point: clomiphene RAISES systemic estradiol while relieving breast tissue at the receptor, so a SERM does not lower estrogen the way an AI does. (Reclassified from 'shares-target': the two do NOT share a molecular target — ERalpha vs D2.) cabergoline.json comparisons + monitoring; clomiphene monograph (ERalpha hypothalamus/pituitary, raises LH/FSH/T; zuclomiphene estrogenic); corpus MoA 'ISSUE: Gynecomastia' (SERM vs cabergoline for different arms) Watch: Prolactin (cabergoline target) and estradiol/LH/FSH (clomiphene effects). Recognize that clomiphene will push E2 UP while relieving breast tissue at the receptor — do not misread rising E2 as treatment failure. Watch clomiphene's visual/mood effects and cabergoline's dopaminergic AEs separately.
  • Enclomiphene redundant Established
    Enclomiphene IS the purified active trans-isomer of clomiphene — the exact molecule doing clomiphene's work. Commercial clomiphene is roughly a 50:50 mix of enclomiphene (the potent, shorter-acting ER antagonist that raises LH/FSH/T) plus zuclomiphene (a long-lived, weakly estrogenic isomer). Taking enclomiphene alongside clomiphene is pharmacologically redundant: they occupy the same hypothalamic/pituitary ERalpha, drive the same GnRH->LH/FSH->testosterone cascade, and stack the same ER-antagonist effect with no new mechanism — just more of the same target engagement, plus (from the clomiphene) the extra slow-accumulating zuclomiphene estrogenic load that pure enclomiphene avoids. Clomiphene and enclomiphene monographs (enclomiphene = purified trans-isomer of clomiphene, same ERalpha target and same GnRH->LH/FSH->T cascade); IDrugs review of enclomiphene as ER antagonist, PMID 19204885 Watch: LH/FSH, total/free testosterone, estradiol; there is no additive mechanism to expect beyond a single agent, and clomiphene adds slow-accumulating zuclomiphene (estrogenic) — watch for estrogenic and clomiphene-specific visual side effects that track the clomiphene component.
  • Human chorionic gonadotropin redundant Established
    Both aim to raise testosterone and preserve testicular function/fertility, but at OPPOSITE ends of the HPG axis. Clomiphene is a SERM that blocks estrogen negative feedback at the hypothalamus, raising the body's own LH and FSH — it therefore REQUIRES an intact, responsive pituitary and testis. hCG works downstream, mimicking LH directly at the Leydig-cell LHCGR, so it works even when the pituitary is suppressed or absent (e.g., on TRT). The overlapping endpoint (drive Leydig cells, raise T, keep testes active) makes them partly redundant; stacking both drives Leydig cells from two directions (endogenous LH plus an LH-mimic), compounding testosterone and, via aromatization, estradiol — to which clomiphene's own mixed-estrogenic isomer adds. hCG monograph comparisons (downstream LHCGR vs upstream SERM, intact-axis requirement); clomiphene monograph (raises LH/FSH and endogenous T, preserves ITT/testes) Watch: Testosterone (additive over-shoot), estradiol (both can raise it — gynecomastia/mood/fluid), LH/FSH (to gauge whether central stimulation is even working), and hematocrit.

History & legal status

"Developed in the early 1960s (research codes MER-41 / MRL-41 / NSC-35770; USAN assigned 1964). FDA-approved 1967 for OVULATION INDUCTION in anovulatory infertile women (trade names Clomid, Serophene, Milophene, Clomifene/Clostilbegyt internationally); approved indication remains female infertility (ChEMBL max_phase 4, first approval 1967; no black-box warning, not withdrawn). Use in MEN (hypogonadism, idiopathic male infertility, post-cycle HPG restoration) is widespread but OFF-LABEL — there is no FDA-approved male indication for racemic clomiphene. The purified enclomiphene isomer (Androxal, Repros Therapeutics) advanced through phase II/III for male secondary hypogonadism in the 2010s but did not secure full FDA approval at that time, though it has since become widely available through compounding/telehealth channels. Legally, clomiphene is a prescription drug (not a controlled substance). In sport it is BANNED at all times by WADA (Prohibited List, class S4 'Hormone and Metabolic Modulators') because athletes use it to restart endogenous testosterone after anabolic-steroid cycles and to mask/counter HPG suppression."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Quantitative human ER binding constants (Ki/EC50) for the SEPARATED enclomiphene vs zuclomiphene isomers at ERalpha and ERbeta are not well established publicly — the magnitude of zuclomiphene's agonism vs enclomiphene's antagonism is inferred, not precisely measured.
  • Long-term (multi-year) efficacy and safety of clomiphene/enclomiphene for male hypogonadism — including cardiovascular, bone-density, and prostate outcomes — are largely unstudied; most male data are short (weeks–months) and observational.
  • Whether zuclomiphene accumulation (long half-life, multi-cycle build-up) meaningfully drives the side-effect profile (mood, visual, estrogenic) in chronically dosed men, versus being clinically negligible at the ~100 nmol/L combined levels seen.
  • The exact CYP isozyme map and metabolite activity for each isomer in humans (and how CYP2D6/3A polymorphisms affect response) is incompletely characterized.
  • Mechanism and true incidence of the rare severe ocular events (CRVO, persistent visual phenomena) — causal pathway, susceptible subpopulations, and whether it is an estrogenic-prothrombotic vs direct retinal effect remain unresolved (Contested).
  • Real-world dose–response and durability of testosterone restoration with RACEMIC clomiphene in men (as opposed to purified enclomiphene) lack rigorous RCT characterization; high heterogeneity (I2 up to ~89%) in the existing meta-analysis limits firm effect estimates.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring (NOT dosing guidance). Bloodwork before and during use: total and free testosterone plus LH and FSH (confirm the axis is responding — LH/FSH should RISE, distinguishing a working central SERM from exogenous-testosterone suppression); estradiol (E2 typically RISES on clomiphene — watch for estrogenic symptoms like breast tenderness); a baseline semen analysis if fertility is a concern (clomiphene aims to preserve sperm, unlike testosterone). Track IGF-1 (enclomiphene lowered it in trials), a lipid panel, and hematocrit/CBC. Clinical signs to watch and act on: any VISUAL disturbance (blurring, floaters, light streaks, scotomata) is a stop-and-refer signal — discontinue and obtain ophthalmologic evaluation, especially given rare retinal vein occlusion reports; new or worsening MOOD changes, irritability, anxiety or exacerbation of pre-existing psychiatric illness; gynecomastia/breast tenderness; headache, dizziness, or hot-flash-like vasomotor symptoms. Higher vigilance in anyone with thrombosis risk factors (the estrogenic isomer is a theoretical prothrombotic contributor). Because zuclomiphene accumulates over repeated cycles, effects (and side effects) can lag and linger after stopping — symptoms persisting for weeks after discontinuation are consistent with the long-lived isomer, not necessarily a new problem."

Caveats

Clomiphene works UPSTREAM at the brain, not directly on the testes — so it only works if the pituitary and testes are intact and capable of responding; in true secondary (hypothalamic-pituitary failure) hypogonadism it can fail (e.g., gonadotropin-deficient men show no LH/FSH rise — PMID 11344554). Distinguish from two other classes the audience may confuse it with: aromatase inhibitors (anastrozole/letrozole) cut estrogen SYNTHESIS rather than block its receptor, and hCG mimics LH directly at the testes — different points in the same loop. The drug is a SERM, so it is an ER ANTAGONIST in the brain but can act as an estrogen-like AGONIST in other tissues (the zuclomiphene isomer especially) — this tissue selectivity, plus the slow accumulation of zuclomiphene over weeks, explains its estrogenic side effects and is why purified enclomiphene was developed. Magnitude claims (e.g., 'doubling testosterone') are population averages from meta-analyses, not guarantees; visual disturbance is a recognized class effect of clomiphene specifically.
