LGD-3303

Ligand's bone/muscle SARM — a potent, more-androgenic-reputed cousin of LGD-4033 that never left the rat lab, so every human claim is extrapolation.

Rodent / preclinical 8 citations
Class
Nonsteroidal selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM); pyrrolo-quinolinone chemotype (Ligand Pharmaceuticals)
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR / NR3C4) — full agonist on the anabolic (muscle/bone) program, partial agonist on classic androgenic tissues (prostate, preputial gland)
Human status
No human clinical data. LGD-3303 was a preclinical Ligand Pharmaceuticals osteoporosis/muscle-wasting SARM characterized only in rodents (and later, for doping-control metabolism, in horses and in vitro). A ClinicalTrials.gov search returns zero registered trials (verified: 0 records), and there are no published human efficacy, safety, pharmacokinetic, or endocrine studies. It never advanced to approval and is not a medicine anywhere. It is nonetheless sold on the gray market as a research chemical and is explicitly prohibited in human and equine sport (WADA-class anabolic agent). Every claim about how it behaves in people — dose, potency, suppression severity, side effects — is extrapolation from rat data plus unverified user anecdote, not human evidence.

At a glance

  • Increases skeletal-muscle mass (drove levator ani muscle above intact/eugonadal levels in castrated rats) — rodent
  • Increases bone mineral density and content at cortical and cancellous sites; adds new periosteal bone — rodent (osteoporosis rationale)
  • Additive with the bisphosphonate alendronate for bone — rodent
  • Only partially stimulates prostate (never above ~50% of intact) — the tissue-selective signature — rodent
  • Nonsteroidal and nonaromatizable: does not itself generate estradiol (no DHT metabolite inferred from the non-steroid scaffold)
  • Crosses the blood-brain barrier; produces AR-dependent (flutamide-blockable) sexual/behavioral effects — rodent
  • Expected to suppress the HPG axis (own testosterone, LH/FSH) — established AR-class effect; community-reputed as strongly suppressive but no human LGD-3303 data
  • No demonstrated human efficacy or safety — every human 'benefit' is extrapolation from rat data

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Binds the androgen receptor directly and selectively Established

    LGD-3303 is a small nonsteroidal molecule (9-chloro-2-ethyl-1-methyl-3-(2,2,2-trifluoroethyl)-3H-pyrrolo-[3,2-f]quinolin-7(6H)-one) that binds the androgen receptor (AR) with high potency. In competitive binding and transcriptional-reporter assays it behaved as a potent AR agonist with little or no cross-reactivity against related nuclear receptors (e.g. progesterone/glucocorticoid receptors) — so its actions run through AR, not off-target steroid receptors. Vajda 2009 J Bone Miner Res, PMID 18847323, DOI 10.1359/jbmr.081007 (in vitro binding/transcription)

  2. Acts as a full agonist on muscle but only a partial agonist on prostate Evidence-rodent

    This is the defining SARM behavior. In orchidectomized (castrated) male rats, oral LGD-3303 drove the levator ani muscle ABOVE eugonadal (intact) levels, yet on the ventral prostate it never rose above ~50% of eugonadal weight even at high doses — i.e. it maxes out as a partial agonist in classic androgenic tissue. That split (full anabolic agonist, partial androgenic agonist) is what 'tissue-selective' means here. Vajda 2009 J Bone Miner Res, PMID 18847323 (prostate <50% eugonadal); Vajda 2009 J Pharmacol Exp Ther, PMID 19017848, DOI 10.1124/jpet.108.146811 (partial agonist on prostate/preputial gland)

  3. Selectivity comes from the receptor, not from where the drug goes Evidence-rodent

    A common assumption is that SARMs are muscle-selective because they concentrate in muscle. LGD-3303 disproves that for itself: measured drug concentrations were actually HIGHER in prostate than in levator ani muscle, and the muscle-over-prostate selectivity held whether the drug was given orally (spiky levels) or by continuous infusion (flat levels). The authors conclude selectivity is driven by altered molecular interactions at the AR itself, not by tissue distribution or pharmacokinetics. Vajda 2009 J Pharmacol Exp Ther, PMID 19017848, DOI 10.1124/jpet.108.146811

  4. AR activation transcribes the anabolic gene program in muscle and bone Established

    Once LGD-3303 activates AR, the receptor translocates to the nucleus and turns on androgen-responsive genes that drive skeletal-muscle protein accretion (hypertrophy) and bone formation. This is the same core anabolic transcriptional mechanism as testosterone/DHT acting on AR — LGD-3303 just biases it toward muscle/bone. (The general AR->nuclear-translocation->transcription cascade is textbook AR biology; the muscle/bone bias for LGD-3303 specifically is the rodent finding.) Canonical androgen-receptor transcriptional biology (textbook AR MoA); LGD-3303 muscle/bone anabolic output demonstrated in Vajda 2009, PMID 18847323

  5. Builds cortical and cancellous bone — including new bone on the outer (periosteal) surface Evidence-rodent

    In ovariectomized (estrogen-deficient, osteopenic) female rats, LGD-3303 raised bone mineral density and bone mineral content at both cortical and cancellous sites. Part of the cortical gain came from anabolic activity on the periosteal surface (laying down new bone on the outside of the shaft). Combined with the bisphosphonate alendronate the effects were additive — antiresorptive plus anabolic — which was the intended osteoporosis rationale. Vajda 2009 J Bone Miner Res, PMID 18847323, DOI 10.1359/jbmr.081007

  6. Nonsteroidal and nonaromatizable — does not itself generate estrogen Established

    LGD-3303 is a nonsteroidal, nonaromatizable AR ligand. Unlike testosterone it is not a substrate for aromatase, so it does not itself generate estradiol; its downstream effects are whatever the parent molecule does at AR, not the effects of an estrogenic metabolite. Because it is nonsteroidal it is also not a 5-alpha-reductase substrate (no DHT metabolite) — this follows from its non-steroid scaffold rather than from a direct metabolic measurement. Kudwa 2010 Endocrinology, PMID 20392832, DOI 10.1210/en.2009-1289 (states nonsteroidal, nonaromatizable); non-DHT status is a structural inference from the nonsteroidal scaffold, not a measured 5-alpha-reduction assay

  7. Crosses the blood-brain barrier and engages AR in the CNS Evidence-rodent

    LGD-3303 effectively crosses the blood-brain barrier and produces centrally-mediated, androgen-dependent behavioral changes in rats — it altered male-directed sexual preference, proceptive and lordosis behavior in female rats, and those effects were blocked by the AR antagonist flutamide, confirming they are AR-driven. Practical read-across: CNS AR engagement is the plausible substrate for reported mood/libido changes, but the specific human behavioral effects are not established. Kudwa 2010 Endocrinology, PMID 20392832, DOI 10.1210/en.2009-1289

  8. Still suppresses the HPG axis — the 'selectivity' does not spare your own hormones Established

    Like all AR agonists, systemic androgen signaling provides negative feedback to the hypothalamus and pituitary (lowering GnRH -> LH/FSH), which suppresses the body's own testosterone production and, over time, can shrink the testes and impair fertility. Tissue-selectivity in muscle/bone does NOT make a SARM axis-sparing. Direct human HPG data for LGD-3303 do not exist, so this tag reflects the established AR-class feedback mechanism (and human Phase I data for related SARMs such as LGD-4033) rather than a compound-specific human measurement; the community claim that LGD-3303 is 'more suppressive' than LGD-4033 is uncontrolled anecdote. Established AR/HPG negative-feedback biology (textbook); human dose-dependent testosterone suppression demonstrated for the related SARM LGD-4033 (Basaria 2013, PMID 22459616) — no human LGD-3303 endocrine data

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Potent nonsteroidal androgen receptor (AR/NR3C4) agonist with little or no cross-reactivity against related nuclear receptors in competitive-binding and transcriptional-reporter assays. Functionally a FULL agonist in anabolic tissue (levator ani muscle, bone) but a PARTIAL agonist in classic androgenic tissue (ventral prostate, preputial gland), with prostate response capped near ~50% of eugonadal even at high exposure. Nonsteroidal and nonaromatizable, so it produces no estradiol of its own (and, as a non-steroid, no DHT metabolite).

Signalling cascade. AR binding -> ligand-specific receptor conformation and differential coregulator recruitment -> nuclear translocation -> transcription of androgen-responsive anabolic genes in muscle and bone. The muscle-vs-prostate split is driven at the receptor level, NOT by tissue drug distribution — measured tissue concentrations were higher in prostate than muscle, and selectivity was preserved under both oral (peaky) and continuous-infusion (flat) exposure.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Orally bioavailable in rats (active by daily oral gavage); characterized both orally and by continuous subcutaneous infusion in rodent PK/PD work. No human PK exists.
Absorption / bioavailability
Orally active in rodents; quantitative human oral bioavailability unknown. Behavioral CNS effects in rats confirm meaningful systemic and central exposure.
Half-life
Not established in humans. The equine study reported a metabolite detection window out to ~96 h, but that reflects assay sensitivity and study sampling, not a human plasma half-life.
Metabolism
Hepatic phase I/II metabolism. Equine in vivo profiling tentatively identified eight metabolites (one carboxylated, several hydroxylated) plus glucuronic-acid conjugates; equine in vitro (liver microsome) work identified four phase I metabolites. A monohydroxylated metabolite (after beta-glucuronidase hydrolysis) is proposed as the best doping-control marker due to intensity and prolonged detection. Human-specific metabolic pathways are not published but are expected to be qualitatively similar (hydroxylation + glucuronidation).
Excretion
Renal/urinary and biliary elimination of hydroxylated and glucuronidated metabolites (characterized in horse urine/plasma). Human excretion kinetics and half-life unknown.
Notes
Crosses the blood-brain barrier (demonstrated by AR-dependent CNS/behavioral effects in rats). Distribution is NOT the source of muscle selectivity — prostate drug levels exceeded muscle levels.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

Rodent-only. In castrated/orchidectomized male rats, oral LGD-3303 dose-dependently increased levator ani muscle weight ABOVE intact (eugonadal) levels, while ventral prostate weight plateaued and never exceeded ~50% of eugonadal even at high doses — a classic ceiling-effect (partial-agonist) dose-response in androgenic tissue against a full-agonist response in anabolic tissue. In gonadectomized female rats, oral doses in the ~3–30 mg/kg range (per the behavioral study) produced AR-dependent behavioral effects, and muscle/bone gains were seen in the efficacy studies. These are RAT mg/kg figures and do NOT translate to a human dose — no human dose-response has ever been measured, and this monograph gives no human dosing guidance.

How it compares

  • vs LGD-4033 (ligandrol)
    same: Both are Ligand-origin oral nonsteroidal SARMs targeting AR with a muscle/bone-over-prostate anabolic bias; both prohibited in sport; both HPG-suppressive.
    different: LGD-4033 has actual human Phase I safety/PK data (Basaria 2013, PMID 22459616: dose-dependent testosterone and HDL suppression) and is far more widely used; LGD-3303 has ZERO human data and is chemically distinct (pyrrolo-quinolinone). LGD-3303 is community-reputed as more androgenic and more suppressive, but that comparison is uncontrolled anecdote, not measured.
  • vs Testosterone / anabolic-androgenic steroids
    same: Both act through the androgen receptor and both suppress the HPG axis via negative feedback.
    different: Testosterone is a steroidal full agonist that aromatizes to estradiol and is 5-alpha-reduced to DHT, and stimulates prostate fully. LGD-3303 is nonsteroidal, nonaromatizable, and only a partial prostate agonist — the intended 'cleaner androgenic profile'. But testosterone has decades of human data; LGD-3303 has none.
  • vs RAD-140 (testolone)
    same: Both potent oral nonsteroidal SARMs marketed gray-market for muscle; both suppressive; both banned in sport.
    different: RAD-140 has documented human case reports of drug-induced liver injury and more human anecdote/pharmacology; LGD-3303 has none of that and remains essentially a preclinical molecule.
  • vs S-4 (andarine) / other bone- or cutting-oriented SARMs
    same: Both are nonsteroidal SARMs acting on AR, both suppressive, both sport-banned.
    different: LGD-3303 was explicitly developed for the bone/osteoporosis indication with demonstrated periosteal/cortical anabolic action in rats and additivity with bisphosphonates; andarine (S-4) is a cutting-oriented aryl-propionamide SARM with a different (often vision-related) side-effect profile. LGD-3303's distinguishing feature is strong cortical-bone anabolism.

History & legal status

LGD-3303 was discovered and characterized by Ligand Pharmaceuticals (San Diego) in the mid-to-late 2000s as a candidate SARM for osteoporosis, frailty, and muscle wasting, within Ligand's broader androgen program. The pivotal rat efficacy and PK/PD papers appeared in 2009 (J Bone Miner Res; J Pharmacol Exp Ther), with rat behavioral pharmacology in 2010 (Endocrinology). Development did not advance into human clinical trials — a ClinicalTrials.gov search returns zero registered studies, and there are no approvals in any jurisdiction. It has since surfaced only in (1) the gray-market 'research chemical' SARM trade and (2) antidoping science: it is a WADA-prohibited anabolic agent, banned in both human and equine sport, prompting the 2021 (equine in vitro) and 2023 (equine in vivo) metabolite-profiling studies aimed at building detection assays. In short: a shelved preclinical pharma asset that found a second life as an illicit performance drug and a doping-control target.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Human pharmacokinetics: bioavailability, half-life, and the actual human dose that produces effects — entirely unknown (rat mg/kg does not translate).
  • Human efficacy: whether the rat muscle/bone gains reproduce in people, and at what magnitude, has never been tested.
  • Human safety: real-world hepatotoxicity, cardiovascular, and lipid risk for THIS compound (only class read-across exists).
  • Severity and reversibility of HPG suppression in humans — including whether it truly is 'more suppressive' than LGD-4033 as claimed.
  • Long-term prostate safety: whether the partial-agonist behavior seen in rats holds in the human prostate over time.
  • Whether gray-market 'LGD-3303' products actually contain LGD-3303 (mislabeling/substitution is common and unquantified for this specific compound).

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Framed as harm-reduction for someone who has already chosen to use — NOT an endorsement, and NO dosing guidance is given. Before use, establish baselines; during/after use, watch for the AR-class harms. Bloodwork worth tracking with a clinician: (1) HPG axis — total/free testosterone, LH, FSH (to detect suppression and gauge recovery); (2) liver — ALT, AST, bilirubin, plus symptoms of DILI (dark urine, jaundice, right-upper-quadrant pain, itching) given oral SARM hepatotoxicity risk; (3) lipids — full panel, watching for an HDL-C drop; (4) hematocrit/hemoglobin for erythrocytosis; (5) blood pressure. Because gray-market product identity/dose is unverifiable, third-party lab testing of the actual product is a key risk lever — but the safest option remains not using an untested, unapproved, sport-banned compound. Any jaundice, chest pain, severe mood change, or markedly abnormal labs warrants stopping and seeking medical care. Recovery of the axis may need clinician-guided evaluation; do not self-prescribe ancillary drugs. (No doses, cycle lengths, or titration schedules are provided.)

Caveats

All efficacy data are from rats (muscle, bone) with supporting behavioral work in rats and metabolite work in horses/in-vitro — there is NO human trial of LGD-3303, so human potency, dosing, and safety are unknown. 'Selective' is often misread as 'safe' or 'axis-sparing': LGD-3303 is a full anabolic agonist and, like every AR agonist, is expected to suppress the HPG axis; it is reputed in user communities as more androgenic and more suppressive than LGD-4033, but that specific comparison has no controlled human data. Gray-market 'LGD-3303' is unregulated: products are frequently mislabeled, underdosed, or substituted with other SARMs/steroids, so a purchased vial may not contain what the label says. SARM-class human harms documented for OTHER SARMs (LGD-4033, RAD-140) — drug-induced liver injury, HDL-cholesterol suppression, testosterone suppression, cardiovascular concerns — are the realistic risk template here even though LGD-3303 itself is untested in people. This monograph is mechanism education and harm-reduction only: it is not medical advice and contains no dosing protocol.
