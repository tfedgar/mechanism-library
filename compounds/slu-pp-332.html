SLU-PP-332

A lab-stage "exercise in a molecule" that flips on the same metabolic genes a workout does — but so far only in mice and cells.

Rodent / preclinical 11 citations
Class
Synthetic pan-ERR agonist (small-molecule activator of estrogen-related receptors α/β/γ); a candidate "exercise mimetic
Primary target
Estrogen-related receptors (ERRα, ERRβ, ERRγ) — orphan nuclear receptors
Human status
NO human data. Be blunt with the audience: there are zero published human clinical trials of SLU-PP-332 — every result (endurance, fat loss, insulin sensitivity, mitochondrial boost) comes from mice and cultured cells. The original compound also lacked oral bioavailability in those studies, so it isn't even a finished oral drug; chemists have since made follow-ups (SLU-PP-915) to fix that. This sits firmly in the 'rodent-hyped, human-unproven' bucket — closer to NMN/rapamycin-for-longevity than to creatine or beta-alanine, which have real human RCTs. It is a preclinical chemical probe / early drug candidate, not a supplement and not approved for any use. Anyone selling it for human consumption is selling a research chemical, and it is on the World Anti-Doping Agency prohibited list, so athletes using it would be doping.

At a glance

  • Enhances aerobic/endurance exercise capacity in mice (running)
  • Increases mitochondrial number and function in muscle cells
  • Shifts muscle toward oxidative type IIa fibers
  • Raises whole-body energy expenditure and fat-burning (fatty-acid oxidation)
  • Reduces fat mass and obesity in diet-induced and ob/ob mouse models
  • Improves insulin sensitivity / glycemic control in mice
  • Reproduces the 'acute aerobic exercise' transcriptional program without exercise
  • Flagged by anti-doping researchers (WADA-prohibited class) for its performance-enhancing potential

Mechanism, step by step

  1. The drug binds and switches ON the ERR receptors Established

    ERRα/β/γ are 'orphan nuclear receptors' — protein switches inside cells that control which metabolic genes get read. Most nuclear receptors wait for a natural hormone, but ERRs have no known natural ligand and sit in a low-activity default state. SLU-PP-332 is a synthetic agonist that slots in and turns them up. It activates all three but is most potent on ERRα. Billon et al., ACS Chem Biol 2023, PMID 36988910

  2. Activated ERRα triggers the 'acute aerobic exercise' gene program Evidence-rodent

    Once switched on, ERRα drives transcription of the same set of genes a real bout of aerobic exercise turns on — including markers like Ddit4 and Slc25a25. Knock out ERRα and the drug's benefit on endurance disappears, which is the key experiment showing ERRα is the gene that matters, not just a passenger. Billon et al., ACS Chem Biol 2023, PMID 36988910

  3. It ramps up mitochondria — the cell's power plants Evidence-rodent

    ERRs are master regulators of mitochondrial biogenesis and energy use (oxidative phosphorylation, the Krebs cycle, fatty-acid oxidation). In skeletal muscle cells, SLU-PP-332 increased mitochondrial function and cellular respiration — meaning the cells build and run more energy machinery, the way trained muscle does. Billon et al., ACS Chem Biol 2023, PMID 36988910

  4. Muscle shifts toward oxidative, fatigue-resistant fibers Evidence-rodent

    In mice given the drug, the proportion of type IIa oxidative muscle fibers went up. These are the mixed-fuel, endurance-leaning fibers that burn both fat and glucose and resist fatigue — exactly the kind endurance training builds. Billon et al., ACS Chem Biol 2023, PMID 36988910

  5. The body burns more fat and spends more energy Evidence-rodent

    In obese and diabetic mouse models, SLU-PP-332 increased whole-body energy expenditure and fatty-acid oxidation — the animals burned more fuel, including fat, even without exercising. This is the systemic 'exercise-like' metabolic effect playing out beyond the single muscle cell. Billon et al., J Pharmacol Exp Ther 2024, PMID 37739806

  6. Downstream payoff: less fat, better blood sugar, more endurance Evidence-rodent

    The net result in mice: reduced fat-mass accumulation, lower obesity, improved insulin sensitivity, and enhanced running endurance. A systematic review of the whole ERR-agonist field reaches the same summary — and explicitly states human clinical trials are still needed to confirm any of this. Billon et al., J Pharmacol Exp Ther 2024, PMID 37739806; de Souza-Lima et al. (systematic review), Rev Med Chil 2026, PMID 42024694

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. SLU-PP-332 is a synthetic pan-agonist of all three estrogen-related receptors (ERRalpha/ESRRA, ERRbeta/ESRRB, ERRgamma/ESRRG) - orphan nuclear receptors that are CONSTITUTIVELY active (they drive transcription without any known endogenous hormone ligand; they do NOT bind estrogen despite the name). It is an acyl-hydrazone, derived by rational optimization of the ERRbeta/gamma-selective agonist GSK4716. POTENCY (Established, in vitro): in a full-length ERR cell-based cotransfection/reporter assay, EC50 = 98 nM at ERRalpha, 230 nM at ERRbeta, 430 nM at ERRgamma (Billon 2023, PMID 36988910). So it is most potent at ERRalpha, with only modest selectivity: ~4.4-fold ERRalpha over ERRgamma and ~2.3-fold ERRalpha over ERRbeta - i.e. functionally a pan-agonist, not an alpha-selective drug. By contrast its parent GSK4716 had EC50 >5000 nM at ERRalpha. SELECTIVITY (Established, in vitro): no measurable activity at classical estrogen receptors ERalpha or ERbeta, or at other nuclear receptors tested - it is selective for the ERR subfamily. Direct binding to ERRalpha confirmed by limited-proteolysis protection and to ERRgamma by differential scanning fluorimetry. Acts as a TRUE AGONIST that further enhances the receptor's already-high constitutive activity (opposite of inverse agonists like C29 or GSK5182, which suppress it).

Signalling cascade. ERRs sit at the TOP of the mitochondrial/oxidative-metabolism transcriptional network and are the principal effectors of the coactivator PGC-1alpha. Mechanistic chain (Established for ERR biology; Evidence-rodent/in vitro for SLU-PP-332 specifically): (1) SLU-PP-332 enters the cell (lipophilic small molecule), binds the ligand-binding domain 'agonist pocket' of ERRalpha/beta/gamma near the solvent-exposed surface (structurally characterized for the GSK4716 scaffold via ERRgamma LBD crystal structure PDB 2GPP; the naphthalene group of SLU-PP-332 makes pi-pi stacking with Phe328 unique to ERRalpha, explaining its alpha potency gain). (2) Ligand stabilizes the active receptor conformation, promoting recruitment of coactivators - classically PGC-1alpha/PGC-1beta, and a recently described alternative direct recruitment of the general transcription factor TFIIH (Billon 2023). (3) ERR binds ERR-response elements (ERREs) in target-gene promoters/introns. (4) This transcriptionally drives the oxidative/mitochondrial program: oxidative phosphorylation complex genes (e.g. NDUFB8/complex I, ATP5A/complex V), cytochrome c, TCA-cycle and fatty-acid-oxidation genes, mitochondrial biogenesis (increased mtDNA copy number, mitochondrial number on EM), and the metabolic gene PDK4. (5) Critically, in skeletal muscle SLU-PP-332 reproduces the ACUTE AEROBIC EXERCISE gene signature - most prominently inducing Ddit4/REDD1 (the master driver of the acute-exercise program) and Slc25a25, plus Per1/Per2, Foxo1, Nr4a1, Alas2. A single dose raised Ddit4 ~3-fold and Slc25a25 ~11-fold in mouse muscle at 1 h, matching a 40-min treadmill run; effects were transient (gone by 3-6 h). (6) Net phenotype: more type IIa oxidative muscle fibers, higher SDH activity, increased maximal mitochondrial respiration, increased fatty-acid oxidation and whole-body energy expenditure. In muscle this acute-exercise program is ERRalpha-DEPENDENT (lost in muscle-specific ERRalpha-knockout mice); in heart the cardioprotective effect is mainly ERRgamma-dependent (Xu 2024, PMID 37961903).

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Rodent studies used INTRAPERITONEAL (i.p.) injection exclusively - SLU-PP-332 is NOT orally bioavailable, which is the single biggest limitation of the parent compound and the reason the same group developed the chemically distinct, orally active analog SLU-PP-915 (Billon 2026, PMID 41421047). No human, oral, or clinical formulation exists. It is sold by chemical suppliers as a research-grade powder; any non-clinical human use is off-label/unapproved with no PK characterization in humans. (Established for the i.p./oral distinction; Evidence-rodent.)
Absorption / bioavailability
Oral bioavailability: effectively poor/inadequate for in vivo use - explicitly described as lacking oral bioavailability (Billon 2026, PMID 41421047). After 30 mg/kg i.p. in mice, tissue distribution favored skeletal muscle (the target tissue) over plasma: at 2 h, ~0.6 microM in skeletal muscle vs ~0.2 microM in plasma (Billon 2023, PMID 36988910). Human absorption parameters are UNKNOWN.
Half-life
No formally reported terminal plasma half-life in any species. Indirect rodent data: measurable muscle/plasma levels at 2 h and 6 h after i.p. dosing, and efficacy studies used twice-daily (b.i.d.) dosing, implying a relatively short functional duration consistent with the need for repeat dosing. The downstream gene response (Ddit4, Slc25a25) is markedly TRANSIENT - induced at 1 h, largely gone by 3-6 h - mirroring acute exercise rather than the parent drug's pharmacokinetic half-life. Human half-life: UNKNOWN. (Evidence-rodent.)
Metabolism
Hepatic. In vitro metabolism characterized in human liver S9 fraction and human liver microsomes (HLM) by LC-HRMS/MS (Moller 2026, PMID 41588687): SLU-PP-332 yielded 9 metabolites total - 6 Phase-I (oxidative, CYP-mediated) and 3 Phase-II conjugates (e.g. glucuronidation/sulfation-type conjugation). A separate doping-control in vitro study independently characterized SLU-PP-332 Phase-I metabolites (Avliyakulov 2026, PMID 41688415). Specific CYP isoforms responsible were not definitively assigned in the abstracts reviewed. Note the orally active analog SLU-PP-915 produced ONLY Phase-I metabolites (7, no Phase-II conjugates) - a metabolic-stability contrast that partly explains 915's better oral exposure. (Evidence-human in vitro for metabolite identity; enzyme-level certainty: Contested/incomplete.)
Excretion
Not explicitly reported in the reviewed literature. Given hepatic Phase-I oxidation plus Phase-II conjugation, biliary/renal elimination of conjugated metabolites is the expected route, but quantitative excretion/clearance data are UNKNOWN in any species. (Speculative on route; no data.)
Notes
(1) No ester/prodrug - SLU-PP-332 is the active molecule itself; SLU-PP-915 is a separate compound, not a prodrug of 332. (2) BBB penetration: not characterized; ERRs are expressed in brain but CNS exposure of SLU-PP-332 is unstudied. (3) The compound's tissue-selective accumulation in skeletal muscle (~3x plasma) is pharmacologically favorable for an exercise mimetic. (4) WADA-relevant: because it mimics exercise/metabolic-modulator effects, both SLU-PP-332 and SLU-PP-915 are being characterized for anti-doping detection; metabolite panels now exist for urine/blood screening (Moller 2026 PMID 41588687; Avliyakulov 2026 PMID 41688415).

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

ALL quantitative dose-response data are RODENT (mouse) and in vitro; there is NO human dose-response data of any kind - no human PK, no human efficacy, no defined human dose. IN VITRO (Established): half-maximal transcriptional activation at EC50 ERRalpha 98 nM / ERRbeta 230 nM / ERRgamma 430 nM; in C2C12 muscle cells, 24 h treatment increased maximal mitochondrial respiration and mitochondrial biogenesis, and induced the ERR target gene Pdk4 (PMID 36988910). MOUSE EXERCISE/MUSCLE (Evidence-rodent): single i.p. dose ~50 mg/kg reproduced the acute-exercise gene program (Ddit4 ~3-fold, Slc25a25 ~11-fold at 1 h), matching a 40-min treadmill bout; 7-day dosing at 50 mg/kg b.i.d. i.p. increased treadmill endurance ~70% longer / ~45% farther vs vehicle and raised grip strength after ~2 weeks; a 15-day course increased oxidative type IIa fibers, SDH activity, OXPHOS complex I/V protein, cytochrome c and mtDNA. Tolerability: 50 mg/kg b.i.d. i.p. for 10 days produced no overt toxicity, normal CBC, electrolytes and no rise in creatine kinase (no skeletal-muscle damage marker). MOUSE METABOLIC (Evidence-rodent): in diet-induced-obese and ob/ob mice, SLU-PP-332 increased energy expenditure and fatty-acid oxidation, reduced fat-mass accumulation, reduced obesity and improved insulin sensitivity (PMID 37739806). MOUSE HEART FAILURE (Evidence-rodent): in pressure-overload (transverse aortic constriction) HF, SLU-PP-332 (and 915) improved ejection fraction, reduced fibrosis and improved survival WITHOUT affecting cardiac hypertrophy (PMID 37961903). MOUSE AGING KIDNEY (Evidence-rodent): 8-week treatment of 21-month-old mice reversed age-related albuminuria, podocyte loss, mitochondrial dysfunction and inflammation (PMID 37717940). HUMAN: only an ex vivo signal - SLU-PP-332 applied to cultured myoblasts from inactive women up-regulated PGC-1alpha/ERRalpha/SIRT1/FNDC5 and promoted myotube formation (pilot, n=20 split active/inactive; PMID 40692696). The human-effective dose, ceiling effects, and dose-toxicity relationship are entirely UNKNOWN.

How it compares

  • vs SLU-PP-915
    same: Same developers (Burris/Walker group); chemically a pan-ERR agonist with the same exercise-mimetic mechanism and similar efficacy (comparable endurance gains and heart-failure benefit). Both WADA-flagged exercise mimetics.
    different: 915 is a STRUCTURALLY DISTINCT scaffold that is ORALLY BIOAVAILABLE - the key advantage over 332, which requires i.p. injection. 915 produced only Phase-I metabolites (no Phase-II conjugates) i.e. different metabolic handling; it synergizes with exercise training to further boost Ddit4/mitochondrial genes. 915 is the lead being advanced for chronic/therapeutic exploration; 332 remains mainly a research probe.
  • vs GSK4716
    same: The chemical PARENT scaffold (acyl-hydrazone) from which SLU-PP-332 was derived; also an ERR agonist acting at the same agonist pocket.
    different: GSK4716 is ERRbeta/gamma-selective with essentially NO ERRalpha activity (EC50 >5000 nM at ERRalpha, 1200 nM at ERRgamma). SLU-PP-332's added naphthalene group introduced pi-pi stacking with ERRalpha Phe328, gaining ~50-fold ERRalpha potency (to 98 nM) - converting a beta/gamma tool into a pan-agonist with ERRalpha activity, which is what enables the muscle exercise-mimetic effect.
  • vs C29 and GSK5182 (ERRalpha inverse agonists)
    same: Bind the same ERR receptors/ligand-binding domain region.
    different: OPPOSITE pharmacology - these are inverse agonists that SUPPRESS ERR's constitutive activity (C29: ERRalpha IC50 ~60 nM, ~40% inhibition; GSK5182 a classic ERRgamma inverse agonist). They are explored for cancer, diabetes and neurodegeneration where you want LESS ERR activity, whereas SLU-PP-332 boosts ERR activity for exercise-mimetic/metabolic benefit. Useful as a conceptual mirror: the same receptor can be pushed either direction.
  • vs Other 'exercise mimetic' classes (e.g. PPARdelta agonists like GW501516/cardarine, AMPK activators like AICAR)
    same: Shared GOAL - pharmacologically reproduce endurance-exercise adaptations (mitochondrial biogenesis, fat oxidation, fiber-type shift) without training; all are WADA-prohibited and abused in sport.
    different: Different molecular target/node: SLU-PP-332 acts at ERR nuclear receptors (upstream of/parallel to PGC-1alpha), whereas GW501516 acts at PPARdelta and AICAR at AMPK. SLU-PP-332's distinctive signature is reproducing the ACUTE-exercise Ddit4/REDD1 program; its safety record in humans is non-existent, unlike the longer (cautionary) history of GW501516.

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • 5-Amino-1MQ shares target Speculative
    SLU-PP-332 is a pan-ERR (alpha/beta/gamma) agonist 'exercise mimetic' that upregulates mitochondrial biogenesis, oxidative muscle fibers, fatty-acid oxidation, and energy expenditure (mice only). 5-Amino-1MQ raises NAD+/SIRT1 signaling, which drives the same PGC-1alpha-type mitochondrial-biogenesis and fatty-acid-oxidation programs, and ERRalpha is a canonical PGC-1alpha/SIRT1 transcriptional partner. The two therefore converge on overlapping mitochondrial/oxidative-metabolism transcription, so their exercise-mimetic and energy-expenditure effects could be additive. Both are rodent-only research chemicals; SLU-PP-332 also showed cardiac effects at high exposure (ERRs are central in heart), so the shared metabolic push is theoretical and the combined cardiac/metabolic load is unstudied. SLU-PP-332 ERR -> FAO/energy expenditure (rodent); 5-Amino-1MQ NAD+/SIRT1/PGC-1alpha/mitochondrial axis (PMID 24717514). Convergence on PGC-1alpha/ERR programs inferred; no co-use study. Watch: Resting heart rate and any exertional cardiac symptoms (SLU-PP-332 cardiac signal), plus body composition and metabolic markers; no human data for either compound alone or combined.
  • Cardarine redundant Evidence-rodent
    SLU-PP-332 is an ERRα/β/γ agonist explicitly characterized as an exercise mimetic that 'increases oxidative muscle fibers, fatty acid oxidation, and enhances exercise endurance' and raises energy expenditure — the same phenotype Cardarine produces via PPARδ. PPARδ and ERR/PGC-1α biology are tightly interlinked upstream of mitochondrial fat-oxidation genes, so the two agents largely duplicate one another's endpoint at different receptor entry points (note SLU-PP-332 also reportedly improves glucose oxidation, whereas Cardarine shifts muscle away from glucose — the overlap is the oxidative-fiber/FAO program, not every fuel-handling detail). Both are WADA-prohibited exercise mimetics; SLU-PP-332 efficacy is mouse-only and it showed cardiac effects at high exposure, so combining two unvalidated mimetics compounds unknown risk without adding a distinct mechanism. SLU-PP-332 ERRα/β/γ exercise-mimetic FAO/oxidative-fiber and energy-expenditure profile: PMID 41688415. Cardarine PPARδ FAO program: monograph; Narkar 2008 PMID 18674809. Watch: Resting heart rate and any exertional cardiac symptoms (SLU-PP-332 carries a high-exposure cardiac signal), fasting lipids and glucose; treat the combination as two unproven investigational agents, not a validated stack.
  • MOTS-c redundant Evidence-rodent
    Both are exercise-mimetics whose benefits funnel through the PGC-1alpha/oxidative-metabolism axis. MOTS-c acts via AMPK -> PGC-1alpha to raise fatty-acid oxidation, mitochondrial efficiency and endurance; SLU-PP-332 (ERRalpha/beta/gamma agonist) increases oxidative muscle fibers, fatty-acid oxidation and energy expenditure, and the ERR receptors are the canonical transcriptional partners of PGC-1alpha. Stacking them is largely redundant on the same oxidative-metabolism transcriptional program. SLU-PP-332 ERRalpha/beta/gamma - oxidative fibers, fatty-acid oxidation, energy expenditure (slu-pp-332.json); mots-c.json (AMPK/PGC-1alpha oxidative program, PMID 41520850) Watch: Given overlapping oxidative/energy-expenditure drive, watch resting heart rate and exercise tolerance; SLU-PP-332 has a rodent cardiac signal at high exposure (separate concern). Effect overlap means limited independent benefit.

History & legal status

"ORIGIN: Designed ~2022-2023 by the laboratories of Thomas P. Burris and John K. Walker (Saint Louis University / Saint Louis College of Pharmacy / Washington University in St. Louis; with the Salk Institute / Ronald Evans group) via structure-based rational drug design, optimizing the ERRbeta/gamma agonist GSK4716 for ERRalpha activity. The name reflects its origin: 'SLU' = Saint Louis University. First fully described in ACS Chemical Biology (Billon et al. 2023, PMID 36988910); a preprint of the heart-failure work appeared on bioRxiv in Feb 2022 (DOI 10.1101/2022.02.14.480431). REGULATORY: NOT an approved drug anywhere - no FDA/EMA approval, no completed or registered human clinical trials identified. It remains a preclinical research compound / chemical probe, sold by laboratory chemical suppliers for research use only. ANTI-DOPING: As an exercise mimetic / metabolic modulator, it falls under WADA's Prohibited List class S4.5 (metabolic modulators / hormone and metabolic modulators); dedicated detection methods and human in-vitro metabolite maps have been published specifically so anti-doping labs can catch illicit use (Moller 2026 PMID 41588687; Avliyakulov 2026 PMID 41688415). LEGAL STATUS: It is an unapproved investigational substance - not scheduled as a controlled substance, but not a legal dietary supplement or medicine; marketing it for human consumption would be unlawful in the US (unapproved new drug) and EU. Banned in sport at all times."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • NO human pharmacokinetics or pharmacodynamics exist - half-life, oral absorption (332 is non-oral in rodents), effective dose, and dose-toxicity relationship in humans are all completely undefined; everything known is mouse or in vitro.
  • Long-term safety is unstudied - the longest rodent dosing was a few weeks; chronic effects on liver, heart (cell-cycle/E2F1 suppression seen in cardiomyocytes), kidney, reproductive axis and especially CANCER risk (ERRalpha is linked to breast and other cancers) are unknown.
  • Tissue selectivity of action - because it is a pan-agonist with only ~2-4 fold ERRalpha selectivity, which therapeutic vs off-target effects dominate in a whole human at a given exposure is unknown; the relative roles of ERRalpha vs beta vs gamma differ by tissue (alpha in muscle, gamma in heart).
  • Whether the rodent endurance/metabolic benefits translate to humans at all - the only human-derived data is a single ex vivo myoblast pilot (n=20); no in-vivo human efficacy signal exists.
  • The two distinct ERR transcription mechanisms (classic PGC-1alpha recruitment vs newly described direct TFIIH recruitment) and whether SLU-PP-332 biases toward one - this could determine which gene programs and side effects predominate, and is unresolved.
  • Drug-drug interactions and CYP-isoform-specific metabolism - the specific human CYPs handling SLU-PP-332 were not definitively assigned, so interaction potential and inter-individual metabolic variability are unknown.
  • Effects on circadian/metabolic timing and on adaptation to REAL exercise - whether chronically force-firing the acute-exercise program (Ddit4, Per1/2) blunts or augments genuine training adaptation in humans is untested.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only - this is an unapproved compound with no human safety data, and the only honest position is that risk is uncharacterized; the following are sensible things to WATCH (not an endorsement of use, and explicitly NO dosing guidance). LIVER: baseline and periodic liver function tests (ALT, AST, ALP, bilirubin) - hepatic Phase-I/II metabolism and a qualitative rodent/anecdotal signal of mildly raised liver enzymes make transaminases the top thing to track; rising trends are a stop signal. LIPIDS/GLUCOSE: a fasting lipid panel and fasting glucose/insulin (or HbA1c) - rodent data show shifts in cholesterol, triglycerides, HDL, glucose and insulin (an exercise-like metabolic shift), so changes here are expected and should be interpreted in context. CARDIAC: given ERRgamma's central cardiac role and the cell-cycle gene changes seen in cardiomyocytes, watch for any new exertional symptoms, palpitations or shortness of breath and seek evaluation (ECG/echo) if they occur - there is no human cardiac safety data. MUSCLE: creatine kinase (CK) to screen for muscle injury (notably CK was NOT elevated in mice, a reassuring but rodent-only finding). RENAL: basic metabolic panel and urine albumin if used chronically (kidney is an ERR-target tissue). GENERAL: complete blood count and electrolytes (normal in the mouse tolerability panel) as a baseline. RED FLAGS to stop and seek medical care: jaundice/dark urine, RUQ pain, unexplained fatigue with rising LFTs, chest pain or dyspnea. Anyone with personal or family history of hormone-sensitive cancer (e.g. breast) should regard the ERRalpha-cancer association as an unquantified reason for extra caution. SPORT: it is WADA-prohibited and detectable - athletes face sanctions."

Caveats

Translation gap: a molecule that mimics exercise genes in a mouse may not deliver the same benefits — or the same safety — in a human. Selectivity: ERRs sit at the center of metabolism in many tissues (heart, kidney, muscle, fat), so broad pan-ERR activation could have off-target or long-term effects not captured in short rodent studies. Pharmacokinetics: the original SLU-PP-332 was not orally bioavailable, limiting real-world use. No long-term safety, dosing, or human-efficacy data exist. The cancer angle deserves caution too — ERRα is implicated in some tumor metabolism, so chronic activation is an open safety question, not a settled one (Contested/Speculative). Do not let 'exercise in a pill' marketing imply proven human fat loss or performance gains — none has been demonstrated in people.
