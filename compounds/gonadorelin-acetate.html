Gonadorelin acetate

The body's own GnRH decapeptide, injected in pulses to keep the LH/FSH signal switched on so the testes keep working during testosterone therapy.

Human / approved 10 citations
Class
Native gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH / LHRH) decapeptide; short-acting pituitary secretagogue (agonist)
Primary target
Pituitary GnRH receptor (GnRHR), a Gq/11-coupled GPCR on anterior-pituitary gonadotrope cells
Human status
Established, FDA-approved drug (gonadorelin, parent approved 1982 as the hydrochloride/Factrel; the acetate salt marketed as Lutrepulse/Lutrelef/Fertagyl) with decades of human use - but the approvals are for diagnostic pituitary gonadotropin testing and for pulsatile-pump treatment of hypogonadotropic hypogonadism/infertility, NOT for adjunct-to-TRT use. Its current popularity at US TRT clinics as an hCG substitute (after the 2020 US hCG shortage/compounding changes) is real-world, largely compounded, off-label practice that has outrun the published trial evidence: there are no large randomized trials showing that intermittent gonadorelin injections preserve fertility or testicular volume as well as hCG in men on testosterone. The strong human evidence base is in hypogonadotropic hypogonadism (pump-delivered pulses), where efficacy for inducing testosterone and spermatogenesis is well documented (e.g., Pitteloud 2002, PMID 12213860; Christiansen & Skakkebaek 2002, PMID 12006717).

At a glance

  • Stimulates a pulse of pituitary LH and FSH release (peak LH ~30-60 min after IV dosing in diagnostic testing)
  • Downstream, aims to maintain Leydig-cell intratesticular testosterone and Sertoli-cell/inhibin B function - to preserve testicular volume and fertility during TRT (benefit in the TRT setting is unproven)
  • Acts one step higher in the axis than hCG (pituitary vs. direct testicular LH-mimic); does not itself supply androgen or estrogen
  • Effect is entirely dependent on pulsatile dosing and an intact, responsive pituitary-testis axis
  • Continuous (non-pulsatile) exposure produces the OPPOSITE effect: receptor down-regulation and gonadotropin suppression
  • Used clinically as a diagnostic pituitary-stimulation (GnRH) test and, historically, via pump for hypogonadotropic hypogonadism and infertility

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Gonadorelin is identical to the body's own GnRH Established

    Gonadorelin is a synthetic copy of the natural 10-amino-acid hypothalamic hormone GnRH (also called LHRH). Because it is the native sequence (not a modified long-acting analog), it behaves like the real thing: a quick, on-then-off signal to the pituitary. The acetate is just the salt form of the same peptide. ChEMBL: gonadorelin parent CHEMBL1007 (decapeptide pGlu-His-Trp-Ser-Tyr-Gly-Leu-Arg-Pro-Gly-NH2, natural product, FDA-approved 1982, USAN 1975); acetate salt CHEMBL1200511 (synonyms Lutrelef/Lutrepulse/Fertagyl)

  2. It binds the GnRH receptor on pituitary gonadotropes Established

    Injected gonadorelin travels to the anterior pituitary and binds the GnRH receptor, a G-protein-coupled receptor sitting on the surface of gonadotrope cells. This receptor is the single switch that controls release of both LH and FSH. Stojilkovic & Catt, J Neuroendocrinol 1995, PMID 8563717

  3. Receptor activates the Gq/phospholipase-C / calcium pathway Established

    The activated receptor couples to Gq/11, switches on phospholipase C, and generates IP3 and diacylglycerol. IP3 releases stored calcium and calcium floods into the cell; this calcium rise, together with protein kinase C, is what physically drives LH and FSH out of the gonadotrope and ramps up new hormone synthesis. Stojilkovic & Catt, J Neuroendocrinol 1995, PMID 8563717

  4. A pulse of LH/FSH is released into the blood Established

    Within minutes of an injection, LH and FSH surge in the bloodstream. In diagnostic testing with IV gonadorelin, the LH peak occurs around 30-60 minutes after injection, then falls back as the drug clears. This transient spike is the intended effect and is the basis for its diagnostic use as a pituitary (GnRH) stimulation test. (Note: the timing figure comes from pediatric diagnostic-test data, not from adult TRT-adjunct studies.) Ahn et al., Children (Basel) 2023, PMID 38002921 (GnRH/gonadorelin stimulation test; peak LH 30-60 min after IV gonadorelin)

  5. Pulsatile (not continuous) delivery is essential to avoid shutdown Established

    The pituitary is wired to respond to intermittent pulses of GnRH. Steady, continuous exposure (as with long-acting GnRH agonists like leuprolide) causes the gonadotrope to down-regulate its receptors and go silent - the mechanism deliberately exploited for chemical castration. Gonadorelin's very short half-life means each dose comes and goes quickly, so if timed as pulses it stimulates rather than suppresses. Give the same drug as a continuous flat level and you get the opposite, suppressive effect. Kiesel & Runnebaum, Gynakol Geburtshilfliche Rundsch 1992, PMID 1325231 (review; continuous GnRH-analog exposure causes receptor down-regulation and pituitary desensitization)

  6. Pulse frequency is decoded to tune LH vs FSH Established

    The gonadotrope reads how fast the pulses arrive: faster GnRH pulses favor LH-beta, slower pulses favor FSH-beta, partly by changing how many GnRH receptors sit on the cell surface. This 'frequency decoding' is why the natural system can dial LH and FSH somewhat independently - and why an artificial pulse regimen cannot perfectly reproduce physiology. The specific surface-receptor-density mechanism was worked out in rat gonadotrope cell lines; the pulse-frequency principle itself is established across species. Kaiser et al., PNAS 1995, PMID 8618885 (rat GH3 cell-line, in vitro mechanism); Thompson & Kaiser, Mol Cell Endocrinol 2014, PMID 24056171 (review)

  7. LH and FSH drive the testes: testosterone + spermatogenesis Established

    The released LH tells Leydig cells to make intratesticular testosterone; FSH supports Sertoli cells and sperm production (reflected by inhibin B). By keeping this endogenous LH/FSH signal alive during exogenous testosterone therapy - which otherwise suppresses it - gonadorelin aims to preserve testicular size, intratesticular testosterone, and fertility. This is the same downstream goal as hCG, but reached one step higher in the axis. Note: this LH/FSH-to-testis physiology is well established, but the direct benefit of GONADORELIN in a TRT setting is not (see human status/caveats). Pitteloud et al., J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2002, PMID 12213860 (pulsatile GnRH normalized LH in 97% and testosterone in 93% of IHH men; FSH-Sertoli-inhibin B axis normalized less uniformly than LH-Leydig-T)

  8. Requires an intact pituitary-testis axis to work Evidence-human

    Because gonadorelin acts on the pituitary, it only helps if the pituitary and testes can still respond. In men whose axis was never primed (e.g., absent prior puberty, low baseline inhibin B, or prior cryptorchidism) responses are weaker, slower, and often incomplete. In a typical TRT patient with a previously normal axis, the machinery is intact - but the specific benefit of gonadorelin at TRT clinics has thin direct trial evidence versus its well-studied use in hypogonadotropic hypogonadism. Pitteloud et al. 2002, PMID 12213860 (prior pubertal development, baseline inhibin B >60 pg/mL, and absence of cryptorchidism were the independent predictors of outcome); Zheng et al., J Clin Endocrinol Metab 2017, PMID 28368486 (response depends on functional gonadotrope reserve; good response in 60%)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Gonadorelin is the native GnRH decapeptide (pGlu-His-Trp-Ser-Tyr-Gly-Leu-Arg-Pro-Gly-NH2) and binds the pituitary GnRH receptor (GnRHR), a class-A Gq/11-coupled GPCR expressed on anterior-pituitary gonadotropes. Unlike superagonists (leuprolide, triptorelin), it is not modified for potency or duration, so its receptor occupancy is brief.

Signalling cascade. Agonist binding -> Gq/11 -> phospholipase C-beta -> hydrolysis of PIP2 to IP3 + DAG -> IP3-mediated release of intracellular Ca2+ and Ca2+ influx, plus PKC activation -> Ca2+/PKC-driven exocytosis of stored LH and FSH and increased transcription of the gonadotropin subunit genes (CGA, LHB, FSHB). Pulse FREQUENCY is decoded (in part via surface GnRHR density) to differentially favor LHbeta (faster pulses) or FSHbeta (slower pulses); the surface-density mechanism was demonstrated in rat gonadotrope cell lines.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Parenteral only (IV, SC, or historically intranasal); not orally bioavailable. TRT-clinic use is typically subcutaneous injection; classical HH treatment used a programmable SC/IV pump delivering pulses roughly every 90-120 min.
Absorption / bioavailability
Peptide; negligible oral bioavailability due to gastrointestinal proteolysis. Absorbed after SC injection; IV gives an immediate, defined pulse used diagnostically.
Half-life
Very short - on the order of a few minutes after IV administration (native GnRH); this ultra-short half-life is the defining pharmacologic feature and the reason pulsatile delivery stimulates rather than desensitizes the receptor. (Exact minute-level figures vary by assay; treat as a qualitative 'minutes' half-life rather than a precise value.)
Metabolism
Rapid enzymatic cleavage by peptidases (endopeptidases cleaving the Tyr5-Gly6 and other bonds) in plasma, pituitary, kidney and liver. Not cytochrome-P450 dependent.
Excretion
Peptide fragments cleared renally; parent peptide largely degraded before excretion.
Notes
Because the half-life is minutes, a single SC injection produces only a transient LH/FSH pulse; sustained axis support in classical HH required frequent pump-delivered pulses. Infrequent injection schedules therefore give pharmacologically short-lived, sub-physiologic stimulation - a key reason the TRT-adjunct rationale is mechanistically debated.

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

Biphasic and frequency-dependent, not simple dose-linear. Physiologic pulsatile exposure STIMULATES gonadotropin output; continuous or high sustained exposure DESENSITIZES/down-regulates the receptor and SUPPRESSES LH/FSH (the basis of GnRH-agonist chemical castration). Pulse frequency shapes the LH:FSH ratio. In diagnostic testing a standard IV bolus produces a measurable LH rise peaking ~30-60 min. This monograph gives no therapeutic dosing/timing protocol.

How it compares

  • vs hCG (human chorionic gonadotropin)
    same: Both aim to keep the testes active during TRT and preserve testicular volume/fertility by driving Leydig-cell function.
    different: hCG acts DIRECTLY on the testicular LH receptor (bypasses the pituitary) with a long half-life and steady stimulation; gonadorelin acts on the PITUITARY and needs an intact axis plus pulsatile delivery. Gonadorelin rose at US TRT clinics mainly as a compounded substitute after hCG supply/regulatory disruption, but has far less TRT-specific evidence; the older HH comparison did not show it beats hCG (PMID 3142911).
  • vs Long-acting GnRH agonists (leuprolide, triptorelin, goserelin)
    same: Same receptor (GnRHR); triptorelin/gonadorelin are even used similarly in diagnostic stimulation testing.
    different: Same target, OPPOSITE clinical use: continuous long-acting agonism desensitizes/down-regulates the receptor to SUPPRESS gonadotropins (used for prostate cancer, endometriosis, precocious puberty). Gonadorelin's short half-life is precisely what allows stimulation instead of shutdown.
  • vs GnRH antagonists (cetrorelix, degarelix)
    same:
    different: Antagonists block the receptor to suppress LH/FSH immediately without an initial flare; gonadorelin is a native agonist that stimulates. Mechanistically opposite intent.
  • vs Clomiphene / enclomiphene (SERMs)
    same:
    different: SERMs raise LH/FSH indirectly by blocking estrogen negative feedback at the hypothalamus/pituitary (oral, endogenous-axis modulation); gonadorelin is the GnRH signal itself, given by injection. Different level of the axis and different practicality.

History &amp; legal status

GnRH was isolated and sequenced by Schally and Guillemin (Nobel Prize 1977). Gonadorelin received its USAN in 1975 and FDA approval in 1982 (as the hydrochloride, Factrel/AY-24031; ChEMBL CHEMBL1200516) for diagnostic evaluation of pituitary gonadotropin reserve; the acetate salt (ChEMBL CHEMBL1200511) was marketed as Lutrepulse/Lutrelef (pump) and Fertagyl for pulsatile treatment of hypothalamic amenorrhea and hypogonadotropic hypogonadism/infertility. It is a native peptide, not withdrawn, with no black-box warning (confirmed via ChEMBL: withdrawn_flag false, black_box_warning false). Branded pump products were later discontinued in some markets for commercial reasons. Its 2020s resurgence in US men's-health/TRT clinics as a compounded hCG alternative followed hCG supply constraints and FDA compounding restrictions on hCG - an off-label, largely compounded use not covered by the original diagnostic/HH approvals.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • No large randomized trials show that intermittent SC gonadorelin injections (the TRT-clinic regimen) actually preserve fertility, sperm counts, or testicular volume as well as hCG in men on testosterone.
  • Optimal (or even adequate) injection frequency for TRT-adjunct use is unknown; pharmacology suggests infrequent dosing may be sub-physiologic given the minutes-long half-life.
  • Long-term safety of chronic intermittent gonadorelin in otherwise-eugonadal TRT users (including anti-GnRH antibody formation rate) is not well characterized.
  • Real-world potency/purity of COMPOUNDED gonadorelin varies and is not FDA-assured.
  • Whether gonadorelin meaningfully protects spermatogenesis (FSH/Sertoli axis) versus mainly nudging LH/testosterone is unclear from TRT-population data; in HH studies the FSH-Sertoli-inhibin B axis normalized less uniformly than the LH-Leydig-T axis.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Harm-reduction monitoring only (not a protocol): baseline and periodic total testosterone, LH and FSH to confirm the axis is actually responding (a flat LH/FSH suggests the regimen is not working or the axis is suppressed); estradiol if estrogenic symptoms appear; semen analysis and testicular exam/volume if fertility preservation is the goal (inhibin B is a research marker of Sertoli-cell function); watch for injection-site reactions and, over time, loss of response that could signal anti-GnRH antibodies or inadequate pulsatility. Anyone considering this should do so under a clinician who can measure the axis, and be aware that source/compounding quality matters. This is education and monitoring guidance, not medical advice, dosing, or an endorsement of off-label use.

Caveats

Big caveat: the mechanism is solid and the drug is old and well-characterized, but the specific TRT-adjunct use is off-label and under-evidenced. Almost all rigorous human data come from pulsatile-PUMP delivery (or frequent SC pulses ~every 90-120 min) in hypogonadotropic hypogonadism, not from the once-or-twice-daily subcutaneous injections used at TRT clinics - and pharmacology predicts that infrequent, sub-physiologic dosing may give only weak, transient LH/FSH bumps. In a head-to-head from-inception study in complete isolated HH, pulsatile SC GnRH was no better (and if anything slightly slower) than hCG/hMG for testicular growth and sperm output (40% vs 80% producing sperm at 2 years, not significant) (Liu et al. 1988, PMID 3142911). Because it is a peptide, anti-GnRH antibody formation has been reported and can abolish the response (Blumenfeld et al. 1988, PMID 3139467). Much TRT-clinic product is compounded, so purity/dose consistency is not FDA-assured. This monograph is mechanism education and harm-reduction only - not a protocol or endorsement.
