Library / Anabolic-androgenic steroids

Andarine

A non-steroidal "designer androgen" that hits muscle and bone like testosterone but largely spares the prostate — and is most infamous for the yellow-tinted vision some users report.

Limited human data 19 citations
Class
Selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM); aryl-propionamide chemotype (same chemical family as bicalutamide and ostarine)
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR / NR3C4) — the same intracellular ligand-activated transcription factor that testosterone and DHT bind
Human status
Not approved for any human use, anywhere. S-4 was an early SARM developed in the GTx/Dalton (Univ. of Tennessee) program; it advanced through early preclinical and limited clinical work but development was discontinued — it never completed late-phase trials or gained FDA/EMA approval. It is sold only on the grey/black market as a research-chemical / unapproved 'supplement' (frequently mislabeled or contaminated), banned in sport by WADA since 2008, and has prompted FDA warnings about SARMs. Almost all efficacy and selectivity data are from rodents and in-vitro work; human data are limited to doping-control detection studies and class-level adverse-event case reports.

At a glance

  • Increases skeletal-muscle mass and strength (full AR agonist in muscle) — robust in rodents
  • Increases bone mineral density and prevents bone loss (rodent osteopenia/orchidectomy/ovariectomy models)
  • Reduces body fat / favors a leaner 'recomp' — why users brand it a 'cutting' compound
  • Largely spares prostate growth vs DHT (the defining 'selective' feature)
  • Suppresses the HPG axis: lowers LH/FSH and therefore natural testosterone production
  • Signature reported visual disturbance: yellow/green tint, slow dark-adaptation, night-vision trouble (anecdotal, reversible, mechanism unconfirmed)
  • Class-level harms: drug-induced liver injury (case reports) and adverse lipid/cardiovascular signals (notably lowered HDL)
  • Anti-cancer activity against hepatocellular carcinoma cells in vitro via PI3K/AKT/mTOR downregulation (cell-line only)

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Binds the androgen receptor as a non-steroidal ligand Established

    S-4 is not a steroid — it has no four-ring steroid backbone. It is an aryl-propionamide molecule that still slots into the same ligand-binding pocket of the androgen receptor (AR) that testosterone and DHT use. Because it is built differently, it engages the receptor differently, which is the whole basis for its 'selective' behavior. PMID 16099859 (Gao et al., Endocrinology 2005); PMID 18801930 (Narayanan et al., Mol Endocrinol 2008)

  2. Activates AR gene transcription (the genomic anabolic signal) Established

    Once bound, the receptor changes shape, moves into the cell nucleus, and switches on the same anabolic genes androgens normally drive — telling muscle to grow and bone to hold mineral. This classic ligand -> nuclear translocation -> gene transcription route is the genomic mechanism shared by all androgens and SARMs. Androgen receptor MoA card (corpus); PMID 18801930 (Narayanan et al., Mol Endocrinol 2008)

  3. Recruits different co-regulators in muscle vs prostate — the source of 'selectivity' Evidence-rodent

    The shape S-4 forces on the receptor recruits a different mix of helper proteins (co-activators/co-repressors) depending on the tissue, and also fires off rapid non-genomic kinase signals (ERK/MAPK) differently than DHT. The net result in animals: a full anabolic 'go' signal in muscle and bone, but only a weak partial signal in the prostate. This is why SARMs are pitched as 'anabolic without the prostate growth.' Shown for the closely related aryl-propionamide S-22; mechanism is class-level, not proven uniquely for S-4 in humans. PMID 18801930 (Narayanan et al., Mol Endocrinol 2008); PMID 16099859 (Gao et al., Endocrinology 2005)

  4. Builds muscle and bone, spares the prostate (in rodents) Evidence-rodent

    In castrated and ovariectomized rats, S-4 restored muscle mass and strength and increased bone mineral density to (or above) intact levels, while prostate and seminal-vesicle weights stayed near 16-17% of what full DHT produced. That tissue gap is the headline finding behind the SARM concept. PMID 16099859 (Gao et al., Endocrinology 2005); PMID 17063395 (Kearbey et al., Pharm Res 2007); PMID 19728047 (Kearbey et al., Pharm Res 2009)

  5. Still suppresses the HPG axis — your own testosterone shuts down Evidence-rodent

    S-4 also acts as an agonist at the pituitary, lowering LH and FSH in a dose-dependent way in castrated rats. In plain terms: the brain reads the drug as 'enough androgen around,' so it dials down the signal to make natural testosterone. This is the same suppression seen with anabolic steroids — 'tissue-selective' does NOT mean 'no suppression.' PMID 16099859 (Gao et al., Endocrinology 2005); narrative review PMID 33148520 (Machek et al., Steroids 2020)

  6. Reported yellow/green-tinted vision and trouble adjusting to the dark Speculative / user-report

    The side effect S-4 is famous for in user communities is a yellowish or greenish tint to vision and slow dark-adaptation, often worse at night and reversible after stopping. This is NOT documented in indexed clinical trials of S-4 and is not a proven androgen-receptor effect — the leading idea is that S-4 (or a metabolite) transiently interferes with a non-AR target in the retina. Treat as anecdote-driven and biologically unconfirmed, not established pharmacology. (Note: largely user-report/anecdote, not from controlled human data.) No indexed PubMed primary trial; user-reported (e.g., r/SARMs corpus). Mechanism not validated; flagged as Contested/Speculative.

  7. Class-level liver and cardiovascular/lipid risk Evidence-human

    Across SARMs as a class, real-world use is linked to drug-induced liver injury (jaundice, raised liver enzymes) in case reports, and reviews flag adverse lipid changes (notably dropped HDL) and theoretical cardiovascular strain from over-driving AR signaling. These are documented for the SARM category and chemically related compounds rather than from clean S-4 human trials — but they are the real harm signals to teach. Hepatotoxicity case report PMID 35340496 (Khan et al., Cureus 2022); cardiovascular/lipid review PMID 37571268 (Hall & Vrolijk, Nutrients 2023); review PMID 33148520 (Machek et al., Steroids 2020)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Andarine (S-4) is a non-steroidal, aryl-propionamide selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM) that binds the ligand-binding pocket of the androgen receptor (AR / NR3C4) as an orthosteric ligand. It is chemically derived from the antiandrogen bicalutamide scaffold. Reported AR binding affinity is HIGH but submaximal vs the endogenous agonist: relative AR binding affinity roughly 25-50% that of dihydrotestosterone (DHT), with an absolute Ki commonly cited in the low-to-mid nanomolar range (~4 nM order of magnitude in the original GTx/Ohio State characterization) [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 15994457]. Functionally it is TISSUE-SELECTIVE: a FULL agonist on AR in skeletal muscle and bone (anabolic compartments) but only a PARTIAL agonist / weak agonist in the prostate and seminal vesicles (androgenic compartments) [Evidence-rodent; PMID 16099859]. In the pituitary it acts as an agonist, suppressing gonadotropins [Evidence-rodent; PMID 16099859]. Unlike testosterone/DHT, S-4 is NOT a substrate for 5-alpha-reductase or CYP19 aromatase, so it cannot be amplified to a more potent androgen in skin/prostate nor converted to estrogen [Established for the aryl-propionamide class; PMID 15994457].

Signalling cascade. S-4 enters cells and binds cytoplasmic AR, displacing heat-shock proteins; the receptor undergoes a ligand-specific conformational change, dimerizes, and translocates to the nucleus where it binds androgen-response elements (AREs) and regulates transcription of androgen-responsive genes (genomic pathway) [Evidence-in vitro/rodent; PMID 18801930]. The molecular basis of tissue selectivity is thought to be ligand-induced conformational differences that drive DIFFERENTIAL COFACTOR RECRUITMENT: S-4 (and the closely related analog S-22) recruit a coactivator/corepressor profile to the AR distinct from that recruited by DHT, so the same receptor activates a different downstream gene set in muscle vs prostate [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 18801930]. Beyond classical genomic signaling, aryl-propionamide SARMs also engage rapid NON-GENOMIC signaling: S-22 triggered rapid phosphorylation of MAPK-pathway kinases (MEK/ERK1/2, p38) and induced Xenopus oocyte maturation through pathways distinct from steroids, and these non-genomic events feed back to modulate AR and cofactor occupancy at target promoters (e.g., the PSA enhancer) in a ligand-dependent manner [Evidence-in vitro; PMID 18801930]. Net downstream effect in muscle/bone: increased IGF-1 expression, myosin heavy-chain/contractile protein synthesis, and osteoblast activity, producing anabolic growth without proportionate prostate stimulation [Evidence-rodent; PMID 16099859].

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Orally active (designed as an oral non-steroidal androgen); illicit human use is oral (liquid/capsule). No approved human formulation, ester, or injectable exists. [Evidence-rodent for oral activity; human PK formally uncharacterized — PMID 15204699]
Absorption / bioavailability
Rapidly absorbed. Oral bioavailability is dose-dependent (saturable): COMPLETE (~100%) at low doses in rats and 91% in dogs at pharmacologically relevant doses, declining at higher doses [Evidence-rodent/dog; PMID 15204699, PMID 16815963]. Human bioavailability not formally established.
Half-life
Short in rats: terminal half-life ~2.6-5.3 h across the dose range [Evidence-rodent; PMID 15204699]. In dogs ~200 min (~3.3 h) with linear kinetics [Evidence-dog; PMID 16815963]. Human half-life not rigorously published; the short animal half-life is the basis for the (unproven) human practice of divided daily dosing. Volume of distribution in rats ~0.448 L/kg (moderate); clearance ~1.0-2.1 mL/min/kg (rat), ~4.6 mL/min/kg (dog) [PMID 15204699, PMID 16815963].
Metabolism
Eliminated largely by HEPATIC metabolism via both phase I and phase II enzymes [Evidence-rodent/dog; PMID 16815963]. Major phase I routes: reduction of the A-ring nitro group (nitroreduction), deacetylation of the B-ring acetamide, and hydrolytic cleavage of the central amide bond (yielding the B-ring fragment); plus oxidative/hydroxylated metabolites. Phase II: glucuronide and sulfate conjugation of the hydroxylated and deacetylated metabolites [Evidence-human in vitro + animal; PMID 16815963, PMID 20623476]. SPECIES DIFFERENCE: dogs favor deacetylation + nitroreduction; rats favor amide hydrolysis + nitroreduction. Specific human CYP isoforms responsible are not definitively assigned in the public literature (gap). Not a 5-alpha-reductase or aromatase substrate [PMID 15994457].
Excretion
Excreted as conjugated and unconjugated metabolites; human urinary doping-control studies confirm glucuronides/sulfates of the monohydroxylated, deacetylated, and amide-hydrolysis (B-ring removed) metabolites as the main urinary species, with little intact parent [Evidence-human; PMID 20623476].
Notes
No prodrug/ester chemistry — S-4 is the active molecule. Lipophilic small molecule; CNS/pituitary penetration is implied by its gonadotropin suppression (LH/FSH fall), indicating it crosses into the hypothalamic-pituitary axis [Evidence-rodent; PMID 16099859]. Because parent drug is rapidly and extensively metabolized, blood/urine detection in anti-doping relies on metabolites, not parent [PMID 20623476].

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

RODENT (best-characterized): In castrated (orchidectomized) male rats, S-4 at 3 and 10 mg/kg/day fully restored levator ani and soleus muscle mass and strength to intact levels and increased total-body bone mineral density MORE than DHT, while returning prostate and seminal vesicle weight to only ~16-17% of intact controls at 3 mg/kg (i.e., near-maximal anabolic effect with minimal androgenic effect) [Evidence-rodent; PMID 16099859]. The same doses dose-dependently suppressed plasma LH and FSH [PMID 16099859]. In ovariectomized female rats, S-4 maintained whole-body and trabecular BMD, increased bone strength, and reduced body fat [Evidence-rodent; PMID 17063395]. A maximal anabolic effect is thus reached around 3 mg/kg in rats with a relatively flat top end. HUMAN dose-response is UNKNOWN: S-4 never completed published human efficacy dosing studies, so there is NO validated human dose-effect curve for muscle, strength, fat, or side effects. Human exposure is entirely off-label/illicit and self-reported; commonly reported anecdotal ranges and the characteristic visual side effects are not derived from controlled human dose-finding and should not be treated as established dose-response data [Evidence-human: anecdote only]. Allometric scaling from rodent mg/kg to humans is not validated for this compound — explicitly a gap.

How it compares

  • vs Ostarine / Enobosarm (MK-2866)
    same: Both are non-steroidal SARMs, orally active, AR-binding, muscle/bone-selective with reduced prostate effect; both suppress LH/FSH and lower HDL
    different: Ostarine is also an aryl-propionamide and is the most clinically advanced SARM (multiple human phase II/III muscle-wasting and breast-cancer trials, e.g. POWER trials), giving it real human efficacy/safety data S-4 lacks. Ostarine does NOT cause the characteristic yellow-vision/night-blindness reported with S-4. [MoA-validated PMIDs for ostarine: 14600402, 18590822]
  • vs Ligandrol (LGD-4033)
    same: Non-steroidal SARM, oral, AR orthosteric agonist, anabolic in muscle/bone with HPG suppression and HDL reduction
    different: Ligandrol is a different chemotype (aryl-pyrrolidinone-derived), markedly more potent/higher AR affinity and 'bulkier' anabolic reputation, longer human half-life (~24-36 h, so once-daily), and has documented human pharmacology and case reports of liver injury; no S-4-type visual side effect. [MoA-validated PMIDs for ligandrol: 16492776, 34734312]
  • vs Bicalutamide (parent antiandrogen scaffold)
    same: Shares the aryl-propionamide core chemistry from which S-4 was derived; both bind AR
    different: Bicalutamide is a pure AR ANTAGONIST used clinically in prostate cancer, whereas structural modification turned S-4 into a tissue-selective partial agonist/agonist. Illustrates how small changes on the same scaffold flip antagonist-to-SARM behavior. [Class review: PMID 15994457]
  • vs Dihydrotestosterone (DHT) / testosterone (endogenous reference)
    same: All are AR agonists driving anabolic muscle/bone effects via the same receptor
    different: DHT/testosterone are steroidal, are amplified by 5-alpha-reductase and (T) aromatized to estrogen, and strongly stimulate prostate; S-4 is non-steroidal, NOT a 5AR/aromatase substrate, and spares the prostate (~16-17% of intact prostate weight at anabolically effective doses). [PMID 16099859, PMID 15994457]

Interactions & stacking mechanism only — not a protocol

  • Anadrol compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Anadrol is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral anabolic steroid that is directly hepatotoxic (cholestatic/hepatocellular injury), and andarine carries a SARM-class drug-induced-liver-injury signal (its hepatic phase I/II metabolism, including nitroreduction, can generate reactive intermediates). Running two orally-processed, liver-stressing AR agonists together stacks injury risk on the same organ. andarine-s-4.json (side_effect_mechanisms: hepatotoxicity, SARM-class case reports PMID 35340496); anadrol-oxymetholone.json (17aa oral, hepatotoxic); PMID 41245032 (SARM DILI) Watch: Baseline and follow-up ALT, AST, GGT, alkaline phosphatase and total/direct bilirubin; watch for jaundice, dark urine, pale stool, right-upper-quadrant pain or unexplained fatigue and stop if they appear.
  • Ligandrol compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-rodent
    Both are AR agonists that act at the pituitary/hypothalamus to mimic androgen negative feedback, lowering LH and FSH and therefore endogenous testosterone. Stacking a second suppressive androgen deepens and prolongs the shutdown of natural testosterone production rather than sparing it; 'tissue-selective' does not mean 'non-suppressive.' andarine-s-4.json (mechanism_step 5: agonist at pituitary lowers LH/FSH, PMID 16099859; Evidence-human for SARM class, PMID 30503797); ligandrol-lgd-4033.json (HPG suppression) Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone and SHBG to gauge depth of suppression; in men watch for low libido, testicular shrinkage and mood change. Human recovery time after stopping is not well characterized.
  • Stanozolol compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-human
    Two AR agonists that both drive down HDL and both stress the liver. Stanozolol is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral known for marked HDL suppression and hepatotoxicity; andarine adds AR-driven hepatic lipid changes and a SARM-class DILI signal, so the adverse lipid shift and liver-enzyme elevation compound on the same pathways. andarine-s-4.json (lowered HDL + hepatotoxicity, SARM-class, PMID 30503797, PMID 35340496); stanozolol-winstrol.json (marked HDL drop, 17aa hepatotoxic) Watch: Fasting lipid panel (HDL especially) and a full liver panel (ALT/AST/GGT/ALP/bilirubin); watch for signs of cholestasis and continued HDL decline.
  • Trenbolone compounds risk ⚠ Evidence-rodent
    Both are AR agonists that suppress the HPG axis, so combined use deepens LH/FSH and endogenous-testosterone shutdown through the shared central mechanism. The overlap is specifically on AR occupancy and axis suppression; trenbolone's additional progesterone/glucocorticoid-receptor activity and renal strain are its own, not shared. andarine-s-4.json (AR agonist + HPG suppression, PMID 16099859, PMID 30503797); trenbolone.json (strong AR agonist, suppression, PGR/GR activity) Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone for combined suppression; separately track prolactin and kidney markers for trenbolone's own effects, and HDL for the shared androgenic lipid load.
  • Clomiphene citrate counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    Same axis-opposing mechanism as enclomiphene: clomiphene antagonizes hypothalamic/pituitary estrogen receptors, lifts negative feedback, and raises LH/FSH to restore the endogenous testosterone that andarine's pituitary AR agonism had suppressed. It works in the opposite direction on the same LH/FSH -> testosterone circuit. andarine-s-4.json (HPG suppression); clomiphene-citrate-clomid.json (ERalpha antagonist restarts LH/FSH; visual side effect its own class effect); PMID 26496621 Watch: LH, FSH, total testosterone and estradiol to confirm recovery. Separately, clomiphene has its own documented visual side effects (blurring, floaters, scotomata) that are a stop-and-refer signal and are mechanistically distinct from andarine's separate, anecdote-only visual reports. Harm-reduction context, not a dosing or PCT instruction.
  • Enclomiphene counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    Andarine suppresses the HPG axis by acting as an AR agonist at the pituitary/hypothalamus, dropping LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone. Enclomiphene is a SERM that antagonizes estrogen receptors on hypothalamic feedback neurons, removing estrogenic negative feedback and driving LH/FSH back up to restart the axis. It therefore acts in the opposite direction on the same LH/FSH -> testosterone circuit that andarine shuts down. andarine-s-4.json (mechanism_step 5 HPG suppression); enclomiphene-enclomiphene-citrate-trans-.json (ERalpha antagonist raises LH/FSH); PMID 26496621; PMID 23875626 Watch: LH, FSH and total testosterone to confirm the axis is recovering; note enclomiphene can push FSH/LH high and may lower IGF-1. Harm-reduction context, not a dosing or PCT protocol.
  • Human chorionic gonadotropin counteracts ↓ Evidence-human
    Andarine suppresses LH, the signal that tells testicular Leydig cells to make testosterone, causing testicular atrophy. hCG is an LH mimic that binds LHCGR directly on the testis, bypassing the suppressed pituitary to maintain intratesticular testosterone and testicular size. It counteracts the downstream consequence of andarine's central suppression but does nothing to reverse the central LH/FSH shutdown itself. andarine-s-4.json (HPG suppression, testicular atrophy); human-chorionic-gonadotropin-hcg.json (LHCGR agonist on Leydig cells, raises intratesticular T + E2) Watch: Total testosterone and estradiol (hCG raises intratesticular T and E2) plus testicular size; LH/FSH stay low because the pituitary signal is not restored. Harm-reduction context only, no dosing.
  • RAD-140 shares target Evidence-rodent
    Both are non-steroidal SARMs occupying the same androgen receptor, so they compete for AR binding and their liabilities overlap on the same pathways rather than complementing each other: both suppress the HPG axis (lower LH/FSH), both lower HDL through hepatic AR effects, and both carry a SARM-class drug-induced-liver-injury signal (RAD-140 has published human DILI case reports). Layering them adds AR occupancy and hepatic/lipid burden through the identical mechanism. andarine-s-4.json (primary_target AR; HPG + HDL + DILI class effects, PMID 30503797); rad-140-testolone.json (AR agonist); PMID 36561105 (RAD-140 DILI) Watch: LH/FSH and total/free testosterone for combined suppression; HDL for the shared lipid shift; ALT/AST/GGT/ALP and bilirubin for the shared liver-injury signal.
  • Testosterone shares target Established
    Both act on the same androgen receptor (AR / NR3C4), binding the same ligand pocket, so they co-occupy and compete for one receptor rather than working through separate pathways. Andarine is a non-steroidal AR ligand (full agonist in muscle/bone, weak partial agonist in prostate) and is NOT a substrate for 5-alpha-reductase or aromatase, whereas testosterone is a full agonist that is additionally amplified to DHT and aromatized to estradiol. Because they engage AR with different intrinsic efficacy and recruit different cofactors, the combined effect is not simply additive; a lower-efficacy SARM occupying AR could theoretically dampen part of testosterone's fuller androgenic signal in high-androgen tissue while adding its own anabolic drive in muscle (mechanistic inference, not proven in humans). andarine-s-4.json (primary_target AR; pharmacology: binds AR ligand-binding pocket, not a 5AR/aromatase substrate, PMID 15994457); PMID 16099859; testosterone-trt.json (AR + 5AR/aromatase) Watch: Total/free testosterone, SHBG, estradiol, hematocrit and a fasting lipid panel to track the combined androgenic load; note that adding a SARM does not offset testosterone's own aromatization or its hematocrit-raising effect.

History &amp; legal status

"Andarine (S-4; S-3-(4-acetylamino-phenoxy)-2-hydroxy-2-methyl-N-(4-nitro-3-trifluoromethyl-phenyl)-propionamide) is an aryl-propionamide SARM developed in the late 1990s-early 2000s at the University of Tennessee/Ohio State University by James Dalton and Duane Miller and licensed to GTx, Inc. (Memphis). It was a first-generation lead used to establish the SARM concept (muscle/bone anabolic, prostate-sparing) but was largely superseded in clinical development by the related compound ostarine/enobosarm; S-4 itself did not advance through pivotal human efficacy trials and was never approved [PMID 15994457, PMID 30503797]. REGULATORY STATUS: No SARM, including andarine, is approved by the FDA, EMA, or any major regulator for any indication; the FDA has issued public warnings about SARM-containing 'supplements' and liver-injury and other risks. The World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) has prohibited all SARMs (including andarine) in and out of competition since 2008 (class S1.2, 'other anabolic agents') — andarine and its metabolites are routine doping-control targets [PMID 20623476, PMID 23427117]. It is widely sold illicitly online and has repeatedly been detected in black-market 'supplement' products [PMID 20355219, PMID 21204286]. In the US it is not a controlled substance but is illegal to sell as a dietary supplement or for human consumption; several jurisdictions have moved to restrict SARM sales."

Key unknowns / open questions

  • The mechanism (and even formal confirmation) of the signature yellow-vision/night-blindness effect is unestablished — no peer-reviewed ophthalmologic or animal study has identified the retinal target or whether a metabolite is responsible, and whether repeated/high exposure can cause lasting visual change is unknown.
  • No validated human dose-response curve exists for muscle, strength, fat loss, HPG suppression, or toxicity — all human dosing is illicit and self-reported, so effective and harmful exposure thresholds in humans are genuinely unknown.
  • Human pharmacokinetics (true half-life, bioavailability, and the specific human CYP isoenzymes and transporters handling S-4) are not rigorously published; rodent/dog values may not translate.
  • Long-term cardiovascular consequences of the HDL reduction and chronic androgenic load from S-4 specifically have never been studied in humans.
  • The magnitude, frequency, and reversibility of S-4-specific hepatotoxicity (versus product contamination) are unquantified — published liver-injury cases are SARM-class/product-level, not pure-S-4.
  • Time course and completeness of HPG-axis (testosterone/LH/FSH/spermatogenesis) recovery after stopping S-4 in humans is not characterized, and the real-world purity/dose of black-market product is highly variable and confounds all anecdotal data.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

"Harm-reduction monitoring only (NOT an endorsement or protocol; no dosing guidance). Reasonable things to track and discuss with a clinician: (1) Vision — any yellow/green tint, glare, or slowed dark adaptation; an ophthalmology review is warranted if visual symptoms appear or persist. (2) Liver — baseline and follow-up ALT, AST, GGT, alkaline phosphatase, and total/direct bilirubin; watch for jaundice, dark urine, pale stool, right-upper-quadrant pain, or unexplained fatigue (stop and seek care). (3) Hormonal axis — total and free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG, and estradiol to detect suppression; in men watch for low libido, erectile changes, mood changes and testicular shrinkage; in women watch for virilization (voice deepening, hirsutism, menstrual change), some of which can be irreversible. (4) Lipids — fasting lipid panel for HDL reduction and adverse LDL/triglyceride shifts. (5) Hematology — CBC/hematocrit for polycythemia, and blood pressure. (6) Source/contamination risk — black-market SARM products are frequently mislabeled or adulterated, which is itself a major hazard. Discontinuation plus medical evaluation is the appropriate response to any of: visual change, jaundice/abnormal LFTs, chest symptoms, or marked hormonal symptoms."

Caveats

EVIDENCE TIER WARNING: The flattering parts of the S-4 story (muscle/bone gains, prostate sparing, HPG suppression) come overwhelmingly from castrated/ovariectomized RODENT studies (PMID 16099859, 17063395, 19728047) and the molecular-selectivity work was done on the related compound S-22, not S-4 itself (PMID 18801930). Do not present rodent tissue-selectivity as a proven human outcome. // The signature 'yellow vision' side effect is NOT in indexed PubMed primary literature for S-4 — it is community/anecdotal (reversible, often dose-dependent), and the retina/non-AR mechanism is unverified; teach it explicitly as Contested/Speculative. // Liver and cardiovascular/lipid harms (PMID 35340496, 37571268, 33148520) are real signals but are largely class-level (across SARMs and contaminated products), not from clean controlled S-4 human trials. // The anti-HCC finding (PMID 36990257) is cell-line only (in vitro). // Grey-market 'andarine' products are frequently underdosed, overdosed, or contain a different compound entirely — the label is not the molecule. // Retraction status checked live and CLEAR for the load-bearing sources (PMIDs 16099859, 18801930, 33148520, 36990257); re-check at publish time. // Harm-reduction framing only — no dosing provided. // SEARCH NOTE: 19 records for the andarine/S-4 SARM query and 8 for the SARM review query (manageable, no ESearch cap issue).
