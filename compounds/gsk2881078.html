GSK2881078

A GlaxoSmithKline oral non-steroidal SARM that is one of the very few in its class with published Phase 2 human muscle-strength data — modest, sex-dependent strength gains in men with COPD (significant only on the percent-predicted endpoint, not the absolute-kg endpoint), offset by predictable HDL-cholesterol drops, liver-enzyme bumps, and two reported cases of rhabdomyolysis. Development appears dormant; it is not approved anywhere.

Androgen receptor (AR, NR3C4) — tissue-selective agonist favoring skeletal muscle over classical androgenic tissue

Investigational; NOT approved by any regulator anywhere. Development by GlaxoSmithKline reached Phase 2A and appears to have stalled — no active or newer trials are registered. Human data comprise: a first-in-human single/repeat-dose Phase 1 study in healthy young men (NCT02045940, condition-coded 'Cachexia' but conducted in healthy volunteers), a first-in-human PK/PD paper covering men and post-menopausal women (PMID 28449232, Clark 2017), a Phase 1b lean-mass dose-ranging study with a CYP3A4 cohort (PMID 29982690, Neil 2018; registered as NCT02567773), and one Phase 2A trial in COPD-associated muscle weakness (PMID 36283827, Mohan 2023, NCT03359473). All registered trials are COMPLETED. GSK2881078 is a WADA-prohibited anabolic agent and appears extensively in doping-control literature; it is also used illicitly by recreational athletes despite not being a legal or approved product.

At a glance

  • Dose-dependent increase in lean body mass (DXA/MRI-confirmed) in healthy older adults
  • Modest leg-strength signal in men with COPD (~7% over placebo on the percent-predicted endpoint; the absolute-kg endpoint 90% CI crossed zero); not significant in women
  • No improvement in patient-reported outcomes or functional quality-of-life measures in the COPD trial
  • Greater anabolic and biomarker sensitivity in women than men at lower doses
  • Reversible lowering of HDL-cholesterol (on-target hepatic androgen effect)
  • Lowering of sex-hormone-binding globulin (SHBG)
  • Transient elevations in liver transaminases (ALT)
  • Rare rhabdomyolysis (2 reported cases in GSK2881078-exposed trial participants) and class-level drug-induced liver injury risk
  • Very long half-life (>100 h) supporting once-daily oral dosing

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Binds the androgen receptor ligand-binding domain as a non-steroidal agonist Evidence-in vitro

    GSK2881078 is a small, orally-available non-steroidal molecule that slots into the same ligand-binding pocket of the androgen receptor (AR) that testosterone and DHT use. In a prostate-cell (PC3(AR)2) reporter-gene assay it activated the AR with an EC50 around 4 nanomolar (3.99x10^-9 M) — a potent agonist in that system; in a yeast androgen screen it was far weaker (EC50 ~4.44x10^-6 M), a reminder that in vitro potency is assay-dependent. In silico docking to the AR LBD showed a binding pose slightly different from other SARMs like RAD-140 and GLPG0492, which is hypothesized (not proven) to underlie tissue selectivity. PMID 30825507 (Zierau/Keiler 2019, J Steroid Biochem Mol Biol)

  2. Recruits a tissue-selective set of coregulators (the proposed basis of 'selectivity') Contested

    Unlike steroidal androgens, a non-steroidal SARM bound to the AR is thought to change the receptor's shape so it recruits a different mix of coactivator/corepressor proteins in different tissues. The intended result is strong AR-driven transcription in muscle but weaker activity in prostate and other androgenic tissue. This is the class-level rationale for SARMs; the exact coregulator profile specific to GSK2881078 in human muscle has NOT been directly mapped, so the 'selectivity' is inferred from potency differences across assays and from the clinical side-effect pattern, not demonstrated at the molecular level for this compound. PMID 18590822 (Ting & Chang 2008, review); PMID 30825507 (Zierau/Keiler 2019)

  3. Activated AR drives a myo-anabolic transcriptional program in skeletal muscle Established

    In muscle, ligand-bound AR translocates to the nucleus and acts as a transcription factor, shifting the balance toward net protein accretion. The general biology of androgens in muscle — increased protein synthesis, satellite-cell activation/myonuclear accretion, and growth-factor modulation (IGF-1 up; effects on myostatin are complex — rodent data show androgens can raise myostatin protein even while driving hypertrophy) — is established. Note: this is the established biology of AR/androgens in muscle broadly; for GSK2881078 SPECIFICALLY there is no published human muscle transcriptomics, so this step is inferred from the drug class and from downstream readouts (lean mass, strength). PMID 18590822 (Ting & Chang 2008); PMID 32858949 (Yoshida 2020, IGF-1/mTOR muscle biology — general, not GSK-specific)

  4. Produces dose-dependent gains in lean body mass in humans Evidence-human

    In a Phase 1b repeat-dose study in healthy OLDER men and post-menopausal women, GSK2881078 increased lean mass in a dose-dependent way versus placebo, measured by DXA and MRI thigh scans. Women responded at lower doses than men — a real, measured sex difference. This is a lean-mass (body-composition) endpoint, not a strength or function endpoint. PMID 29982690 (Neil 2018, J Clin Endocrinol Metab)

  5. Translates to a modest strength signal — men only, and only on one endpoint — in a Phase 2 disease population Evidence-human

    In the Phase 2A COPD trial (NCT03359473), 13 weeks of GSK2881078 plus a home-exercise program increased leg strength in men on the percent-change endpoint (adjusted +7.0% vs placebo, 90% CI 0.5 to 13.6, which excludes zero). BUT the absolute one-rep-max endpoint in men was +11.8 kg with a 90% CI of -0.5 to 24.0 — which CROSSES zero — so even in men the result was borderline and endpoint-dependent. In women, neither endpoint reached significance. Lean body mass increased, but there was NO improvement in patient-reported outcomes or day-to-day function. So the anabolic signal is real but the clinically meaningful benefit is small and unproven. PMID 36283827 (Mohan 2023, Thorax); NCT03359473

  6. Lowers HDL cholesterol and SHBG as an on-target pharmacodynamic effect Evidence-human

    Across the first-in-human and later studies, GSK2881078 dose-dependently lowered HDL-cholesterol and sex-hormone-binding globulin (SHBG). These are expected on-target consequences of hepatic AR activation (androgens suppress hepatic HDL and SHBG production) and were used as pharmacodynamic biomarkers of drug activity. Women were more sensitive to these changes at lower doses. The HDL reduction was reversible on stopping. PMID 28449232 (Clark 2017, Br J Clin Pharmacol); PMID 36283827 (Mohan 2023)

  7. Can perturb the liver and muscle — transient transaminase elevations and rare muscle breakdown Evidence-human

    Transient rises in ALT/hepatic transaminases were seen in the Phase 1b and Phase 2 studies, and a systematic review reported two participants exposed to GSK2881078 in a clinical trial who developed rhabdomyolysis (muscle breakdown that can injure the kidneys). Whether the liver signal reflects direct drug-induced liver injury versus the muscle-anabolic program raising enzymes is not resolved for this compound, but the SARM class carries a documented drug-induced-liver-injury signal (mean ALT elevation ~7.1% across trials in the review). PMID 36283827 (Mohan 2023); PMID 37218811 (Vignali 2023, J Xenobiot); PMID 29982690 (Neil 2018)

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Non-steroidal small-molecule agonist at the androgen receptor (AR, NR3C4) ligand-binding domain. In a PC3(AR)2 prostate-cell reporter-gene assay GSK2881078 activated the AR with EC50 ~3.99x10^-9 M (~4 nM); in a yeast androgen screen it was far weaker (EC50 ~4.44x10^-6 M), illustrating assay dependence. In silico docking to the AR LBD showed a binding mode distinct from RAD-140 and GLPG0492 — consistent with (but not proof of) the class idea that subtle pose differences drive tissue-selective coregulator recruitment.

Signalling cascade. Ligand-bound AR dissociates from chaperones, translocates to the nucleus, and binds androgen-response elements to modulate transcription. In skeletal muscle this favors net protein accretion (increased protein synthesis, satellite-cell/myonuclear support, growth-factor modulation including IGF-1). In liver, AR activation suppresses HDL-cholesterol and SHBG production — observed as dose-dependent pharmacodynamic biomarkers in humans. Muscle transcriptomics specific to GSK2881078 in humans have not been published; the muscle cascade is inferred from androgen biology plus downstream lean-mass/strength readouts.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Oral, once daily (repeat-dose regimens used a brief twice-daily loading phase in some studies)
Absorption / bioavailability
Orally bioavailable; dose-proportional increases in systemic exposure across the studied range in the first-in-human study
Half-life
Long — greater than 100 hours, supporting once-daily dosing and meaningful accumulation to steady state
Metabolism
Hepatic; CYP3A4 involvement was specifically studied — co-administration with the CYP3A4 inhibitor itraconazole (in the NCT02567773 program) produced a PK change judged unlikely to be clinically significant
Excretion
Not fully characterized in the public clinical literature; metabolite profiling has been done chiefly in doping-control and equine models (Phase I metabolites mainly hydroxylated; no glucuronide/sulfate conjugates detected in the equine study)
Notes
The very long half-life means both effects and any adverse pharmacodynamic changes (e.g. HDL lowering) persist well after a dose. Equine liver-microsome and human hair/urine metabolite studies exist primarily for anti-doping detection (peak urinary ~920 pg/mL at 8 h after a 760 ug dose; detectable to ~day 42).

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

Clear dose-response in humans for BOTH the desired effect and the biomarkers of concern. Lean mass increased dose-dependently (PMID 29982690). HDL-cholesterol and SHBG fell dose-dependently (PMID 28449232) — e.g. in women at 0.35 mg, HDL fell by ~0.518 mmol/L (95% CI -0.703 to -0.334) and SHBG by ~39.1 nmol/L (95% CI -48.5 to -29.7) vs placebo. Women consistently showed effects at LOWER doses than men. The Phase 2 COPD trial used 1.0 mg/day (women) and 2.0 mg/day (men). Because desired and undesired effects rise together, no demonstrated 'clean' dose separates muscle benefit from HDL lowering. NO safe or therapeutic dose has been established; no dosing protocol is provided here.

How it compares

  • vs Testosterone / anabolic-androgenic steroids
    same: Both act through the androgen receptor to build muscle and lower HDL/SHBG
    different: GSK2881078 is oral, non-steroidal, and intended to be tissue-selective (more muscle, less prostate/virilizing effect); it is not aromatized to estrogen and has a very long half-life. It lacks steroids' decades of clinical experience and their better-characterized (if imperfect) risk profile.
  • vs Enobosarm (ostarine, MK-2866)
    same:
    different: Enobosarm is the most clinically advanced SARM with larger Phase 2/3 cachexia and breast-cancer programs and far more human data; GSK2881078's distinction is being one of few with a completed muscle-strength Phase 2, but its overall dataset is smaller and its development looks dormant.
  • vs RAD-140 (testolone) / GLPG0492
    same:
    different: Head-to-head in vitro, GSK2881078 shares potent AR agonism but a distinct AR-LBD docking pose; RAD-140 is largely preclinical/illicit-market with no comparable human strength trial, whereas GSK2881078 has Phase 2 human data.

History & legal status

Developed by GlaxoSmithKline's Muscle Metabolism unit as an oral SARM for muscle weakness/cachexia and functional limitation in chronic and acute illness. First-in-human study began ~2014 (NCT02045940); PK/PD paper published 2017 (Clark); Phase 1b lean-mass study published 2018 (Neil, NCT02567773); Phase 2A COPD trial ran 2018–2019 and published in Thorax in 2023 (Mohan, NCT03359473). No regulatory approval anywhere and no newer trials registered — the program appears discontinued or shelved. GSK2881078 is on the WADA Prohibited List as an anabolic agent, and substantial literature exists purely for anti-doping detection in human urine/hair and in equine sport. It is also encountered as an illicitly sold 'research chemical' among recreational users despite being unapproved.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Whether GSK2881078 produces any clinically meaningful functional or quality-of-life benefit (Phase 2 showed a borderline, endpoint-dependent strength signal in men but no patient-reported improvement)
  • Why the strength effect reached significance in men but not women, despite women being MORE sensitive on lean mass and biomarkers
  • Long-term cardiovascular consequences of sustained HDL lowering
  • True incidence and mechanism of the rhabdomyolysis and liver signals
  • Whether tissue 'selectivity' holds up over long-term human exposure (prostate, virilization, fertility, bone effects not characterized long-term)
  • Current development status and the reason GSK apparently halted the program
  • Effects in younger/athletic populations (all human efficacy studies were in older or diseased adults)

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

For clinicians assessing someone who has used (or is using) a product sold as GSK2881078 — this is monitoring guidance, NOT an endorsement of use and NOT a protocol: (1) Liver — baseline and periodic ALT/AST/bilirubin; investigate symptoms of hepatotoxicity (fatigue, jaundice, dark urine, right-upper-quadrant pain) given the SARM-class DILI signal. (2) Muscle/kidney — if muscle pain, weakness, or dark urine, check creatine kinase and renal function to catch rhabdomyolysis. (3) Lipids — a fasting lipid panel will typically show suppressed HDL, which is expected and reversible on stopping. (4) Endocrine — SHBG and testosterone may be suppressed; consider hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis assessment. (5) Emphasize that products sold online are unregulated and frequently mislabeled or adulterated, that no safe dose is established, and that discontinuation is the intervention that reverses the biochemical changes. Refer for medical evaluation rather than attempting self-management.

Caveats

GSK2881078 is a pipeline compound with THIN and almost entirely GSK-generated human data — treat all efficacy claims as preliminary. What is genuinely established for this compound in humans: it activates the androgen receptor, increases lean mass, and lowers HDL/SHBG. What is NOT established: (1) any clinically meaningful functional benefit — the Phase 2 COPD trial showed a strength gain in MEN ONLY, significant only on the percent-predicted endpoint (the absolute-kg endpoint's 90% CI crossed zero), with NO improvement in patient-reported outcomes; (2) long-term safety — trials ran weeks, not years; (3) that its 'selectivity' actually spares androgenic tissue in humans over time (this is inferred, not molecularly demonstrated for this drug). Real safety signals include reversible HDL lowering, transient liver-enzyme elevations, and two reported cases of rhabdomyolysis in trial participants; the broader SARM class carries documented drug-induced liver injury and tendon-rupture reports (tendon rupture has NOT been specifically reported for GSK2881078). This is not an approved drug, there is no established safe dose, product sold as 'GSK2881078' online is unregulated and often mislabeled, and none of this is medical advice or an endorsement of use. Sex matters: women were more sensitive to both anabolic and lipid effects at lower doses.
