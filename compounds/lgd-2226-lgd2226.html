LGD-2226

Main target: Androgen receptor. Evidence is animal- and lab-only — no human trials yet.

EvidenceRodent / preclinical
ApprovalNot FDA-approved

Ligand Pharmaceuticals' early tissue-selective SARM — a proof-of-concept quinolinone that built muscle and bone while largely sparing the prostate in rats, then quietly disappeared before humans. A history-book reference, not a usable drug.

Rodent / preclinical 9 citations
Class
Nonsteroidal selective androgen receptor modulator (SARM); 6-N,N-bis(2,2,2-trifluoroethyl)amino-4-trifluoromethyl-quinolin-2(1H)-one chemotype (bicyclic quinolinone/aminoquinolinone class)
Primary target
Androgen receptor (AR, NR3C4) — high-affinity, nonsteroidal, nonaromatizable selective agonist; full anabolic agonist in muscle and bone, reduced/partial agonist in prostate
Human status
No human efficacy or safety data exist for LGD-2226. The entire evidence base is preclinical: Ligand Pharmaceuticals' in vitro receptor/cell studies and orchidectomized-rat models (PMID 17023534, 17034117, 18063690). It served as an early proof-of-concept for tissue-selective AR agonism and is cited as a canonical reference in SARM history/appraisal reviews, but it was discontinued during development and never advanced to a registered human trial for LGD2226 specifically (a ClinicalTrials.gov search returns zero LGD-2226 trials). It is NOT approved for any indication anywhere. Any circulating 'LGD-2226' in the gray/research-chemical market is unregulated, unverified, and not backed by human data; SARMs are prohibited in sport by WADA, and LGD-2226 specifically appears in anti-doping metabolite work (equine in vitro; PMID 34714606).

At a glance

  • Increased skeletal muscle anabolic activity and body composition (rodent, castrated models)
  • Increased bone mass AND increased bone strength on biomechanical testing (rodent) — above sham levels
  • Reduced/partial stimulation of the prostate relative to steroidal androgens (the defining 'tissue-selective' effect, rodent)
  • Maintained male sexual behavior in a rat mating-behavior model
  • No estrogen conversion (nonaromatizable) and no DHT amplification (not 5-alpha-reduced)
  • NOTE: all efficacy is preclinical/rodent and in vitro — no demonstrated human efficacy exists for LGD2226

Mechanism, step by step

The step-by-step story of how it works — each step tagged by how sure we are.

8 steps7 rodent / preclinical 1 contested / speculative

  1. Binds the androgen receptor selectively Evidence-in vitro

    LGD2226 is a nonsteroidal small molecule that binds directly and with high affinity to the androgen receptor (AR), and shows virtually no meaningful affinity for other intracellular (nuclear) receptors (estrogen, progesterone, glucocorticoid, mineralocorticoid). This is what makes it an 'AR-selective' ligand rather than a broad steroid. PMID 17023534; PMID 17034117

  2. It is not aromatized to estrogen and not 5-alpha-reduced Evidence-rodent

    Unlike testosterone, LGD2226 is nonaromatizable and nonsteroidal, so it is not converted into estrogen by aromatase (CYP19) or amplified into DHT by 5-alpha-reductase. Its effects come from the AR itself rather than from downstream estrogenic or DHT metabolites — which is why it can drive bone and sexual effects without the estrogen route. PMID 17023534

  3. Induces a distinct receptor shape ('unique conformation') Evidence-in vitro

    When LGD2226 sits in the AR ligand-binding pocket it produces a different pattern of protein-protein interactions than testosterone or steroidal androgens, implying it bends the receptor's ligand-binding domain into a distinct conformation. This altered surface is the proposed molecular basis for why it behaves differently in different tissues. PMID 17023534

  4. The altered shape is proposed to recruit different coregulators in different tissues Contested

    The prevailing class model (not proven specifically for LGD2226) is that the ligand-specific AR conformation changes which coactivator/corepressor proteins are recruited. Because tissues differ in their coregulator makeup, the same receptor can act as a full agonist in muscle/bone and only a partial agonist in prostate. This is the leading explanation for SARM tissue selectivity, but it is an incompletely proven, extrapolated model. PMID 19357508; PMID 17086931

  5. Activated AR turns on muscle anabolic genes Evidence-in vitro

    In muscle cells the liganded AR drives transcription of skeletal muscle genes. LGD2226 activated the human skeletal alpha-actin promoter in muscle (C2C12) cells but not in prostate, liver, or breast cell lines — a concrete example of cell-specific gene activation that tracks with its anabolic muscle effect. PMID 18063690

  6. Drives muscle growth and stronger bone in castrated rodents Evidence-rodent

    In orchidectomized (castrated) rats, oral LGD2226 increased muscle and bone anabolic activity, and biomechanical testing showed the treated bones were stronger than sham controls — i.e., the anabolic signal translated into functional tissue, not just numbers on a scan. PMID 17023534

  7. Only partly stimulates the prostate ('tissue selectivity') Evidence-rodent

    At doses that drove muscle and bone anabolism, LGD2226 had reduced impact on prostate growth compared with a steroidal androgen — behaving as a full agonist anabolically but a weaker/partial agonist in the prostate. This dissociation is the entire point of the SARM concept and what LGD2226 was built to demonstrate. PMID 17023534

  8. Preserves male sexual behavior in a rat model Evidence-rodent

    LGD2226 was also active in a male-rat sexual-behavior model (mounts, intromissions, ejaculations, copulation efficiency), showing the compound reaches and activates AR in tissues governing sex function — evidence the anabolic and libido-supporting arms of androgen action can be retained without full prostate stimulation. PMID 17023534

Receptor pharmacology

How tightly it grabs its targets, and what that binding does.

Binding & selectivity. High-affinity, selective nonsteroidal ligand for the androgen receptor (AR/NR3C4) with virtually no meaningful affinity for other intracellular (nuclear) receptors — estrogen, progesterone, glucocorticoid, mineralocorticoid. It is nonaromatizable and nonsteroidal, so it does not generate estrogenic metabolites and is not a 5-alpha-reductase substrate. Functionally it is a full anabolic agonist in muscle and bone but a reduced/partial agonist in prostate (PMID 17023534, 17034117).

Signalling cascade. Ligand binds AR ligand-binding domain → induces a ligand-specific conformation distinct from testosterone/steroidal androgens (documented by a unique protein-protein interaction pattern) → altered receptor surface topology → proposed tissue-dependent coregulator (coactivator/corepressor) recruitment → nuclear translocation and binding to androgen-response elements → tissue-specific transcription (e.g. skeletal alpha-actin in muscle) → anabolic phenotype in muscle/bone with attenuated prostate response. The coregulator step is the class-level explanatory model and is not proven end-to-end for this molecule (PMID 19357508, 17086931); the distinct conformation and cell-specific gene activation are directly shown for LGD2226 (PMID 17023534, 18063690).

Pharmacokinetics

How the body absorbs it, moves it around, and clears it out (ADME).

Route / form
Oral (orally active in rats — a design goal of the quinolinone series; the parent van Oeveren compound class was selected specifically for oral availability). No human PK exists.
Absorption / bioavailability
Orally bioavailable in rodents; specific human bioavailability, Cmax, and Tmax are not established (no human data). No published quantitative rodent PK parameters for LGD2226 as a named value in the accessible primary reports.
Half-life
Unknown in humans; not reported for LGD2226.
Metabolism
Not characterized in humans. As a fluorinated aminoquinolinone it is not a substrate for aromatase or 5-alpha-reductase (by design). An equine in vitro (liver microsome) metabolite study for doping control profiled seven nonsteroidal SARMs including LGD-2226, identifying three phase I metabolites for it (PMID 34714606); human hepatic metabolism is presumed but not defined.
Excretion
Not characterized in humans; no reported human elimination half-life or excretion route.
Notes
Any PK figure quoted for 'LGD-2226' from vendor/forum sources is not supported by peer-reviewed human data. Treat all PK as an evidence gap.

Dose–response & effect magnitudes

How the effect changes with dose, where the data exists.

Dose-response is defined only in rodent and cell-based models. In orchidectomized rats, oral LGD2226 produced anabolic effects on muscle and bone (increasing bone strength on biomechanical testing above sham) while producing a blunted, submaximal (partial-agonist) effect on prostate growth relative to a full steroidal androgen — i.e., the anabolic and prostate responses are dissociated, which is the qualitative signature of a SARM (PMID 17023534). No formal multi-dose human dose-response curve exists; no human minimally effective dose and no human therapeutic index exist. Per harm-reduction scope, this monograph gives NO dosing protocol.

How it compares

How it stacks up against similar compounds.

  • vs Testosterone / steroidal androgens
    same: Both act through the androgen receptor and both are expected to suppress the HPG axis / endogenous testosterone when effective.
    different: LGD2226 is nonsteroidal and nonaromatizable — no estrogen conversion, no 5-alpha-reduction to DHT, and it induces a DISTINCT AR conformation. It aims to give the anabolic (muscle/bone) and libido benefits of testosterone with less prostate stimulation and no estrogenic route (PMID 17023534).
  • vs LGD-4033 (ligandrol) and LGD-3303 (also Ligand-derived)
    same: All three are nonsteroidal SARMs targeting the AR with the same tissue-selective design goal.
    different: Commonly confused by name but chemically and developmentally distinct. LGD-4033 (ligandrol) reached human trials and has human safety reports including a documented case of drug-induced liver injury (PMID 39421081); LGD-3303 is a pyrroloquinolinone with its own rodent PK/PD work (PMID 19017848). LGD-2226 has NO human data and was discontinued earlier. Do not transfer claims between them.
  • vs Ostarine (MK-2866/enobosarm) and RAD140 (testolone)
    same: Same mechanistic class (nonsteroidal AR modulators), same claimed muscle/bone-vs-prostate dissociation, same WADA-prohibited status.
    different: Those aryl-propionamide (ostarine) and other-chemotype SARMs advanced further clinically and have human safety/efficacy data and case reports of liver injury; LGD-2226 (quinolinone) never generated comparable human evidence and is essentially a preclinical reference compound.

History & legal status

Where it came from, and its approval and legal status.

Developed by Ligand Pharmaceuticals (San Diego) in the mid-2000s as part of an effort to create orally active, tissue-selective androgens for osteoporosis, frailty, muscle wasting, and sexual dysfunction. It emerged from the 6-dialkylamino-4-trifluoromethylquinolinone medicinal-chemistry program (van Oeveren 2006, PMID 17034117) and was characterized as a proof-of-concept SARM by Miner et al. 2007 (PMID 17023534). It became a canonical reference compound in SARM history and appraisal reviews (PMID 17086931, 19357508). It was DISCONTINUED during development and never reached a registered human efficacy trial under the LGD-2226 name (a ClinicalTrials.gov search returns zero LGD-2226 trials); the specific reason for discontinuation is not documented in the accessible peer-reviewed record. It has no marketing approval in any jurisdiction. Like all SARMs it is prohibited in sport by WADA and is not a legal dietary supplement; LGD-2226 specifically appears in doping-control metabolite literature (equine in vitro; PMID 34714606). Any consumer-facing 'LGD-2226' is an unapproved research chemical.

Key unknowns / open questions

What we still don't know.

  • Human pharmacokinetics: bioavailability, half-life, metabolism, and elimination are entirely unknown for LGD2226.
  • Human efficacy: whether the rodent muscle/bone gains translate to humans was never tested.
  • Human safety, including hepatotoxicity, cardiovascular/lipid effects, and HPG-axis suppression, is uncharacterized for this specific compound.
  • The reason for discontinuation (a specific toxicity finding vs portfolio/attrition) is not documented in the accessible peer-reviewed record.
  • Whether the 'unique AR conformation' seen in vitro produces genuinely durable prostate sparing in humans (rodent selectivity does not guarantee human selectivity).
  • The completeness of the coregulator-recruitment tissue-selectivity model for LGD2226 specifically (extrapolated from the class, not proven end-to-end).
  • Identity/purity of any material sold as 'LGD-2226' — gray-market products are frequently mislabeled or contaminated with other SARMs.

What to monitor

What to keep an eye on if it's used — bloodwork and symptoms.

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

Educational harm-reduction only — this is not endorsement of use, and no dosing is provided. Because LGD-2226 has no human safety data and SARMs as a class have documented human harms, anyone who has taken a product labeled 'LGD-2226' should be aware of what clinicians typically monitor with SARM exposure: (1) LIVER — liver function tests (ALT, AST, bilirubin), because SARM-class drug-induced liver injury is real and can be severe; new fatigue, dark urine, jaundice, or right-upper-quadrant pain warrant prompt medical evaluation and stopping. (2) LIPIDS — a fasting lipid panel, watching for HDL-cholesterol suppression. (3) HORMONES — total/free testosterone, LH, and FSH to detect HPG-axis suppression; a follow-up panel after cessation can help confirm recovery. (4) HEMATOLOGY — hematocrit/hemoglobin (androgens can raise red-cell mass). (5) PROSTATE (older males) — PSA and urinary symptoms. (6) In females — signs of virilization (voice change, hirsutism), some of which can be irreversible. Product-identity risk is high: material sold under this name is unregulated and may contain a different or contaminated compound. Athletes: SARMs are WADA-prohibited in and out of competition. Any of this should be done under medical supervision — and, given the complete absence of human data, the safest course is not to use it at all.

Caveats

Read this as a historical/mechanistic entry, not a usage guide. (1) Evidence tier is thin and old: the pivotal data are rodent and cell-based from ~2006-2008; there is no human pharmacokinetics, no human efficacy, and no human safety dataset for LGD2226. (2) 'Prostate-sparing' is a relative, rodent finding — reduced, not zero, prostate stimulation, and rodent prostate selectivity does not guarantee human safety. (3) The tissue-selectivity mechanism (coregulator recruitment) is a plausible but still incompletely proven model, extrapolated from the SARM class rather than demonstrated end-to-end for this molecule (PMID 19357508). (4) It was discontinued during development; the specific reason (a toxicity finding vs portfolio/attrition) is NOT documented in the accessible peer-reviewed record, and no dosing protocol should be inferred. (5) SARMs as a class carry documented human harms (drug-induced liver injury, HDL suppression, HPG-axis suppression) — but those specific human reports are for OTHER SARMs (e.g. LGD-4033/ligandrol, ostarine), not for LGD2226, which has no human data at all. (6) LGD-2226 is frequently confused with LGD-4033 (ligandrol) and LGD-3303 — they are distinct molecules with distinct data; do not transfer claims between them.
