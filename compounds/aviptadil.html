Aviptadil

Synthetic copy of the natural 28-amino-acid neuropeptide VIP: a lung-protective vasodilator that convincingly relaxes vessels, calms macrophages, and boosts surfactant in the lab and in early trials — but failed its rigorous NIH-run Phase 3 COVID-ARDS readout (TESICO) and missed its sponsor Phase 2b/3 primary endpoint (Youssef 2022), so the human survival benefit remains unproven.

Human / approved 10 citations
Class
Vasoactive intestinal peptide (VIP) analog; a 28-amino-acid peptide agonist of class B1 secretin-family GPCRs (VPAC1, VPAC2, and, with lower affinity, PAC1)
Primary target
VPAC1 and VPAC2 G-protein-coupled receptors (also PAC1), predominantly Gs-coupled -> adenylate cyclase -> cAMP
Human status
Established peptide with a long regulatory history but NO current major approval for its headline indication. Aviptadil (as RLF-100/ZYESAMI) held US FDA Orphan Drug Designation for ARDS and for pulmonary arterial hypertension and received FDA Fast Track designation for ARDS/acute lung injury in COVID-19, and was studied under IND — but it is NOT FDA-approved for COVID-19, ARDS, or PAH. Its COVID trials were negative: the NIH-run TESICO trial (NCT04843761; modified ITT n=461) found no improvement in the day-90 six-category ordinal outcome (OR 1.11, 95% CI 0.80-1.55, p=0.54) and no mortality benefit (38% vs 36%, HR 1.04, 95% CI 0.77-1.41, p=0.78), and was stopped for futility; the sponsor-run 60-day RCT (Youssef et al. 2022, Crit Care Med, PMID 36044317; NCT04311697, n=196 analyzed) did NOT meet its primary endpoint (alive and free of respiratory failure at day 60; OR 1.6, 95% CI 0.86-3.11). A VIP/phentolamine combination (Invicorp) IS approved in several countries (e.g., UK, Scandinavia) for intracavernosal treatment of erectile dysfunction — a genuinely established, separate use. Community interest in aviptadil for anti-inflammatory/longevity/erectile purposes is running well ahead of any approved indication for the single agent.

At a glance

  • Selective pulmonary vasodilation (small, transient in humans)
  • Bronchodilation / airway smooth-muscle relaxation
  • Anti-inflammatory: lowers TNF-alpha, IL-6, iNOS-driven NO; raises IL-10 (largely rodent/in vitro)
  • Promotes regulatory T-cell phenotype (immune dampening)
  • Upregulates pulmonary surfactant (phospholipid + SP-A) in rodent models
  • Systemic vasodilation at higher IV doses -> flushing, hypotension, tachycardia, diarrhea
  • Cavernosal smooth-muscle relaxation supporting penile erection (with phentolamine)
  • Claimed but UNPROVEN survival/recovery benefit in COVID ARDS — negative NIH Phase 3 (TESICO) and missed sponsor Phase 2b/3 primary endpoint (Youssef 2022)

Mechanism, step by step

  1. Binds VPAC1/VPAC2 (and PAC1) GPCRs Established

    Aviptadil is an exact synthetic copy of native human VIP, so it activates the same three class-B GPCRs VIP normally uses: VPAC1, VPAC2, and (less potently) PAC1. VPAC1 is constitutive on macrophages, lung epithelium and vascular smooth muscle; VPAC2 is induced on activated immune cells. This is a receptor-agonist mechanism, not enzyme inhibition. Dickson & Finlayson, Pharmacol Ther 2009, PMID 19109992

  2. Gs coupling raises intracellular cAMP Established

    The receptors preferentially couple to Gs, so agonism activates adenylate cyclase and drives up cAMP (with some cell-dependent calcium and phospholipase-D signaling also reported). cAMP is the shared second messenger behind essentially all of aviptadil's downstream effects — vasodilation, anti-inflammatory tone, and surfactant gene expression. Dickson & Finlayson, Pharmacol Ther 2009, PMID 19109992

  3. Vascular/airway smooth-muscle relaxation -> vasodilation and bronchodilation Evidence-human

    In smooth muscle, the cAMP rise relaxes the cell. In the lung this dilates pulmonary vessels and airways. A single inhaled 100-microg dose of aviptadil produced a real but small and short-lived SELECTIVE pulmonary vasodilation in a human right-heart-catheter study (20 patients), improving stroke volume and mixed-venous O2 saturation without dropping systemic blood pressure — confirming the vasodilator mechanism operates in people, even if modestly. Leuchte et al., Eur Respir J 2008, PMID 18978135

  4. Macrophage 'deactivation' -> suppressed TNF-alpha, IL-6, iNOS/NO Evidence-rodent

    Via cAMP, VIP/aviptadil suppresses the pro-inflammatory transcription factors NF-kappaB and IRF-1, cutting production of inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), TNF-alpha and IL-6 while nudging up anti-inflammatory IL-10. VIP and PACAP were formally dubbed 'macrophage deactivating factors' for this. Demonstrated in mouse/rat peritoneal macrophages and the RAW 264.7 line, in vitro and in vivo; this is the mechanistic basis for the 'anti-cytokine-storm' rationale in ARDS. Delgado et al., J Immunol 1999, PMID 10202009

  5. Shifts adaptive immunity toward regulatory T cells Evidence-human

    Beyond innate cells, VIP can convert naive CD4+ T cells into FoxP3+ regulatory T cells. In a human sarcoidosis Phase 2 study (20 patients), 4 weeks of inhaled VIP significantly lowered TNF-alpha production by bronchoalveolar-lavage cells and increased functional regulatory T cells — described by the authors as the first human demonstration of VIP's immunoregulatory effect. Prasse et al., Am J Respir Crit Care Med 2010, PMID 20442436

  6. Upregulates pulmonary surfactant in alveolar type II cells Evidence-rodent

    VPAC receptors are dense on alveolar type II cells, which make surfactant. In rat ATII cells and lung explants, VIP induces surfactant protein A via enhanced gene transcription requiring a PKC -> c-Fos pathway (and prior work showed increased surfactant phospholipid synthesis). Because surfactant keeps alveoli open and modulates lung host defense, this is the 'lung-protective' arm of the ARDS rationale — but the direct surfactant data are from rodent cells/explants, not human ARDS lungs. Li et al., Peptides 2010, PMID 20688121

  7. Proposed direct antiviral / cytoprotective effect on SARS-CoV-2 entry Evidence-in vitro

    In vitro (CaCo-2 epithelial cells), VIP downregulates ACE2 and TMPRSS2 and triggers their shedding via the sheddase ADAM10, reducing SARS-CoV-2 pseudovirus entry. This is the most speculative link in the ARDS story: it is cell-culture data only and underpins the theoretical 'block viral entry in pneumocytes' claim used to justify the COVID trials. Gutzler et al., Int J Mol Sci 2025, PMID 40141308

Receptor pharmacology

Binding & selectivity. Aviptadil is chemically identical to endogenous human VIP (28-residue peptide: His-Ser-Asp-Ala-Val-Phe-Thr-Asp-Asn-Tyr-Thr-Arg-Leu-Arg-Lys-Gln-Met-Ala-Val-Lys-Lys-Tyr-Leu-Asn-Ser-Ile-Leu-Asn). It binds the class B1 secretin-family GPCRs VPAC1 and VPAC2 with high affinity and PAC1 with lower affinity (PAC1 is PACAP-preferring). VIP and PACAP share ~68% sequence identity and act through this shared receptor set. Receptor expression is tissue-specific: VPAC1 is constitutive on macrophages, lung epithelium and vascular smooth muscle; VPAC2 is inducible on activated lymphocytes and smooth muscle.

Signalling cascade. Predominantly Gs -> adenylate cyclase -> cAMP -> PKA, with documented secondary signaling through calcium and phospholipase D depending on cell type, and PKC -> c-Fos in alveolar type II cells driving surfactant protein A transcription (rodent data). cAMP elevation underlies vasodilation, the anti-inflammatory transcriptional program (NF-kappaB/IRF-1 suppression), and surfactant gene induction. Receptors undergo classic GPCR desensitization on sustained agonism.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Route / form
Intravenous continuous infusion for systemic/ARDS use (ZYESAMI/RLF-100); nebulized/inhaled for pulmonary-targeted use (sarcoidosis, PAH, inhaled COVID trials); intracavernosal for erectile dysfunction (with phentolamine, Invicorp). It is a peptide — not orally bioavailable (would be digested).
Absorption / bioavailability
No meaningful oral bioavailability. IV gives 100% systemic exposure by definition. Inhaled delivery targets the lung with limited systemic spillover, which is why inhaled dosing produced pulmonary effects (vasodilation, BAL cytokine changes) without systemic hypotension in human studies.
Half-life
Native VIP plasma disappearance half-time is approximately 1 minute by first-order kinetics in a human infusion study; apparent metabolic clearance ~9 mL/kg/min and apparent volume of distribution ~14 mL/kg. This ultra-short half-life is why systemic therapy requires continuous infusion rather than bolus dosing.
Metabolism
Rapid proteolytic degradation by peptidases in plasma and tissue (as for native VIP); not a cytochrome-P450 substrate, so classic CYP drug-drug interactions are not the concern — additive vasodilation with other vasodilators is the practical interaction.
Excretion
Cleared by peptidase-mediated proteolysis rather than intact renal/biliary excretion; degradation products follow normal amino-acid handling.
Notes
The ~1-minute half-life is the single most important PK fact for interpreting community claims: aviptadil cannot be a 'take-it-and-forget-it' systemic anti-inflammatory. In TESICO it was given as a daily 12-hour IV infusion for 3 days precisely to sustain plasma levels.

Dose–response &amp; effect magnitudes

Dose-response differs by compartment. Inhaled/low systemic exposure yields the desired local effects (a single inhaled 100-microg dose produced measurable selective pulmonary vasodilation and improved mixed-venous O2 saturation without systemic BP change; PMID 18978135). As systemic exposure rises, on-target VIP effects emerge and become dose-limiting: in a human infusion study, increasing the infusion rate (0.6 -> 1.3 -> 3.3 pmol/kg/min) raised pulse rate and blood-pressure amplitude and caused cutaneous flushing, with slight but significant rises in glucose, free fatty acids and calcium at the top dose (PMID 730072) — mirroring Verner-Morrison (VIPoma) physiology. The TESICO regimen escalated the IV target over 3 days (600 -> 1200 -> 1800 pmol/kg) to sustain exposure while managing vasodilator side effects. Key point: higher exposure buys more vasodilation and more side effects, but in the definitive NIH trial did NOT buy a survival benefit — efficacy did not track with the pharmacologically active dose.

How it compares

  • vs Inhaled nitric oxide (iNO) / inhaled epoprostenol (selective pulmonary vasodilators used in ARDS/PH)
    same: All are selective pulmonary vasodilators that can improve oxygenation/right-heart load without systemic hypotension when delivered to the lung.
    different: Aviptadil additionally has anti-inflammatory (macrophage-deactivating, Treg-promoting) and surfactant-upregulating actions via cAMP, whereas iNO (cGMP) and prostacyclin (cAMP via IP receptors) are essentially pure vasodilators. Like them, aviptadil's human pulmonary vasodilation is modest and short-lived — and none of the three has an established ARDS mortality benefit.
  • vs PACAP (endogenous sibling peptide)
    same: ~68% sequence identity; both activate VPAC1/VPAC2 and are 'macrophage deactivating factors'.
    different: PACAP is the high-affinity PAC1 ligand; aviptadil/VIP binds PAC1 with lower affinity. Aviptadil is the one that reached Phase 3 as a drug.
  • vs Engineered stabilized VIP analogs (e.g., RO 25-1553 and related)
    same: Same VPAC agonism and pulmonary vasodilator/bronchodilator intent.
    different: Engineered analogs were designed to resist proteolysis and extend the ~1-minute half-life of native VIP; aviptadil itself is unmodified native sequence and thus very short-acting.
  • vs Dexamethasone / tocilizumab (proven COVID anti-inflammatories)
    same: Shared 'dampen the cytokine response in COVID lung injury' rationale.
    different: Dexamethasone and (in selected patients) IL-6 blockade have RCT-proven mortality benefit in COVID; aviptadil does NOT — it failed its definitive NIH trial. Mechanistically aviptadil is broader (vasodilation + surfactant) but clinically unproven.

History &amp; legal status

VIP was discovered by Said and Mutt in the early 1970s as a gut vasodilator peptide. Aviptadil is the synthetic native-sequence VIP. It received US FDA Orphan Drug Designation for ARDS and for pulmonary arterial hypertension and EMEA orphan designation for ARDS and sarcoid, obtained IND clearance for human trials, and was granted FDA Fast Track designation for ARDS/acute lung injury in COVID-19 — but never a full marketing approval for these indications. Inhaled VIP was explored for sarcoidosis and PAH in the 2000s-2010s. During COVID-19 it was repurposed as RLF-100/ZYESAMI (NRx Pharmaceuticals / Relief Therapeutics / APR Applied Pharma Research); early single-arm and small-RCT signals generated substantial media and community attention, but the sponsor's 60-day Phase 2b/3 RCT (Youssef 2022, NCT04311697) missed its primary endpoint and the pivotal NIH-run TESICO trial (2023, NCT04843761) was negative and stopped for futility. Two inhaled programs were halted (AVICOVID-2, NCT04360096, terminated; AVICOVID-3, NCT05137795, withdrawn with zero enrollment). Separately, a VIP + phentolamine combination (Invicorp) is an approved intracavernosal therapy for erectile dysfunction in several countries — the one clearly established human indication for VIP as a drug.

Key unknowns / open questions

  • Whether aviptadil provides ANY clinical benefit in ARDS — the definitive NIH trial (TESICO) was negative and the sponsor Phase 2b/3 missed its primary endpoint, so the burden of proof now sits heavily against it.
  • Whether a specific narrow subgroup (e.g., the high-flow-nasal-oxygen or mechanically ventilated subgroups nominally favored in Youssef 2022) could benefit — unconfirmed and plausibly a false positive after a failed primary.
  • Whether inhaled (lung-targeted) dosing could outperform IV by decoupling local benefit from systemic vasodilator side effects — inhaled COVID trials were terminated/withdrawn and did not establish efficacy.
  • Whether the in-vitro antiviral (ACE2/TMPRSS2 shedding) effect occurs at all in vivo in humans.
  • The real-world safety of aviptadil sourced outside clinical trials for off-label anti-inflammatory/'longevity'/erectile use — no controlled data, and the native peptide's ~1-min half-life makes gray-market single-injection systemic use pharmacologically incoherent.
  • Whether engineered long-acting VIP/VPAC analogs (not aviptadil itself) could revive the pulmonary-vasodilator concept with better PK.

What to monitor

Harm-reduction context — not a dosing protocol.

For harm-reduction awareness only — not a protocol and not medical advice. Because the dominant effects are vasodilatory, the parameters clinicians watch during legitimate use are blood pressure (for hypotension), heart rate (for tachycardia), and symptomatic flushing during and after infusion; GI tolerance (secretory diarrhea, the VIPoma-like effect); and, given the critically ill populations studied, oxygenation and overall respiratory/organ-failure status. Blood glucose can shift transiently at high systemic exposure. For the intracavernosal ED combination, the relevant watch-outs are prolonged erection/priapism (seek urgent medical care if an erection persists beyond a few hours) and injection-site effects. Anyone considering aviptadil should know it is not an approved treatment for ARDS/COVID/inflammation and did not show benefit in its definitive trials; unsupervised systemic use carries real hypotension risk and no demonstrated benefit. This library does not provide dosing or 'how to run it' guidance.

Caveats

HYPE-VS-DATA: The mechanism is real and elegant (vasodilation + anti-inflammation + surfactant, all via cAMP), and early single-arm and small-RCT signals were encouraging — but the sponsor's own Phase 2b/3 RCT (Youssef 2022, PMID 36044317) missed its primary endpoint, and the definitive, NIH-run TESICO trial (PMID 37348524) showed NO benefit on clinical status or mortality and was stopped for futility. Positive-sounding secondary/subgroup analyses (e.g., the nominal day-60 survival signal, OR 2.0, and the ventilated-subgroup signal in Youssef 2022) came after a failed primary and are hypothesis-generating only — they should not be read as proof of efficacy. HUMAN-VS-RODENT: The strongest-sounding arms of the story (surfactant upregulation, iNOS/NF-kappaB suppression, direct antiviral entry-blocking) are rodent, explant, or in-vitro; the confirmed HUMAN effects are a modest transient selective pulmonary vasodilation and a BAL-level immunoregulatory shift in sarcoidosis. PK REALITY: Native VIP has an ~1-minute plasma half-life, so any systemic effect requires continuous IV infusion — the community framing of aviptadil as a convenient injectable 'anti-inflammatory' ignores this. SAFETY: Predictable vasodilator effects (hypotension, flushing, tachycardia, diarrhea) are the main issue; in TESICO the day-5 composite safety endpoint was numerically higher with aviptadil (63% vs 56%) but NOT statistically significant (OR 1.40, 95% CI 0.94-2.08; p=0.10).
