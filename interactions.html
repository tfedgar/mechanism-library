Interactions & stacking

How they combine.

Mechanistic interactions across the library — shared targets, enzyme competition, and compounding vs counteracting effects — grounded in the monographs and adversarially verified. Mechanism only, never a protocol. 271 verified interactions.

  • Amycretin × Clenbuterol Evidence-human
    Amycretin raises resting heart rate modestly via the incretin class effect (amycretin-specific magnitude unquantified), and its GI side effects can cause vomiting/diarrhea with fluid and electrolyte loss. Clenbuterol is a beta-2 agonist that raises heart rate and drives hypokalemia (intracellular potassium shift). The tachycardia stacks, and amycretin-driven GI fluid/electrolyte loss can worsen clenbuterol's hypokalemia, a combined cardiac-strain and arrhythmia-risk concern. Watch: Watch for palpitations, resting tachycardia, tremor and muscle cramps; monitor heart rate, blood pressure and serum potassium/electrolytes (especially during GI illness or dehydration).
  • Amycretin × Insulin Established
    Amycretin's GLP-1R arm lowers glucose (glucose-dependent, so low intrinsic hypoglycemia risk alone) and its amylin/calcitonin arm suppresses prandial glucagon, which can blunt counter-regulation. Added to exogenous insulin, which lowers glucose independent of glucose level, the glucose-lowering effects stack and hypoglycemia risk rises. Amycretin also slows gastric emptying, delaying meal-carbohydrate absorption so insulin action can mismatch food timing, further raising hypo risk. Watch: Watch for hypoglycemia symptoms (shakiness, sweating, confusion); monitor blood glucose closely, especially around meals given delayed gastric emptying. Harm-reduction only.
  • Amycretin × Retatrutide Evidence-human
    Retatrutide is a GLP-1R+GIPR+GCGR triple agonist and amycretin is a GLP-1R+amylin agonist, so they share the GLP-1R arm (redundant/additive appetite suppression and GI effects). Both also raise resting heart rate via the incretin class effect (retatrutide's HR rise is human-observed; amycretin's own magnitude is not separately quantified), so the tachycardia and GI-driven dehydration risk compound. Watch: Watch for additive resting heart-rate rise/palpitations, severe combined GI symptoms and dehydration, and excessive/too-fast weight loss; monitor heart rate, blood pressure and hydration.
  • Amycretin × Tirzepatide Established
    Both are unimolecular incretin co-agonists that share the GLP-1 receptor arm (tirzepatide adds GIP, amycretin adds amylin). Overlapping GLP-1R agonism plus each drug's second appetite axis produces strongly additive hindbrain satiety and markedly slowed gastric emptying, compounding on-target GI adverse events and, because both delay emptying, potentially reducing or making erratic the absorption of co-ingested ORAL drugs. Tirzepatide's gastric-emptying delay is documented to alter oral-drug (e.g. contraceptive) absorption. Watch: Watch for severe additive nausea/vomiting, dehydration, and unreliable absorption of any oral medications taken alongside; monitor heart rate and hydration/electrolytes during GI illness.
  • Anadrol × Anastrozole Contested
    A key teaching contrast. An aromatase inhibitor lowers estradiol by blocking testosterone->estrogen conversion, which counteracts the gyno/bloat of AROMATIZING androgens. But Anadrol does NOT aromatize (saturated A-ring, 17-alpha-methyl DHT derivative), so its estrogen-like gyno and water retention are NOT estradiol-driven - they are attributed to intrinsic ER/progestogenic-like activity plus mineralocorticoid sodium retention. An AI therefore has no estradiol substrate to act on for Anadrol's bloat and will not fix it. Worse, 17-alpha-methyl-DHT analogs can themselves act as weak competitive aromatase inhibitors in vitro, so ADDING an AI on top can compound estradiol suppression - over-crashing whatever estradiol comes from any co-run aromatizing compounds, causing joint/lipid/mood problems from low E2. Watch: Estradiol (sensitive assay) - watch for symptoms of crashed E2 (joint pain/dryness, low libido, low mood, worse lipids) rather than expecting the AI to fix Anadrol's water/gyno. Lipid panel, since low E2 further worsens HDL.
  • Anadrol × Andarine Evidence-human
    Anadrol is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral anabolic steroid that is directly hepatotoxic (cholestatic/hepatocellular injury), and andarine carries a SARM-class drug-induced-liver-injury signal (its hepatic phase I/II metabolism, including nitroreduction, can generate reactive intermediates). Running two orally-processed, liver-stressing AR agonists together stacks injury risk on the same organ. Watch: Baseline and follow-up ALT, AST, GGT, alkaline phosphatase and total/direct bilirubin; watch for jaundice, dark urine, pale stool, right-upper-quadrant pain or unexplained fatigue and stop if they appear.
  • Anadrol × Boldenone undecylenate Established
    Both are AR agonists that strongly stimulate red-cell production; the curated erythropoiesis MoA card names boldenone AND oxymetholone specifically among the most potent hematocrit-raising androgens. Combining two of the strongest erythropoietic AAS compounds their effects on red-cell mass add, pushing hematocrit and blood viscosity higher. They differ in that Anadrol is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral carrying a hepatotoxicity route that non-alkylated injectable boldenone lacks. Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin (this pairing is among the most likely to trigger erythrocytosis); blood pressure; signs of hyperviscosity (headache, flushing, visual change). Because Anadrol adds 17-alpha-alkyl hepatotoxicity that boldenone does not carry, also track ALT/AST/GGT/bilirubin.
  • Anadrol × DHB Established
    Two hazards stack. (1) Erythrocytosis: oxymetholone is one of the most potent hematocrit-raising androgens (its FDA anemia use), boosting EPO and suppressing hepcidin, and DHB independently drives the same axis, so combined red-cell mass rises faster than either alone. (2) Hepatic burden: Anadrol is 17-alpha-alkylated and overtly hepatotoxic (cholestasis, enzyme rise, peliosis, tumors), and DHB's ORAL form (M1T) is also 17-alpha-methylated with strong hepatic activity; injectable non-methylated DHB raised liver WEIGHT in rats (an organ-mass signal, not proven cholestatic injury). If DHB is taken as oral M1T alongside Anadrol, the two 17aa orals compound cholestatic liver-injury risk (impaired BSEP/ABCB11 bile transport); injectable DHB carries mainly the hematocrit half of this interaction. Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin; ALT/AST/GGT/ALP and bilirubin; jaundice, dark urine, right-upper-quadrant pain. Injectable DHB carries mainly the hematocrit risk; the additive hepatotoxicity applies specifically to the oral M1T form.
  • Anadrol × Dianabol Established
    Both are oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS. The 17a-alkyl 'armor' that lets each survive first-pass metabolism is the same feature that makes them hepatotoxic (impaired canalicular bile transport via BSEP/ABCB11 and MRP2 -> cholestasis; at the severe end peliosis/tumors) and that shifts hepatic lipid handling to crash HDL and raise LDL. Running two 17aa orals stacks the identical liver-load and lipid-toxicity mechanism, so cholestatic and atherogenic injury is additive, not merely coexisting. Anadrol additionally drives red-cell mass hard (raises EPO, suppresses hepcidin to free iron), adding an erythrocytosis/viscosity burden Dbol contributes to less. Watch: ALT/AST/GGT and bilirubin (jaundice, dark urine, RUQ pain are stop signals); full lipid panel expecting a deep HDL trough; hematocrit/hemoglobin for viscosity/thrombosis risk; blood pressure.
  • Anadrol × Ephedrine Evidence-human
    Anadrol strongly raises red blood cell mass and hematocrit (boosting EPO and suppressing hepcidin), which increases blood viscosity, blood pressure, and clot/stroke/MI risk. Ephedrine layers on alpha-1 vasoconstriction and a pressor/tachycardic drive from beta-1 stimulation. Driving high-viscosity, erythrocytotic blood at ephedrine-elevated pressures and heart rate compounds the thrombotic and ischemic hazard beyond either agent alone; this is the same androgen-plus-sympathomimetic cardiovascular stacking seen in the ephedra + anabolic-steroid cardiomyopathy literature. Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin (viscosity), blood pressure, and heart rate; red-flag symptoms of clot or ischemia (sudden weakness/numbness, speech difficulty, chest pain, calf swelling) warrant urgent care.
  • Anadrol × Halotestin Established
    Both are oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS whose alkyl group resists first-pass hepatic clearance and concentrates the drug in hepatocytes, where it drives cholestasis (impaired canalicular bile-acid transport), transaminitis, and at the severe end peliosis hepatis and hepatic adenoma/carcinoma. Running two 17aa orals together loads the same hepatocellular/cholestatic insult onto one liver rather than spreading it across non-alkylated injectables, so the hepatotoxic burden is additive. Fluoxymesterone is regarded as one of the more hepatotoxic orals; oxymetholone is dose-dependently hepatotoxic (25-43% of subjects >5x baseline enzymes in RCTs). Watch: Baseline and periodic ALT/AST, GGT, ALP, total/direct bilirubin; watch for jaundice, dark urine, right-upper-quadrant pain, pruritus. Rising transaminases or bilirubin is a signal to stop and seek medical evaluation.
  • Anadrol × Masteron Evidence-human
    Both are AR agonists that stimulate erythropoiesis and raise red-cell mass. Anadrol is notorious for strongly raising hematocrit (it is FDA-approved for anemia, boosting EPO and suppressing hepcidin); Masteron raises red-cell mass as a class androgen effect. Run together the erythrocytosis is additive, pushing hematocrit/hemoglobin higher and increasing blood viscosity and clot/stroke risk. Both also suppress HDL, so the atherogenic lipid hit compounds as well. (Distinct hepatic routes: Anadrol is 17a-alkylated and markedly hepatotoxic; Masteron is injectable and much less so, so hepatotoxicity does not simply add.) Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin and full blood count (polycythemia raises thrombotic risk); blood pressure; HDL/LDL lipid panel for the combined dyslipidemia; liver enzymes (mainly for Anadrol's 17a-alkyl hepatotoxicity).
  • Anadrol × Methyltestosterone Established
    Two mechanisms compound. (1) Both are 17aa oral AAS, so hepatotoxicity (cholestasis, peliosis) is additive on the liver. (2) Both are AR agonists that stimulate erythropoiesis; Anadrol is a notably strong hematocrit-raiser, so co-use pushes red-cell mass and blood viscosity higher than methyltestosterone alone, compounding thrombosis risk. Watch: Hemoglobin and hematocrit for erythrocytosis (viscosity/clot risk); full liver panel (ALT/AST/GGT/ALP/bilirubin) and jaundice signs. Watch blood pressure and headaches.
  • Anadrol × Nandrolone Established
    Two overlapping harms compound here. First erythrocytosis: both AR agonists drive red-cell mass (EPO up, hepcidin down), and oxymetholone is one of the strongest hematocrit-raising steroids — it is FDA-approved for anemia — so the combination sharply compounds polycythemia (viscosity/thrombosis risk). Second, they DIVERGE on hepatotoxicity: oxymetholone is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral that stresses the liver (cholestasis, enzyme spikes) and crashes HDL, whereas nandrolone is NOT 17aa; so on liver/lipids Anadrol dominates and nandrolone adds little there, but the shared erythrocytosis is genuinely additive. Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin (compounded polycythemia); on the oral, liver enzymes/bilirubin (ALT/AST/GGT — watch for jaundice, dark urine, RUQ pain) and a full lipid panel (expect steep HDL fall); blood pressure.
  • Anadrol × Oxandrolone Established
    Both are oral 17-alpha-alkylated DHT-derived AAS that lower HDL and both carry hepatotoxic potential (oxandrolone comparatively mild). Combined, they add first-pass hepatic burden and compound the HDL-crash/atherogenic lipid shift - the same class mechanism as with harsher orals, just with a milder partner. The additive lipid and liver signal still applies. Watch: Full lipid panel (HDL, LDL/ApoB) for the additive crash; ALT/AST/GGT and bilirubin for liver strain. Blood pressure as a CV-risk driver.
  • Anadrol × Stanozolol Established
    Stanozolol is another oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS and has caused fatal sub-massive hepatic necrosis/liver failure on its own. Paired with Anadrol, two 17aa orals compound cholestatic and oxidative liver injury through the same shared mechanism (impaired bile transport via BSEP/MRP2 + ROS). Both also suppress HDL, so the atherogenic lipid crash is additive. Watch: Liver panel (ALT/AST/GGT, bilirubin, ALP) with special vigilance for jaundice and RUQ pain; full lipid panel (expect very low HDL). Blood pressure as a downstream CV-risk driver.
  • Anadrol × Superdrol Established
    Both are oral 17-alpha-alkylated AAS. The 17aa 'armor' that makes them orally active is exactly what makes them hepatotoxic - they resist first-pass clearance, linger in hepatocytes, impair canalicular bile transporters (BSEP/ABCB11, MRP2) and drive oxidative stress. Taking two 17aa orals together stacks first-pass hepatic load, and reviews note that using multiple orals simultaneously increases the frequency and severity of liver injury (raised ALT/AST/GGT, cholestasis, and at the severe end peliosis/tumors). Both also crash HDL, so atherogenic lipid toxicity compounds too. Watch: ALT, AST, GGT, bilirubin and ALP; watch for jaundice, dark urine, right-upper-quadrant pain (cholestasis/peliosis signals). Full lipid panel for the additive HDL crash.
  • Anadrol × Testosterone Established
    Both are androgen-receptor agonists that drive erythropoiesis by the same pathway - raising EPO and suppressing hepcidin to free iron for the marrow. Anadrol is one of the strongest hematocrit-raisers (its FDA anemia indication) and testosterone raises hematocrit too, so together their erythropoietic effects ADD, pushing hematocrit/hemoglobin higher than either alone. High hematocrit thickens blood (viscosity), and secondary polycythemia on testosterone is tied to more major adverse cardiac events and venous clots. Watch: Hematocrit and hemoglobin (watch for climbing values, the classic >52% concern for viscosity/clot risk); blood pressure; and warning signs of thrombosis - headaches, visual changes, chest pain, one-sided weakness, calf swelling.
  • Anastrozole × Masteron Established
    Anastrozole inhibits aromatase (CYP19A1) to lower estradiol. Masteron contributes no aromatizable substrate at all, and its AR-vs-ER crosstalk pushes estrogen SIGNALING in the same (downward) direction as the AI - so the two do NOT oppose each other; instead their estrogen-lowering effects compound. On a Masteron-dominant regimen there is little drug-derived estrogen for an AI to remove, so adding/over-titrating an AI risks driving the small remaining (mostly testosterone-derived or baseline) estradiol below the healthy range. Because estradiol has a U-shaped risk curve, a low-E2 state stacks joint pain, worse lipids and bone/mood harm on top of Masteron's own HDL crash. (Masteron's crosstalk is receptor-level, not enzyme inhibition, so it does not add to anastrozole's mechanism directly - but the low-E2 downside does compound.) Watch: Sensitive estradiol to catch over-suppression; HDL/LDL lipid panel (both low E2 and Masteron independently worsen HDL); joint symptoms, libido and mood as low-estrogen red flags.
  • Anastrozole × Proviron Contested
    Proviron is a non-aromatizing DHT-derived androgen that binds SHBG and is popularly regarded as a mild anti-estrogen (though strong anti-estrogen action is community lore, not established). Like Masteron, it provides no aromatase substrate, so anastrozole has nothing to block from it, but their estrogen-lowering tendencies can stack: Proviron's estrogen-lowering feel plus anastrozole's blockade of estrogen synthesis can compound into estradiol over-suppression. The genuine mechanistic edge is additive risk of a crashed-E2 state with no counteracting benefit gained, because Proviron does not raise estrogen in the first place; the additivity is inference, not trial-measured. Watch: Estradiol watched for over-suppression; recognize that pairing a non-aromatizing androgen with a synthesis blocker offers no estrogen-control upside and raises crash risk. Track libido, mood, joint symptoms, HDL, and bone on chronic use.
  • Andarine × Trenbolone Evidence-rodent
    Both are AR agonists that suppress the HPG axis, so combined use deepens LH/FSH and endogenous-testosterone shutdown through the shared central mechanism. The overlap is specifically on AR occupancy and axis suppression; trenbolone's additional progesterone/glucocorticoid-receptor activity and renal strain are its own, not shared. Watch: LH, FSH, total/free testosterone for combined suppression; separately track prolactin and kidney markers for trenbolone's own effects, and HDL for the shared androgenic lipid load.
  • Boldenone undecylenate × DHB Established
    Both are androgen-receptor agonists that drive erythropoiesis (raise EPO, act on EPOR, suppress hepcidin, raise erythroferrone), so red-cell mass and hematocrit climb from two directions at once. This pathway does NOT require aromatization: aromatase-deficient men still show androgen-driven erythrocytosis (PMID 19159688), so DHB's non-aromatizing nature does not spare it. Boldenone is a classically strong hematocrit-raiser, and DHB is its 5-alpha-reduced sibling (dihydroboldenone) sharing the same delta-1 skeleton, so the two additively push polycythemia. Elevated hematocrit raises blood viscosity and clot/stroke/MI risk. Watch: Serial hematocrit/hemoglobin (and ferritin/EPO trend); watch for headaches, hypertension, ruddy complexion, or visual disturbance signaling hyperviscosity.
  • Boldenone undecylenate × Enclomiphene Speculative
    Both can raise estradiol, so together they can compound estrogenic side-effect risk. Enclomiphene raises endogenous testosterone that is partly aromatized (lifting systemic E2) and does NOT block ER in breast/peripheral tissue, so its central ER antagonism won't neutralize peripheral E2. Boldenone can aromatize to estradiol, adding a second E2 source. Caveat: boldenone's aromatization is weak and its monograph notes it often LOWERS measured E2 on a testosterone base via mundane displacement chemistry, so the additive-estrogen effect is inconsistent rather than guaranteed. A separate, more reliable compounding concern is hematocrit — boldenone robustly raises red-cell mass. (Axis tension also applies: boldenone, an AR agonist, suppresses LH/FSH, opposing enclomiphene's central drive.) Watch: Estradiol on a sensitive assay plus breast exam/symptoms (tenderness, swelling) for gynecomastia. Hematocrit/hemoglobin on a full blood count for boldenone-driven erythrocytosis. LH/FSH to see whether the androgen is suppressing the axis enclomiphene is trying to drive.
  • Boldenone undecylenate × Masteron Evidence-human
    Boldenone's most reliable clinical effect is a marked rise in red-cell mass/hematocrit, and Masteron adds its own class-androgen erythropoietic drive. Together they compound erythrocytosis and raise blood viscosity/thrombotic risk. Directional difference on estrogen: boldenone aromatizes (adds E2) while Masteron does not, so Masteron's receptor-level AR-vs-ER crosstalk at most partially offsets, and does NOT eliminate, boldenone's estrogenic potential. Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin and CBC (additive polycythemia); blood pressure; estradiol (boldenone still aromatizes despite Masteron).
  • Boldenone undecylenate × Nandrolone Established
    Both are injectable, esterified, non-17-alpha-alkylated AR agonists with strong erythropoietic and HPG-suppressive effects and a 'lean/slow' profile. Stacked, they compound erythrocytosis (additive EPO/hepcidin effect) and deepen HPG/testosterone suppression (both long esters mean slow recovery). They differ downstream — nandrolone aromatizes weakly to estradiol and is progestogenic (raises prolactin), while boldenone is a poor aromatizer and not progestogenic — so nandrolone adds a prolactin/progestin dimension EQ lacks. Watch: Hematocrit/hemoglobin (additive erythrocytosis); LH/FSH/testosterone (compounded suppression, slow recovery); prolactin and E2 (from the nandrolone side). Erectile/libido issues can arise from the nandrolone prolactin/progestin axis.
  • CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin × Cardarine Evidence-human
    Same collision as with MK-677: CJC-1295/Ipamorelin raise endogenous GH (and IGF-1), and GH excess is shown in human studies to induce insulin resistance — increased FFA/lipid oxidation crowds out glucose disposal. Cardarine's PPARδ-driven glucose→fat fuel switch pushes muscle in the identical direction, so co-use can compound reduced glucose tolerance / higher fasting glucose. The fat-oxidation effects are otherwise aligned (both favor lipid fuel), which is why the shared liability is glycemic, not opposition. Watch: Fasting glucose and insulin, HbA1c; look for insulin-resistance signs. GH peptides can also cause fluid retention and carpal-tunnel-type symptoms — separate from the shared glucose mechanism.
  • CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin × Hexarelin Evidence-human
    This blend contains ipamorelin, which is ALSO a GHS-R1a agonist — so pairing it with hexarelin is redundant on GHS-R1a and adds desensitization risk at that receptor, while the CJC-1295 (GHRH-R) component synergizes with hexarelin for a larger GH pulse (GHRP-plus-GHRH synergy). The compounding concern is amplified GH excess. Note ipamorelin is specifically a 'clean' secretagogue with minimal cortisol/prolactin effect, so the HPA/prolactin load in this stack comes from hexarelin's non-selectivity, not from ipamorelin. Watch: IGF-1 (watch for a waning response suggesting desensitization); GH-excess signs (edema, arthralgia); fasting glucose; and, because of hexarelin's own non-selectivity, prolactin and a morning cortisol.
  • Cagrilintide × Insulin Evidence-human
    Amylin is physiologically co-secreted with insulin and complements it: cagrilintide slows gastric emptying, blunts post-meal glucagon, and reduces food intake, lowering post-prandial glucose. On a fixed exogenous-insulin regimen, this reduced glucose load plus reduced/variable nutrient delivery from delayed emptying compounds hypoglycemia risk and makes meal-time insulin matching harder (a well-documented hazard for amylin analogues plus insulin — pramlintide, the approved first-gen amylin analogue, carries a boxed hypoglycemia warning when combined with insulin). Watch: Monitor blood glucose closely for hypoglycemia (highest risk with insulin or sulfonylureas), especially around meals given variable gastric emptying. Watch for symptomatic lows as weight loss improves insulin sensitivity.
  • Cagrilintide × Retatrutide Speculative
    Retatrutide (incretin/glucagon agonist) and cagrilintide (amylin/CTR agonist) both slow gastric emptying and stimulate the area postrema, so nausea, vomiting and GI dysmotility would stack. Retatrutide's glucagon arm can additionally raise heart rate, an effect not offset by amylin. This is a mechanistic inference; the stack has not been studied clinically. Watch: Watch GI tolerability and dehydration (renal function with vomiting). Monitor resting heart rate and report palpitations/syncope. Report severe persistent abdominal pain (pancreatitis class concern).
  • Cagrilintide × T3 Speculative
    Cagrilintide markedly slows gastric emptying, which can alter the absorption kinetics of narrow-therapeutic-index oral drugs taken concurrently. This absorption-shift effect is documented for oral thyroid hormone (levothyroxine/T4) with GLP-1-class agents that also delay emptying — altered levothyroxine absorption plus weight-loss-driven reductions in thyroid-hormone requirement have precipitated iatrogenic thyrotoxicosis. Oral T3 (liothyronine) is similarly narrow-margin, so its exposure could shift. NOTE: the direct evidence is for T4, not T3, and for GLP-1 agents, not amylin analogues — so applying it to oral T3 + cagrilintide is a double extrapolation (mechanistically plausible via shared emptying-delay, but not directly demonstrated). Watch: Monitor thyroid status (TSH, free T3/T4) and for signs of over-replacement (palpitations, tremor, heat intolerance, atrial fibrillation) when combining. Report new palpitations/arrhythmia.
  • Cagrilintide × Tirzepatide Speculative
    Both strongly delay gastric emptying and engage brainstem nausea circuitry — tirzepatide via GLP-1/GIP receptors, cagrilintide via amylin/CTR receptors in the area postrema. Stacking them would make the emptying delay and nausea/vomiting/constipation additive; marked emptying delay raises risks of retained gastric contents and aspiration under anesthesia. Mechanistically strong but not studied in humans as a stack. Watch: Watch GI tolerability and hydration; with vomiting, check renal function (creatinine/eGFR). Flag the profoundly delayed emptying to any clinician before procedures/anesthesia (aspiration risk). Report severe persistent abdominal pain (pancreatitis is a class concern).
  • Cardarine × Anadrol Evidence-human
    Both carry a hepatotoxicity signal that would summate via different routes. Anadrol is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral androgen and one of the more hepatotoxic AAS: 17aa substitution resists hepatic clearance and prolongs hepatocyte exposure, it impairs the canalicular bile transporters BSEP/MRP2 (cholestasis), and it drives oxidative/reactive-oxygen hepatocyte injury (with peliosis/adenoma on prolonged use) — an ESTABLISHED human hepatotoxin. Cardarine has a documented human hepatocellular-injury signal (marked transaminase rise in the abuse literature), plausibly via PPARδ-driven shifts in hepatic substrate handling plus extensive oxidative hepatic metabolism of a highly lipophilic molecule. Two agents that each stress the hepatocyte compound the overall liver burden. Watch: ALT, AST, GGT, bilirubin at baseline and periodically; jaundice, pruritus, dark urine, RUQ pain warrant stopping and medical review. Anadrol also strongly raises hematocrit — a separate liability to track — but the SHARED mechanistic edge with Cardarine is hepatotoxicity.
  • Cardarine × Ostarine Evidence-human
    This is the single documented real-world Cardarine stack harm. A published case report describes a man who bought Cardarine online and stacked it with the SARM ostarine and developed massive rhabdomyolysis (CK up to ~86,435 U/L) together with severe hepatocellular injury (AST ~2558 U/L); the dominant finding was the muscle breakdown, with liver enzyme rise partly attributable to it and to the co-used SARM. Ostarine (an androgen-receptor modulator) is independently an established cause of (often cholestatic) drug-induced liver injury in recreational users; Cardarine drives aggressive metabolic remodeling of myofibers and has its own human hepatotoxicity/rhabdomyolysis signal. Stacked, the muscle-breakdown and liver insults compound (causation cannot be cleanly split between the two). Watch: Creatine kinase and renal function/urinalysis for myoglobinuria (severe myalgia with cola-colored urine is an emergency), plus ALT, AST, bilirubin (jaundice, dark urine, RUQ pain). Stop and seek care on any of these.
  • Clenbuterol × DNP Established
    Two thermogenic agents acting through different mechanisms that converge on heat production and cardiac demand. DNP uncouples mitochondrial oxidative phosphorylation, dissipating the proton gradient as heat and forcing a compensatory rise in metabolic rate, oxygen demand and heart rate; clenbuterol independently raises resting energy expenditure (~+21%) and thermogenesis via beta-2/cAMP while directly stimulating the heart and lowering potassium. Combined, the extra heat load (DNP) plus adrenergic tachycardia and hypokalemia (clenbuterol) compound the risk of dangerous hyperthermia and tachyarrhythmia, with clenbuterol's long half-life meaning the adrenergic contribution cannot be quickly withdrawn if DNP-driven hyperthermia develops. Watch: Core temperature and any sign of hyperthermia (profuse sweating, flushing, agitation) — this is the priority with DNP; plus heart rate, blood pressure, ECG, and serum potassium. Rising temperature or tachycardia warrants stopping and urgent medical review, not 'waiting it out' given clenbuterol's long persistence.
  • Clenbuterol × Ephedrine Established
    Both are sympathomimetics that converge on the same adrenergic/cAMP output. Clenbuterol is a long-acting direct beta-2 agonist; ephedrine is a mixed-acting agent that releases endogenous noradrenaline AND weakly agonizes alpha-1/beta-1/beta-2. Stacked, they additively drive heart rate, contractility, blood pressure and tremor, and both push potassium into cells (beta-2/cAMP -> Na/K-ATPase), so their hypokalemia stacks on top of their tachycardia. Clenbuterol's ~35 h half-life means its adrenergic load persists for many hours, so adding ephedrine layers a second stimulant surge onto an already tonically stimulated adrenergic system. This is a real-world pairing (bodybuilders run 'ECA' during clen off-weeks), which is exactly why the additive cardiac/electrolyte load matters. Watch: Resting heart rate and blood pressure (watch for sustained tachycardia and widened pulse pressure), palpitations, tremor, anxiety and insomnia; ECG for arrhythmia/SVT if symptomatic; serum potassium and magnesium. Anyone with hypertension, arrhythmia or prior cardiac disease is at substantially higher combined risk.
  • Clenbuterol × Halotestin Contested
    Two independent routes to low potassium and cardiovascular strain converge. Clenbuterol is a beta-2 agonist whose cAMP/PKA activation of Na+/K+-ATPase drives potassium into cells (hypokalemia) and causes tachycardia, widened pulse pressure and cardiac stress. Fluoxymesterone inhibits 11-beta-HSD2, letting cortisol illicitly activate the mineralocorticoid receptor ('apparent mineralocorticoid excess'), promoting renal sodium/water retention, potassium wasting, and a pressor effect. Stacked, the hypokalemia and blood-pressure/cardiac burdens add up, raising arrhythmia risk. Note the clenbuterol hypokalemia arm is well established in humans, but the Halotestin 11b-HSD2/mineralocorticoid arm is shown only in vitro (human enzyme) and inferred in-body — so the convergence is real but its clinical magnitude from the Halo side is unproven. Watch: Serum potassium and sodium, blood pressure, resting heart rate; watch for palpitations, cramps, or ECG (ST/T-wave, QT) changes. Low potassium plus tachycardia is an arrhythmia risk warranting medical attention.
  • Clenbuterol × T3 Established
    T3 acts through cardiac TRalpha to raise heart rate, contractility and cardiac output (SERCA up, phospholamban down) and up-regulates cardiac beta-adrenergic receptors, sensitizing the heart to catecholamine/beta-2 drive. Clenbuterol supplies exactly that beta-adrenergic drive. So T3 both directly stimulates the heart AND primes it to respond more strongly to clenbuterol, producing additive tachycardia and arrhythmia risk. Thyrotoxic states also cause an intracellular potassium shift, which compounds clenbuterol's beta-2-mediated hypokalemia. Both also raise metabolic rate/thermogenesis, adding heat and cardiac demand. Watch: Resting and exertional heart rate, blood pressure, palpitations; ECG for atrial fibrillation/SVT and ST-T changes; serum potassium (additive hypokalemia risk) and, if clinically indicated, thyroid function. Watch for signs of thyrotoxicosis layered on adrenergic overdrive (tremor, heat intolerance, weakness).
  • Clenbuterol × Testosterone Established
    Shared cardiovascular strain plus a documented contamination hazard. On the mechanism side, testosterone raises hematocrit and, at supraphysiologic exposure, contributes to cardiac hypertrophy, adding to clenbuterol's beta-adrenergic cardiac load (raised heart rate, arrhythmia risk, and — on chronic misuse — LV hypertrophy).   • Testosterone × Andarine Established
    Both act on the same androgen receptor (AR / NR3C4), binding the same ligand pocket, so they co-occupy and compete for one receptor rather than working through separate pathways. Andarine is a non-steroidal AR ligand (full agonist in muscle/bone, weak partial agonist in prostate) and is NOT a substrate for 5-alpha-reductase or aromatase, whereas testosterone is a full agonist that is additionally amplified to DHT and aromatized to estradiol. Because they engage AR with different intrinsic efficacy and recruit different cofactors, the combined effect is not simply additive; a lower-efficacy SARM occupying AR could theoretically dampen part of testosterone's fuller androgenic signal in high-androgen tissue while adding its own anabolic drive in muscle (mechanistic inference, not proven in humans).   • Testosterone × Dutasteride Established
    5α-reductase (type 1+2) converts testosterone into DHT in skin, scalp, prostate and genital tissue; dutasteride blocks ~90-95% of that conversion. On testosterone it therefore removes the DHT arm specifically — reducing DHT-driven scalp miniaturization, prostate growth and sebum — while leaving testosterone's own androgen-receptor signaling intact. Critically this does NOT blunt the anabolic (muscle/strength) effect of testosterone, which acts on the AR directly rather than via DHT (Bhasin's graded-testosterone-plus-dutasteride RCT showed identical fat-free-mass and strength gains). Serum testosterone rises modestly (backed-up substrate, stays in-range) and some of that backed-up testosterone can be shunted toward aromatization to estradiol, though a large estradiol rise was not a headline trial finding. Watch: PSA (dutasteride roughly halves it — a measured value must be interpreted as ~double, or a real cancer can be masked); scalp/prostate DHT-dependent effects; estradiol and breast tissue given the possible extra shunt toward aromatization; do not expect changes to muscle gains.
  • Testosterone × Finasteride Established
    Direct enzyme-substrate relationship at the same node. Testosterone is the substrate that 5α-reductase type 2 converts into DHT; finasteride forms a near-irreversible dead-end complex with that enzyme, so exogenous testosterone can no longer be efficiently converted to DHT in scalp, prostate and genital skin. Serum/scalp DHT falls ~65-70% while the unconverted testosterone drifts slightly higher, and because more T is available to aromatase, estradiol (and estrone) nudge up. Finasteride is thus a mechanistic counterweight sitting one step downstream of testosterone on the same pathway: it blunts DHT-driven hair loss and prostate growth from TRT but does NOT touch testosterone's own androgen-receptor effects (muscle anabolism, hematocrit). Watch: Distinguish DHT-mediated signs (scalp/prostate) from systemic androgen effects finasteride does NOT block (hematocrit, libido from T itself). Watch estradiol and breast tissue, since the small E2 rise can add to testosterone's own aromatization. On finasteride, know that PSA is roughly halved and must be interpreted accordingly for prostate-cancer screening.

Their off-target ACTH/cortisol/prolactin elevations and appetite effects compound on the same mechanism, and hexarelin is a notably strong HPA/sleep-architecture disruptor. Watch: IGF-1 (shared ceiling/desensitization); morning cortisol/ACTH and prolactin for compounded off-target stimulation; sleep quality and appetite/weight.
  • Human chorionic gonadotropin × Clomiphene citrate Established
    Both aim to raise testosterone and preserve testicular function/fertility, but at OPPOSITE ends of the HPG axis. Clomiphene is a SERM that blocks estrogen negative feedback at the hypothalamus, raising the body's own LH and FSH — it therefore REQUIRES an intact, responsive pituitary and testis. hCG works downstream, mimicking LH directly at the Leydig-cell LHCGR, so it works even when the pituitary is suppressed or absent (e.g., on TRT). The overlapping endpoint (drive Leydig cells, raise T, keep testes active) makes them partly redundant; stacking both drives Leydig cells from two directions (endogenous LH plus an LH-mimic), compounding testosterone and, via aromatization, estradiol — to which clomiphene's own mixed-estrogenic isomer adds. Watch: Testosterone (additive over-shoot), estradiol (both can raise it — gynecomastia/mood/fluid), LH/FSH (to gauge whether central stimulation is even working), and hematocrit.
  • Human chorionic gonadotropin × Enclomiphene Evidence-human
    Both aim to raise endogenous/testicular testosterone, but they enter the LH→Leydig pathway at DIFFERENT points via non-overlapping molecular targets — so they are largely redundant rather than sharing a target. Enclomiphene works at the TOP of the axis (blocks hypothalamic estrogen receptors → more endogenous LH/FSH → drives the testis physiologically). hCG BYPASSES the brain, acting directly as an LH-receptor (LHCGR) agonist on Leydig cells. They converge on the same downstream effector (Leydig-cell / intratesticular testosterone) by different routes — partly redundant, partly complementary. Because hCG raises intratesticular T and E2 and, through feedback, suppresses the pituitary's own LH output, it can blunt or work against enclomiphene's central 'restart' logic if the goal is axis self-sufficiency. Watch: Total/free testosterone and estradiol (both can rise, E2 especially with hCG-driven aromatization). Track LH/FSH — hCG feedback suppresses endogenous LH, so a FALLING LH/FSH signals the pituitary drive enclomiphene is meant to build is being overridden. Hematocrit/hemoglobin on a full blood count; semen analysis and testicular exam if fertility is the goal.
  • Ligandrol × Testosterone Established
    Testosterone and Ligandrol act through the same androgen receptor, so adding Ligandrol on top of testosterone is largely redundant AR agonism with additive HPTA suppression. The key mechanistic contrast for teaching: testosterone is aromatized to estradiol and 5-alpha-reduced to DHT, giving it estrogenic (gyno, water) and DHT-driven (prostate, scalp) arms; Ligandrol is NON-aromatizable and not a 5AR substrate, so it adds AR drive WITHOUT adding estrogen or DHT. Net effect of the pair is more AR signalling and deeper gonadotropin/testosterone suppression, but the estrogen/DHT side-effect profile is driven entirely by the testosterone component. Watch: LH/FSH and total/free testosterone (deeper axis suppression); estradiol and hematocrit (driven by the testosterone, not the LGD); lipids/HDL; liver panel (bilirubin) for the LGD-side DILI signal.
  • MK-677 × CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin Established
    MK-677 is an orally-active non-peptide agonist of the SAME receptor Ipamorelin hits, GHS-R1a (the ghrelin receptor), signaling through the same Gq/PLC/PKC/Ca2+ cascade. Adding it to the CJC/Ipamorelin stack is largely redundant on the ghrelin-receptor arm rather than additive on a new pathway, and because MK-677 gives a long, sustained (once-daily oral) IGF-1 elevation, combining it compounds the GH-class liabilities (greater cumulative IGF-1, more fluid retention, a bigger hit to insulin sensitivity) with no new mechanism recruited. Watch: IGF-1 (cumulative over-exposure above age-adjusted range), fasting glucose/HbA1c (MK-677 sustained exposure is notably insulin-desensitizing), and signs of edema/carpal-tunnel from additive GH excess.
  • MK-677 × GHRP-2 Established (identical GHS-R1a target); MK-677 glucose effect Evidence-human
    MK-677 is an orally-active nonpeptide full agonist at the SAME receptor GHRP-2 uses (GHS-R1a). Because MK-677 gives sustained 24-h receptor occupancy while GHRP-2 gives sharp pulses, adding pulsatile GHRP-2 on top of continuous MK-677 offers little extra GH (the receptor is already driven) but compounds the shared downsides: additive appetite/food intake and, critically, additive GH-induced insulin resistance and rising fasting glucose, which MK-677 alone reliably causes in humans. Watch: Fasting glucose and HbA1c (compounded GH-driven insulin resistance is the key hazard); appetite/weight; IGF-1 to gauge whether the added peptide is doing anything beyond MK-677 alone.
  • MK-677 × GHRP-6 Established
    MK-677 is a non-peptide GHS-R1a full agonist acting on the exact same ghrelin receptor as GHRP-6. Running both does not add a new pathway — they co-occupy one receptor, and continuous GHS-R1a occupancy drives beta-arrestin desensitization/internalization, so the combined GH response is blunted rather than doubled. Their GH-axis, appetite, water-retention and insulin-resistance liabilities also stack on the same mechanism. Watch: IGF-1 (combined ceiling and desensitization), fasting glucose/insulin/HbA1c (both raise insulin resistance via GH), appetite/weight; note MK-677's long half-life sustains exposure and favors desensitization.
  • MK-677 × Hexarelin Established
    Both are full agonists at the very same receptor, GHS-R1a (the ghrelin receptor), driving the same pituitary somatotroph pathway to raise GH/IGF-1 — so stacking them is largely redundant rather than additive on the GH-releasing target. Chronic co-agonism of one receptor also raises the risk of GHS-R1a desensitization, so the GH output per dose can wane, and both share a GH-driven insulin-resistance liability. (Unlike hexarelin, MK-677 is comparatively GH-selective and does not meaningfully raise prolactin, so the cortisol/prolactin concern here is hexarelin's, not a compounding of the two.) Watch: IGF-1 as the integrated GH readout (watch for a plateau or fall despite continued use, suggesting desensitization); fasting glucose/HbA1c since both can worsen insulin sensitivity; appetite/weight and fluid retention (MK-677 is notable for both).
  • MOTS-c × Metformin Evidence-human
    Both converge on the same energy-sensing node: MOTS-c disrupts the folate/de-novo-purine (AICAR/ZMP) cycle so the AICAR intermediate builds up and tips on AMPK, and metformin also activates AMPK (via a rising AMP/ATP ratio from complex-I inhibition, LKB1-dependent). Their mechanisms of action are described in the literature as 'largely overlapping' (insulin sensitization, enhanced glucose utilization, suppression of mitochondrial respiration, folate-AICAR-AMPK convergence). Because they both funnel into the AMPK/glucose-disposal axis, running them together is largely redundant on that axis while still able to additively lower glucose. (Note: whether metformin actually RAISES circulating MOTS-c is unresolved - the one clinical paper concluded the metformin-MOTS-c relationship needs further study and that muscle production may limit metformin's effect on circulating levels.) Watch: Fasting glucose, HbA1c and hypoglycemia-awareness (shakiness, sweating, confusion), since the glucose-lowering effect can stack; a metabolic panel to gauge whether MOTS-c adds anything beyond metformin's AMPK activation.
  • MOTS-c × SLU-PP-332 Evidence-rodent
    Both are exercise-mimetics whose benefits funnel through the PGC-1alpha/oxidative-metabolism axis. MOTS-c acts via AMPK -> PGC-1alpha to raise fatty-acid oxidation, mitochondrial efficiency and endurance; SLU-PP-332 (ERRalpha/beta/gamma agonist) increases oxidative muscle fibers, fatty-acid oxidation and energy expenditure, and the ERR receptors are the canonical transcriptional partners of PGC-1alpha. Stacking them is largely redundant on the same oxidative-metabolism transcriptional program. Watch: Given overlapping oxidative/energy-expenditure drive, watch resting heart rate and exercise tolerance; SLU-PP-332 has a rodent cardiac signal at high exposure (separate concern). Effect overlap means limited independent benefit.
  • Masteron × Dutasteride Established
    Dutasteride inhibits both 5-alpha-reductase type 1 and 2 for near-total DHT suppression, acting on the testosterone-to-DHT conversion step. Masteron is a pre-formed 5-alpha-reduced androgen, so dutasteride cannot reduce Masteron or blunt its direct androgenic effects on scalp, prostate and skin. The compounds do not meaningfully interact via Masteron's own action; dutasteride only affects DHT arising from any co-run aromatizable androgen. This corrects the common belief that a 5-ARI makes Masteron 'hair-safe.' Watch: Hair/scalp and prostate/PSA (no benefit expected against Masteron's direct androgenic drive); do NOT rely on a 5-ARI to make Masteron 'hair-safe.'
  • Masteron × Finasteride Established
    Finasteride blocks 5-alpha-reductase type 2 to stop testosterone becoming DHT, reducing DHT-driven hair loss and prostate stimulation. Masteron IS ALREADY a 5-alpha-reduced steroid (2a-methyl-DHT), so it is not a substrate for the enzyme; its androgenic scalp/prostate/skin action is delivered directly to the androgen receptor. Finasteride therefore does NOT blunt Masteron's hair loss, acne or prostate effects - a widely misunderstood point and a key harm-reduction correction. Finasteride still lowers DHT made from any co-administered testosterone, but it offers no protection against Masteron itself. Watch: Scalp recession and acne (expect NO protection from a 5-ARI against Masteron's own androgenic effects); PSA/prostate symptoms; recognise finasteride only helps with testosterone-derived DHT, not Masteron.
  • Masteron × Proviron Contested
    Proviron (mesterolone, 1a-methyl-DHT) and Masteron (2a-methyl-DHT) are both DHT-derived, non-aromatizing, already-5-alpha-reduced androgens marketed as mild 'anti-estrogens' that bind SHBG. Their mechanisms overlap heavily: neither aromatizes, both are 5-alpha-reduced (so finasteride does not blunt them), both are claimed to displace testosterone from SHBG to raise free-T, and both mildly oppose estrogen via the androgen receptor rather than by blocking aromatase. Stacking them is largely redundant on the estrogen/SHBG axis while adding androgenic side-effect load. Note the SHBG free-T claim itself is Contested - a measured drostanolone-SHBG affinity was never established. Watch: SHBG and free/total testosterone (overlapping, hard-to-titrate effect of uncertain magnitude); androgenic side effects (hair, acne, prostate) which add rather than cancel.
  • Metformin × Cardarine Evidence-rodent
    Both are 'exercise-mimetic'-type metabolic agents that promote fat oxidation, but through DIFFERENT nodes rather than a shared molecular target, so their fuel-partitioning effects overlap. Metformin activates AMPK (via Complex I / rising AMP), shifting cells toward burning fat and away from lipogenesis. Cardarine activates the nuclear receptor PPARdelta, which transcriptionally upregulates fatty-acid-oxidation genes (e.g. CPT1). AMPK and PPARdelta are distinct switches (Cardarine's monograph notes they act at different nodes and cooperate rather than sharing a target), but they converge on the same catabolic glucose-to-fat fuel-switch program, making the metabolic actions partly redundant. Caveat for direction: metformin may blunt some aerobic training adaptations while Cardarine is framed as an endurance/exercise mimetic, and Cardarine carries rodent carcinogenicity concerns. Watch: This is a mechanistic overlap rather than an acute toxicity; general metabolic monitoring (fasting glucose/insulin, lipid panel). No dosing guidance.
  • SLU-PP-332 × Cardarine Evidence-rodent
    SLU-PP-332 is an ERRα/β/γ agonist explicitly characterized as an exercise mimetic that 'increases oxidative muscle fibers, fatty acid oxidation, and enhances exercise endurance' and raises energy expenditure — the same phenotype Cardarine produces via PPARδ. PPARδ and ERR/PGC-1α biology are tightly interlinked upstream of mitochondrial fat-oxidation genes, so the two agents largely duplicate one another's endpoint at different receptor entry points (note SLU-PP-332 also reportedly improves glucose oxidation, whereas Cardarine shifts muscle away from glucose — the overlap is the oxidative-fiber/FAO program, not every fuel-handling detail). Both are WADA-prohibited exercise mimetics; SLU-PP-332 efficacy is mouse-only and it showed cardiac effects at high exposure, so combining two unvalidated mimetics compounds unknown risk without adding a distinct mechanism. Watch: Resting heart rate and any exertional cardiac symptoms (SLU-PP-332 carries a high-exposure cardiac signal), fasting lipids and glucose; treat the combination as two unproven investigational agents, not a validated stack.
  • Testosterone × GHK-Cu Evidence-human
    Testosterone (and the estradiol it aromatizes to) independently increases dermal collagen, fibroblast proliferation, angiogenesis and wound-healing capacity - sex steroids are established regulators of neocollagenesis and skin thickness. This overlaps the same skin-collagen/repair endpoint GHK-Cu targets locally, so on that endpoint the two are partly redundant/additive rather than mechanistically distinct. They reach the endpoint by different routes (androgen/estrogen receptor signaling vs copper-dependent fibroblast activation). Watch: Testosterone's own risks dominate - hematocrit (erythrocytosis), estradiol, lipids, PSA. GHK-Cu adds no specific lab here beyond copper caution for systemic use; the interaction is a shared skin-collagen effect, not a new toxicity.
