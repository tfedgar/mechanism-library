The 19-nortestosterone steroids, compared

Why nandrolone, trenbolone and trestolone share a "progesterone problem" — and why tren is the harshest of the family

adversarially verified · revised63 citations

The one-carbon difference that starts the whole story

A "19-nor" steroid is testosterone with a single carbon removed — the methyl group at position 19. That's it. That tiny edit changes three things at once: how the molecule behaves at the androgen receptor (AR), whether enzymes can amplify or de-fang it in specific tissues, and whether it picks up progesterone-receptor (PR) activity that testosterone doesn't have. Those three levers explain almost every practical difference between these compounds.

Before the table, one honesty flag the gym world usually skips. Chemically, only nandrolone, trenbolone, and trestolone (MENT) are true 19-nors (the estrane skeleton, C19 removed). Boldenone (EQ) and DHB (1-testosterone) are Δ1 (1-ene) androstanes — they still have the C19 methyl. Forum culture groups all five together because they feel related (dry-ish gains, "not just test"), but boldenone/DHB are structurally in a different family. I've included them because the comparison the audience actually wants is "these five injectables," but I'll keep the chemistry straight throughout. Established (PMID 11252818; PMID 16621347; boldenone A-ring PMID 18555683)

Reconciling the differences: three levers

Lever 1 — the 5α-reductase switch runs three different ways. With testosterone, 5α-reductase makes things worse in skin, scalp and prostate by converting it to the stronger DHT. The 19-nors escape this, but each by a different trick. Nandrolone is reduced — but to dihydronandrolone, a weaker androgen, so it loses potency exactly in the tissues you don't want hit; that's the real basis of its "muscle-favoring" reputation. Established (PMID 4021486) Trenbolone's locked 4,9,11-triene means it is not a substrate at all — no dial-up, no dial-down, full potency everywhere, which is part of why it feels so much stronger. Established (PMID 21266670) Trestolone's 7α-methyl group physically blocks the enzyme; in human implant trials PSA actually fell. [Evidence-human] (PMID 17460095) And DHB is already 5α-reduced, so a 5α-reductase inhibitor like finasteride does nothing to its scalp/skin effects. [Evidence-rodent] (PMID 16621347) Same "prostate-milder-than-test" headline, four different mechanisms.

Lever 2 — the aromatase story is genuinely mixed, and two popular claims are wrong. People assume "19-nor = no estrogen." Not quite. Trenbolone and DHB truly cannot aromatize (triene lock; 5α-reduced A-ring). Established (PMID 21266670; 16621347) Nandrolone and boldenone aromatize weakly — less than test, not zero. Evidence (PMID 3865479; 17656084) But trestolone (MENT) aromatizes into 7α-methyl-estradiol, which is a full, potent estrogen — roughly as strong as your own estradiol at the receptor. So "MENT is estrogen-free" is a straight myth. [Evidence-in vitro] (PMID 8142308; 22065861) Two more myths to bury: the "EQ is ~50% as estrogenic as test" number has no peer-reviewed source Contested (PMID 17656084), and the viral "EQ turns into horse estrogen that destroys your aromatase" story is biochemically implausible and contradicted by human metabolite data. When EQ appears to lower estradiol, the boring explanation — weak aromatization, metabolism leaning toward estrone, and any strong androgen turning the aromatase gene down — is the honest one. Contested (PMID 26199450; 16308875)

Lever 3 — the progesterone-receptor channel is the true 19-nor signature. Removing C19 gives the estrane skeleton intrinsic progesterone-receptor agonism — the nortestosterone series is literally the chemical parent of the 19-nor progestins. Established (PMID 4065046) This is the mechanism behind "Deca dick / Deca gyno" and "tren gyno": PR activation in breast tissue plus raised prolactin drives gynecomastia and sexual dysfunction that is independent of estrogen, which is exactly why an aromatase inhibitor alone often fails to fix it — the lever you'd actually pull is dopaminergic (D2) prolactin control, not estrogen blockade. [Evidence-human] (PMID 19941958) Trenbolone sits at the top of this axis: in vitro it binds PR with higher affinity than progesterone itself. [Evidence-in vitro] (PMID 11252818) Nandrolone's PR activity is weaker but clinically real and famous. Established (PMID 19941958) Trestolone binds PR at about progesterone's level. [Evidence-in vitro] (PMID 9879986) Boldenone and DHB — the non-19-nors — largely don't have this channel, which is a clean tell that they belong to a different chemical family.

Why trenbolone is the harshest of the family

Tren stacks every aggravating lever at once. It grips the AR as hard as DHT and barely binds SHBG, so a huge free fraction stays active. Established (PMID 11252818) Its triene structure removes both metabolic off-switches — no aromatization, no 5α-reduction — so it is maximally potent in every tissue with no estrogenic "softening" and no cardioprotective estradiol to offset the lipid hit. Established (PMID 21266670) It carries the strongest PR/prolactin liability of the group. [Evidence-in vitro] (PMID 11252818) On top of that it engages the glucocorticoid receptor [Evidence-rodent] (PMID 24928725), is lipophilic enough to cross into the brain (the basis for the reported insomnia, night sweats, mood/aggression effects, with a rodent hippocampal-accumulation signal) [Evidence-rodent] (PMID 25461682), and — because it doesn't aromatize — strips away the estrogen that normally cushions the cardiovascular system.

The crucial caveat: there are no human efficacy or safety trials for trenbolone. Its "potency" is receptor-binding and rat data; its cardiovascular and renal risk is extrapolated from mixed-AAS cohorts (long-term users showed LVEF ~52% vs ~63% in non-users, with dose-dependent coronary plaque). [Evidence-human, class not tren-specific] (PMID 28533317) Even the beloved "SARM-like, prostate-sparing" claim is dose-dependent in rats — at high doses tren enlarged the rat prostate by 68%. [Evidence-rodent] (PMID 21266670) So "harshest" is a mechanistic verdict backed by anecdote and animal data, not a human trial. That distinction is the point.

What to watch (monitoring, not a protocol)

For this whole family: lipids (expect HDL to fall — worst with the non-aromatizing tren/DHB), hematocrit (EQ's signature; also tren, MENT), LH/FSH and endogenous testosterone (all suppress the axis; nandrolone's long ester makes recovery slow), and — uniquely for the true 19-nors — prolactin, because a normal estradiol reading alongside breast or sexual symptoms points to the progestin/prolactin route rather than estrogen. [Evidence-human/class] (PMID 26370185; 39212549; 39370302) This is educational mechanism content — what to monitor and discuss with a clinician — not medical advice, and deliberately contains no doses.

Key takeaways

  • A '19-nor' is just testosterone minus one carbon (C19). Chemically, only nandrolone, trenbolone and trestolone/MENT are true 19-nors (estranes); boldenone (EQ) and DHB are Δ1 androstanes that gym culture wrongly lumps in — they still have C19.
  • The true 19-nors' defining feature is intrinsic progesterone-receptor activity plus prolactin elevation, causing gyno and 'Deca/tren dick' that an aromatase inhibitor does NOT fix — the mechanism is progestin/prolactin, not estrogen. Trenbolone binds PR even harder than progesterone itself.
  • Each compound dodges 5α-reductase differently: nandrolone is reduced to a WEAKER metabolite (prostate-milder), trenbolone isn't a substrate at all, MENT's 7α-methyl blocks it, and DHB is already reduced (so finasteride can't help it).
  • '19-nor = no estrogen' is false: nandrolone and boldenone aromatize weakly, and trestolone aromatizes into 7α-methyl-estradiol, a POTENT estrogen. Only trenbolone and DHB truly cannot aromatize.
  • Trenbolone is the harshest because it stacks every aggravating lever: DHT-level AR grip, no aromatization AND no 5α-reduction (full potency everywhere, no estrogenic cushion), the strongest PR/prolactin load, plus GR and CNS effects.
  • Evidence honesty gap: nandrolone is a real approved drug with RCTs; trestolone has small human contraceptive trials; but trenbolone, boldenone and DHB have essentially NO human trials — their profiles are rat, in-vitro, and anecdote. Two popular EQ claims ('50% aromatization', 'horse estrogen destroys aromatase') are folklore.

Trenbolone, boldenone, and DHB have zero controlled human efficacy or safety trials; their comparative "potency" and side-effect rankings rest on in-vitro receptor binding, rodent studies, and anecdote.
