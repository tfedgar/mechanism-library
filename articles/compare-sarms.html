SARMs, compared

Seven compounds sold under one label — but only five actually touch the androgen receptor, and none of them are the "all the muscle, none of the downsides" pill the marketing promises.

adversarially verified · revised28 citations

The one-paragraph version

"SARM" gets slapped on a shelf of very different molecules. Five of the seven here really are selective androgen receptor modulators — they bind the same receptor testosterone uses (ostarine, ligandrol, RAD-140, S-23, andarine). Two are impostors: cardarine is a PPARδ agonist (a metabolic gene-switch, not a hormone) and MK-677 is a ghrelin-receptor agonist (a growth-hormone trigger). Neither touches the androgen receptor at all — cardarine works through a completely different nuclear receptor Established (PMID 14525954), and MK-677 through the ghrelin receptor Established (PMID 34737341). Across the real SARMs, the story rhymes: modest-to-real muscle signal, genuine suppression of your own testosterone, an unfavorable lipid shift, and a stack of liver-injury case reports — with the "human proof" ranging from one 21-day trial to literally zero human trials. This is mechanism education, not advice, and there are no protocols here — only what a clinician would watch.

Reconciling the differences: one receptor, many dials

The five real SARMs all do the same core trick, then diverge on how loud and how selective. Each binds the androgen receptor (AR) — the exact pocket testosterone and DHT use — but as a non-steroidal molecule. That non-steroidal shape matters twice over: the body can't aromatize it to estrogen or 5-alpha-reduce it to a stronger androgen Established (PMID 19357508), and it bends the receptor into a slightly different shape than testosterone does. That altered shape decides which "helper" proteins (coactivators) the receptor grabs, and because different tissues carry different helpers, the same drug can shout "grow" in muscle and bone while only whispering in the prostate [Evidence-rodent] (PMID 18801930). That coactivator-recruitment story is the textbook explanation for "selectivity" — but be honest: it's mostly demonstrated in rodents and on sibling compounds, not proven for each molecule in humans.

Selectivity is a spectrum, not a switch — and S-23 sits at the wrong end. Andarine is the poster child: in castrated rats it rebuilt muscle and bone while leaving the prostate at ~16–17% of the DHT effect [Evidence-rodent] (PMID 16099859). At the other extreme, S-23 is a full agonist in muscle AND prostate — it was literally designed as a male birth-control pill because it shuts down sperm production so hard [Evidence-rodent] (PMID 18772237). So calling S-23 "selective" is almost a contradiction. Ligandrol and RAD-140 sit in between: they look prostate-sparing on paper, but the human reality checks are sobering — RAD-140 still raised PSA in trial patients, and ligandrol's "no PSA change" came from a 21-day study far too short to prove anything about the prostate [Evidence-human, limited] (PMID 34565686) (PMID 22459616).

"Selective" never meant "no suppression." Every real SARM here reads to your brain as "plenty of androgen around," so the hypothalamus and pituitary dial down LH and FSH, and your own testosterone falls [Evidence-human] (PMID 22459616). The non-steroidal chemistry removes the estrogen arm of the problem, not the shutdown. And the lipid story is consistent and unflattering: these compounds lower HDL ("good" cholesterol) — a cardiovascular red flag that a 3-week trial won't catch but months of use might [Evidence-human] (PMID 22459616) (PMID 26401842).

The liver is the recurring casualty — and it's a different mechanism than the old orals. People assume "not a steroid = safe for the liver." Wrong. Ostarine, ligandrol, and especially RAD-140 all carry published drug-induced liver-injury case reports, often cholestatic (bile-flow) with jaundice [Evidence-human] (PMID 34368386) (PMID 32637435) (PMID 36561105). Two honest caveats: this is not the 17-alpha-alkyl toxicity of old oral steroids (SARMs aren't alkylated — it's a different, idiosyncratic injury), and much of the signal comes from higher, longer, real-world doses and contaminated grey-market products, not the clean compound at trial doses. The tightly-controlled trials mostly didn't see it — which tells you the danger scales with dose, duration, and product quality.

The evidence tiers are wildly unequal — read the table as a ladder. Ostarine has the deepest human dataset (Phase II and III), and its most important lesson is deflating: it reliably added lean mass but failed to translate that into better physical function in its big cancer trials [Evidence-human] (PMID 22031847) (PMID 23499390). "More muscle on the scan" did not equal "works as a drug." Ligandrol rests on a single 21-day trial. RAD-140's only human dosing was in cancer patients, where it hit dose-limiting liver toxicity. And S-23 and andarine have no human efficacy trials at all — every flattering number is a rat. So when someone quotes confident dosing and results, ask which tier they're standing on.

Now the two impostors — the whole reason this comparison exists. They're sold in the same "SARM" bins, but mechanistically they're strangers:

  • - Cardarine (GW501516) is a PPARδ agonist, not an androgen at all. It flips a metabolic gene-switch in muscle, shifting fuel-burning from sugar toward fat and improving blood lipids in short human trials [Established/Evidence-human] (PMID 14525954) (PMID 17110604). Its "exercise in a pill" endurance reputation is a mouse study (PMID 18674809), not a human performance trial. The headline fact: GSK killed it after long-term rodent studies grew tumors in multiple organs — there is no established safe human dose and no long-term human safety data [Evidence-rodent] (PMID 30679176) (PMID 22037942).
  • - MK-677 (ibutamoren) is a ghrelin-receptor (GHSR-1a) agonist — it tricks the "hunger hormone" switch into telling the pituitary to release more of your own growth hormone, raising IGF-1 by roughly 40–90% [Established/Evidence-human] (PMID 34737341) (PMID 18981485). It has zero androgen-receptor activity, so it does not suppress testosterone the way the SARMs do. Its trade-off is the opposite one: because GH opposes insulin, MK-677 raises blood sugar and worsens insulin sensitivity, and in its year-long trial the extra lean mass came with no clear strength or function gain — plus a heart-failure safety signal that stopped a separate trial early [Evidence-human] (PMID 18981485) (PMID 21067829).

The practical upshot: any "recomp stack" marketing that lumps cardarine and MK-677 in with the androgen SARMs is conflating three unrelated receptors. Knowing which dial a compound actually turns is the difference between understanding the risk and guessing at it.

What to monitor (harm-reduction, not endorsement)

For the AR-SARMs, a clinician would track: a liver panel (ALT, AST, ALP, and especially bilirubin — cholestatic injury is the best-documented serious harm); hormones (total/free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG) for shutdown; a fasting lipid panel for HDL suppression; and hematocrit. For MK-677, the priority flips to glucose/insulin/HbA1c plus watching for edema and cardiac symptoms. For cardarine, add CK/creatine kinase (a real-world case had massive rhabdomyolysis) and GI vigilance given the cancer signal (PMID 34678947). Across all seven, remember the grey-market products are frequently mislabeled or spiked — so any lab abnormality may reflect an undisclosed compound, not the one on the label (PMID 39145153). None of this is medical advice; it's what someone evaluating these would watch to catch harm early.

Key takeaways

  • Five of the seven are real SARMs (ostarine, ligandrol, RAD-140, S-23, andarine) — cardarine is a PPARδ agonist and MK-677 is a ghrelin-receptor/GH agonist, and neither touches the androgen receptor at all.
  • "Selective" is a spectrum, not a switch: andarine is genuinely prostate-sparing in rodents, while S-23 is a full agonist in muscle AND prostate (it was built as a male contraceptive). Even the 'sparing' compounds still raised PSA (RAD-140) or were only tested too briefly to prove prostate safety (ligandrol).
  • 'Selective' never meant 'no suppression' or 'safe' — every AR-SARM still shuts down your own testosterone, lowers HDL, and carries published cholestatic liver-injury case reports (worst signal: RAD-140).
  • Evidence tiers are wildly unequal: ostarine has Phase II/III human data (but failed physical-function endpoints), ligandrol has one 21-day trial, RAD-140's only human dosing was in cancer patients with dose-limiting liver toxicity, and S-23 and andarine have zero human efficacy trials.
  • The two impostors have opposite trade-offs from the SARMs: cardarine improves lipids but was abandoned over rodent multi-organ cancers, and MK-677 doesn't suppress testosterone but raises blood sugar/insulin resistance and showed a heart-failure signal — its extra lean mass came with no strength gain.
