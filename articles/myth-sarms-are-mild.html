The "SARMs are mild, no PCT needed" myth

Androgenic SARMs suppress your own hormones and wreck your cholesterol in human data — and the "test base" and "nofap" workarounds don't do what the forums claim.

adversarially verified · revised13 citations

The pitch you've heard a hundred times

"SARMs are basically steroids-lite. They barely touch your natural test, so you don't even need a real PCT — maybe a bottle of some OTC 'natural test booster,' maybe nothing. Just run it, keep your gains, no shutdown."

It's a clean story. It's also wrong in the places that matter — and the interesting part is that the evidence proving it wrong comes from the exact same studies the "mild" crowd points to when they say SARMs are safe.

The myth

The myth has three moving parts:

  1. 1. "They barely suppress." SARMs supposedly leave your hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis — the brain-to-testicle signalling loop that makes your own testosterone — mostly untouched.
  2. 2. "So no PCT is needed." Because there's supposedly no real shutdown, there's nothing to recover from.
  3. 3. "And a 'test base' or a nofap streak covers you." Either you run a little testosterone underneath the SARM as insurance, or you "keep your natural test high" through no-fap so the SARM has less to suppress.

Every part of this leaks.

The reality: they suppress, in humans, on purpose

Here's the thing the marketing skips: an androgenic SARM works by activating the androgen receptor (AR) — the identical receptor testosterone and DHT use. That's the whole point; it's how they build muscle. But that same receptor lives in your hypothalamus and pituitary, and when it senses "plenty of androgen around," it dials down the signal (GnRH → LH/FSH) that tells your testicles to make testosterone. Any effective AR agonist suppresses your own production — it's not a side effect, it's the mechanism running in reverse Established (PMID 22459616).

This isn't a rodent guess. Look at the single most-cited human SARM trial — the one people quote to argue LGD-4033 is safe. In a placebo-controlled study of 76 healthy young men taking ligandrol for just 21 days, researchers saw dose-dependent suppression of total testosterone, sex-hormone-binding globulin (SHBG), and — at the top dose — FSH and free testosterone too Evidence (PMID 22459616). Three weeks. Low milligram doses. In healthy men. And that's the low end of what people actually run.

The same fingerprint shows up across the class:

  • - Ostarine (enobosarm) — the most-studied SARM of all, with real Phase 2/3 human trials for lean mass (PMID 22031847) — suppresses the HPG axis through the same androgen-feedback loop that any AR agonist triggers; SARM safety reviews treat this as a class property Evidence (PMID 26401842).
  • - RAD-140 (testolone) — its only controlled human dosing was in a cancer trial, but even there, SHBG dropped in 18 out of 18 patients, a hard biomarker proving the drug is systemically engaging the androgen receptor everywhere, including where it drives shutdown Evidence (PMID 34565686). A published case describes reversible hypogonadism after supplement use — though that product was adulterated with several other hormones, so RAD-140 alone can't be cleanly blamed for it Contested (PMID 39145153).

So "barely suppresses" is false. In the very trials cited to sell them as gentle, androgenic SARMs measurably shut down your natural testosterone.

The part nobody puts on the label: your cholesterol tanks

Suppression is only half the story. The same LGD-4033 trial that showed hormone shutdown also showed HDL ("good") cholesterol and triglycerides falling dose-dependently over those same 21 days Evidence (PMID 22459616). A drop in HDL is a pro-atherogenic shift — the direction that, sustained over months, raises cardiovascular risk. It's a class effect: oral AR agonists ramp up hepatic lipase and reshuffle lipoprotein handling, and SARM safety reviews consistently flag the HDL hit Evidence (PMID 37218811, PMID 26401842).

Here's why this matters more than the shutdown: a 3-week study is far too short to show what chronically crushed HDL does to your arteries. In the trial the lipids bounced back after stopping. But real-world users don't run three weeks — they run months, at doses never tested in a controlled human trial Established (PMID 22459616). The reassuring "it reversed" is measuring a window that doesn't resemble how people actually use these.

And that's before the liver. All three of these compounds — LGD-4033, RAD-140, ostarine — carry repeated published case reports of cholestatic drug-induced liver injury (jaundice, itching, sky-high bilirubin) in real-world users, even though the short controlled trials showed no liver-enzyme changes Evidence (PMID 32637435, PMID 36561105, PMID 34368386). That gap — clean at 21 days, ugly in the case-report pile — is the whole point: short trials don't capture the harm profile of real use.

Why "no PCT needed" doesn't follow

Post-cycle therapy exists to restart a suppressed axis. Since androgenic SARMs do suppress the axis — demonstrated in humans — the premise that there's nothing to recover from is simply untrue Established (PMID 22459616, PMID 34565686).

Two honesty caveats, because this is where the brand lives:

  • - Recovery in the trials looked fast — but the trials were tiny exposures. Hormones normalised after 21 days of low-dose LGD Evidence (PMID 22459616). Whether the axis bounces back the same way after months of supratherapeutic dosing is genuinely unstudied — nobody has the data, and anyone claiming certainty either way is guessing Speculation.
  • - This piece is not telling you which drugs to run PCT with or how. It's saying the "you don't need to think about recovery" premise is built on a claim the human data contradicts.

The "test base" ritual

"Run a low dose of testosterone underneath the SARM so you don't shut down." Think about what that actually is: you're adding an exogenous androgen to solve suppression caused by an androgen. A test base doesn't prevent shutdown — exogenous testosterone is a suppressive androgen; it replaces your own production rather than protecting it Established (PMID 22459616, PMID 26401842). What a "test base" really does is add a second suppressive compound (with its own aromatization, its own lipid and cardiovascular load) on top of the first. It's not insurance against shutdown; it's a heavier androgen load that happens to keep systemic testosterone from reading "low" on a blood test while your natural signalling stays off. Calling that "mild" is the myth eating its own tail.

The "nofap as a test base" ritual

This one's popular and worth being precise about. The claim: abstaining from ejaculation keeps your natural testosterone elevated enough to blunt SARM suppression.

The mechanism says no. Suppression happens because the drug activates the androgen receptor in your brain and switches off the LH/FSH signal — a receptor-level event that has nothing to do with your behaviour Established (PMID 22459616). Even if abstinence nudged testosterone (the human evidence for a meaningful, lasting rise is thin and contested), a small endogenous bump can't override a receptor being actively told "there's plenty of androgen here, stand down." You cannot out-behaviour a pharmacological feedback signal. Treating a nofap streak as a "test base" is Speculation with no mechanistic path to work — the drug is suppressing you upstream of anything abstinence touches Established (PMID 22459616, PMID 34565686).

What to actually watch

Not a protocol — signals. If someone is using these, the monitorable red flags in the human data are:

  • - Hormones: total and free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG — to catch the shutdown these drugs demonstrably cause Evidence (PMID 22459616, PMID 34565686).
  • - Lipids: a fasting panel, expecting HDL to fall — the cardiovascular flag that a short trial can't fully price in Evidence (PMID 22459616, PMID 37218811).
  • - Liver: ALT, AST, and especially bilirubin — the documented injury is cholestatic, so bilirubin can spike even when transaminases look mild; jaundice, dark urine, or itching means stop and see a doctor Evidence (PMID 32637435, PMID 34368386).
  • - Product reality: grey-market SARMs are routinely mislabelled, underdosed, overdosed, or spiked with actual steroids and other hormones — so the bottle often isn't what the label says, and your labs may not reflect "pure" anything Evidence (PMID 39145153).

Bottom line

"SARMs are mild, no PCT needed" survives only if you don't read the trials it's supposedly based on. Those trials show androgenic SARMs suppress your own hormones and drop your HDL — in humans, in weeks, at low doses. "Test base" adds another suppressive androgen; "nofap as a test base" fights a receptor with a habit. None of them make an AR agonist stop being an AR agonist. This is education, not medical advice — and definitely not a green light.

Key takeaways

  • Androgenic SARMs suppress your own testosterone by design: they activate the same androgen receptor testosterone uses, and that receptor drives the brain's shutdown signal. The single most-cited 'SARMs are safe' human trial (LGD-4033, 76 healthy men, 21 days) showed dose-dependent drops in testosterone, SHBG, and FSH [Evidence] (PMID 22459616).
  • The same trial showed HDL ('good') cholesterol and triglycerides falling — a pro-atherogenic shift — and this HDL hit is a documented SARM class effect [Evidence] (PMID 22459616, PMID 37218811).
  • RAD-140's only human dosing (a cancer trial) saw SHBG drop in 18/18 patients — hard proof of systemic androgen-receptor engagement — and ostarine, the most-studied SARM, suppresses the axis through the same feedback loop; suppression is a class property, not a quirk [Evidence] (PMID 34565686, PMID 22031847, PMID 26401842).
  • 'No PCT needed' rests on a false premise: since these drugs demonstrably suppress the HPG axis, there is something to recover from. Recovery after tiny trial exposures looked fast, but recovery after months of real-world dosing is unstudied [Established/Speculation] (PMID 22459616).
  • A 'test base' doesn't prevent shutdown — exogenous testosterone is itself a suppressive androgen; you're stacking a second suppressive compound, not buying insurance [Established] (PMID 22459616, PMID 26401842).
  • 'Nofap as a test base' has no mechanistic path to work: suppression is a receptor-level feedback event upstream of anything abstinence could influence, and can't be out-behaved [Established/Speculation] (PMID 22459616).
  • Monitor hormones (T, LH, FSH, SHBG), a fasting lipid panel (expect HDL to fall), and liver labs including bilirubin (injury is cholestatic); grey-market products are frequently mislabelled or spiked with undisclosed hormones [Evidence] (PMID 34368386, PMID 39145153).

Human evidence for these compounds is thin and short. The cornerstone LGD-4033 data come from a single 21-day Phase 1 trial in 76 healthy men (PMID 22459616); RAD-140's only controlled human dosing is in metastatic breast-cancer patients, not athletes (PMID 34565686); ostarine has the most human data but never gained approval (PMID 22031847, 23499390, 27138015). Ostarine-specific HPG suppression in humans rests on class-level pharmacology and case/PED reports rather than a dedicated suppression trial (PMID 26401842); liver-injury and hypogonadism evidence is largely case reports (lower tier than RCTs, but a consistent, repeated signal). The RAD-140 hypogonadism case report (PMID 39145153) involved a supplement adulterated with testosterone, estradiol, GH, MK-677, and cardarine, so RAD-140 alone is not cleanly implicated there — it is included as a contested, real-world signal, with the 18/18 SHBG drop (PMID 34565686) carrying the actual target-engagement weight. Recovery/reversibility data exist only for short, low-dose exposures — the long-term suppression, cardiovascular, and fertility consequences of real-world dosing are genuinely unknown. The 'nofap raises testosterone meaningfully' premise is itself contested and thin; the debunk here rests on the established suppression mechanism, not on resolving that side claim. No dosing or protocols are given. Educational only, not medical advice, and not an endorsement of use — all three compounds are unapproved and WADA-banned.
